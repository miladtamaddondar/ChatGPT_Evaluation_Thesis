1. Find the eigenvalues of \( A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix} \) by solving \( \det(A - \lambda I) = 0 \): \( \det\begin{pmatrix} 1-\lambda & -2 \\ 1 & 3-\lambda \end{pmatrix} = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0 \).
2. Solve for \( \lambda \): \( \lambda = \frac{4 \pm \sqrt{(-4)^2 - 4 \cdot 1 \cdot 5}}{2} = \frac{4 \pm \sqrt{16-20}}{2} = \frac{4 \pm 2i}{2} = 2 \pm i \).
3. For \( \lambda = 2+i \), solve \( (A - (2+i)I)\vec{v} = 0 \): \( \begin{pmatrix} 1-(2+i) & -2 \\ 1 & 3-(2+i) \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix} \).
4. Set up the eigenvector equation: \( (-1-i)v_1 - 2v_2 = 0 \), \( v_1 + (1-i)v_2 = 0 \). From the first equation, \( v_1 = \frac{-2}{-1-i}v_2 \).
5. Simplify \( \frac{-2}{-1-i} = \frac{2}{1+i} = \frac{2(1-i)}{(1+i)(1-i)} = \frac{2(1-i)}{2} = 1-i \), so \( v_1 = (1-i)v_2 \).
6. Take \( v_2 = 1 \), so eigenvector for \( 2+i \) is \( \vec{v}_1 = \begin{pmatrix} 1-i \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \).
7. Similarly, for \( \lambda = 2-i \), eigenvector is \( \vec{v}_2 = \begin{pmatrix} 1+i \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \).
8. Form \( P = \begin{pmatrix} 1-i & 1+i \\ 1 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \) and \( C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \) (since \( C \) must be of the form \( \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix} \) and \( 2 \pm i \) corresponds to \( a=2, b=1 \)).
9. Final answer: \( P = \begin{pmatrix} 1-i & 1+i \\ 1 & 1 \end{pmatrix},\ C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \).
