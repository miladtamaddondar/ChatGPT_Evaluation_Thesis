1. The QR decomposition expresses \( A \) as \( A = QR \), where \( Q \) has orthonormal columns (already given) and \( R \) is upper triangular.  
2. To find \( R \), use \( R = Q^T A \) since \( Q \) is orthonormal (\( Q^T Q = I \)).  
3. Compute \( Q^T \): \( Q^T = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{5}{6} & \frac{1}{6} & -\frac{3}{6} & \frac{1}{6} \\ -\frac{1}{6} & \frac{5}{6} & \frac{1}{6} & \frac{3}{6} \end{bmatrix} \).  
4. Multiply \( Q^T \) by \( A \):  
\[
R = Q^T A = 
\begin{bmatrix}
\frac{5}{6} & \frac{1}{6} & -\frac{3}{6} & \frac{1}{6} \\
-\frac{1}{6} & \frac{5}{6} & \frac{1}{6} & \frac{3}{6}
\end{bmatrix}
\begin{bmatrix}
5 & 9 \\
1 & 7 \\
-3 & -5 \\
1 & 5
\end{bmatrix}
\]
5. Compute the (1,1) entry: \( \frac{5}{6} \cdot 5 + \frac{1}{6} \cdot 1 + (-\frac{3}{6}) \cdot (-3) + \frac{1}{6} \cdot 1 = \frac{25}{6} + \frac{1}{6} + \frac{9}{6} + \frac{1}{6} = \frac{36}{6} = 6 \).  
6. Compute the (1,2) entry: \( \frac{5}{6} \cdot 9 + \frac{1}{6} \cdot 7 + (-\frac{3}{6}) \cdot (-5) + \frac{1}{6} \cdot 5 = \frac{45}{6} + \frac{7}{6} + \frac{15}{6} + \frac{5}{6} = \frac{72}{6} = 12 \).  
7. Compute the (2,1) entry: \( -\frac{1}{6} \cdot 5 + \frac{5}{6} \cdot 1 + \frac{1}{6} \cdot (-3) + \frac{3}{6} \cdot 1 = -\frac{5}{6} + \frac{5}{6} - \frac{3}{6} + \frac{3}{6} = 0 \).  
8. Compute the (2,2) entry: \( -\frac{1}{6} \cdot 9 + \frac{5}{6} \cdot 7 + \frac{1}{6} \cdot (-5) + \frac{3}{6} \cdot 5 = -\frac{9}{6} + \frac{35}{6} - \frac{5}{6} + \frac{15}{6} = \frac{36}{6} = 6 \).  
9. Thus, \( R = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 12 \\ 0 & 6 \end{bmatrix} \).
