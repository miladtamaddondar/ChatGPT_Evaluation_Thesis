1. The coordinate vector $\left[ \mathbf{x} \right]_B = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}$ means $\mathbf{x} = 3\mathbf{b}_1 + 2\mathbf{b}_2$ where $\mathbf{b}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}$ and $\mathbf{b}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix}$.
2. Multiply the first basis vector by 3: $3\mathbf{b}_1 = 3 \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix}$.
3. Multiply the second basis vector by 2: $2\mathbf{b}_2 = 2 \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix}$.
4. Add the results to get $\mathbf{x}$: $\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 7 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}$.
5. The vector $\mathbf{x}$ is $\begin{bmatrix} 7 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}$.
