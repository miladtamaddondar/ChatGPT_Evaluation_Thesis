1. To find the matrix of \( T \) with respect to basis \( \mathcal{B} \), we need to express \( T(\mathbf{b}_1) \) and \( T(\mathbf{b}_2) \) as linear combinations of \( \mathbf{b}_1 \) and \( \mathbf{b}_2 \). 
2. Compute \( T(\mathbf{b}_1) = A\mathbf{b}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \). 
3. Set \( T(\mathbf{b}_1) = c_1 \mathbf{b}_1 + c_2 \mathbf{b}_2 \), so \( \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} = c_1 \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} + c_2 \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} \). 
4. This gives the system: \( 2 = c_1 + 5c_2 \), \( 2 = c_1 + 4c_2 \). 
5. Subtract the second equation from the first: \( (c_1 + 5c_2) - (c_1 + 4c_2) = 2 - 2 \implies c_2 = 0 \). 
6. Substitute \( c_2 = 0 \) into the first equation: \( 2 = c_1 \implies c_1 = 2 \). 
7. Thus, \( T(\mathbf{b}_1) = 2\mathbf{b}_1 + 0\mathbf{b}_2 \), so the first column of the \( \mathcal{B} \)-matrix is \( \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \). 
8. Compute \( T(\mathbf{b}_2) = A\mathbf{b}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 9 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \). 
9. Set \( T(\mathbf{b}_2) = d_1 \mathbf{b}_1 + d_2 \mathbf{b}_2 \), so \( \begin{bmatrix} 9 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} = d_1 \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} + d_2 \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} \). 
10. This gives the system: \( 9 = d_1 + 5d_2 \), \( 7 = d_1 + 4d_2 \). 
11. Subtract the second equation from the first: \( (d_1 + 5d_2) - (d_1 + 4d_2) = 9 - 7 \implies d_2 = 2 \). 
12. Substitute \( d_2 = 2 \) into the first equation: \( 9 = d_1 + 10 \implies d_1 = -1 \). 
13. Thus, \( T(\mathbf{b}_2) = -1\mathbf{b}_1 + 2\mathbf{b}_2 \), so the second column of the \( \mathcal{B} \)-matrix is \( \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \). 
14. Therefore, the \( \mathcal{B} \)-matrix for \( T \) is \( [T]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 0 & 2 \end{bmatrix} \).
