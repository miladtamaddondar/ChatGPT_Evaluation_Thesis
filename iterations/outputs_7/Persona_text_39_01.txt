1. The matrix equation $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 4 \\ 2 & -6 & 8 \end{bmatrix} = \mathbf{u}\mathbf{v}^T$ means the $2 \times 3$ matrix is the outer product of $\mathbf{u}$ and $\mathbf{v}$.
2. Let $\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} v_1 \\ v_2 \\ v_3 \end{bmatrix}$, so $\mathbf{u}\mathbf{v}^T = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} v_1 & v_2 & v_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1v_1 & 1v_2 & 1v_3 \\ 2v_1 & 2v_2 & 2v_3 \end{bmatrix}$.
3. Set the corresponding entries equal: $1v_1 = 1$, $1v_2 = -3$, $1v_3 = 4$, $2v_1 = 2$, $2v_2 = -6$, $2v_3 = 8$.
4. Solve for $v_1$: $1v_1 = 1 \implies v_1 = 1$.
5. Solve for $v_2$: $1v_2 = -3 \implies v_2 = -3$.
6. Solve for $v_3$: $1v_3 = 4 \implies v_3 = 4$.
7. The vector $\mathbf{v}$ is $\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix}$.
