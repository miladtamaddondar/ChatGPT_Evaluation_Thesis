1. We are given \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 9 \\ 1 & 7 \\ -3 & -5 \\ 1 & 5 \end{bmatrix}\) and \(Q = \begin{bmatrix} 5/6 & -1/6 \\ 1/6 & 5/6 \\ -3/6 & 1/6 \\ 1/6 & 3/6 \end{bmatrix}\); we need to find an upper triangular matrix \(R\) such that \(A = QR\).  
2. Since \(Q\) has orthonormal columns (from Gram-Schmidt), \(R = Q^T A\) gives us the upper triangular matrix for the QR decomposition.  
3. Compute \(Q^T\): \(Q^T = \begin{bmatrix} 5/6 & 1/6 & -3/6 & 1/6 \\ -1/6 & 5/6 & 1/6 & 3/6 \end{bmatrix}\).  
4. Compute \(R = Q^T A\):  \[R = \begin{bmatrix} 5/6 & 1/6 & -3/6 & 1/6 \\ -1/6 & 5/6 & 1/6 & 3/6 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 9 \\ 1 & 7 \\ -3 & -5 \\ 1 & 5 \end{bmatrix}\]  
5. Compute the first row of \(R\):  First column: \((5/6) \cdot 5 + (1/6) \cdot 1 + (-3/6) \cdot (-3) + (1/6) \cdot 1 = 25/6 + 1/6 + 9/6 + 1/6 = 36/6 = 6\)  Second column: \((5/6) \cdot 9 + (1/6) \cdot 7 + (-3/6) \cdot (-5) + (1/6) \cdot 5 = 45/6 + 7/6 + 15/6 + 5/6 = 72/6 = 12\)  
6. Compute the second row of \(R\):  First column: \((-1/6) \cdot 5 + (5/6) \cdot 1 + (1/6) \cdot (-3) + (3/6) \cdot 1 = -5/6 + 5/6 - 3/6 + 3/6 = 0\)  Second column: \((-1/6) \cdot 9 + (5/6) \cdot 7 + (1/6) \cdot (-5) + (3/6) \cdot 5 = -9/6 + 35/6 - 5/6 + 15/6 = 36/6 = 6\)  
7. Therefore, \(R = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 12 \\ 0 & 6 \end{bmatrix}\) is upper triangular, as required by QR decomposition.
