To find a basis for \(\mathbb{R}^2\) consisting of \(\mathbf{v}_1\) and another eigenvector \(\mathbf{v}_2\) of \(A\), first find the eigenvalues of \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 0.6 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.7 \end{bmatrix}\) by solving \(\det(A - \lambda I) = 0\): \(\det\left(\begin{bmatrix} 0.6-\lambda & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.7-\lambda \end{bmatrix}\right) = (0.6-\lambda)(0.7-\lambda) - (0.3)(0.4) = 0\).
2. Expand: \((0.6-\lambda)(0.7-\lambda) - 0.12 = 0 \implies 0.42 - 0.6\lambda - 0.7\lambda + \lambda^2 - 0.12 = 0 \implies \lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3 = 0\).
3. Solve the quadratic: \(\lambda = \frac{1.3 \pm \sqrt{1.69 - 1.2}}{2} = \frac{1.3 \pm \sqrt{0.49}}{2} = \frac{1.3 \pm 0.7}{2}\), so \(\lambda_1 = 1\), \(\lambda_2 = 0.3\).
4. \(\mathbf{v}_1\) is given and corresponds to \(\lambda_1 = 1\): \(\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 3/7 \\ 4/7 \end{bmatrix}\).
5. Find an eigenvector \(\mathbf{v}_2\) for \(\lambda_2 = 0.3\): Solve \((A - 0.3I)\mathbf{v}_2 = 0\), so \(A - 0.3I = \begin{bmatrix} 0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.4 \end{bmatrix}\).
6. Write the system: \(0.3x + 0.3y = 0\), \(0.4x + 0.4y = 0\), which reduces to \(x + y = 0\).
7. The general solution is \(\mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix}\) (or any scalar multiple).
8. Therefore, a basis for \(\mathbb{R}^2\) consisting of eigenvectors is \(\left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 3/7 \\ 4/7 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} \right\}\).
