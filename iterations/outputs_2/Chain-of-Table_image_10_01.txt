2. The pivot columns in the echelon form are columns 1, 3, and 4, so the basis for \(\text{Col }A\) is \(\left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} -4 \\ 5 \\ -3 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \right\}\). 
3. The number of pivot columns is 3, so \(\dim(\text{Col }A) = 3\). 
4. To find a basis for \(\text{Nul }A\), solve \(A\vec{x} = 0\): Let \(x_2 = s\), \(x_4 = t\), then from the last nonzero row, \(5x_4 = 0 \implies x_4 = 0\), from the second nonzero row, \(5x_3 - 7x_4 = 0 \implies x_3 = 0\), and from the first row, \(x_1 - 3x_2 + 2x_3 - 4x_4 = 0 \implies x_1 = 3x_2\), so the general solution is \(\vec{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 3s \\ s \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}\). 
5. Therefore, a basis for \(\text{Nul }A\) is \(\left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \right\}\). 
6. The dimension of \(\text{Nul }A\) is 1.
