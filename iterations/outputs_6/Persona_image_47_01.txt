1. To find the orthogonal projection of \(\mathbf{b}\) onto \(\text{Col }A\), we use \(\mathbf{p} = A(A^TA)^{-1}A^T\mathbf{b}\).
2. Compute \(A^T A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 3 & 1 \\ -2 & 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1^2+3^2+(-2)^2 & 1\cdot5+3\cdot1+(-2)\cdot4 \\ 5\cdot1+1\cdot3+4\cdot(-2) & 5^2+1^2+4^2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 14 & 2 \\ 2 & 42 \end{bmatrix}\).
3. Compute \(A^T\mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ -2 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1\cdot4 + 3\cdot(-2) + (-2)\cdot(-3) \\ 5\cdot4 + 1\cdot(-2) + 4\cdot(-3) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 - 6 + 6 \\ 20 - 2 - 12 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix}\).
4. Compute \((A^T A)^{-1}\) using the formula for the inverse of a \(2 \times 2\) matrix: \(\frac{1}{ad-bc}\begin{bmatrix} d & -b \\ -c & a \end{bmatrix}\) where \(a=14, b=2, c=2, d=42\), so determinant is \(14\cdot42 - 2\cdot2 = 588 - 4 = 584\), so \((A^T A)^{-1} = \frac{1}{584} \begin{bmatrix} 42 & -2 \\ -2 & 14 \end{bmatrix}\).
5. Compute \((A^T A)^{-1}A^T\mathbf{b} = \frac{1}{584} \begin{bmatrix} 42 & -2 \\ -2 & 14 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix} = \frac{1}{584} \begin{bmatrix} 42\cdot4 + (-2)\cdot6 \\ -2\cdot4 + 14\cdot6 \end{bmatrix} = \frac{1}{584} \begin{bmatrix} 168 - 12 \\ -8 + 84 \end{bmatrix} = \frac{1}{584} \begin{bmatrix} 156 \\ 76 \end{bmatrix}\).
6. Simplify to get \(\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 156/584 \\ 76/584 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3/11 \\ 19/146 \end{bmatrix}\).
7. Compute the projection \(\mathbf{p} = A\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 3 & 1 \\ -2 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 3/11 \\ 19/146 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1\cdot(3/11) + 5\cdot(19/146) \\ 3\cdot(3/11) + 1\cdot(19/146) \\ -2\cdot(3/11) + 4\cdot(19/146) \end{bmatrix}\).
8. Calculate each entry: first, \(1\cdot(3/11) + 5\cdot(19/146) = 3/11 + 95/146 = 39.818/146 \approx 0.2726 + 0.6507 = 0.9233\), but let's use fractions: \(3/11 = 39.818/146\), \(95/146\), so \(39.818 + 95 = 134.818/146\), but \(3/11 = 39.818/146\) is not exact, so let's convert \(3/11\) to \(146\) denominator: \(3/11 = 3 \times 146 / (11 \times 146) = 438/1606\), but \(146/11 = 13.273\), so \(3/11 = 3 \times 13.273 = 39.818\), but let's just add as fractions: \(3/11 + 95/146 = (3 \times 146 + 95 \times 11)/(11 \times 146) = (438 + 1045)/1606 = 1483/1606\).
9. Second entry: \(3\cdot(3/11) + 19/146 = 9/11 + 19/146 = (9 \times 146 + 19 \times 11)/1606 = (1314 + 209)/1606 = 1523/1606\).
10. Third entry: \(-2\cdot(3/11) + 4\cdot(19/146) = -6/11 + 76/146 = (-6 \times 146 + 76 \times 11)/1606 = (-876 + 836)/1606 = -40/1606 = -20/803\).
11. So the orthogonal projection is \(\mathbf{p} = \begin{bmatrix} 1483/1606 \\ 1523/1606 \\ -20/803 \end{bmatrix}\).
12. The least-squares solution to \(A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{b}\) is \(\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 3/11 \\ 19/146 \end{bmatrix}\).
