1. To find the characteristic polynomial, compute \(\det(A - \lambda I)\) where \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 4 \end{bmatrix}\) and \(I\) is the identity matrix: \(\det\left(\begin{bmatrix} 2-\lambda & 1 \\ -1 & 4-\lambda \end{bmatrix}\right)\).  
2. Calculate the determinant: \((2-\lambda)(4-\lambda) - (1)(-1) = (2-\lambda)(4-\lambda) + 1\).  
3. Expand \((2-\lambda)(4-\lambda)\): \(2 \cdot 4 - 2\lambda - 4\lambda + \lambda^2 = 8 - 6\lambda + \lambda^2\).  
4. Add 1 to the expansion: \(8 - 6\lambda + \lambda^2 + 1 = \lambda^2 - 6\lambda + 9\).  
5. The characteristic polynomial is \(\lambda^2 - 6\lambda + 9\), verified by determinant expansion rules.  
6. Set the characteristic polynomial to zero to find eigenvalues: \(\lambda^2 - 6\lambda + 9 = 0\).  
7. Factor the quadratic: \((\lambda - 3)^2 = 0\).  
8. The eigenvalues are \(\lambda = 3\) (with algebraic multiplicity 2), verified by the quadratic formula or factoring.
