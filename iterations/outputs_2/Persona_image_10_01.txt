1. The matrix \( A \) and its echelon form are given as \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ -3 & 9 & -1 & 5 \\ 2 & -6 & 4 & -3 \\ -4 & 12 & 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( \text{Echelon form of } A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 & -7 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \). 
2. To find a basis for \(\text{Col }A\), select the columns of \(A\) that correspond to the pivot columns in the echelon form, which are columns 1, 3, and 4, so the basis for \(\text{Col }A\) is \(\left\{ \begin{bmatrix}1\\-3\\2\\-4\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}2\\-1\\4\\2\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}-4\\5\\-3\\7\end{bmatrix} \right\}\). 
3. The dimension of \(\text{Col }A\) is the number of pivot columns, which is 3. 
4. To find a basis for \(\text{Nul }A\), solve \(A\vec{x} = \vec{0}\) using the echelon form; let \(x_2 = s\) (free variable), and back-substitute: from row 3, \(5x_4 = 0 \implies x_4 = 0\); from row 2, \(5x_3 - 7x_4 = 0 \implies x_3 = 0\); from row 1, \(x_1 - 3x_2 = 0 \implies x_1 = 3x_2\); so the general solution is \(\vec{x} = \begin{bmatrix}3s\\s\\0\\0\end{bmatrix}\), so a basis for \(\text{Nul }A\) is \(\left\{ \begin{bmatrix}3\\1\\0\\0\end{bmatrix} \right\}\). 
5. The dimension of \(\text{Nul }A\) is the number of free variables, which is 1.
