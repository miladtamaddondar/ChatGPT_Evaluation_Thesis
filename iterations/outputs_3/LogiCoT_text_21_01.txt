1. To find a vector $\mathbf{x}$ such that $A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{b}$, write the system as $\begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 & -2 \\ -2 & 1 & 6 \\ 3 & -2 & -5\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}-1 \\ 7 \\ -3\end{bmatrix}$.
2. To check if a unique solution exists, compute $\det(A)$: $\det(A) = 1\begin{vmatrix}1 & 6 \\ -2 & -5\end{vmatrix} - 0\begin{vmatrix}-2 & 6 \\ 3 & -5\end{vmatrix} - 2\begin{vmatrix}-2 & 1 \\ 3 & -2\end{vmatrix}$.
3. Calculate minors: $\begin{vmatrix}1 & 6 \\ -2 & -5\end{vmatrix} = (1)(-5) - (6)(-2) = -5 + 12 = 7$ and $\begin{vmatrix}-2 & 1 \\ 3 & -2\end{vmatrix} = (-2)(-2) - (1)(3) = 4 - 3 = 1$.
4. Substitute minors: $\det(A) = 1 \cdot 7 - 0 \cdot (\cdots) - 2 \cdot 1 = 7 - 0 - 2 = 5$.
5. Since $\det(A) \neq 0$, $A$ is invertible, so the solution $\mathbf{x}$ is unique by the invertible matrix theorem.
6. To find $\mathbf{x}$, use $\mathbf{x} = A^{-1}\mathbf{b}$; first, compute $A^{-1}$ using the formula $A^{-1} = \frac{1}{\det(A)}\operatorname{adj}(A)$.
7. Compute the matrix of cofactors $C$ for $A$: $C_{11} = \begin{vmatrix}1 & 6 \\ -2 & -5\end{vmatrix} = 7$, $C_{12} = -\begin{vmatrix}-2 & 6 \\ 3 & -5\end{vmatrix} = -((-2)(-5) - (6)(3)) = -(10 - 18) = 8$, $C_{13} = \begin{vmatrix}-2 & 1 \\ 3 & -2\end{vmatrix} = 1$, $C_{21} = -\begin{vmatrix}0 & -2 \\ -2 & -5\end{vmatrix} = -((0)(-5) - (-2)(-2)) = -(0 - 4) = 4$, $C_{22} = \begin{vmatrix}1 & -2 \\ 3 & -5\end{vmatrix} = (1)(-5) - (-2)(3) = -5 + 6 = 1$, $C_{23} = -\begin{vmatrix}1 & 0 \\ 3 & -2\end{vmatrix} = -((1)(-2) - (0)(3)) = -(-2 - 0) = 2$, $C_{31} = \begin{vmatrix}0 & -2 \\ 1 & 6\end{vmatrix} = (0)(6) - (-2)(1) = 0 + 2 = 2$, $C_{32} = -\begin{vmatrix}1 & -2 \\ -2 & 6\end{vmatrix} = -((1)(6) - (-2)(-2)) = -(6 - 4) = -2$, $C_{33} = \begin{vmatrix}1 & 0 \\ -2 & 1\end{vmatrix} = (1)(1) - (0)(-2) = 1 - 0 = 1$.
8. The cofactor matrix is $C = \begin{bmatrix}7 & 8 & 1 \\ 4 & 1 & 2 \\ 2 & -2 & 1\end{bmatrix}$; the adjugate is its transpose: $\operatorname{adj}(A) = \begin{bmatrix}7 & 4 & 2 \\ 8 & 1 & -2 \\ 1 & 2 & 1\end{bmatrix}$.
9. Thus $A^{-1} = \frac{1}{5}\begin{bmatrix}7 & 4 & 2 \\ 8 & 1 & -2 \\ 1 & 2 & 1\end{bmatrix}$.
10. Compute $\mathbf{x} = A^{-1}\mathbf{b} = \frac{1}{5}\begin{bmatrix}7 & 4 & 2 \\ 8 & 1 & -2 \\ 1 & 2 & 1\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}-1 \\ 7 \\ -3\end{bmatrix}$.
11. Compute each component: $x_1 = \frac{1}{5}[7(-1) + 4(7) + 2(-3)] = \frac{1}{5}[-7 + 28 - 6] = \frac{1}{5}[15] = 3$, $x_2 = \frac{1}{5}[8(-1) + 1(7) + (-2)(-3)] = \frac{1}{5}[-8 + 7 + 6] = \frac{1}{5}[5] = 1$, $x_3 = \frac{1}{5}[1(-1) + 2(7) + 1(-3)] = \frac{1}{5}[-1 + 14 - 3] = \frac{1}{5}[10] = 2$.
12. Therefore, the unique solution is $\boxed{\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix}3 \\ 1 \\ 2\end{bmatrix}}$.
