1. To find a nonzero vector $\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \\ z \end{bmatrix}$ orthogonal to both $\mathbf{u}_1$ and $\mathbf{u}_2$, set up the equations $\mathbf{u}_1 \cdot \mathbf{v} = 0$ and $\mathbf{u}_2 \cdot \mathbf{v} = 0$: $1x + 1y + (-2)z = 0$ and $5x + (-1)y + 2z = 0$.
2. Write the augmented matrix for this system: $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & -2 & 0 \\ 5 & -1 & 2 & 0 \end{bmatrix}$.
3. Perform $R_2 \to R_2 - 5R_1$: $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & -2 & 0 \\ 0 & -6 & 12 & 0 \end{bmatrix}$.
4. Divide $R_2$ by $-6$ to get a leading 1: $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & -2 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -2 & 0 \end{bmatrix}$.
5. Perform $R_1 \to R_1 - R_2$: $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -2 & 0 \end{bmatrix}$.
6. The solution is $x = 0$, $y = 2z$, so any vector of the form $\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 2z \\ z \end{bmatrix}$ with $z \neq 0$ is orthogonal to both $\mathbf{u}_1$ and $\mathbf{u}_2$.
7. Choosing $z = 1$ gives a nonzero vector: $\boxed{\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 2 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}}$.
