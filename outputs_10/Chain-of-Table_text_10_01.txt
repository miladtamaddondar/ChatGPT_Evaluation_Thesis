2. The pivot columns in the echelon form are columns 1, 3, and 4, so a basis for $\text{Col }A$ is $\left\{ \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 3 \\ 2 \\ -4\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}2 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 2\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}-4 \\ 5 \\ -3 \\ 7\end{bmatrix} \right\}$.

To find a basis for $\text{Nul }A$, solve $A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0}$ using the echelon form: from the last nonzero row $5x_4 = 0 \implies x_4 = 0$, from the second nonzero row $5x_3 - 7x_4 = 0 \implies x_3 = 0$, from the first row $x_1 - 3x_2 + 2x_3 - 4x_4 = 0 \implies x_1 = 3x_2$, so the general solution is $\mathbf{x} = x_2 \begin{bmatrix}3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0\end{bmatrix}$, and thus a basis for $\text{Nul }A$ is $\left\{ \begin{bmatrix}3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0\end{bmatrix} \right\}$.
