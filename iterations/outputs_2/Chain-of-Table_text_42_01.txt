1. Set up the equation $\mathbf{y} = a_1\mathbf{u}_1 + a_2\mathbf{u}_2 + a_3\mathbf{u}_3 + \mathbf{z}$, where $\mathbf{z}$ is orthogonal to $W$, so $\mathbf{y} = \mathbf{w} + \mathbf{z}$ with $\mathbf{w} \in W$ and $\mathbf{z} \perp W$.
2. Express $\mathbf{w} = a_1\mathbf{u}_1 + a_2\mathbf{u}_2 + a_3\mathbf{u}_3$ and set up the system: $a_1\mathbf{u}_1 + a_2\mathbf{u}_2 + a_3\mathbf{u}_3 = \mathbf{y}$, i.e., $a_1\begin{bmatrix}1\\1\\0\\1\end{bmatrix} + a_2\begin{bmatrix}-1\\3\\1\\-2\end{bmatrix} + a_3\begin{bmatrix}-1\\0\\1\\1\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}4\\3\\3\\-1\end{bmatrix}$.
3. Write the augmented matrix: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 & 3 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 & -1 \end{array}\right]$.
4. Subtract row 1 from row 2: $R_2 \leftarrow R_2 - R_1$ gives $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 & -1 \end{array}\right]$.
5. Subtract row 1 from row 4: $R_4 \leftarrow R_4 - R_1$ gives $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 0 & -1 & 2 & -5 \end{array}\right]$.
6. Subtract $1/4$ of row 2 from row 3: $R_3 \leftarrow R_3 - \frac{1}{4}R_2$ gives $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 0 & \frac{3}{4} & \frac{13}{4} \\ 0 & -1 & 2 & -5 \end{array}\right]$.
7. Add row 2 to 4 times row 4: $R_4 \leftarrow 4R_4 + R_2$ gives $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 0 & \frac{3}{4} & \frac{13}{4} \\ 0 & 0 & 9 & -21 \end{array}\right]$.
8. Multiply row 3 by 4/3: $R_3 \leftarrow \frac{4}{3}R_3$ gives $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & \frac{13}{3} \\ 0 & 0 & 9 & -21 \end{array}\right]$.
9. Subtract 9 times row 3 from row 4: $R_4 \leftarrow R_4 - 9R_3$ gives $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & \frac{13}{3} \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & -54 \end{array}\right]$.
10. Back-substitute: from row 4, $0 = -54$ so the system is inconsistent, meaning $\mathbf{y}$ is not in $W$ and we must use orthogonal projection.
11. Compute the Gram matrix $G = UU^T$ and $U = [\mathbf{u}_1\ \mathbf{u}_2\ \mathbf{u}_3]$, then solve $U^T U \mathbf{a} = U^T \mathbf{y}$ for $\mathbf{a} = (a_1, a_2, a_3)^T$.
12. Compute $U^T U = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & -4 & 0 \\ -4 & 15 & 6 \\ 0 & 6 & 3 \end{bmatrix}$ and $U^T\mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 2 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix}$.
13. Write the new augmented matrix: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 3 & -4 & 0 & 6 \\ -4 & 15 & 6 & 2 \\ 0 & 6 & 3 & 6 \end{array}\right]$.
14. Multiply row 1 by $1/3$: $R_1 \leftarrow \frac{1}{3}R_1$ gives $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -\frac{4}{3} & 0 & 2 \\ -4 & 15 & 6 & 2 \\ 0 & 6 & 3 & 6 \end{array}\right]$.
15. Add 4 times row 1 to row 2: $R_2 \leftarrow R_2 + 4R_1$ gives $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -\frac{4}{3} & 0 & 2 \\ 0 & \frac{23}{3} & 6 & 10 \\ 0 & 6 & 3 & 6 \end{array}\right]$.
16. Multiply row 2 by $3/23$: $R_2 \leftarrow \frac{3}{23}R_2$ gives $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -\frac{4}{3} & 0 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & \frac{18}{23} & \frac{30}{23} \\ 0 & 6 & 3 & 6 \end{array}\right]$.
17. Subtract 6 times row 2 from row 3: $R_3 \leftarrow R_3 - 6R_2$ gives $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -\frac{4}{3} & 0 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & \frac{18}{23} & \frac{30}{23} \\ 0 & 0 & -\frac{21}{23} & -\frac{48}{23} \end{array}\right]$.
18. Multiply row 3 by $-23/21$: $R_3 \leftarrow -\frac{23}{21}R_3$ gives $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -\frac{4}{3} & 0 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & \frac{18}{23} & \frac{30}{23} \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & \frac{16}{7} \end{array}\right]$.
19. Subtract $\frac{18}{23}$ times row 3 from row 2: $R_2 \leftarrow R_2 - \frac{18}{23}R_3$ gives $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -\frac{4}{3} & 0 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & -\frac{6}{7} \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & \frac{16}{7} \end{array}\right]$.
20. Add $\frac{4}{3}$ times row 2 to row 1: $R_1 \leftarrow R_1 + \frac{4}{3}R_2$ gives $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & 0 & \frac{10}{7} \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & -\frac{6}{7} \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & \frac{16}{7} \end{array}\right]$.
21. The solution is $a_1 = \frac{10}{7},\ a_2 = -\frac{6}{7},\ a_3 = \frac{16}{7}$, so $\mathbf{w} = \frac{10}{7}\mathbf{u}_1 - \frac{6}{7}\mathbf{u}_2 + \frac{16}{7}\mathbf{u}_3$ and $\mathbf{z} = \mathbf{y} - \mathbf{w}$.
22. Therefore, $\boxed{\mathbf{y} = \mathbf{w} + \mathbf{z} = \left(\frac{10}{7}\mathbf{u}_1 - \frac{6}{7}\mathbf{u}_2 + \frac{16}{7}\mathbf{u}_3\right) + \left(\mathbf{y} - \mathbf{w}\right)}$.
