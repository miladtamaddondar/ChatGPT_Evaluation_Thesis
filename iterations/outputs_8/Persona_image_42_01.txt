1. To write \(\mathbf{y}\) as the sum of a vector in \(W\) and a vector orthogonal to \(W\), we first express \(\mathbf{y}\) as \(\mathbf{y} = \mathbf{y}_W + \mathbf{y}_{W^\perp}\), where \(\mathbf{y}_W\) is the projection onto \(W\).  
2. Let \(\mathbf{y}_W = c_1 \mathbf{u}_1 + c_2 \mathbf{u}_2 + c_3 \mathbf{u}_3\), so we solve \(\mathbf{y} = c_1 \mathbf{u}_1 + c_2 \mathbf{u}_2 + c_3 \mathbf{u}_3 + \mathbf{y}_{W^\perp}\).  
3. Set up the equation: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} c_1 \\ c_2 \\ c_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix}\).  
4. Write the system: \(c_1 - c_2 - c_3 = 4\), \(c_1 + 3c_2 = 3\), \(c_2 + c_3 = 3\), \(c_1 - 2c_2 + c_3 = -1\).  
5. From equation 3, \(c_3 = 3 - c_2\).  
6. Substitute \(c_3\) into equation 1: \(c_1 - c_2 - (3 - c_2) = 4 \implies c_1 - 3 + 0 = 4 \implies c_1 = 7\).  
7. Substitute \(c_1 = 7\) into equation 2: \(7 + 3c_2 = 3 \implies 3c_2 = -4 \implies c_2 = -\frac{4}{3}\).  
8. Substitute \(c_2 = -\frac{4}{3}\) into step 5: \(c_3 = 3 - (-\frac{4}{3}) = 3 + \frac{4}{3} = \frac{13}{3}\).  
9. Check equation 4: \(7 - 2(-\frac{4}{3}) + \frac{13}{3} = 7 + \frac{8}{3} + \frac{13}{3} = 7 + 7 = 14\), but equation 4 should be \(-1\), so there is an error; let's solve the system using matrices.  
10. Write the augmented matrix and row reduce: \(\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 & 3 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 & -1 \end{array}\right]\).  
11. Subtract row 1 from row 2 and row 4: row 2: \( (1-1, 3-(-1), 0-(-1), 3-4) = (0, 4, 1, -1) \); row 4: \( (1-1, -2-(-1), 1-(-1), -1-4) = (0, -1, 2, -5) \).  
12. The matrix is now: \(\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 0 & -1 & 2 & -5 \end{array}\right]\).  
13. Row 3 stays the same; now, replace row 4 with row 4 + row 3: \( (0, -1+1, 2+1, -5+3) = (0, 0, 3, -2) \).  
14. The matrix is now: \(\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 0 & 0 & 3 & -2 \end{array}\right]\).  
15. From row 4: \(3c_3 = -2 \implies c_3 = -\frac{2}{3}\).  
16. From row 3: \(c_2 + c_3 = 3 \implies c_2 = 3 - c_3 = 3 + \frac{2}{3} = \frac{11}{3}\).  
17. From row 2: \(4c_2 + c_3 = -1 \implies 4(\frac{11}{3}) + (-\frac{2}{3}) = -1 \implies \frac{44}{3} - \frac{2}{3} = \frac{42}{3} = 14\), which does not match \(-1\), so let's check the arithmetic.  
18. Let's solve using least squares: \(A = [\mathbf{u}_1 \ \mathbf{u}_2 \ \mathbf{u}_3]\), so \(A^T A \vec{c} = A^T \mathbf{y}\).  
19. Compute \(A^T A\) and \(A^T \mathbf{y}\):  
20. \(A^T A = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 2 \\ 0 & 15 & -2 \\ 2 & -2 & 3 \end{bmatrix}\), \(A^T \mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 9 \\ 15 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}\).  
21. Solve \(A^T A \vec{c} = A^T \mathbf{y}\): \(\begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 2 \\ 0 & 15 & -2 \\ 2 & -2 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} c_1 \\ c_2 \\ c_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 9 \\ 15 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}\).  
22. Use a calculator or solve by hand (let's use substitution/elimination):  
23. From equation 2: \(15c_2 - 2c_3 = 15 \implies c_2 = 1 + \frac{2}{15}c_3\).  
24. Substitute \(c_2\) into equation 3: \(2c_1 - 2c_2 + 3c_3 = 2\).  
25. \(2c_1 - 2(1 + \frac{2}{15}c_3) + 3c_3 = 2 \implies 2c_1 - 2 - \frac{4}{15}c_3 + 3c_3 = 2\).  
26. \(2c_1 + \left(3 - \frac{4}{15}\right)c_3 = 4\), \(3 - \frac{4}{15} = \frac{41}{15}\), so \(2c_1 + \frac{41}{15}c_3 = 4\).  
27. From equation 1: \(3c_1 + 2c_3 = 9\).  
28. Solve for \(c_1\) from equation 1: \(c_1 = \frac{9 - 2c_3}{3}\).  
29. Substitute \(c_1\) into previous result: \(2(\frac{9 - 2c_3}{3}) + \frac{41}{15}c_3 = 4\).  
30. \(\frac{18 - 4c_3}{3} + \frac{41}{15}c_3 = 4\).  
31. Multiply both sides by 15: \(5(18 - 4c_3) + 41c_3 = 60\).  
32. \(90 - 20c_3 + 41c_3 = 60\).  
33. \(90 + 21c_3 = 60\).  
34. \(21c_3 = 60 - 90 = -30 \implies c_3 = -\frac{30}{21} = -\frac{10}{7}\).  
35. Substitute \(c_3\) into step 28: \(c_1 = \frac{9 - 2(-\frac{10}{7})}{3} = \frac{9 + \frac{20}{7}}{3} = \frac{\frac{63}{7} + \frac{20}{7}}{3} = \frac{\frac{83}{7}}{3} = \frac{83}{21}\).  
36. Substitute \(c_3\) into step 23: \(c_2 = 1 + \frac{2}{15}(-\frac{10}{7}) = 1 - \frac{20}{105} = 1 - \frac{4}{21} = \frac{17}{21}\).  
37. The vector in \(W\) is \(\mathbf{y}_W = \frac{83}{21}\mathbf{u}_1 + \frac{17}{21}\mathbf{u}_2 - \frac{10}{7}\mathbf{u}_3\).  
38. The orthogonal vector is \(\mathbf{y}_{W^\perp} = \mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W\).  
39. Final answer: \(\mathbf{y} = \mathbf{y}_W + \mathbf{y}_{W^\perp} = \left(\frac{83}{21}\mathbf{u}_1 + \frac{17}{21}\mathbf{u}_2 - \frac{10}{7}\mathbf{u}_3\right) + \left(\mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W\right)\).
