1. To write $\mathbf{y}$ as the sum of a vector in $W$ and one orthogonal to $W$, we first find the orthogonal projection of $\mathbf{y}$ onto $W$ using the formula $\mathbf{y}_W = U(U^TU)^{-1}U^T\mathbf{y}$, where $U = [\mathbf{u}_1 \ \mathbf{u}_2 \ \mathbf{u}_3]$.
2. Construct the matrix $U = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$.
3. Compute $U^T U = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 & 1 & -2 \\ -1 & 0 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 15 & 5 \\ 1 & 5 & 3 \end{bmatrix}$.
4. Compute $U^T \mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 & 1 & -2 \\ -1 & 0 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 + 3 + 0 - 1 \\ -4 + 9 + 3 + 2 \\ -4 + 0 + 3 - 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 10 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix}$.
5. Solve $(U^T U)\mathbf{c} = U^T \mathbf{y}$ for $\mathbf{c}$, so $\begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 15 & 5 \\ 1 & 5 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} c_1 \\ c_2 \\ c_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 10 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix}$.
6. Write the system: $3c_1 + c_3 = 6$, $15c_2 + 5c_3 = 10$, $c_1 + 5c_2 + 3c_3 = -2$.
7. From the second equation, $15c_2 + 5c_3 = 10 \implies 3c_2 + c_3 = 2 \implies c_3 = 2 - 3c_2$.
8. Substitute $c_3$ into the first equation: $3c_1 + (2 - 3c_2) = 6 \implies 3c_1 - 3c_2 = 4 \implies c_1 - c_2 = \frac{4}{3} \implies c_1 = c_2 + \frac{4}{3}$.
9. Substitute $c_1$ and $c_3$ into the third equation: $(c_2 + \frac{4}{3}) + 5c_2 + 3(2 - 3c_2) = -2$.
10. Expand: $c_2 + \frac{4}{3} + 5c_2 + 6 - 9c_2 = -2 \implies (c_2 + 5c_2 - 9c_2) + \frac{4}{3} + 6 = -2$.
11. Simplify: $(-3c_2) + \frac{22}{3} = -2 \implies -3c_2 = -2 - \frac{22}{3} = -\frac{28}{3} \implies c_2 = \frac{28}{9}$.
12. Find $c_1 = c_2 + \frac{4}{3} = \frac{28}{9} + \frac{12}{9} = \frac{40}{9}$.
13. Find $c_3 = 2 - 3c_2 = 2 - 3 \cdot \frac{28}{9} = 2 - \frac{84}{9} = \frac{18 - 84}{9} = -\frac{66}{9} = -\frac{22}{3}$.
14. The projection is $\mathbf{y}_W = c_1 \mathbf{u}_1 + c_2 \mathbf{u}_2 + c_3 \mathbf{u}_3 = \frac{40}{9} \mathbf{u}_1 + \frac{28}{9} \mathbf{u}_2 - \frac{22}{3} \mathbf{u}_3$.
15. Compute $\mathbf{y}_W = \frac{40}{9} \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} + \frac{28}{9} \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix} - \frac{22}{3} \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}$.
16. Compute each term: $\frac{40}{9} \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{40}{9} \\ \frac{40}{9} \\ 0 \\ \frac{40}{9} \end{bmatrix}$, $\frac{28}{9} \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -\frac{28}{9} \\ \frac{84}{9} \\ \frac{28}{9} \\ -\frac{56}{9} \end{bmatrix}$, $-\frac{22}{3} \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{22}{3} \\ 0 \\ -\frac{22}{3} \\ -\frac{22}{3} \end{bmatrix}$.
17. Add the three vectors: first entry: $\frac{40}{9} - \frac{28}{9} + \frac{22}{3} = \frac{12}{9} + \frac{66}{9} = \frac{78}{9} = \frac{26}{3}$.
18. Second entry: $\frac{40}{9} + \frac{84}{9} + 0 = \frac{124}{9}$.
19. Third entry: $0 + \frac{28}{9} - \frac{22}{3} = \frac{28}{9} - \frac{66}{9} = -\frac{38}{9}$.
20. Fourth entry: $\frac{40}{9} - \frac{56}{9} - \frac{22}{3} = -\frac{16}{9} - \frac{66}{9} = -\frac{82}{9}$.
21. Thus, $\mathbf{y}_W = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{26}{3} \\ \frac{124}{9} \\ -\frac{38}{9} \\ -\frac{82}{9} \end{bmatrix}$.
22. The orthogonal component is $\mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} \frac{26}{3} \\ \frac{124}{9} \\ -\frac{38}{9} \\ -\frac{82}{9} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 - \frac{26}{3} \\ 3 - \frac{124}{9} \\ 3 + \frac{38}{9} \\ -1 + \frac{82}{9} \end{bmatrix}$.
23. Simplify: $4 - \frac{26}{3} = \frac{12 - 26}{3} = -\frac{14}{3}$, $3 - \frac{124}{9} = \frac{27 - 124}{9} = -\frac{97}{9}$, $3 + \frac{38}{9} = \frac{27 + 38}{9} = \frac{65}{9}$, $-1 + \frac{82}{9} = \frac{-9 + 82}{9} = \frac{73}{9}$.
24. So the orthogonal component is $\begin{bmatrix} -\frac{14}{3} \\ -\frac{97}{9} \\ \frac{65}{9} \\ \frac{73}{9} \end{bmatrix}$.
25. The final answer: $\mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{26}{3} \\ \frac{124}{9} \\ -\frac{38}{9} \\ -\frac{82}{9} \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} -\frac{14}{3} \\ -\frac{97}{9} \\ \frac{65}{9} \\ \frac{73}{9} \end{bmatrix}$, where the first vector is in $W$ and the second is orthogonal to $W$.
