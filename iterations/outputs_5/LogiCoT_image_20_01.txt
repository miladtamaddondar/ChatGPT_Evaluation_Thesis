1. To find a basis for \(\mathbb{R}^2\) consisting of \(\mathbf{v}_1\) and another eigenvector \(\mathbf{v}_2\) of \(A\), first find all eigenvalues of \(A\) by solving \(\det(A - \lambda I) = 0\): \(\det\left(\begin{bmatrix}0.6-\lambda & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.7-\lambda\end{bmatrix}\right) = (0.6-\lambda)(0.7-\lambda) - (0.3)(0.4) = 0\).
2. Expanding the determinant: \((0.6-\lambda)(0.7-\lambda) - 0.12 = 0.42 - 0.6\lambda - 0.7\lambda + \lambda^2 - 0.12 = \lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3 = 0\).
3. Solve the quadratic equation: \(\lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3 = 0\) gives \(\lambda = \frac{1.3 \pm \sqrt{1.69 - 1.2}}{2} = \frac{1.3 \pm 0.7}{2}\), so \(\lambda_1 = 1\) and \(\lambda_2 = 0.3\).
4. We are given \(\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix}3/7 \\ 4/7\end{bmatrix}\) as an eigenvector for \(\lambda_1 = 1\); now find an eigenvector \(\mathbf{v}_2\) for \(\lambda_2 = 0.3\) by solving \((A - 0.3I)\mathbf{v}_2 = 0\).
5. Compute \(A - 0.3I = \begin{bmatrix}0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.4\end{bmatrix}\); set up the equation: \(\begin{bmatrix}0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.4\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}x \\ y\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0 \\ 0\end{bmatrix}\).
6. This gives \(0.3x + 0.3y = 0\) and \(0.4x + 0.4y = 0\); both reduce to \(x + y = 0\), so take \(x = 1, y = -1\).
7. Thus, \(\mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}\) is an eigenvector for \(\lambda_2 = 0.3\); verify \(A\mathbf{v}_2 = 0.3\mathbf{v}_2\): \(A\begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0.6 - 0.3 \\ 0.4 - 0.7\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0.3 \\ -0.3\end{bmatrix} = 0.3\begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}\).
8. The set \(\left\{\begin{bmatrix}3/7 \\ 4/7\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}\right\}\) forms a basis for \(\mathbb{R}^2\) consisting of \(\mathbf{v}_1\) and another eigenvector \(\mathbf{v}_2\) of \(A\).
