1. The characteristic polynomial of a matrix \(A\) is given by \(\det(A - \lambda I)\); for \(A = \begin{bmatrix}2 & 1 \\ -1 & 4\end{bmatrix}\), compute \(A - \lambda I = \begin{bmatrix}2-\lambda & 1 \\ -1 & 4-\lambda\end{bmatrix}\).
2. The determinant is \((2-\lambda)(4-\lambda) - (1)(-1) = (2-\lambda)(4-\lambda) + 1\).
3. Expanding \((2-\lambda)(4-\lambda)\) gives \(8 - 2\lambda - 4\lambda + \lambda^2 = \lambda^2 - 6\lambda + 8\), so the characteristic polynomial is \(\lambda^2 - 6\lambda + 8 + 1 = \lambda^2 - 6\lambda + 9\).
4. The characteristic polynomial is \(\boxed{\lambda^2 - 6\lambda + 9}\); verify by re-expanding: \((2-\lambda)(4-\lambda) + 1 = 8 - 2\lambda - 4\lambda + \lambda^2 + 1 = \lambda^2 - 6\lambda + 9\), which matches.
5. To find the eigenvalues, solve \(\lambda^2 - 6\lambda + 9 = 0\); this factors as \((\lambda - 3)^2 = 0\).
6. Thus, the eigenvalue is \(\boxed{\lambda = 3}\) with algebraic multiplicity 2, since the quadratic has a repeated root.
