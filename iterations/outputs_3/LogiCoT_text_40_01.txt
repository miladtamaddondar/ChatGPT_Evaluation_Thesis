2. Form the change-of-coordinates matrix $P$ from $\mathcal{B}$ to the standard basis by placing these as columns: $P = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 \\ -3 & -5 & 0 \end{bmatrix}$.
3. Verify $P$ is invertible by computing its determinant: $\det(P) = 1 \cdot (1 \cdot 0 - 2 \cdot (-5)) - 2 \cdot (0 \cdot 0 - 2 \cdot (-3)) + 1 \cdot (0 \cdot -5 - 1 \cdot (-3)) = 1 \cdot (0 + 10) - 2 \cdot (0 + 6) + 1 \cdot (0 + 3) = 10 - 12 + 3 = 1$.
4. Since $\det(P) = 1 \neq 0$, $P$ is invertible, confirming the columns are linearly independent and $\mathcal{B}$ is a basis.

6. Equate coefficients with $t^2 = 0 \cdot 1 + 0 \cdot t + 1 \cdot t^2$, yielding the system: $a + 2b + c = 0$, $b + 2c = 0$, $-3a - 5b = 1$.
7. Solve $b + 2c = 0 \implies b = -2c$; substitute into $a + 2b + c = 0$ to get $a + 2(-2c) + c = 0 \implies a - 4c + c = 0 \implies a = 3c$.
8. Substitute $a = 3c$, $b = -2c$ into $-3a - 5b = 1$: $-3(3c) - 5(-2c) = 1 \implies -9c + 10c = 1 \implies c = 1$.
9. Back-substitute $c = 1$ to get $b = -2$, $a = 3$.
