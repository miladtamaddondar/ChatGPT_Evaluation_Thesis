1. Write the given matrix as \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 8 & 6 \\ 5 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \). 
2. Calculate the determinant: \( \det(A) = (8)(4) - (6)(5) = 32 - 30 = 2 \). 
3. Verify that the determinant is nonzero, so the matrix is invertible. 
4. Write the formula for the inverse of a \(2 \times 2\) matrix: \( A^{-1} = \frac{1}{\det(A)} \begin{bmatrix} d & -b \\ -c & a \end{bmatrix} \), where \( A = \begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{bmatrix} \). 
5. Substitute the values: \( A^{-1} = \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} 4 & -6 \\ -5 & 8 \end{bmatrix} \). 
6. Multiply each entry by \( \frac{1}{2} \): \( A^{-1} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -3 \\ -2.5 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \). 
7. Verify by multiplying \( A \) and \( A^{-1} \): \( \begin{bmatrix} 8 & 6 \\ 5 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -3 \\ -2.5 & 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} (8)(2)+(6)(-2.5) & (8)(-3)+(6)(4) \\ (5)(2)+(4)(-2.5) & (5)(-3)+(4)(4) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 16-15 & -24+24 \\ 10-10 & -15+16 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \). 
8. Therefore, the inverse is \( \boxed{\begin{bmatrix} 2 & -3 \\ -2.5 & 4 \end{bmatrix}} \).
