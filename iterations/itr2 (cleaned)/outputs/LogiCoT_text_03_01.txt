1. Write the matrix \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 9 \\ 4 & 5 \end{bmatrix} \) and recall that LU factorization expresses \( A = LU \), where \( L \) is lower triangular and \( U \) is upper triangular. 
2. Set \( L = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ l_{21} & 1 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( U = \begin{bmatrix} u_{11} & u_{12} \\ 0 & u_{22} \end{bmatrix} \), and write the product \( LU = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ l_{21} & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} u_{11} & u_{12} \\ 0 & u_{22} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} u_{11} & u_{12} \\ l_{21} u_{11} & l_{21} u_{12} + u_{22} \end{bmatrix} \). 
3. Equate \( LU \) to \( A \) to get the system: \( u_{11} = 6 \), \( u_{12} = 9 \), \( l_{21} u_{11} = 4 \), and \( l_{21} u_{12} + u_{22} = 5 \). 
4. Solve for \( l_{21} \) using \( l_{21} u_{11} = 4 \) to get \( l_{21} = \frac{4}{6} = \frac{2}{3} \). 
5. Substitute \( l_{21} \) and \( u_{12} \) into \( l_{21} u_{12} + u_{22} = 5 \) to get \( \frac{2}{3} \cdot 9 + u_{22} = 5 \), so \( 6 + u_{22} = 5 \), thus \( u_{22} = -1 \). 
6. Therefore, \( L = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ \frac{2}{3} & 1 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( U = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 9 \\ 0 & -1 \end{bmatrix} \). 
7. Verify by multiplying \( L \) and \( U \): \( \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ \frac{2}{3} & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 9 \\ 0 & -1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 9 \\ \frac{2}{3} \cdot 6 + 0 & \frac{2}{3} \cdot 9 + (-1) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 9 \\ 4 & 5 \end{bmatrix} \), confirming correctness. 
8. The LU factorization of \( \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 9 \\ 4 & 5 \end{bmatrix} \) is \( L = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ \frac{2}{3} & 1 \end{bmatrix} \), \( U = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 9 \\ 0 & -1 \end{bmatrix} \).
