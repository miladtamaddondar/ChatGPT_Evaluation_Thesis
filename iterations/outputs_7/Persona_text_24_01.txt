1. To find all $\mathbf{x}$ mapped to zero, solve $A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0}$, i.e., $\begin{bmatrix}1 & -4 & 7 & -5 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & 3 \\ 2 & -6 & 6 & -4\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}x_1\\x_2\\x_3\\x_4\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0\\0\\0\end{bmatrix}$.
2. Write the system: $x_1 - 4x_2 + 7x_3 - 5x_4 = 0$, $x_2 - 4x_3 + 3x_4 = 0$, $2x_1 - 6x_2 + 6x_3 - 4x_4 = 0$.
3. Notice that the third equation is $2$ times the first, so it is redundant.
4. Express $x_2$ from the second equation: $x_2 = 4x_3 - 3x_4$.
5. Substitute $x_2$ into the first equation: $x_1 - 4(4x_3 - 3x_4) + 7x_3 - 5x_4 = 0$.
6. Simplify: $x_1 - 16x_3 + 12x_4 + 7x_3 - 5x_4 = 0$.
7. Combine like terms: $x_1 - 9x_3 + 7x_4 = 0$.
8. Solve for $x_1$: $x_1 = 9x_3 - 7x_4$.
9. Let $x_3 = s$, $x_4 = t$ where $s, t \in \mathbb{R}$, so $x_1 = 9s - 7t$, $x_2 = 4s - 3t$.
10. The solution set is $\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix}9s - 7t \\ 4s - 3t \\ s \\ t\end{bmatrix}$ for all $s, t \in \mathbb{R}$.
