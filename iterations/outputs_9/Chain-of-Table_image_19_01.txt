1. Compute \( T(\mathbf{b}_1) = A\mathbf{b}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \).
2. Set up the augmented matrix to write \( T(\mathbf{b}_1) \) as a linear combination of \( \mathbf{b}_1 \) and \( \mathbf{b}_2 \): \( x\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} + y\begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \) gives the augmented matrix \( \left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & 5 & 2 \\ 1 & 4 & 2 \end{array}\right] \).
3. Subtract row 1 from row 2: \( R_2 \gets R_2 - R_1 \), resulting in \( \left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & 5 & 2 \\ 0 & -1 & 0 \end{array}\right] \).
4. Multiply row 2 by \(-1\): \( R_2 \gets -R_2 \), resulting in \( \left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & 5 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \end{array}\right] \).
5. Subtract 5 times row 2 from row 1: \( R_1 \gets R_1 - 5R_2 \), resulting in \( \left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & 0 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \end{array}\right] \), so \( T(\mathbf{b}_1) = 2\mathbf{b}_1 + 0\mathbf{b}_2 \).
6. Compute \( T(\mathbf{b}_2) = A\mathbf{b}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 9 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \).
7. Set up the augmented matrix to write \( T(\mathbf{b}_2) \) as a linear combination of \( \mathbf{b}_1 \) and \( \mathbf{b}_2 \): \( x\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} + y\begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 9 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \) gives the augmented matrix \( \left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & 5 & 9 \\ 1 & 4 & 7 \end{array}\right] \).
8. Subtract row 1 from row 2: \( R_2 \gets R_2 - R_1 \), resulting in \( \left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & 5 & 9 \\ 0 & -1 & -2 \end{array}\right] \).
9. Multiply row 2 by \(-1\): \( R_2 \gets -R_2 \), resulting in \( \left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & 5 & 9 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 \end{array}\right] \).
10. Subtract 5 times row 2 from row 1: \( R_1 \gets R_1 - 5R_2 \), resulting in \( \left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & 0 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 \end{array}\right] \), so \( T(\mathbf{b}_2) = -1\mathbf{b}_1 + 2\mathbf{b}_2 \).
11. The \(\mathcal{B}\)-matrix for \(T\) is \( [T]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 0 & 2 \end{bmatrix} \).
