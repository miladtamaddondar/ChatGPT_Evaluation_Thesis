1. The characteristic polynomial of a matrix \( A \) is given by \( \det(A - \lambda I) \).
2. Subtract \( \lambda \) from the diagonal entries: \( A - \lambda I = \begin{bmatrix}2-\lambda & 1 \\ -1 & 4-\lambda\end{bmatrix} \).
3. Compute the determinant: \( \det(A - \lambda I) = (2-\lambda)(4-\lambda) - (1)(-1) \).
4. Expand the determinant: \( (2-\lambda)(4-\lambda) + 1 = (8 - 2\lambda - 4\lambda + \lambda^2) + 1 \).
5. Simplify the expression: \( \lambda^2 - 6\lambda + 9 \).
6. The characteristic polynomial is \( \lambda^2 - 6\lambda + 9 \).
7. Set the characteristic polynomial to zero to find eigenvalues: \( \lambda^2 - 6\lambda + 9 = 0 \).
8. Factor the quadratic: \( (\lambda - 3)^2 = 0 \).
9. The eigenvalue is \( \lambda = 3 \) (with algebraic multiplicity 2).
