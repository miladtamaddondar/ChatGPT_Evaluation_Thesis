1. To find the standard matrix of a rotation by $\frac{3\pi}{2}$ radians counterclockwise, recall the general rotation matrix: $R(\theta) = \begin{bmatrix} \cos\theta & -\sin\theta \\ \sin\theta & \cos\theta \end{bmatrix}$.
2. Substitute $\theta = \frac{3\pi}{2}$: $\cos\left(\frac{3\pi}{2}\right) = 0$ and $\sin\left(\frac{3\pi}{2}\right) = -1$.
3. Plug these values into the rotation matrix: $R\left(\frac{3\pi}{2}\right) = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & -(-1) \\ -1 & 0 \end{bmatrix}$.
4. Simplify the matrix: $R\left(\frac{3\pi}{2}\right) = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 0 \end{bmatrix}$.
5. The standard matrix of $T$ is $\boxed{\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 0 \end{bmatrix}}$.
