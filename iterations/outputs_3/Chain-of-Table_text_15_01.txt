1. Find the eigenvalues of $A$ by solving $\det(A - \lambda I) = 0$: $\det\begin{pmatrix} 1-\lambda & -2 \\ 1 & 3-\lambda \end{pmatrix} = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0$.
2. Solve for $\lambda$: $\lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0 \implies \lambda = 2 \pm i$.
3. Let $C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}$, which matches the required form with $a=2$, $b=1$.
4. Find an eigenvector for $\lambda = 2+i$: $(A - (2+i)I)\vec{v} = 0 \implies \begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix} = 0$.
5. Express $y$ in terms of $x$ using the first row: $(-1-i)x - 2y = 0 \implies y = \frac{-1-i}{2}x$.
6. Let $x=1$, then $y = \frac{-1-i}{2}$, so the eigenvector is $\vec{v} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ \frac{-1-i}{2} \end{pmatrix}$.
7. Separate real and imaginary parts: $\vec{v}_1 = \operatorname{Re}(\vec{v}) = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ -\frac{1}{2} \end{pmatrix}$, $\vec{v}_2 = \operatorname{Im}(\vec{v}) = \begin{pmatrix} 0 \\ -\frac{1}{2} \end{pmatrix}$.
8. Form $P$ from these as columns: $P = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ -\frac{1}{2} & -\frac{1}{2} \end{pmatrix}$.
9. The answer: $A = P C P^{-1}$ with $P = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ -\frac{1}{2} & -\frac{1}{2} \end{pmatrix}$ and $C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}$.
