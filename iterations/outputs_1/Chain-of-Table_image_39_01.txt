1. Write the equation \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 4 \\ 2 & -6 & 8 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \mathbf{v}^T\) and let \(\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} v_1 \\ v_2 \\ v_3 \end{bmatrix}\).  
2. Express the right side: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} v_1 & v_2 & v_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} v_1 & v_2 & v_3 \\ 2v_1 & 2v_2 & 2v_3 \end{bmatrix}\).  
3. Set up the system by equating entries: \(v_1 = 1\), \(v_2 = -3\), \(v_3 = 4\), \(2v_1 = 2\), \(2v_2 = -6\), \(2v_3 = 8\).  
4. Solve for \(v_1, v_2, v_3\): \(v_1 = 1\), \(v_2 = -3\), \(v_3 = 4\).  
5. Final answer: \(\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix}\).
