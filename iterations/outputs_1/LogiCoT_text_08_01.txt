1. To construct a \( 4 \times 3 \) matrix of rank 1, recall that a matrix has rank 1 if all its rows are scalar multiples of a single nonzero row; let's choose a nonzero row vector \(\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 & 3 \end{bmatrix}\).  
2. Choose four scalars for the rows, say \(a_1 = 1\), \(a_2 = 4\), \(a_3 = -2\), \(a_4 = 0.5\), and form the matrix as \(A = \begin{bmatrix} a_1\mathbf{v} \\ a_2\mathbf{v} \\ a_3\mathbf{v} \\ a_4\mathbf{v} \end{bmatrix}\).  
3. Calculate each row: \(a_1\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 & 3 \end{bmatrix}\), \(a_2\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 8 & -4 & 12 \end{bmatrix}\), \(a_3\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} -4 & 2 & -6 \end{bmatrix}\), \(a_4\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -0.5 & 1.5 \end{bmatrix}\).  
4. Write the matrix: \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 & 3 \\ 8 & -4 & 12 \\ -4 & 2 & -6 \\ 1 & -0.5 & 1.5 \end{bmatrix}\).  
5. Verify rank: Since all rows are scalar multiples of \(\mathbf{v}\), the row space is one-dimensional, so \(\text{rank}(A) = 1\).  
6. Check using minors: Any \(2 \times 2\) minor (e.g., using first two rows and first two columns) is \(\begin{vmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 8 & -4 \end{vmatrix} = 2 \times (-4) - 8 \times (-1) = -8 + 8 = 0\), so all \(2 \times 2\) minors are zero, confirming rank is at most 1.  
7. Final answer: A \(4 \times 3\) matrix of rank 1 is \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 & 3 \\ 8 & -4 & 12 \\ -4 & 2 & -6 \\ 1 & -0.5 & 1.5 \end{bmatrix}\).
