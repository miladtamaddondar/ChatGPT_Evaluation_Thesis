1. The given matrix is $A = \begin{bmatrix}1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ 3 & 9 & -1 & 5 \\ 2 & -6 & 4 & -3 \\ -4 & 12 & 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix}$ and its echelon form is $\begin{bmatrix}1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 & -7 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}$.
2. To find a basis for $\text{Col }A$, we note the pivot columns in the echelon form are columns 1, 3, and 4, so the corresponding columns of $A$ form a basis: $\left\{ \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 3 \\ 2 \\ -4\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}2 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 2\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}-4 \\ 5 \\ -3 \\ 7\end{bmatrix} \right\}$.
3. To find a basis for $\text{Nul }A$, we solve $A\vec{x} = 0$ using the reduced echelon form: let $x_2 = s$, $x_4 = t$ be free variables, then back-substitute to get $x_3 = \frac{7}{5}t$, $x_1 = 3s - 2x_3 + 4t = 3s - 2(\frac{7}{5}t) + 4t = 3s + (\frac{20}{5} - \frac{14}{5})t = 3s + \frac{6}{5}t$, so the general solution is $\vec{x} = s\begin{bmatrix}3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0\end{bmatrix} + t\begin{bmatrix}\frac{6}{5} \\ 0 \\ \frac{7}{5} \\ 1\end{bmatrix}$, giving the basis $\left\{ \begin{bmatrix}3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}\frac{6}{5} \\ 0 \\ \frac{7}{5} \\ 1\end{bmatrix} \right\}$.
4. The dimension of $\text{Col }A$ is $3$ and the dimension of $\text{Nul }A$ is $2$.
