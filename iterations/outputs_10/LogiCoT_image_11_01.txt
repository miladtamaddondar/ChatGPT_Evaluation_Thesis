1. To find the eigenvalues, set up the characteristic equation \(\det(A - \lambda I) = 0\), where \(A = \begin{bmatrix}0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 2 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 1\end{bmatrix}\) and \(I\) is the \(3 \times 3\) identity matrix.
2. Compute \(A - \lambda I = \begin{bmatrix}0 - \lambda & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 2 - \lambda & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 - \lambda\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}-\lambda & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 2 - \lambda & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 - \lambda\end{bmatrix}\).
3. The determinant of this upper triangular matrix is the product of its diagonal entries: \(\det(A - \lambda I) = (-\lambda)(2 - \lambda)(1 - \lambda)\).
4. Set the determinant equal to zero: \((-\lambda)(2 - \lambda)(1 - \lambda) = 0\).
5. The solutions are \(\lambda = 0\), \(\lambda = 1\), and \(\lambda = 2\), which are the eigenvalues of the matrix.
6. Final answer: The eigenvalues are \(\boxed{0, 1, 2}\).
