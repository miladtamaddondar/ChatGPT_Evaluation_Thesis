1. To find values of \( k \) such that \( AB = BA \), first compute \( AB \) and \( BA \).
2. Compute \( AB = \begin{bmatrix}2 & 5 \\ -3 & 1\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}4 & -5 \\ 3 & k\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}2 \times 4 + 5 \times 3 & 2 \times (-5) + 5 \times k \\ -3 \times 4 + 1 \times 3 & -3 \times (-5) + 1 \times k\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}8 + 15 & -10 + 5k \\ -12 + 3 & 15 + k\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}23 & -10 + 5k \\ -9 & 15 + k\end{bmatrix} \).
3. Compute \( BA = \begin{bmatrix}4 & -5 \\ 3 & k\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}2 & 5 \\ -3 & 1\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}4 \times 2 + (-5) \times (-3) & 4 \times 5 + (-5) \times 1 \\ 3 \times 2 + k \times (-3) & 3 \times 5 + k \times 1\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}8 + 15 & 20 - 5 \\ 6 - 3k & 15 + k\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}23 & 15 \\ 6 - 3k & 15 + k\end{bmatrix} \).
4. Set corresponding entries equal: \( AB = BA \implies \begin{bmatrix}23 & -10 + 5k \\ -9 & 15 + k\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}23 & 15 \\ 6 - 3k & 15 + k\end{bmatrix} \).
5. Equate the (1,2) entry: \( -10 + 5k = 15 \implies 5k = 25 \implies k = 5 \).
6. Equate the (2,1) entry: \( -9 = 6 - 3k \implies -9 - 6 = -3k \implies -15 = -3k \implies k = 5 \).
7. The (2,2) entry gives \( 15 + k = 15 + k \), which is always true.
8. The (1,1) entry gives \( 23 = 23 \), which is always true.
9. Therefore, the only value of \( k \) that makes \( AB = BA \) is \( \boxed{5} \).
