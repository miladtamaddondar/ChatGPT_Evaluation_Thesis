1. The matrix $A$ is $\begin{bmatrix}1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ 3 & 9 & -1 & 5 \\ 2 & -6 & 4 & -3 \\ -4 & 12 & 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix}$ and its echelon form is $\begin{bmatrix}1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 & -7 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}$.
2. The pivot columns in the echelon form are columns 1, 3, and 4, so a basis for $\text{Col } A$ is $\left\{ \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 3 \\ 2 \\ -4\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}2 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 2\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}-4 \\ 5 \\ -3 \\ 7\end{bmatrix} \right\}$.
3. To find a basis for $\text{Nul } A$, solve $A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0}$ using the echelon form: let $x_2 = t$, $x_4 = s$ (free variables), then back-substitute to get $x_3 = \frac{7}{5}s$, $x_1 = 3t - 2x_3 + 4x_4 = 3t - 2\left(\frac{7}{5}s\right) + 4s = 3t + \frac{6}{5}s$.
4. Therefore, the general solution is $\mathbf{x} = t\begin{bmatrix}3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0\end{bmatrix} + s\begin{bmatrix}\frac{6}{5} \\ 0 \\ \frac{7}{5} \\ 1\end{bmatrix}$, so a basis for $\text{Nul } A$ is $\left\{ \begin{bmatrix}3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}\frac{6}{5} \\ 0 \\ \frac{7}{5} \\ 1\end{bmatrix} \right\}$.
5. The dimension of $\text{Col } A$ is $3$ and the dimension of $\text{Nul } A$ is $2$.
