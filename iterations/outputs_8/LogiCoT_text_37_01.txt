1. The coordinate vector $[q]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}$ means $q = -1 \cdot p_1 + 1 \cdot p_2 + 2 \cdot p_3$ by the definition of coordinates in a basis.
2. Substitute the given polynomials: $q = -1(1 + t^2) + 1(t - 3t^2) + 2(1 + t - 3t^2)$.
3. Distribute the scalars: $q = -1 - t^2 + t - 3t^2 + 2(1 + t - 3t^2)$.
4. Expand the last term: $q = -1 - t^2 + t - 3t^2 + 2 \cdot 1 + 2 \cdot t - 2 \cdot 3t^2$.
5. Simplify the last term: $q = -1 - t^2 + t - 3t^2 + 2 + 2t - 6t^2$.
6. Combine like terms: constants $(-1 + 2 = 1)$, $t$ terms $(t + 2t = 3t)$, $t^2$ terms $(-t^2 - 3t^2 - 6t^2 = -10t^2)$.
7. Therefore, $q = 1 + 3t - 10t^2$.
