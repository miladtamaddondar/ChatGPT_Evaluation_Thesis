1. To write \(\mathbf{y}\) as the sum of a vector in \(W\) (the span of \(\mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_2, \mathbf{u}_3\)) and a vector orthogonal to \(W\), we first express \(\mathbf{y} = \mathbf{y}_W + \mathbf{y}_\perp\), where \(\mathbf{y}_W \in W\) and \(\mathbf{y}_\perp \perp W\). 
2. We seek \(\mathbf{y}_W = c_1\mathbf{u}_1 + c_2\mathbf{u}_2 + c_3\mathbf{u}_3\) such that \(\mathbf{y}_\perp = \mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W\) is orthogonal to each \(\mathbf{u}_i\), i.e., \(\mathbf{u}_i^T(\mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W) = 0\) for \(i=1,2,3\). 
3. This leads to the system: \(\mathbf{U}^T\mathbf{U}\mathbf{c} = \mathbf{U}^T\mathbf{y}\), where \(\mathbf{U} = [\mathbf{u}_1\ \mathbf{u}_2\ \mathbf{u}_3]\) and \(\mathbf{c} = \begin{bmatrix}c_1\\c_2\\c_3\end{bmatrix}\). 
4. Compute \(\mathbf{U}^T\mathbf{U}\): \(\mathbf{U}^T\mathbf{U} = \begin{bmatrix}3 & 0 & 2\\ 0 & 15 & -4\\ 2 & -4 & 4\end{bmatrix}\) by calculating each entry as the dot product of the corresponding \(\mathbf{u}_i\) and \(\mathbf{u}_j\). 
5. Compute \(\mathbf{U}^T\mathbf{y}\): \(\mathbf{U}^T\mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix}6\\ 18\\ -2\end{bmatrix}\) by taking the dot product of each \(\mathbf{u}_i\) with \(\mathbf{y}\). 
6. Solve the linear system \(\begin{bmatrix}3 & 0 & 2\\ 0 & 15 & -4\\ 2 & -4 & 4\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}c_1\\c_2\\c_3\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}6\\ 18\\ -2\end{bmatrix}\) using Gaussian elimination or matrix inversion. 
7. The solution is \(c_1 = 2\), \(c_2 = 1\), \(c_3 = 0\) (verified by substitution into the system and checking each equation). 
8. Thus, \(\mathbf{y}_W = 2\mathbf{u}_1 + 1\mathbf{u}_2 + 0\mathbf{u}_3 = 2\begin{bmatrix}1\\1\\0\\1\end{bmatrix} + 1\begin{bmatrix}-1\\3\\1\\-2\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}2\\2\\0\\2\end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix}-1\\3\\1\\-2\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}1\\5\\1\\0\end{bmatrix}\). 
9. Compute \(\mathbf{y}_\perp = \mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W = \begin{bmatrix}4\\3\\3\\-1\end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix}1\\5\\1\\0\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}3\\-2\\2\\-1\end{bmatrix}\). 
10. The final answer: \(\mathbf{y} = \underbrace{\begin{bmatrix}1\\5\\1\\0\end{bmatrix}}_{\mathbf{y}_W \in W} + \underbrace{\begin{bmatrix}3\\-2\\2\\-1\end{bmatrix}}_{\mathbf{y}_\perp \perp W}\).
