Form the normal equations $A^T A \mathbf{x} = A^T \mathbf{b}$; first, compute $A^T A = \begin{bmatrix} 4 & 2 & 2 \\ 2 & 2 & 0 \\ 2 & 0 & 2 \end{bmatrix}$ and $A^T \mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 14 \\ 4 \\ 10 \end{bmatrix}$, so the augmented matrix is $\left[ \begin{array}{ccc|c} 4 & 2 & 2 & 14 \\ 2 & 2 & 0 & 4 \\ 2 & 0 & 2 & 10 \end{array} \right]$.
2. Row 2 $\to$ Row 2 $- \frac{1}{2}$ Row 1: $\left[ \begin{array}{ccc|c} 4 & 2 & 2 & 14 \\ 0 & 1 & -1 & -3 \\ 2 & 0 & 2 & 10 \end{array} \right]$.
3. Row 3 $\to$ Row 3 $- \frac{1}{2}$ Row 1: $\left[ \begin{array}{ccc|c} 4 & 2 & 2 & 14 \\ 0 & 1 & -1 & -3 \\ 0 & -1 & 1 & 3 \end{array} \right]$.
4. Row 3 $\to$ Row 3 + Row 2: $\left[ \begin{array}{ccc|c} 4 & 2 & 2 & 14 \\ 0 & 1 & -1 & -3 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{array} \right]$.
5. Row 1 $\to$ Row 1 $-2 \times$ Row 2: $\left[ \begin{array}{ccc|c} 4 & 0 & 4 & 20 \\ 0 & 1 & -1 & -3 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{array} \right]$.
6. Row 1 $\to$ \frac{1}{4} Row 1: $\left[ \begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & 1 & 5 \\ 0 & 1 & -1 & -3 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{array} \right]$.
7. The general solution is $x_1 + x_3 = 5$, $x_2 - x_3 = -3$, so $x_1 = 5 - t$, $x_2 = -3 + t$, $x_3 = t$ for any $t \in \mathbb{R}$; thus, all least-squares solutions are $\boxed{\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 5 - t \\ -3 + t \\ t \end{bmatrix},\ t \in \mathbb{R}}$.
