1. Find the eigenvalues of \( A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix} \) by solving \( \det(A - \lambda I) = 0 \): \( \det\left(\begin{pmatrix} 1-\lambda & -2 \\ 1 & 3-\lambda \end{pmatrix}\right) = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0 \).  
2. Solve the quadratic equation \( \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0 \): The roots are \( \lambda = \frac{4 \pm \sqrt{16-20}}{2} = \frac{4 \pm 2i}{2} = 2 \pm i \).  
3. Verify the eigenvalues: Substitute \( \lambda = 2+i \) into the characteristic polynomial: \( (2+i)^2 - 4(2+i) + 5 = (4 + 4i + i^2) - (8 + 4i) + 5 = (4 + 4i - 1) - 8 - 4i + 5 = (3 + 4i) - 8 - 4i + 5 = 0 \), so the calculation is correct.  
4. Find an eigenvector for \( \lambda = 2+i \): Solve \( (A - (2+i)I)\vec{v} = 0 \), so \( \begin{pmatrix} 1-(2+i) & -2 \\ 1 & 3-(2+i) \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix} \).  
5. Set up the system: \( (-1-i)x - 2y = 0 \) and \( x + (1-i)y = 0 \); from the second equation, \( x = -(1-i)y \).  
6. Substitute \( x = -(1-i)y \) into the first equation: \( (-1-i)(-(1-i)y) - 2y = ((1-i)(1+i)y) - 2y = (1^2 - i^2)y - 2y = (1 + 1)y - 2y = 2y - 2y = 0 \), so any nonzero \( y \) works.  
7. Choose \( y = 1 \), then \( x = -(1-i) \), so an eigenvector is \( \vec{v}_1 = \begin{pmatrix} -(1-i) \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \).  
8. The eigenvector for \( \lambda = 2-i \) is the complex conjugate: \( \vec{v}_2 = \begin{pmatrix} -(1+i) \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \).  
9. Form the matrix \( P \) with columns \( \vec{v}_1 \) and \( \vec{v}_2 \): \( P = \begin{pmatrix} -(1-i) & -(1+i) \\ 1 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \).  
10. The matrix \( C \) is diagonal with the eigenvalues: \( C = \begin{pmatrix} 2+i & 0 \\ 0 & 2-i \end{pmatrix} \).  
11. To write \( C \) in the form \( \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix} \), note that \( 2+i = 2 + i \) and \( 2-i = 2 - i \), so \( C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \).  
12. Verify \( C \) has the correct form: \( a = 2, b = 1 \), so \( C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \) matches the required structure.  
13. Therefore, the solution is \( P = \begin{pmatrix} -(1-i) & -(1+i) \\ 1 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \) and \( C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \), so \( A = P C P^{-1} \).
