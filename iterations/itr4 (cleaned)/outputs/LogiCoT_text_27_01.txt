1. Since $T$ is linear and $T(\mathbf{e}_1) = \mathbf{y}_1$, $T(\mathbf{e}_2) = \mathbf{y}_2$, the standard matrix $A$ of $T$ has columns $\mathbf{y}_1$ and $\mathbf{y}_2$, so $A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 5 & 6 \end{bmatrix}$; this follows from the definition of the standard matrix for a linear transformation.  
2. To find $T\left(\begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix}\right)$, write $\begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} = 5\mathbf{e}_1 - 3\mathbf{e}_2$ and use linearity: $T\left(\begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix}\right) = 5T(\mathbf{e}_1) - 3T(\mathbf{e}_2) = 5\mathbf{y}_1 - 3\mathbf{y}_2$; this uses the property $T(a\mathbf{u} + b\mathbf{v}) = aT(\mathbf{u}) + bT(\mathbf{v})$.  
3. Calculate $5\mathbf{y}_1 = 5\begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 10 \\ 25 \end{bmatrix}$ and $-3\mathbf{y}_2 = -3\begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ -18 \end{bmatrix}$; this follows from scalar multiplication of vectors.  
4. Add the results: $\begin{bmatrix} 10 \\ 25 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ -18 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 13 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix}$; this follows from vector addition.  
5. Thus, $T\left(\begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix}\right) = \begin{bmatrix} 13 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix}$; this is the image of the given vector under $T$.  
6. For a general vector $\begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix}$, linearity gives $T\left(\begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix}\right) = x_1\mathbf{y}_1 + x_2\mathbf{y}_2$; this uses the same principle as before.  
7. Substitute $\mathbf{y}_1$ and $\mathbf{y}_2$: $x_1\begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix} + x_2\begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2x_1 - x_2 \\ 5x_1 + 6x_2 \end{bmatrix}$; this follows from distributivity and vector addition.  
8. Thus, $T\left(\begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix}\right) = \begin{bmatrix} 2x_1 - x_2 \\ 5x_1 + 6x_2 \end{bmatrix}$; this is the formula for the image of any vector under $T$.
