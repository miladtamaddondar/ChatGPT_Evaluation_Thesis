1. Find the eigenvalues of \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -3 & 4 \end{bmatrix}\) by solving \(\det(A - \lambda I) = 0\): \(\det\begin{bmatrix} -\lambda & 1 \\ -3 & 4-\lambda \end{bmatrix} = (-\lambda)(4-\lambda) - (-3)(1) = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 3 = 0\).
2. Factor and solve \(\lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 3 = 0\): \((\lambda - 1)(\lambda - 3) = 0\), so the eigenvalues are \(\lambda_1 = 1\) and \(\lambda_2 = 3\).
3. Find the eigenvector for \(\lambda_1 = 1\): Solve \((A - I)\vec{v} = 0\), so \(A - I = \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ -3 & 3 \end{bmatrix}\), which reduces to \(\begin{bmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\), so \(v_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}\).
4. Find the eigenvector for \(\lambda_2 = 3\): Solve \((A - 3I)\vec{v} = 0\), so \(A - 3I = \begin{bmatrix} -3 & 1 \\ -3 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\), which reduces to \(\begin{bmatrix} -3 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\), so \(v_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix}\).
5. The basis \(\mathcal{B}\) for \(\mathbb{R}^2\) that diagonalizes \(A\) is \(\left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} \right\}\).
