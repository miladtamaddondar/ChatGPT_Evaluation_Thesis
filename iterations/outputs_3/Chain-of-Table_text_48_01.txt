1. To find a nonzero vector $\mathbf{v}$ orthogonal to both $\mathbf{u}_1$ and $\mathbf{u}_2$, we solve $\mathbf{u}_1 \cdot \mathbf{v} = 0$ and $\mathbf{u}_2 \cdot \mathbf{v} = 0$, letting $\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \\ z \end{bmatrix}$, so the system is: $x + y - 2z = 0$ and $5x - y + 2z = 0$.
2. Write the augmented matrix for the system: $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & -2 & 0 \\ 5 & -1 & 2 & 0 \end{bmatrix}$.
3. Use $R_2 \leftarrow R_2 - 5R_1$: $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & -2 & 0 \\ 0 & -6 & 12 & 0 \end{bmatrix}$.
4. Use $R_2 \leftarrow -\frac{1}{6} R_2$: $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & -2 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -2 & 0 \end{bmatrix}$.
5. Use $R_1 \leftarrow R_1 - R_2$: $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -2 & 0 \end{bmatrix}$.
6. The solution is $x = 0$, $y - 2z = 0 \implies y = 2z$, so $\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 2z \\ z \end{bmatrix}$ for $z \neq 0$.
7. Choosing $z = 1$, a nonzero vector orthogonal to both $\mathbf{u}_1$ and $\mathbf{u}_2$ is $\boxed{\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 2 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}}$.
