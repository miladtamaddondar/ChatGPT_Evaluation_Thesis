1. Write the vectors as columns of a matrix and set up the augmented matrix: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & -3 & 1 \\ -3 & 2 & 1 & -8 & -6 \\ 2 & -3 & 6 & 7 & 9 \end{bmatrix}\).
2. Use \(R_3 \leftarrow R_3 + 3R_1\): \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & -3 & 1 \\ 0 & 2 & -8 & -5 & 0 \\ 2 & -3 & 6 & 7 & 9 \end{bmatrix}\).
3. Use \(R_4 \leftarrow R_4 - 2R_1\): \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & -3 & 1 \\ 0 & 2 & -8 & -5 & 0 \\ 0 & -3 & 12 & 5 & 5 \end{bmatrix}\).
4. Use \(R_3 \leftarrow R_3 - 2R_2\): \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & -3 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 & -2 \\ 0 & -3 & 12 & 5 & 5 \end{bmatrix}\).
5. Use \(R_4 \leftarrow R_4 + 3R_2\): \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & -3 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 & -2 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & -4 & 8 \end{bmatrix}\).
6. Use \(R_4 \leftarrow R_4 + (-4)R_3\): \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & -3 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 & -2 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\).
7. The nonzero columns corresponding to the leading ones are columns 1, 2, and 4, so a basis is \(\left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ -3 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \\ 2 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ -8 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \right\}\).
