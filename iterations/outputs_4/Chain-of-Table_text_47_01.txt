1. To find the least-squares solution, form the normal equations: $A^T A \mathbf{x} = A^T \mathbf{b}$. Compute $A^T A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 3 & 1 \\ -2 & 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 14 & 7 \\ 7 & 42 \end{bmatrix}$ and $A^T \mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ -2 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 16 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix}$, so the augmented matrix is $\left[ \begin{array}{cc|c} 14 & 7 & 16 \\ 7 & 42 & 6 \end{array} \right]$.
2. Divide row 1 by 14 to make the leading coefficient 1: $R_1 \to \frac{1}{14}R_1$, resulting in $\left[ \begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & \frac{1}{2} & \frac{8}{7} \\ 7 & 42 & 6 \end{array} \right]$.
3. Subtract 7 times row 1 from row 2: $R_2 \to R_2 - 7R_1$, resulting in $\left[ \begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & \frac{1}{2} & \frac{8}{7} \\ 0 & 38.5 & 6 - 8 \right] = \left[ \begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & \frac{1}{2} & \frac{8}{7} \\ 0 & \frac{77}{2} & -2 \end{array} \right]$.
4. Divide row 2 by $\frac{77}{2}$ to make the leading coefficient 1: $R_2 \to \frac{2}{77}R_2$, resulting in $\left[ \begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & \frac{1}{2} & \frac{8}{7} \\ 0 & 1 & -\frac{4}{77} \end{array} \right]$.
5. Subtract $\frac{1}{2}$ times row 2 from row 1: $R_1 \to R_1 - \frac{1}{2}R_2$, resulting in $\left[ \begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & 0 & \frac{68}{77} \\ 0 & 1 & -\frac{4}{77} \end{array} \right]$.
6. The least-squares solution is $\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{68}{77} \\ -\frac{4}{77} \end{bmatrix}$.
