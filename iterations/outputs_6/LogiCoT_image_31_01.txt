1. Let \( W \) be the set of all vectors of the form \( \begin{bmatrix} 3a + b \\ 4 \\ a - 5b \end{bmatrix} \) where \( a, b \in \mathbb{R} \); note that the second entry is always 4, regardless of \( a \) or \( b \).  
2. To check if \( W \) is a vector space, test closure under addition: let \( \mathbf{u} = \begin{bmatrix} 3a_1 + b_1 \\ 4 \\ a_1 - 5b_1 \end{bmatrix} \), \( \mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 3a_2 + b_2 \\ 4 \\ a_2 - 5b_2 \end{bmatrix} \), then \( \mathbf{u} + \mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 3(a_1 + a_2) + (b_1 + b_2) \\ 8 \\ (a_1 + a_2) - 5(b_1 + b_2) \end{bmatrix} \).  
3. The second entry of \( \mathbf{u} + \mathbf{v} \) is 8, not 4, so \( \mathbf{u} + \mathbf{v} \notin W \); thus, \( W \) is not closed under addition.  
4. Therefore, \( W \) is not a vector space, as it fails closure under addition.
