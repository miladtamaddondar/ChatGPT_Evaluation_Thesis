To construct a \( 4 \times 3 \) matrix of rank 1, recall that a matrix has rank 1 if all its rows (or columns) are scalar multiples of a single nonzero row (or column), so we can write the matrix as the outer product of a nonzero column vector \( \mathbf{u} \in \mathbb{R}^4 \) and a nonzero row vector \( \mathbf{v}^T \in \mathbb{R}^{1 \times 3} \): \( A = \mathbf{u} \mathbf{v}^T \). 
2. Choose \( \mathbf{u} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( \mathbf{v}^T = \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 6 & 7 \end{bmatrix} \). 
3. Compute the outer product: \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 6 & 7 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \times 5 & 1 \times 6 & 1 \times 7 \\ 2 \times 5 & 2 \times 6 & 2 \times 7 \\ 3 \times 5 & 3 \times 6 & 3 \times 7 \\ 4 \times 5 & 4 \times 6 & 4 \times 7 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 6 & 7 \\ 10 & 12 & 14 \\ 15 & 18 & 21 \\ 20 & 24 & 28 \end{bmatrix} \). 
4. The final answer: a \( 4 \times 3 \) matrix of rank 1 is \( \boxed{\begin{bmatrix} 5 & 6 & 7 \\ 10 & 12 & 14 \\ 15 & 18 & 21 \\ 20 & 24 & 28 \end{bmatrix}} \).
