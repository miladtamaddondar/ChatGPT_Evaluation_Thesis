1. First, find the eigenvalues of $A$ by solving $\det(A - \lambda I) = 0$: $\det\begin{pmatrix} 1-\lambda & -2 \\ 1 & 3-\lambda \end{pmatrix} = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0$.
2. Solve for $\lambda$: $\lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0$ gives $\lambda = 2 \pm i$.
3. The complex eigenvalue $\lambda = 2 + i$ has an eigenvector $v$ found by solving $(A - (2+i)I)v = 0$: $A - (2+i)I = \begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix}$.
4. Set up the system: $(-1-i)x - 2y = 0$ and $x + (1-i)y = 0$.
5. Solve $x + (1-i)y = 0$ for $x$: $x = -(1-i)y$.
6. Substitute into the first equation: $(-1-i)(-(1-i)y) - 2y = 0$.
7. Expand: $(-1-i)(-1+i) = (1 - i + i - i^2) = (1 + 1) = 2$, so $2y - 2y = 0$ (always true).
8. Thus, the eigenvector is $v = \begin{pmatrix} -(1-i) \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}$.
9. Let $v_1 = \operatorname{Re}(v) = \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}$ and $v_2 = \operatorname{Im}(v) = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}$.
10. Form $P$ with columns $v_1$ and $v_2$: $P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}$.
11. The matrix $C$ is $\begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix}$ with $a = 2$, $b = 1$ from the eigenvalue $2+i$.
12. Thus, $C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}$.
13. The invertible matrix $P$ and $C$ satisfy $A = P C P^{-1}$, where $P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}$ and $C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}$.
