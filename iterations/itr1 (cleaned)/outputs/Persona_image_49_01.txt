1. The problem asks us to find an upper triangular matrix \( R \) such that \( A = QR \), given \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 9 \\ 1 & 7 \\ -3 & -5 \\ 1 & 5 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( Q = \begin{bmatrix} 5/6 & -1/6 \\ 1/6 & 5/6 \\ -3/6 & 1/6 \\ 1/6 & 3/6 \end{bmatrix} \).
2. Since \( Q \) is already orthonormal (from Gram-Schmidt), \( R \) can be found by \( R = Q^T A \).
3. Compute \( Q^T \): \( Q^T = \begin{bmatrix} 5/6 & 1/6 & -3/6 & 1/6 \\ -1/6 & 5/6 & 1/6 & 3/6 \end{bmatrix} \).
4. Compute \( R = Q^T A \): \( R = \begin{bmatrix} 5/6 & 1/6 & -3/6 & 1/6 \\ -1/6 & 5/6 & 1/6 & 3/6 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 9 \\ 1 & 7 \\ -3 & -5 \\ 1 & 5 \end{bmatrix} \).
5. Calculate the first row of \( R \): \( \left(\frac{5}{6}, \frac{1}{6}, -\frac{3}{6}, \frac{1}{6}\right) \cdot \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ 1 \\ -3 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \frac{25}{6} + \frac{1}{6} + \frac{9}{6} + \frac{1}{6} = \frac{36}{6} = 6 \), and \( \left(\frac{5}{6}, \frac{1}{6}, -\frac{3}{6}, \frac{1}{6}\right) \cdot \begin{bmatrix} 9 \\ 7 \\ -5 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix} = \frac{45}{6} + \frac{7}{6} + \frac{15}{6} + \frac{5}{6} = \frac{72}{6} = 12 \).
6. Calculate the second row of \( R \): \( \left(-\frac{1}{6}, \frac{5}{6}, \frac{1}{6}, \frac{3}{6}\right) \cdot \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ 1 \\ -3 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = -\frac{5}{6} + \frac{5}{6} - \frac{3}{6} + \frac{3}{6} = 0 \), and \( \left(-\frac{1}{6}, \frac{5}{6}, \frac{1}{6}, \frac{3}{6}\right) \cdot \begin{bmatrix} 9 \\ 7 \\ -5 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix} = -\frac{9}{6} + \frac{35}{6} - \frac{5}{6} + \frac{15}{6} = \frac{36}{6} = 6 \).
7. Thus, \( R = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 12 \\ 0 & 6 \end{bmatrix} \).
8. Check the work by computing \( QR \): \( Q \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 12 \\ 0 & 6 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 5/6 & -1/6 \\ 1/6 & 5/6 \\ -3/6 & 1/6 \\ 1/6 & 3/6 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 12 \\ 0 & 6 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 9 \\ 1 & 7 \\ -3 & -5 \\ 1 & 5 \end{bmatrix} = A \).
9. Final answer: The upper triangular matrix is \( R = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 12 \\ 0 & 6 \end{bmatrix} \).
