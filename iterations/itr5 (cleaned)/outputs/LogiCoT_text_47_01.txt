To find the orthogonal projection of $\mathbf{b}$ onto $\text{Col }A$, we first compute the least-squares solution $\hat{\mathbf{x}}$ by solving the normal equations $A^TA\hat{\mathbf{x}} = A^T\mathbf{b}$, which is justified because the projection is $A\hat{\mathbf{x}}$ and the normal equations ensure the residual is orthogonal to $\text{Col }A$.

Compute $A^TA = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 3 & 1 \\ -2 & 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1^2 + 3^2 + (-2)^2 & 1\cdot5 + 3\cdot1 + (-2)\cdot4 \\ 5\cdot1 + 1\cdot3 + 4\cdot(-2) & 5^2 + 1^2 + 4^2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1+9+4 & 5+3-8 \\ 5+3-8 & 25+1+16 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 14 & 0 \\ 0 & 42 \end{bmatrix}$, which is correct because each entry is the dot product of the corresponding columns.

Compute $A^T\mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ -2 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1\cdot4 + 3\cdot(-2) + (-2)\cdot(-3) \\ 5\cdot4 + 1\cdot(-2) + 4\cdot(-3) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 - 6 + 6 \\ 20 - 2 - 12 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix}$, which is correct by direct computation.

Solve $A^TA\hat{\mathbf{x}} = A^T\mathbf{b}$, i.e., $\begin{bmatrix} 14 & 0 \\ 0 & 42 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix}$, so $x_1 = 4/14 = 2/7$ and $x_2 = 6/42 = 1/7$, which is correct because the coefficient matrix is diagonal and invertible.
