1. We are given $W = \left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 3a + b \\ 4 \\ a - 5b \end{bmatrix} : a, b \in \mathbb{R} \right\}$. To check if $W$ is a vector space, we test closure under addition: let $\vec{u} = \begin{bmatrix} 3a_1 + b_1 \\ 4 \\ a_1 - 5b_1 \end{bmatrix}$ and $\vec{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 3a_2 + b_2 \\ 4 \\ a_2 - 5b_2 \end{bmatrix}$, then $\vec{u} + \vec{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 3(a_1 + a_2) + (b_1 + b_2) \\ 8 \\ (a_1 + a_2) - 5(b_1 + b_2) \end{bmatrix}$, but the second entry is $8$ instead of $4$, so $W$ is not closed under addition and thus not a vector space. Therefore, $W$ is not a vector space; for example, $\begin{bmatrix} 3 \cdot 1 + 0 \\ 4 \\ 1 - 0 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 3 \cdot 0 + 1 \\ 4 \\ 0 - 5 \cdot 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 8 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix}$, but $8 \neq 4$, so $\begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 8 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix} \notin W$.
