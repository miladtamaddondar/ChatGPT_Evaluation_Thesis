The standard matrix for a rotation by angle \(\theta\) counterclockwise in \(\mathbb{R}^2\) is \(\begin{bmatrix} \cos\theta & -\sin\theta \\ \sin\theta & \cos\theta \end{bmatrix}\); this is a fundamental property of linear transformations representing rotations. 2. Substitute \(\theta = \frac{3\pi}{2}\) into the matrix: \(\cos\left(\frac{3\pi}{2}\right) = 0\) and \(\sin\left(\frac{3\pi}{2}\right) = -1\), so the matrix becomes \(\begin{bmatrix} 0 & -(-1) \\ -1 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\). 3. Simplify the matrix: \(\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\); this is correct because it matches the expected result for a \(270^\circ\) (or \(\frac{3\pi}{2}\) radians) counterclockwise rotation. 4. The standard matrix of \(T\) is \(\boxed{\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 0 \end{bmatrix}}\).
