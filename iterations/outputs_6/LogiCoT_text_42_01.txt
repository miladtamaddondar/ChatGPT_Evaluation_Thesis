1. To write $\mathbf{y}$ as the sum of a vector in $W = \text{span}\{\mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_2, \mathbf{u}_3\}$ and a vector orthogonal to $W$, we seek $\mathbf{y} = \mathbf{y}_W + \mathbf{y}_\perp$ where $\mathbf{y}_W \in W$ and $\mathbf{y}_\perp \perp W$.
2. Express $\mathbf{y}_W$ as a linear combination: $\mathbf{y}_W = a_1\mathbf{u}_1 + a_2\mathbf{u}_2 + a_3\mathbf{u}_3$ for scalars $a_1, a_2, a_3$.
3. The orthogonal projection $\mathbf{y}_W$ is the unique vector in $W$ such that $\mathbf{y}_\perp = \mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W$ is orthogonal to each $\mathbf{u}_i$, i.e., $\mathbf{u}_i^\top (\mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W) = 0$ for $i = 1,2,3$.
4. This gives the system: $\mathbf{u}_i^\top \mathbf{y} = \mathbf{u}_i^\top \mathbf{y}_W = a_1 \mathbf{u}_i^\top \mathbf{u}_1 + a_2 \mathbf{u}_i^\top \mathbf{u}_2 + a_3 \mathbf{u}_i^\top \mathbf{u}_3$ for $i=1,2,3$.
5. Compute the Gram matrix $G$ with entries $G_{ij} = \mathbf{u}_i^\top \mathbf{u}_j$: $G = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & -4 & 0 \\ -4 & 15 & 6 \\ 0 & 6 & 3 \end{bmatrix}$, verified by direct dot products.
6. Compute the vector $\mathbf{b}$ with entries $b_i = \mathbf{u}_i^\top \mathbf{y}$: $b_1 = 6$, $b_2 = 8$, $b_3 = 0$, so $\mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 8 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}$.
7. The coefficients satisfy $G \begin{bmatrix} a_1 \\ a_2 \\ a_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 8 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}$, a $3\times3$ linear system.
8. Write the system explicitly: $3a_1 - 4a_2 + 0a_3 = 6$, $-4a_1 + 15a_2 + 6a_3 = 8$, $0a_1 + 6a_2 + 3a_3 = 0$.
9. Solve the third equation for $a_3$: $6a_2 + 3a_3 = 0 \implies a_3 = -2a_2$.
10. Substitute $a_3 = -2a_2$ into the second equation: $-4a_1 + 15a_2 + 6(-2a_2) = 8 \implies -4a_1 + 15a_2 - 12a_2 = 8 \implies -4a_1 + 3a_2 = 8$.
11. Now use the first equation: $3a_1 - 4a_2 = 6$.
12. Solve the two equations: $3a_1 - 4a_2 = 6$ and $-4a_1 + 3a_2 = 8$.
13. Multiply the first equation by 4: $12a_1 - 16a_2 = 24$; multiply the second by 3: $-12a_1 + 9a_2 = 24$.
14. Add the two equations: $(12a_1 - 16a_2) + (-12a_1 + 9a_2) = 24 + 24 \implies -7a_2 = 48 \implies a_2 = -\frac{48}{7}$.
15. Substitute $a_2$ into the first equation: $3a_1 - 4(-\frac{48}{7}) = 6 \implies 3a_1 + \frac{192}{7} = 6 \implies 3a_1 = 6 - \frac{192}{7} = \frac{42 - 192}{7} = -\frac{150}{7} \implies a_1 = -\frac{50}{7}$.
16. Recall $a_3 = -2a_2 = -2 \left(-\frac{48}{7}\right) = \frac{96}{7}$.
17. Thus, $\mathbf{y}_W = a_1\mathbf{u}_1 + a_2\mathbf{u}_2 + a_3\mathbf{u}_3 = -\frac{50}{7}\mathbf{u}_1 - \frac{48}{7}\mathbf{u}_2 + \frac{96}{7}\mathbf{u}_3$.
18. Compute $\mathbf{y}_W$ explicitly: $-\frac{50}{7}\begin{bmatrix}1\\1\\0\\1\end{bmatrix} - \frac{48}{7}\begin{bmatrix}-1\\3\\1\\-2\end{bmatrix} + \frac{96}{7}\begin{bmatrix}-1\\0\\1\\1\end{bmatrix}$.
19. Calculate each component: First entry: $-\frac{50}{7}(1) - \frac{48}{7}(-1) + \frac{96}{7}(-1) = -\frac{50}{7} + \frac{48}{7} - \frac{96}{7} = -\frac{98}{7} = -14$.
20. Second entry: $-\frac{50}{7}(1) - \frac{48}{7}(3) + \frac{96}{7}(0) = -\frac{50}{7} - \frac{144}{7} + 0 = -\frac{194}{7}$.
21. Third entry: $-\frac{50}{7}(0) - \frac{48}{7}(1) + \frac{96}{7}(1) = 0 - \frac{48}{7} + \frac{96}{7} = \frac{48}{7}$.
22. Fourth entry: $-\frac{50}{7}(1) - \frac{48}{7}(-2) + \frac{96}{7}(1) = -\frac{50}{7} + \frac{96}{7} + \frac{96}{7} = \frac{142}{7}$.
23. Thus, $\mathbf{y}_W = \begin{bmatrix} -14 \\ -\frac{194}{7} \\ \frac{48}{7} \\ \frac{142}{7} \end{bmatrix}$.
24. The orthogonal component is $\mathbf{y}_\perp = \mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} -14 \\ -\frac{194}{7} \\ \frac{48}{7} \\ \frac{142}{7} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 18 \\ 3 + \frac{194}{7} \\ 3 - \frac{48}{7} \\ -1 - \frac{142}{7} \end{bmatrix}$.
25. Simplify: $3 + \frac{194}{7} = \frac{21 + 194}{7} = \frac{215}{7}$; $3 - \frac{48}{7} = \frac{21 - 48}{7} = -\frac{27}{7}$; $-1 - \frac{142}{7} = -\frac{7 + 142}{7} = -\frac{149}{7}$.
26. Thus, $\mathbf{y}_\perp = \begin{bmatrix} 18 \\ \frac{215}{7} \\ -\frac{27}{7} \\ -\frac{149}{7} \end{bmatrix}$.
27. Final answer: $\boxed{\mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} -14 \\ -\frac{194}{7} \\ \frac{48}{7} \\ \frac{142}{7} \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 18 \\ \frac{215}{7} \\ -\frac{27}{7} \\ -\frac{149}{7} \end{bmatrix}}$ where the first vector is in $W$ and the second is orthogonal to $W$.
