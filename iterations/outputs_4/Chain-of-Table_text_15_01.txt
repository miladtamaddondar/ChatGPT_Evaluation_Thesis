1. To find $P$ and $C$ such that $A = PCP^{-1}$ with $C = \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix}$, first compute the eigenvalues of $A$: $\det(A - \lambda I) = \begin{vmatrix} 1-\lambda & -2 \\ 1 & 3-\lambda \end{vmatrix} = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5$.
2. Set the characteristic polynomial to zero: $\lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0 \implies \lambda = 2 \pm i$.
3. For $\lambda = 2 + i$, solve $(A - \lambda I)x = 0$: $A - (2+i)I = \begin{pmatrix} -1 - i & -2 \\ 1 & 1 - i \end{pmatrix}$.
4. Row reduce $\begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix}$: Multiply first row by $-1$ to get $\begin{pmatrix} 1+i & 2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix}$.
5. Subtract first row from second: Row 2 = Row 2 - Row 1 $\implies \begin{pmatrix} 1+i & 2 \\ -i & -1-i \end{pmatrix}$.
6. The eigenvector for $\lambda = 2+i$ is any nonzero solution to $(1+i)x_1 + 2x_2 = 0$, so $x_1 = -\frac{2}{1+i}x_2$.
7. Rationalize denominator: $-\frac{2}{1+i} = -\frac{2(1-i)}{(1+i)(1-i)} = -\frac{2(1-i)}{2} = -(1-i) = -1 + i$.
8. So eigenvector is $x = \begin{pmatrix} -1 + i \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}$.
9. Similarly, for $\lambda = 2-i$, eigenvector is $x = \begin{pmatrix} -1 - i \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}$.
10. Let $P = \begin{pmatrix} -1+i & -1-i \\ 1 & 1 \end{pmatrix}$ and $C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}$.
11. Final answer: $A = P C P^{-1}$ where $P = \begin{pmatrix} -1+i & -1-i \\ 1 & 1 \end{pmatrix}$ and $C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}$.
