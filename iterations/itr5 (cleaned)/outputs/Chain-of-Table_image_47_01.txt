2. Compute \(A^T A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 3 & 1 \\ -2 & 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 14 & 0 \\ 0 & 42 \end{bmatrix}\).
3. Compute \(A^T \vec{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ -2 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 + 3(-2) + (-2)(-3) \\ 5(4) + 1(-2) + 4(-3) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 - 6 + 6 \\ 20 - 2 - 12 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix}\).
4. Write the augmented matrix for the normal equations: \(\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 14 & 0 & 4 \\ 0 & 42 & 6 \end{array}\right]\).
5. Divide Row 1 by 14: \(\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & 0 & \frac{2}{7} \\ 0 & 42 & 6 \end{array}\right]\).
6. Divide Row 2 by 42: \(\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & 0 & \frac{2}{7} \\ 0 & 1 & \frac{1}{7} \end{array}\right]\).
7. The least-squares solution is \(\vec{x} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{7} \\ \frac{1}{7} \end{bmatrix}\).
8. The orthogonal projection of \(\vec{b}\) onto \(\text{Col }A\) is \(A\vec{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 3 & 1 \\ -2 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{7} \\ \frac{1}{7} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{7} + \frac{5}{7} \\ \frac{6}{7} + \frac{1}{7} \\ -\frac{4}{7} + \frac{4}{7} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}\).
