1. Recognize that the matrix \(\left[ Q r_1 \; Q r_2 \; \dots \; Q r_p \right]\) consists of the images of the vectors \(r_1, \dots, r_p\) under the linear transformation represented by \(Q\). 2. Observe that the matrix \(\left[ r_1 \; r_2 \; \dots \; r_p \right]\) is an \(n \times p\) matrix whose columns are the vectors \(r_1, \dots, r_p\). 3. Recall that multiplying \(Q\) (an \(m \times n\) matrix) by the \(n \times p\) matrix \(\left[ r_1 \; r_2 \; \dots \; r_p \right]\) yields an \(m \times p\) matrix whose columns are \(Q r_1, Q r_2, \dots, Q r_p\). 4. Therefore, \(\left[ Q r_1 \; Q r_2 \; \dots \; Q r_p \right] = Q \left[ r_1 \; r_2 \; \dots \; r_p \right]\). 5. The answer is: \(\boxed{\left[ Q r_1 \; Q r_2 \; \dots \; Q r_p \right] = Q \left[ r_1 \; r_2 \; \dots \; r_p \right]}\).
