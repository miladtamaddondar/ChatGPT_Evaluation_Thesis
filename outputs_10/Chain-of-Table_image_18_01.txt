1. Find the eigenvalues of \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -3 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \) by solving \( \det(A - \lambda I) = 0 \): \( \det\left(\begin{bmatrix} 0-\lambda & 1 \\ -3 & 4-\lambda \end{bmatrix}\right) = (0-\lambda)(4-\lambda) - (-3)(1) = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 3 = 0 \).  
2. Solve the characteristic equation \( \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 3 = 0 \): The roots are \( \lambda = 1 \) and \( \lambda = 3 \).  
3. Find the eigenvector for \( \lambda = 1 \): Solve \( (A - I)\vec{v} = 0 \) where \( A - I = \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ -3 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \), so \( -v_1 + v_2 = 0 \implies v_2 = v_1 \), thus one eigenvector is \( \vec{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \).  
4. Find the eigenvector for \( \lambda = 3 \): Solve \( (A - 3I)\vec{v} = 0 \) where \( A - 3I = \begin{bmatrix} -3 & 1 \\ -3 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \), so \( -3v_1 + v_2 = 0 \implies v_2 = 3v_1 \), thus one eigenvector is \( \vec{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} \).  
5. The basis \( \mathcal{B} = \left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} \right\} \) diagonalizes \( A \), so the answer is \( \boxed{\mathcal{B} = \left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} \right\}} \).
