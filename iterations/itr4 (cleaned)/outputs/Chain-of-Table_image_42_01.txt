1. Let \( \mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} \), \( \mathbf{u}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \), \( \mathbf{u}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix} \), \( \mathbf{u}_3 = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \); express \( \mathbf{y} = \mathbf{y}_W + \mathbf{y}_\perp \), where \( \mathbf{y}_W \) is in the span of \( \{\mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_2, \mathbf{u}_3\} \) and \( \mathbf{y}_\perp \) is orthogonal to \( W \).
2. Set \( \mathbf{y}_W = c_1\mathbf{u}_1 + c_2\mathbf{u}_2 + c_3\mathbf{u}_3 \), so \( \mathbf{y} = c_1\mathbf{u}_1 + c_2\mathbf{u}_2 + c_3\mathbf{u}_3 + \mathbf{y}_\perp \).
3. To find \( c_1, c_2, c_3 \), solve \( \mathbf{y} = c_1\mathbf{u}_1 + c_2\mathbf{u}_2 + c_3\mathbf{u}_3 \), i.e., \( \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} c_1 \\ c_2 \\ c_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} \).
4. Write the augmented matrix: \( \left[ \begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 & 3 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 & -1 \end{array} \right] \).
5. Subtract row 1 from row 2 and row 4: \( R_2 \to R_2 - R_1 \), \( R_4 \to R_4 - R_1 \), yielding \( \left[ \begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 0 & -1 & 2 & -5 \end{array} \right] \).
6. Swap row 2 and row 3 to bring a leading 1 up: \( R_2 \leftrightarrow R_3 \), yielding \( \left[ \begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & -1 & 2 & -5 \end{array} \right] \).
7. Eliminate below and above the leading 1 in column 2: \( R_3 \to R_3 - 4R_2 \), \( R_4 \to R_4 + R_2 \), \( R_1 \to R_1 + R_2 \), yielding \( \left[ \begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & 0 & 7 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 0 & 0 & -3 & -13 \\ 0 & 0 & 3 & -2 \end{array} \right] \).
8. Divide row 3 by -3: \( R_3 \to R_3 / (-3) \), yielding \( \left[ \begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & 0 & 7 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & \frac{13}{3} \\ 0 & 0 & 3 & -2 \end{array} \right] \).
9. Eliminate above and below the leading 1 in column 3: \( R_2 \to R_2 - R_3 \), \( R_4 \to R_4 - 3R_3 \), yielding \( \left[ \begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & 0 & 7 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & 3 - \frac{13}{3} = -\frac{4}{3} \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & \frac{13}{3} \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & -2 - 3 \cdot \frac{13}{3} = -2 - 13 = -15 \end{array} \right] \).
10. The system is consistent, so \( c_1 = 7 \), \( c_2 = -\frac{4}{3} \), \( c_3 = \frac{13}{3} \).
11. Therefore, \( \mathbf{y}_W = 7\mathbf{u}_1 - \frac{4}{3}\mathbf{u}_2 + \frac{13}{3}\mathbf{u}_3 \), and \( \mathbf{y}_\perp = \mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W = \mathbf{0} \).
12. Final answer: \( \mathbf{y} = \left( 7\mathbf{u}_1 - \frac{4}{3}\mathbf{u}_2 + \frac{13}{3}\mathbf{u}_3 \right) + \mathbf{0} \).
