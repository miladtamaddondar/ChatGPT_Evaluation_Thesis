Write the equation $A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0}$, where $A = \begin{bmatrix}1 & -4 & 7 & -5 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & 3 \\ 2 & -6 & 6 & -4 \end{bmatrix}$ and $\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix}x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \\ x_4\end{bmatrix}$, so the system is: $1x_1 - 4x_2 + 7x_3 - 5x_4 = 0$, $0x_1 + 1x_2 - 4x_3 + 3x_4 = 0$, $2x_1 - 6x_2 + 6x_3 - 4x_4 = 0$.
2. Write the augmented matrix: $\begin{bmatrix}1 & -4 & 7 & -5 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & 3 & 0 \\ 2 & -6 & 6 & -4 & 0\end{bmatrix}$.
3. Subtract $2$ times row 1 from row 3 to eliminate $x_1$ in row 3: Row 3 becomes $[2 - 2 \cdot 1, -6 - 2 \cdot (-4), 6 - 2 \cdot 7, -4 - 2 \cdot (-5), 0 - 2 \cdot 0] = [0, 2, -8, 6, 0]$.
4. The matrix is now $\begin{bmatrix}1 & -4 & 7 & -5 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 2 & -8 & 6 & 0\end{bmatrix}$, which is correct because the row operation preserves the solution set.
5. Subtract $2$ times row 2 from row 3 to eliminate $x_2$ in row 3: Row 3 becomes $[0, 2 - 2 \cdot 1, -8 - 2 \cdot (-4), 6 - 2 \cdot 3, 0 - 2 \cdot 0] = [0, 0, 0, 0, 0]$.
6. The matrix is now $\begin{bmatrix}1 & -4 & 7 & -5 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0\end{bmatrix}$, which is correct as the third equation is a linear combination of the first two, indicating dependent rows.
7. Write the corresponding equations: $x_1 - 4x_2 + 7x_3 - 5x_4 = 0$ and $x_2 - 4x_3 + 3x_4 = 0$.
8. Express $x_2$ in terms of $x_3$ and $x_4$ from the second equation: $x_2 = 4x_3 - 3x_4$.
9. Substitute $x_2$ into the first equation: $x_1 - 4(4x_3 - 3x_4) + 7x_3 - 5x_4 = 0$.
10. Simplify: $x_1 - 16x_3 + 12x_4 + 7x_3 - 5x_4 = 0$.
11. Combine like terms: $x_1 + (-16x_3 + 7x_3) + (12x_4 - 5x_4) = 0$ gives $x_1 - 9x_3 + 7x_4 = 0$.
12. Solve for $x_1$: $x_1 = 9x_3 - 7x_4$.
13. Let $x_3 = s$ and $x_4 = t$ for free variables $s, t \in \mathbb{R}$, so $x_1 = 9s - 7t$ and $x_2 = 4s - 3t$.
14. The general solution is $\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix}x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \\ x_4\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}9s - 7t \\ 4s - 3t \\ s \\ t\end{bmatrix} = s\begin{bmatrix}9 \\ 4 \\ 1 \\ 0\end{bmatrix} + t\begin{bmatrix}-7 \\ -3 \\ 0 \\ 1\end{bmatrix}$ for $s, t \in \mathbb{R}$.
