1. To write $\mathbf{y}$ as the sum of a vector in $W$ and a vector orthogonal to $W$, we first find the orthogonal projection of $\mathbf{y}$ onto $W$, which is the column space of $\mathbf{U} = [\mathbf{u}_1\ \mathbf{u}_2\ \mathbf{u}_3]$.  
2. Set up the equation $\mathbf{y} = \mathbf{U}\mathbf{c} + \mathbf{z}$, where $\mathbf{U}\mathbf{c}$ is in $W$ and $\mathbf{z}$ is orthogonal to $W$; to find $\mathbf{c}$, solve the normal equations $\mathbf{U}^T\mathbf{U}\mathbf{c} = \mathbf{U}^T\mathbf{y}$.  
3. Compute $\mathbf{U}^T\mathbf{U}$:  $\mathbf{U}^T\mathbf{U} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 & 1 & -2 \\ -1 & 0 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 2 \\ 0 & 15 & 1 \\ 2 & 1 & 3 \end{bmatrix}$.  
4. Compute $\mathbf{U}^T\mathbf{y}$:  $\mathbf{U}^T\mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 & 1 & -2 \\ -1 & 0 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 2 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}$.  
5. Solve the system $\begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 2 \\ 0 & 15 & 1 \\ 2 & 1 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} c_1 \\ c_2 \\ c_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 2 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}$ for $c_1, c_2, c_3$.  
6. From the second equation: $0c_1 + 15c_2 + 1c_3 = 2 \implies 15c_2 + c_3 = 2$.  
7. Express $c_3 = 2 - 15c_2$ and substitute into the first and third equations: first equation: $3c_1 + 0c_2 + 2c_3 = 6 \implies 3c_1 + 2c_3 = 6 \implies 3c_1 + 2(2 - 15c_2) = 6 \implies 3c_1 + 4 - 30c_2 = 6 \implies 3c_1 - 30c_2 = 2 \implies c_1 = \frac{2}{3} + 10c_2$; third equation: $2c_1 + c_2 + 3c_3 = 0 \implies 2c_1 + c_2 + 3(2 - 15c_2) = 0 \implies 2c_1 + c_2 + 6 - 45c_2 = 0 \implies 2c_1 - 44c_2 = -6 \implies 2c_1 = -6 + 44c_2$.  
8. Substitute $c_1 = \frac{2}{3} + 10c_2$ into $2c_1 = -6 + 44c_2$: $2(\frac{2}{3} + 10c_2) = -6 + 44c_2 \implies \frac{4}{3} + 20c_2 = -6 + 44c_2 \implies \frac{4}{3} + 20c_2 + 6 = 44c_2 \implies \frac{22}{3} = 24c_2 \implies c_2 = \frac{22}{3 \times 24} = \frac{11}{36}$.  
9. Now, $c_1 = \frac{2}{3} + 10 \cdot \frac{11}{36} = \frac{2}{3} + \frac{110}{36} = \frac{2}{3} + \frac{55}{18} = \frac{12}{18} + \frac{55}{18} = \frac{67}{18}$; $c_3 = 2 - 15 \cdot \frac{11}{36} = 2 - \frac{165}{36} = \frac{72}{36} - \frac{165}{36} = -\frac{93}{36} = -\frac{31}{12}$.  
10. The projection is $\mathbf{U}\mathbf{c} = c_1\mathbf{u}_1 + c_2\mathbf{u}_2 + c_3\mathbf{u}_3 = \frac{67}{18}\begin{bmatrix}1\\1\\0\\1\end{bmatrix} + \frac{11}{36}\begin{bmatrix}-1\\3\\1\\-2\end{bmatrix} - \frac{31}{12}\begin{bmatrix}-1\\0\\1\\1\end{bmatrix}$.  
11. Compute each term: $\frac{67}{18}\begin{bmatrix}1\\1\\0\\1\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}\frac{67}{18}\\\frac{67}{18}\\0\\\frac{67}{18}\end{bmatrix}$, $\frac{11}{36}\begin{bmatrix}-1\\3\\1\\-2\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}-\frac{11}{36}\\\frac{33}{36}\\\frac{11}{36}\\-\frac{22}{36}\end{bmatrix}$, $-\frac{31}{12}\begin{bmatrix}-1\\0\\1\\1\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}\frac{31}{12}\\0\\-\frac{31}{12}\\-\frac{31}{12}\end{bmatrix}$.  
12. Add the three vectors: first entry: $\frac{67}{18} - \frac{11}{36} + \frac{31}{12} = \frac{134}{36} - \frac{11}{36} + \frac{93}{36} = \frac{216}{36} = 6$; second entry: $\frac{67}{18} + \frac{33}{36} + 0 = \frac{134}{36} + \frac{33}{36} = \frac{167}{36}$; third entry: $0 + \frac{11}{36} - \frac{31}{12} = \frac{11}{36} - \frac{93}{36} = -\frac{82}{36} = -\frac{41}{18}$; fourth entry: $\frac{67}{18} - \frac{22}{36} - \frac{31}{12} = \frac{134}{36} - \frac{22}{36} - \frac{93}{36} = \frac{19}{36}$.  
13. Thus, the projection is $\mathbf{U}\mathbf{c} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ \frac{167}{36} \\ -\frac{41}{18} \\ \frac{19}{36} \end{bmatrix}$.  
14. The orthogonal component is $\mathbf{z} = \mathbf{y} - \mathbf{U}\mathbf{c} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ \frac{167}{36} \\ -\frac{41}{18} \\ \frac{19}{36} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -2 \\ 3 - \frac{167}{36} \\ 3 + \frac{41}{18} \\ -1 - \frac{19}{36} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -2 \\ \frac{108 - 167}{36} \\ \frac{54 + 41}{18} \\ \frac{-36 - 19}{36} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -2 \\ -\frac{59}{36} \\ \frac{95}{18} \\ -\frac{55}{36} \end{bmatrix}$.  
15. Therefore, the final answer is: $\boxed{\mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ \frac{167}{36} \\ -\frac{41}{18} \\ \frac{19}{36} \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} -2 \\ -\frac{59}{36} \\ \frac{95}{18} \\ -\frac{55}{36} \end{bmatrix}}$, where the first vector is in $W$ and the second is orthogonal to $W$.
