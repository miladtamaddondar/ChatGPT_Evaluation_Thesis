1. To find another eigenvector, first find the eigenvalues of $A$: $\det(A - \lambda I) = \begin{vmatrix}0.6-\lambda & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.7-\lambda\end{vmatrix} = (0.6-\lambda)(0.7-\lambda) - (0.4)(0.3) = 0$ gives $(0.6-\lambda)(0.7-\lambda) - 0.12 = 0$.
2. Expand: $(0.6-\lambda)(0.7-\lambda) - 0.12 = 0.42 - 0.6\lambda - 0.7\lambda + \lambda^2 - 0.12 = \lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3 = 0$.
3. Solve quadratic: $\lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3 = 0 \implies \lambda = \frac{1.3 \pm \sqrt{1.69 - 1.2}}{2} = \frac{1.3 \pm 0.7}{2}$, so $\lambda_1 = 1$, $\lambda_2 = 0.3$.
4. For $\lambda_2 = 0.3$, solve $(A - 0.3I)\mathbf{v}_2 = 0$: $A - 0.3I = \begin{bmatrix}0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.4\end{bmatrix}$.
5. Write augmented matrix: $\left[\begin{array}{cc|c}0.3 & 0.3 & 0 \\ 0.4 & 0.4 & 0\end{array}\right]$.
6. Row 2 $\to$ Row 2 $- \frac{4}{3}$ Row 1: $\left[\begin{array}{cc|c}0.3 & 0.3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0\end{array}\right]$.
7. Row 1 $\to$ Row 1 divided by $0.3$: $\left[\begin{array}{cc|c}1 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0\end{array}\right]$.
8. The solution is $v_{2,1} + v_{2,2} = 0 \implies v_{2,2} = -v_{2,1}$, so $\mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}$.
9. Final answer: A basis for $\mathbb{R}^2$ consisting of $\mathbf{v}_1$ and another eigenvector $\mathbf{v}_2$ of $A$ is $\left\{\begin{bmatrix}\frac{3}{7} \\ \frac{4}{7}\end{bmatrix},\ \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}\right\}$.
