1. Write the vectors as columns of a matrix: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ -3 & 9 & -1 & 5 \\ 2 & -6 & 4 & -3 \\ -4 & 12 & 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix}\).
2. Notice that \(\mathbf{v}_2 = -3\mathbf{v}_1\), so remove \(\mathbf{v}_2\): \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & -4 \\ -3 & -1 & 5 \\ 2 & 4 & -3 \\ -4 & 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix}\).
3. Add 3 times row 1 to row 2: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & -4 \\ 0 & 5 & -7 \\ 2 & 4 & -3 \\ -4 & 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix}\).
4. Subtract 2 times row 1 from row 3: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & -4 \\ 0 & 5 & -7 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 \\ -4 & 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix}\).
5. Add 4 times row 1 to row 4: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & -4 \\ 0 & 5 & -7 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 \\ 0 & 10 & -9 \end{bmatrix}\).
6. Subtract 2 times row 2 from row 4: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & -4 \\ 0 & 5 & -7 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 \end{bmatrix}\).
7. Subtract row 3 from row 4: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & -4 \\ 0 & 5 & -7 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\).
8. The nonzero rows show the vectors are linearly independent, so a basis is \(\left\{\begin{bmatrix}1\\-3\\2\\-4\end{bmatrix},\begin{bmatrix}2\\-1\\4\\2\end{bmatrix},\begin{bmatrix}-4\\5\\-3\\7\end{bmatrix}\}\) and the dimension is \(3\).
