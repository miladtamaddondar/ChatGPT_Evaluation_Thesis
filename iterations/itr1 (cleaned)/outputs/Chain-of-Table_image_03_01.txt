1. Start with the matrix \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 9 \\ 4 & 5 \end{bmatrix} \). 
2. To perform LU factorization, write \( A = LU \) where \( L = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ l_{21} & 1 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( U = \begin{bmatrix} u_{11} & u_{12} \\ 0 & u_{22} \end{bmatrix} \). 
3. Set \( u_{11} = 6 \), \( u_{12} = 9 \) from the first row of \( A \). 
4. Compute \( l_{21} = \frac{4}{6} = \frac{2}{3} \) using \( 4 = l_{21} \cdot 6 \). 
5. Compute \( u_{22} = 5 - l_{21} \cdot 9 = 5 - \frac{2}{3} \cdot 9 = 5 - 6 = -1 \). 
6. Thus, \( L = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ \frac{2}{3} & 1 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( U = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 9 \\ 0 & -1 \end{bmatrix} \). 
7. Final answer: The LU factorization is \( \boxed{ \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 9 \\ 4 & 5 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ \frac{2}{3} & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 9 \\ 0 & -1 \end{bmatrix} } \).
