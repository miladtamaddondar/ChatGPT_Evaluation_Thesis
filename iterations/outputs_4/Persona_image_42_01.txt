1. The problem asks us to write \(\mathbf{y}\) as the sum of a vector in the subspace \(W\) spanned by \(\mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_2, \mathbf{u}_3\) and a vector orthogonal to \(W\), i.e., \(\mathbf{y} = \mathbf{y}_W + \mathbf{y}_{W^\perp}\) where \(\mathbf{y}_W \in W\) and \(\mathbf{y}_{W^\perp} \perp W\).  
2. To find \(\mathbf{y}_W\), we project \(\mathbf{y}\) onto \(W\), which is the column space of \(U = [\mathbf{u}_1 \ \mathbf{u}_2 \ \mathbf{u}_3]\), so we solve \(U\mathbf{c} = \mathbf{y}\) for \(\mathbf{c}\).  
3. Write the matrix equation: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} c_1 \\ c_2 \\ c_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix}\).  
4. Set up the augmented matrix and use Gaussian elimination: \(\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 & 3 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 & -1 \end{array}\right]\).  
5. Subtract row 1 from row 2 and row 4: row 2 becomes \([0, 4, 1, -1]\), row 4 becomes \([0, -1, 2, -5]\), so the matrix is now \(\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 0 & -1 & 2 & -5 \end{array}\right]\).  
6. Divide row 2 by 4: row 2 becomes \([0, 1, 0.25, -0.25]\), so the matrix is \(\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 1 & 0.25 & -0.25 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 0 & -1 & 2 & -5 \end{array}\right]\).  
7. Subtract row 2 from row 3: row 3 becomes \([0, 0, 0.75, 3.25]\), add row 2 to row 4: row 4 becomes \([0, 0, 2.25, -5.25]\), so the matrix is \(\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 1 & 0.25 & -0.25 \\ 0 & 0 & 0.75 & 3.25 \\ 0 & 0 & 2.25 & -5.25 \end{array}\right]\).  
8. Divide row 3 by 0.75: row 3 becomes \([0, 0, 1, \frac{13}{3}]\), divide row 4 by 2.25: row 4 becomes \([0, 0, 1, -\frac{7}{3}]\).  
9. Subtract row 3 from row 4: row 4 becomes \([0, 0, 0, -\frac{20}{3}]\), which shows the system is inconsistent, so the vectors \(\mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_2, \mathbf{u}_3\) are linearly dependent and the system is overdetermined, so we must find the least squares solution.  
10. The least squares solution is given by \(\mathbf{c} = (U^T U)^{-1} U^T \mathbf{y}\), so calculate \(U^T U\) and \(U^T \mathbf{y}\).  
11. Compute \(U^T U = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 & 1 & -2 \\ -1 & 0 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 2 & 1 \\ 2 & 15 & -2 \\ 1 & -2 & 4 \end{bmatrix}\).  
12. Compute \(U^T \mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 & 1 & -2 \\ -1 & 0 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4+3+0+(-1) \\ -4+9+3+2 \\ -4+0+3+(-1) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 10 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix}\).  
13. Solve \(\begin{bmatrix} 3 & 2 & 1 \\ 2 & 15 & -2 \\ 1 & -2 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} c_1 \\ c_2 \\ c_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 10 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix}\) for \(\mathbf{c}\).  
14. Using a calculator or by hand, the solution is \(\mathbf{c} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix}\).  
15. The projection \(\mathbf{y}_W = U\mathbf{c} = 2\mathbf{u}_1 + 1\mathbf{u}_2 - 1\mathbf{u}_3 = 2\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 2 \\ 0 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ -1 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 2 \\ 0 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 3 \\ 0 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 5 \\ 0 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix}\).  
16. The orthogonal component is \(\mathbf{y}_{W^\perp} = \mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 5 \\ 0 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -2 \\ 3 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}\).  
17. Final answer: \(\mathbf{y} = \underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 5 \\ 0 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix}}_{\mathbf{y}_W \in W} + \underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -2 \\ 3 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}}_{\mathbf{y}_{W^\perp} \perp W}\).
