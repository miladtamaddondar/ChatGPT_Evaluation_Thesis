1. Find the eigenvalues of $A$: $\det(A - \lambda I) = \begin{vmatrix} 1-\lambda & -2 \\ 1 & 3-\lambda \end{vmatrix} = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0$.
2. Solve for $\lambda$: $\lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0 \implies \lambda = 2 \pm i$.
3. For $\lambda = 2+i$, solve $(A - (2+i)I)\vec{v} = 0$: $A - (2+i)I = \begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix}$.
4. Row 2 = Row 2 + (1/(-1-i)) * Row 1: $\begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 0 & 1-i - \frac{1}{-1-i}(-2) \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 0 & 1-i + \frac{2}{1+i} \end{pmatrix}$.
5. Find eigenvector: Let $v_1 = 1$, then $(-1-i) + (-2)v_2 = 0 \implies v_2 = \frac{-1-i}{2}$, so eigenvector is $\vec{v} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ \frac{-1-i}{2} \end{pmatrix}$.
6. The complex conjugate eigenvector for $2-i$ is $\vec{w} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ \frac{-1+i}{2} \end{pmatrix}$.
7. Let $P = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ \frac{-1-i}{2} & \frac{-1+i}{2} \end{pmatrix}$.
8. $C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}$.
9. Final answer: $A = P C P^{-1}$, where $P = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ \frac{-1-i}{2} & \frac{-1+i}{2} \end{pmatrix}$ and $C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}$.
