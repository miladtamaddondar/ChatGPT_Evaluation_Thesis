1. To find the image of \( p(t) = 5 + 3t \) under \( T \), evaluate \( p(-1) \), \( p(0) \), and \( p(1) \): \( p(-1) = 5 + 3(-1) = 2 \), \( p(0) = 5 + 3(0) = 5 \), \( p(1) = 5 + 3(1) = 8 \).
2. The image is \( T(p) = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 5 \\ 8 \end{bmatrix} \).
3. To find the matrix for \( T \), apply \( T \) to each basis vector of \( \mathbb{P}_2 \): for \( 1 \), \( T(1) = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \).
4. For \( t \), \( T(t) = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \).
5. For \( t^2 \), \( T(t^2) = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \).
6. The matrix for \( T \) relative to the given bases is \( \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 1 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \).
