1. To find the matrix of \( T \) in the basis \( \mathcal{B} = \{\mathbf{b}_1, \mathbf{b}_2\} \), we need to express \( T(\mathbf{b}_1) \) and \( T(\mathbf{b}_2) \) as linear combinations of \( \mathbf{b}_1 \) and \( \mathbf{b}_2 \).  
2. Compute \( T(\mathbf{b}_1) = A\mathbf{b}_1 = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 1\\-1 & 3\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}1\\1\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}1+1\\-1+3\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}2\\2\end{bmatrix} \).  
3. Compute \( T(\mathbf{b}_2) = A\mathbf{b}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 1\\-1 & 3\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}5\\4\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}5+4\\-5+12\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}9\\7\end{bmatrix} \).  
4. To write \( \begin{bmatrix}2\\2\end{bmatrix} \) as a linear combination of \( \mathbf{b}_1 \) and \( \mathbf{b}_2 \), solve \( c_1\begin{bmatrix}1\\1\end{bmatrix} + c_2\begin{bmatrix}5\\4\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}2\\2\end{bmatrix} \), which gives the system: \( c_1 + 5c_2 = 2 \), \( c_1 + 4c_2 = 2 \).  
5. Subtract the second equation from the first: \( (c_1 + 5c_2) - (c_1 + 4c_2) = 2 - 2 \implies c_2 = 0 \), then \( c_1 = 2 \), so \( T(\mathbf{b}_1) = 2\mathbf{b}_1 \).  
6. To write \( \begin{bmatrix}9\\7\end{bmatrix} \) as a linear combination of \( \mathbf{b}_1 \) and \( \mathbf{b}_2 \), solve \( d_1\begin{bmatrix}1\\1\end{bmatrix} + d_2\begin{bmatrix}5\\4\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}9\\7\end{bmatrix} \), which gives: \( d_1 + 5d_2 = 9 \), \( d_1 + 4d_2 = 7 \).  
7. Subtract the second equation from the first: \( (d_1 + 5d_2) - (d_1 + 4d_2) = 9 - 7 \implies d_2 = 2 \), then \( d_1 = 9 - 5 \times 2 = 9 - 10 = -1 \), so \( T(\mathbf{b}_2) = -1\mathbf{b}_1 + 2\mathbf{b}_2 \).  
8. The matrix of \( T \) in the basis \( \mathcal{B} \) is \( [T]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix}2 & -1\\0 & 2\end{bmatrix} \), where the first column is the coordinates of \( T(\mathbf{b}_1) \) and the second column is the coordinates of \( T(\mathbf{b}_2) \) in the basis \( \mathcal{B} \).
