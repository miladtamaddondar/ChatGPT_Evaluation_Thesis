1. Write the vectors as columns of a matrix: \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ -3 & 9 & -1 & 5 \\ 2 & -6 & 4 & -3 \\ -4 & 12 & 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix}\).
2. Observe that \(\mathbf{v}_2 = -3\mathbf{v}_1\), so \(\mathbf{v}_2\) is dependent on \(\mathbf{v}_1\).
3. Subtract \(2 \times \mathbf{v}_1\) from \(\mathbf{v}_3\): \( \mathbf{v}_3 - 2\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} - 2\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 5 \\ 0 \\ 10 \end{bmatrix} \).
4. Subtract \(-4 \times \mathbf{v}_1\) from \(\mathbf{v}_4\): \( \mathbf{v}_4 - (-4)\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} -4 \\ 5 \\ -3 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} + 4\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ -7 \\ 5 \\ -9 \end{bmatrix} \).
5. Now consider the set \(\{\mathbf{v}_1, \mathbf{w}_3, \mathbf{w}_4\}\) where \(\mathbf{w}_3 = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 5 \\ 0 \\ 10 \end{bmatrix}\), \(\mathbf{w}_4 = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ -7 \\ 5 \\ -9 \end{bmatrix}\).
6. Check if \(\mathbf{w}_3\) and \(\mathbf{w}_4\) are linearly independent: set \(a\mathbf{w}_3 + b\mathbf{w}_4 = 0\) and solve for \(a, b\).
7. The first entry gives \(0a + 0b = 0\), the second gives \(5a - 7b = 0\), the third gives \(0a + 5b = 0\), the fourth gives \(10a - 9b = 0\).
8. From the third equation, \(5b = 0\) so \(b = 0\); from the second, \(5a = 0\) so \(a = 0\).
9. Since only the trivial solution exists, \(\mathbf{w}_3\) and \(\mathbf{w}_4\) are linearly independent.
10. Therefore, \(\{\mathbf{v}_1, \mathbf{w}_3, \mathbf{w}_4\}\) forms a basis for the subspace.
11. The dimension of the subspace is \(3\).
