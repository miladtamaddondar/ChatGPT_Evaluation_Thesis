1. To have \( AB = 0 \), each column of \( B \) must be in the null space of \( A \), so solve \( A\mathbf{x} = 0 \).  
2. Set up the system: \( \begin{bmatrix} 3 & -6 \\ -1 & 2 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \).  
3. Write equations: \( 3x_1 - 6x_2 = 0 \) and \( -x_1 + 2x_2 = 0 \).  
4. Solve the first equation for \( x_1 \): \( 3x_1 = 6x_2 \implies x_1 = 2x_2 \).  
5. The null space is spanned by \( \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \), so any nonzero scalar multiple of this vector is a solution.  
6. Choose two different nonzero multiples for the columns of \( B \), e.g., \( \mathbf{b}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \), \( \mathbf{b}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \).  
7. Construct \( B = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 4 \\ 1 & 2 \end{bmatrix} \).  
8. The answer is \( B = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 4 \\ 1 & 2 \end{bmatrix} \) since \( AB = 0 \) and both columns are nonzero and different.
