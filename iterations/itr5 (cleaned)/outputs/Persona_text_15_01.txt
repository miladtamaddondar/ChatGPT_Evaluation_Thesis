1. First, find the eigenvalues of $A$ by solving $\det(A - \lambda I) = 0$: $\det\begin{pmatrix} 1-\lambda & -2 \\ 1 & 3-\lambda \end{pmatrix} = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0$.
2. Solve the quadratic equation for $\lambda$: $\lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0 \implies \lambda = 2 \pm i$.
3. Let $\lambda = 2 + i$ and find a corresponding eigenvector by solving $(A - \lambda I)\vec{v} = 0$: $A - (2+i)I = \begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix}$.
4. Set up the system $(-1-i)x - 2y = 0$ and $x + (1-i)y = 0$; from the first equation, $x = \frac{2}{-1-i}y$.
5. Simplify $\frac{2}{-1-i} = \frac{2(-1+i)}{(-1-i)(-1+i)} = \frac{2(-1+i)}{1+1} = -1+i$, so $x = (-1+i)y$.
6. Choose $y = 1$, so the eigenvector is $\vec{v}_1 = \begin{pmatrix} -1+i \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}$.
7. The complex conjugate eigenvalue $\lambda_2 = 2 - i$ has eigenvector $\vec{v}_2 = \begin{pmatrix} -1-i \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}$.
8. Form a real basis using the real and imaginary parts: $\vec{u}_1 = \operatorname{Re}(\vec{v}_1) = \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}$, $\vec{u}_2 = \operatorname{Im}(\vec{v}_1) = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}$.
9. Let $P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}$, whose columns are $\vec{u}_1$ and $\vec{u}_2$.
10. The corresponding real canonical form is $C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}$.
11. Therefore, $A = P C P^{-1}$ with $P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}$ and $C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}$.
