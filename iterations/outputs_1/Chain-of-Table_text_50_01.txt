The inner product is $\langle f, g \rangle = f(-3)g(-3) + f(-1)g(-1) + f(1)g(1) + f(3)g(3)$, so first compute the Gram matrix $G$ for $p_0, p_1$: $G = \begin{bmatrix} \langle p_0, p_0 \rangle & \langle p_0, p_1 \rangle \\ \langle p_1, p_0 \rangle & \langle p_1, p_1 \rangle \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 & 0 \\ 0 & 20 \end{bmatrix}$.
2. Compute $\langle p_2, p_0 \rangle = p_2(-3)\cdot1 + p_2(-1)\cdot1 + p_2(1)\cdot1 + p_2(3)\cdot1 = 9+1+1+9=20$ and $\langle p_2, p_1 \rangle = (-3)^2\cdot(-3) + (-1)^2\cdot(-1) + 1^2\cdot1 + 3^2\cdot3 = -27-1+1+27=0$.
3. The projection of $p_2$ onto $\text{Span}\{p_0, p_1\}$ is $\mathrm{proj}_W(p_2) = \frac{\langle p_2, p_0 \rangle}{\langle p_0, p_0 \rangle}p_0 + \frac{\langle p_2, p_1 \rangle}{\langle p_1, p_1 \rangle}p_1 = \frac{20}{4}p_0 + 0\cdot p_1 = 5$.
4. The orthogonal component is $q = p_2 - \mathrm{proj}_W(p_2) = t^2 - 5$.
5. Evaluate $q$ at $-3, -1, 1, 3$: $q(-3) = 9-5=4$, $q(-1)=1-5=-4$, $q(1)=1-5=-4$, $q(3)=9-5=4$, so the vector is $(4, -4, -4, 4)$.
6. To scale $q$ so its value vector is $(1, -1, -1, 1)$, divide by $4$: $q(t) = \frac{1}{4}(t^2-5)$.
7. The orthogonal basis is $\{p_0, p_1, q\}$ with $q(t) = \frac{1}{4}(t^2-5)$, and the orthogonal projection of $p_2$ onto $\text{Span}\{p_0, p_1\}$ is $5$.
