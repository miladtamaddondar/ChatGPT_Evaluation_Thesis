1. Write the characteristic equation \(\det(A - \lambda I) = 0\), so \(A - \lambda I = \begin{bmatrix}0-\lambda & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 2-\lambda & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 1-\lambda\end{bmatrix}\). 
2. Compute the determinant: \(\det(A - \lambda I) = (0-\lambda)\begin{vmatrix}2-\lambda & 5 \\ 0 & 1-\lambda\end{vmatrix} = -\lambda[(2-\lambda)(1-\lambda) - 0]\). 
3. Expand: \(-\lambda[(2-\lambda)(1-\lambda)] = -\lambda[2 - 2\lambda - \lambda + \lambda^2] = -\lambda[2 - 3\lambda + \lambda^2]\). 
4. Set determinant to zero: \(-\lambda[2 - 3\lambda + \lambda^2] = 0\). 
5. The solutions are \(\lambda = 0\) and \(2 - 3\lambda + \lambda^2 = 0\). 
6. Solve quadratic: \(\lambda^2 - 3\lambda + 2 = 0\), so \((\lambda - 1)(\lambda - 2) = 0\), giving \(\lambda = 1, 2\). 
7. Therefore, the eigenvalues are \(\boxed{0, 1, 2}\).
