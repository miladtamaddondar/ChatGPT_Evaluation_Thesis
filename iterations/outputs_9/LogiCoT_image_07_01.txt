3. The product \( Q R \) is an \( m \times p \) matrix, and its \( i \)-th column is \( Q r_i \), matching the columns of \([Qr_1 \ Qr_2 \ \ldots \ Qr_p]\).  
4. Therefore, \([Qr_1 \ Qr_2 \ \ldots \ Qr_p] = Q R\), where neither \( Q \) nor \( R \) is an identity matrix.
