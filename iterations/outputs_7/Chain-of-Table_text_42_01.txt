2. Row 2 minus Row 1: $R_2 \to R_2 - R_1$ gives $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 & -1 \end{array}\right]$.
3. Row 4 minus Row 1: $R_4 \to R_4 - R_1$ gives $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 0 & -1 & 2 & -5 \end{array}\right]$.
4. Row 3 minus $(1/4)$ of Row 2: $R_3 \to R_3 - (1/4)R_2$ gives $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 0 & 3/4 & 13/4 \\ 0 & -1 & 2 & -5 \end{array}\right]$.
5. Row 4 plus $(1/4)$ of Row 2: $R_4 \to R_4 + (1/4)R_2$ gives $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 0 & 3/4 & 13/4 \\ 0 & 0 & 9/4 & -21/4 \end{array}\right]$.
6. Row 4 minus $(9/3)$ times Row 3: $R_4 \to R_4 - (9/3)R_3 = R_4 - 3R_3$ gives $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 0 & 3/4 & 13/4 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & -10 \end{array}\right]$.
7. Back-substitute: From Row 3, $(3/4)c_3 = 13/4 \implies c_3 = 13/3$. From Row 2, $4c_2 + c_3 = -1 \implies 4c_2 + 13/3 = -1 \implies 4c_2 = -1 - 13/3 = -16/3 \implies c_2 = -4/3$. From Row 1, $c_1 - c_2 - c_3 = 4 \implies c_1 - (-4/3) - 13/3 = 4 \implies c_1 + 4/3 - 13/3 = 4 \implies c_1 - 9/3 = 4 \implies c_1 = 4 + 3 = 7$.
