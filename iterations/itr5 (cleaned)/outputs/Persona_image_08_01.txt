1. To construct a \(4 \times 3\) matrix with rank 1, choose two nonzero vectors \( \mathbf{u} \in \mathbb{R}^4 \) and \( \mathbf{v} \in \mathbb{R}^3 \) and form their outer product \( A = \mathbf{u} \mathbf{v}^T \).
2. Let \( \mathbf{u} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( \mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \), then \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & 2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & 2 \\ 2 & -2 & 4 \\ 3 & -3 & 6 \\ 4 & -4 & 8 \end{bmatrix} \).
3. The matrix \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & 2 \\ 2 & -2 & 4 \\ 3 & -3 & 6 \\ 4 & -4 & 8 \end{bmatrix} \) is a \(4 \times 3\) matrix with rank 1.
