1. To write \(A\) in the form \(A = P C P^{-1}\) with \(C = \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix}\), we first find the eigenvalues of \(A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix}\) by solving \(\det(A - \lambda I) = 0\).  
2. Compute the characteristic polynomial: \(\det\begin{pmatrix} 1-\lambda & -2 \\ 1 & 3-\lambda \end{pmatrix} = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5\).  
3. The eigenvalues are roots of \(\lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0\), so \(\lambda = 2 \pm i\).  
4. Since the eigenvalues are complex conjugates, \(C\) will have the form \(\begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}\), matching the structure \(a = 2, b = 1\).  
5. Find an eigenvector for \(\lambda = 2+i\): solve \((A - (2+i)I)\vec{v} = 0\), so \(A - (2+i)I = \begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix}\).  
6. Set up the system: \((-1-i)x - 2y = 0\) and \(x + (1-i)y = 0\); from the second equation, \(x = -(1-i)y\).  
7. Substitute into the first equation: \((-1-i)(-(1-i)y) - 2y = (1+i)(1-i)y - 2y = (1^2 - i^2)y - 2y = (1-(-1))y - 2y = 2y - 2y = 0\), so any \(y \neq 0\) works.  
8. Take \(y = 1\), then \(x = -(1-i)\), so the eigenvector is \(\vec{v}_1 = \begin{pmatrix} -(1-i) \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}\).  
9. The complex conjugate eigenvector for \(\lambda = 2-i\) is \(\vec{v}_2 = \begin{pmatrix} -(1+i) \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}\).  
10. Form \(P\) from the real and imaginary parts of \(\vec{v}_1\): \(\vec{v}_1 = \begin{pmatrix} -1+i \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}\), so real part is \(\begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}\), imaginary part is \(\begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}\); thus, \(P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}\).  
11. Verify \(P\) is invertible: \(\det(P) = (-1)(0) - (1)(1) = -1 \neq 0\), so \(P\) is invertible.  
12. The matrix \(C\) is \(\begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}\), corresponding to the real canonical form for complex eigenvalues.  
13. Final answer: \(P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}\), \(C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}\), and \(A = P C P^{-1}\).
