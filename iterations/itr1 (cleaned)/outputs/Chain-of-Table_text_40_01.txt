1. Write each vector in $\mathcal{B}$ in terms of the standard basis $\{1, t, t^2\}$: $1 - 3t^2 = 1\cdot 1 + 0\cdot t + (-3)\cdot t^2$, $2 + t - 5t^2 = 2\cdot 1 + 1\cdot t + (-5)\cdot t^2$, $1 + 2t = 1\cdot 1 + 2\cdot t + 0\cdot t^2$.
2. Form the change-of-coordinates matrix $P$ by placing these as columns: $P = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 \\ -3 & -5 & 0 \end{bmatrix}$.
3. To write $t^2$ as a linear combination, set $a(1 - 3t^2) + b(2 + t - 5t^2) + c(1 + 2t) = t^2$, so in coordinates: $a\begin{bmatrix}1\\0\\-3\end{bmatrix} + b\begin{bmatrix}2\\1\\-5\end{bmatrix} + c\begin{bmatrix}1\\2\\0\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0\\0\\1\end{bmatrix}$.
4. Set up the augmented matrix: $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & 0 \\ -3 & -5 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$.
5. Use $R_3 + 3R_1 \to R_3$: $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 3 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$.
6. Use $R_3 - R_2 \to R_3$: $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$.
7. Use $R_2 - 2R_3 \to R_2$: $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & -2 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$.
8. Use $R_1 - R_3 \to R_1$: $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 0 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & -2 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$.
9. Use $R_1 - 2R_2 \to R_1$: $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 & 3 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & -2 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$.
10. The solution is $a = 3$, $b = -2$, $c = 1$, so $t^2 = 3(1 - 3t^2) - 2(2 + t - 5t^2) + 1(1 + 2t)$.
