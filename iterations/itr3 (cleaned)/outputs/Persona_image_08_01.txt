To construct a \(4 \times 3\) matrix with rank 1, recall that a matrix has rank 1 if all its rows (or columns) are scalar multiples of a single nonzero row (or column), so we can write such a matrix as the outer product of a nonzero \(4 \times 1\) column vector and a nonzero \(1 \times 3\) row vector. 
2. Let \(\mathbf{u} = \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \\ 4\end{bmatrix}\) and \(\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix}5 & 6 & 7\end{bmatrix}\), then their outer product is \(A = \mathbf{u}\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \\ 4\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}5 & 6 & 7\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}5 & 6 & 7 \\ 10 & 12 & 14 \\ 15 & 18 & 21 \\ 20 & 24 & 28\end{bmatrix}\). 
3. The matrix \(A = \begin{bmatrix}5 & 6 & 7 \\ 10 & 12 & 14 \\ 15 & 18 & 21 \\ 20 & 24 & 28\end{bmatrix}\) is a \(4 \times 3\) matrix with rank 1, since every row is a scalar multiple of the first row.
