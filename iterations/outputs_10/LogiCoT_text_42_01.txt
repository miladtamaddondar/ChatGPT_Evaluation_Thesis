1. To write $\mathbf{y}$ as the sum of a vector in $W = \text{span}\{\mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_2, \mathbf{u}_3\}$ and a vector orthogonal to $W$, we seek $\mathbf{y} = \mathbf{y}_W + \mathbf{y}_\perp$ where $\mathbf{y}_W \in W$ and $\mathbf{y}_\perp \perp W$.
2. Let $\mathbf{y}_W = a_1\mathbf{u}_1 + a_2\mathbf{u}_2 + a_3\mathbf{u}_3$ for scalars $a_1, a_2, a_3$; then $\mathbf{y}_\perp = \mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W$.
3. By the projection theorem, $\mathbf{y}_\perp$ is orthogonal to each $\mathbf{u}_i$, so $\mathbf{u}_i^T (\mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W) = 0$ for $i=1,2,3$.
4. This gives the system: $\mathbf{u}_i^T \mathbf{y} = \mathbf{u}_i^T (a_1\mathbf{u}_1 + a_2\mathbf{u}_2 + a_3\mathbf{u}_3)$ for $i=1,2,3$.
5. Compute the Gram matrix $G$ with $G_{ij} = \mathbf{u}_i^T \mathbf{u}_j$ and the vector $b$ with $b_i = \mathbf{u}_i^T \mathbf{y}$.
6. Calculate $G_{11} = (1,1,0,1)\cdot(1,1,0,1) = 1+1+0+1=3$; $G_{12} = (1,1,0,1)\cdot(-1,3,1,-2) = -1+3+0-2=0$; $G_{13} = (1,1,0,1)\cdot(-1,0,1,1) = -1+0+0+1=0$.
7. Calculate $G_{22} = (-1,3,1,-2)\cdot(-1,3,1,-2) = 1+9+1+4=15$; $G_{23} = (-1,3,1,-2)\cdot(-1,0,1,1) = 1+0+1-2=0$; $G_{33} = (-1,0,1,1)\cdot(-1,0,1,1) = 1+0+1+1=3$.
8. Thus, the Gram matrix is $G = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 15 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 3 \end{bmatrix}$, which is diagonal, so the $\mathbf{u}_i$ are mutually orthogonal.
9. Compute $b_1 = (1,1,0,1)\cdot(4,3,3,-1) = 4+3+0-1=6$; $b_2 = (-1,3,1,-2)\cdot(4,3,3,-1) = -4+9+3+2=10$; $b_3 = (-1,0,1,1)\cdot(4,3,3,-1) = -4+0+3-1=-2$.
10. The system $G \mathbf{a} = \mathbf{b}$ is $\begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 15 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} a_1 \\ a_2 \\ a_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 10 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix}$.
11. Solve for $a_1$: $3a_1 = 6 \implies a_1 = 2$; $a_2$: $15a_2 = 10 \implies a_2 = \frac{2}{3}$; $a_3$: $3a_3 = -2 \implies a_3 = -\frac{2}{3}$.
12. Thus, $\mathbf{y}_W = 2\mathbf{u}_1 + \frac{2}{3}\mathbf{u}_2 - \frac{2}{3}\mathbf{u}_3$.
13. Compute $2\mathbf{u}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 2 \\ 0 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}$, $\frac{2}{3}\mathbf{u}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} -\frac{2}{3} \\ 2 \\ \frac{2}{3} \\ -\frac{4}{3} \end{bmatrix}$, $-\frac{2}{3}\mathbf{u}_3 = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} \\ 0 \\ -\frac{2}{3} \\ -\frac{2}{3} \end{bmatrix}$.
14. Add the vectors: $\mathbf{y}_W = \begin{bmatrix} 2-\frac{2}{3}+\frac{2}{3} \\ 2+2+0 \\ 0+\frac{2}{3}-\frac{2}{3} \\ 2-\frac{4}{3}-\frac{2}{3} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 4 \\ 0 \\ 2-2 = 0 \end{bmatrix}$.
15. Thus, $\mathbf{y}_W = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 4 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}$.
16. Compute $\mathbf{y}_\perp = \mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 4 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix}$.
17. Final answer: $\boxed{\mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 4 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix}}$, where the first term is in $W$ and the second is orthogonal to $W$.
