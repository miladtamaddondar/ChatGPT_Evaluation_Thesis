a. To find the image under $T$ of $p(t) = 5 + 3t$, we evaluate $p$ at $-1$, $0$, and $1$: $T(p) = \begin{bmatrix}p(-1) \\ p(0) \\ p(1)\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}5 + 3(-1) \\ 5 + 3(0) \\ 5 + 3(1)\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 5 \\ 8\end{bmatrix}$.
b. To find the matrix for $T$ relative to the basis $\{1, t, t^2\}$ for $\mathbb{P}_2$ and the standard basis for $\mathbb{R}^3$, we apply $T$ to each basis vector and write the result as a column: $T(1) = \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 1 \\ 1\end{bmatrix}$, $T(t) = \begin{bmatrix}-1 \\ 0 \\ 1\end{bmatrix}$, $T(t^2) = \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 0 \\ 1\end{bmatrix}$, so the matrix is $\begin{bmatrix}1 & -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 1 & 1 & 1\end{bmatrix}$.
