1. Find the eigenvalues of $A$: $\det\begin{pmatrix} 1-\lambda & -2 \\ 1 & 3-\lambda \end{pmatrix} = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0$ gives $\lambda = 2 \pm i$.
2. Find an eigenvector for $\lambda = 2+i$: Solve $\begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix} = 0$. Row 1: $(-1-i)x - 2y = 0 \implies x = -\frac{2}{-1-i}y = \frac{2}{1+i}y = (1-i)y$.
3. Choose $y=1$, so eigenvector is $\begin{pmatrix} 1-i \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}$.
4. Let $P = \begin{pmatrix} \operatorname{Re}(1-i) & \operatorname{Im}(1-i) \\ \operatorname{Re}(1) & \operatorname{Im}(1) \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}$.
5. The corresponding $C$ is $\begin{pmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}$, since $C = \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix}$ with $a=2$, $b=1$.
6. Final answer: $P = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}$, $C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}$, so $A = P C P^{-1}$.
