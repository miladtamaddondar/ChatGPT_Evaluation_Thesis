1. Write the polynomials in $\mathcal{B}$ as columns in terms of the standard basis: $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 \\ -3 & -5 & 0 \end{bmatrix}$; this is the change-of-coordinates matrix from $\mathcal{B}$ to the standard basis.
2. To write $t^2$ as a linear combination, set up the augmented matrix: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 2 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & 0 \\ -3 & -5 & 0 & 1 \end{array}\right]$.
3. $R_3 + 3R_1 \to R_3$: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 2 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 3 & 1 \end{array}\right]$.
4. $R_3 - R_2 \to R_3$: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 2 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 1 \end{array}\right]$.
5. $R_2 - 2R_3 \to R_2$: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 2 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & -2 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 1 \end{array}\right]$.
6. $R_1 - R_3 \to R_1$: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 2 & 0 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & -2 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 1 \end{array}\right]$.
7. $R_1 - 2R_2 \to R_1$: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & 0 & 3 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & -2 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 1 \end{array}\right]$.
8. Thus, $t^2 = 3(1 - 3t^2) - 2(2 + t - 5t^2) + 1(1 + 2t)$ in terms of $\mathcal{B}$.
