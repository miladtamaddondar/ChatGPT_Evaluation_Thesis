1. To write \( A \) in the form \( A = P C P^{-1} \) with \( C = \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix} \), we need to find the eigenvalues of \( A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix} \) by solving \( \det(A - \lambda I) = 0 \).
2. Compute the characteristic polynomial: \( \det\left(\begin{pmatrix} 1-\lambda & -2 \\ 1 & 3-\lambda \end{pmatrix}\right) = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 \).
3. Set the characteristic polynomial to zero: \( \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0 \).
4. Solve for \( \lambda \): \( \lambda = \frac{4 \pm \sqrt{(-4)^2 - 4 \cdot 1 \cdot 5}}{2} = \frac{4 \pm \sqrt{16 - 20}}{2} = \frac{4 \pm 2i}{2} = 2 \pm i \).
5. Since the eigenvalues are complex conjugates, \( C \) will be \( \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \), matching the form \( \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix} \) with \( a = 2, b = 1 \).
6. Find an eigenvector for \( \lambda = 2 + i \): Solve \( (A - (2+i)I)\vec{v} = 0 \), so \( A - (2+i)I = \begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix} \).
7. Let \( \vec{v} = \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix} \), then \( (-1-i)x - 2y = 0 \) and \( x + (1-i)y = 0 \).
8. From the first equation, \( (-1-i)x = 2y \implies x = \frac{2y}{-1-i} \).
9. Rationalize denominator: \( x = \frac{2y}{-1-i} \cdot \frac{-1+i}{-1+i} = \frac{2y(-1+i)}{(-1-i)(-1+i)} = \frac{2y(-1+i)}{1+1} = y(-1+i) \).
10. Choose \( y = 1 \), so \( \vec{v}_1 = \begin{pmatrix} -1+i \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \).
11. The real and imaginary parts of \( \vec{v}_1 \) are \( \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \) and \( \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix} \), which will form the columns of \( P \).
12. Set \( P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \).
13. Verify \( P \) is invertible: \( \det(P) = (-1)(0) - (1)(1) = -1 \neq 0 \), so \( P \) is invertible.
14. The matrix \( C \) is \( \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \), as found from the real and imaginary parts of the eigenvalue.
15. Final answer: \( P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \), \( C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \), and \( A = P C P^{-1} \).
