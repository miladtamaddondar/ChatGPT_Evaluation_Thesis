1. The $\mathcal{B}$-matrix for $T$ is the matrix representation of $T$ with respect to the basis $\mathcal{B}$, denoted $[T]_{\mathcal{B}}$, and is given by $[T]_{\mathcal{B}} = P^{-1}AP$ where $P$ is the change-of-basis matrix from $\mathcal{B}$ to the standard basis.  
2. Construct $P$ by placing the basis vectors $\mathbf{b}_1$ and $\mathbf{b}_2$ as columns: $P = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix}$, verified by definition of change-of-basis matrix.  
3. Compute $P^{-1}$ using the formula for the inverse of a $2 \times 2$ matrix: $P^{-1} = \frac{1}{(1)(4)-(5)(1)}\begin{bmatrix} 4 & -5 \\ -1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} = \frac{1}{-1}\begin{bmatrix} 4 & -5 \\ -1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -4 & 5 \\ 1 & -1 \end{bmatrix}$, verified by checking the determinant is $-1 \neq 0$.  
4. Compute $AP$ to get $A$ acting on the basis vectors: $AP = A\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} (1+1) & (5+4) \\ (-1+3) & (-5+12) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 9 \\ 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix}$, verified by matrix multiplication.  
5. Compute $[T]_{\mathcal{B}} = P^{-1}AP = \begin{bmatrix} -4 & 5 \\ 1 & -1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 9 \\ 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} (-4)(2) + (5)(2) & (-4)(9) + (5)(7) \\ (1)(2) + (-1)(2) & (1)(9) + (-1)(7) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -8+10 & -36+35 \\ 2-2 & 9-7 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 0 & 2 \end{bmatrix}$, verified by direct calculation.  
6. The $\mathcal{B}$-matrix for $T$ is $\boxed{\begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 0 & 2 \end{bmatrix}}$.
