1. To find $q$ in $\mathbb{P}_2$ given its coordinate vector $[q]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}$ with respect to the basis $\mathcal{B} = \{p_1, p_2, p_3\}$, we use the definition $q = -1\cdot p_1 + 1\cdot p_2 + 2\cdot p_3$.
2. Substitute the given polynomials: $q = -1\cdot (1 + t^2) + 1\cdot (t - 3t^2) + 2\cdot (1 + t - 3t^2)$.
3. Distribute the coefficients: $q = (-1)\cdot 1 + (-1)\cdot t^2 + 1\cdot t + 1\cdot (-3t^2) + 2\cdot 1 + 2\cdot t + 2\cdot (-3t^2)$.
4. Expand and combine like terms: $q = -1 - t^2 + t - 3t^2 + 2 + 2t - 6t^2$.
5. Group like terms: constant terms $(-1 + 2)$, $t$ terms $(t + 2t)$, $t^2$ terms $(-t^2 - 3t^2 - 6t^2)$.
6. Simplify each group: constants $= 1$, $t$ terms $= 3t$, $t^2$ terms $= -10t^2$.
7. Therefore, $q = 1 + 3t - 10t^2$.
