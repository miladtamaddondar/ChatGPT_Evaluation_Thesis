1. Write the vectors as columns of a matrix: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ -3 & 9 & -1 & 5 \\ 2 & -6 & 4 & -3 \\ -4 & 12 & 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix}\).
2. Row 2 + 3 × Row 1 → Row 2: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 & -7 \\ 2 & -6 & 4 & -3 \\ -4 & 12 & 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix}\).
3. Row 3 - 2 × Row 1 → Row 3: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 & -7 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 5 \\ -4 & 12 & 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix}\).
4. Row 4 + 4 × Row 1 → Row 4: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 & -7 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 10 & -9 \end{bmatrix}\).
5. Row 4 - 2 × Row 2 → Row 4: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 & -7 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 5 \end{bmatrix}\).
6. Row 4 - Row 3 → Row 4: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 & -7 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\).
7. The matrix is now in row echelon form. The pivot columns are 1, 3, and 4, so the basis is \(\left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -6 \\ 4 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} -4 \\ 12 \\ -3 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \right\}\) and the dimension is 3.
