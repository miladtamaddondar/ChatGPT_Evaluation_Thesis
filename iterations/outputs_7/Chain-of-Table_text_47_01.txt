2. Compute $A^T A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 3 & 1 \\ -2 & 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1^2 + 3^2 + (-2)^2 & 1 \cdot 5 + 3 \cdot 1 + (-2) \cdot 4 \\ 5 \cdot 1 + 1 \cdot 3 + 4 \cdot (-2) & 5^2 + 1^2 + 4^2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 + 9 + 4 & 5 + 3 - 8 \\ 5 + 3 - 8 & 25 + 1 + 16 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 14 & 0 \\ 0 & 42 \end{bmatrix}$.
3. Compute $A^T \mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ -2 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \cdot 4 + 3 \cdot (-2) + (-2) \cdot (-3) \\ 5 \cdot 4 + 1 \cdot (-2) + 4 \cdot (-3) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 - 6 + 6 \\ 20 - 2 - 12 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix}$.
4. The normal equations are $\begin{bmatrix} 14 & 0 \\ 0 & 42 \end{bmatrix} \mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix}$.
5. Write the augmented matrix: $\left[ \begin{array}{cc|c} 14 & 0 & 4 \\ 0 & 42 & 6 \end{array} \right]$.
6. Row 1: Divide by 14: $R_1 \to \frac{1}{14} R_1$: $\left[ \begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & 0 & \frac{2}{7} \\ 0 & 42 & 6 \end{array} \right]$.
7. Row 2: Divide by 42: $R_2 \to \frac{1}{42} R_2$: $\left[ \begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & 0 & \frac{2}{7} \\ 0 & 1 & \frac{1}{7} \end{array} \right]$.
8. Solution: $\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{7} \\ \frac{1}{7} \end{bmatrix}$ is the least-squares solution.
9.
