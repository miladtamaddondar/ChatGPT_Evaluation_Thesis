1. Find the eigenvalues of \(A\) by solving \(\det(A - \lambda I) = 0\): \(\det\left(\begin{bmatrix}0.6-\lambda & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.7-\lambda\end{bmatrix}\right) = (0.6-\lambda)(0.7-\lambda) - 0.12 = \lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3 = 0\).
2. Solve the quadratic equation: \(\lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3 = 0\) gives \(\lambda = 1\) and \(\lambda = 0.3\).
3. Find the eigenvector for \(\lambda = 1\): Solve \((A - I)\mathbf{v} = 0\) gives \(\begin{bmatrix}-0.4 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & -0.3\end{bmatrix}\mathbf{v} = 0\).
4. Row reduce \(\begin{bmatrix}-0.4 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & -0.3\end{bmatrix}\): Add row 1 and row 2 to get \(\begin{bmatrix}0 & 0 \\ 0.4 & -0.3\end{bmatrix}\), so the system reduces to \(0.4v_1 - 0.3v_2 = 0\).
5. Solve \(0.4v_1 - 0.3v_2 = 0\) gives \(v_2 = \frac{4}{3}v_1\), so an eigenvector is any scalar multiple of \(\begin{bmatrix}3 \\ 4\end{bmatrix}\), and normalized to sum to 1 gives \(\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix}\frac{3}{7} \\ \frac{4}{7}\end{bmatrix}\).
6. Find the eigenvector for \(\lambda = 0.3\): Solve \((A - 0.3I)\mathbf{v} = 0\) gives \(\begin{bmatrix}0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.4\end{bmatrix}\mathbf{v} = 0\).
7. Row reduce \(\begin{bmatrix}0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.4\end{bmatrix}\): Subtract \(\frac{4}{3}\) times row 1 from row 2 to get \(\begin{bmatrix}0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0 & 0\end{bmatrix}\), so the system reduces to \(0.3v_1 + 0.3v_2 = 0\).
8. Solve \(0.3v_1 + 0.3v_2 = 0\) gives \(v_2 = -v_1\), so an eigenvector is any scalar multiple of \(\begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}\).
9. Thus, a basis for \(\mathbb{R}^2\) consisting of eigenvectors is \(\left\{\begin{bmatrix}\frac{3}{7} \\ \frac{4}{7}\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}\right\}\).
