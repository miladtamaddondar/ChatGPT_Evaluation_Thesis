1. Let the columns of the matrix be \( \mathbf{a}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \\ -1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \), \( \mathbf{a}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \\ 4 \\ -4 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \), \( \mathbf{a}_3 = \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ -4 \\ -3 \\ 7 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \). Start Gram-Schmidt with \( \mathbf{v}_1 = \mathbf{a}_1 \).
2. Compute \( \mathbf{v}_2 = \mathbf{a}_2 - \frac{\langle \mathbf{a}_2, \mathbf{v}_1 \rangle}{\langle \mathbf{v}_1, \mathbf{v}_1 \rangle} \mathbf{v}_1 \). \( \langle \mathbf{a}_2, \mathbf{v}_1 \rangle = 2\cdot1 + 1\cdot(-1) + 4\cdot(-1) + (-4)\cdot1 + 2\cdot1 = 2 - 1 - 4 - 4 + 2 = -5 \), \( \langle \mathbf{v}_1, \mathbf{v}_1 \rangle = 1^2 + (-1)^2 + (-1)^2 + 1^2 + 1^2 = 1+1+1+1+1=5 \), so \( \mathbf{v}_2 = \mathbf{a}_2 - \left(-1\right)\mathbf{v}_1 = \mathbf{a}_2 + \mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 2+1 \\ 1+(-1) \\ 4+(-1) \\ -4+1 \\ 2+1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 0 \\ 3 \\ -3 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} \).
3. Compute \( \mathbf{v}_3 = \mathbf{a}_3 - \frac{\langle \mathbf{a}_3, \mathbf{v}_1 \rangle}{\langle \mathbf{v}_1, \mathbf{v}_1 \rangle} \mathbf{v}_1 - \frac{\langle \mathbf{a}_3, \mathbf{v}_2 \rangle}{\langle \mathbf{v}_2, \mathbf{v}_2 \rangle} \mathbf{v}_2 \). First, \( \langle \mathbf{a}_3, \mathbf{v}_1 \rangle = 5\cdot1 + (-4)\cdot(-1) + (-3)\cdot(-1) + 7\cdot1 + 1\cdot1 = 5+4+3+7+1=20 \), so \( \frac{20}{5} = 4 \). Then \( \mathbf{a}_3 - 4\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 5-4\cdot1 \\ -4-4\cdot(-1) \\ -3-4\cdot(-1) \\ 7-4\cdot1 \\ 1-4\cdot1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ 3 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} \). Next, \( \langle \mathbf{a}_3, \mathbf{v}_2 \rangle = 5\cdot3 + (-4)\cdot0 + (-3)\cdot3 + 7\cdot(-3) + 1\cdot3 = 15+0-9-21+3 = -12 \), \( \langle \mathbf{v}_2, \mathbf{v}_2 \rangle = 3^2 + 0^2 + 3^2 + (-3)^2 + 3^2 = 9+0+9+9+9=36 \), so \( \frac{-12}{36} = -\frac{1}{3} \). Thus, \( \mathbf{v}_3 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ 3 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} - \left(-\frac{1}{3}\right)\begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 0 \\ 3 \\ -3 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1+1 \\ 0+0 \\ 1+1 \\ 3-1 \\ -3+1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 0 \\ 2 \\ 2 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix} \).
4. The orthogonal basis for the column space is \( \left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \\ -1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 0 \\ 3 \\ -3 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 0 \\ 2 \\ 2 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix} \right\} \).
