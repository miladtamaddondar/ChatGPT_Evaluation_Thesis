1. To find a basis for the eigenspace corresponding to \(\lambda = 4\), we solve \((A - 4I)\vec{x} = 0\). 2. Compute \(A - 4I = \begin{bmatrix} 3-4 & 0 & 2 & 0 \\ 1 & 3-4 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1-4 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 4-4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 0 & 2 & 0 \\ 1 & -1 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\). 3. Let \(\vec{x} = \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \\ x_4 \end{bmatrix}\), then the system is \(\begin{cases} -x_1 + 2x_3 = 0 \\ x_1 - x_2 + x_3 = 0 \\ x_2 - 3x_3 = 0 \\ 0 = 0 \end{cases}\). 4. From the third equation, \(x_2 = 3x_3\). 5. Substitute \(x_2 = 3x_3\) into the second equation: \(x_1 - 3x_3 + x_3 = x_1 - 2x_3 = 0 \implies x_1 = 2x_3\). 6. The first equation is \(-x_1 + 2x_3 = -2x_3 + 2x_3 = 0\), which is satisfied. 7. The fourth equation is always true. 8. The general solution is \(\vec{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 2x_3 \\ 3x_3 \\ x_3 \\ x_4 \end{bmatrix} = x_3\begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} + x_4\begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}\). 9. Thus, a basis for the eigenspace corresponding to \(\lambda = 4\) is \(\left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \right\}\).
