1. Identify the basis \(\mathcal{B}\) for \(\mathbb{P}_3\) as \(\{1,\, 2t,\, -2+4t^2,\, -12t+8t^3\}\). 
2. Express \(p(t) = 7 - 12t - 8t^2 + 12t^3\) as a linear combination of the basis elements: \(p(t) = a\cdot 1 + b\cdot 2t + c\cdot (-2+4t^2) + d\cdot (-12t+8t^3)\). 
3. Expand the linear combination: \(a + 2b t + c(-2) + 4c t^2 + d(-12t) + 8d t^3 = a - 2c + (2b - 12d)t + 4c t^2 + 8d t^3\). 
4. Match coefficients with \(p(t)\): constant: \(a-2c=7\), \(t\): \(2b-12d=-12\), \(t^2\): \(4c=-8\), \(t^3\): \(8d=12\). 
5. Solve \(4c = -8 \implies c = -2\). 
6. Solve \(8d = 12 \implies d = \frac{3}{2}\). 
7. Substitute \(c\) into \(a-2c=7\): \(a-2(-2)=7 \implies a+4=7 \implies a=3\). 
8. Substitute \(d\) into \(2b-12d=-12\): \(2b-12(\frac{3}{2})=-12 \implies 2b-18=-12 \implies 2b=6 \implies b=3\). 
9. The coordinate vector of \(p\) relative to \(\mathcal{B}\) is \(\begin{bmatrix}3\\3\\-2\\\frac{3}{2}\end{bmatrix}\).
