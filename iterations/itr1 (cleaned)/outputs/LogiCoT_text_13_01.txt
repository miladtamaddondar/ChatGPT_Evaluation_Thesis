1. Find the eigenspace for \(\lambda = 4\) by solving \((A - 4I)\vec{x} = 0\): \(A - 4I = \begin{bmatrix}-1 & 0 & 2 & 0 \\ 1 & -1 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0\end{bmatrix}\).  
2. Set up the system \(\begin{bmatrix}-1 & 0 & 2 & 0 \\ 1 & -1 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \\ x_4\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0\end{bmatrix}\).  
3. Write the equations: \(-x_1 + 2x_3 = 0\), \(x_1 - x_2 + x_3 = 0\), \(x_2 - 3x_3 = 0\), \(0 = 0\).  
4. Solve the third equation for \(x_2\): \(x_2 = 3x_3\).  
5. Substitute \(x_2 = 3x_3\) into the second equation: \(x_1 - 3x_3 + x_3 = x_1 - 2x_3 = 0\), so \(x_1 = 2x_3\).  
6. Substitute \(x_1 = 2x_3\) into the first equation: \(-2x_3 + 2x_3 = 0\), which is always true, confirming consistency.  
7. \(x_4\) is free since it does not appear in any equation.  
8. The general solution is \(\begin{bmatrix}x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \\ x_4\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}2x_3 \\ 3x_3 \\ x_3 \\ x_4\end{bmatrix} = x_3\begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ 0\end{bmatrix} + x_4\begin{bmatrix}0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 1\end{bmatrix}\).  
9. Therefore, a basis for the eigenspace corresponding to \(\lambda = 4\) is \(\left\{ \begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ 0\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 1\end{bmatrix} \right\}\).
