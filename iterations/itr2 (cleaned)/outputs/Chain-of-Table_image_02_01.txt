1. Write the formula for changing from coordinates in basis \( B \) to the standard basis: \( \mathbf{x} = 3\begin{bmatrix}1\\1\\3\end{bmatrix} + 2\begin{bmatrix}2\\-1\\1\end{bmatrix} \). 
2. Multiply out the scalars: \( 3\begin{bmatrix}1\\1\\3\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}3\\3\\9\end{bmatrix} \) and \( 2\begin{bmatrix}2\\-1\\1\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}4\\-2\\2\end{bmatrix} \). 
3. Add the vectors: \( \begin{bmatrix}3\\3\\9\end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix}4\\-2\\2\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}7\\1\\11\end{bmatrix} \). 
4. The answer is \( \mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix}7\\1\\11\end{bmatrix} \).
