1. To write \( A \) in the form \( A = P C P^{-1} \) with \( C = \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix} \), we first find the eigenvalues of \( A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix} \) by solving \( \det(A - \lambda I) = 0 \).
2. Compute \( \det \begin{pmatrix} 1-\lambda & -2 \\ 1 & 3-\lambda \end{pmatrix} = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0 \).
3. Solve the quadratic equation: \( \lambda = \frac{4 \pm \sqrt{16-20}}{2} = \frac{4 \pm 2i}{2} = 2 \pm i \).
4. The matrix \( C \) will have the form \( \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \), since this form matches the real and imaginary parts of the eigenvalues.
5. Find an eigenvector for \( \lambda = 2 + i \) by solving \( (A - (2+i)I)\vec{v} = 0 \), so \( \begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix} \vec{v} = 0 \).
6. Let \( \vec{v} = \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix} \), then \( (-1-i)x - 2y = 0 \implies x = \frac{-2}{-1-i}y \).
7. Simplify \( \frac{-2}{-1-i} = \frac{-2(-1+i)}{(-1-i)(-1+i)} = \frac{2(-1+i)}{2} = -1 + i \), so \( x = (-1+i)y \).
8. Choose \( y = 1 \), so the eigenvector is \( \vec{v} = \begin{pmatrix} -1+i \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \).
9. The real and imaginary parts of \( \vec{v} \) are \( \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \) and \( \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix} \), so set \( P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \).
10. Thus, the answer is \( P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \) and \( C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \), so \( A = P C P^{-1} \).
