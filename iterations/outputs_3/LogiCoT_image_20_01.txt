1. To find a basis for \(\mathbb{R}^2\) consisting of \(\mathbf{v}_1\) and another eigenvector \(\mathbf{v}_2\) of \(A\), first verify that \(\mathbf{v}_1\) is an eigenvector by checking if \(A\mathbf{v}_1 = \lambda_1 \mathbf{v}_1\) for some \(\lambda_1\): \(A\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix}0.6 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.7\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}\frac{3}{7} \\ \frac{4}{7}\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0.6 \cdot \frac{3}{7} + 0.3 \cdot \frac{4}{7} \\ 0.4 \cdot \frac{3}{7} + 0.7 \cdot \frac{4}{7}\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}\frac{1.8}{7} + \frac{1.2}{7} \\ \frac{1.2}{7} + \frac{2.8}{7}\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}\frac{3}{7} \\ \frac{4}{7}\end{bmatrix} = \mathbf{v}_1\), so \(\lambda_1 = 1\) and \(\mathbf{v}_1\) is an eigenvector. 
2. To find another eigenvector, compute the characteristic polynomial of \(A\): \(\det(A - \lambda I) = \det\left(\begin{bmatrix}0.6-\lambda & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.7-\lambda\end{bmatrix}\right) = (0.6-\lambda)(0.7-\lambda) - (0.3)(0.4) = 0.42 - 0.6\lambda - 0.7\lambda + \lambda^2 - 0.12 = \lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3\). 
3. Solve for the eigenvalues: \(\lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3 = 0\) gives \(\lambda = \frac{1.3 \pm \sqrt{1.69 - 1.2}}{2} = \frac{1.3 \pm 0.7}{2}\), so \(\lambda_1 = 1\) and \(\lambda_2 = 0.3\). 
4. For \(\lambda_2 = 0.3\), solve \((A - 0.3I)\mathbf{v}_2 = 0\): \(A - 0.3I = \begin{bmatrix}0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.4\end{bmatrix}\), so \(\begin{bmatrix}0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.4\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}x \\ y\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0 \\ 0\end{bmatrix}\) gives \(0.3x + 0.3y = 0\) and \(0.4x + 0.4y = 0\), so \(x = -y\). 
5. Thus, \(\mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}\) (or any nonzero scalar multiple) is an eigenvector for \(\lambda_2 = 0.3\). 
6. Therefore, a basis for \(\mathbb{R}^2\) consisting of eigenvectors of \(A\) is \(\left\{\begin{bmatrix}\frac{3}{7} \\ \frac{4}{7}\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}\right\}\).
