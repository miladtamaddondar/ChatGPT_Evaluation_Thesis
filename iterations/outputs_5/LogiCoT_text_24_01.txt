1. We are asked to find all $\mathbf{x} \in \mathbb{R}^4$ such that $A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0}$, i.e., to solve the homogeneous system $A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0}$, where $A = \begin{bmatrix}1 & -4 & 7 & -5 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & 3 \\ 2 & -6 & 6 & -4 \end{bmatrix}$.
2. Write the system as equations: $x_1 - 4x_2 + 7x_3 - 5x_4 = 0$, $x_2 - 4x_3 + 3x_4 = 0$, $2x_1 - 6x_2 + 6x_3 - 4x_4 = 0$.
3. Observe that the third equation is $2$ times the first equation: $2(x_1 - 4x_2 + 7x_3 - 5x_4) = 2x_1 - 8x_2 + 14x_3 - 10x_4$, but the third equation is $2x_1 - 6x_2 + 6x_3 - 4x_4$, which is not exactly $2$ times the first; let's subtract $2$ times the first equation from the third: $[2x_1 - 6x_2 + 6x_3 - 4x_4] - 2[x_1 - 4x_2 + 7x_3 - 5x_4] = (2x_1 - 2x_1) + (-6x_2 + 8x_2) + (6x_3 - 14x_3) + (-4x_4 + 10x_4) = 2x_2 - 8x_3 + 6x_4 = 0$.
4. Add this new equation to the system: $2x_2 - 8x_3 + 6x_4 = 0$; now the system is $x_1 - 4x_2 + 7x_3 - 5x_4 = 0$, $x_2 - 4x_3 + 3x_4 = 0$, $2x_2 - 8x_3 + 6x_4 = 0$.
5. Notice that the third equation is $2$ times the second: $2(x_2 - 4x_3 + 3x_4) = 2x_2 - 8x_3 + 6x_4$, so the third equation is redundant.
6. Now, solve the second equation for $x_2$: $x_2 = 4x_3 - 3x_4$.
7. Substitute $x_2$ into the first equation: $x_1 - 4(4x_3 - 3x_4) + 7x_3 - 5x_4 = 0$.
8. Expand and simplify: $x_1 - 16x_3 + 12x_4 + 7x_3 - 5x_4 = 0$.
9. Combine like terms: $x_1 + (-16x_3 + 7x_3) + (12x_4 - 5x_4) = 0$ gives $x_1 - 9x_3 + 7x_4 = 0$.
10. Solve for $x_1$: $x_1 = 9x_3 - 7x_4$.
11. Now, express the general solution in terms of free variables $x_3$ and $x_4$: $\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \\ x_4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 9x_3 - 7x_4 \\ 4x_3 - 3x_4 \\ x_3 \\ x_4 \end{bmatrix}$.
12. Write the solution as a linear combination: $\mathbf{x} = x_3 \begin{bmatrix} 9 \\ 4 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} + x_4 \begin{bmatrix} -7 \\ -3 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}$.
13. Final answer: All $\mathbf{x} \in \mathbb{R}^4$ mapped to zero by $A$ are $\boxed{\mathbf{x} = x_3 \begin{bmatrix} 9 \\ 4 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} + x_4 \begin{bmatrix} -7 \\ -3 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}, \text{ for } x_3, x_4 \in \mathbb{R}}$.
