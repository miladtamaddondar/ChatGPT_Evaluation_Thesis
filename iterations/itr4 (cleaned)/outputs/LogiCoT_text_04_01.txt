2. Multiply each column of \(A\) by the corresponding diagonal entry in \(D\): first column by 2, second by 3, third by 5, giving \(AD = \begin{bmatrix}1 \times 2 & 1 \times 3 & 1 \times 5 \\ 1 \times 2 & 2 \times 3 & 3 \times 5 \\ 1 \times 2 & 4 \times 3 & 5 \times 5\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}2 & 3 & 5 \\ 2 & 6 & 15 \\ 2 & 12 & 25\end{bmatrix}\).  
3. Verify correctness: multiplying by a diagonal matrix on the right scales columns, which matches the result above.

To compute \(DA\), recall that multiplying a matrix \(A\) by a diagonal matrix \(D\) on the left scales the rows of \(A\) by the corresponding diagonal entries of \(D\): \(DA = \begin{bmatrix}2 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 5\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}1 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 1 & 4 & 5\end{bmatrix}\).  
5. Multiply each row of \(A\) by the corresponding diagonal entry in \(D\): first row by 2, second by 3, third by 5, giving \(DA = \begin{bmatrix}2 \times 1 & 2 \times 1 & 2 \times 1 \\ 3 \times 1 & 3 \times 2 & 3 \times 3 \\ 5 \times 1 & 5 \times 4 & 5 \times 5\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}2 & 2 & 2 \\ 3 & 6 & 9 \\ 5 & 20 & 25\end{bmatrix}\).  
6. Verify correctness: multiplying by a diagonal matrix on the left scales rows, which matches the result above.
