To find a basis for $\mathbb{R}^2$ consisting of $\mathbf{v}_1$ and another eigenvector $\mathbf{v}_2$ of $A$, first verify that $\mathbf{v}_1$ is an eigenvector by computing $A\mathbf{v}_1$ and checking if it is a scalar multiple of $\mathbf{v}_1$: $A\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix}0.6 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.7\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}3/7 \\ 4/7\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0.6 \cdot \frac{3}{7} + 0.3 \cdot \frac{4}{7} \\ 0.4 \cdot \frac{3}{7} + 0.7 \cdot \frac{4}{7}\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}\frac{1.8}{7} + \frac{1.2}{7} \\ \frac{1.2}{7} + \frac{2.8}{7}\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}\frac{3}{7} \\ \frac{4}{7}\end{bmatrix} = \mathbf{v}_1$, so $\mathbf{v}_1$ is an eigenvector with eigenvalue $1$.
2. To find another eigenvector, compute the characteristic polynomial of $A$: $\det(A - \lambda I) = \det\begin{bmatrix}0.6-\lambda & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.7-\lambda\end{bmatrix} = (0.6-\lambda)(0.7-\lambda) - (0.4)(0.3) = (0.42 - 0.6\lambda - 0.7\lambda + \lambda^2) - 0.12 = \lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3$.
3. Solve the characteristic equation $\lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3 = 0$ to find the eigenvalues: $\lambda = \frac{1.3 \pm \sqrt{1.69 - 1.2}}{2} = \frac{1.3 \pm \sqrt{0.49}}{2} = \frac{1.3 \pm 0.7}{2}$, so $\lambda_1 = 1$ and $\lambda_2 = 0.3$.
4. To find an eigenvector $\mathbf{v}_2$ corresponding to $\lambda_2 = 0.3$, solve $(A - 0.3I)\mathbf{v}_2 = 0$: $A - 0.3I = \begin{bmatrix}0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.4\end{bmatrix}$, so $\begin{bmatrix}0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.4\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}x \\ y\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0 \\ 0\end{bmatrix}$.
5. The equation $0.3x + 0.3y = 0$ implies $x = -y$, so an eigenvector is $\mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}$.
6. Verify that $\mathbf{v}_1$ and $\mathbf{v}_2$ are linearly independent: $\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix}3/7 \\ 4/7\end{bmatrix}$ is not a scalar multiple of $\mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}$, so they are linearly independent.
7. Therefore, a basis for $\mathbb{R}^2$ consisting of $\mathbf{v}_1$ and another eigenvector $\mathbf{v}_2$ of $A$ is $\left\{\begin{bmatrix}3/7 \\ 4/7\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}\right\}$.
