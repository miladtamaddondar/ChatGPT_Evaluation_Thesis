1. We want to write \(\begin{bmatrix} 6 & 9 \\ 4 & 5 \end{bmatrix} = LU\) where \(L\) is lower triangular and \(U\) is upper triangular.
2. Let \(L = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ l_{21} & 1 \end{bmatrix}\) and \(U = \begin{bmatrix} u_{11} & u_{12} \\ 0 & u_{22} \end{bmatrix}\).
3. Multiply \(L\) and \(U\): \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ l_{21} & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} u_{11} & u_{12} \\ 0 & u_{22} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} u_{11} & u_{12} \\ l_{21}u_{11} & l_{21}u_{12} + u_{22} \end{bmatrix}\).
4. Set \(u_{11} = 6\) and \(u_{12} = 9\) to match the first row of the original matrix.
5. Solve for \(l_{21}\) using \(l_{21}u_{11} = 4\), so \(l_{21} = \frac{4}{6} = \frac{2}{3}\).
6. Solve for \(u_{22}\) using \(l_{21}u_{12} + u_{22} = 5\), so \(u_{22} = 5 - l_{21}u_{12} = 5 - \frac{2}{3} \times 9 = 5 - 6 = -1\).
7. The LU factorization is \(L = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ \frac{2}{3} & 1 \end{bmatrix}\), \(U = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 9 \\ 0 & -1 \end{bmatrix}\).
