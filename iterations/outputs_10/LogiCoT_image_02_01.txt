1. To find the vector \(\mathbf{x}\) in the standard basis, use the formula \(\mathbf{x} = [\mathbf{x}]_B^1 \mathbf{b}_1 + [\mathbf{x}]_B^2 \mathbf{b}_2\), where \(\mathbf{b}_1\) and \(\mathbf{b}_2\) are the basis vectors and \([\mathbf{x}]_B = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}\).  
2. Substitute the given values: \(\mathbf{x} = 3 \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} + 2 \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}\).  
3. Multiply the scalars: \(3 \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 3 \\ 9 \end{bmatrix}\) and \(2 \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ -2 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}\).  
4. Add the results: \(\begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 3 \\ 9 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ -2 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 7 \\ 1 \\ 11 \end{bmatrix}\).  
5. Therefore, the vector \(\mathbf{x}\) in the standard basis is \(\boxed{\begin{bmatrix} 7 \\ 1 \\ 11 \end{bmatrix}}\).
