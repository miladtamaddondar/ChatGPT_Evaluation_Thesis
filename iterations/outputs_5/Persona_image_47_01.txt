1. To find the orthogonal projection of \(\mathbf{b}\) onto \(\text{Col }A\), we use \(\mathbf{p} = A(A^TA)^{-1}A^T\mathbf{b}\).
2. Compute \(A^T A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 3 & 1 \\ -2 & 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1^2+3^2+(-2)^2 & 1\cdot5+3\cdot1+(-2)\cdot4 \\ 5\cdot1+1\cdot3+4\cdot(-2) & 5^2+1^2+4^2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 14 & 4 \\ 4 & 42 \end{bmatrix}\).
3. Compute \(A^T\mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ -2 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1\cdot4 + 3\cdot(-2) + (-2)\cdot(-3) \\ 5\cdot4 + 1\cdot(-2) + 4\cdot(-3) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 - 6 + 6 \\ 20 - 2 - 12 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix}\).
4. Compute \((A^T A)^{-1}\) where \(A^T A = \begin{bmatrix} 14 & 4 \\ 4 & 42 \end{bmatrix}\), so \(\det = 14 \cdot 42 - 4 \cdot 4 = 588 - 16 = 572\), and \((A^T A)^{-1} = \frac{1}{572} \begin{bmatrix} 42 & -4 \\ -4 & 14 \end{bmatrix}\).
5. Compute \((A^T A)^{-1}A^T\mathbf{b} = \frac{1}{572} \begin{bmatrix} 42 & -4 \\ -4 & 14 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix} = \frac{1}{572} \begin{bmatrix} 42\cdot4 + (-4)\cdot6 \\ -4\cdot4 + 14\cdot6 \end{bmatrix} = \frac{1}{572} \begin{bmatrix} 168 - 24 \\ -16 + 84 \end{bmatrix} = \frac{1}{572} \begin{bmatrix} 144 \\ 68 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{36}{143} \\ \frac{17}{143} \end{bmatrix}\).
6. The least-squares solution is \(\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{36}{143} \\ \frac{17}{143} \end{bmatrix}\).
7. The orthogonal projection is \(\mathbf{p} = A\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 3 & 1 \\ -2 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \frac{36}{143} \\ \frac{17}{143} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1\cdot\frac{36}{143} + 5\cdot\frac{17}{143} \\ 3\cdot\frac{36}{143} + 1\cdot\frac{17}{143} \\ -2\cdot\frac{36}{143} + 4\cdot\frac{17}{143} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{36+85}{143} \\ \frac{108+17}{143} \\ \frac{-72+68}{143} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{121}{143} \\ \frac{125}{143} \\ \frac{-4}{143} \end{bmatrix}\).
8. The orthogonal projection of \(\mathbf{b}\) onto \(\text{Col }A\) is \(\boxed{\begin{bmatrix} \frac{121}{143} \\ \frac{125}{143} \\ \frac{-4}{143} \end{bmatrix}}\) and the least-squares solution is \(\boxed{\begin{bmatrix} \frac{36}{143} \\ \frac{17}{143} \end{bmatrix}}\).
