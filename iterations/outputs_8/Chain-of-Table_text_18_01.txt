1. To diagonalize $A = \begin{bmatrix}0 & 1 \\ -3 & 4\end{bmatrix}$, first find its eigenvalues by solving $\det(A - \lambda I) = 0$: $\det\begin{bmatrix}0-\lambda & 1 \\ -3 & 4-\lambda\end{bmatrix} = (0-\lambda)(4-\lambda) - (-3)(1) = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 3 = 0$.
2. Factor and solve: $(\lambda-1)(\lambda-3) = 0$, so the eigenvalues are $\lambda_1 = 1$ and $\lambda_2 = 3$.
3. Find the eigenvector for $\lambda_1 = 1$: Solve $(A - I)\mathbf{v} = 0$, so $A-I = \begin{bmatrix}-1 & 1 \\ -3 & 3\end{bmatrix}$. Row reduce: Subtract $-3$ times row 1 from row 2 to get $\begin{bmatrix}-1 & 1 \\ 0 & 0\end{bmatrix}$.
4. The solution is $-v_1 + v_2 = 0 \implies v_1 = v_2$, so an eigenvector is $\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 1\end{bmatrix}$.
5. Find the eigenvector for $\lambda_2 = 3$: Solve $(A - 3I)\mathbf{v} = 0$, so $A-3I = \begin{bmatrix}-3 & 1 \\ -3 & 1\end{bmatrix}$. Row reduce: Subtract row 1 from row 2 to get $\begin{bmatrix}-3 & 1 \\ 0 & 0\end{bmatrix}$.
6. The solution is $-3v_1 + v_2 = 0 \implies v_2 = 3v_1$, so an eigenvector is $\mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 3\end{bmatrix}$.
7. Therefore, a basis $\mathcal{B} = \left\{\begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 1\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 3\end{bmatrix}\right\}$ diagonalizes $A$, and $[T]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 \\ 0 & 3\end{bmatrix}$.
