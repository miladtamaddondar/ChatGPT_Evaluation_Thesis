1. Find the eigenvalues of $A$ by solving $\det(A - \lambda I) = 0$: $\det\left(\begin{bmatrix}0-\lambda & 1 \\ -3 & 4-\lambda\end{bmatrix}\right) = (0-\lambda)(4-\lambda) - (-3)(1) = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 3 = 0$.
2. Factor and solve the characteristic equation: $(\lambda-1)(\lambda-3) = 0 \implies \lambda_1 = 1, \lambda_2 = 3$.
3. Find the eigenvector for $\lambda_1 = 1$: Solve $(A - I)\mathbf{v} = 0$, i.e., $\begin{bmatrix}-1 & 1 \\ -3 & 3\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}v_1 \\ v_2\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0 \\ 0\end{bmatrix}$.
4. Row reduce: $R_2 \to R_2 - 3R_1$: $\begin{bmatrix}-1 & 1 \\ 0 & 0\end{bmatrix}$.
5. The solution is $-v_1 + v_2 = 0 \implies v_2 = v_1$, so an eigenvector is $\begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 1\end{bmatrix}$.
6. Find the eigenvector for $\lambda_2 = 3$: Solve $(A - 3I)\mathbf{v} = 0$, i.e., $\begin{bmatrix}-3 & 1 \\ -3 & 1\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}v_1 \\ v_2\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0 \\ 0\end{bmatrix}$.
7. Row reduce: $R_2 \to R_2 - R_1$: $\begin{bmatrix}-3 & 1 \\ 0 & 0\end{bmatrix}$.
8. The solution is $-3v_1 + v_2 = 0 \implies v_2 = 3v_1$, so an eigenvector is $\begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 3\end{bmatrix}$.
9. Therefore, a basis $\mathcal{B} = \left\{\begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 1\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 3\end{bmatrix}\right\}$ diagonalizes $A$, and $[T]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 \\ 0 & 3\end{bmatrix}$.
