1. To write \( A \) in the form \( A = P C P^{-1} \) with \( C = \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix} \), we need to find the eigenvalues of \( A \) since \( C \) must have complex eigenvalues of the form \( a \pm bi \).  
2. The characteristic polynomial of \( A \) is \( \det(A - \lambda I) = \det\begin{pmatrix} 1-\lambda & -2 \\ 1 & 3-\lambda \end{pmatrix} \).  
3. Expanding the determinant: \( (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) - (-2)(1) = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 \).  
4. Expanding further: \( (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = 3 - \lambda - 3\lambda + \lambda^2 + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 \).  
5. The characteristic equation is \( \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0 \).  
6. Solving for \( \lambda \): \( \lambda = \frac{4 \pm \sqrt{16-20}}{2} = \frac{4 \pm \sqrt{-4}}{2} = \frac{4 \pm 2i}{2} = 2 \pm i \).  
7. Thus, \( C \) must have eigenvalues \( 2 \pm i \), so \( a = 2, b = 1 \), thus \( C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \).  
8. To find \( P \), find an eigenvector for \( \lambda = 2+i \): \( (A - (2+i)I)\vec{v} = 0 \).  
9. \( A - (2+i)I = \begin{pmatrix} 1-(2+i) & -2 \\ 1 & 3-(2+i) \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix} \).  
10. Let \( \vec{v} = \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix} \), then \( (-1-i)x - 2y = 0 \) and \( x + (1-i)y = 0 \).  
11. From the second equation: \( x = -(1-i)y \).  
12. Substitute into the first: \( (-1-i)(-(1-i)y) - 2y = 0 \).  
13. Compute \( (-1-i)(-(1-i)) = (1+i)(1-i) = 1-i^2 = 1+1 = 2 \), so \( 2y - 2y = 0 \), which is always true for any \( y \neq 0 \).  
14. Thus, an eigenvector for \( 2+i \) is \( \vec{v}_1 = \begin{pmatrix} -(1-i) \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \).  
15. The eigenvector for \( 2-i \) is the complex conjugate: \( \vec{v}_2 = \begin{pmatrix} -(1+i) \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \).  
16. Form \( P \) with real columns by taking real and imaginary parts: let \( \vec{u}_1 = \operatorname{Re}(\vec{v}_1) = \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \), \( \vec{u}_2 = \operatorname{Im}(\vec{v}_1) = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix} \).  
17. Thus, \( P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \).  
18. Verify invertibility: \( \det(P) = (-1)(0) - (1)(1) = -1 \neq 0 \), so \( P \) is invertible.  
19. The answer: \( P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \), \( C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \), and \( A = P C P^{-1} \).
