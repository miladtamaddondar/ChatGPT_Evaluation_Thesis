1. Find the null space of \( A = \begin{bmatrix}3 & -6 \\ -1 & 2\end{bmatrix} \) by solving \( A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0} \): \( 3x_1 - 6x_2 = 0 \) and \( -x_1 + 2x_2 = 0 \).  
2. Solve \( 3x_1 - 6x_2 = 0 \) to get \( x_1 = 2x_2 \); verify with the second equation: \( -x_1 + 2x_2 = -2x_2 + 2x_2 = 0 \), so both equations are satisfied.  
3. The null space is all multiples of \( \begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 1\end{bmatrix} \); choose two different nonzero vectors in the null space, e.g., \( \begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 1\end{bmatrix} \) and \( \begin{bmatrix}4 \\ 2\end{bmatrix} \).  
4. Construct \( B = \begin{bmatrix}2 & 4 \\ 1 & 2\end{bmatrix} \) using these vectors as columns.  
5. Verify \( AB = \begin{bmatrix}3 & -6 \\ -1 & 2\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}2 & 4 \\ 1 & 2\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}3(2) + (-6)(1) & 3(4) + (-6)(2) \\ -1(2) + 2(1) & -1(4) + 2(2)\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}6 - 6 & 12 - 12 \\ -2 + 2 & -4 + 4\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0\end{bmatrix} \), confirming correctness.  
6. Final answer: \( B = \begin{bmatrix}2 & 4 \\ 1 & 2\end{bmatrix} \) is a \( 2 \times 2 \) matrix with two different nonzero columns such that \( AB \) is the zero matrix.
