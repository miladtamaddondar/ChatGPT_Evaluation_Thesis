1. To find a basis for $\mathbb{R}^2$ consisting of $\mathbf{v}_1$ and another eigenvector $\mathbf{v}_2$ of $A$, first find the eigenvalues of $A$ by solving $\det(A - \lambda I) = 0$: $\det\left(\begin{bmatrix}0.6 - \lambda & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.7 - \lambda\end{bmatrix}\right) = (0.6-\lambda)(0.7-\lambda) - (0.3)(0.4) = 0$.
2. Expand and simplify: $(0.6-\lambda)(0.7-\lambda) - 0.12 = 0 \implies 0.42 - 0.6\lambda - 0.7\lambda + \lambda^2 - 0.12 = 0 \implies \lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3 = 0$.
3. Solve the quadratic equation: $\lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3 = 0 \implies \lambda = \frac{1.3 \pm \sqrt{1.69 - 1.2}}{2} = \frac{1.3 \pm \sqrt{0.49}}{2} = \frac{1.3 \pm 0.7}{2}$.
4. Thus, the eigenvalues are $\lambda_1 = 1$ and $\lambda_2 = 0.3$.
5. Since $\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix}3/7 \\ 4/7\end{bmatrix}$ is given as an eigenvector, check which eigenvalue it corresponds to by computing $A\mathbf{v}_1$: $A\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix}0.6 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.7\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}3/7 \\ 4/7\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0.6 \cdot 3/7 + 0.3 \cdot 4/7 \\ 0.4 \cdot 3/7 + 0.7 \cdot 4/7\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}(1.8 + 1.2)/7 \\ (1.2 + 2.8)/7\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}3/7 \\ 4/7\end{bmatrix}$.
6. Therefore, $\mathbf{v}_1$ is an eigenvector for $\lambda_1 = 1$.
7. To find another eigenvector $\mathbf{v}_2$ for $\lambda_2 = 0.3$, solve $(A - 0.3I)\mathbf{v}_2 = 0$: $A - 0.3I = \begin{bmatrix}0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.4\end{bmatrix}$.
8. Write the augmented matrix for the system: $\left[\begin{array}{cc|c}0.3 & 0.3 & 0 \\ 0.4 & 0.4 & 0\end{array}\right]$.
9. Row 2 minus $\frac{4}{3}$ times Row 1: $\left[\begin{array}{cc|c}0.3 & 0.3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0\end{array}\right]$.
10. The first equation is $0.3x_1 + 0.3x_2 = 0 \implies x_1 + x_2 = 0$, so $x_2 = -x_1$.
11. Thus, an eigenvector for $\lambda_2 = 0.3$ is $\mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}$.
12. Therefore, a basis for $\mathbb{R}^2$ consisting of $\mathbf{v}_1$ and another eigenvector $\mathbf{v}_2$ of $A$ is $\left\{\begin{bmatrix}3/7 \\ 4/7\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}\right\}$.
