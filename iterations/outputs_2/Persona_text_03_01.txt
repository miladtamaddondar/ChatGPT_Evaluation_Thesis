1. To perform LU factorization, we want to write the matrix \( A = \begin{bmatrix}6 & 9 \\ 4 & 5\end{bmatrix} \) as \( LU \), where \( L \) is lower triangular and \( U \) is upper triangular.  
2. Let \( L = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 \\ l_{21} & 1\end{bmatrix} \) and \( U = \begin{bmatrix}u_{11} & u_{12} \\ 0 & u_{22}\end{bmatrix} \), so \( LU = \begin{bmatrix}u_{11} & u_{12} \\ l_{21}u_{11} & l_{21}u_{12} + u_{22}\end{bmatrix} \).  
3. Equate \( LU \) to \( A \): \( u_{11} = 6 \), \( u_{12} = 9 \), \( l_{21}u_{11} = 4 \), \( l_{21}u_{12} + u_{22} = 5 \).  
4. Solve for \( l_{21} \): \( l_{21} = \frac{4}{u_{11}} = \frac{4}{6} = \frac{2}{3} \).  
5. Solve for \( u_{22} \): \( l_{21}u_{12} + u_{22} = 5 \implies \frac{2}{3} \cdot 9 + u_{22} = 5 \implies 6 + u_{22} = 5 \implies u_{22} = -1 \).  
6. Therefore, \( L = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 \\ \frac{2}{3} & 1\end{bmatrix} \) and \( U = \begin{bmatrix}6 & 9 \\ 0 & -1\end{bmatrix} \).  
7. Final answer: The LU factorization of \( \begin{bmatrix}6 & 9 \\ 4 & 5\end{bmatrix} \) is \( \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 \\ \frac{2}{3} & 1\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}6 & 9 \\ 0 & -1\end{bmatrix} \).
