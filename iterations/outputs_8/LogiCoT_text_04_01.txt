1. Compute \( AD \) by multiplying matrix \( A \) with matrix \( D \): \( AD = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 1 & 4 & 5\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}2 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 5\end{bmatrix} \); since \( D \) is diagonal, each column of \( A \) is scaled by the corresponding diagonal entry of \( D \).
2. Multiply each column of \( A \) by the corresponding diagonal entry of \( D \): first column by 2, second by 3, third by 5; \( AD = \begin{bmatrix}1 \times 2 & 1 \times 3 & 1 \times 5 \\ 1 \times 2 & 2 \times 3 & 3 \times 5 \\ 1 \times 2 & 4 \times 3 & 5 \times 5\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}2 & 3 & 5 \\ 2 & 6 & 15 \\ 2 & 12 & 25\end{bmatrix} \); this is correct by the definition of matrix multiplication with a diagonal matrix on the right.
3. Compute \( DA \) by multiplying matrix \( D \) with matrix \( A \): \( DA = \begin{bmatrix}2 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 5\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}1 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 1 & 4 & 5\end{bmatrix} \); since \( D \) is diagonal, each row of \( A \) is scaled by the corresponding diagonal entry of \( D \).
4. Multiply each row of \( A \) by the corresponding diagonal entry of \( D \): first row by 2, second by 3, third by 5; \( DA = \begin{bmatrix}2 \times 1 & 2 \times 1 & 2 \times 1 \\ 3 \times 1 & 3 \times 2 & 3 \times 3 \\ 5 \times 1 & 5 \times 4 & 5 \times 5\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}2 & 2 & 2 \\ 3 & 6 & 9 \\ 5 & 20 & 25\end{bmatrix} \); this is correct by the definition of matrix multiplication with a diagonal matrix on the left.
5. Final answers: \( AD = \begin{bmatrix}2 & 3 & 5 \\ 2 & 6 & 15 \\ 2 & 12 & 25\end{bmatrix} \), \( DA = \begin{bmatrix}2 & 2 & 2 \\ 3 & 6 & 9 \\ 5 & 20 & 25\end{bmatrix} \).
