To find the change-of-coordinates matrix $P_{\mathcal{B} \to \mathcal{E}}$, write each vector in $\mathcal{B}$ in terms of the standard basis $\mathcal{E} = \{1, t, t^2\}$: $1 - 3t^2 = 1 \cdot 1 + 0 \cdot t + (-3) \cdot t^2$, $2 + t - 5t^2 = 2 \cdot 1 + 1 \cdot t + (-5) \cdot t^2$, $1 + 2t = 1 \cdot 1 + 2 \cdot t + 0 \cdot t^2$.
2. The coordinate vectors are $\begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 0 \\ -3\end{bmatrix}$, $\begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 1 \\ -5\end{bmatrix}$, and $\begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 2 \\ 0\end{bmatrix}$, so $P_{\mathcal{B} \to \mathcal{E}} = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 2 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 \\ -3 & -5 & 0\end{bmatrix}$.
3. To write $t^2$ as a linear combination of $\mathcal{B}$, set $t^2 = a(1 - 3t^2) + b(2 + t - 5t^2) + c(1 + 2t)$ and expand: $t^2 = (a + 2b + c) + (b + 2c)t + (-3a - 5b)t^2$.
4. Equate coefficients with $t^2 = 0 \cdot 1 + 0 \cdot t + 1 \cdot t^2$ to get the system: $a + 2b + c = 0$, $b + 2c = 0$, $-3a - 5b = 1$.
5. Solve $b + 2c = 0$ to get $b = -2c$.
6. Substitute $b = -2c$ into $a + 2b + c = 0$ to get $a + 2(-2c) + c = 0 \implies a - 4c + c = 0 \implies a = 3c$.
7. Substitute $a = 3c$ and $b = -2c$ into $-3a - 5b = 1$ to get $-3(3c) - 5(-2c) = 1 \implies -9c + 10c = 1 \implies c = 1$.
8. Substitute $c = 1$ into $a = 3c$ and $b = -2c$ to get $a = 3$, $b = -2$.
9. Therefore, $t^2 = 3(1 - 3t^2) - 2(2 + t - 5t^2) + 1(1 + 2t)$.
10. The change-of-coordinates matrix is $\begin{bmatrix}1 & 2 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 \\ -3 & -5 & 0\end{bmatrix}$ and $t^2 = 3(1 - 3t^2) - 2(2 + t - 5t^2) + (1 + 2t)$.
