1. Form the normal equations $A^T A \mathbf{x} = A^T \mathbf{b}$; compute $A^T A = \begin{bmatrix} 4 & 2 & 2 \\ 2 & 2 & 0 \\ 2 & 0 & 2 \end{bmatrix}$ and $A^T \mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 14 \\ 4 \\ 10 \end{bmatrix}$, so the augmented matrix is $\left[ \begin{array}{ccc|c} 4 & 2 & 2 & 14 \\ 2 & 2 & 0 & 4 \\ 2 & 0 & 2 & 10 \end{array} \right]$.
2. Divide row 1 by 4 to get a leading 1: $R_1 \to \frac{1}{4}R_1$, resulting in $\left[ \begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0.5 & 0.5 & 3.5 \\ 2 & 2 & 0 & 4 \\ 2 & 0 & 2 & 10 \end{array} \right]$.
3. Subtract $2 \times$ row 1 from row 2: $R_2 \to R_2 - 2R_1$, resulting in $\left[ \begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0.5 & 0.5 & 3.5 \\ 0 & 1 & -1 & -3 \\ 2 & 0 & 2 & 10 \end{array} \right]$.
4. Subtract $2 \times$ row 1 from row 3: $R_3 \to R_3 - 2R_1$, resulting in $\left[ \begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0.5 & 0.5 & 3.5 \\ 0 & 1 & -1 & -3 \\ 0 & -1 & 1 & 3 \end{array} \right]$.
5. Add row 2 to row 3: $R_3 \to R_3 + R_2$, resulting in $\left[ \begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0.5 & 0.5 & 3.5 \\ 0 & 1 & -1 & -3 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{array} \right]$.
6. Express the solution in terms of the free variable $x_3 = t$: $x_2 - x_3 = -3 \implies x_2 = -3 + t$, $x_1 + 0.5x_2 + 0.5x_3 = 3.5 \implies x_1 + 0.5(-3 + t) + 0.5t = 3.5 \implies x_1 - 1.5 + t = 3.5 \implies x_1 = 5 - t$.
7. The least-squares solutions are $\boxed{\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 5 - t \\ -3 + t \\ t \end{bmatrix}, \quad t \in \mathbb{R}}$.
