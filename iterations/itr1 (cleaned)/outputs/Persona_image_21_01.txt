1. We are given \( T(\mathbf{x}) = A\mathbf{x} \) with \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -2 \\ -2 & 1 & 6 \\ 3 & -2 & -5 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( \mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 7 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} \), and we need to solve \( A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{b} \) for \( \mathbf{x} \).  
2. Write the augmented matrix: \( \left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & -2 & -1 \\ -2 & 1 & 6 & 7 \\ 3 & -2 & -5 & -3 \end{array}\right] \).  
3. Use row operations: Add 2 times row 1 to row 2: \( -2+2(1)=0, 1+2(0)=1, 6+2(-2)=2, 7+2(-1)=5 \), so row 2 becomes \( [0\ 1\ 2\ |\ 5] \).  
4. Subtract 3 times row 1 from row 3: \( 3-3(1)=0, -2-3(0)=-2, -5-3(-2)=1, -3-3(-1)=0 \), so row 3 becomes \( [0\ -2\ 1\ |\ 0] \).  
5. The matrix is now \( \left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & -2 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & 5 \\ 0 & -2 & 1 & 0 \end{array}\right] \).  
6. Add 2 times row 2 to row 3: \( -2+2(1)=0, 1+2(2)=5, 0+2(5)=10 \), so row 3 becomes \( [0\ 0\ 5\ |\ 10] \).  
7. Divide row 3 by 5: \( [0\ 0\ 1\ |\ 2] \).  
8. The matrix is now \( \left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & -2 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 2 \end{array}\right] \).  
9. Substitute \( x_3 = 2 \) into row 2: \( x_2 + 2x_3 = 5 \implies x_2 + 4 = 5 \implies x_2 = 1 \).  
10. Substitute \( x_2 = 1 \) and \( x_3 = 2 \) into row 1: \( x_1 - 2x_3 = -1 \implies x_1 - 4 = -1 \implies x_1 = 3 \).  
11. The solution is \( \mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \).  
12. Since the system has a unique solution (no free variables and all pivots present), \( \mathbf{x} \) is unique.
