1. To construct a \(4 \times 3\) matrix with rank 1, recall that a matrix has rank 1 if all its rows are scalar multiples of a single row (or all columns are scalar multiples of a single column).  
2. Choose a nonzero \(4 \times 1\) column vector, for example, \(\mathbf{u} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix}\).  
3. Choose a nonzero \(1 \times 3\) row vector, for example, \(\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 6 & 7 \end{bmatrix}\).  
4. Form the matrix \(A = \mathbf{u} \mathbf{v}\), which is the outer product: \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 6 & 7 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 6 & 7 \\ 10 & 12 & 14 \\ 15 & 18 & 21 \\ 20 & 24 & 28 \end{bmatrix}\).  
5. Verify correctness: All rows of \(A\) are scalar multiples of the first row, so the rank is 1 by definition.  
6. Final answer: A \(4 \times 3\) matrix with rank 1 is \(\begin{bmatrix} 5 & 6 & 7 \\ 10 & 12 & 14 \\ 15 & 18 & 21 \\ 20 & 24 & 28 \end{bmatrix}\).
