1. Find the null space of \( A = \begin{bmatrix}3 & -6 \\ -1 & 2\end{bmatrix} \) by solving \( A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0} \): \( 3x_1 - 6x_2 = 0 \) and \( -x_1 + 2x_2 = 0 \).  
2. Solve the first equation: \( 3x_1 = 6x_2 \implies x_1 = 2x_2 \); verify with the second: \( -x_1 + 2x_2 = -2x_2 + 2x_2 = 0 \), so both equations are satisfied.  
3. The null space consists of all vectors \( \mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix}2t \\ t\end{bmatrix} \) for \( t \in \mathbb{R} \), so any nonzero scalar multiple of \( \begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 1\end{bmatrix} \) is in the null space.  
4. To construct \( B \) with two different nonzero columns, choose two distinct nonzero values for \( t \), say \( t = 1 \) and \( t = -1 \), giving columns \( \begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 1\end{bmatrix} \) and \( \begin{bmatrix}-2 \\ -1\end{bmatrix} \).  
5. Form \( B = \begin{bmatrix}2 & -2 \\ 1 & -1\end{bmatrix} \) and verify \( AB = \begin{bmatrix}3 & -6 \\ -1 & 2\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}2 & -2 \\ 1 & -1\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}3 \cdot 2 + (-6) \cdot 1 & 3 \cdot (-2) + (-6) \cdot (-1) \\ -1 \cdot 2 + 2 \cdot 1 & -1 \cdot (-2) + 2 \cdot (-1)\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}6 - 6 & -6 + 6 \\ -2 + 2 & 2 - 2\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0\end{bmatrix} \), confirming \( AB \) is the zero matrix.  
6. Therefore, one such \( B \) is \( \boxed{\begin{bmatrix}2 & -2 \\ 1 & -1\end{bmatrix}} \), and it uses two different nonzero columns from the null space of \( A \).
