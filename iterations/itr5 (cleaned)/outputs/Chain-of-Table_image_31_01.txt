1. The set \( W \) consists of all vectors of the form \( \begin{bmatrix} 3a+b \\ 4 \\ a-5b \end{bmatrix} \) where \( a, b \in \mathbb{R} \). Let's check if \( W \) is a vector space by testing closure under addition: take \( \mathbf{u} = \begin{bmatrix} 3a_1+b_1 \\ 4 \\ a_1-5b_1 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( \mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 3a_2+b_2 \\ 4 \\ a_2-5b_2 \end{bmatrix} \).
2. Add \( \mathbf{u} + \mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} (3a_1+b_1) + (3a_2+b_2) \\ 4+4 \\ (a_1-5b_1)+(a_2-5b_2) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3(a_1+a_2)+(b_1+b_2) \\ 8 \\ (a_1+a_2)-5(b_1+b_2) \end{bmatrix} \).
3. The second entry is always 8, not 4, so \( \mathbf{u}+\mathbf{v} \notin W \) unless \( 8=4 \), which is false. Thus, \( W \) is not closed under addition.
4. Final answer: \( W \) is not a vector space because it is not closed under addition; for example, \( \begin{bmatrix} 3a+b \\ 4 \\ a-5b \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 3c+d \\ 4 \\ c-5d \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3(a+c)+(b+d) \\ 8 \\ (a+c)-5(b+d) \end{bmatrix} \notin W \) since the second entry is 8, not 4.
