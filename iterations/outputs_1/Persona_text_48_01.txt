1. To find a nonzero vector $\mathbf{v}$ in $\mathbb{R}^3$ that is orthogonal to both $\mathbf{u}_1$ and $\mathbf{u}_2$, we can use the cross product since the cross product of two vectors in $\mathbb{R}^3$ is orthogonal to both.  
2. Compute $\mathbf{v} = \mathbf{u}_1 \times \mathbf{u}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix} \times \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ -1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}$.  
3. Use the formula for the cross product: $\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} (1)(2) - (-2)(-1) \\ (-2)(5) - (1)(2) \\ (1)(-1) - (1)(5) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 - 2 \\ -10 - 2 \\ -1 - 5 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ -12 \\ -6 \end{bmatrix}$.  
4. Therefore, a nonzero vector $\mathbf{v}$ in $\mathbb{R}^3$ that is orthogonal to both $\mathbf{u}_1$ and $\mathbf{u}_2$ is $\boxed{\begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ -12 \\ -6 \end{bmatrix}}$.
