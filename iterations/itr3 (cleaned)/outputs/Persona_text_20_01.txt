2. To find the eigenvalues of $A$, solve $\det(A - \lambda I) = 0$: $\det\left(\begin{bmatrix} 0.6-\lambda & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.7-\lambda \end{bmatrix}\right) = (0.6-\lambda)(0.7-\lambda) - (0.3)(0.4) = 0$.  
3. Expand the determinant: $(0.6-\lambda)(0.7-\lambda) - 0.12 = 0 \implies 0.42 - 0.6\lambda - 0.7\lambda + \lambda^2 - 0.12 = 0 \implies \lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3 = 0$.  
4. Solve the quadratic equation: $\lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3 = 0 \implies \lambda = \frac{1.3 \pm \sqrt{1.69 - 1.2}}{2} = \frac{1.3 \pm \sqrt{0.49}}{2} = \frac{1.3 \pm 0.7}{2}$, so $\lambda_1 = 1$, $\lambda_2 = 0.3$.  
5. Since $\mathbf{v}_1$ is already an eigenvector, it must correspond to $\lambda_1 = 1$; now, find an eigenvector $\mathbf{v}_2$ for $\lambda_2 = 0.3$ by solving $(A - 0.3I)\mathbf{v}_2 = 0$.  
6. Compute $A - 0.3I = \begin{bmatrix} 0.6-0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.7-0.3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.4 \end{bmatrix}$.  
7. Set up the equation: $\begin{bmatrix} 0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}$, which gives $0.3x + 0.3y = 0$ and $0.4x + 0.4y = 0$.  
8. Both equations reduce to $x + y = 0$, so $y = -x$; thus, an eigenvector is $\mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix}$.  
9. Therefore, a basis for $\mathbb{R}^2$ consisting of $\mathbf{v}_1$ and another eigenvector $\mathbf{v}_2$ of $A$ is $\left\{ \begin{bmatrix} \frac{3}{7} \\ \frac{4}{7} \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} \right\}$.
