1. To find $R$, use the formula $R = Q^T A$ since $Q$ has orthonormal columns.
2. Compute $Q^T$: $Q^T = \begin{bmatrix} 5/6 & 1/6 & -3/6 & 1/6 \\ -1/6 & 5/6 & 1/6 & 3/6 \end{bmatrix}$.
3. Multiply $Q^T$ by $A$: $R = Q^T A = \begin{bmatrix} 5/6 & 1/6 & -3/6 & 1/6 \\ -1/6 & 5/6 & 1/6 & 3/6 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 9 \\ 1 & 7 \\ -3 & -5 \\ 1 & 5 \end{bmatrix}$.
4. Calculate the $(1,1)$ entry: $R_{11} = (5/6) \cdot 5 + (1/6) \cdot 1 + (-3/6) \cdot (-3) + (1/6) \cdot 1 = (25 + 1 + 9 + 1)/6 = 36/6 = 6$.
5. Calculate the $(1,2)$ entry: $R_{12} = (5/6) \cdot 9 + (1/6) \cdot 7 + (-3/6) \cdot (-5) + (1/6) \cdot 5 = (45 + 7 + 15 + 5)/6 = 72/6 = 12$.
6. Calculate the $(2,1)$ entry: $R_{21} = (-1/6) \cdot 5 + (5/6) \cdot 1 + (1/6) \cdot (-3) + (3/6) \cdot 1 = (-5 + 5 - 3 + 3)/6 = 0/6 = 0$.
7. Calculate the $(2,2)$ entry: $R_{22} = (-1/6) \cdot 9 + (5/6) \cdot 7 + (1/6) \cdot (-5) + (3/6) \cdot 5 = (-9 + 35 - 5 + 15)/6 = 36/6 = 6$.
8. Thus, $R = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 12 \\ 0 & 6 \end{bmatrix}$.
9. Check $A = QR$: $QR = \begin{bmatrix} 5/6 & -1/6 \\ 1/6 & 5/6 \\ -3/6 & 1/6 \\ 1/6 & 3/6 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 12 \\ 0 & 6 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 9 \\ 1 & 7 \\ -3 & -5 \\ 1 & 5 \end{bmatrix} = A$.
10. The upper triangular matrix $R$ such that $A = QR$ is $R = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 12 \\ 0 & 6 \end{bmatrix}$.
