1. To find a basis for the eigenspace for \(\lambda = 4\), solve \((A - 4I)\vec{x} = 0\).
2. Compute \(A - 4I = \begin{bmatrix}3-4 & 0 & 2 & 0 \\ 1 & 3-4 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1-4 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 4-4\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}-1 & 0 & 2 & 0 \\ 1 & -1 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0\end{bmatrix}\).
3. Let \(\vec{x} = \begin{bmatrix}x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \\ x_4\end{bmatrix}\), so the system is \(\begin{cases}-x_1 + 2x_3 = 0 \\ x_1 - x_2 + x_3 = 0 \\ x_2 - 3x_3 = 0 \\ 0 = 0\end{cases}\).
4. From the third equation, \(x_2 = 3x_3\).
5. Substitute \(x_2 = 3x_3\) into the second equation: \(x_1 - 3x_3 + x_3 = x_1 - 2x_3 = 0\), so \(x_1 = 2x_3\).
6. The first equation \(-x_1 + 2x_3 = -2x_3 + 2x_3 = 0\) is satisfied for all \(x_3\).
7. The variable \(x_4\) is free, so let \(x_3 = t\) and \(x_4 = s\), where \(t, s \in \mathbb{R}\).
8. Therefore, \(\vec{x} = \begin{bmatrix}2t \\ 3t \\ t \\ s\end{bmatrix} = t\begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ 0\end{bmatrix} + s\begin{bmatrix}0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 1\end{bmatrix}\).
9. Thus, a basis for the eigenspace corresponding to \(\lambda = 4\) is \(\left\{ \begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ 0\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 1\end{bmatrix} \right\}\).
