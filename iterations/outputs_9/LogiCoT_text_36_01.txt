1. Observe that $A$ is a $4 \times 6$ matrix, so its columns live in $\mathbb{R}^4$ and its null space is a subspace of $\mathbb{R}^6$.
2. The number of pivot columns (leading 1's) in $A$ is the rank of $A$; from the matrix, the pivots are in columns 1, 3, and 4, so $\operatorname{rank}A = 3$.
3. By the Rank-Nullity Theorem, $\operatorname{rank}A + \dim(\operatorname{Nul}A) = n$ where $n$ is the number of columns, so $3 + \dim(\operatorname{Nul}A) = 6$.
4. Solving for $\dim(\operatorname{Nul}A)$ gives $\dim(\operatorname{Nul}A) = 6 - 3 = 3$.
5. The dimension of the column space is the rank, so $\dim(\operatorname{Col}A) = 3$.
6. Final answer: $\boxed{\dim(\operatorname{Nul}A) = 3,\ \dim(\operatorname{Col}A) = 3}$.
