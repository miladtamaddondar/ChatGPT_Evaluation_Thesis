2. Use row reduction to find the pivot columns: Apply Gaussian elimination to \(A\). 
3. Subtract 1 times column 1 from column 4 and 2 times column 1 from column 5: \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & -3 & 1 \\ -3 & 2 & 1 & 1 & 0 \\ 2 & -3 & 6 & 5 & 5 \end{bmatrix}\). 
4. Add 3 times row 1 to row 3 and subtract 2 times row 1 from row 4: \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & -3 & 1 \\ 0 & 2 & -8 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & -3 & 12 & 5 & 5 \end{bmatrix}\). 
5. Subtract 2 times row 2 from row 3 and add 3 times row 2 to row 4: \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & -3 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 7 & -2 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & -4 & 8 \end{bmatrix}\). 
6. Add \(\frac{4}{7}\) times row 3 to row 4: \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & -3 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 7 & -2 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & \frac{44}{7} \end{bmatrix}\). 
7. The pivot columns are columns 1, 2, 4, and 5, so the basis is given by the original vectors \(\mathbf{v}_1, \mathbf{v}_2, \mathbf{v}_4, \mathbf{v}_5\). 
8. Therefore, a basis for the space spanned by the vectors is \(\left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ -3 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \\ 2 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ -8 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \\ -6 \\ 9 \end{bmatrix} \right\}\).
