1. Find the eigenvalues of \( A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix} \) by solving \( \det(A - \lambda I) = 0 \): \( \det\begin{pmatrix} 1-\lambda & -2 \\ 1 & 3-\lambda \end{pmatrix} = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0 \).  
2. Solve the characteristic equation \( \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0 \) to get eigenvalues \( \lambda = 2 \pm i \) using the quadratic formula \( \lambda = \frac{4 \pm \sqrt{16-20}}{2} = \frac{4 \pm 2i}{2} = 2 \pm i \).  
3. Verify the eigenvalues are complex conjugates, matching the form \( \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix} \) where \( a = 2, b = 1 \).  
4. Find an eigenvector for \( \lambda = 2+i \) by solving \( (A - (2+i)I)\vec{v} = 0 \): \( A - (2+i)I = \begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix} \).  
5. Set up the system: \( (-1-i)x - 2y = 0 \) and \( x + (1-i)y = 0 \); from the second equation, \( x = -(1-i)y \).  
6. Substitute \( x \) into the first equation: \( (-1-i)(-(1-i)y) - 2y = (1-i+i-1)y - 2y = (0 - 2)y = -2y = 0 \), so any nonzero \( y \) works; choose \( y = 1 \), then \( x = -(1-i) \).  
7. The eigenvector for \( \lambda = 2+i \) is \( \vec{v}_1 = \begin{pmatrix} -(1-i) \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \); for \( \lambda = 2-i \), the eigenvector is the complex conjugate \( \vec{v}_2 = \begin{pmatrix} -(1+i) \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \).  
8. Form the matrix \( P \) using the real and imaginary parts of \( \vec{v}_1 \): \( \vec{v}_1 = \begin{pmatrix} -1+i \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} + i\begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix} \), so \( P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \).  
9. The matrix \( C \) is \( \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \) since it has eigenvalues \( 2 \pm i \) and matches the required form.  
10. Therefore, \( A = P C P^{-1} \) where \( P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \) and \( C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \).
