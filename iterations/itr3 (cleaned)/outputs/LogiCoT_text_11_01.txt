1. Write the matrix \( A = \begin{bmatrix}0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 2 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \) and recall that eigenvalues are found by solving \( \det(A - \lambda I) = 0 \). 
2. Compute \( A - \lambda I = \begin{bmatrix}0-\lambda & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 2-\lambda & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 1-\lambda \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}-\lambda & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 2-\lambda & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 1-\lambda \end{bmatrix} \). 
3. Since this is an upper triangular matrix, its determinant is the product of the diagonal entries: \( \det(A - \lambda I) = (-\lambda)(2-\lambda)(1-\lambda) \). 
4. Set the determinant equal to zero: \( (-\lambda)(2-\lambda)(1-\lambda) = 0 \). 
5. The solutions are \( \lambda = 0 \), \( \lambda = 2 \), and \( \lambda = 1 \), so the eigenvalues of \( A \) are \( 0, 1, 2 \).
