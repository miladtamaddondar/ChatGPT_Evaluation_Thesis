1. The given matrix is $A = \begin{bmatrix}1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ 3 & 9 & -1 & 5 \\ 2 & -6 & 4 & -3 \\ -4 & 12 & 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix}$ and its echelon form is $\begin{bmatrix}1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 & -7 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}$.
2. To find a basis for $\text{Col } A$, we identify the pivot columns in the echelon form, which are columns 1, 3, and 4.
3. The basis for $\text{Col } A$ consists of the corresponding columns of the original matrix $A$: $\left\{ \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 3 \\ 2 \\ -4\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}2 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 2\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}-4 \\ 5 \\ -3 \\ 7\end{bmatrix} \right\}$.
4. To find a basis for $\text{Nul } A$, we write the system $A\vec{x} = \vec{0}$ using the echelon form, which gives the equations: $x_1 - 3x_2 + 2x_3 - 4x_4 = 0$, $5x_3 - 7x_4 = 0$, and $5x_4 = 0$.
5. From $5x_4 = 0$, we get $x_4 = 0$; from $5x_3 - 7x_4 = 0$ and $x_4 = 0$, we get $x_3 = 0$; substituting $x_3 = 0$ and $x_4 = 0$ into the first equation gives $x_1 - 3x_2 = 0$ so $x_1 = 3x_2$.
6. Letting $x_2 = t$, the general solution is $\vec{x} = \begin{bmatrix}3t \\ t \\ 0 \\ 0\end{bmatrix} = t\begin{bmatrix}3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0\end{bmatrix}$, so a basis for $\text{Nul } A$ is $\left\{ \begin{bmatrix}3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0\end{bmatrix} \right\}$.
