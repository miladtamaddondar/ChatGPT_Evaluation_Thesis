1. To find a basis for the subspace spanned by the vectors, we write them as columns of a matrix: \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ -3 & 9 & -1 & 5 \\ 2 & -6 & 4 & -3 \\ -4 & 12 & 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix} \).  
2. We perform row reduction (Gaussian elimination) to find the linearly independent columns: Subtract \(-3\) times column 1 from column 2, \(-1\) times column 1 from column 3, and \(-4\) times column 1 from column 4 to get \( A' = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ -3 & 0 & 2 & -7 \\ 2 & 0 & 2 & 5 \\ -4 & 0 & 6 & 9 \end{bmatrix} \).  
3. Next, focus on columns 1, 3, and 4: Subtract 2 times column 3 from column 2 and 6 times column 3 from column 4 to further simplify, resulting in \( A'' = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ -3 & 0 & 2 & 0 \\ 2 & 0 & 2 & -7 \\ -4 & 0 & 6 & -3 \end{bmatrix} \).  
4. Now, observe that column 2 is all zeros, so it is not linearly independent; check if columns 1, 3, and 4 are linearly independent by setting \( a\mathbf{v}_1 + b\mathbf{v}_3 + c\mathbf{v}_4 = 0 \) and solving for \( a, b, c \):  
5. Set up the equation: \( a\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix} + b\begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} + c\begin{bmatrix} -4 \\ 5 \\ -3 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \).  
6. This gives the system: \( a + 2b - 4c = 0 \), \( -3a - b + 5c = 0 \), \( 2a + 4b - 3c = 0 \), \( -4a + 2b + 7c = 0 \).  
7. Solve for \( a, b, c \): From equation 1, \( a = -2b + 4c \); substitute into the other equations to check for nontrivial solutions.  
8. Substitute into equation 2: \( -3(-2b + 4c) - b + 5c = 6b - 12c - b + 5c = 5b - 7c = 0 \implies b = \frac{7}{5}c \).  
9. Substitute \( b \) and \( a \) into equation 3: \( 2(-2b + 4c) + 4b - 3c = -4b + 8c + 4b - 3c = 5c = 0 \implies c = 0 \implies b = 0 \implies a = 0 \).  
10. Since the only solution is the trivial one, columns 1, 3, and 4 are linearly independent, so a basis is \( \left\{ \mathbf{v}_1, \mathbf{v}_3, \mathbf{v}_4 \right\} \).  
11. The dimension of the subspace is 3.
