1. We are asked to find all vectors \(\mathbf{x} \in \mathbb{R}^4\) such that \(A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0}\), where \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -4 & 7 & -5 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & 3 \\ 2 & -6 & 6 & -4 \end{bmatrix}\); this is equivalent to finding the null space of \(A\).  
2. Let \(\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \\ x_4 \end{bmatrix}\), so the system is: \(\begin{cases} x_1 - 4x_2 + 7x_3 - 5x_4 = 0 \\ x_2 - 4x_3 + 3x_4 = 0 \\ 2x_1 - 6x_2 + 6x_3 - 4x_4 = 0 \end{cases}\).  
3. Substitute \(x_2\) from the second equation: \(x_2 = 4x_3 - 3x_4\), and verify by plugging into the other equations.  
4. Substitute \(x_2\) into the first equation: \(x_1 - 4(4x_3 - 3x_4) + 7x_3 - 5x_4 = 0 \implies x_1 - 16x_3 + 12x_4 + 7x_3 - 5x_4 = 0\).  
5. Simplify: \(x_1 - 9x_3 + 7x_4 = 0 \implies x_1 = 9x_3 - 7x_4\); check this by substituting back.  
6. Substitute \(x_1\) and \(x_2\) into the third equation: \(2(9x_3 - 7x_4) - 6(4x_3 - 3x_4) + 6x_3 - 4x_4 = 0\).  
7. Expand: \(18x_3 - 14x_4 - 24x_3 + 18x_4 + 6x_3 - 4x_4 = 0\).  
8. Combine like terms: \((18 - 24 + 6)x_3 + (-14 + 18 - 4)x_4 = 0 \implies 0x_3 + 0x_4 = 0\), which is always true, confirming consistency.  
9. Thus, the general solution is \(x_1 = 9x_3 - 7x_4\), \(x_2 = 4x_3 - 3x_4\), \(x_3 = x_3\), \(x_4 = x_4\), so \(\mathbf{x} = x_3 \begin{bmatrix} 9 \\ 4 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} + x_4 \begin{bmatrix} -7 \\ -3 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}\).  
10. Final answer: All \(\mathbf{x} \in \mathbb{R}^4\) mapped to zero by \(A\) are \(\mathbf{x} = t \begin{bmatrix} 9 \\ 4 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} + s \begin{bmatrix} -7 \\ -3 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}\) for \(t, s \in \mathbb{R}\).
