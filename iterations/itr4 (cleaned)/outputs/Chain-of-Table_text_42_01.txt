Set up the system $\mathbf{y} = c_1\mathbf{u}_1 + c_2\mathbf{u}_2 + c_3\mathbf{u}_3 + \mathbf{z}$, where $\mathbf{z}$ is orthogonal to $W$; equivalently, solve for $c_1, c_2, c_3$ in $\mathbf{y} = c_1\mathbf{u}_1 + c_2\mathbf{u}_2 + c_3\mathbf{u}_3$ by writing the augmented matrix: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 & 3 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 & -1 \end{array}\right]$.
2. Subtract row 1 from row 2 to eliminate $c_1$ in row 2: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 & -1 \end{array}\right]$.
3. Subtract row 1 from row 4 to eliminate $c_1$ in row 4: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 0 & -1 & 2 & -5 \end{array}\right]$.
4. Divide row 2 by 4 to make the leading coefficient 1: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 1 & \frac{1}{4} & -\frac{1}{4} \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 0 & -1 & 2 & -5 \end{array}\right]$.
5. Subtract row 2 from row 3 to eliminate $c_2$ in row 3: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 1 & \frac{1}{4} & -\frac{1}{4} \\ 0 & 0 & \frac{3}{4} & \frac{13}{4} \\ 0 & -1 & 2 & -5 \end{array}\right]$.
6. Add row 2 to row 4 to eliminate $c_2$ in row 4: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 1 & \frac{1}{4} & -\frac{1}{4} \\ 0 & 0 & \frac{3}{4} & \frac{13}{4} \\ 0 & 0 & \frac{9}{4} & -\frac{21}{4} \end{array}\right]$.
7. Multiply row 3 by $\frac{4}{3}$ to make the leading coefficient 1: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 1 & \frac{1}{4} & -\frac{1}{4} \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & \frac{13}{3} \\ 0 & 0 & \frac{9}{4} & -\frac{21}{4} \end{array}\right]$.
8. Subtract $\frac{9}{4}$ times row 3 from row 4 to eliminate $c_3$ in row 4: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 1 & \frac{1}{4} & -\frac{1}{4} \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & \frac{13}{3} \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & -14 \end{array}\right]$.
9. Back substitute: from row 4, $0 = -14$ shows the system is inconsistent, so $\mathbf{y}$ is not in $W$. To write $\mathbf{y}$ as the sum of a vector in $W$ and a vector orthogonal to $W$, project $\mathbf{y}$ onto $W$ using the Gram-Schmidt process or projection formula.
10. Let $U = [\mathbf{u}_1\ \mathbf{u}_2\ \mathbf{u}_3]$, then the projection is $\mathbf{y}_W = U(U^TU)^{-1}U^T\mathbf{y}$, and $\mathbf{y} = \mathbf{y}_W + (\mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W)$, where $\mathbf{y}_W \in W$ and $\mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W$ is orthogonal to $W$.
