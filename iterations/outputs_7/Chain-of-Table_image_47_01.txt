1. Write the normal equations for the least-squares solution: \(A^T A \vec{x} = A^T \vec{b}\), where \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 3 & 1 \\ -2 & 4 \end{bmatrix}\) and \(\vec{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ -2 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix}\).
2. Compute \(A^T A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 3 & 1 \\ -2 & 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1^2+3^2+(-2)^2 & 1\cdot5+3\cdot1+(-2)\cdot4 \\ 5\cdot1+1\cdot3+4\cdot(-2) & 5^2+1^2+4^2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 14 & 4 \\ 4 & 42 \end{bmatrix}\).
3. Compute \(A^T \vec{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ -2 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1\cdot4+3\cdot(-2)+(-2)\cdot(-3) \\ 5\cdot4+1\cdot(-2)+4\cdot(-3) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4-6+6 \\ 20-2-12 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix}\).
4. Form the augmented matrix for the normal equations: \(\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 14 & 4 & 4 \\ 4 & 42 & 6 \end{array}\right]\).
5. Row 1 divided by 14: \(\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & \frac{2}{7} & \frac{2}{7} \\ 4 & 42 & 6 \end{array}\right]\).
6. Row 2 minus 4 times Row 1: \(\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & \frac{2}{7} & \frac{2}{7} \\ 0 & \frac{290}{7} & \frac{34}{7} \end{array}\right]\).
7. Row 2 divided by \(\frac{290}{7}\): \(\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & \frac{2}{7} & \frac{2}{7} \\ 0 & 1 & \frac{34}{290} \end{array}\right]\).
8. Row 1 minus \(\frac{2}{7}\) times Row 2: \(\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & 0 & \frac{2}{7} - \frac{2}{7} \cdot \frac{34}{290} \\ 0 & 1 & \frac{17}{145} \end{array}\right]\).
9. Simplify Row 1: \(\frac{2}{7} - \frac{2}{7} \cdot \frac{34}{290} = \frac{2}{7} - \frac{68}{2030} = \frac{580-68}{2030} = \frac{512}{2030} = \frac{256}{1015}\), so the solution is \(\vec{x} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{256}{1015} \\ \frac{17}{145} \end{bmatrix}\).
10. The least-squares solution is \(\boxed{\vec{x} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{256}{1015} \\ \frac{17}{145} \end{bmatrix}}\).
11. The orthogonal projection of \(\vec{b}\) onto \(\operatorname{Col} A\) is \(A\vec{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 3 & 1 \\ -2 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \frac{256}{1015} \\ \frac{17}{145} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \cdot \frac{256}{1015} + 5 \cdot \frac{17}{145} \\ 3 \cdot \frac{256}{1015} + 1 \cdot \frac{17}{145} \\ -2 \cdot \frac{256}{1015} + 4 \cdot \frac{17}{145} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{256}{1015} + \frac{85}{145} \\ \frac{768}{1015} + \frac{17}{145} \\ -\frac{512}{1015} + \frac{68}{145} \end{bmatrix}\).
12. Simplify each entry: \(\frac{85}{145} = \frac{595}{1015}\), so first entry is \(\frac{256+595}{1015} = \frac{851}{1015}\); \(\frac{17}{145} = \frac{119}{1015}\), so second entry is \(\frac{768+119}{1015} = \frac{887}{1015}\); \(\frac{68}{145} = \frac{476}{1015}\), so third entry is \(\frac{-512+476}{1015} = \frac{-36}{1015}\).
13. The orthogonal projection is \(\boxed{\operatorname{proj}_{\operatorname{Col} A} \vec{b} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{851}{1015} \\ \frac{887}{1015} \\ \frac{-36}{1015} \end{bmatrix}}\).
