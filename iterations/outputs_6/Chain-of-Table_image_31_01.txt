1. Write the general vector in \( W \) as \( \begin{bmatrix} 3a + b \\ 4 \\ a - 5b \end{bmatrix} \), where \( a, b \in \mathbb{R} \).
2. Express the vector as a linear combination: \( \begin{bmatrix} 3a + b \\ 4 \\ a - 5b \end{bmatrix} = a\begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} + b\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ -5 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 4 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \).
3. Notice the constant \( 4 \) in the second entry means every vector in \( W \) has a \( 4 \) in the second coordinate, so \( W \) does not contain the zero vector \( \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \).
4. Example: The zero vector is not in \( W \) because the second entry is always \( 4 \), so \( W \) is not a vector space.
