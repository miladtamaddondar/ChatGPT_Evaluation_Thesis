1. To write \( A \) in the form \( A = P C P^{-1} \) where \( C = \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix} \), we first find the eigenvalues of \( A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix} \) by solving \( \det(A - \lambda I) = 0 \).  
2. Compute the characteristic polynomial: \( \det \begin{pmatrix} 1-\lambda & -2 \\ 1 & 3-\lambda \end{pmatrix} = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 \).  
3. Set the characteristic polynomial to zero: \( \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0 \implies \lambda = \frac{4 \pm \sqrt{16-20}}{2} = \frac{4 \pm 2i}{2} = 2 \pm i \).  
4. The matrix \( C \) should have the form \( \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix} \) with eigenvalues \( a \pm bi \), so set \( a = 2 \), \( b = 1 \), thus \( C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \).  
5. Find an eigenvector for \( \lambda = 2+i \): \( (A - (2+i)I)x = 0 \implies \begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{pmatrix} = 0 \).  
6. From the first row: \( (-1-i)x_1 - 2x_2 = 0 \implies x_1 = \frac{-2}{-1-i}x_2 = \frac{2}{1+i}x_2 \).  
7. Rationalize denominator: \( \frac{2}{1+i} = \frac{2(1-i)}{(1+i)(1-i)} = \frac{2(1-i)}{2} = 1-i \), so \( x_1 = (1-i)x_2 \).  
8. Take \( x_2 = 1 \), so eigenvector is \( v = \begin{pmatrix} 1-i \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \).  
9. The real and imaginary parts of \( v \) are \( \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \) and \( \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix} \), so set \( P = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \).  
10. The invertible matrix \( P \) and \( C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \) satisfy \( A = P C P^{-1} \).
