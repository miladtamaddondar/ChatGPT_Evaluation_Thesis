1. The standard matrix for a counterclockwise rotation by angle \(\theta\) in \(\mathbb{R}^2\) is \(\begin{bmatrix} \cos\theta & -\sin\theta \\ \sin\theta & \cos\theta \end{bmatrix}\).
2. Substitute \(\theta = \frac{3\pi}{2}\) into the rotation matrix to get \(\begin{bmatrix} \cos\left(\frac{3\pi}{2}\right) & -\sin\left(\frac{3\pi}{2}\right) \\ \sin\left(\frac{3\pi}{2}\right) & \cos\left(\frac{3\pi}{2}\right) \end{bmatrix}\).
3. Compute \(\cos\left(\frac{3\pi}{2}\right) = 0\) and \(\sin\left(\frac{3\pi}{2}\right) = -1\).
4. Substitute these values to get the matrix \(\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\).
5. The standard matrix of \(T\) is \(\boxed{\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 0 \end{bmatrix}}\).
