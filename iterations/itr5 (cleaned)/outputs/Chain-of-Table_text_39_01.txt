The equation $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 4 \\ 2 & -6 & 8 \end{bmatrix} = \mathbf{u}\mathbf{v}^T$ means $\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} v_1 & v_2 & v_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 4 \\ 2 & -6 & 8 \end{bmatrix}$, so set up the equations: $1 \cdot v_1 = 1$, $1 \cdot v_2 = -3$, $1 \cdot v_3 = 4$, $2 \cdot v_1 = 2$, $2 \cdot v_2 = -6$, $2 \cdot v_3 = 8$.
2. Write the augmented matrix for the first row: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & -3 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 4 \end{array}\right]$ since $v_1 = 1$, $v_2 = -3$, $v_3 = 4$.
3. The second row equations $2v_1 = 2$, $2v_2 = -6$, $2v_3 = 8$ are satisfied by $v_1 = 1$, $v_2 = -3$, $v_3 = 4$, so the solution is $\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix}$.
