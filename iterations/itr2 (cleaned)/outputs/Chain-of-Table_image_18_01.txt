1. Find the eigenvalues of \(A\) by solving \(\det(A - \lambda I) = 0\): \(\begin{vmatrix} 0-\lambda & 1 \\ -3 & 4-\lambda \end{vmatrix} = (0-\lambda)(4-\lambda) - (-3)(1) = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 3 = 0\).  
2. Factor and solve for \(\lambda\): \(\lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 3 = (\lambda-1)(\lambda-3) = 0 \implies \lambda_1 = 1, \lambda_2 = 3\).  
3. Find eigenvector for \(\lambda_1 = 1\) by solving \((A - I)\vec{v} = 0\): \(A - I = \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ -3 & 3 \end{bmatrix}\), so \(\begin{bmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ -3 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}\).  
4. The first row gives \(-x + y = 0 \implies y = x\), so an eigenvector is \(\vec{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}\).  
5. Find eigenvector for \(\lambda_2 = 3\) by solving \((A - 3I)\vec{v} = 0\): \(A - 3I = \begin{bmatrix} -3 & 1 \\ -3 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\), so \(\begin{bmatrix} -3 & 1 \\ -3 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}\).  
6. The first row gives \(-3x + y = 0 \implies y = 3x\), so an eigenvector is \(\vec{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix}\).  
7. Therefore, a basis \(\mathcal{B}\) for \(\mathbb{R}^2\) such that \([T]_{\mathcal{B}}\) is diagonal is \(\left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} \right\}\).
