1. To diagonalize $A$, first find its eigenvalues by solving $\det(A - \lambda I) = 0$: $\det\begin{bmatrix}0-\lambda & 1 \\ -3 & 4-\lambda\end{bmatrix} = (0-\lambda)(4-\lambda) - (-3)(1) = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 3 = 0$.
2. Factor the characteristic polynomial: $\lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 3 = (\lambda - 1)(\lambda - 3) = 0$.
3. The eigenvalues are $\lambda_1 = 1$ and $\lambda_2 = 3$.
4. Find an eigenvector for $\lambda_1 = 1$ by solving $(A - I)\mathbf{v} = 0$: $A - I = \begin{bmatrix}-1 & 1 \\ -3 & 3\end{bmatrix}$.
5. Set up the system $-v_1 + v_2 = 0$, $-3v_1 + 3v_2 = 0$; both equations are equivalent, so $v_2 = v_1$.
6. Take $v_1 = 1$, $v_2 = 1$; thus, an eigenvector for $\lambda_1 = 1$ is $\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 1\end{bmatrix}$.
7. Find an eigenvector for $\lambda_2 = 3$ by solving $(A - 3I)\mathbf{v} = 0$: $A - 3I = \begin{bmatrix}-3 & 1 \\ -3 & 1\end{bmatrix}$.
8. Set up the system $-3v_1 + v_2 = 0$, $-3v_1 + v_2 = 0$; so $v_2 = 3v_1$.
9. Take $v_1 = 1$, $v_2 = 3$; thus, an eigenvector for $\lambda_2 = 3$ is $\mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 3\end{bmatrix}$.
10. The basis $\mathcal{B}$ for $\mathbb{R}^2$ such that $[T]_{\mathcal{B}}$ is diagonal is $\left\{\begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 1\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 3\end{bmatrix}\right\}$.
