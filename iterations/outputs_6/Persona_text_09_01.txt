1. Write the vectors as columns of a matrix: \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ -3 & 9 & -1 & 5 \\ 2 & -6 & 4 & -3 \\ -4 & 12 & 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix} \).
2. Observe that \(\mathbf{v}_2 = -3\mathbf{v}_1\), so \(\mathbf{v}_2\) is dependent on \(\mathbf{v}_1\).
3. Subtract \(2\) times \(\mathbf{v}_1\) from \(\mathbf{v}_3\): \(\mathbf{v}_3 - 2\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} - 2\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 5 \\ 0 \\ 10 \end{bmatrix}\).
4. Subtract \(-4\) times \(\mathbf{v}_1\) from \(\mathbf{v}_4\): \(\mathbf{v}_4 - (-4)\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} -4 \\ 5 \\ -3 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} + 4\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ -7 \\ 5 \\ -9 \end{bmatrix}\).
5. Now consider the set \(\{ \mathbf{v}_1, \mathbf{w}_1, \mathbf{w}_2 \}\) where \(\mathbf{w}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 5 \\ 0 \\ 10 \end{bmatrix}\), \(\mathbf{w}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ -7 \\ 5 \\ -9 \end{bmatrix}\).
6. Check if \(\mathbf{w}_2\) is a linear combination of \(\mathbf{v}_1\) and \(\mathbf{w}_1\) by solving \(a\mathbf{v}_1 + b\mathbf{w}_1 = \mathbf{w}_2\).
7. Set up the system: \(a\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix} + b\begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 5 \\ 0 \\ 10 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ -7 \\ 5 \\ -9 \end{bmatrix}\).
8. From the first entry: \(a = 0\); from the third entry: \(2a = 5 \implies a = 2.5\), which contradicts the first, so \(\mathbf{w}_2\) is not in the span of \(\mathbf{v}_1\) and \(\mathbf{w}_1\).
9. Thus, \(\{\mathbf{v}_1, \mathbf{w}_1, \mathbf{w}_2\}\) is linearly independent and forms a basis for the subspace.
10. The basis is \(\left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 5 \\ 0 \\ 10 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ -7 \\ 5 \\ -9 \end{bmatrix} \right\}\) and the dimension is \(3\).
