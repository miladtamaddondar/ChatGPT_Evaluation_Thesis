1. To find another eigenvector, first find the eigenvalues of \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 0.6 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.7 \end{bmatrix}\) by solving \(\det(A - \lambda I) = 0\): \(\det\left(\begin{bmatrix} 0.6-\lambda & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.7-\lambda \end{bmatrix}\right) = (0.6-\lambda)(0.7-\lambda) - (0.4)(0.3) = 0\).
2. Expand and solve: \((0.6-\lambda)(0.7-\lambda) - 0.12 = 0 \implies 0.42 - 0.6\lambda - 0.7\lambda + \lambda^2 - 0.12 = 0 \implies \lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3 = 0\).
3. Solve quadratic: \(\lambda = \frac{1.3 \pm \sqrt{1.69 - 1.2}}{2} = \frac{1.3 \pm 0.7}{2}\) so \(\lambda_1 = 1\), \(\lambda_2 = 0.3\).
4. For \(\lambda_2 = 0.3\), solve \((A - 0.3I)\vec{v}_2 = 0\): \(A - 0.3I = \begin{bmatrix} 0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.4 \end{bmatrix}\).
5. Write the augmented matrix: \(\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 0.3 & 0.3 & 0 \\ 0.4 & 0.4 & 0 \end{array}\right]\).
6. Row 2 minus \(\frac{4}{3}\) times Row 1: \(\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 0.3 & 0.3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{array}\right]\).
7. The first row gives \(0.3x + 0.3y = 0 \implies x + y = 0\), so \(\vec{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix}\) (any scalar multiple).
8. The basis for \(\mathbb{R}^2\) consisting of \(\vec{v}_1\) and another eigenvector is \(\left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 3/7 \\ 4/7 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} \right\}\).
