1. To find the characteristic polynomial, we compute $\det(A - \lambda I)$ where $A = \begin{bmatrix}2 & 1 \\ -1 & 4\end{bmatrix}$ and $I$ is the $2 \times 2$ identity matrix: $A - \lambda I = \begin{bmatrix}2-\lambda & 1 \\ -1 & 4-\lambda\end{bmatrix}$.
2. The determinant is $(2-\lambda)(4-\lambda) - (1)(-1) = (2-\lambda)(4-\lambda) + 1$.
3. Expanding $(2-\lambda)(4-\lambda)$ gives $8 - 2\lambda - 4\lambda + \lambda^2 = \lambda^2 - 6\lambda + 8$, so the characteristic polynomial is $\lambda^2 - 6\lambda + 9$.
4. To find the eigenvalues, set the characteristic polynomial equal to zero: $\lambda^2 - 6\lambda + 9 = 0$.
5. Factoring gives $(\lambda - 3)^2 = 0$, so the eigenvalue is $\lambda = 3$ with algebraic multiplicity 2.
