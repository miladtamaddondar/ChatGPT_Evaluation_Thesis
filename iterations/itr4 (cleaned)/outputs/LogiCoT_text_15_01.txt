1. To find $P$ and $C$ such that $A = PCP^{-1}$ with $C = \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix}$, we first find the eigenvalues of $A$ by solving $\det(A - \lambda I) = 0$.
2. Compute the characteristic polynomial: $\det\left(\begin{pmatrix} 1-\lambda & -2 \\ 1 & 3-\lambda \end{pmatrix}\right) = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5$.
3. Set the characteristic polynomial to zero: $\lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0$.
4. Solve for $\lambda$ using the quadratic formula: $\lambda = \frac{4 \pm \sqrt{16 - 20}}{2} = \frac{4 \pm 2i}{2} = 2 \pm i$.
5. Since $A$ has complex conjugate eigenvalues, it is similar to a real matrix of the form $C = \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix}$ with $a = 2$, $b = 1$.
6. To find $P$, compute an eigenvector for $\lambda = 2 + i$: Solve $(A - (2+i)I)\vec{v} = 0$.
7. $A - (2+i)I = \begin{pmatrix} 1-(2+i) & -2 \\ 1 & 3-(2+i) \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix}$.
8. Set up the system: $(-1-i)x - 2y = 0$, $x + (1-i)y = 0$.
9. From the second equation: $x = -(1-i)y$.
10. Substitute into the first equation: $(-1-i)(-(1-i)y) - 2y = (1+i)(1-i)y - 2y = (1^2 - i^2)y - 2y = (1 - (-1))y - 2y = 2y - 2y = 0$ (verifying the eigenvector is correct).
11. Choose $y = 1$, so $x = -(1-i)$, thus the eigenvector is $\vec{v}_1 = \begin{pmatrix} -(1-i) \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}$.
12. The conjugate eigenvector for $\lambda = 2 - i$ is $\vec{v}_2 = \begin{pmatrix} -(1+i) \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}$.
13. Let $P$ have columns as the real and imaginary parts of $\vec{v}_1$: $\text{Re}(\vec{v}_1) = \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}$, $\text{Im}(\vec{v}_1) = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}$, so $P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}$.
14. The matrix $C$ is $\begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}$, matching the required form with $a=2$, $b=1$.
15. Therefore, $A = P C P^{-1}$ with $P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}$ and $C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}$.
