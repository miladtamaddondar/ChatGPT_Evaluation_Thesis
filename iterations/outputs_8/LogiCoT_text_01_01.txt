1. To find the inverse of a 2x2 matrix \(A = \begin{bmatrix}a & b \\ c & d\end{bmatrix}\), use the formula \(A^{-1} = \frac{1}{ad-bc}\begin{bmatrix}d & -b \\ -c & a\end{bmatrix}\) if \(ad-bc \neq 0\).
2. For the given matrix \(A = \begin{bmatrix}8 & 6 \\ 5 & 4\end{bmatrix}\), compute the determinant: \(\det(A) = (8)(4) - (6)(5) = 32 - 30 = 2\).
3. Since \(\det(A) = 2 \neq 0\), the matrix is invertible (by the invertibility criterion for square matrices).
4. Apply the inverse formula: \(A^{-1} = \frac{1}{2}\begin{bmatrix}4 & -6 \\ -5 & 8\end{bmatrix}\).
5. Simplify the entries: \(A^{-1} = \begin{bmatrix}2 & -3 \\ -2.5 & 4\end{bmatrix}\).
6. Final answer: The inverse of \(\begin{bmatrix}8 & 6 \\ 5 & 4\end{bmatrix}\) is \(\begin{bmatrix}2 & -3 \\ -2.5 & 4\end{bmatrix}\).
