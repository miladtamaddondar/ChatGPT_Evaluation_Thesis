4. The change-of-coordinates matrix \(P\) from \(\mathcal{B}\) to the standard basis is formed by these vectors as columns: \(P = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 \\ -3 & -5 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\).  
5. To write \(t^2\) as a linear combination of the polynomials in \(\mathcal{B}\), let \(t^2 = a(1 - 3t^2) + b(2 + t - 5t^2) + c(1 + 2t)\).  
6. Expand the right side: \(a(1 - 3t^2) + b(2 + t - 5t^2) + c(1 + 2t) = (a + 2b + c) + (b + 2c)t + (-3a - 5b)t^2\).  
7. Set coefficients equal to those of \(t^2\): \(a + 2b + c = 0\), \(b + 2c = 0\), \(-3a - 5b = 1\).  
8. Solve \(b + 2c = 0 \implies b = -2c\); substitute into the other equations: \(a + 2(-2c) + c = 0 \implies a - 4c + c = 0 \implies a = 3c\).  
9. Substitute \(a = 3c\), \(b = -2c\) into \(-3a - 5b = 1\): \(-3(3c) - 5(-2c) = 1 \implies -9c + 10c = 1 \implies c = 1\).  
10. Then \(a = 3c = 3\), \(b = -2c = -2\), so \(t^2 = 3(1 - 3t^2) - 2(2 + t - 5t^2) + 1(1 + 2t)\) in terms of \(\mathcal{B}\).  
11. Final answer: The change-of-coordinates matrix is \(P = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 \\ -3 & -5 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\), and \(t^2 = 3(1 - 3t^2) - 2(2 + t - 5t^2) + 1(1 + 2t)\) as a linear combination of the polynomials in \(\mathcal{B}\).
