1. The $\mathcal{B}$-matrix for $T$ is the matrix representation of $T$ with respect to the basis $\mathcal{B}$, denoted $[T]_{\mathcal{B}}$, whose columns are $[T(\mathbf{b}_1)]_{\mathcal{B}}$ and $[T(\mathbf{b}_2)]_{\mathcal{B}}$.
2. Compute $T(\mathbf{b}_1) = A\mathbf{b}_1 = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 1 \\ -1 & 3\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 1\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}1+1 \\ -1+3\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 2\end{bmatrix}$.
3. Find coordinates $[T(\mathbf{b}_1)]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix}a_1 \\ a_2\end{bmatrix}$ such that $T(\mathbf{b}_1) = a_1\mathbf{b}_1 + a_2\mathbf{b}_2$, i.e., $\begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 2\end{bmatrix} = a_1\begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 1\end{bmatrix} + a_2\begin{bmatrix}5 \\ 4\end{bmatrix}$.
4. Set up the system: $2 = a_1 + 5a_2$, $2 = a_1 + 4a_2$.
5. Subtract the second equation from the first: $(a_1 + 5a_2) - (a_1 + 4a_2) = 2 - 2 \implies a_2 = 0$.
6. Substitute $a_2 = 0$ into $2 = a_1 + 5a_2$ to get $a_1 = 2$.
7. Therefore, $[T(\mathbf{b}_1)]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 0\end{bmatrix}$.
8. Compute $T(\mathbf{b}_2) = A\mathbf{b}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 1 \\ -1 & 3\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}5 \\ 4\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}5+4 \\ -5+12\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}9 \\ 7\end{bmatrix}$.
9. Find coordinates $[T(\mathbf{b}_2)]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix}b_1 \\ b_2\end{bmatrix}$ such that $T(\mathbf{b}_2) = b_1\mathbf{b}_1 + b_2\mathbf{b}_2$, i.e., $\begin{bmatrix}9 \\ 7\end{bmatrix} = b_1\begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 1\end{bmatrix} + b_2\begin{bmatrix}5 \\ 4\end{bmatrix}$.
10. Set up the system: $9 = b_1 + 5b_2$, $7 = b_1 + 4b_2$.
11. Subtract the second equation from the first: $(b_1 + 5b_2) - (b_1 + 4b_2) = 9 - 7 \implies b_2 = 2$.
12. Substitute $b_2 = 2$ into $9 = b_1 + 5b_2$ to get $b_1 = 9 - 10 = -1$.
13. Therefore, $[T(\mathbf{b}_2)]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix}-1 \\ 2\end{bmatrix}$.
14. The $\mathcal{B}$-matrix for $T$ is $[T]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix}2 & -1 \\ 0 & 2\end{bmatrix}$.
