1. Let \( W = \left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 3a + b \\ 4 \\ a - 5b \end{bmatrix} : a, b \in \mathbb{R} \right\} \). Observe that the second entry is always 4, regardless of \( a \) or \( b \).  
2. To check if \( W \) is a vector space, test closure under addition: Let \( \vec{u} = \begin{bmatrix} 3a_1 + b_1 \\ 4 \\ a_1 - 5b_1 \end{bmatrix} \), \( \vec{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 3a_2 + b_2 \\ 4 \\ a_2 - 5b_2 \end{bmatrix} \). Then \( \vec{u} + \vec{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 3(a_1 + a_2) + (b_1 + b_2) \\ 8 \\ (a_1 + a_2) - 5(b_1 + b_2) \end{bmatrix} \).  
3. The second entry is 8, not 4, so \( \vec{u} + \vec{v} \notin W \).  
4. Therefore, \( W \) is not a vector space.  
5. Example: \( \begin{bmatrix} 3 \cdot 1 + 0 \\ 4 \\ 1 - 5 \cdot 0 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 3 \cdot 0 + 1 \\ 4 \\ 0 - 5 \cdot 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 8 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix} \notin W \) since the second entry is not 4.  
6. Final answer: \( W \) is not a vector space because it is not closed under addition; for example, \( \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 4 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 4 \\ -5 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 8 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix} \notin W \).
