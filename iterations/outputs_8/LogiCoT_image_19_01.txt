1. To find the matrix of \( T \) in the basis \( \mathcal{B} = \{\mathbf{b}_1, \mathbf{b}_2\} \), we need to compute \([T]_{\mathcal{B}}\) such that \( [T(\mathbf{x})]_{\mathcal{B}} = [T]_{\mathcal{B}} [\mathbf{x}]_{\mathcal{B}} \).  
2. Compute \( T(\mathbf{b}_1) = A\mathbf{b}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \).  
3. Compute \( T(\mathbf{b}_2) = A\mathbf{b}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 9 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \).  
4. Express \( T(\mathbf{b}_1) = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \) as a linear combination of \( \mathbf{b}_1 \) and \( \mathbf{b}_2 \): solve \( c_1\mathbf{b}_1 + c_2\mathbf{b}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \).  
5. Set up the system: \( c_1\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} + c_2\begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \), so \( c_1 + 5c_2 = 2 \) and \( c_1 + 4c_2 = 2 \).  
6. Subtract the second equation from the first: \( (c_1 + 5c_2) - (c_1 + 4c_2) = 2 - 2 \implies c_2 = 0 \).  
7. Substitute \( c_2 = 0 \) into \( c_1 + 5c_2 = 2 \) to get \( c_1 = 2 \).  
8. Thus, \( T(\mathbf{b}_1) = 2\mathbf{b}_1 + 0\mathbf{b}_2 \), so the first column of \( [T]_{\mathcal{B}} \) is \( \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \).  
9. Express \( T(\mathbf{b}_2) = \begin{bmatrix} 9 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \) as a linear combination of \( \mathbf{b}_1 \) and \( \mathbf{b}_2 \): solve \( d_1\mathbf{b}_1 + d_2\mathbf{b}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 9 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \).  
10. Set up the system: \( d_1 + 5d_2 = 9 \) and \( d_1 + 4d_2 = 7 \).  
11. Subtract the second equation from the first: \( (d_1 + 5d_2) - (d_1 + 4d_2) = 9 - 7 \implies d_2 = 2 \).  
12. Substitute \( d_2 = 2 \) into \( d_1 + 5d_2 = 9 \) to get \( d_1 + 10 = 9 \implies d_1 = -1 \).  
13. Thus, \( T(\mathbf{b}_2) = -1\mathbf{b}_1 + 2\mathbf{b}_2 \), so the second column of \( [T]_{\mathcal{B}} \) is \( \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \).  
14. Therefore, the \( \mathcal{B} \)-matrix for \( T \) is \( [T]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 0 & 2 \end{bmatrix} \).
