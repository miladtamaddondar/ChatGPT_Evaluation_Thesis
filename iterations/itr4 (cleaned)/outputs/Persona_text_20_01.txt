1. To find a basis for $\mathbb{R}^2$ consisting of $\mathbf{v}_1$ and another eigenvector $\mathbf{v}_2$ of $A$, first note that $\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix}3/7 \\ 4/7\end{bmatrix}$ is already given as an eigenvector.  
2. Find the eigenvalues of $A$ by solving $\det(A - \lambda I) = 0$: $\det\left(\begin{bmatrix}0.6-\lambda & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.7-\lambda\end{bmatrix}\right) = (0.6-\lambda)(0.7-\lambda) - (0.3)(0.4) = 0.42 - 0.6\lambda - 0.7\lambda + \lambda^2 - 0.12 = \lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3 = 0$.
3. Solve the quadratic equation $\lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3 = 0$ to get the eigenvalues: $\lambda = \frac{1.3 \pm \sqrt{1.69 - 1.2}}{2} = \frac{1.3 \pm \sqrt{0.49}}{2} = \frac{1.3 \pm 0.7}{2}$, so $\lambda_1 = 1$ and $\lambda_2 = 0.3$.
4. Since $\mathbf{v}_1$ is an eigenvector, check which eigenvalue it corresponds to by calculating $A\mathbf{v}_1$: $A\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix}0.6 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.7\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}3/7 \\ 4/7\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0.6 \cdot 3/7 + 0.3 \cdot 4/7 \\ 0.4 \cdot 3/7 + 0.7 \cdot 4/7\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}(1.8+1.2)/7 \\ (1.2+2.8)/7\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}3/7 \\ 4/7\end{bmatrix} = \mathbf{v}_1$, so $\mathbf{v}_1$ is an eigenvector for $\lambda=1$.
5. Find an eigenvector $\mathbf{v}_2$ for $\lambda_2 = 0.3$ by solving $(A - 0.3I)\mathbf{v}_2 = 0$: $A - 0.3I = \begin{bmatrix}0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.4\end{bmatrix}$, so $\begin{bmatrix}0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.4\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}x \\ y\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0 \\ 0\end{bmatrix}$.
6. This gives $0.3x + 0.3y = 0$ and $0.4x + 0.4y = 0$, so $x + y = 0$, hence $y = -x$; choose $x=1$, $y=-1$, so $\mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}$.
7. Therefore, a basis for $\mathbb{R}^2$ consisting of $\mathbf{v}_1$ and another eigenvector $\mathbf{v}_2$ of $A$ is $\left\{\begin{bmatrix}3/7 \\ 4/7\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}\right\}$.
