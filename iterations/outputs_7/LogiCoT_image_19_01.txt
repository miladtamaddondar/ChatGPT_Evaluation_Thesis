1. The matrix of \( T \) in the basis \( \mathcal{B} \) is \( [T]_{\mathcal{B}} = P^{-1}AP \), where \( P \) is the change of basis matrix with columns \( \mathbf{b}_1 \) and \( \mathbf{b}_2 \).  
2. Construct \( P = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \) since \( \mathbf{b}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \), \( \mathbf{b}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} \).  
3. Compute \( \det(P) = (1)(4) - (1)(5) = 4 - 5 = -1 \), confirming \( P \) is invertible.  
4. Find \( P^{-1} = \frac{1}{-1} \begin{bmatrix} 4 & -5 \\ -1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -4 & 5 \\ 1 & -1 \end{bmatrix} \).  
5. Compute \( AP = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 9 \\ 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix} \).  
6. Compute \( [T]_{\mathcal{B}} = P^{-1}AP = \begin{bmatrix} -4 & 5 \\ 1 & -1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 9 \\ 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} (-4)(2)+5(2) & (-4)(9)+5(7) \\ (1)(2)+(-1)(2) & (1)(9)+(-1)(7) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -8+10 & -36+35 \\ 2-2 & 9-7 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 0 & 2 \end{bmatrix} \).  
7. The \( \mathcal{B} \)-matrix for \( T \) is \( \boxed{\begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 0 & 2 \end{bmatrix}} \).
