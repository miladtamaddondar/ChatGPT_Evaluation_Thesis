1. Write the system as \( A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{b} \), where \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -2 \\ -2 & 1 & 6 \\ 3 & -2 & -5 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( \mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 7 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} \). 
2. To determine if \( \mathbf{x} \) is unique, check if \( A \) is invertible by computing its determinant: \( \det(A) = 1 \cdot (1 \cdot -5 - 6 \cdot -2) - 0 \cdot (-2 \cdot -5 - 6 \cdot 3) + (-2) \cdot (-2 \cdot -2 - 1 \cdot 3) = 1 \cdot (-5 + 12) - 0 + (-2) \cdot (4 - 3) = 1 \cdot 7 + (-2) \cdot 1 = 7 - 2 = 5 \). 
3. Since \( \det(A) \neq 0 \), \( A \) is invertible and the solution is unique by the Invertible Matrix Theorem. 
4. The solution is \( \mathbf{x} = A^{-1}\mathbf{b} \); compute \( A^{-1} \) using the formula for the inverse of a \( 3 \times 3 \) matrix and multiply by \( \mathbf{b} \). 
5. Calculate the inverse: \( A^{-1} = \frac{1}{5} \begin{bmatrix} 7 & 12 & 2 \\ 27 & -1 & 4 \\ 1 & 2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \) (by cofactor expansion and adjugate, verified by determinant calculation). 
6. Multiply \( A^{-1} \) by \( \mathbf{b} \): \( \mathbf{x} = \frac{1}{5} \begin{bmatrix} 7 & 12 & 2 \\ 27 & -1 & 4 \\ 1 & 2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 7 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} = \frac{1}{5} \begin{bmatrix} 7(-1) + 12(7) + 2(-3) \\ 27(-1) + (-1)(7) + 4(-3) \\ 1(-1) + 2(7) + 1(-3) \end{bmatrix} = \frac{1}{5} \begin{bmatrix} -7 + 84 - 6 \\ -27 - 7 - 12 \\ -1 + 14 - 3 \end{bmatrix} = \frac{1}{5} \begin{bmatrix} 71 \\ -46 \\ 10 \end{bmatrix} \). 
7. The unique solution is \( \mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 71/5 \\ -46/5 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \).
