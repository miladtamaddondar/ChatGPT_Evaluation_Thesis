1. The basis \(\mathcal{B}\) for \(\mathbb{P}_3\) is given as \(\{1, 2t, -2 + 4t^2, -12t + 8t^3\}\). 
2. We want to express \(p(t) = 7 - 12t - 8t^2 + 12t^3\) as a linear combination of the basis elements: \(p(t) = a_1 \cdot 1 + a_2 \cdot 2t + a_3 \cdot (-2 + 4t^2) + a_4 \cdot (-12t + 8t^3)\). 
3. Expanding the right side: \(a_1 + 2a_2 t + a_3(-2) + a_3(4t^2) + a_4(-12t) + a_4(8t^3) = a_1 - 2a_3 + (2a_2 - 12a_4)t + 4a_3 t^2 + 8a_4 t^3\). 
4. Equate coefficients with \(p(t)\): constant: \(a_1 - 2a_3 = 7\), \(t\): \(2a_2 - 12a_4 = -12\), \(t^2\): \(4a_3 = -8\), \(t^3\): \(8a_4 = 12\). 
5. Solve \(4a_3 = -8 \implies a_3 = -2\). 
6. Solve \(8a_4 = 12 \implies a_4 = \frac{3}{2}\). 7. Substitute \(a_3\) into the constant equation: \(a_1 - 2(-2) = 7 \implies a_1 + 4 = 7 \implies a_1 = 3\). 
8. Substitute \(a_4\) into the \(t\) coefficient equation: \(2a_2 - 12(\frac{3}{2}) = -12 \implies 2a_2 - 18 = -12 \implies 2a_2 = 6 \implies a_2 = 3\). 
9. Therefore, the coordinate vector of \(p\) relative to \(\mathcal{B}\) is \(\boxed{(3,\, 3,\, -2,\, \frac{3}{2})}\).
