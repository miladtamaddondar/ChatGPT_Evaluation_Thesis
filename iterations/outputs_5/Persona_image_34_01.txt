2. Apply row reduction to \( A \) to find its reduced row echelon form (RREF).
3. Subtract 2 times column 1 from column 5: new column 5 = \( [2,1,-6,9] - 2*[1,0,-3,2] = [0,1,0,5] \).
4. Add 3 times column 1 to column 3: new column 3 = \( [-3,-4,1,6] + 3*[1,0,-3,2] = [0,-4,-8,12] \).
5. Subtract column 2 from column 5: new column 5 = \( [0,1,0,5] - [0,1,2,-3] = [0,0,-2,8] \).
6. Add 4 times column 2 to column 3: new column 3 = \( [0,-4,-8,12] + 4*[0,1,2,-3] = [0,0,0,0] \).
7. The matrix now has columns: \( [1,0,-3,2], [0,1,2,-3], [0,0,0,0], [1,-3,-8,7], [0,0,-2,8] \).
8. Subtract column 1 from column 4: new column 4 = \( [1,-3,-8,7] - [1,0,-3,2] = [0,-3,-5,5] \).
9. Add 3 times column 2 to column 4: new column 4 = \( [0,-3,-5,5] + 3*[0,1,2,-3] = [0,0,1,-4] \).
10. The nonzero columns after row reduction are columns 1, 2, and 4: \( [1,0,-3,2], [0,1,2,-3], [0,0,1,-4] \).
11. Therefore, a basis for the space spanned by the vectors is \( \left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ -3 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \\ 2 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix} \right\} \).
