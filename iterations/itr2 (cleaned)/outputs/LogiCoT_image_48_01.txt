1. To find a nonzero vector \(\mathbf{v}\) in \(\mathbb{R}^3\) orthogonal to both \(\mathbf{u}_1\) and \(\mathbf{u}_2\), we use the cross product: \(\mathbf{v} = \mathbf{u}_1 \times \mathbf{u}_2\). 
2. Compute the cross product: \(\mathbf{v} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ -2 \end{pmatrix} \times \begin{pmatrix} 5 \\ -1 \\ 2 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} (1)(2) - (-2)(-1) \\ (-2)(5) - (1)(2) \\ (1)(-1) - (1)(5) \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 2 - 2 \\ -10 - 2 \\ -1 - 5 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 0 \\ -12 \\ -6 \end{pmatrix}\). 
3. Verify orthogonality: \(\mathbf{u}_1 \cdot \mathbf{v} = 1 \cdot 0 + 1 \cdot (-12) + (-2) \cdot (-6) = 0 - 12 + 12 = 0\), \(\mathbf{u}_2 \cdot \mathbf{v} = 5 \cdot 0 + (-1) \cdot (-12) + 2 \cdot (-6) = 0 + 12 - 12 = 0\), so \(\mathbf{v}\) is orthogonal to both. 
4. The required nonzero vector is \(\boxed{\mathbf{v} = \begin{pmatrix} 0 \\ -12 \\ -6 \end{pmatrix}}\).
