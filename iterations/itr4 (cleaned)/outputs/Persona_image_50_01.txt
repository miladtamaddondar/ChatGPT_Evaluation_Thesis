**(a) Compute the orthogonal projection of \( p_2 \) onto the subspace spanned by \( p_0 \) and \( p_1 \):**
1. The inner product is defined as \( \langle f, g \rangle = f(-3)g(-3) + f(-1)g(-1) + f(1)g(1) + f(3)g(3) \).
2. Evaluate \( p_0, p_1, p_2 \) at the points: \( p_0(t) = 1 \) so \( (1,1,1,1) \), \( p_1(t) = t \) so \( (-3,-1,1,3) \), \( p_2(t) = t^2 \) so \( (9,1,1,9) \).
3. Compute \( \langle p_2, p_0 \rangle = 9 \cdot 1 + 1 \cdot 1 + 1 \cdot 1 + 9 \cdot 1 = 20 \).
4. Compute \( \langle p_0, p_0 \rangle = 1+1+1+1 = 4 \).
5. Compute \( \langle p_2, p_1 \rangle = 9 \cdot (-3) + 1 \cdot (-1) + 1 \cdot 1 + 9 \cdot 3 = -27-1+1+27 = 0 \).
6. Compute \( \langle p_1, p_1 \rangle = (-3)^2 + (-1)^2 + 1^2 + 3^2 = 9+1+1+9 = 20 \).
7. The projection formula is \( \text{proj}_{U}(p_2) = \frac{\langle p_2, p_0 \rangle}{\langle p_0, p_0 \rangle}p_0 + \frac{\langle p_2, p_1 \rangle}{\langle p_1, p_1 \rangle}p_1 \).
8. Substitute values: \( \frac{20}{4}p_0 + \frac{0}{20}p_1 = 5p_0 + 0p_1 = 5 \).
9. The orthogonal projection of \( p_2 \) onto the subspace spanned by \( p_0 \) and \( p_1 \) is \( 5 \).
**(b) Find a polynomial \( q \) orthogonal to \( p_0 \) and \( p_1 \), and scale it as required:**
10. Let \( q(t) = at^2 + bt + c \), require \( \langle q, p_0 \rangle = 0 \) and \( \langle q, p_1 \rangle = 0 \).
11. \( \langle q, p_0 \rangle = a(9+1+1+9) + b(-3-1+1+3) + c(1+1+1+1) = 20a + 0b + 4c = 0 \).
12. \( \langle q, p_1 \rangle = a(9 \cdot -3 + 1 \cdot -1 + 1 \cdot 1 + 9 \cdot 3) + b((-3)^2 + (-1)^2 + 1^2 + 3^2) + c(-3-1+1+3) = a(0) + b(20) + c(0) = 20b = 0 \).
13. From step 12, \( b = 0 \).
14. From step 11, \( 20a + 4c = 0 \implies 5a + c = 0 \implies c = -5a \).
15. So \( q(t) = a t^2 - 5a \).
16. The vector of values at \( (-3, -1, 1, 3) \) is \( (9a-5a, 1a-5a, 1a-5a, 9a-5a) = (4a, -4a, -4a, 4a) \).
17. We want this to be \( (1, -1, -1, 1) \), so \( 4a = 1 \implies a = \frac{1}{4} \).
18. Thus, \( q(t) = \frac{1}{4}t^2 - \frac{5}{4} \).
19. The answer: (a) The orthogonal projection is \( 5 \). (b) The required polynomial is \( q(t) = \frac{1}{4}t^2 - \frac{5}{4} \).
