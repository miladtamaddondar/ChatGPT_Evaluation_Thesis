1. To find the inverse of a \(2 \times 2\) matrix \(\begin{bmatrix}a & b \\ c & d\end{bmatrix}\), use the formula \(\frac{1}{ad-bc}\begin{bmatrix}d & -b \\ -c & a\end{bmatrix}\).
2. For the given matrix, \(a=8\), \(b=6\), \(c=5\), \(d=4\).
3. Compute the determinant: \(ad-bc = (8)(4) - (6)(5) = 32 - 30 = 2\).
4. Write the adjugate matrix: \(\begin{bmatrix}4 & -6 \\ -5 & 8\end{bmatrix}\).
5. Multiply the adjugate by \(\frac{1}{2}\): \(\frac{1}{2}\begin{bmatrix}4 & -6 \\ -5 & 8\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}2 & -3 \\ -2.5 & 4\end{bmatrix}\).
6. The inverse of \(\begin{bmatrix}8 & 6 \\ 5 & 4\end{bmatrix}\) is \(\begin{bmatrix}2 & -3 \\ -2.5 & 4\end{bmatrix}\).
