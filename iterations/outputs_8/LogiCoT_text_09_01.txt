1. Write the vectors as columns of a matrix \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ -3 & 9 & -1 & 5 \\ 2 & -6 & 4 & -3 \\ -4 & 12 & 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix} \) to analyze their linear dependence.  
2. Perform row operations to row reduce \( A \); first, notice that \(\mathbf{v}_2 = -3\mathbf{v}_1\), so \(\mathbf{v}_2\) is dependent on \(\mathbf{v}_1\).  
3. Verify: \(-3 \times \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -3 \\ 9 \\ -6 \\ 12 \end{bmatrix} = \mathbf{v}_2\), so \(\mathbf{v}_2\) can be removed from the set.  
4. Form the reduced set \(\{\mathbf{v}_1, \mathbf{v}_3, \mathbf{v}_4\}\) and check if \(\mathbf{v}_3\) is a linear combination of \(\mathbf{v}_1\): Set \(a\mathbf{v}_1 = \mathbf{v}_3\), so \(a\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}\); solving gives \(a=2\) from the first entry, but \(-3a = -1 \implies a = 1/3\), so no solution, so \(\mathbf{v}_3\) is independent of \(\mathbf{v}_1\).  
5. Check if \(\mathbf{v}_4\) is a linear combination of \(\mathbf{v}_1\) and \(\mathbf{v}_3\): Set \(a\mathbf{v}_1 + b\mathbf{v}_3 = \mathbf{v}_4\), i.e., \(a\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix} + b\begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -4 \\ 5 \\ -3 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix}\).  
6. Write the system: \(a + 2b = -4\), \(-3a - b = 5\), \(2a + 4b = -3\), \(-4a + 2b = 7\); solve for \(a\) and \(b\).  
7. From the first equation, \(a = -4 - 2b\); substitute into the second: \(-3(-4 - 2b) - b = 5 \implies 12 + 6b - b = 5 \implies 5b = -7 \implies b = -7/5\), then \(a = -4 - 2(-7/5) = -4 + 14/5 = (-20 + 14)/5 = -6/5\).  
8. Check the third equation: \(2a + 4b = 2(-6/5) + 4(-7/5) = -12/5 - 28/5 = -40/5 = -8\), but the third entry of \(\mathbf{v}_4\) is \(-3\), so \(-8 \neq -3\); thus, \(\mathbf{v}_4\) is not a linear combination of \(\mathbf{v}_1\) and \(\mathbf{v}_3\).  
9. Therefore, \(\{\mathbf{v}_1, \mathbf{v}_3, \mathbf{v}_4\}\) is a linearly independent set and forms a basis for the subspace.  
10. The dimension of the subspace is 3, since there are 3 linearly independent vectors in the basis.
