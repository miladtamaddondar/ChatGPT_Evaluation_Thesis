1. Write the transformation equations as a system: \( x_1 + x_2 = 3 \) and \( 4x_1 + 5x_2 = 8 \).
2. Express the system in matrix form: \( \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 4 & 5 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ 8 \end{pmatrix} \).
3. Verify the coefficient matrix is invertible by computing its determinant: \( \det\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 4 & 5 \end{pmatrix} = 1 \cdot 5 - 1 \cdot 4 = 1 \), so the matrix is invertible.
4. Find the inverse of the matrix: \( \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 4 & 5 \end{pmatrix}^{-1} = \frac{1}{1} \begin{pmatrix} 5 & -1 \\ -4 & 1 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 5 & -1 \\ -4 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \).
5. Multiply both sides by the inverse to solve for \( \mathbf{x} \): \( \begin{pmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 5 & -1 \\ -4 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ 8 \end{pmatrix} \).
6. Compute \( x_1 = 5 \cdot 3 + (-1) \cdot 8 = 15 - 8 = 7 \).
7. Compute \( x_2 = -4 \cdot 3 + 1 \cdot 8 = -12 + 8 = -4 \).
8. The solution is \( \mathbf{x} = \begin{pmatrix} 7 \\ -4 \end{pmatrix} \).
