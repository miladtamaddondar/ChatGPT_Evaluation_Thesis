1. Start by letting \( B = \begin{bmatrix}b_1 & b_2\end{bmatrix} \) where \( b_1 = \begin{bmatrix}x_1 \\ y_1\end{bmatrix} \), \( b_2 = \begin{bmatrix}x_2 \\ y_2\end{bmatrix} \), and require \( AB = 0 \Rightarrow Ab_1 = 0 \) and \( Ab_2 = 0 \).
2. Write the system for a generic column \( b = \begin{bmatrix}x \\ y\end{bmatrix} \): \( \begin{bmatrix}3 & -6 \\ 1 & -2\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}x \\ y\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0 \\ 0\end{bmatrix} \), which gives equations: \( 3x - 6y = 0 \), \( x - 2y = 0 \).
3. Augmented matrix: \( \left[\begin{array}{cc|c}3 & -6 & 0 \\ 1 & -2 & 0\end{array}\right] \).
4. Row 1 \(\to\) Row 1 divided by 3: \( \left[\begin{array}{cc|c}1 & -2 & 0 \\ 1 & -2 & 0\end{array}\right] \).
5. Row 2 \(\to\) Row 2 - Row 1: \( \left[\begin{array}{cc|c}1 & -2 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0\end{array}\right] \).
6. The solution is \( x - 2y = 0 \Rightarrow x = 2y \), so the general solution is \( \begin{bmatrix}x \\ y\end{bmatrix} = y\begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 1\end{bmatrix} \).
7. Choose two different nonzero values for \( y \), say \( y = 1 \) and \( y = -1 \), giving \( b_1 = \begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 1\end{bmatrix} \), \( b_2 = \begin{bmatrix}-2 \\ -1\end{bmatrix} \).
8. Final answer: \( B = \begin{bmatrix}2 & -2 \\ 1 & -1\end{bmatrix} \).
