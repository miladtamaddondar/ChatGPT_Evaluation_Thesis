1. Write the matrix \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 2 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \) and recall that eigenvalues are found by solving \( \det(A - \lambda I) = 0 \). 
2. Compute \( A - \lambda I = \begin{bmatrix} 0-\lambda & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 2-\lambda & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 1-\lambda \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -\lambda & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 2-\lambda & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 1-\lambda \end{bmatrix} \). 
3. The determinant is \( \det(A - \lambda I) = (-\lambda) \cdot \det\begin{bmatrix} 2-\lambda & 5 \\ 0 & 1-\lambda \end{bmatrix} \) because the first row has only one nonzero entry at (1,1). 
4. Compute the 2x2 determinant: \( \det\begin{bmatrix} 2-\lambda & 5 \\ 0 & 1-\lambda \end{bmatrix} = (2-\lambda)(1-\lambda) - 0 = (2-\lambda)(1-\lambda) \). 
5. Thus, \( \det(A - \lambda I) = -\lambda (2-\lambda)(1-\lambda) \). 
6. Set the determinant to zero: \( -\lambda (2-\lambda)(1-\lambda) = 0 \). 
7. The solutions are \( \lambda = 0, 1, 2 \), which are the eigenvalues of the matrix.
