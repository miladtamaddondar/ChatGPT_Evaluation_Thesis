1. Write the problem in terms of matrices: \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 3 & 1 \\ -2 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \), \( \mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ -2 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} \).  
2. The orthogonal projection of \( \mathbf{b} \) onto \( \text{Col } A \) is \( \mathbf{p} = A(A^T A)^{-1}A^T \mathbf{b} \); first compute \( A^T A \): \( A^T A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 3 & 1 \\ -2 & 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1^2+3^2+(-2)^2 & 1 \cdot 5 + 3 \cdot 1 + (-2) \cdot 4 \\ 5 \cdot 1 + 1 \cdot 3 + 4 \cdot (-2) & 5^2 + 1^2 + 4^2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1+9+4 & 5+3-8 \\ 5+3-8 & 25+1+16 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 14 & 0 \\ 0 & 42 \end{bmatrix} \).  
3. Verify \( A^T A \) is correct: \( (1^2 + 3^2 + (-2)^2) = 1 + 9 + 4 = 14 \), \( (1 \cdot 5 + 3 \cdot 1 + (-2) \cdot 4) = 5 + 3 - 8 = 0 \), \( (5^2 + 1^2 + 4^2) = 25 + 1 + 16 = 42 \), so \( A^T A = \begin{bmatrix} 14 & 0 \\ 0 & 42 \end{bmatrix} \) is correct.  
4. Compute \( (A^T A)^{-1} \): Since \( A^T A \) is diagonal, \( (A^T A)^{-1} = \begin{bmatrix} 1/14 & 0 \\ 0 & 1/42 \end{bmatrix} \).  
5. Compute \( A^T \mathbf{b} \): \( A^T \mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ -2 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \cdot 4 + 3 \cdot (-2) + (-2) \cdot (-3) \\ 5 \cdot 4 + 1 \cdot (-2) + 4 \cdot (-3) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 - 6 + 6 \\ 20 - 2 - 12 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix} \).  
6. Verify \( A^T \mathbf{b} \): First entry: \( 4 - 6 + 6 = 4 \); second entry: \( 20 - 2 - 12 = 6 \), so \( A^T \mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix} \) is correct.  
7. Compute \( (A^T A)^{-1} A^T \mathbf{b} \): \( \begin{bmatrix} 1/14 & 0 \\ 0 & 1/42 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4/14 \\ 6/42 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2/7 \\ 1/7 \end{bmatrix} \).  
8. Compute \( \mathbf{p} = A \begin{bmatrix} 2/7 \\ 1/7 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 3 & 1 \\ -2 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 2/7 \\ 1/7 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \cdot 2/7 + 5 \cdot 1/7 \\ 3 \cdot 2/7 + 1 \cdot 1/7 \\ -2 \cdot 2/7 + 4 \cdot 1/7 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2/7 + 5/7 \\ 6/7 + 1/7 \\ -4/7 + 4/7 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \).  
9. The orthogonal projection of \( \mathbf{b} \) onto \( \text{Col } A \) is \( \mathbf{p} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \).  
10. The least-squares solution to \( A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{b} \) is \( \mathbf{x} = (A^T A)^{-1} A^T \mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 2/7 \\ 1/7 \end{bmatrix} \).
