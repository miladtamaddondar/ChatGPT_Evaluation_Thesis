1. Display the given matrix \( A = \begin{bmatrix}1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ -3 & 9 & -1 & 5 \\ 2 & -6 & 4 & -3 \\ -4 & 12 & 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix} \).  
2. The echelon form of \( A \) is given as \( \begin{bmatrix}1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 & -7 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \).  
3. The number of nonzero rows in the echelon form is 3, so the rank of \( A \) is 3.  
4. The pivot columns in the echelon form are columns 1, 3, and 4, so the corresponding columns of \( A \) form a basis for \( \text{Col } A \): \( \left\{ \begin{bmatrix}1\\-3\\2\\-4\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}2\\-1\\4\\2\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}-4\\5\\-3\\7\end{bmatrix} \right\} \).  
5. To find a basis for \( \text{Nul } A \), write the system \( A\vec{x} = \vec{0} \) using the echelon form: \( x_1 - 3x_2 + 2x_3 - 4x_4 = 0 \), \( 5x_3 - 7x_4 = 0 \), \( 5x_4 = 0 \).  
6. From the third equation, \( x_4 = 0 \); from the second, \( 5x_3 = 0 \implies x_3 = 0 \); from the first, \( x_1 - 3x_2 = 0 \implies x_1 = 3x_2 \).  
7. The general solution is \( \vec{x} = \begin{bmatrix}3x_2\\x_2\\0\\0\end{bmatrix} = x_2 \begin{bmatrix}3\\1\\0\\0\end{bmatrix} \), so a basis for \( \text{Nul } A \) is \( \left\{ \begin{bmatrix}3\\1\\0\\0\end{bmatrix} \right\} \).  
8. The dimension of \( \text{Col } A \) is 3 and the dimension of \( \text{Nul } A \) is 1, which matches the Rank-Nullity Theorem since \( 3 + 1 = 4 \) (number of columns of \( A \)).  
9. Final answers: Basis for \( \text{Col } A \) is \( \left\{ \begin{bmatrix}1\\-3\\2\\-4\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}2\\-1\\4\\2\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}-4\\5\\-3\\7\end{bmatrix} \right\} \), basis for \( \text{Nul } A \) is \( \left\{ \begin{bmatrix}3\\1\\0\\0\end{bmatrix} \right\} \), dimensions are 3 and 1 respectively.
