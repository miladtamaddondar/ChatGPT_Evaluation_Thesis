1. Write the equation to solve: We want to find $\mathbf{x}$ such that $A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{b}$, where $A = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 & -2 \\ -2 & 1 & 6 \\ 3 & -2 & -5 \end{bmatrix}$ and $\mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix}-1 \\ 7 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix}$.
2. Set up the augmented matrix: The augmented matrix is $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & -2 & -1 \\ -2 & 1 & 6 & 7 \\ 3 & -2 & -5 & -3 \end{array}\right]$.
3. Use row 1 to eliminate $x_1$ from rows 2 and 3: Row 2 $\to$ Row 2 + 2(Row 1): $(-2)+2(1)=0$, $1+2(0)=1$, $6+2(-2)=2$, $7+2(-1)=5$; Row 2 is now $[0\ 1\ 2\ |\ 5]$; Row 3 $\to$ Row 3 - 3(Row 1): $3-3(1)=0$, $-2-3(0)=-2$, $-5-3(-2)=1$, $-3-3(-1)=0$; Row 3 is now $[0\ -2\ 1\ |\ 0]$.
4. Use row 2 to eliminate $x_2$ from row 3: Row 3 $\to$ Row 3 + 2(Row 2): $0+2(0)=0$, $-2+2(1)=0$, $1+2(2)=5$, $0+2(5)=10$; Row 3 is now $[0\ 0\ 5\ |\ 10]$.
5. Solve for $x_3$ from row 3: $5x_3 = 10 \implies x_3 = 2$.
6. Back-substitute $x_3$ into row 2: $x_2 + 2x_3 = 5 \implies x_2 + 2(2) = 5 \implies x_2 = 1$.
7. Back-substitute $x_2$ and $x_3$ into row 1: $x_1 - 2x_3 = -1 \implies x_1 - 2(2) = -1 \implies x_1 = 3$.
8. The solution is $\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix}3 \\ 1 \\ 2\end{bmatrix}$.
9. Check uniqueness by considering the determinant of $A$: $\det(A) = 1\begin{vmatrix}1 & 6 \\ -2 & -5\end{vmatrix} - 0\begin{vmatrix}-2 & 6 \\ 3 & -5\end{vmatrix} + (-2)\begin{vmatrix}-2 & 1 \\ 3 & -2\end{vmatrix} = 1((-5)-(6)) - 0 + (-2)((-2)(-2)-(3)(1)) = 1(-11) + 0 + (-2)(4-3) = -11 + 0 + (-2)(1) = -11 - 2 = -13$, which is nonzero, so $A$ is invertible and the solution is unique.
10. Final answer: The unique solution is $\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix}3 \\ 1 \\ 2\end{bmatrix}$.
