1. Find the eigenvalues of \( A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix} \) by solving \( \det(A - \lambda I) = 0 \): \(\det\begin{pmatrix} 1-\lambda & -2 \\ 1 & 3-\lambda \end{pmatrix} = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0\).  
2. Solve the quadratic equation for \(\lambda\): \(\lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0 \implies \lambda = \frac{4 \pm \sqrt{16-20}}{2} = 2 \pm i\).  
3. Let \(\lambda_1 = 2+i\), \(\lambda_2 = 2-i\); find an eigenvector for \(\lambda_1\) by solving \((A - \lambda_1 I)\vec{v} = 0\): \(A - \lambda_1 I = \begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix}\).  
4. Set up the system: \((-1-i)x - 2y = 0\) and \(x + (1-i)y = 0\); let \(y = 1\), then \(x = -(1-i)\).  
5. The eigenvector for \(\lambda_1\) is \(\vec{v}_1 = \begin{pmatrix} -(1-i) \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}\).  
6. The real and imaginary parts of \(\vec{v}_1\) are \(\vec{u}_1 = \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}\), \(\vec{u}_2 = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}\).  
7. Form \(P\) using these as columns: \(P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}\).  
8. The canonical form \(C\) is \(\begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix}\) where \(a = \text{Re}(\lambda_1) = 2\), \(b = \text{Im}(\lambda_1) = 1\), so \(C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}\).  
9. The answer is: \(P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}\), \(C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}\), and \(A = P C P^{-1}\).
