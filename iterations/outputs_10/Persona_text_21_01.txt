1. To find $\mathbf{x}$ such that $A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{b}$, set up the augmented matrix: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c}1 & 0 & -2 & -1 \\ -2 & 1 & 6 & 7 \\ 3 & -2 & -5 & -3\end{array}\right]$.
2. Use row 1 to eliminate $x_1$ in rows 2 and 3: Row 2 + 2×Row 1 $\rightarrow$ Row 2: $[0\ 1\ 2\ |\ 5]$, Row 3 - 3×Row 1 $\rightarrow$ Row 3: $[0\ -2\ 1\ |\ 0]$.
3. Use row 2 to eliminate $x_2$ in row 3: Row 3 + 2×Row 2 $\rightarrow$ Row 3: $[0\ 0\ 5\ |\ 10]$.
4. Solve for $x_3$ from row 3: $5x_3 = 10 \implies x_3 = 2$.
5. Substitute $x_3 = 2$ into row 2: $x_2 + 2(2) = 5 \implies x_2 = 1$.
6. Substitute $x_2 = 1$, $x_3 = 2$ into row 1: $x_1 - 2(2) = -1 \implies x_1 = 3$.
7. The solution is $\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix}3 \\ 1 \\ 2\end{bmatrix}$.
8. Since each variable has a unique value and the system is consistent, the solution is unique.
