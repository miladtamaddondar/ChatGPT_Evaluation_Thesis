1. Let \( U = [\mathbf{u}_1 \ \mathbf{u}_2] = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} & -\frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{1}{3} & \frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} \); this forms a \(3 \times 2\) matrix whose columns are \(\mathbf{u}_1\) and \(\mathbf{u}_2\).
2. Compute \( U^T U \): \( U^T U = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} & \frac{2}{3} \\ -\frac{2}{3} & \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} & -\frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{1}{3} & \frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \) because the columns are orthonormal.
3. Verify correctness: Since \(\mathbf{u}_1\) and \(\mathbf{u}_2\) are orthonormal, \(U^T U = I\) by definition.
4. Compute \( U U^T \): \( U U^T = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} & -\frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{1}{3} & \frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} & \frac{2}{3} \\ -\frac{2}{3} & \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \) (incorrect, let's compute explicitly).
5. Compute \( U U^T \) explicitly: \( U U^T = \mathbf{u}_1 \mathbf{u}_1^T + \mathbf{u}_2 \mathbf{u}_2^T \).
6. Compute \( \mathbf{u}_1 \mathbf{u}_1^T = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{1}{3} \\ \frac{2}{3} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} & \frac{2}{3} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{4}{9} & \frac{2}{9} & \frac{4}{9} \\ \frac{2}{9} & \frac{1}{9} & \frac{2}{9} \\ \frac{4}{9} & \frac{2}{9} & \frac{4}{9} \end{bmatrix} \).
7. Compute \( \mathbf{u}_2 \mathbf{u}_2^T = \begin{bmatrix} -\frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} -\frac{2}{3} & \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{4}{9} & -\frac{4}{9} & -\frac{2}{9} \\ -\frac{4}{9} & \frac{4}{9} & \frac{2}{9} \\ -\frac{2}{9} & \frac{2}{9} & \frac{1}{9} \end{bmatrix} \).
8. Add the two matrices: \( U U^T = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{8}{9} & -\frac{2}{9} & \frac{2}{9} \\ -\frac{2}{9} & \frac{5}{9} & \frac{4}{9} \\ \frac{2}{9} & \frac{4}{9} & \frac{5}{9} \end{bmatrix} \).
9. Verify correctness: Each entry is the sum of corresponding entries from steps 6 and 7, confirming the calculation.
10. The projection operator onto \( W \) is \( \text{proj}_W \mathbf{y} = U U^T \mathbf{y} \).
11. Compute \( U^T \mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} & \frac{2}{3} \\ -\frac{2}{3} & \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 8 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} \cdot 4 + \frac{1}{3} \cdot 8 + \frac{2}{3} \cdot 1 \\ -\frac{2}{3} \cdot 4 + \frac{2}{3} \cdot 8 + \frac{1}{3} \cdot 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{8}{3} + \frac{8}{3} + \frac{2}{3} \\ -\frac{8}{3} + \frac{16}{3} + \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} \).
12. Compute \( U (U^T \mathbf{y}) = U \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} = 6 \mathbf{u}_1 + 3 \mathbf{u}_2 = 6 \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{1}{3} \\ \frac{2}{3} \end{bmatrix} + 3 \begin{bmatrix} -\frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 2 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} -2 \\ 2 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 4 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix} \).
13. Thus, \( \text{proj}_W \mathbf{y} = (U U^T) \mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 4 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix} \).
