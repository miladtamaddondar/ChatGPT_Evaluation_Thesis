1. Let \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 9 \\ 4 & 5 \end{bmatrix} \) and assume \( A = LU \) where \( L = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ l_{21} & 1 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( U = \begin{bmatrix} u_{11} & u_{12} \\ 0 & u_{22} \end{bmatrix} \).
2. Set \( u_{11} = 6 \) and \( u_{12} = 9 \) from the first row of \( A \), so \( U = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 9 \\ 0 & u_{22} \end{bmatrix} \).
3. Solve for \( l_{21} \) using the first column of the second row: \( 4 = l_{21} \cdot 6 \implies l_{21} = \frac{4}{6} = \frac{2}{3} \).
4. Solve for \( u_{22} \) using the second row, second column: \( 5 = l_{21} \cdot 9 + u_{22} \implies 5 = \frac{2}{3} \cdot 9 + u_{22} \implies 5 = 6 + u_{22} \implies u_{22} = -1 \).
5. The LU factorization is \( L = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ \frac{2}{3} & 1 \end{bmatrix} \), \( U = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 9 \\ 0 & -1 \end{bmatrix} \).
