1. The matrix \( A \) is given as \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ -3 & 9 & -1 & 5 \\ 2 & -6 & 4 & -3 \\ -4 & 12 & 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix} \).
2. The echelon form of \( A \) is \( \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 & -7 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \).
3. The pivot columns in the echelon form are columns 1, 3, and 4, so the basis for \( \operatorname{Col} A \) is the set of columns 1, 3, and 4 of \( A \): \( \left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} -4 \\ 5 \\ -3 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \right\} \).
4. The number of pivot columns is 3, so \( \dim(\operatorname{Col} A) = 3 \).
5. To find a basis for \( \operatorname{Nul} A \), set up the equation \( A\vec{x} = 0 \) and write the system using the echelon form: \( x_1 - 3x_2 + 2x_3 - 4x_4 = 0 \), \( 5x_3 - 7x_4 = 0 \), \( 5x_4 = 0 \).
6. From the third equation, \( x_4 = 0 \); from the second, \( 5x_3 = 0 \implies x_3 = 0 \); from the first, \( x_1 - 3x_2 = 0 \implies x_1 = 3x_2 \).
7. Letting \( x_2 = t \), the general solution is \( \vec{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 3t \\ t \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} = t \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \), so the basis for \( \operatorname{Nul} A \) is \( \left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \right\} \).
8. There is one free variable, so \( \dim(\operatorname{Nul} A) = 1 \).
