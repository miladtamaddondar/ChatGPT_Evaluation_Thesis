1. Find the eigenvalues of \( A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix} \) by solving \( \det(A - \lambda I) = 0 \): \(\det\left(\begin{pmatrix} 1-\lambda & -2 \\ 1 & 3-\lambda \end{pmatrix}\right) = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0\).
2. Solve the characteristic equation: \( \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0 \implies \lambda = 2 \pm i \).
3. For \( \lambda = 2+i \), solve \( (A - \lambda I)\vec{v} = 0 \): \( \begin{pmatrix} 1-(2+i) & -2 \\ 1 & 3-(2+i) \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix} \).
4. Set up the system: \( (-1-i)x - 2y = 0 \) and \( x + (1-i)y = 0 \). From the second equation, \( x = -(1-i)y \).
5. Substitute \( x \) into the first equation: \( (-1-i)(-(1-i)y) - 2y = (1+i)(1-i)y - 2y = (1^2 - i^2)y - 2y = (1-(-1))y - 2y = 2y - 2y = 0 \), so any nonzero \( y \) gives an eigenvector.
6. Choose \( y = 1 \), then \( x = -(1-i) \), so an eigenvector is \( \vec{v}_1 = \begin{pmatrix} -(1-i) \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \).
7. The complex conjugate eigenvalue \( 2-i \) gives eigenvector \( \vec{v}_2 = \begin{pmatrix} -(1+i) \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \).
8. Form \( P = \begin{pmatrix} -(1-i) & -(1+i) \\ 1 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \) and \( C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \) (since \( C \) is of the form \( \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix} \) with \( a = 2, b = 1 \)).
9. Final answer: \( P = \begin{pmatrix} -(1-i) & -(1+i) \\ 1 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \), \( C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \), so \( A = P C P^{-1} \).
