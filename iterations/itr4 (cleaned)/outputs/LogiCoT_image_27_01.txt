Since \( T \) is linear and \( T(\mathbf{e}_1) = \mathbf{y}_1 \), \( T(\mathbf{e}_2) = \mathbf{y}_2 \), the matrix of \( T \) in the standard basis is \( A = [\mathbf{y}_1 \ \mathbf{y}_2] = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 5 & 6 \end{bmatrix} \); this follows because the columns of \( A \) are the images of the basis vectors.  
2. To find \( T\left(\begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix}\right) \), compute \( A \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} = 5\mathbf{y}_1 + (-3)\mathbf{y}_2 \) by linearity.  
3. Calculate: \( 5\mathbf{y}_1 = 5\begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 10 \\ 25 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( -3\mathbf{y}_2 = -3\begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ -18 \end{bmatrix} \); this uses scalar multiplication.  
4. Add the results: \( \begin{bmatrix} 10 \\ 25 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ -18 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 13 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \); this uses vector addition.  
5. Therefore, \( T\left(\begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix}\right) = \begin{bmatrix} 13 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \).  
6. For a general vector \( \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} \), \( T\left(\begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix}\right) = x_1\mathbf{y}_1 + x_2\mathbf{y}_2 \) by linearity.  
7. Substitute: \( x_1\mathbf{y}_1 = x_1\begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2x_1 \\ 5x_1 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( x_2\mathbf{y}_2 = x_2\begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -x_2 \\ 6x_2 \end{bmatrix} \).  
8. Add: \( \begin{bmatrix} 2x_1 \\ 5x_1 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} -x_2 \\ 6x_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2x_1 - x_2 \\ 5x_1 + 6x_2 \end{bmatrix} \).  
9. Therefore, \( T\left(\begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix}\right) = \begin{bmatrix} 2x_1 - x_2 \\ 5x_1 + 6x_2 \end{bmatrix} \).
