1. The original matrix is \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ -3 & 9 & -1 & 5 \\ 2 & -6 & 4 & -3 \\ -4 & 12 & 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix} \).
2. The echelon form of \( A \) is \( \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 & -7 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \).
3. The pivot columns in the echelon form are columns 1, 3, and 4, so a basis for \( \text{Col}\,A \) is \( \left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} -4 \\ 5 \\ -3 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \right\} \).
4. To find a basis for \( \text{Nul}\,A \), solve \( A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0} \) using the echelon form: Let \( x_2 = t \), \( x_4 = s \), then back-substitute to get \( x_3 = \frac{7}{5}s \), \( x_1 = 3t - 2x_3 + 4s \). This gives the general solution \( \mathbf{x} = t\begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} + s\begin{bmatrix} -\frac{18}{5} \\ 0 \\ \frac{7}{5} \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \), so a basis for \( \text{Nul}\,A \) is \( \left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} -\frac{18}{5} \\ 0 \\ \frac{7}{5} \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \right\} \).
5. The dimension of \( \text{Col}\,A \) is 3, and the dimension of \( \text{Nul}\,A \) is 1.
