1. We are given \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 9 \\ 1 & 7 \\ -3 & -5 \\ 1 & 5 \end{bmatrix}\) and \(Q = \begin{bmatrix} 5/6 & -1/6 \\ 1/6 & 5/6 \\ -3/6 & 1/6 \\ 1/6 & 3/6 \end{bmatrix}\), and need to find upper triangular \(R\) such that \(A = QR\).  
2. Since \(Q\) has orthonormal columns (from Gram-Schmidt), \(R = Q^T A\) (since \(Q^T Q = I\)), and \(R\) will be upper triangular.  
3. Compute \(Q^T\): \(Q^T = \begin{bmatrix} 5/6 & 1/6 & -3/6 & 1/6 \\ -1/6 & 5/6 & 1/6 & 3/6 \end{bmatrix}\).  
4. Compute \(R = Q^T A\):   \[ R_{1,1} = (5/6) \cdot 5 + (1/6) \cdot 1 + (-3/6) \cdot (-3) + (1/6) \cdot 1 = (25/6) + (1/6) + (9/6) + (1/6) = 36/6 = 6 \] \[ R_{1,2} = (5/6) \cdot 9 + (1/6) \cdot 7 + (-3/6) \cdot (-5) + (1/6) \cdot 5 = (45/6) + (7/6) + (15/6) + (5/6) = 72/6 = 12 \] \[ R_{2,1} = (-1/6) \cdot 5 + (5/6) \cdot 1 + (1/6) \cdot (-3) + (3/6) \cdot 1 = (-5/6) + (5/6) + (-3/6) + (3/6) = 0/6 = 0 \] \[ R_{2,2} = (-1/6) \cdot 9 + (5/6) \cdot 7 + (1/6) \cdot (-5) + (3/6) \cdot 5 = (-9/6) + (35/6) + (-5/6) + (15/6) = (36/6) = 6 \] So \(R = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 12 \\ 0 & 6 \end{bmatrix}\).  
5. Verify correctness: Multiply \(Q\) and \(R\):   \[ QR = \begin{bmatrix} 5/6 & -1/6 \\ 1/6 & 5/6 \\ -3/6 & 1/6 \\ 1/6 & 3/6 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 12 \\ 0 & 6 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} (5/6) \cdot 6 + (-1/6) \cdot 0 & (5/6) \cdot 12 + (-1/6) \cdot 6 \\ (1/6) \cdot 6 + (5/6) \cdot 0 & (1/6) \cdot 12 + (5/6) \cdot 6 \\ (-3/6) \cdot 6 + (1/6) \cdot 0 & (-3/6) \cdot 12 + (1/6) \cdot 6 \\ (1/6) \cdot 6 + (3/6) \cdot 0 & (1/6) \cdot 12 + (3/6) \cdot 6 \end{bmatrix} \] \[ = \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 9 \\ 1 & 7 \\ -3 & -5 \\ 1 & 5 \end{bmatrix} = A \]
6. Final answer: The upper triangular matrix \(R\) is \(\boxed{\begin{bmatrix} 6 & 12 \\ 0 & 6 \end{bmatrix}}\), and \(A = QR\) is verified.
