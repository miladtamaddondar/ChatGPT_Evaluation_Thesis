1. To factor \( A = \begin{bmatrix}6 & 9 \\ 4 & 5\end{bmatrix} \) as \( LU \), we first write \( L = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 \\ l_{21} & 1\end{bmatrix} \) and \( U = \begin{bmatrix}u_{11} & u_{12} \\ 0 & u_{22}\end{bmatrix} \).
2. Set \( u_{11} = 6 \) and \( u_{12} = 9 \) from the first row of \( A \).
3. Solve for \( l_{21} \) using \( l_{21} \cdot u_{11} = 4 \) so \( l_{21} = \frac{4}{6} = \frac{2}{3} \).
4. Solve for \( u_{22} \) using \( l_{21} \cdot u_{12} + u_{22} = 5 \) so \( \frac{2}{3} \cdot 9 + u_{22} = 5 \) which gives \( 6 + u_{22} = 5 \) so \( u_{22} = -1 \).
5. Thus, \( L = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 \\ \frac{2}{3} & 1\end{bmatrix} \) and \( U = \begin{bmatrix}6 & 9 \\ 0 & -1\end{bmatrix} \).
6. The LU factorization of \( A \) is \( \begin{bmatrix}6 & 9 \\ 4 & 5\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 \\ \frac{2}{3} & 1\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}6 & 9 \\ 0 & -1\end{bmatrix} \).
