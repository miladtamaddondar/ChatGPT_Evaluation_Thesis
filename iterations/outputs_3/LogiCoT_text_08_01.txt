To construct a \( 4 \times 3 \) matrix of rank 1, recall that such a matrix can be written as the outer product of a nonzero \( 4 \times 1 \) column vector and a nonzero \( 1 \times 3 \) row vector: let \( \mathbf{u} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ -1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( \mathbf{v}^T = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & -2 & 5 \end{bmatrix} \), so the matrix is \( A = \mathbf{u} \mathbf{v}^T \). 
2. Compute \( A \): \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ -1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 3 & -2 & 5 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \times 3 & 1 \times (-2) & 1 \times 5 \\ 2 \times 3 & 2 \times (-2) & 2 \times 5 \\ -1 \times 3 & -1 \times (-2) & -1 \times 5 \\ 0 \times 3 & 0 \times (-2) & 0 \times 5 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & -2 & 5 \\ 6 & -4 & 10 \\ -3 & 2 & -5 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \). 
3. Verify the rank: since every row is a scalar multiple of the first row (e.g., row 2 is 2 times row 1, row 3 is -1 times row 1, row 4 is 0 times row 1), the rows are linearly dependent and only one is linearly independent, so the rank is 1. 
4. Check using the definition: the largest nonzero minor is \( 1 \times 1 \), since any \( 2 \times 2 \) minor is zero (e.g., \( \begin{vmatrix} 3 & -2 \\ 6 & -4 \end{vmatrix} = 3 \times (-4) - 6 \times (-2) = -12 + 12 = 0 \)), confirming rank 1. 
5. Final answer: \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & -2 & 5 \\ 6 & -4 & 10 \\ -3 & 2 & -5 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \) is a \( 4 \times 3 \) matrix of rank 1.
