1. To find a vector $\mathbf{x}$ such that $T(\mathbf{x}) = A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{b}$, set up the equation: $\begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 & -2 \\ -2 & 1 & 6 \\ 3 & -2 & -5\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}-1 \\ 7 \\ -3\end{bmatrix}$.

2. Write the corresponding system of linear equations: $1x_1 + 0x_2 - 2x_3 = -1$, $-2x_1 + 1x_2 + 6x_3 = 7$, $3x_1 - 2x_2 - 5x_3 = -3$.

3. Express $x_1$ from the first equation: $x_1 - 2x_3 = -1 \implies x_1 = -1 + 2x_3$.

4. Substitute $x_1$ into the second equation: $-2(-1 + 2x_3) + x_2 + 6x_3 = 7 \implies 2 - 4x_3 + x_2 + 6x_3 = 7 \implies x_2 + 2x_3 = 5 \implies x_2 = 5 - 2x_3$.

5. Substitute $x_1$ and $x_2$ into the third equation: $3(-1 + 2x_3) - 2(5 - 2x_3) - 5x_3 = -3 \implies -3 + 6x_3 - 10 + 4x_3 - 5x_3 = -3 \implies (6x_3 + 4x_3 - 5x_3) + (-3 - 10) = -3 \implies (5x_3) - 13 = -3$.

6. Solve for $x_3$: $5x_3 - 13 = -3 \implies 5x_3 = 10 \implies x_3 = 2$.

7. Substitute $x_3 = 2$ into previous expressions: $x_1 = -1 + 2(2) = 3$, $x_2 = 5 - 2(2) = 1$.

8. The solution is $\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix}3 \\ 1 \\ 2\end{bmatrix}$.

9. To determine uniqueness, check if $A$ is invertible by computing its determinant: $\det(A) = 1\begin{vmatrix}1 & 6 \\ -2 & -5\end{vmatrix} - 0\begin{vmatrix}-2 & 6 \\ 3 & -5\end{vmatrix} - 2\begin{vmatrix}-2 & 1 \\ 3 & -2\end{vmatrix} = 1((1)(-5) - (6)(-2)) - 2((-2)(-2) - (1)(3)) = 1(-5 + 12) - 2(4 - 3) = 1(7) - 2(1) = 7 - 2 = 5$.

10. Since $\det(A) = 5 \neq 0$, $A$ is invertible and the solution $\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix}3 \\ 1 \\ 2\end{bmatrix}$ is unique.
