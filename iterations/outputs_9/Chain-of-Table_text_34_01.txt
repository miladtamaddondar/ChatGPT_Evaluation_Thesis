1. Write the vectors as columns of a matrix: $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & -3 & 1 \\ -3 & 2 & 1 & -8 & -6 \\ 2 & -3 & 6 & 7 & 9 \end{bmatrix}$.
2. Swap row 1 and row 2 to get a leading 1 in the top left: $\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 & -4 & -3 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 & -3 & 2 & 2 \\ -3 & 2 & 1 & -8 & -6 \\ 2 & -3 & 6 & 7 & 9 \end{bmatrix}$.
3. Swap row 1 and row 2 to restore the original order (since row 2 already had a leading 1): $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 2 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & -3 & 1 \\ -3 & 2 & 1 & -8 & -6 \\ 2 & -3 & 6 & 7 & 9 \end{bmatrix}$.
4. Add 3 times row 1 to row 3: $R_3 \leftarrow R_3 + 3R_1$: $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 2 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & -3 & 1 \\ 0 & 2 & -8 & -2 & 0 \\ 2 & -3 & 6 & 7 & 9 \end{bmatrix}$.
5. Subtract 2 times row 1 from row 4: $R_4 \leftarrow R_4 - 2R_1$: $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 2 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & -3 & 1 \\ 0 & 2 & -8 & -2 & 0 \\ 0 & -3 & 12 & 3 & 5 \end{bmatrix}$.
6. Subtract 2 times row 2 from row 3: $R_3 \leftarrow R_3 - 2R_2$: $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 2 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & -3 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 4 & -2 \\ 0 & -3 & 12 & 3 & 5 \end{bmatrix}$.
7. Add 3 times row 2 to row 4: $R_4 \leftarrow R_4 + 3R_2$: $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 2 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & -3 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 4 & -2 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & -6 & 8 \end{bmatrix}$.
8. Add $\frac{3}{2}$ times row 3 to row 4: $R_4 \leftarrow R_4 + \frac{3}{2}R_3$: $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 2 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & -3 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 4 & -2 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 5 \end{bmatrix}$.
9. The matrix is now in row-echelon form. The pivot columns are columns 1, 2, 4, and 5, so a basis for the space spanned by the vectors is $\left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ -3 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \\ 2 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ -8 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \\ -6 \\ 9 \end{bmatrix} \right\}$.
