1. To find the orthogonal projection of \(\mathbf{b}\) onto \(\text{Col }A\), we use the formula \(\text{proj}_{\text{Col }A}(\mathbf{b}) = A(A^TA)^{-1}A^T\mathbf{b}\); this formula projects \(\mathbf{b}\) onto the column space of \(A\) using properties of orthogonal projections in linear algebra.  
2. Compute \(A^T\): \(A^T = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix}\); this is the transpose of \(A\), which is needed for the projection formula.  
3. Compute \(A^TA\): \(A^TA = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 3 & 1 \\ -2 & 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1^2 + 3^2 + (-2)^2 & 1\cdot5 + 3\cdot1 + (-2)\cdot4 \\ 5\cdot1 + 1\cdot3 + 4\cdot(-2) & 5^2 + 1^2 + 4^2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1+9+4 & 5+3-8 \\ 5+3-8 & 25+1+16 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 14 & 0 \\ 0 & 42 \end{bmatrix}\); this uses matrix multiplication and verifies by checking each entry.  
4. Compute \((A^TA)^{-1}\): Since \(A^TA\) is diagonal, \((A^TA)^{-1} = \begin{bmatrix} 1/14 & 0 \\ 0 & 1/42 \end{bmatrix}\); the inverse of a diagonal matrix is the reciprocal of each diagonal entry.  
5. Compute \(A^T\mathbf{b}\): \(A^T\mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ -2 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1\cdot4 + 3\cdot(-2) + (-2)\cdot(-3) \\ 5\cdot4 + 1\cdot(-2) + 4\cdot(-3) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 - 6 + 6 \\ 20 - 2 - 12 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix}\); this is standard matrix-vector multiplication.  
6. Compute \((A^TA)^{-1}A^T\mathbf{b}\): \(\begin{bmatrix} 1/14 & 0 \\ 0 & 1/42 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4/14 \\ 6/42 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2/7 \\ 1/7 \end{bmatrix}\); this is diagonal matrix multiplication.  
7. Compute \(A((A^TA)^{-1}A^T\mathbf{b})\): \(A\begin{bmatrix} 2/7 \\ 1/7 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 3 & 1 \\ -2 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 2/7 \\ 1/7 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1\cdot(2/7) + 5\cdot(1/7) \\ 3\cdot(2/7) + 1\cdot(1/7) \\ -2\cdot(2/7) + 4\cdot(1/7) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2/7 + 5/7 \\ 6/7 + 1/7 \\ -4/7 + 4/7 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}\); this is matrix-vector multiplication.  
8. The orthogonal projection of \(\mathbf{b}\) onto \(\text{Col }A\) is \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}\); this follows from the previous computation and matches the projection formula.  
9. A least-squares solution to \(A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{b}\) is \(\mathbf{x} = (A^TA)^{-1}A^T\mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 2/7 \\ 1/7 \end{bmatrix}\); this is the standard least-squares formula and matches our calculations.
