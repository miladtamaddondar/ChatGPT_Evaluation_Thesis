Express \( T(1) \) in terms of the basis: \( T(1) = 3 \cdot 1 + 5 \cdot t + 0 \cdot t^2 \), so the coordinate vector is \( \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 5 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \). 4. Compute \( T(t) \): Let \( a_0 = 0, a_1 = 1, a_2 = 0 \), so \( T(t) = 3(0) + (5 \cdot 0 - 2 \cdot 1)t + (4 \cdot 1 + 0)t^2 = -2t + 4t^2 \). 5. Express \( T(t) \) in terms of the basis: \( T(t) = 0 \cdot 1 + (-2) \cdot t + 4 \cdot t^2 \), so the coordinate vector is \( \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ -2 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} \). 6. Compute \( T(t^2) \): Let \( a_0 = 0, a_1 = 0, a_2 = 1 \), so \( T(t^2) = 3(0) + (5 \cdot 0 - 2 \cdot 0)t + (4 \cdot 0 + 1)t^2 = t^2 \). 7. Express \( T(t^2) \) in terms of the basis: \( T(t^2) = 0 \cdot 1 + 0 \cdot t + 1 \cdot t^2 \), so the coordinate vector is \( \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \). 8. The matrix representation of \( T \) relative to \( \mathcal{B} \) is formed by placing these coordinate vectors as columns: \( [T]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 0 \\ 5 & -2 & 0 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \).
