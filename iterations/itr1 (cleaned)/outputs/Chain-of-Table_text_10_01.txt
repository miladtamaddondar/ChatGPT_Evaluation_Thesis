1. Display the given matrix $A$: $A = \begin{bmatrix}1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ 3 & 9 & -1 & 5 \\ 2 & -6 & 4 & -3 \\ -4 & 12 & 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix}$.
2. The echelon form of $A$ is: $\begin{bmatrix}1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 & -7 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}$.
3. The pivot columns in the echelon form are columns 1, 3, and 4, so a basis for $\text{Col } A$ is $\left\{ \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 3 \\ 2 \\ -4\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}2 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 2\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}-4 \\ 5 \\ -3 \\ 7\end{bmatrix} \right\}$.
4. To find a basis for $\text{Nul } A$, set up $A\vec{x} = \vec{0}$ and write the system from the echelon form: $x_1 - 3x_2 + 2x_3 - 4x_4 = 0$, $5x_3 - 7x_4 = 0$, $5x_4 = 0$.
5. From $5x_4 = 0$, get $x_4 = 0$; from $5x_3 - 7x_4 = 0$, get $x_3 = 0$; from $x_1 - 3x_2 + 2x_3 - 4x_4 = 0$, get $x_1 = 3x_2$.
6. The general solution is $\vec{x} = x_2\begin{bmatrix}3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0\end{bmatrix}$, so a basis for $\text{Nul } A$ is $\left\{ \begin{bmatrix}3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0\end{bmatrix} \right\}$.
7. The dimension of $\text{Col } A$ is $3$ (number of pivot columns), and the dimension of $\text{Nul } A$ is $1$ (number of free variables).
