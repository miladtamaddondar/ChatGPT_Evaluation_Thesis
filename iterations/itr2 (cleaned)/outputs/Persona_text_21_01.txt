2. To solve $A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{b}$, we write the augmented matrix $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c}1 & 0 & -2 & -1 \\ -2 & 1 & 6 & 7 \\ 3 & -2 & -5 & -3\end{array}\right]$.  
3. We use row operations to reduce the matrix: First, leave Row 1 as is: $[1\ 0\ -2\ |\ -1]$.  
4. Add $2$ times Row 1 to Row 2: $-2 + 2(1) = 0$, $1 + 2(0) = 1$, $6 + 2(-2) = 2$, $7 + 2(-1) = 5$, so Row 2 becomes $[0\ 1\ 2\ |\ 5]$.  
5. Subtract $3$ times Row 1 from Row 3: $3 - 3(1) = 0$, $-2 - 3(0) = -2$, $-5 - 3(-2) = -5 + 6 = 1$, $-3 - 3(-1) = -3 + 3 = 0$, so Row 3 becomes $[0\ -2\ 1\ |\ 0]$.  
6. Next, add $2$ times Row 2 to Row 3: $-2 + 2(1) = 0$, $1 + 2(2) = 5$, $0 + 2(5) = 10$, so Row 3 is $[0\ 0\ 5\ |\ 10]$.

8. Now back substitute: Row 2 is $[0\ 1\ 2\ |\ 5]$, so $x_2 + 2x_3 = 5$, with $x_3 = 2$, so $x_2 + 2(2) = 5$ thus $x_2 = 1$.  
9. Row 1 is $[1\ 0\ -2\ |\ -1]$, so $x_1 - 2x_3 = -1$, with $x_3 = 2$, so $x_1 - 4 = -1$ thus $x_1 = 3$.  
10. Therefore, the solution is $\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix}3 \\ 1 \\ 2\end{bmatrix}$.  
11. Since we obtained a unique solution with a pivot in every column, $\mathbf{x}$ is unique.
