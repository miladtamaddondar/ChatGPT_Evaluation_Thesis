1. Find the eigenvalues of $A$: $\det\left(\begin{pmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix} - \lambda I\right) = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0 \implies \lambda = 2 \pm i$.
2. Find an eigenvector for $\lambda = 2+i$: Solve $\left(\begin{pmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix} - (2+i)I\right)\vec{v} = 0 \implies \begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix}\vec{v} = 0$.
3. Let $v_1 = 1$, then $(-1-i)\cdot 1 - 2v_2 = 0 \implies v_2 = \frac{-1-i}{2}$, so eigenvector is $\vec{v} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ \frac{-1-i}{2} \end{pmatrix}$.
4. Separate real and imaginary parts: $\vec{v} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ \frac{-1}{2} - \frac{1}{2}i \end{pmatrix} = \vec{v}_r + i\vec{v}_i$ where $\vec{v}_r = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ -\frac{1}{2} \end{pmatrix}$, $\vec{v}_i = \begin{pmatrix} 0 \\ -\frac{1}{2} \end{pmatrix}$.
5. Construct $P$ from $\vec{v}_r$ and $\vec{v}_i$: $P = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ -\frac{1}{2} & -\frac{1}{2} \end{pmatrix}$.
6. The canonical form $C = \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix}$ with $a = 2$, $b = 1$ (since eigenvalues are $2 \pm i$), so $C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}$.
7. Final answer: $A = P C P^{-1}$ where $P = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ -\frac{1}{2} & -\frac{1}{2} \end{pmatrix}$ and $C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}$.
