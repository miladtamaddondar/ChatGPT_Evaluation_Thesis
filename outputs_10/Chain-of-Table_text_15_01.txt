1. Find the eigenvalues of $A$ by solving $\det(A - \lambda I) = 0$: $\det\begin{pmatrix} 1-\lambda & -2 \\ 1 & 3-\lambda \end{pmatrix} = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0$.
2. Solve for $\lambda$: $\lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0 \implies \lambda = 2 \pm i$.
3. Let $C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}$, since $a = 2$, $b = 1$ from the eigenvalue $2+i$.
4. Find the eigenvector for $\lambda = 2+i$: $(A - (2+i)I)\vec{v} = 0 \implies \begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix} = 0$.
5. From the first row: $(-1-i)x - 2y = 0 \implies x = -\frac{2}{-1-i}y = \frac{2}{1+i}y$.
6. Rationalize denominator: $\frac{2}{1+i} = \frac{2(1-i)}{(1+i)(1-i)} = \frac{2(1-i)}{2} = 1-i$.
7. Thus, $x = (1-i)y$, so an eigenvector is $\vec{v} = \begin{pmatrix} 1-i \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}$.
8. Use real and imaginary parts to form $P$: $\vec{v}_1 = \operatorname{Re}\vec{v} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}$, $\vec{v}_2 = \operatorname{Im}\vec{v} = \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}$, so $P = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}$.
9. Therefore, $A = P C P^{-1}$ with $P = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}$ and $C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}$.
