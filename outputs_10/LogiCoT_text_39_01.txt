1. Recognize that $\mathbf{u}\mathbf{v}^T$ is the outer product of a $2 \times 1$ vector $\mathbf{u}$ and a $3 \times 1$ vector $\mathbf{v}$, resulting in a $2 \times 3$ matrix; thus, $\mathbf{u}\mathbf{v}^T = \begin{bmatrix} u_1 v_1 & u_1 v_2 & u_1 v_3 \\ u_2 v_1 & u_2 v_2 & u_2 v_3 \end{bmatrix}$, where $\mathbf{u} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}$ and $\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} v_1 \\ v_2 \\ v_3 \end{bmatrix}$. 2. Substitute $\mathbf{u}$ into the outer product to get $\mathbf{u}\mathbf{v}^T = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \cdot v_1 & 1 \cdot v_2 & 1 \cdot v_3 \\ 2 \cdot v_1 & 2 \cdot v_2 & 2 \cdot v_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} v_1 & v_2 & v_3 \\ 2v_1 & 2v_2 & 2v_3 \end{bmatrix}$. 3. Set this equal to the given matrix: $\begin{bmatrix} v_1 & v_2 & v_3 \\ 2v_1 & 2v_2 & 2v_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 4 \\ 2 & -6 & 8 \end{bmatrix}$. 4. Equate corresponding entries to get the system: $v_1 = 1$, $v_2 = -3$, $v_3 = 4$, $2v_1 = 2$, $2v_2 = -6$, $2v_3 = 8$. 5. Verify that $v_1 = 1$, $v_2 = -3$, $v_3 = 4$ satisfy all equations: $2 \cdot 1 = 2$, $2 \cdot (-3) = -6$, $2 \cdot 4 = 8$, which matches the second row of the matrix. 6. Conclude that the solution is $\boxed{\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix}}$.
