To find a basis for $\mathbb{R}^2$ consisting of $\mathbf{v}_1$ and another eigenvector $\mathbf{v}_2$ of $A$, first note that $\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix}3/7 \\ 4/7\end{bmatrix}$ is already an eigenvector, so we need to find another eigenvector $\mathbf{v}_2$ corresponding to a different eigenvalue.  
2. Find the eigenvalues of $A$ by solving $\det(A - \lambda I) = 0$: $\det\left(\begin{bmatrix}0.6-\lambda & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.7-\lambda\end{bmatrix}\right) = (0.6-\lambda)(0.7-\lambda) - (0.4)(0.3) = 0$.  
3. Expand the determinant: $(0.6-\lambda)(0.7-\lambda) - 0.12 = 0 \implies 0.42 - 0.6\lambda - 0.7\lambda + \lambda^2 - 0.12 = 0 \implies \lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3 = 0$.  
4. Solve the quadratic equation: $\lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3 = 0 \implies \lambda = \frac{1.3 \pm \sqrt{(1.3)^2 - 4 \cdot 0.3}}{2} = \frac{1.3 \pm \sqrt{1.69 - 1.2}}{2} = \frac{1.3 \pm \sqrt{0.49}}{2} = \frac{1.3 \pm 0.7}{2}$.  
5. Thus, the eigenvalues are $\lambda_1 = \frac{1.3 + 0.7}{2} = 1$ and $\lambda_2 = \frac{1.3 - 0.7}{2} = 0.3$.  
6. Since $\mathbf{v}_1$ is already given, it must correspond to $\lambda_1 = 1$ (since $A$ is stochastic and $\mathbf{v}_1$ has positive entries summing to 1), so we now find an eigenvector $\mathbf{v}_2$ corresponding to $\lambda_2 = 0.3$.  
7. Solve $(A - 0.3I)\mathbf{v}_2 = 0$: $A - 0.3I = \begin{bmatrix}0.6-0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.7-0.3\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.4\end{bmatrix}$.  
8. Set up the system: $\begin{bmatrix}0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.4\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}x \\ y\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0 \\ 0\end{bmatrix}$, which gives $0.3x + 0.3y = 0$ and $0.4x + 0.4y = 0$.  
9. Both equations are equivalent, so $x + y = 0 \implies y = -x$, so an eigenvector is $\mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}$ (or any nonzero scalar multiple).  
10. Therefore, a basis for $\mathbb{R}^2$ consisting of $\mathbf{v}_1$ and another eigenvector $\mathbf{v}_2$ of $A$ is $\left\{\begin{bmatrix}3/7 \\ 4/7\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}\right\}$.
