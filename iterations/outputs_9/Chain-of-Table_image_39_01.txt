1. Write the equation for the matrix equality: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 4 \\ 2 & -6 & 8 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \mathbf{v}^T\).
2. Let \(\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} v_1 \\ v_2 \\ v_3 \end{bmatrix}\), so \(\mathbf{u}\mathbf{v}^T = \begin{bmatrix} 1v_1 & 1v_2 & 1v_3 \\ 2v_1 & 2v_2 & 2v_3 \end{bmatrix}\).
3. Set up the system by equating corresponding entries: \(1 = v_1\), \(-3 = v_2\), \(4 = v_3\), \(2 = 2v_1\), \(-6 = 2v_2\), \(8 = 2v_3\).
4. Solve for \(v_1, v_2, v_3\) using the first row: \(v_1 = 1\), \(v_2 = -3\), \(v_3 = 4\).
5. Verify with the second row: \(2v_1 = 2 \implies v_1 = 1\), \(2v_2 = -6 \implies v_2 = -3\), \(2v_3 = 8 \implies v_3 = 4\).
6. Final answer: \(\boxed{\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix}}\).
