1. Find the eigenvalues of \(A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix}\): Solve \(\det(A - \lambda I) = 0\), so \(\det\begin{pmatrix} 1-\lambda & -2 \\ 1 & 3-\lambda \end{pmatrix} = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0\).  
2. Solve for \(\lambda\): \(\lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0 \implies \lambda = \frac{4 \pm \sqrt{16-20}}{2} = \frac{4 \pm 2i}{2} = 2 \pm i\).  
3. Find an eigenvector for \(\lambda = 2 + i\): Solve \((A - (2+i)I)\vec{v} = 0\), so \(A - (2+i)I = \begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix}\).  
4. Set up the system: \((-1-i)x - 2y = 0\) and \(x + (1-i)y = 0\).  
5. Express \(x\) in terms of \(y\) from the second equation: \(x = -(1-i)y\).  
6. Substitute into the first equation: \((-1-i)(-(1-i)y) - 2y = (1-i^2 + i - i^2)y - 2y = (1+1 + i + i)y - 2y = (2 + 2i - 2)y = (2i)y = 0\), which is satisfied for any \(y\), so choose \(y = 1\), then \(x = -(1-i)\).  
7. Thus, the eigenvector is \(\vec{v}_1 = \begin{pmatrix} -(1-i) \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}\).  
8. The complex conjugate eigenvector for \(\lambda = 2 - i\) is \(\vec{v}_2 = \begin{pmatrix} -(1+i) \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}\).  
9. Form \(P\) from the real and imaginary parts of \(\vec{v}_1\): \(\vec{v}_1 = \begin{pmatrix} -1 + i \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} + i\begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}\), so \(P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}\).  
10. The corresponding \(C\) matrix is \(\begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix}\) with \(a = 2, b = 1\), so \(C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}\).  
11. Therefore, the answer is: \(P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}\), \(C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}\), and \(A = P C P^{-1}\).
