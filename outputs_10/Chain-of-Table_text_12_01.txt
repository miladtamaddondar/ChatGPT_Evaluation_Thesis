1. Write the matrix \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \) and set up the characteristic polynomial \( \det(A - \lambda I) = 0 \): \( \det\left(\begin{bmatrix} 2-\lambda & 1 \\ -1 & 4-\lambda \end{bmatrix}\right) = 0 \).
2. Compute the determinant: \( (2-\lambda)(4-\lambda) - (1)(-1) = (2-\lambda)(4-\lambda) + 1 \).
3. Expand the determinant: \( (2-\lambda)(4-\lambda) + 1 = 8 - 2\lambda - 4\lambda + \lambda^2 + 1 = \lambda^2 - 6\lambda + 9 \).
4. Write the characteristic polynomial: \( \lambda^2 - 6\lambda + 9 = 0 \).
5. Factor the polynomial: \( (\lambda - 3)^2 = 0 \).
6. Solve for the eigenvalues: \( \lambda = 3 \) (with algebraic multiplicity 2).
