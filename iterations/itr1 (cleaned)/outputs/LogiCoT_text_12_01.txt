1. Write the matrix \(A = \begin{bmatrix}2 & 1 \\ -1 & 4\end{bmatrix}\) and recall the characteristic polynomial is \(\det(A - \lambda I)\). 
2. Compute \(A - \lambda I = \begin{bmatrix}2-\lambda & 1 \\ -1 & 4-\lambda\end{bmatrix}\). 
3. Calculate the determinant: \((2-\lambda)(4-\lambda) - (-1)(1) = (2-\lambda)(4-\lambda) + 1\). 
4. Expand \((2-\lambda)(4-\lambda) = 8 - 2\lambda - 4\lambda + \lambda^2 = \lambda^2 - 6\lambda + 8\). 
5. Add 1 to get the characteristic polynomial: \(\lambda^2 - 6\lambda + 9\). 
6. Set the characteristic polynomial to zero: \(\lambda^2 - 6\lambda + 9 = 0\). 
7. Factor the quadratic: \((\lambda - 3)^2 = 0\). 
8. The eigenvalues are \(\lambda = 3\) (with algebraic multiplicity 2).
