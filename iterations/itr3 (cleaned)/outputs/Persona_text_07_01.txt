1. The matrix \(\left[ Q r_1 \; Q r_2 \; \dots \; Q r_p \right]\) consists of the images of the vectors \(r_1, \dots, r_p\) under the linear transformation represented by \(Q\).  
2. We can express this as the matrix product \(Q \left[ r_1 \; r_2 \; \dots \; r_p \right]\), where \(\left[ r_1 \; r_2 \; \dots \; r_p \right]\) is the \(n \times p\) matrix whose columns are the vectors \(r_1, \dots, r_p\).  
3. Therefore, the answer is: \(\left[ Q r_1 \; Q r_2 \; \dots \; Q r_p \right] = Q \left[ r_1 \; r_2 \; \dots \; r_p \right]\).
