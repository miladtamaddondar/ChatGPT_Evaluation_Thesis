1. To write $\mathbf{y}$ as the sum of a vector in $W$ and a vector orthogonal to $W$, we need to project $\mathbf{y}$ onto $W$ and subtract this projection from $\mathbf{y}$: $\mathbf{y} = \mathbf{y}_W + \mathbf{y}_{W^\perp}$.
2. Let $\mathbf{y}_W = a_1\mathbf{u}_1 + a_2\mathbf{u}_2 + a_3\mathbf{u}_3$ be the projection of $\mathbf{y}$ onto $W$.
3. Set up the equation $\mathbf{y}_W = a_1\mathbf{u}_1 + a_2\mathbf{u}_2 + a_3\mathbf{u}_3$ and write as a matrix equation: $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} a_1 \\ a_2 \\ a_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix}$.
4. Solve the normal equations $A^T A \vec{a} = A^T \mathbf{y}$, where $A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$ and $\vec{a} = \begin{bmatrix} a_1 \\ a_2 \\ a_3 \end{bmatrix}$.
5. Compute $A^T A = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 15 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 3 \end{bmatrix}$ and $A^T \mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 16 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix}$.
6. Solve for $\vec{a}$: $a_1 = 6/3 = 2$, $a_2 = 16/15$, $a_3 = 3/3 = 1$.
7. Compute $\mathbf{y}_W = 2\mathbf{u}_1 + \frac{16}{15}\mathbf{u}_2 + 1\mathbf{u}_3 = 2\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} + \frac{16}{15}\begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}$.
8. Calculate $\mathbf{y}_W = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 2 \\ 0 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} -\frac{16}{15} \\ \frac{48}{15} \\ \frac{16}{15} \\ -\frac{32}{15} \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 - \frac{16}{15} - 1 \\ 2 + \frac{48}{15} + 0 \\ 0 + \frac{16}{15} + 1 \\ 2 - \frac{32}{15} + 1 \end{bmatrix}$.
9. Simplify: $\mathbf{y}_W = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{14}{15} \\ \frac{78}{15} \\ \frac{31}{15} \\ \frac{43}{15} \end{bmatrix}$.
10. The orthogonal component is $\mathbf{y}_{W^\perp} = \mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} \frac{14}{15} \\ \frac{78}{15} \\ \frac{31}{15} \\ \frac{43}{15} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{46}{15} \\ -\frac{33}{15} \\ \frac{14}{15} \\ -\frac{58}{15} \end{bmatrix}$.
11. Final answer: $\mathbf{y} = \boxed{\begin{bmatrix} \frac{14}{15} \\ \frac{78}{15} \\ \frac{31}{15} \\ \frac{43}{15} \end{bmatrix}} + \boxed{\begin{bmatrix} \frac{46}{15} \\ -\frac{33}{15} \\ \frac{14}{15} \\ -\frac{58}{15} \end{bmatrix}}$ where the first vector is in $W$ and the second is orthogonal to $W$.
