1. To construct a \(4 \times 3\) matrix with rank 1, recall that a matrix has rank 1 if all its rows (or columns) are scalar multiples of each other. 2. Let’s choose a nonzero column vector \(\mathbf{u} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix}\) of size \(4 \times 1\). 3. Let’s choose a nonzero row vector \(\mathbf{v}^T = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\) of size \(1 \times 3\). 4. The outer product \(\mathbf{u} \mathbf{v}^T\) gives a \(4 \times 3\) matrix with rank 1: \(\mathbf{A} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 & 0 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 & 0 \\ 4 & -2 & 0 \\ 6 & -3 & 0 \\ 8 & -4 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\). 5. Therefore, \(\mathbf{A} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 & 0 \\ 4 & -2 & 0 \\ 6 & -3 & 0 \\ 8 & -4 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\) is a \(4 \times 3\) matrix with rank 1.
