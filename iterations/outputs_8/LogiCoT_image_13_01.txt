1. To find a basis for the eigenspace for \(\lambda = 4\), solve \((A - 4I)\vec{x} = 0\).  
2. Compute \(A - 4I = \begin{bmatrix} 3-4 & 0 & 2 & 0 \\ 1 & 3-4 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1-4 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 4-4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 0 & 2 & 0 \\ 1 & -1 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\).  
3. Set up the homogeneous system: \(\begin{bmatrix} -1 & 0 & 2 & 0 \\ 1 & -1 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \\ x_4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}\).  
4. The last row gives \(0 = 0\), so \(x_4\) is free; let \(x_4 = t\).  
5. The first three equations are: (1) \(-x_1 + 2x_3 = 0\), (2) \(x_1 - x_2 + x_3 = 0\), (3) \(x_2 - 3x_3 = 0\).  
6. From (3), \(x_2 = 3x_3\); substitute into (2): \(x_1 - 3x_3 + x_3 = x_1 - 2x_3 = 0\), so \(x_1 = 2x_3\).  
7. From (1), \(-x_1 + 2x_3 = -2x_3 + 2x_3 = 0\), which is always satisfied, confirming consistency.  
8. Thus, the general solution is \(\vec{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 2x_3 \\ 3x_3 \\ x_3 \\ t \end{bmatrix} = x_3 \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} + t \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}\).  
9. Therefore, a basis for the eigenspace corresponding to \(\lambda = 4\) is \(\left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \right\}\).
