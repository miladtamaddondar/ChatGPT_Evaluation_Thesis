To find another eigenvector, first find the eigenvalues of \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 0.6 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.7 \end{bmatrix}\): Compute \(\det(A - \lambda I) = 0\), so \(\begin{vmatrix} 0.6-\lambda & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.7-\lambda \end{vmatrix} = (0.6-\lambda)(0.7-\lambda) - 0.12 = \lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3 = 0\).
2. Solve the quadratic: \(\lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3 = 0\) gives \(\lambda = 1\) and \(\lambda = 0.3\).
3. For \(\lambda = 1\), the given eigenvector is \(\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 3/7 \\ 4/7 \end{bmatrix}\).
4. For \(\lambda = 0.3\), solve \((A - 0.3I)\mathbf{v}_2 = 0\): \(A - 0.3I = \begin{bmatrix} 0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.4 \end{bmatrix}\).
5. Set up the augmented matrix: \(\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 0.3 & 0.3 & 0 \\ 0.4 & 0.4 & 0 \end{array}\right]\).
6. Row 2 minus \(\frac{4}{3}\) times Row 1: \(\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 0.3 & 0.3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{array}\right]\).
7. The first row gives \(0.3x + 0.3y = 0\) or \(x + y = 0\), so \(\mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix}\) (up to scaling).
8. The basis for \(\mathbb{R}^2\) consisting of eigenvectors is \(\left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 3/7 \\ 4/7 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} \right\}\).
