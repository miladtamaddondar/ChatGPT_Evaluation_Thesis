1. Let \( B = \begin{bmatrix}b_1 & b_2\end{bmatrix} \), where \( b_1 = \begin{bmatrix}x_1 \\ y_1\end{bmatrix} \), \( b_2 = \begin{bmatrix}x_2 \\ y_2\end{bmatrix} \); we want \( AB = 0 \), so \( Ab_1 = 0 \) and \( Ab_2 = 0 \).
2. Set up the system for \( b_1 \): \( \begin{bmatrix}3 & -6 \\ 1 & -2\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}x_1 \\ y_1\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0 \\ 0\end{bmatrix} \).
3. Write the augmented matrix: \( \left[\begin{array}{cc|c}3 & -6 & 0 \\ 1 & -2 & 0\end{array}\right] \).
4. Row 2 minus \(\frac{1}{3}\) times Row 1: \( R_2 \rightarrow R_2 - \frac{1}{3}R_1 \), result: \( \left[\begin{array}{cc|c}3 & -6 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0\end{array}\right] \).
5. The equation is \( 3x_1 - 6y_1 = 0 \Rightarrow x_1 = 2y_1 \); choose \( y_1 = 1 \), so \( b_1 = \begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 1\end{bmatrix} \).
6. For \( b_2 \), choose a different nonzero value: let \( y_2 = -1 \), then \( x_2 = 2(-1) = -2 \), so \( b_2 = \begin{bmatrix}-2 \\ -1\end{bmatrix} \).
7. Thus, \( B = \begin{bmatrix}2 & -2 \\ 1 & -1\end{bmatrix} \) is a \( 2 \times 2 \) matrix with two different nonzero columns such that \( AB = 0 \).
