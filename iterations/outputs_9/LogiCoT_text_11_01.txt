1. To find the eigenvalues, set up the characteristic equation \(\det(A - \lambda I) = 0\), where \(A = \begin{bmatrix}0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 2 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\) and \(I\) is the \(3 \times 3\) identity matrix.
2. Compute \(A - \lambda I = \begin{bmatrix}0-\lambda & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 2-\lambda & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 1-\lambda \end{bmatrix}\).
3. The determinant of this upper triangular matrix is the product of its diagonal entries: \((0-\lambda)(2-\lambda)(1-\lambda)\).
4. Set the determinant equal to zero: \((-\lambda)(2-\lambda)(1-\lambda) = 0\).
5. Solve for \(\lambda\): the solutions are \(\lambda = 0\), \(\lambda = 2\), and \(\lambda = 1\).
6. Final answer: The eigenvalues of the matrix are \(\boxed{0,\, 1,\, 2}\).
