1. The problem asks to find an invertible matrix \( P \) and a matrix \( C \) of the form \( \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix} \) such that \( A = PCP^{-1} \) for \( A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix} \); this is equivalent to finding a similarity transformation to a matrix of the given form, which is a real canonical form for matrices with complex eigenvalues.
2. Compute the eigenvalues of \( A \) by solving \( \det(A - \lambda I) = 0 \): \( \det\left(\begin{pmatrix} 1-\lambda & -2 \\ 1 & 3-\lambda \end{pmatrix}\right) = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0 \).
3. The characteristic equation \( \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0 \) has roots \( \lambda = 2 \pm i \); verify by using the quadratic formula: \( \lambda = \frac{4 \pm \sqrt{16-20}}{2} = 2 \pm i \).
4. For a real matrix with complex eigenvalues \( 2 \pm i \), the real canonical form is \( C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \), matching the required form \( \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix} \) with \( a = 2, b = 1 \).
5. Find an eigenvector for \( \lambda = 2 + i \): solve \( (A - (2+i)I)\vec{v} = 0 \), so \( A - (2+i)I = \begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix} \).
6. Set up \( \begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix} \); from the first row: \( (-1-i)x - 2y = 0 \implies x = -\frac{2}{-1-i}y \).
7. Rationalize denominator: \( -\frac{2}{-1-i} = \frac{2}{1+i} = \frac{2(1-i)}{(1+i)(1-i)} = \frac{2(1-i)}{2} = 1-i \), so \( x = (1-i)y \).
8. Take \( y = 1 \), so eigenvector is \( \vec{v} = \begin{pmatrix} 1-i \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \); verify by substitution: \( (-1-i)(1-i) - 2(1) = (-1-i+1-i) - 2 = (-2i) - 2 = -2 - 2i \), but should be zero, so check calculation.
9. Actually, \( (-1-i)(1-i) = -1 + i - i + i^2 = -1 + i - i - 1 = -2 \), so \( -2 - 2 = -4 \), so there is a calculation error; let's try again.
10. Use the second row: \( 1 \cdot x + (1-i) \cdot y = 0 \implies x = -(1-i)y \), so the eigenvector is \( \begin{pmatrix} -(1-i) \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \).
11. Let \( y = 1 \), so \( \vec{v} = \begin{pmatrix} -(1-i) \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \); verify in the first row: \( (-1-i)(-(1-i)) - 2(1) = (1-i+i-1) - 2 = (0) - 2 = -2 \), so there is a sign error; let's try \( y = 1 \), \( x = -(1-i) \).
12. Substitute \( x = -(1-i) \), \( y = 1 \) into the first row: \( (-1-i)(-(1-i)) - 2(1) = (1-i+i-1) - 2 = (0) - 2 = -2 \), still not zero; let's try the other row.
13. Substitute into the second row: \( 1 \cdot (-(1-i)) + (1-i) \cdot 1 = -(1-i) + (1-i) = 0 \), so this is correct for the second row.
14. Since the eigenvector is complex, to get a real basis, take the real and imaginary parts: \( \vec{v}_1 = \text{Re}(\vec{v}) = \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \), \( \vec{v}_2 = \text{Im}(\vec{v}) = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix} \).
15. Form the matrix \( P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \) from these real and imaginary parts; verify that \( P \) is invertible by checking \( \det(P) = (-1)(0) - (1)(1) = -1 \neq 0 \).
16. The real canonical form is \( C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \), as previously found.
17. Verify \( A = PCP^{-1} \) by computing \( P^{-1} = \frac{1}{-1} \begin{pmatrix} 0 & -1 \\ -1 & -1 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \).
18. Compute \( PCP^{-1} = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \).
19. First, \( \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} -1 \cdot 2 + 1 \cdot -1 & -1 \cdot 1 + 1 \cdot 2 \\ 1 \cdot 2 + 0 \cdot -1 & 1 \cdot 1 + 0 \cdot 2 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} -2 - 1 & -1 + 2 \\ 2 + 0 & 1 + 0 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} -3 & 1 \\ 2 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \).
20. Then, \( \begin{pmatrix} -3 & 1 \\ 2 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 1 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} -3 \cdot 0 + 1 \cdot 1 & -3 \cdot 1 + 1 \cdot 1 \\ 2 \cdot 0 + 1 \cdot 1 & 2 \cdot 1 + 1 \cdot 1 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -3 + 1 \\ 1 & 2 + 1 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix} \), which matches \( A \).
21. Therefore, the solution is \( P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \), \( C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \), and \( A = PCP^{-1} \) as required.
