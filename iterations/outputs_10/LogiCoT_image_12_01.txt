1. Let \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \); to find the characteristic polynomial, compute \( \det(A - \lambda I) \).  
2. \( A - \lambda I = \begin{bmatrix} 2-\lambda & 1 \\ -1 & 4-\lambda \end{bmatrix} \), so \( \det(A - \lambda I) = (2-\lambda)(4-\lambda) - (-1)(1) \).  
3. Expanding, \( (2-\lambda)(4-\lambda) + 1 = 8 - 2\lambda - 4\lambda + \lambda^2 + 1 = \lambda^2 - 6\lambda + 9 \).  
4. The characteristic polynomial is \( \lambda^2 - 6\lambda + 9 \); verify by re-expanding: \( (2-\lambda)(4-\lambda) + 1 = 8 - 6\lambda + \lambda^2 + 1 = \lambda^2 - 6\lambda + 9 \).  
5. To find eigenvalues, solve \( \lambda^2 - 6\lambda + 9 = 0 \).  
6. Factor: \( (\lambda - 3)^2 = 0 \), so \( \lambda = 3 \) is a repeated root.  
7. The eigenvalues of \( A \) are \( \lambda = 3 \) (with algebraic multiplicity 2).
