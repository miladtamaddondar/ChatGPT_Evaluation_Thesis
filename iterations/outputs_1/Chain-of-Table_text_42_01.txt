1. Let $\mathbf{y} = \mathbf{w} + \mathbf{z}$ where $\mathbf{w} \in W = \operatorname{span}\{\mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_2, \mathbf{u}_3\}$ and $\mathbf{z} \perp W$; write $\mathbf{w} = a_1\mathbf{u}_1 + a_2\mathbf{u}_2 + a_3\mathbf{u}_3$ so $\mathbf{y} = a_1\mathbf{u}_1 + a_2\mathbf{u}_2 + a_3\mathbf{u}_3 + \mathbf{z}$.
2. To find $a_1, a_2, a_3$, set up the normal equations: for $i=1,2,3$, $\langle \mathbf{y} - (a_1\mathbf{u}_1 + a_2\mathbf{u}_2 + a_3\mathbf{u}_3), \mathbf{u}_i \rangle = 0$.
3. Compute the Gram matrix $G$ where $G_{ij} = \langle \mathbf{u}_i, \mathbf{u}_j \rangle$ and vector $\mathbf{b}$ where $b_i = \langle \mathbf{y}, \mathbf{u}_i \rangle$.
4. Calculate dot products: $\langle \mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_1 \rangle = 1+1+0+1=3$, $\langle \mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_2 \rangle = 1\cdot(-1)+1\cdot3+0\cdot1+1\cdot(-2) = -1+3+0-2=0$, $\langle \mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_3 \rangle = 1\cdot(-1)+1\cdot0+0\cdot1+1\cdot1 = -1+0+0+1=0$, $\langle \mathbf{u}_2, \mathbf{u}_2 \rangle = (-1)^2+3^2+1^2+(-2)^2 = 1+9+1+4=15$, $\langle \mathbf{u}_2, \mathbf{u}_3 \rangle = (-1)\cdot(-1)+3\cdot0+1\cdot1+(-2)\cdot1 = 1+0+1-2=0$, $\langle \mathbf{u}_3, \mathbf{u}_3 \rangle = (-1)^2+0^2+1^2+1^2 = 1+0+1+1=3$.
5. The Gram matrix is $G = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 15 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 3 \end{bmatrix}$.
6. Compute $b_1 = \langle \mathbf{y}, \mathbf{u}_1 \rangle = 4\cdot1 + 3\cdot1 + 3\cdot0 + (-1)\cdot1 = 4+3+0-1=6$, $b_2 = 4\cdot(-1) + 3\cdot3 + 3\cdot1 + (-1)\cdot(-2) = -4+9+3+2=10$, $b_3 = 4\cdot(-1) + 3\cdot0 + 3\cdot1 + (-1)\cdot1 = -4+0+3-1=-2$.
7. The normal equations are $G\begin{bmatrix} a_1 \\ a_2 \\ a_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 10 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix}$, i.e., $\begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 15 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} a_1 \\ a_2 \\ a_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 10 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix}$.
8. Solve for $a_1, a_2, a_3$: $a_1 = 6/3 = 2$, $a_2 = 10/15 = 2/3$, $a_3 = -2/3$.
9. Compute $\mathbf{w} = 2\mathbf{u}_1 + \frac{2}{3}\mathbf{u}_2 - \frac{2}{3}\mathbf{u}_3 = 2\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} + \frac{2}{3}\begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix} - \frac{2}{3}\begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}$.
10. Calculate each component: first, $2\mathbf{u}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 2 \\ 0 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}$, $\frac{2}{3}\mathbf{u}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} -2/3 \\ 2 \\ 2/3 \\ -4/3 \end{bmatrix}$, $-\frac{2}{3}\mathbf{u}_3 = \begin{bmatrix} 2/3 \\ 0 \\ -2/3 \\ -2/3 \end{bmatrix}$.
11. Add: $\mathbf{w} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 2 \\ 0 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} -2/3 \\ 2 \\ 2/3 \\ -4/3 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 2/3 \\ 0 \\ -2/3 \\ -2/3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 - 2/3 + 2/3 \\ 2 + 2 + 0 \\ 0 + 2/3 - 2/3 \\ 2 - 4/3 - 2/3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 4 \\ 0 \\ 2 - 2 = 0 \end{bmatrix}$.
12. Thus, $\mathbf{w} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 4 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}$ and $\mathbf{z} = \mathbf{y} - \mathbf{w} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 4 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix}$.
13. Final answer: $\boxed{\mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 4 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix}}$ where the first vector is in $W$ and the second is orthogonal to $W$.
