1. To find the matrix representation of \( T \) relative to the basis \( \mathcal{B} = \{1, t, t^2\} \), apply \( T \) to each basis vector: \( T(1) = T(1 + 0t + 0t^2) = 3 \cdot 1 + (5 \cdot 1 - 2 \cdot 0)t + (4 \cdot 0 + 0)t^2 = 3 + 5t \).  
2. Write \( T(1) \) as a coordinate vector in \( \mathcal{B} \): \( T(1) = 3 \cdot 1 + 5 \cdot t + 0 \cdot t^2 \implies [T(1)]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 5 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \).  
3. Apply \( T \) to the second basis vector: \( T(t) = T(0 + 1t + 0t^2) = 3 \cdot 0 + (5 \cdot 0 - 2 \cdot 1)t + (4 \cdot 1 + 0)t^2 = -2t + 4t^2 \).  
4. Write \( T(t) \) as a coordinate vector in \( \mathcal{B} \): \( T(t) = 0 \cdot 1 + (-2) \cdot t + 4 \cdot t^2 \implies [T(t)]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ -2 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} \).  
5. Apply \( T \) to the third basis vector: \( T(t^2) = T(0 + 0t + 1t^2) = 3 \cdot 0 + (5 \cdot 0 - 2 \cdot 0)t + (4 \cdot 0 + 1)t^2 = t^2 \).  
6. Write \( T(t^2) \) as a coordinate vector in \( \mathcal{B} \): \( T(t^2) = 0 \cdot 1 + 0 \cdot t + 1 \cdot t^2 \implies [T(t^2)]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \).  
7. Form the matrix representation by placing these coordinate vectors as columns: \( [T]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 0 \\ 5 & -2 & 0 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \).
