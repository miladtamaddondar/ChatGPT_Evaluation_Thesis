1. Write the equation $T(\mathbf{x}) = (3, 8)$ as a system: $T(x_1, x_2) = (x_1 + x_2, 4x_1 + 5x_2) = (3, 8)$, so $x_1 + x_2 = 3$ and $4x_1 + 5x_2 = 8$.
2. Express the system in matrix form: $\begin{pmatrix}1 & 1 \\ 4 & 5\end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix}x_1 \\ x_2\end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix}3 \\ 8\end{pmatrix}$, which is valid because the transformation is linear and the matrix represents $T$.
3. Compute the determinant of the coefficient matrix: $\det\begin{pmatrix}1 & 1 \\ 4 & 5\end{pmatrix} = (1)(5) - (1)(4) = 1$, confirming the matrix is invertible and the system has a unique solution.
4. Find the inverse of the matrix: For $\begin{pmatrix}a & b \\ c & d\end{pmatrix}$, the inverse is $\frac{1}{ad-bc}\begin{pmatrix}d & -b \\ -c & a\end{pmatrix}$, so the inverse is $\begin{pmatrix}5 & -1 \\ -4 & 1\end{pmatrix}$.
5. Multiply the inverse by the right-hand side: $\begin{pmatrix}x_1 \\ x_2\end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix}5 & -1 \\ -4 & 1\end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix}3 \\ 8\end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix}5 \cdot 3 + (-1) \cdot 8 \\ -4 \cdot 3 + 1 \cdot 8\end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix}15 - 8 \\ -12 + 8\end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix}7 \\ -4\end{pmatrix}$.
6. Final answer: $\boxed{\mathbf{x} = (7, -4)}$.
