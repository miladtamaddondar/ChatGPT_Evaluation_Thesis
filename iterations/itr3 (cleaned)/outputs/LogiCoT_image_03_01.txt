1. Write the given matrix as \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 9 \\ 4 & 5 \end{bmatrix} \). 
2. Assume \( A = LU \) where \( L = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ l_{21} & 1 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( U = \begin{bmatrix} u_{11} & u_{12} \\ 0 & u_{22} \end{bmatrix} \). 
3. Multiply \( L \) and \( U \) to get \( LU = \begin{bmatrix} u_{11} & u_{12} \\ l_{21}u_{11} & l_{21}u_{12} + u_{22} \end{bmatrix} \). 
4. Set up equations by equating \( LU \) to \( A \): \( u_{11} = 6 \), \( u_{12} = 9 \), \( l_{21}u_{11} = 4 \), \( l_{21}u_{12} + u_{22} = 5 \). 
5. Solve for \( l_{21} \): \( l_{21} = \frac{4}{6} = \frac{2}{3} \). 
6. Solve for \( u_{22} \): \( l_{21}u_{12} + u_{22} = 5 \implies \frac{2}{3} \times 9 + u_{22} = 5 \implies 6 + u_{22} = 5 \implies u_{22} = -1 \). 
7. Thus, \( L = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ \frac{2}{3} & 1 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( U = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 9 \\ 0 & -1 \end{bmatrix} \). 
8. Verify by multiplying \( L \) and \( U \): \( \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ \frac{2}{3} & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 9 \\ 0 & -1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 9 \\ 4 & 5 \end{bmatrix} \), which matches \( A \). 
9. Therefore, the LU factorization is \( \boxed{A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ \frac{2}{3} & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 9 \\ 0 & -1 \end{bmatrix}} \).
