1. Write the transformation equations as a system: \( x_1 + x_2 = 3 \) and \( 4x_1 + 5x_2 = 8 \).  
2. Express the system in matrix form: \( \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 4 & 5 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 8 \end{bmatrix} \).  
3. Verify the coefficient matrix is invertible by checking its determinant: \( \det\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 4 & 5 \end{bmatrix} = (1)(5) - (1)(4) = 1 \neq 0 \), so it is invertible.  
4. Find the inverse of the matrix: \( \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 4 & 5 \end{bmatrix}^{-1} = \frac{1}{1} \begin{bmatrix} 5 & -1 \\ -4 & 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 5 & -1 \\ -4 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \).  
5. Multiply both sides by the inverse to solve for \( \mathbf{x} \): \( \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 5 & -1 \\ -4 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 8 \end{bmatrix} \).  
6. Compute \( x_1 = 5 \cdot 3 + (-1) \cdot 8 = 15 - 8 = 7 \) and \( x_2 = -4 \cdot 3 + 1 \cdot 8 = -12 + 8 = -4 \).  
7. Final answer: \( \mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 7 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix} \).
