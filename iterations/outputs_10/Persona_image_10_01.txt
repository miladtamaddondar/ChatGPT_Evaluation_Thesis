1. The matrix \( A \) is given as \( \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ -3 & 9 & -1 & 5 \\ 2 & -6 & 4 & -3 \\ -4 & 12 & 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix} \).
2. The echelon form of \( A \) is \( \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 & -7 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \).
3. The pivot columns in the echelon form are columns 1, 3, and 4, so the basis for \( \operatorname{Col} A \) is the set of columns 1, 3, and 4 of \( A \): \( \left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} -4 \\ 5 \\ -3 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \right\} \).
4. The dimension of \( \operatorname{Col} A \) is 3 because there are 3 pivot columns.
5. To find a basis for \( \operatorname{Nul} A \), set up the equation \( A\vec{x} = \vec{0} \) and use the echelon form to write the system: \( x_1 - 3x_2 + 2x_3 - 4x_4 = 0 \), \( 5x_3 - 7x_4 = 0 \), \( 5x_4 = 0 \).
6. From the third equation, \( x_4 = 0 \); from the second, \( x_3 = 0 \); from the first, \( x_1 - 3x_2 = 0 \Rightarrow x_1 = 3x_2 \).
7. The general solution is \( \vec{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 3x_2 \\ x_2 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} = x_2 \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \), so a basis for \( \operatorname{Nul} A \) is \( \left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \right\} \).
8. The dimension of \( \operatorname{Nul} A \) is 1 because there is 1 free variable.
