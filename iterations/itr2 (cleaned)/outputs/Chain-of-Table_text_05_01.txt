4. Equate (1,2) entries: \( -10 + 5k = 15 \implies 5k = 25 \implies k = 5 \).
5. Equate (2,1) entries: \( -9 = 6 - 3k \implies -9 - 6 = -3k \implies -15 = -3k \implies k = 5 \).
6. Equate (2,2) entries: \( 15 + k = 15 + k \) is always true.
7. Conclusion: The value of \( k \) that makes \( AB = BA \) is \( \boxed{5} \).
