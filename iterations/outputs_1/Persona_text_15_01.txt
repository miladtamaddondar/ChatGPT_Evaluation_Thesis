1. First, we need to find the eigenvalues of $A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix}$ by solving $\det(A - \lambda I) = 0$; so, $\det\left(\begin{pmatrix} 1-\lambda & -2 \\ 1 & 3-\lambda \end{pmatrix}\right) = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) - (-2)(1) = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2$.
2. Expanding $(1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = (3 - \lambda - 3\lambda + \lambda^2) + 2 = (\lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 3) + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5$.
3. Set the characteristic equation to zero: $\lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0$.
4. Solve for $\lambda$: $\lambda = \frac{4 \pm \sqrt{(-4)^2 - 4 \cdot 1 \cdot 5}}{2} = \frac{4 \pm \sqrt{16 - 20}}{2} = \frac{4 \pm \sqrt{-4}}{2} = \frac{4 \pm 2i}{2} = 2 \pm i$.
5. Since the eigenvalues are $2 + i$ and $2 - i$, $A$ is similar to a matrix $C$ of the form $\begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix}$, where $a = 2$ and $b = 1$.
6. Next, find an eigenvector for $\lambda = 2 + i$ by solving $(A - (2+i)I)\vec{v} = 0$; $A - (2+i)I = \begin{pmatrix} 1-(2+i) & -2 \\ 1 & 3-(2+i) \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix}$.
7. Let $\vec{v} = \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix}$, so $(-1-i)x - 2y = 0$ and $x + (1-i)y = 0$.
8. From the first equation, $(-1-i)x = 2y \implies x = \frac{2y}{-1-i}$.
9. Rationalize the denominator: $-1-i = -(1+i)$, so $\frac{1}{-1-i} = \frac{-1+i}{(-1-i)(-1+i)} = \frac{-1+i}{1+1} = \frac{-1+i}{2}$.
10. Thus, $x = 2y \cdot \frac{-1+i}{2} = y(-1+i)$.
11. So, an eigenvector is $\vec{v}_1 = \begin{pmatrix} -1+i \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}$.
12. The complex conjugate eigenvector for $\lambda = 2 - i$ is $\vec{v}_2 = \begin{pmatrix} -1-i \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}$.
13. To form a real invertible matrix $P$, take the real and imaginary parts of $\vec{v}_1$: $\operatorname{Re}(\vec{v}_1) = \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}$, $\operatorname{Im}(\vec{v}_1) = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}$.
14. Set $P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}$.
15. The matrix $C$ is $\begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}$, since $a = 2$, $b = 1$.
16. Therefore, the invertible matrix $P$ and the matrix $C$ such that $A = P C P^{-1}$ are $P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}$ and $C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}$.
