1. Write the matrix \(A = \begin{bmatrix}6 & 9 \\ 4 & 5\end{bmatrix}\) and recall that LU factorization expresses \(A\) as \(A = LU\), where \(L\) is lower triangular and \(U\) is upper triangular.
2. Assume \(L = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 \\ l_{21} & 1\end{bmatrix}\) and \(U = \begin{bmatrix}u_{11} & u_{12} \\ 0 & u_{22}\end{bmatrix}\), so \(A = LU = \begin{bmatrix}u_{11} & u_{12} \\ l_{21}u_{11} & l_{21}u_{12} + u_{22}\end{bmatrix}\).
3. Equate the entries: \(u_{11} = 6\), \(u_{12} = 9\), \(l_{21}u_{11} = 4\), and \(l_{21}u_{12} + u_{22} = 5\).
4. Solve for \(l_{21}\): \(l_{21}u_{11} = 4 \implies l_{21} = \frac{4}{6} = \frac{2}{3}\).
5. Substitute \(l_{21}\) and \(u_{12}\) into \(l_{21}u_{12} + u_{22} = 5\): \(\frac{2}{3} \cdot 9 + u_{22} = 5 \implies 6 + u_{22} = 5 \implies u_{22} = -1\).
6. Thus, \(L = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 \\ \frac{2}{3} & 1\end{bmatrix}\) and \(U = \begin{bmatrix}6 & 9 \\ 0 & -1\end{bmatrix}\).
7. Verify by multiplying \(L\) and \(U\): \(\begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 \\ \frac{2}{3} & 1\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}6 & 9 \\ 0 & -1\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}6 & 9 \\ \frac{2}{3} \cdot 6 + 1 \cdot 0 & \frac{2}{3} \cdot 9 + 1 \cdot (-1)\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}6 & 9 \\ 4 & 5\end{bmatrix}\), which matches \(A\).
8. Final answer: The LU factorization is \(L = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 \\ \frac{2}{3} & 1\end{bmatrix}\), \(U = \begin{bmatrix}6 & 9 \\ 0 & -1\end{bmatrix}\).
