Find scalars $a$ and $b$ such that $a\mathbf{b}_1 + b\mathbf{b}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}9 \\ 7\end{bmatrix}$, i.e., $a\begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 1\end{bmatrix} + b\begin{bmatrix}5 \\ 4\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}9 \\ 7\end{bmatrix}$, so $a + 5b = 9$ and $a + 4b = 7$. 7. Subtract the second equation from the first: $(a + 5b) - (a + 4b) = 9 - 7 \implies b = 2$. 8. Substitute $b = 2$ into $a + 5b = 9$ to get $a + 10 = 9 \implies a = -1$. 9.
