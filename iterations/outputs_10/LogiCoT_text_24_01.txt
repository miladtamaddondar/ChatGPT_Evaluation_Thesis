1. We are asked to find all $\mathbf{x} \in \mathbb{R}^4$ such that $A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0}$, i.e., the null space of $A$; this is a homogeneous system of linear equations.
2. Write the system $A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0}$ explicitly: $\begin{bmatrix}1 & -4 & 7 & -5 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & 3 \\ 2 & -6 & 6 & -4\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \\ x_4\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0 \\ 0 \\ 0\end{bmatrix}$.
3. This gives the equations: $x_1 - 4x_2 + 7x_3 - 5x_4 = 0$, $x_2 - 4x_3 + 3x_4 = 0$, $2x_1 - 6x_2 + 6x_3 - 4x_4 = 0$.
4. To solve, write the augmented matrix and row reduce: $\begin{bmatrix}1 & -4 & 7 & -5 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & 3 & 0 \\ 2 & -6 & 6 & -4 & 0\end{bmatrix}$.
5. Subtract $2$ times row 1 from row 3: Row 3 becomes $[2-2\cdot1, -6-2\cdot(-4), 6-2\cdot7, -4-2\cdot(-5), 0-2\cdot0] = [0, 2, -8, 6, 0]$.
6. The new matrix is $\begin{bmatrix}1 & -4 & 7 & -5 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 2 & -8 & 6 & 0\end{bmatrix}$, which is correct by direct computation.
7. Subtract $2$ times row 2 from row 3: Row 3 becomes $[0, 2-2\cdot1, -8-2\cdot(-4), 6-2\cdot3, 0-2\cdot0] = [0, 0, 0, 0, 0]$.
8. Now the matrix is $\begin{bmatrix}1 & -4 & 7 & -5 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0\end{bmatrix}$, which is row echelon form, confirming the system has infinitely many solutions.
9. Express the leading variables in terms of the free variables: From row 2, $x_2 - 4x_3 + 3x_4 = 0 \implies x_2 = 4x_3 - 3x_4$.
10. From row 1, $x_1 - 4x_2 + 7x_3 - 5x_4 = 0$; substitute $x_2$: $x_1 - 4(4x_3 - 3x_4) + 7x_3 - 5x_4 = 0$.
11. Simplify: $x_1 - 16x_3 + 12x_4 + 7x_3 - 5x_4 = 0 \implies x_1 - 9x_3 + 7x_4 = 0 \implies x_1 = 9x_3 - 7x_4$.
12. Let $x_3 = s$, $x_4 = t$ for $s, t \in \mathbb{R}$; then $x_2 = 4s - 3t$, $x_1 = 9s - 7t$.
13. Therefore, the general solution is $\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix}9s - 7t \\ 4s - 3t \\ s \\ t\end{bmatrix} = s\begin{bmatrix}9 \\ 4 \\ 1 \\ 0\end{bmatrix} + t\begin{bmatrix}-7 \\ -3 \\ 0 \\ 1\end{bmatrix}$.
14. Final answer: All $\mathbf{x} \in \mathbb{R}^4$ mapped to zero by $A$ are $\boxed{\mathbf{x} = s\begin{bmatrix}9 \\ 4 \\ 1 \\ 0\end{bmatrix} + t\begin{bmatrix}-7 \\ -3 \\ 0 \\ 1\end{bmatrix},\quad s, t \in \mathbb{R}}$.
