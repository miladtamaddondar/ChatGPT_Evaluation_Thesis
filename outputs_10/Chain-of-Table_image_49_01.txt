Let's solve for the upper triangular matrix \( R \) such that \( A = QR \).

1. Write the equation \( A = QR \) and note that \( R = Q^T A \) since \( Q \) is orthogonal (its columns are orthonormal): \( R = Q^T A \).
2. Compute \( Q^T \): \( Q^T = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{5}{6} & \frac{1}{6} & -\frac{3}{6} & \frac{1}{6} \\ -\frac{1}{6} & \frac{5}{6} & \frac{1}{6} & \frac{3}{6} \end{bmatrix} \).
3. Compute \( R = Q^T A \): \( R = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{5}{6} & \frac{1}{6} & -\frac{3}{6} & \frac{1}{6} \\ -\frac{1}{6} & \frac{5}{6} & \frac{1}{6} & \frac{3}{6} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 9 \\ 1 & 7 \\ -3 & -5 \\ 1 & 5 \end{bmatrix} \).
4. Compute the first row of \( R \): \( \left[ \frac{5}{6} \cdot 5 + \frac{1}{6} \cdot 1 + (-\frac{3}{6}) \cdot (-3) + \frac{1}{6} \cdot 1, \frac{5}{6} \cdot 9 + \frac{1}{6} \cdot 7 + (-\frac{3}{6}) \cdot (-5) + \frac{1}{6} \cdot 5 \right] = [\frac{25}{6} + \frac{1}{6} + \frac{9}{6} + \frac{1}{6}, \frac{45}{6} + \frac{7}{6} + \frac{15}{6} + \frac{5}{6}] = [\frac{36}{6}, \frac{72}{6}] = [6, 12] \).
5. Compute the second row of \( R \): \( \left[ -\frac{1}{6} \cdot 5 + \frac{5}{6} \cdot 1 + \frac{1}{6} \cdot (-3) + \frac{3}{6} \cdot 1, -\frac{1}{6} \cdot 9 + \frac{5}{6} \cdot 7 + \frac{1}{6} \cdot (-5) + \frac{3}{6} \cdot 5 \right] = [ -\frac{5}{6} + \frac{5}{6} - \frac{3}{6} + \frac{3}{6}, -\frac{9}{6} + \frac{35}{6} - \frac{5}{6} + \frac{15}{6} ] = [0, \frac{36}{6}] = [0, 6] \).
6. Write the final matrix \( R \): \( R = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 12 \\ 0 & 6 \end{bmatrix} \).
7. Check the work by computing \( QR \): \( \begin{bmatrix} \frac{5}{6} & -\frac{1}{6} \\ \frac{1}{6} & \frac{5}{6} \\ -\frac{3}{6} & \frac{1}{6} \\ \frac{1}{6} & \frac{3}{6} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 12 \\ 0 & 6 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 9 \\ 1 & 7 \\ -3 & -5 \\ 1 & 5 \end{bmatrix} = A \).
8. Therefore, the upper triangular matrix \( R \) such that \( A = QR \) is \( \boxed{\begin{bmatrix} 6 & 12 \\ 0 & 6 \end{bmatrix}} \).
