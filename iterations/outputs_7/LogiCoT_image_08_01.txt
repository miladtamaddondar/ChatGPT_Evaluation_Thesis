1. To construct a \(4 \times 3\) matrix with rank 1, recall that a rank 1 matrix can be written as the outer product of a nonzero \(4 \times 1\) column vector and a nonzero \(1 \times 3\) row vector: \(A = \mathbf{u}\mathbf{v}^T\).  
2. Choose \(\mathbf{u} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix}\) and \(\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 6 & 7 \end{bmatrix}\), both nonzero vectors.  
3. Compute the outer product: \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 6 & 7 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \times 5 & 1 \times 6 & 1 \times 7 \\ 2 \times 5 & 2 \times 6 & 2 \times 7 \\ 3 \times 5 & 3 \times 6 & 3 \times 7 \\ 4 \times 5 & 4 \times 6 & 4 \times 7 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 6 & 7 \\ 10 & 12 & 14 \\ 15 & 18 & 21 \\ 20 & 24 & 28 \end{bmatrix}\).  
4. Verify correctness: All rows are scalar multiples of the first row, so the row space is 1-dimensional, confirming the rank is 1.  
5. Final answer: A \(4 \times 3\) matrix with rank 1 is \(\begin{bmatrix} 5 & 6 & 7 \\ 10 & 12 & 14 \\ 15 & 18 & 21 \\ 20 & 24 & 28 \end{bmatrix}\).
