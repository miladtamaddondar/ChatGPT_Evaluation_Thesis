Write the matrix \( A = \begin{bmatrix}6 & 9 \\ 4 & 5\end{bmatrix} \) and set up \( A = LU \) with \( L = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 \\ l_{21} & 1\end{bmatrix} \), \( U = \begin{bmatrix}u_{11} & u_{12} \\ 0 & u_{22}\end{bmatrix} \). 2. Multiply \( L \) and \( U \) to get \( LU = \begin{bmatrix}u_{11} & u_{12} \\ l_{21}u_{11} & l_{21}u_{12} + u_{22}\end{bmatrix} \) and set equal to \( A \). 3. Equate entries: \( u_{11} = 6 \), \( u_{12} = 9 \), \( l_{21}u_{11} = 4 \), \( l_{21}u_{12} + u_{22} = 5 \). 4. Solve for \( l_{21} \): \( l_{21} = 4/6 = 2/3 \). 5. Solve for \( u_{22} \): \( (2/3) \cdot 9 + u_{22} = 5 \implies 6 + u_{22} = 5 \implies u_{22} = -1 \). 6. Thus, \( L = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 \\ 2/3 & 1\end{bmatrix} \), \( U = \begin{bmatrix}6 & 9 \\ 0 & -1\end{bmatrix} \). 7. Verify \( LU = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 \\ 2/3 & 1\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}6 & 9 \\ 0 & -1\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}6 & 9 \\ 4 & 5\end{bmatrix} \) by calculation: first row \( (1)(6) + (0)(0) = 6 \), \( (1)(9) + (0)(-1) = 9 \); second row \( (2/3)(6) + (1)(0) = 4 \), \( (2/3)(9) + (1)(-1) = 6 - 1 = 5 \). 8. The LU factorization is \( \boxed{A = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 \\ 2/3 & 1\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}6 & 9 \\ 0 & -1\end{bmatrix}} \).
