1. We are asked to find all vectors \(\mathbf{x} \in \mathbb{R}^4\) such that \(A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0}\), where \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -4 & 7 & -5 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & 3 \\ 2 & -6 & 6 & -4 \end{bmatrix}\); this is equivalent to finding the null space of \(A\).  
2. Write the system of equations: \(1x_1 - 4x_2 + 7x_3 - 5x_4 = 0\), \(x_2 - 4x_3 + 3x_4 = 0\), \(2x_1 - 6x_2 + 6x_3 - 4x_4 = 0\).  
3. Observe that the third equation is a linear combination of the first two: \(2x_1 - 6x_2 + 6x_3 - 4x_4 = 2(x_1 - 4x_2 + 7x_3 - 5x_4) - (x_2 - 4x_3 + 3x_4)\), so the system is consistent and the third equation is redundant.  
4. Solve the second equation for \(x_2\): \(x_2 = 4x_3 - 3x_4\).  
5. Substitute \(x_2\) into the first equation: \(x_1 - 4(4x_3 - 3x_4) + 7x_3 - 5x_4 = 0\) which simplifies to \(x_1 - 16x_3 + 12x_4 + 7x_3 - 5x_4 = 0\).  
6. Combine like terms: \(x_1 - 9x_3 + 7x_4 = 0\), so \(x_1 = 9x_3 - 7x_4\).  
7. Therefore, the general solution is \(\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \\ x_4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 9x_3 - 7x_4 \\ 4x_3 - 3x_4 \\ x_3 \\ x_4 \end{bmatrix} = x_3\begin{bmatrix} 9 \\ 4 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} + x_4\begin{bmatrix} -7 \\ -3 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}\).  
8. The null space of \(A\) is thus \(\text{span}\left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 9 \\ 4 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} -7 \\ -3 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \right\}\), which is verified by plugging these vectors into the original equations and confirming that they yield the zero vector.
