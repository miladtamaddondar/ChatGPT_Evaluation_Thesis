1. Write the augmented matrix: \(\left[\begin{array}{cc|cc} 8 & 6 & 1 & 0 \\ 5 & 4 & 0 & 1 \end{array}\right]\).
2. Make the leading 1 in row 1 by dividing row 1 by 8: \(\left[\begin{array}{cc|cc} 1 & \frac{3}{4} & \frac{1}{8} & 0 \\ 5 & 4 & 0 & 1 \end{array}\right]\).
3. Eliminate the 5 below the leading 1 by replacing row 2 with row 2 minus 5 times row 1: \(\left[\begin{array}{cc|cc} 1 & \frac{3}{4} & \frac{1}{8} & 0 \\ 0 & \frac{1}{4} & -\frac{5}{8} & 1 \end{array}\right]\).
4. Make the leading 1 in row 2 by multiplying row 2 by 4: \(\left[\begin{array}{cc|cc} 1 & \frac{3}{4} & \frac{1}{8} & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -\frac{5}{2} & 4 \end{array}\right]\).
5. Eliminate the \(\frac{3}{4}\) above the leading 1 in row 2 by replacing row 1 with row 1 minus \(\frac{3}{4}\) times row 2: \(\left[\begin{array}{cc|cc} 1 & 0 & 2 & -3 \\ 0 & 1 & -\frac{5}{2} & 4 \end{array}\right]\).
6. The inverse is \(\left[\begin{array}{cc} 2 & -3 \\ -\frac{5}{2} & 4 \end{array}\right]\).
