1. Display the given matrix \( A \) and its echelon form: \( A = \begin{bmatrix}1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ -3 & 9 & -1 & 5 \\ 2 & -6 & 4 & -3 \\ -4 & 12 & 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix} \), echelon form \( U = \begin{bmatrix}1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 & -7 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \).
2. Identify pivot columns in \( U \) (columns with leading 1s or first nonzero entry in each row): columns 1, 3, and 4.
3. By the definition of the column space, the basis for \( \text{Col } A \) is given by the columns of \( A \) corresponding to the pivot columns in \( U \): \( \left\{ \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}2 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}-4 \\ 5 \\ -3 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \right\} \).
4. The dimension of \( \text{Col } A \) is the number of pivot columns, which is 3.
5. To find a basis for \( \text{Nul } A \), set up the homogeneous system \( U\mathbf{x} = 0 \): \( x_1 - 3x_2 + 2x_3 - 4x_4 = 0 \), \( 5x_3 - 7x_4 = 0 \), \( 5x_4 = 0 \).
6. Solve for free variables: From the third equation, \( x_4 = 0 \); from the second, \( x_3 = 0 \); from the first, \( x_1 - 3x_2 = 0 \implies x_1 = 3x_2 \).
7. The general solution is \( \mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix}3x_2 \\ x_2 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} = x_2 \begin{bmatrix}3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \), so a basis for \( \text{Nul } A \) is \( \left\{ \begin{bmatrix}3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \right\} \).
8. The dimension of \( \text{Nul } A \) is the number of free variables, which is 1.
9. By the Rank-Nullity Theorem, \( \text{rank}(A) + \text{nullity}(A) = 3 + 1 = 4 \), which matches the number of columns in \( A \), verifying correctness.
