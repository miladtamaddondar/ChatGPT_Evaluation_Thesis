1. The original matrix is \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ -3 & 9 & -1 & 5 \\ 2 & -6 & 4 & -3 \\ -4 & 12 & 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix} \).
2. The echelon form of \( A \) is \( \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 & -7 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \).
3. The pivot columns are columns 1, 3, and 4, so a basis for \(\text{Col }A\) is \( \left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} -4 \\ 5 \\ -3 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \right\} \).
4. To find a basis for \(\text{Nul }A\), solve \(A\vec{x} = 0\) using the echelon form: let \(x_2 = t\), \(x_4 = s\), then back-substitute to get \(x_3 = \frac{7}{5}s\), \(x_1 = 3t - 2x_3 + 4s\), so the basis for \(\text{Nul }A\) is \( \left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} -\frac{6}{5} \\ 0 \\ \frac{7}{5} \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \right\} \).
5. The dimension of \(\text{Col }A\) is 3 and the dimension of \(\text{Nul }A\) is 2.
