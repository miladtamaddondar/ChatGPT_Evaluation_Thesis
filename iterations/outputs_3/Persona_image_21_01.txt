We are given \( T(\mathbf{x}) = A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{b} \), where \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -2 \\ -2 & 1 & 6 \\ 3 & -2 & -5 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( \mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 7 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} \); we need to solve \( A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{b} \) and determine if \( \mathbf{x} \) is unique. 
2. Write the augmented matrix: \( \left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & -2 & -1 \\ -2 & 1 & 6 & 7 \\ 3 & -2 & -5 & -3 \end{array}\right] \). 
3. Use row 1 to eliminate the \( x_1 \) term in rows 2 and 3: Row 2 \( +2 \times \) Row 1: \( (0, 1, 2, 5) \); Row 3 \( -3 \times \) Row 1: \( (0, -2, 1, 0) \), so the new matrix is \( \left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & -2 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & 5 \\ 0 & -2 & 1 & 0 \end{array}\right] \). 
4. Use row 2 to eliminate the \( x_2 \) term in row 3: Row 3 \( +2 \times \) Row 2: \( (0, 0, 5, 10) \), so the new matrix is \( \left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & -2 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 & 10 \end{array}\right] \). 
5. Solve for \( x_3 \) from row 3: \( 5x_3 = 10 \implies x_3 = 2 \). 
6. Substitute \( x_3 = 2 \) into row 2: \( x_2 + 2x_3 = 5 \implies x_2 + 4 = 5 \implies x_2 = 1 \). 
7. Substitute \( x_2 = 1 \), \( x_3 = 2 \) into row 1: \( x_1 - 2x_3 = -1 \implies x_1 - 4 = -1 \implies x_1 = 3 \). 
8. The solution is \( \mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \). 
9. Since we obtained a unique solution with no free variables, \( \mathbf{x} \) is unique.
