1. Find the eigenvalues of \(A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix}\) by solving \(\det(A - \lambda I) = 0\): \(\det\begin{pmatrix} 1-\lambda & -2 \\ 1 & 3-\lambda \end{pmatrix} = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0\).  
2. Solve the quadratic equation \(\lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0\): \(\lambda = \frac{4 \pm \sqrt{(-4)^2 - 4 \cdot 1 \cdot 5}}{2} = \frac{4 \pm \sqrt{16 - 20}}{2} = \frac{4 \pm 2i}{2} = 2 \pm i\).  
3. Since the eigenvalues are complex conjugates, \(A\) is similar to a real matrix of the form \(C = \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix}\) where \(a = 2\), \(b = 1\).  
4. Find a complex eigenvector for \(\lambda = 2 + i\): Solve \((A - (2+i)I)\mathbf{v} = 0\), so \(A - (2+i)I = \begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix}\).  
5. Set up the system: \((-1-i)v_1 - 2v_2 = 0\), \(v_1 + (1-i)v_2 = 0\); from the first equation, \(v_1 = \frac{-2}{-1-i}v_2 = \frac{2}{1+i}v_2\).  
6. Simplify \(\frac{2}{1+i}\) by multiplying numerator and denominator by \(1-i\): \(\frac{2(1-i)}{(1+i)(1-i)} = \frac{2(1-i)}{2} = 1-i\), so \(v_1 = (1-i)v_2\).  
7. Take \(v_2 = 1\), so the eigenvector is \(\mathbf{v} = \begin{pmatrix} 1-i \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}\).  
8. The real and imaginary parts of this eigenvector are \(\mathbf{v}_R = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}\), \(\mathbf{v}_I = \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}\).  
9. Form the invertible matrix \(P\) with columns \(\mathbf{v}_R\) and \(\mathbf{v}_I\): \(P = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}\).  
10. Verify \(P\) is invertible by computing its determinant: \(\det(P) = (1)(0) - (1)(-1) = 1\), so \(P\) is invertible.  
11. The canonical form is \(C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}\) since its eigenvalues are \(2 \pm i\).  
12. Verify \(A = PCP^{-1}\) by computing \(P^{-1} = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 1 \end{pmatrix}\) and \(PCP^{-1} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix} = A\).  
13. Final answer: \(P = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}\), \(C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}\), and \(A = PCP^{-1}\).
