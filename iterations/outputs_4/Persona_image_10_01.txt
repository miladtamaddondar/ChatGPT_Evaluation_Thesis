1. The matrix \( A \) and its echelon form are given as \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ -3 & 9 & -1 & 5 \\ 2 & -6 & 4 & -3 \\ -4 & 12 & 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( \text{Echelon form of } A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 & -7 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \). 
2. The pivot columns in the echelon form are columns 1, 3, and 4, so the basis for \( \text{Col } A \) is given by the corresponding columns of \( A \): \( \left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} -4 \\ 5 \\ -3 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \right\} \). 
3. The dimension of \( \text{Col } A \) (the rank of \( A \)) is 3 because there are 3 pivot columns. 
4. To find a basis for \( \text{Nul } A \), set up the equation \( A\vec{x} = 0 \) and write the variables corresponding to the non-pivot columns (here, column 2) as free variables: let \( \vec{x} = \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \\ x_4 \end{bmatrix} \), then from the echelon form, the system is \( x_1 - 3x_2 + 2x_3 - 4x_4 = 0 \), \( 5x_3 - 7x_4 = 0 \), \( 5x_4 = 0 \). 
5. From \( 5x_4 = 0 \), we get \( x_4 = 0 \); from \( 5x_3 - 7x_4 = 0 \), substitute \( x_4 = 0 \) to get \( x_3 = 0 \); from \( x_1 - 3x_2 + 2x_3 - 4x_4 = 0 \), substitute \( x_3 = 0 \), \( x_4 = 0 \) to get \( x_1 - 3x_2 = 0 \implies x_1 = 3x_2 \). 
6. Thus, the general solution is \( \vec{x} = x_2 \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \), so a basis for \( \text{Nul } A \) is \( \left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \right\} \). 
7. The dimension of \( \text{Nul } A \) is 1 because there is one free variable.
