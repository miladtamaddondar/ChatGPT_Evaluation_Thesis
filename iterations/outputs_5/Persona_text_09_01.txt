1. Write the vectors as columns of a matrix: \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ -3 & 9 & -1 & 5 \\ 2 & -6 & 4 & -3 \\ -4 & 12 & 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix} \).
2. Observe that \(\mathbf{v}_2 = -3\mathbf{v}_1\), so \(\mathbf{v}_2\) is dependent on \(\mathbf{v}_1\).
3. Subtract \(2 \times \mathbf{v}_1\) from \(\mathbf{v}_3\): \(\mathbf{v}_3 - 2\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} - 2\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 5 \\ 0 \\ 10 \end{bmatrix}\).
4. Subtract \(-4 \times \mathbf{v}_1\) from \(\mathbf{v}_4\): \(\mathbf{v}_4 - (-4)\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} -4 \\ 5 \\ -3 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} + 4\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ -7 \\ 5 \\ -9 \end{bmatrix}\).
5. Check if the new vectors \(\mathbf{v}_1, \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 5 \\ 0 \\ 10 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ -7 \\ 5 \\ -9 \end{bmatrix}\) are linearly independent by setting up \(a\mathbf{v}_1 + b\begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 5 \\ 0 \\ 10 \end{bmatrix} + c\begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ -7 \\ 5 \\ -9 \end{bmatrix} = \mathbf{0}\).
6. The first component gives \(a = 0\), the third component gives \(5c = 0 \implies c = 0\), and the second component gives \(5b - 7c = 0 \implies b = 0\).
7. Therefore, the set \(\{\mathbf{v}_1, \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 5 \\ 0 \\ 10 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ -7 \\ 5 \\ -9 \end{bmatrix}\}\) is linearly independent.
8. Thus, a basis for the subspace is \(\left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 5 \\ 0 \\ 10 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ -7 \\ 5 \\ -9 \end{bmatrix} \right\}\).
9. The dimension of the subspace is \(3\).
