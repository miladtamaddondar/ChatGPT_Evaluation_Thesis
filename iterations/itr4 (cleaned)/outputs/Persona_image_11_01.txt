1. To find the eigenvalues of the matrix \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 2 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \), we solve the characteristic equation \( \det(A - \lambda I) = 0 \). 
2. Compute \( A - \lambda I = \begin{bmatrix} 0-\lambda & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 2-\lambda & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 1-\lambda \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -\lambda & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 2-\lambda & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 1-\lambda \end{bmatrix} \). 
3. The determinant is \( \det(A - \lambda I) = (-\lambda) \cdot \det\begin{bmatrix} 2-\lambda & 5 \\ 0 & 1-\lambda \end{bmatrix} \). 
4. Calculate the \( 2 \times 2 \) determinant: \( \det\begin{bmatrix} 2-\lambda & 5 \\ 0 & 1-\lambda \end{bmatrix} = (2-\lambda)(1-\lambda) - 0 = (2-\lambda)(1-\lambda) \). 
5. The characteristic polynomial is \( (-\lambda)(2-\lambda)(1-\lambda) = 0 \). 6. The eigenvalues are \( \lambda_1 = 0 \), \( \lambda_2 = 1 \), \( \lambda_3 = 2 \).
