1. Write the matrix \( A = \begin{bmatrix}6 & 9 \\ 4 & 5\end{bmatrix} \) and recall that LU factorization expresses \( A = LU \), where \( L \) is lower triangular with 1s on the diagonal and \( U \) is upper triangular.
2. Set \( L = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 \\ l_{21} & 1\end{bmatrix} \) and \( U = \begin{bmatrix}u_{11} & u_{12} \\ 0 & u_{22}\end{bmatrix} \), so \( LU = \begin{bmatrix}u_{11} & u_{12} \\ l_{21}u_{11} & l_{21}u_{12} + u_{22}\end{bmatrix} \).
3. Equate \( LU \) to \( A \): \( u_{11} = 6 \), \( u_{12} = 9 \), \( l_{21}u_{11} = 4 \), \( l_{21}u_{12} + u_{22} = 5 \).
4. Solve for \( l_{21} \): \( l_{21}u_{11} = 4 \implies l_{21} = \frac{4}{6} = \frac{2}{3} \); verify by substitution: \( \frac{2}{3} \times 6 = 4 \) is correct.
5. Solve for \( u_{22} \): \( l_{21}u_{12} + u_{22} = 5 \implies \frac{2}{3} \times 9 + u_{22} = 5 \implies 6 + u_{22} = 5 \implies u_{22} = -1 \); verify: \( 6 + (-1) = 5 \) is correct.
6. Write the final factorization: \( L = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 \\ \frac{2}{3} & 1\end{bmatrix} \), \( U = \begin{bmatrix}6 & 9 \\ 0 & -1\end{bmatrix} \).
7. Final answer: The LU factorization is \( \begin{bmatrix}6 & 9 \\ 4 & 5\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 \\ \frac{2}{3} & 1\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}6 & 9 \\ 0 & -1\end{bmatrix} \).
