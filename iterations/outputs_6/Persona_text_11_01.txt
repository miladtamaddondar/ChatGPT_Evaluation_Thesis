1. To find the eigenvalues, set up the characteristic equation $\det(A - \lambda I) = 0$ where $A = \begin{bmatrix}0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 2 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 1\end{bmatrix}$.
2. Subtract $\lambda$ from each diagonal entry: $A - \lambda I = \begin{bmatrix}0-\lambda & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 2-\lambda & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 1-\lambda\end{bmatrix}$.
3. The determinant of this upper triangular matrix is the product of the diagonal entries: $(0-\lambda)(2-\lambda)(1-\lambda)$.
4. Set the determinant equal to zero: $(0-\lambda)(2-\lambda)(1-\lambda) = 0$.
5. The eigenvalues are the solutions: $\boxed{0,\ 1,\ 2}$.
