First, recall that $\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{3}{7} \\ \frac{4}{7} \end{bmatrix}$ is already an eigenvector of $A$; to find another eigenvector, we first find all eigenvalues of $A$ by solving $\det(A - \lambda I) = 0$: $\det\left(\begin{bmatrix}0.6-\lambda & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.7-\lambda\end{bmatrix}\right) = (0.6-\lambda)(0.7-\lambda) - (0.3)(0.4) = 0$.
2. Expanding the determinant: $(0.6-\lambda)(0.7-\lambda) - 0.12 = 0 \implies (0.6 \times 0.7) - 0.6\lambda - 0.7\lambda + \lambda^2 - 0.12 = 0 \implies 0.42 - 1.3\lambda + \lambda^2 - 0.12 = 0$.
3. Simplifying: $0.42 - 0.12 = 0.30$, so $\lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.30 = 0$.
4. Solving the quadratic equation: $\lambda = \frac{1.3 \pm \sqrt{(1.3)^2 - 4 \times 0.3}}{2} = \frac{1.3 \pm \sqrt{1.69 - 1.2}}{2} = \frac{1.3 \pm \sqrt{0.49}}{2} = \frac{1.3 \pm 0.7}{2}$.
5. Therefore, the eigenvalues are $\lambda_1 = 1.0$ and $\lambda_2 = 0.3$.
6. We already have $\mathbf{v}_1$ for $\lambda_1 = 1$; now, find an eigenvector $\mathbf{v}_2$ for $\lambda_2 = 0.3$ by solving $(A - 0.3I)\mathbf{v}_2 = 0$: $A - 0.3I = \begin{bmatrix}0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.4\end{bmatrix}$.
7. Set up the augmented matrix: $\left[\begin{array}{cc|c}0.3 & 0.3 & 0 \\ 0.4 & 0.4 & 0\end{array}\right]$.
8. Row 2 minus $\frac{4}{3}$ times Row 1: $R_2 \to R_2 - \frac{4}{3}R_1$: $\left[\begin{array}{cc|c}0.3 & 0.3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0\end{array}\right]$.
9. The first row gives $0.3x + 0.3y = 0 \implies x + y = 0$, so $y = -x$.
10. Thus, an eigenvector for $\lambda_2 = 0.3$ is $\mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}$ (or any nonzero scalar multiple).
11. Therefore, a basis for $\mathbb{R}^2$ consisting of $\mathbf{v}_1$ and another eigenvector $\mathbf{v}_2$ of $A$ is $\left\{\begin{bmatrix}\frac{3}{7} \\ \frac{4}{7}\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}\right\}$.
