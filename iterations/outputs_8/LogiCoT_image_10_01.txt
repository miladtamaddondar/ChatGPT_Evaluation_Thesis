The given matrix is \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ -3 & 9 & -1 & 5 \\ 2 & -6 & 4 & -3 \\ -4 & 12 & 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix} \) and its echelon form is \( U = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 & -7 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \); this is correct since echelon form is upper triangular with leading ones or pivots.

The pivot columns in the echelon form are columns 1, 3, and 4, so the corresponding columns in \( A \) form a basis for \( \text{Col }A \); these columns are \( \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix} \), \( \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \), and \( \begin{bmatrix} -4 \\ 5 \\ -3 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \).

Therefore, a basis for \( \text{Col }A \) is \( \left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} -4 \\ 5 \\ -3 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \right\} \); this is correct because the pivot columns of the original matrix always form a basis for the column space.  
4. To find a basis for \( \text{Nul }A \), solve \( Ax = 0 \) using the echelon form; let \( x = \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \\ x_4 \end{bmatrix} \).  
5. From the last nonzero row: \( 5x_4 = 0 \implies x_4 = 0 \); this is correct since the only solution is \( x_4 = 0 \).  
6. From the second nonzero row: \( 5x_3 - 7x_4 = 0 \implies 5x_3 = 0 \implies x_3 = 0 \); this is correct since \( x_4 = 0 \).

8. Let \( x_2 = t \), then \( x_1 = 3t, x_3 = 0, x_4 = 0 \), so the general solution is \( x = t \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \); this is correct since all free variables are accounted for.  
9. Therefore, a basis for \( \text{Nul }A \) is \( \left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \right\} \).
