1. To construct a \( 4 \times 3 \) matrix of rank 1, recall that such a matrix can be written as the outer product of a nonzero \( 4 \times 1 \) vector and a nonzero \( 1 \times 3 \) vector: \( A = \mathbf{u}\mathbf{v}^T \).
2. Choose \( \mathbf{u} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( \mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ 6 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \).
3. Compute the outer product \( A = \mathbf{u}\mathbf{v}^T = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 6 & 7 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \cdot 5 & 1 \cdot 6 & 1 \cdot 7 \\ 2 \cdot 5 & 2 \cdot 6 & 2 \cdot 7 \\ 3 \cdot 5 & 3 \cdot 6 & 3 \cdot 7 \\ 4 \cdot 5 & 4 \cdot 6 & 4 \cdot 7 \end{bmatrix} \).
4. Simplify to get \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 6 & 7 \\ 10 & 12 & 14 \\ 15 & 18 & 21 \\ 20 & 24 & 28 \end{bmatrix} \).
5. The final answer is \( \boxed{A = \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 6 & 7 \\ 10 & 12 & 14 \\ 15 & 18 & 21 \\ 20 & 24 & 28 \end{bmatrix}} \), which is a \( 4 \times 3 \) matrix of rank 1.
