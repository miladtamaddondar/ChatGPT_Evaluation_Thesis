1. First, we find the eigenvalues of \( A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix} \) by solving \( \det(A - \lambda I) = 0 \): \( \det \begin{pmatrix} 1-\lambda & -2 \\ 1 & 3-\lambda \end{pmatrix} = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0 \).  
2. The characteristic equation \( \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0 \) has roots \( \lambda = \frac{4 \pm \sqrt{16-20}}{2} = 2 \pm i \).  
3. For \( \lambda = 2+i \), solve \( (A - (2+i)I)\vec{v} = 0 \): \( A - (2+i)I = \begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix} \).  
4. Set up the system: \( (-1-i)v_1 - 2v_2 = 0 \) and \( v_1 + (1-i)v_2 = 0 \), so \( v_1 = - (1-i)v_2 \).  
5. Substitute \( v_1 \) into the first equation: \( (-1-i)(-(1-i)v_2) - 2v_2 = (1-i^2)v_2 - 2v_2 = (1+1)v_2 - 2v_2 = 0 \), so any nonzero \( v_2 \) works.  
6. Choose \( v_2 = 1 \), so eigenvector is \( \vec{v} = \begin{pmatrix} -(1-i) \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \).  
7. The complex eigenvalues mean \( A \) is similar to \( C = \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix} \) where \( a = 2, b = 1 \).  
8. To find \( P \), take the real and imaginary parts of the eigenvector: \( \vec{v}_1 = \operatorname{Re}(\vec{v}) = \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}, \vec{v}_2 = \operatorname{Im}(\vec{v}) = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix} \).  
9. Form \( P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \).  
10. The answer is: \( P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \), \( C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \), and \( A = P C P^{-1} \).
