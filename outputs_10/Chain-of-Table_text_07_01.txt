1. Recognize that \( Q r_1, Q r_2, \dots, Q r_p \) are the products of \( Q \) with each \( r_i \), so the matrix \( \left[ Q r_1 \; Q r_2 \; \dots \; Q r_p \right] \) can be written as \( Q \) times the matrix \( \left[ r_1 \; r_2 \; \dots \; r_p \right] \).  
2. Therefore, \( \left[ Q r_1 \; Q r_2 \; \dots \; Q r_p \right] = Q \left[ r_1 \; r_2 \; \dots \; r_p \right] \).
