The problem asks for an invertible matrix \( P \) and a matrix \( C \) of the form \( \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix} \) such that \( A = PCP^{-1} \) for \( A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix} \); this is a similarity transformation to bring \( A \) into a canonical form.  
2. First, find the eigenvalues of \( A \) by solving \( \det(A - \lambda I) = 0 \): \( \det\left(\begin{pmatrix} 1-\lambda & -2 \\ 1 & 3-\lambda \end{pmatrix}\right) = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0 \).  
3. The characteristic equation \( \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0 \) has roots \( \lambda = 2 \pm i \); verify by quadratic formula: \( \lambda = \frac{4 \pm \sqrt{16-20}}{2} = 2 \pm i \).  
4. Since \( A \) has complex conjugate eigenvalues, it is similar to a real matrix of the form \( \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix} \) where \( a = \text{Re}(\lambda) = 2 \) and \( b = \text{Im}(\lambda) = 1 \), so \( C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \).  
5. Next, find an eigenvector for \( \lambda = 2+i \): solve \( (A - (2+i)I)\vec{v} = 0 \), i.e., \( \begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix}\vec{v} = 0 \).  
6. Let \( \vec{v} = \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix} \), then \( (-1-i)x - 2y = 0 \) and \( x + (1-i)y = 0 \); from the first, \( x = \frac{-2}{1+i}y \).  
7. Rationalize denominator: \( \frac{-2}{1+i} = \frac{-2(1-i)}{(1+i)(1-i)} = \frac{-2(1-i)}{2} = -1 + i \), so \( x = (-1+i)y \).  
8. Choose \( y = 1 \), then eigenvector is \( \vec{v} = \begin{pmatrix} -1+i \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \); its real and imaginary parts are \( \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \) and \( \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix} \).  
9. Form \( P \) from these real and imaginary parts: \( P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \); verify that \( P \) is invertible by checking \( \det P = (-1)(0) - (1)(1) = -1 \neq 0 \).  
10. The similarity transformation is \( A = PCP^{-1} \) with \( C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \) and \( P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \); this is the required solution.
