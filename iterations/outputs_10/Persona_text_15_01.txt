1. First, find the eigenvalues of \(A\) by solving \(\det(A - \lambda I) = 0\), so \(\det\begin{pmatrix} 1-\lambda & -2 \\ 1 & 3-\lambda \end{pmatrix} = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0\).
2. Solve the quadratic equation \(\lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0\) to get \(\lambda = 2 \pm i\).
3. Since the eigenvalues are complex conjugates, \(C\) will be \(\begin{pmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}\).
4. Find an eigenvector for \(\lambda = 2 + i\) by solving \((A - (2+i)I)\vec{v} = 0\), so \(A - (2+i)I = \begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix}\).
5. Let \(\vec{v} = \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix}\), then \((-1-i)x - 2y = 0\) and \(x + (1-i)y = 0\).
6. From the first equation, \(x = \frac{-2}{1+i}y\); rationalize denominator: \(\frac{-2}{1+i} = \frac{-2(1-i)}{(1+i)(1-i)} = \frac{-2(1-i)}{2} = -1 + i\).
7. So, an eigenvector is \(\vec{v} = \begin{pmatrix} -1+i \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}\).
8. Take the real and imaginary parts to form \(P\): real part \(\begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}\), imaginary part \(\begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}\), so \(P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}\).
9. The matrix \(C\) is \(\begin{pmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}\) as constructed from the eigenvalues.
10. Therefore, \(A = P C P^{-1}\) with \(P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}\) and \(C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}\).
