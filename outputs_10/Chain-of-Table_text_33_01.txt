1. The matrix $A$ has $4$ rows and $2$ columns, so $A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -6 \\ -1 & 3 \\ -4 & 12 \\ 3 & -9 \end{bmatrix}$ is a $4 \times 2$ matrix.

2. The null space $\text{Nul }A$ consists of all solutions $x \in \mathbb{R}^2$ to $Ax = 0$, so $\text{Nul }A$ is a subspace of $\mathbb{R}^2$, thus $k = 2$ for part (a).

3. The column space $\text{Col }A$ is the span of the columns of $A$, which are vectors in $\mathbb{R}^4$, so $\text{Col }A$ is a subspace of $\mathbb{R}^4$, thus $k = 4$ for part (b).
