1. To find another eigenvector, first compute the eigenvalues of \(A\) by solving \(\det(A - \lambda I) = 0\): \(\det\left(\begin{bmatrix}0.6-\lambda & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.7-\lambda\end{bmatrix}\right) = 0\).
2. Expand the determinant: \((0.6-\lambda)(0.7-\lambda) - (0.3)(0.4) = 0\).
3. Simplify: \(0.42 - 0.6\lambda - 0.7\lambda + \lambda^2 - 0.12 = 0\).
4. Combine like terms: \(\lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3 = 0\).
5. Solve the quadratic equation: \(\lambda = \frac{1.3 \pm \sqrt{1.69 - 1.2}}{2} = \frac{1.3 \pm 0.7}{2}\).
6. The eigenvalues are \(\lambda_1 = 1\) and \(\lambda_2 = 0.3\).
7. \(\mathbf{v}_1\) is already given as an eigenvector for \(\lambda_1 = 1\): \(\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix}3/7 \\ 4/7\end{bmatrix}\).
8. To find an eigenvector for \(\lambda_2 = 0.3\), solve \((A - 0.3I)\mathbf{v}_2 = 0\): \(\begin{bmatrix}0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.4\end{bmatrix}\mathbf{v}_2 = 0\).
9. Write the system: \(0.3x + 0.3y = 0\) and \(0.4x + 0.4y = 0\).
10. Both equations reduce to \(x + y = 0\), so take \(x = 1, y = -1\).
11. Thus, an eigenvector for \(\lambda_2 = 0.3\) is \(\mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}\).
12. Therefore, a basis for \(\mathbb{R}^2\) consisting of eigenvectors of \(A\) is \(\left\{ \begin{bmatrix}3/7 \\ 4/7\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix} \right\}\).
