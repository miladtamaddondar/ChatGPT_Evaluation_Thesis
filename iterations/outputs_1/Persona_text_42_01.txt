1. To write $\mathbf{y}$ as the sum of a vector in $W$ and a vector orthogonal to $W$, we need to find the orthogonal projection of $\mathbf{y}$ onto $W$ and subtract it from $\mathbf{y}$ to get the orthogonal component.
2. First, we check if $\{\mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_2, \mathbf{u}_3\}$ are linearly independent by forming the matrix $U = [\mathbf{u}_1 \ \mathbf{u}_2 \ \mathbf{u}_3]$ and checking its rank; since the vectors are not scalar multiples and the determinant of the $3 \times 3$ minor is nonzero, they are linearly independent and span a 3-dimensional subspace $W$.
3. Let $\mathbf{y}_W = a_1 \mathbf{u}_1 + a_2 \mathbf{u}_2 + a_3 \mathbf{u}_3$ be the projection of $\mathbf{y}$ onto $W$; to find $a_1, a_2, a_3$, we solve $U \mathbf{a} = \mathbf{y}$ in the least squares sense, i.e., $\mathbf{a} = (U^T U)^{-1} U^T \mathbf{y}$.
4. Compute $U^T U$:\[U^T U = \begin{bmatrix}\mathbf{u}_1^T \mathbf{u}_1 & \mathbf{u}_1^T \mathbf{u}_2 & \mathbf{u}_1^T \mathbf{u}_3 \\\mathbf{u}_2^T \mathbf{u}_1 & \mathbf{u}_2^T \mathbf{u}_2 & \mathbf{u}_2^T \mathbf{u}_3 \\\mathbf{u}_3^T \mathbf{u}_1 & \mathbf{u}_3^T \mathbf{u}_2 & \mathbf{u}_3^T \mathbf{u}_3\end{bmatrix}= \begin{bmatrix}3 & -2 & 0 \\-2 & 15 & 1 \\0 & 1 & 3\end{bmatrix}\]
5. Compute $U^T \mathbf{y}$:\[U^T \mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix}\mathbf{u}_1^T \mathbf{y} \\\mathbf{u}_2^T \mathbf{y} \\\mathbf{u}_3^T \mathbf{y}\end{bmatrix}= \begin{bmatrix}4 + 3 + 0 - 1 \\-4 + 9 + 3 + 2 \\-4 + 0 + 3 - 1\end{bmatrix}= \begin{bmatrix}6 \\10 \\-2\end{bmatrix}\]
6. Solve $(U^T U)\mathbf{a} = U^T \mathbf{y}$, i.e., \[\begin{bmatrix}3 & -2 & 0 \\-2 & 15 & 1 \\0 & 1 & 3\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}a_1 \\ a_2 \\ a_3\end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix}6 \\ 10 \\ -2\end{bmatrix}\]
7. From the third equation: $a_2 + 3a_3 = -2 \implies a_2 = -2 - 3a_3$.
8. Substitute $a_2$ into the second equation: $-2a_1 + 15a_2 + a_3 = 10 \implies -2a_1 + 15(-2 - 3a_3) + a_3 = 10 \implies -2a_1 -30 -45a_3 + a_3 = 10 \implies -2a_1 -44a_3 = 40 \implies a_1 = -22a_3 - 20$.
9. Substitute $a_1$ and $a_2$ into the first equation: $3a_1 - 2a_2 = 6 \implies 3(-22a_3 - 20) - 2(-2 - 3a_3) = 6 \implies -66a_3 - 60 + 4 + 6a_3 = 6 \implies -60a_3 - 56 = 6 \implies -60a_3 = 62 \implies a_3 = -\frac{31}{30}$.
10. Substitute $a_3$ into $a_2 = -2 - 3a_3$: $a_2 = -2 - 3(-\frac{31}{30}) = -2 + \frac{93}{30} = -2 + 3.1 = 1.1 = \frac{11}{10}$.
11. Substitute $a_3$ into $a_1 = -22a_3 - 20$: $a_1 = -22(-\frac{31}{30}) - 20 = \frac{682}{30} - 20 = \frac{682 - 600}{30} = \frac{82}{30} = \frac{41}{15}$.
12. The projection is $\mathbf{y}_W = a_1 \mathbf{u}_1 + a_2 \mathbf{u}_2 + a_3 \mathbf{u}_3 = \frac{41}{15}\mathbf{u}_1 + \frac{11}{10}\mathbf{u}_2 - \frac{31}{30}\mathbf{u}_3$.
13. Compute $\mathbf{y}_W = \frac{41}{15} \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} + \frac{11}{10} \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix} - \frac{31}{30} \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{41}{15} - \frac{11}{10} + \frac{31}{30} \\ \frac{41}{15} + \frac{33}{10} + 0 \\ 0 + \frac{11}{10} - \frac{31}{30} \\ \frac{41}{15} - \frac{22}{10} - \frac{31}{30} \end{bmatrix}$.
14. Simplify each entry: First entry: $\frac{41}{15} - \frac{11}{10} + \frac{31}{30} = \frac{82}{30} - \frac{33}{30} + \frac{31}{30} = \frac{80}{30} = \frac{8}{3}$; Second entry: $\frac{41}{15} + \frac{33}{10} = \frac{82}{30} + \frac{99}{30} = \frac{181}{30}$; Third entry: $\frac{11}{10} - \frac{31}{30} = \frac{33}{30} - \frac{31}{30} = \frac{2}{30} = \frac{1}{15}$; Fourth entry: $\frac{41}{15} - \frac{22}{10} - \frac{31}{30} = \frac{82}{30} - \frac{66}{30} - \frac{31}{30} = \frac{82 - 66 - 31}{30} = \frac{-15}{30} = -\frac{1}{2}$.
15. Thus, $\mathbf{y}_W = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{8}{3} \\ \frac{181}{30} \\ \frac{1}{15} \\ -\frac{1}{2} \end{bmatrix}$.
16. The orthogonal component is $\mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} \frac{8}{3} \\ \frac{181}{30} \\ \frac{1}{15} \\ -\frac{1}{2} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 - \frac{8}{3} \\ 3 - \frac{181}{30} \\ 3 - \frac{1}{15} \\ -1 + \frac{1}{2} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{12 - 8}{3} \\ \frac{90 - 181}{30} \\ \frac{45 - 1}{15} \\ -\frac{2 - 1}{2} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{4}{3} \\ -\frac{91}{30} \\ \frac{44}{15} \\ -\frac{1}{2} \end{bmatrix}$.
17. Therefore, the final answer is: $\boxed{\mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{8}{3} \\ \frac{181}{30} \\ \frac{1}{15} \\ -\frac{1}{2} \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} \frac{4}{3} \\ -\frac{91}{30} \\ \frac{44}{15} \\ -\frac{1}{2} \end{bmatrix}}$ where the first vector is in $W$ and the second is orthogonal to $W$.
