1. The standard basis for \( \mathbb{P}_2 \) is \( \{1, t, t^2\} \).
2. Write each basis vector in \( \mathcal{B} \) in terms of the standard basis: \( 1 - 3t^2 = 1 \cdot 1 + 0 \cdot t + (-3) \cdot t^2 \), \( 2 + t - 5t^2 = 2 \cdot 1 + 1 \cdot t + (-5) \cdot t^2 \), \( 1 + 2t = 1 \cdot 1 + 2 \cdot t + 0 \cdot t^2 \).
3. The change-of-coordinates matrix from \( \mathcal{B} \) to the standard basis is formed by using these as columns: \( P = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 \\ -3 & -5 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \).
4. To write \( t^2 \) as a linear combination of the polynomials in \( \mathcal{B} \), solve \( c_1(1 - 3t^2) + c_2(2 + t - 5t^2) + c_3(1 + 2t) = t^2 \).
5. Expand and collect like terms: \( (c_1 + 2c_2 + c_3) + (c_2 + 2c_3)t + (-3c_1 - 5c_2)t^2 = 0 + 0t + 1t^2 \).
6. Set up the system: \( c_1 + 2c_2 + c_3 = 0 \), \( c_2 + 2c_3 = 0 \), \( -3c_1 - 5c_2 = 1 \).
7. Solve \( c_2 + 2c_3 = 0 \implies c_2 = -2c_3 \).
8. Substitute \( c_2 \) into the first equation: \( c_1 + 2(-2c_3) + c_3 = 0 \implies c_1 - 4c_3 + c_3 = 0 \implies c_1 = 3c_3 \).
9. Substitute \( c_1 \) and \( c_2 \) into the third equation: \( -3(3c_3) - 5(-2c_3) = 1 \implies -9c_3 + 10c_3 = 1 \implies c_3 = 1 \).
10. Then \( c_1 = 3 \) and \( c_2 = -2 \).
11. Thus, \( t^2 = 3(1 - 3t^2) - 2(2 + t - 5t^2) + 1(1 + 2t) \) in terms of the basis \( \mathcal{B} \).
