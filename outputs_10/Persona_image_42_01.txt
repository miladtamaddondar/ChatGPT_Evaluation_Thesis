The problem asks us to write \(\mathbf{y}\) as the sum of a vector in the subspace \(W\) spanned by \(\mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_2, \mathbf{u}_3\) and a vector orthogonal to \(W\), i.e., \(\mathbf{y} = \mathbf{y}_W + \mathbf{y}_{W^\perp}\), where \(\mathbf{y}_W \in W\) and \(\mathbf{y}_{W^\perp}\) is orthogonal to \(W\).  
2. To do this, we need to find the orthogonal projection of \(\mathbf{y}\) onto \(W\), which is \(\mathbf{y}_W\), and then \(\mathbf{y}_{W^\perp} = \mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W\).  
3. First, check if \(\mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_2, \mathbf{u}_3\) are linearly independent; if so, they form a basis for \(W\), otherwise, use a basis subset.  
4. Form the matrix \(U = [\mathbf{u}_1 \ \mathbf{u}_2 \ \mathbf{u}_3]\) and solve \(U\mathbf{c} = \mathbf{y}\) in the least squares sense, i.e., \(\mathbf{c} = (U^T U)^{-1} U^T \mathbf{y}\).  
5. Write \(U = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\) and \(\mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix}\).  
6. Compute \(U^T U = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 15 & 1 \\ 1 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix}\).  
7. Compute \(U^T \mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 7 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}\).  
8. Solve for \(\mathbf{c}\) in \( (U^T U) \mathbf{c} = U^T \mathbf{y} \), i.e., \(\begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 15 & 1 \\ 1 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} c_1 \\ c_2 \\ c_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 7 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}\).  
9. Solve the system: From the second row, \(15c_2 + c_3 = 7 \implies c_3 = 7 - 15c_2\); substitute into the third row: \(c_1 + c_2 + 4c_3 = 2 \implies c_1 + c_2 + 4(7-15c_2) = 2 \implies c_1 + c_2 + 28 - 60c_2 = 2 \implies c_1 - 59c_2 = 2 - 28 = -26 \implies c_1 = -26 + 59c_2\).  
10. Substitute \(c_1\) and \(c_3\) into the first row: \(3c_1 + c_3 = 6 \implies 3(-26 + 59c_2) + (7 - 15c_2) = 6 \implies -78 + 177c_2 + 7 - 15c_2 = 6 \implies -71 + 162c_2 = 6 \implies 162c_2 = 77 \implies c_2 = \frac{77}{162}\).

12. \(c_3 = 7 - 15 \cdot \frac{77}{162} = 7 - \frac{1155}{162} = \frac{1134 - 1155}{162} = \frac{-21}{162} = -\frac{7}{54}\).  
13. Thus, \(\mathbf{c} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{331}{162} \\ \frac{77}{162} \\ -\frac{7}{54} \end{bmatrix}\).  
14. The projection \(\mathbf{y}_W = U\mathbf{c} = \frac{331}{162}\mathbf{u}_1 + \frac{77}{162}\mathbf{u}_2 - \frac{7}{54}\mathbf{u}_3\).

Compute \(\mathbf{y}_W = \frac{331}{162}\begin{bmatrix}1\\1\\0\\1\end{bmatrix} + \frac{77}{162}\begin{bmatrix}-1\\3\\1\\-2\end{bmatrix} - \frac{7}{54}\begin{bmatrix}-1\\0\\1\\1\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{331}{162} - \frac{77}{162} + \frac{7}{54} \\ \frac{331}{162} + \frac{231}{162} + 0 \\ 0 + \frac{77}{162} - \frac{7}{54} \\ \frac{331}{162} - \frac{154}{162} - \frac{7}{54} \end{bmatrix}\).

18.
