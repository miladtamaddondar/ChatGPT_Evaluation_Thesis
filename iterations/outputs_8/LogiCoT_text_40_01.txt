To find the change-of-coordinates matrix from $\mathcal{B}$ to the standard basis $\mathcal{E} = \{1, t, t^2\}$, write each vector in $\mathcal{B}$ in terms of $\mathcal{E}$: $1 - 3t^2 = 1 \cdot 1 + 0 \cdot t + (-3) \cdot t^2$, $2 + t - 5t^2 = 2 \cdot 1 + 1 \cdot t + (-5) \cdot t^2$, $1 + 2t = 1 \cdot 1 + 2 \cdot t + 0 \cdot t^2$.
2. The coordinate vectors of the basis elements in $\mathcal{E}$ are: $[1 - 3t^2]_\mathcal{E} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix}$, $[2 + t - 5t^2]_\mathcal{E} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \\ -5 \end{bmatrix}$, $[1 + 2t]_\mathcal{E} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}$.
3. The change-of-coordinates matrix from $\mathcal{B}$ to $\mathcal{E}$, denoted $P_{\mathcal{E} \leftarrow \mathcal{B}}$, is formed by placing these coordinate vectors as columns: $P_{\mathcal{E} \leftarrow \mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 \\ -3 & -5 & 0 \end{bmatrix}$.
4. To write $t^2$ as a linear combination of the polynomials in $\mathcal{B}$, let $t^2 = a(1 - 3t^2) + b(2 + t - 5t^2) + c(1 + 2t)$ for scalars $a, b, c$.
5. Expand and collect like terms: $t^2 = (a + 2b + c) \cdot 1 + (b + 2c) \cdot t + (-3a - 5b) \cdot t^2$.
6. Set up the system by matching coefficients with $t^2 = 0 \cdot 1 + 0 \cdot t + 1 \cdot t^2$: $a + 2b + c = 0$, $b + 2c = 0$, $-3a - 5b = 1$.
7. Solve $b + 2c = 0$ for $b$: $b = -2c$.
8. Substitute $b = -2c$ into $a + 2b + c = 0$: $a + 2(-2c) + c = 0 \implies a - 4c + c = 0 \implies a = 3c$.
9. Substitute $a = 3c$ and $b = -2c$ into $-3a - 5b = 1$: $-3(3c) - 5(-2c) = 1 \implies -9c + 10c = 1 \implies c = 1$.
10. Substitute $c = 1$ into $a = 3c$ and $b = -2c$: $a = 3$, $b = -2$.
11. Therefore, $t^2 = 3(1 - 3t^2) - 2(2 + t - 5t^2) + 1(1 + 2t)$ as a linear combination of the polynomials in $\mathcal{B}$.
