1. To write $\mathbf{y}$ as the sum of a vector in $W$ and a vector orthogonal to $W$, we first form the matrix $A = [\mathbf{u}_1\ \mathbf{u}_2\ \mathbf{u}_3] = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$.
2. We seek $\mathbf{y} = \mathbf{y}_W + \mathbf{y}^\perp$ where $\mathbf{y}_W \in W$ and $\mathbf{y}^\perp$ is orthogonal to $W$, so $\mathbf{y}_W = A\mathbf{c}$ for some $\mathbf{c}$.
3. The best choice for $\mathbf{y}_W$ is the orthogonal projection of $\mathbf{y}$ onto $W$, given by $\mathbf{y}_W = A(A^T A)^{-1}A^T \mathbf{y}$.
4. Compute $A^T A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 & 1 & -2 \\ -1 & 0 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 15 & 5 \\ 0 & 5 & 4 \end{bmatrix}$.
5. Compute $A^T \mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 & 1 & -2 \\ -1 & 0 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 2 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}$.
6. Solve $(A^T A)\mathbf{c} = A^T \mathbf{y}$, so $\begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 15 & 5 \\ 0 & 5 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} c_1 \\ c_2 \\ c_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 2 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}$.
7. From the first row, $3c_1 = 6 \implies c_1 = 2$.
8. From the third row, $5c_2 + 4c_3 = 0 \implies c_3 = -\frac{5}{4}c_2$.
9. Substitute $c_3$ into the second row: $15c_2 + 5c_3 = 2 \implies 15c_2 + 5(-\frac{5}{4}c_2) = 2 \implies 15c_2 - \frac{25}{4}c_2 = 2 \implies \frac{60-25}{4}c_2 = 2 \implies \frac{35}{4}c_2 = 2 \implies c_2 = \frac{8}{35}$.
10. Substitute $c_2$ into $c_3$: $c_3 = -\frac{5}{4} \cdot \frac{8}{35} = -\frac{40}{140} = -\frac{2}{7}$.
11. Now, $\mathbf{c} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ \frac{8}{35} \\ -\frac{2}{7} \end{bmatrix}$.
12. Compute $\mathbf{y}_W = A\mathbf{c} = 2\mathbf{u}_1 + \frac{8}{35}\mathbf{u}_2 - \frac{2}{7}\mathbf{u}_3$.
13. Calculate $2\mathbf{u}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 2 \\ 0 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}$, $\frac{8}{35}\mathbf{u}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} -\frac{8}{35} \\ \frac{24}{35} \\ \frac{8}{35} \\ -\frac{16}{35} \end{bmatrix}$, $-\frac{2}{7}\mathbf{u}_3 = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{7} \\ 0 \\ -\frac{2}{7} \\ -\frac{2}{7} \end{bmatrix}$.
14. Add the vectors: $\mathbf{y}_W = \begin{bmatrix} 2 - \frac{8}{35} + \frac{2}{7} \\ 2 + \frac{24}{35} + 0 \\ 0 + \frac{8}{35} - \frac{2}{7} \\ 2 - \frac{16}{35} - \frac{2}{7} \end{bmatrix}$.
15. Simplify each entry: $2 - \frac{8}{35} + \frac{2}{7} = 2 - \frac{8}{35} + \frac{10}{35} = 2 + \frac{2}{35} = \frac{72}{35}$.
16. $2 + \frac{24}{35} = \frac{70}{35} + \frac{24}{35} = \frac{94}{35}$.
17. $0 + \frac{8}{35} - \frac{10}{35} = -\frac{2}{35}$.
18. $2 - \frac{16}{35} - \frac{10}{35} = 2 - \frac{26}{35} = \frac{70}{35} - \frac{26}{35} = \frac{44}{35}$.
19. Thus, $\mathbf{y}_W = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{72}{35} \\ \frac{94}{35} \\ -\frac{2}{35} \\ \frac{44}{35} \end{bmatrix}$.
20. The orthogonal component is $\mathbf{y}^\perp = \mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} \frac{72}{35} \\ \frac{94}{35} \\ -\frac{2}{35} \\ \frac{44}{35} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{68}{35} \\ \frac{11}{35} \\ \frac{107}{35} \\ -\frac{79}{35} \end{bmatrix}$.
21. The final answer is $\boxed{\mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{72}{35} \\ \frac{94}{35} \\ -\frac{2}{35} \\ \frac{44}{35} \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} \frac{68}{35} \\ \frac{11}{35} \\ \frac{107}{35} \\ -\frac{79}{35} \end{bmatrix}}$ where the first vector is in $W$ and the second is orthogonal to $W$.
