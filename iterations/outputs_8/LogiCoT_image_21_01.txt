1. We are given \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -2 \\ -2 & 1 & 6 \\ 3 & -2 & -5 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( \mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 7 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} \); we need to solve \( A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{b} \) and check uniqueness by examining the invertibility of \( A \).  
2. Compute the determinant of \( A \): \( \det(A) = 1 \begin{vmatrix} 1 & 6 \\ -2 & -5 \end{vmatrix} - 0 \begin{vmatrix} -2 & 6 \\ 3 & -5 \end{vmatrix} + (-2) \begin{vmatrix} -2 & 1 \\ 3 & -2 \end{vmatrix} \).  
3. Calculate the minors: \( \begin{vmatrix} 1 & 6 \\ -2 & -5 \end{vmatrix} = (1)(-5) - (6)(-2) = -5 + 12 = 7 \), and \( \begin{vmatrix} -2 & 1 \\ 3 & -2 \end{vmatrix} = (-2)(-2) - (1)(3) = 4 - 3 = 1 \).  
4. Substitute back: \( \det(A) = 1 \cdot 7 + 0 \cdot (\cdot) + (-2) \cdot 1 = 7 - 2 = 5 \).  
5. Since \( \det(A) \neq 0 \), \( A \) is invertible, so the solution \( \mathbf{x} \) is unique by the invertible matrix theorem.  
6. The solution is \( \mathbf{x} = A^{-1} \mathbf{b} \); to find \( \mathbf{x} \), use the formula \( \mathbf{x} = A^{-1} \mathbf{b} \).  
7. Find \( A^{-1} \) using the formula \( A^{-1} = \frac{1}{\det(A)} \text{adj}(A) \); compute the adjugate matrix by finding the cofactors of \( A \).  
8. Compute the cofactor matrix:     - \( C_{11} = \begin{vmatrix} 1 & 6 \\ -2 & -5 \end{vmatrix} = 7 \),     - \( C_{12} = -\begin{vmatrix} -2 & 6 \\ 3 & -5 \end{vmatrix} = -((-2)(-5) - (6)(3)) = -(10 - 18) = 8 \),     - \( C_{13} = \begin{vmatrix} -2 & 1 \\ 3 & -2 \end{vmatrix} = 1 \),     - \( C_{21} = -\begin{vmatrix} 0 & -2 \\ -2 & -5 \end{vmatrix} = -((0)(-5) - (-2)(-2)) = -(0 - 4) = 4 \),     - \( C_{22} = \begin{vmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 3 & -5 \end{vmatrix} = (1)(-5) - (-2)(3) = -5 + 6 = 1 \),     - \( C_{23} = -\begin{vmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 3 & -2 \end{vmatrix} = -((1)(-2) - (0)(3)) = -(-2 - 0) = 2 \),     - \( C_{31} = \begin{vmatrix} 0 & -2 \\ 1 & 6 \end{vmatrix} = (0)(6) - (-2)(1) = 0 + 2 = 2 \),     - \( C_{32} = -\begin{vmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ -2 & 6 \end{vmatrix} = -((1)(6) - (-2)(-2)) = -(6 - 4) = -2 \),     - \( C_{33} = \begin{vmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ -2 & 1 \end{vmatrix} = (1)(1) - (0)(-2) = 1 \).  
9. The cofactor matrix is \( \begin{bmatrix} 7 & 8 & 1 \\ 4 & 1 & 2 \\ 2 & -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \); the adjugate is its transpose: \( \text{adj}(A) = \begin{bmatrix} 7 & 4 & 2 \\ 8 & 1 & -2 \\ 1 & 2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \).  
10. The inverse is \( A^{-1} = \frac{1}{5} \begin{bmatrix} 7 & 4 & 2 \\ 8 & 1 & -2 \\ 1 & 2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \).  
11. Compute \( \mathbf{x} = A^{-1} \mathbf{b} = \frac{1}{5} \begin{bmatrix} 7 & 4 & 2 \\ 8 & 1 & -2 \\ 1 & 2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 7 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} \).  
12. Multiply:      - First row: \( 7(-1) + 4(7) + 2(-3) = -7 + 28 - 6 = 15 \),      - Second row: \( 8(-1) + 1(7) + (-2)(-3) = -8 + 7 + 6 = 5 \),      - Third row: \( 1(-1) + 2(7) + 1(-3) = -1 + 14 - 3 = 10 \).  
13. So \( \mathbf{x} = \frac{1}{5} \begin{bmatrix} 15 \\ 5 \\ 10 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \).  
14. The unique solution is \( \boxed{\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}} \), since \( A \) is invertible.
