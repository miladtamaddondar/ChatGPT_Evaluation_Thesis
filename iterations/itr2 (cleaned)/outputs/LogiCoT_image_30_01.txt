3. Substitute \( x_1 \) into the second equation: \( -(-1 + 2x_2) + 3x_2 = 4 \implies 1 - 2x_2 + 3x_2 = 4 \implies 1 + x_2 = 4 \implies x_2 = 3 \).  
4. Substitute \( x_2 = 3 \) into the expression for \( x_1 \): \( x_1 = -1 + 2 \times 3 = -1 + 6 = 5 \).  
5. Verify with the third equation: \( 3x_1 - 2x_2 = 3 \times 5 - 2 \times 3 = 15 - 6 = 9 \), which matches the given value, so the solution is correct.
