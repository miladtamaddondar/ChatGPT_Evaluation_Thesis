1. Recognize that the matrix \(\left[ Q r_1 \; Q r_2 \; \dots \; Q r_p \right]\) consists of the images of the vectors \(r_1, \dots, r_p\) under the linear transformation represented by \(Q\).  
2. Note that multiplying \(Q\) by the matrix \(\left[ r_1 \; r_2 \; \dots \; r_p \right]\) (which is an \(n \times p\) matrix) yields the desired matrix: \(\left[ Q r_1 \; Q r_2 \; \dots \; Q r_p \right] = Q \left[ r_1 \; r_2 \; \dots \; r_p \right]\).  
3. Therefore, the matrix \(\left[ Q r_1 \; Q r_2 \; \dots \; Q r_p \right]\) can be written as the product of the \(m \times n\) matrix \(Q\) and the \(n \times p\) matrix \(\left[ r_1 \; r_2 \; \dots \; r_p \right]\): \(\left[ Q r_1 \; Q r_2 \; \dots \; Q r_p \right] = Q \left[ r_1 \; r_2 \; \dots \; r_p \right]\).
