1. Write out the matrices \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 5 \\ -3 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\) and \(B = \begin{bmatrix} 4 & -5 \\ 3 & k \end{bmatrix}\). 2. Compute \(AB = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 5 \\ -3 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 & -5 \\ 3 & k \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \cdot 4 + 5 \cdot 3 & 2 \cdot (-5) + 5 \cdot k \\ -3 \cdot 4 + 1 \cdot 3 & -3 \cdot (-5) + 1 \cdot k \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 8 + 15 & -10 + 5k \\ -12 + 3 & 15 + k \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 23 & -10 + 5k \\ -9 & 15 + k \end{bmatrix}\). 3. Compute \(BA = \begin{bmatrix} 4 & -5 \\ 3 & k \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 5 \\ -3 & 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \cdot 2 + (-5) \cdot (-3) & 4 \cdot 5 + (-5) \cdot 1 \\ 3 \cdot 2 + k \cdot (-3) & 3 \cdot 5 + k \cdot 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 8 + 15 & 20 - 5 \\ 6 - 3k & 15 + k \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 23 & 15 \\ 6 - 3k & 15 + k \end{bmatrix}\). 4. Set \(AB = BA\) and equate corresponding elements: \(23 = 23\), \(-10 + 5k = 15\), \(-9 = 6 - 3k\), \(15 + k = 15 + k\). 5. Solve \(-10 + 5k = 15\) to get \(5k = 25 \implies k = 5\). 6. Solve \(-9 = 6 - 3k\) to get \(-9 - 6 = -3k \implies -15 = -3k \implies k = 5\). 7. Both equations give \(k = 5\), so the value of \(k\) that makes \(AB = BA\) is \(\boxed{5}\).
