1. Find the eigenvalues of \( A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix} \): Solve \( \det(A - \lambda I) = 0 \), so \( \det\begin{pmatrix} 1-\lambda & -2 \\ 1 & 3-\lambda \end{pmatrix} = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0 \).
2. Solve the characteristic equation \( \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0 \): The roots are \( \lambda = 2 \pm i \).
3. Find an eigenvector for \( \lambda = 2+i \): Solve \( (A - (2+i)I)\vec{v} = 0 \), so \( \begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix} \vec{v} = 0 \).
4. Set \( v_1 = 1 \), then \( (-1-i) \cdot 1 - 2v_2 = 0 \implies v_2 = \frac{-1-i}{2} \), so the eigenvector is \( \vec{v} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ \frac{-1-i}{2} \end{pmatrix} \).
5. The real and imaginary parts of the eigenvector form the columns of \( P \): \( P = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ -\frac{1}{2} & -\frac{1}{2} \end{pmatrix} + i\begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -\frac{1}{2} & \frac{1}{2} \end{pmatrix} \), so the real matrix is \( P = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ -\frac{1}{2} & -\frac{1}{2} \end{pmatrix} \) and \( \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -\frac{1}{2} & \frac{1}{2} \end{pmatrix} \).
6. The corresponding \( C \) matrix is \( C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \).
7. Final answer: \( P = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ -\frac{1}{2} & -\frac{1}{2} \end{pmatrix} \) and \( C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \) such that \( A = P C P^{-1} \).
