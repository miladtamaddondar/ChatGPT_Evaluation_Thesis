1. We are given \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -2 \\ -2 & 1 & 6 \\ 3 & -2 & -5 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( \mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 7 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} \), and need to solve \( A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{b} \) for \( \mathbf{x} \).  
2. To determine if the solution is unique, check if \( A \) is invertible by computing \( \det(A) \).  
3. Compute \( \det(A) = 1 \begin{vmatrix} 1 & 6 \\ -2 & -5 \end{vmatrix} - 0 \begin{vmatrix} -2 & 6 \\ 3 & -5 \end{vmatrix} + (-2) \begin{vmatrix} -2 & 1 \\ 3 & -2 \end{vmatrix} \).  
4. Calculate minors: \( \begin{vmatrix} 1 & 6 \\ -2 & -5 \end{vmatrix} = (1)(-5) - (6)(-2) = -5 + 12 = 7 \); \( \begin{vmatrix} -2 & 1 \\ 3 & -2 \end{vmatrix} = (-2)(-2) - (1)(3) = 4 - 3 = 1 \).  
5. Substitute minors: \( \det(A) = 1 \cdot 7 + 0 \cdot (\cdot) + (-2) \cdot 1 = 7 - 2 = 5 \).  
6. Since \( \det(A) = 5 \neq 0 \), \( A \) is invertible and the solution is unique.  
7. The solution is \( \mathbf{x} = A^{-1}\mathbf{b} \); compute \( A^{-1} \) using the formula \( A^{-1} = \frac{1}{\det(A)} \text{adj}(A) \).  
8. Compute the matrix of cofactors for \( A \):    First row: \( C_{11} = 7, C_{12} = -8, C_{13} = 1 \);    Second row: \( C_{21} = 4, C_{22} = 1, C_{23} = 2 \);    Third row: \( C_{31} = 6, C_{32} = 2, C_{33} = 1 \).  
9. The cofactor matrix is \( \begin{bmatrix} 7 & 8 & 1 \\ -4 & 1 & -2 \\ 6 & 2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \) (note the sign alternation).  
10. The adjugate is the transpose: \( \text{adj}(A) = \begin{bmatrix} 7 & -4 & 6 \\ 8 & 1 & 2 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \).  
11. Thus, \( A^{-1} = \frac{1}{5} \begin{bmatrix} 7 & -4 & 6 \\ 8 & 1 & 2 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \).  
12. Multiply \( A^{-1} \) by \( \mathbf{b} \): \( \mathbf{x} = \frac{1}{5} \begin{bmatrix} 7 & -4 & 6 \\ 8 & 1 & 2 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 7 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} \).  
13. Compute each entry:    First: \( 7(-1) + (-4)(7) + 6(-3) = -7 - 28 - 18 = -53 \);    Second: \( 8(-1) + 1(7) + 2(-3) = -8 + 7 - 6 = -7 \);    Third: \( 1(-1) + (-2)(7) + 1(-3) = -1 - 14 - 3 = -18 \).  
14. So \( \mathbf{x} = \frac{1}{5} \begin{bmatrix} -53 \\ -7 \\ -18 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -\frac{53}{5} \\ -\frac{7}{5} \\ -\frac{18}{5} \end{bmatrix} \).  
15. The solution is \( \boxed{\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} -\frac{53}{5} \\ -\frac{7}{5} \\ -\frac{18}{5} \end{bmatrix}} \), and it is unique since \( A \) is invertible.
