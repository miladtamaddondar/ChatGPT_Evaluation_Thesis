1. Write the vectors as columns of a matrix $A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & -3 & 1 \\ -3 & 2 & 1 & -8 & -6 \\ 2 & -3 & 6 & 7 & 9 \end{bmatrix}$ to analyze their linear independence.  
2. Use row reduction to row echelon form to determine which columns are linearly independent; apply the first row operation: add $3$ times row 1 to row 3 and subtract $2$ times row 1 from row 4, yielding $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & -3 & 1 \\ 0 & 2 & -8 & 9 & 0 \\ 0 & -3 & 12 & 5 & 5 \end{bmatrix}$.  
3. Subtract $2$ times row 2 from row 3 and add $3$ times row 2 to row 4 to eliminate entries below the leading 1 in column 2, resulting in $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & -3 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & -12 & 15 & -2 \\ 0 & 0 & 18 & -4 & 8 \end{bmatrix}$.  
4. Add $1.5$ times row 3 to row 4 to eliminate the entry below the leading $-12$ in column 3, giving $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & -3 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & -12 & 15 & -2 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 18.5 & 5 \end{bmatrix}$.  
5. The leading entries are in columns 1, 2, 3, and 4, so the first four vectors are linearly independent; column 5 can be written as a linear combination of the others.  
6. Therefore, a basis for the span is $\left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ -3 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \\ 2 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} -3 \\ -4 \\ 1 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ -8 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \right\}$.
