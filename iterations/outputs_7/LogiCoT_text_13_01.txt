1. To find the basis for the eigenspace corresponding to \(\lambda = 4\), solve \((A - 4I)\vec{x} = 0\), where \(I\) is the \(4 \times 4\) identity matrix.
2. Compute \(A - 4I = \begin{bmatrix}3-4 & 0 & 2 & 0 \\ 1 & 3-4 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1-4 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 4-4\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}-1 & 0 & 2 & 0 \\ 1 & -1 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0\end{bmatrix}\).
3. Let \(\vec{x} = \begin{bmatrix}x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \\ x_4\end{bmatrix}\), then the system is \(\begin{bmatrix}-1 & 0 & 2 & 0 \\ 1 & -1 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \\ x_4\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0\end{bmatrix}\).
4. Write the equations: (1) \(-x_1 + 2x_3 = 0\), (2) \(x_1 - x_2 + x_3 = 0\), (3) \(x_2 - 3x_3 = 0\), (4) \(0 = 0\).
5. From equation (3), \(x_2 = 3x_3\); substitute into equation (2): \(x_1 - 3x_3 + x_3 = x_1 - 2x_3 = 0\), so \(x_1 = 2x_3\).
6. Substitute \(x_1 = 2x_3\) into equation (1): \(-2x_3 + 2x_3 = 0\), which is always true, verifying consistency.
7. \(x_4\) does not appear in any equation, so it is a free variable; let \(x_3 = t\), \(x_4 = s\), where \(t, s \in \mathbb{R}\).
8. Therefore, the general solution is \(\vec{x} = \begin{bmatrix}2t \\ 3t \\ t \\ s\end{bmatrix} = t\begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ 0\end{bmatrix} + s\begin{bmatrix}0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 1\end{bmatrix}\).
9. Thus, a basis for the eigenspace corresponding to \(\lambda = 4\) is \(\left\{\begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ 0\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 1\end{bmatrix}\right\}\).
