1. To construct a \(4 \times 3\) matrix with rank 1, choose two nonzero vectors \(\mathbf{u} \in \mathbb{R}^4\) and \(\mathbf{v} \in \mathbb{R}^3\) and form their outer product: \(A = \mathbf{u} \mathbf{v}^T\).
2. Let \(\mathbf{u} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix}\) and \(\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}\), then \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & 2 \end{bmatrix}\).
3. Compute the outer product: \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \cdot 1 & 1 \cdot (-1) & 1 \cdot 2 \\ 2 \cdot 1 & 2 \cdot (-1) & 2 \cdot 2 \\ 3 \cdot 1 & 3 \cdot (-1) & 3 \cdot 2 \\ 4 \cdot 1 & 4 \cdot (-1) & 4 \cdot 2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & 2 \\ 2 & -2 & 4 \\ 3 & -3 & 6 \\ 4 & -4 & 8 \end{bmatrix}\).
4. The final answer is: a \(4 \times 3\) matrix with rank 1 is \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & 2 \\ 2 & -2 & 4 \\ 3 & -3 & 6 \\ 4 & -4 & 8 \end{bmatrix}\).
