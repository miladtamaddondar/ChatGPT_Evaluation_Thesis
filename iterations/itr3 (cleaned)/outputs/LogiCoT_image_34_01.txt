1. Write the vectors as columns of a matrix \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & -3 & 1 \\ -3 & 2 & 1 & -8 & -6 \\ 2 & -3 & 6 & 7 & 9 \end{bmatrix}\). 
2. Use Gaussian elimination to row reduce \(A\) to row echelon form: Subtract 0 times row 1 from row 2, add 3 times row 1 to row 3, and subtract 2 times row 1 from row 4 to get \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & -3 & 1 \\ 0 & 2 & 10 & -5 & 0 \\ 0 & -3 & 12 & 5 & 5 \end{bmatrix}\). 
3. Subtract 2 times row 2 from row 3 and add 3 times row 2 to row 4 to get \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & -3 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 18 & 1 & -2 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & -4 & 8 \end{bmatrix}\). 
4. Divide row 3 by 18 and row 4 by -4 to get \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & -3 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & \frac{1}{18} & -\frac{2}{18} \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 & -2 \end{bmatrix}\). 
5. Use back substitution to clear above pivots: Add 3 times row 3 to row 1, add 4 times row 3 to row 2, and add \(\frac{1}{18}\) times row 4 to row 3 to get \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 & 1.1667 & 1.6667 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & -2.7778 & 0.5556 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 0.0556 & -0.1111 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 & -2 \end{bmatrix}\). 
6. The pivot columns are columns 1, 2, 3, and 4, so the corresponding original vectors \(\mathbf{v}_1, \mathbf{v}_2, \mathbf{v}_3, \mathbf{v}_4\) form a basis. 
7. Therefore, the basis for the space spanned by the vectors is \(\left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ -3 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \\ 2 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} -3 \\ -4 \\ 1 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ -8 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \right\}\).
