Compute $A^T A$ to set up the normal equations: $A^T A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 3 & 1 \\ -2 & 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1^2 + 3^2 + (-2)^2 & 1 \cdot 5 + 3 \cdot 1 + (-2) \cdot 4 \\ 5 \cdot 1 + 1 \cdot 3 + 4 \cdot (-2) & 5^2 + 1^2 + 4^2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 14 & 2 \\ 2 & 42 \end{bmatrix}$.
2. Verify $A^T A$ by checking each entry: $(1^2 + 3^2 + (-2)^2) = 1 + 9 + 4 = 14$, $(1 \cdot 5 + 3 \cdot 1 + (-2) \cdot 4) = 5 + 3 - 8 = 0$, but previous calculation gave 2, so correct calculation: $1 \cdot 5 + 3 \cdot 1 + (-2) \cdot 4 = 5 + 3 - 8 = 0$, so $A^T A = \begin{bmatrix} 14 & 0 \\ 0 & 42 \end{bmatrix}$.
3. Compute $A^T \mathbf{b}$: $A^T \mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ -2 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \cdot 4 + 3 \cdot (-2) + (-2) \cdot (-3) \\ 5 \cdot 4 + 1 \cdot (-2) + 4 \cdot (-3) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 - 6 + 6 \\ 20 - 2 - 12 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix}$.
4. Set up and solve the normal equations $(A^T A) \mathbf{x} = A^T \mathbf{b}$: $\begin{bmatrix} 14 & 0 \\ 0 & 42 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix}$, so $x_1 = \frac{4}{14} = \frac{2}{7}$ and $x_2 = \frac{6}{42} = \frac{1}{7}$.
5. The least-squares solution is $\mathbf{x}_{\text{ls}} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{7} \\ \frac{1}{7} \end{bmatrix}$.
