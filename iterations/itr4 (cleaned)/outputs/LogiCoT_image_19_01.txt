1. To find the \(\mathcal{B}\)-matrix for \(T\), we need to find the matrix representation of \(T(\mathbf{x}) = A\mathbf{x}\) with respect to the basis \(\mathcal{B} = \{\mathbf{b}_1, \mathbf{b}_2\}\).  
2. The columns of the \(\mathcal{B}\)-matrix are the coordinates of \(T(\mathbf{b}_1)\) and \(T(\mathbf{b}_2)\) with respect to \(\mathcal{B}\).  
3. Compute \(T(\mathbf{b}_1) = A\mathbf{b}_1 = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 1 \\ -1 & 3\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 1\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 2\end{bmatrix}\).  
4. Compute \(T(\mathbf{b}_2) = A\mathbf{b}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 1 \\ -1 & 3\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}5 \\ 4\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}9 \\ 7\end{bmatrix}\).  
5. Express \(\begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 2\end{bmatrix}\) as a linear combination of \(\mathbf{b}_1\) and \(\mathbf{b}_2\): Solve \(c_1\mathbf{b}_1 + c_2\mathbf{b}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 2\end{bmatrix}\), i.e., \(c_1\begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 1\end{bmatrix} + c_2\begin{bmatrix}5 \\ 4\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 2\end{bmatrix}\).  
6. This gives the system: \(c_1 + 5c_2 = 2\), \(c_1 + 4c_2 = 2\); subtract to get \(c_2 = 0\), so \(c_1 = 2\).  
7. Thus, the coordinates of \(T(\mathbf{b}_1)\) in \(\mathcal{B}\) are \(\begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 0\end{bmatrix}\).  
8. Express \(\begin{bmatrix}9 \\ 7\end{bmatrix}\) as a linear combination of \(\mathbf{b}_1\) and \(\mathbf{b}_2\): Solve \(d_1\mathbf{b}_1 + d_2\mathbf{b}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}9 \\ 7\end{bmatrix}\), i.e., \(d_1 + 5d_2 = 9\), \(d_1 + 4d_2 = 7\).  
9. Subtract to get \(d_2 = 2\), so \(d_1 = 9 - 5 \cdot 2 = -1\).  
10. Thus, the coordinates of \(T(\mathbf{b}_2)\) in \(\mathcal{B}\) are \(\begin{bmatrix}-1 \\ 2\end{bmatrix}\).  
11. The \(\mathcal{B}\)-matrix for \(T\) is \(\begin{bmatrix}2 & -1 \\ 0 & 2\end{bmatrix}\).
