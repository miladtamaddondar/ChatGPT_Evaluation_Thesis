1. The $\mathcal{B}$-matrix for $T$ is the matrix representation of $T$ with respect to the basis $\mathcal{B}$, whose columns are $[T(\mathbf{b}_1)]_{\mathcal{B}}$ and $[T(\mathbf{b}_2)]_{\mathcal{B}}$.
2. Compute $T(\mathbf{b}_1) = A\mathbf{b}_1 = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 1 \\ -1 & 3\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 1\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 2\end{bmatrix}$.
3. Express $\begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 2\end{bmatrix}$ as a linear combination of $\mathbf{b}_1$ and $\mathbf{b}_2$: let $c_1\mathbf{b}_1 + c_2\mathbf{b}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 2\end{bmatrix}$, so $c_1\begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 1\end{bmatrix} + c_2\begin{bmatrix}5 \\ 4\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 2\end{bmatrix}$.
4. This gives the system: $c_1 + 5c_2 = 2$, $c_1 + 4c_2 = 2$.
5. Subtract the second equation from the first: $(c_1 + 5c_2) - (c_1 + 4c_2) = 2 - 2 \implies c_2 = 0$.
6. Substitute $c_2 = 0$ into $c_1 + 5c_2 = 2$ to get $c_1 = 2$.
7. Thus, $[T(\mathbf{b}_1)]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 0\end{bmatrix}$.
8. Compute $T(\mathbf{b}_2) = A\mathbf{b}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 1 \\ -1 & 3\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}5 \\ 4\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}9 \\ 7\end{bmatrix}$.
9. Express $\begin{bmatrix}9 \\ 7\end{bmatrix}$ as a linear combination of $\mathbf{b}_1$ and $\mathbf{b}_2$: $d_1\mathbf{b}_1 + d_2\mathbf{b}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}9 \\ 7\end{bmatrix}$, so $d_1 + 5d_2 = 9$, $d_1 + 4d_2 = 7$.
10. Subtract the second equation from the first: $(d_1 + 5d_2) - (d_1 + 4d_2) = 9 - 7 \implies d_2 = 2$.
11. Substitute $d_2 = 2$ into $d_1 + 5d_2 = 9$ to get $d_1 = 9 - 10 = -1$.
12. Thus, $[T(\mathbf{b}_2)]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix}-1 \\ 2\end{bmatrix}$.
13. The $\mathcal{B}$-matrix for $T$ is $\begin{bmatrix}2 & -1 \\ 0 & 2\end{bmatrix}$.
