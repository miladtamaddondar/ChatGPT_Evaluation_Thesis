1. Let \(\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \\ z \end{bmatrix}\). To be orthogonal to both \(\mathbf{u}_1\) and \(\mathbf{u}_2\), we require \(\mathbf{u}_1 \cdot \mathbf{v} = 0\) and \(\mathbf{u}_2 \cdot \mathbf{v} = 0\), giving the system: \(\begin{cases} x + y - 2z = 0 \\ 5x - y + 2z = 0 \end{cases}\).
2. Write the augmented matrix: \(\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 1 & -2 & 0 \\ 5 & -1 & 2 & 0 \end{array}\right]\).
3. Row 2 minus 5 times Row 1: \(\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 1 & -2 & 0 \\ 0 & -6 & 12 & 0 \end{array}\right]\).
4. Row 2 divided by -6: \(\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 1 & -2 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -2 & 0 \end{array}\right]\).
5. Row 1 minus Row 2: \(\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -2 & 0 \end{array}\right]\).
6. The solution is \(x = 0\), \(y = 2z\), so \(\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 2z \\ z \end{bmatrix}\), where \(z \neq 0\). A nonzero solution is \(\boxed{\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 2 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}}\).
