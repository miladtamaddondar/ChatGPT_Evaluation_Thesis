1. To find the inverse of a \(2 \times 2\) matrix \(\begin{bmatrix}a & b \\ c & d\end{bmatrix}\), use the formula \(\frac{1}{ad-bc}\begin{bmatrix}d & -b \\ -c & a\end{bmatrix}\).
2. For the given matrix \(\begin{bmatrix}8 & 6 \\ 5 & 4\end{bmatrix}\), calculate the determinant: \(8 \times 4 - 5 \times 6 = 32 - 30 = 2\).
3. Swap the diagonal elements and change the signs of the off-diagonal elements: \(\begin{bmatrix}4 & -6 \\ -5 & 8\end{bmatrix}\).
4. Multiply by the reciprocal of the determinant: \(\frac{1}{2}\begin{bmatrix}4 & -6 \\ -5 & 8\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}2 & -3 \\ -2.5 & 4\end{bmatrix}\).
5. The inverse of \(\begin{bmatrix}8 & 6 \\ 5 & 4\end{bmatrix}\) is \(\begin{bmatrix}2 & -3 \\ -2.5 & 4\end{bmatrix}\).
