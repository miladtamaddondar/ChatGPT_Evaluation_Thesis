1. To find the eigenvalues, set up the characteristic equation: \(\det(A - \lambda I) = 0\), where \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 2 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\) and \(I\) is the identity matrix.  
2. Compute \(A - \lambda I = \begin{bmatrix} 0-\lambda & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 2-\lambda & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 1-\lambda \end{bmatrix}\).  
3. The determinant of this upper triangular matrix is the product of its diagonal entries: \((0-\lambda)(2-\lambda)(1-\lambda)\).  
4. Set the determinant equal to zero: \((-\lambda)(2-\lambda)(1-\lambda) = 0\).  
5. The solutions are \(\lambda = 0\), \(\lambda = 2\), and \(\lambda = 1\), which are the eigenvalues of the matrix.
