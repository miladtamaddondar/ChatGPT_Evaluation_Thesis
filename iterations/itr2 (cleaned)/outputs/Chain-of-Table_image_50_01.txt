**(a) Compute the orthogonal projection of \( p_2 \) onto the subspace spanned by \( p_0 \) and \( p_1 \):**
1. The inner product is defined as \( \langle f, g \rangle = f(-3)g(-3) + f(-1)g(-1) + f(1)g(1) + f(3)g(3) \). 
2. Compute the values: \( p_0(t) = 1 \), \( p_1(t) = t \), \( p_2(t) = t^2 \), so \( p_0 = (1,1,1,1) \), \( p_1 = (-3,-1,1,3) \), \( p_2 = (9,1,1,9) \). 
3. Compute \( \langle p_2, p_0 \rangle = 9 \cdot 1 + 1 \cdot 1 + 1 \cdot 1 + 9 \cdot 1 = 20 \). 
4. Compute \( \langle p_0, p_0 \rangle = 1+1+1+1 = 4 \). 
5. Compute \( \langle p_2, p_1 \rangle = 9 \cdot (-3) + 1 \cdot (-1) + 1 \cdot 1 + 9 \cdot 3 = -27 -1 +1 +27 = 0 \). 
6. Compute \( \langle p_1, p_1 \rangle = (-3)^2 + (-1)^2 + 1^2 + 3^2 = 9+1+1+9 = 20 \). 
7. The projection formula is \( \text{proj}_{U}(p_2) = \frac{\langle p_2, p_0 \rangle}{\langle p_0, p_0 \rangle}p_0 + \frac{\langle p_2, p_1 \rangle}{\langle p_1, p_1 \rangle}p_1 \). 
8. Substitute values: \( \text{proj}_{U}(p_2) = \frac{20}{4}p_0 + \frac{0}{20}p_1 = 5p_0 \). 
9. So the orthogonal projection is \( 5 \cdot 1 = 5 \).
**(b) Find a polynomial \( q \) orthogonal to \( p_0 \) and \( p_1 \), and scale so its values at \((-3,-1,1,3)\) are \((1,-1,-1,1)\):**
10. Let \( q(t) = at^2 + bt + c \) and require \( \langle q, p_0 \rangle = 0 \) and \( \langle q, p_1 \rangle = 0 \). 
11. \( \langle q, p_0 \rangle = a(9+1+1+9) + b(-3-1+1+3) + c(1+1+1+1) = a \cdot 20 + b \cdot 0 + c \cdot 4 = 20a + 4c = 0 \). 
12. \( \langle q, p_1 \rangle = a(9 \cdot -3 + 1 \cdot -1 + 1 \cdot 1 + 9 \cdot 3) + b((-3)^2 + (-1)^2 + 1^2 + 3^2) + c(-3-1+1+3) = a \cdot 0 + b \cdot 20 + c \cdot 0 = 20b = 0 \). 
13. So \( b = 0 \), \( 20a + 4c = 0 \implies c = -5a \), so \( q(t) = a(t^2 - 5) \). 
14. The vector of values at \((-3,-1,1,3)\) is \( a(9-5) = 4a \), \( a(1-5) = -4a \), \( a(1-5) = -4a \), \( a(9-5) = 4a \), so the vector is \( (4a, -4a, -4a, 4a) \). 
15. Set \( 4a = 1 \implies a = \frac{1}{4} \), so \( q(t) = \frac{1}{4}(t^2 - 5) \). 
16. The scaled polynomial is \( q(t) = \frac{1}{4}(t^2 - 5) \), which at \((-3,-1,1,3)\) gives \( (1, -1, -1, 1) \).
17. The orthogonal projection of \( p_2 \) onto the subspace spanned by \( p_0 \) and \( p_1 \) is \( 5 \). 18. The orthogonal polynomial \( q(t) \) is \( \frac{1}{4}(t^2 - 5) \), scaled so its values at \((-3,-1,1,3)\) are \( (1, -1, -1, 1) \).
