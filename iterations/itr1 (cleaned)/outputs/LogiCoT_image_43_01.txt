1. Let \( U = [\mathbf{u}_1 \ \mathbf{u}_2] = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} & -\frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{1}{3} & \frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} \); this is correct as each column is \(\mathbf{u}_1\) and \(\mathbf{u}_2\) respectively.  
2. Compute \( U^T U \): \( U^T U = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} & \frac{2}{3} \\ -\frac{2}{3} & \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} & -\frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{1}{3} & \frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \); this is correct as the columns are orthonormal.  
3. Compute \( U U^T \): \( U U^T = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} & -\frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{1}{3} & \frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} & \frac{2}{3} \\ -\frac{2}{3} & \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{8}{9} & -\frac{2}{9} & \frac{2}{9} \\ -\frac{2}{9} & \frac{5}{9} & \frac{4}{9} \\ \frac{2}{9} & \frac{4}{9} & \frac{5}{9} \end{bmatrix} \); this is correct by direct multiplication.  
4. Compute \(\operatorname{proj}_W \mathbf{y}\): Since \(U\) has orthonormal columns, \(\operatorname{proj}_W \mathbf{y} = UU^T \mathbf{y}\); this follows from the projection formula for orthonormal bases.  
5. Compute \( UU^T \mathbf{y} \): \( UU^T \mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{8}{9} & -\frac{2}{9} & \frac{2}{9} \\ -\frac{2}{9} & \frac{5}{9} & \frac{4}{9} \\ \frac{2}{9} & \frac{4}{9} & \frac{5}{9} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 8 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{8}{9} \cdot 4 + (-\frac{2}{9}) \cdot 8 + \frac{2}{9} \cdot 1 \\ -\frac{2}{9} \cdot 4 + \frac{5}{9} \cdot 8 + \frac{4}{9} \cdot 1 \\ \frac{2}{9} \cdot 4 + \frac{4}{9} \cdot 8 + \frac{5}{9} \cdot 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{32}{9} - \frac{16}{9} + \frac{2}{9} \\ -\frac{8}{9} + \frac{40}{9} + \frac{4}{9} \\ \frac{8}{9} + \frac{32}{9} + \frac{5}{9} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{18}{9} \\ \frac{36}{9} \\ \frac{45}{9} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 4 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix} \); this is correct by direct computation.  
6. The final answer is: \( U^T U = I \), \( U U^T = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{8}{9} & -\frac{2}{9} & \frac{2}{9} \\ -\frac{2}{9} & \frac{5}{9} & \frac{4}{9} \\ \frac{2}{9} & \frac{4}{9} & \frac{5}{9} \end{bmatrix} \), and \( \operatorname{proj}_W \mathbf{y} = (UU^T)\mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 4 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix} \).
