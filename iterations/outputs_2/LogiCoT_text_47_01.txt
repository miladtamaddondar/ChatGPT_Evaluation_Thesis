1. To find the orthogonal projection of $\mathbf{b}$ onto $\text{Col }A$, compute the projection formula: $\text{proj}_{\text{Col }A}(\mathbf{b}) = A(A^T A)^{-1}A^T\mathbf{b}$.
2. Compute $A^T A$: $A^T = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix}$, so $A^T A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 3 & 1 \\ -2 & 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1^2 + 3^2 + (-2)^2 & 1\cdot5 + 3\cdot1 + (-2)\cdot4 \\ 5\cdot1 + 1\cdot3 + 4\cdot(-2) & 5^2 + 1^2 + 4^2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1+9+4 & 5+3-8 \\ 5+3-8 & 25+1+16 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 14 & 0 \\ 0 & 42 \end{bmatrix}$.
3. Verify $A^T A$ is invertible by checking its determinant: $\det(A^T A) = 14 \cdot 42 - 0 = 588 \neq 0$, so $A^T A$ is invertible.
4. Compute $(A^T A)^{-1}$: Since $A^T A$ is diagonal, $(A^T A)^{-1} = \begin{bmatrix} 1/14 & 0 \\ 0 & 1/42 \end{bmatrix}$.
5. Compute $A^T \mathbf{b}$: $A^T \mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ -2 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1\cdot4 + 3\cdot(-2) + (-2)\cdot(-3) \\ 5\cdot4 + 1\cdot(-2) + 4\cdot(-3) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 - 6 + 6 \\ 20 - 2 - 12 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix}$.
6. Compute $(A^T A)^{-1}A^T\mathbf{b}$: $(A^T A)^{-1}A^T\mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 1/14 & 0 \\ 0 & 1/42 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4/14 \\ 6/42 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2/7 \\ 1/7 \end{bmatrix}$.
7. Compute $A(A^T A)^{-1}A^T\mathbf{b}$: $A \begin{bmatrix} 2/7 \\ 1/7 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 3 & 1 \\ -2 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 2/7 \\ 1/7 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1\cdot(2/7) + 5\cdot(1/7) \\ 3\cdot(2/7) + 1\cdot(1/7) \\ -2\cdot(2/7) + 4\cdot(1/7) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2/7 + 5/7 \\ 6/7 + 1/7 \\ -4/7 + 4/7 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}$.
8. Thus, the orthogonal projection of $\mathbf{b}$ onto $\text{Col }A$ is $\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}$.
9. For a least-squares solution of $A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{b}$, use $\mathbf{x} = (A^T A)^{-1}A^T\mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 2/7 \\ 1/7 \end{bmatrix}$.
10. Therefore, the least-squares solution is $\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 2/7 \\ 1/7 \end{bmatrix}$.
