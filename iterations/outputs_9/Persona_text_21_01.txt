1. To find $\mathbf{x}$ such that $A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{b}$, set up the augmented matrix: $\begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 & -2 & | & -1 \\ -2 & 1 & 6 & | & 7 \\ 3 & -2 & -5 & | & -3\end{bmatrix}$.
2. Use row 1 as is: $R_1 = [1\ 0\ -2\ |\ -1]$.
3. Eliminate the $-2$ below the leading 1 in column 1: $R_2 \leftarrow R_2 + 2R_1 \implies [0\ 1\ 2\ |\ 5]$.
4. Eliminate the $3$ below the leading 1 in column 1: $R_3 \leftarrow R_3 - 3R_1 \implies [0\ -2\ 1\ |\ 0]$.
5. Use $R_2$ as is: $R_2 = [0\ 1\ 2\ |\ 5]$.
6. Eliminate the $-2$ below the leading 1 in column 2: $R_3 \leftarrow R_3 + 2R_2 \implies [0\ 0\ 5\ |\ 10]$.
7. Solve for $x_3$ from $R_3$: $5x_3 = 10 \implies x_3 = 2$.
8. Substitute $x_3 = 2$ into $R_2$: $x_2 + 2x_3 = 5 \implies x_2 + 4 = 5 \implies x_2 = 1$.
9. Substitute $x_2 = 1$ and $x_3 = 2$ into $R_1$: $x_1 - 2x_3 = -1 \implies x_1 - 4 = -1 \implies x_1 = 3$.
10. The solution is $\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix}3 \\ 1 \\ 2\end{bmatrix}$.
11. Since each variable has a unique value, the solution is unique.
