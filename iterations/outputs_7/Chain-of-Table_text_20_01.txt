3. Solving the quadratic: \(\lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3 = 0\) gives \(\lambda = \frac{1.3 \pm \sqrt{1.69 - 1.2}}{2} = \frac{1.3 \pm 0.7}{2}\), so \(\lambda_1 = 1\), \(\lambda_2 = 0.3\).
4. For \(\lambda_2 = 0.3\), solve \((A - 0.3I)\mathbf{v}_2 = 0\): \(A - 0.3I = \begin{bmatrix}0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.4\end{bmatrix}\).
5. Set up the augmented matrix: \(\left[\begin{array}{cc|c}0.3 & 0.3 & 0 \\ 0.4 & 0.4 & 0\end{array}\right]\).
6. Row 2 minus \(\frac{4}{3}\) times Row 1: \(\left[\begin{array}{cc|c}0.3 & 0.3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0\end{array}\right]\).
7. The first row gives \(0.3x_1 + 0.3x_2 = 0\) or \(x_1 + x_2 = 0\), so \(x_2 = -x_1\).
8. Thus, an eigenvector is \(\mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}\).
9. Final answer: A basis for \(\mathbb{R}^2\) consisting of eigenvectors is \(\left\{\begin{bmatrix}\frac{3}{7} \\ \frac{4}{7}\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}\right\}\).
