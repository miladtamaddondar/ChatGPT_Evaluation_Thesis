1. To find a matrix \( B \) such that \( AB = 0 \), we need each column of \( B \) to be in the null space of \( A \), i.e., for any vector \( x \), \( Ax = 0 \).  
2. Let \( x = \begin{bmatrix}x_1 \\ x_2\end{bmatrix} \), then \( Ax = \begin{bmatrix}3x_1 - 6x_2 \\ -x_1 + 2x_2\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0 \\ 0\end{bmatrix} \).  
3. Setting up the system: \( 3x_1 - 6x_2 = 0 \) and \( -x_1 + 2x_2 = 0 \).  
4. From the first equation, \( 3x_1 = 6x_2 \implies x_1 = 2x_2 \); from the second, \( -x_1 + 2x_2 = 0 \implies x_1 = 2x_2 \), which is consistent.  
5. Therefore, the null space is all scalar multiples of \( \begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 1\end{bmatrix} \); verify: \( A\begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 1\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}3\cdot2 - 6\cdot1 \\ -2 + 2\cdot1\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0 \\ 0\end{bmatrix} \).  
6. To construct \( B \) with two different nonzero columns, choose two distinct nonzero multiples, e.g., \( \begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 1\end{bmatrix} \) and \( \begin{bmatrix}4 \\ 2\end{bmatrix} \).  
7. Thus, \( B = \begin{bmatrix}2 & 4 \\ 1 & 2\end{bmatrix} \) is a \( 2 \times 2 \) matrix with two different nonzero columns from the null space.  
8. Final answer: \( B = \begin{bmatrix}2 & 4 \\ 1 & 2\end{bmatrix} \) satisfies \( AB = 0 \) with two different nonzero columns.
