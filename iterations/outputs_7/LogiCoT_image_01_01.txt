1. To find the inverse of a 2x2 matrix \( A = \begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{bmatrix} \), use the formula \( A^{-1} = \frac{1}{ad-bc} \begin{bmatrix} d & -b \\ -c & a \end{bmatrix} \).  
2. For the given matrix \( \begin{bmatrix} 8 & 6 \\ 5 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \), identify \( a = 8, b = 6, c = 5, d = 4 \).  
3. Compute the determinant: \( \det(A) = (8)(4) - (6)(5) = 32 - 30 = 2 \); since the determinant is nonzero, the matrix is invertible.  
4. Substitute into the inverse formula: \( A^{-1} = \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} 4 & -6 \\ -5 & 8 \end{bmatrix} \).  
5. Multiply each entry by \( \frac{1}{2} \): \( A^{-1} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -3 \\ -2.5 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \).  
6. Final answer: The inverse of \( \begin{bmatrix} 8 & 6 \\ 5 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \) is \( \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -3 \\ -2.5 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \).
