1. Let the matrix be \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 8 & 6 \\ 5 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \); to find the inverse, first compute the determinant: \( \det(A) = 8 \cdot 4 - 6 \cdot 5 = 32 - 30 = 2 \).  
2. Since \( \det(A) \neq 0 \), the matrix is invertible by the invertibility criterion for square matrices.  
3. The formula for the inverse of a \( 2 \times 2 \) matrix \( \begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{bmatrix} \) is \( \frac{1}{ad-bc} \begin{bmatrix} d & -b \\ -c & a \end{bmatrix} \); verify that this formula is applicable since our determinant is nonzero.  
4. Substitute the values: \( A^{-1} = \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} 4 & -6 \\ -5 & 8 \end{bmatrix} \).  
5. Multiply out the scalar: \( A^{-1} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -3 \\ -2.5 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \).  
6. Final answer: The inverse of the matrix is \( \boxed{\begin{bmatrix} 2 & -3 \\ -2.5 & 4 \end{bmatrix}} \).
