The transformation is defined as \( T(\mathbf{x}) = x_1 \mathbf{v}_1 + x_2 \mathbf{v}_2 \), where \( \mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} \), \( \mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} -2 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix} \), and \( \mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 7 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} \); this step simply restates the problem and checks that the transformation is linear by the definition of matrix multiplication.
2. Express \( T(\mathbf{x}) \) as a matrix-vector product: \( T(\mathbf{x}) = x_1 \begin{bmatrix} -2 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix} + x_2 \begin{bmatrix} 7 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -2x_1 + 7x_2 \\ 5x_1 - 3x_2 \end{bmatrix} \); this is correct by the distributive property of matrix addition.
3. Write \( T(\mathbf{x}) \) in the form \( A\mathbf{x} \) by matching coefficients: \( \begin{bmatrix} -2x_1 + 7x_2 \\ 5x_1 - 3x_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -2 & 7 \\ 5 & -3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} \); this is correct because the matrix multiplication yields the same linear combination as above.
4. Therefore, the matrix \( A \) such that \( T(\mathbf{x}) = A\mathbf{x} \) for all \( \mathbf{x} \) is \( \boxed{A = \begin{bmatrix} -2 & 7 \\ 5 & -3 \end{bmatrix}} \); this is verified by direct substitution and matrix multiplication.
