1. To write \(A\) in the form \(A = P C P^{-1}\) with \(C = \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix}\), we first find the eigenvalues of \(A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix}\) by solving \(\det(A - \lambda I) = 0\).
2. Compute the characteristic polynomial: \(\det \begin{pmatrix} 1-\lambda & -2 \\ 1 & 3-\lambda \end{pmatrix} = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5\).
3. Solve for eigenvalues: \(\lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0 \implies \lambda = 2 \pm i\).
4. Since the eigenvalues are complex conjugates, \(C\) will be \(\begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}\).
5. Find an eigenvector for \(\lambda = 2 + i\) by solving \((A - (2+i)I)\vec{v} = 0\).
6. Compute \(A - (2+i)I = \begin{pmatrix} 1-(2+i) & -2 \\ 1 & 3-(2+i) \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix}\).
7. Set up the system: \((-1-i)x - 2y = 0\) and \(x + (1-i)y = 0\).
8. From the second equation, \(x = -(1-i)y\).
9. Substitute into the first: \((-1-i)(-(1-i)y) - 2y = 0 \implies (1+i)(1-i)y - 2y = 0\).
10. Compute \((1+i)(1-i) = 1 - i^2 = 2\), so \(2y - 2y = 0\), so any \(y \neq 0\) works.
11. Let \(y = 1\), then \(x = -(1-i)\), so an eigenvector is \(\vec{v} = \begin{pmatrix} -(1-i) \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}\).
12. The real and imaginary parts of this eigenvector are \(\begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}\) and \(\begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}\).
13. Form \(P\) from these vectors: \(P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}\).
14. The matrix \(C\) is \(\begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}\).
15. The final answer: \(P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}\), \(C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}\), and \(A = P C P^{-1}\).
