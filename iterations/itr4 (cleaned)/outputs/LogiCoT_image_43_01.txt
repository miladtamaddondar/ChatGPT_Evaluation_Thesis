1. Let \( U = [\mathbf{u}_1 \ \mathbf{u}_2] = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} & -\frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{1}{3} & \frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} \); this forms a \(3 \times 2\) matrix by stacking \(\mathbf{u}_1\) and \(\mathbf{u}_2\) as columns.  
2. Compute \( U^T U \): \( U^T U = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} & \frac{2}{3} \\ -\frac{2}{3} & \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} & -\frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{1}{3} & \frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \); this is the identity matrix since \(\mathbf{u}_1\) and \(\mathbf{u}_2\) are orthonormal.  
3. Compute \( U U^T \): \( U U^T = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} & -\frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{1}{3} & \frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} & \frac{2}{3} \\ -\frac{2}{3} & \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{8}{9} & 0 & \frac{2}{9} \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ \frac{2}{9} & 0 & \frac{5}{9} \end{bmatrix} \); this projects vectors onto the subspace spanned by \(\mathbf{u}_1\) and \(\mathbf{u}_2\).  
4. Compute \(\text{proj}_W \mathbf{y} = U U^T \mathbf{y}\): \( U U^T \mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{8}{9} & 0 & \frac{2}{9} \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ \frac{2}{9} & 0 & \frac{5}{9} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 8 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{8}{9} \cdot 4 + 0 \cdot 8 + \frac{2}{9} \cdot 1 \\ 0 \cdot 4 + 1 \cdot 8 + 0 \cdot 1 \\ \frac{2}{9} \cdot 4 + 0 \cdot 8 + \frac{5}{9} \cdot 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{32}{9} + \frac{2}{9} \\ 8 \\ \frac{8}{9} + \frac{5}{9} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{34}{9} \\ 8 \\ \frac{13}{9} \end{bmatrix} \); this verifies the projection using the projection matrix.  
5. The final answers are: \( U^T U = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \), \( U U^T = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{8}{9} & 0 & \frac{2}{9} \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ \frac{2}{9} & 0 & \frac{5}{9} \end{bmatrix} \), and \( \text{proj}_W \mathbf{y} = (U U^T)\mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{34}{9} \\ 8 \\ \frac{13}{9} \end{bmatrix} \).
