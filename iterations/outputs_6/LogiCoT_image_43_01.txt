1. Let \( U = [\mathbf{u}_1 \ \mathbf{u}_2] = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} & -\frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{1}{3} & \frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} \); this is correct as \( U \) is formed by placing \( \mathbf{u}_1 \) and \( \mathbf{u}_2 \) as columns.  
2. Compute \( U^T U \): \( U^T U = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} & \frac{2}{3} \\ -\frac{2}{3} & \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} & -\frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{1}{3} & \frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \); this verifies that \( \mathbf{u}_1 \) and \( \mathbf{u}_2 \) are orthonormal.  
3. Compute \( U U^T \): \( U U^T = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} & -\frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{1}{3} & \frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} & \frac{2}{3} \\ -\frac{2}{3} & \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{8}{9} + \frac{4}{9} & \frac{2}{9} - \frac{4}{9} & \frac{4}{9} - \frac{2}{9} \\ \frac{2}{9} - \frac{4}{9} & \frac{1}{9} + \frac{4}{9} & \frac{2}{9} + \frac{2}{9} \\ \frac{4}{9} - \frac{2}{9} & \frac{2}{9} + \frac{2}{9} & \frac{4}{9} + \frac{1}{9} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{12}{9} & -\frac{2}{9} & \frac{2}{9} \\ -\frac{2}{9} & \frac{5}{9} & \frac{4}{9} \\ \frac{2}{9} & \frac{4}{9} & \frac{5}{9} \end{bmatrix} \); this uses matrix multiplication and is verified by direct computation.  
4. Compute \( \text{proj}_W \mathbf{y} = U U^T \mathbf{y} \): \( U^T \mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} & \frac{2}{3} \\ -\frac{2}{3} & \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 8 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} \cdot 4 + \frac{1}{3} \cdot 8 + \frac{2}{3} \cdot 1 \\ -\frac{2}{3} \cdot 4 + \frac{2}{3} \cdot 8 + \frac{1}{3} \cdot 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{8}{3} + \frac{8}{3} + \frac{2}{3} \\ -\frac{8}{3} + \frac{16}{3} + \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} \); this is verified by dot product calculation.  
5. Now \( U (U^T \mathbf{y}) = U \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} = 6 \mathbf{u}_1 + 3 \mathbf{u}_2 = 6 \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{1}{3} \\ \frac{2}{3} \end{bmatrix} + 3 \begin{bmatrix} -\frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 2 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} -2 \\ 2 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 4 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix} \); this is verified by direct vector addition.  
6. The projection \( \text{proj}_W \mathbf{y} = (U U^T)\mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 4 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix} \); this matches the previous calculation and confirms correctness.
