1. To find the inverse of a 2x2 matrix \( A = \begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{bmatrix} \), use the formula \( A^{-1} = \frac{1}{ad-bc} \begin{bmatrix} d & -b \\ -c & a \end{bmatrix} \).
2. For the given matrix \( \begin{bmatrix} 8 & 6 \\ 5 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \), compute the determinant: \( \det(A) = 8 \times 4 - 6 \times 5 = 32 - 30 = 2 \).
3. Swap the diagonal elements and change the signs of the off-diagonal elements: \( \begin{bmatrix} 4 & -6 \\ -5 & 8 \end{bmatrix} \).
4. Multiply by the reciprocal of the determinant: \( \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} 4 & -6 \\ -5 & 8 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -3 \\ -2.5 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \).
5. The inverse of the matrix is \( \boxed{\begin{bmatrix} 2 & -3 \\ -2.5 & 4 \end{bmatrix}} \).
