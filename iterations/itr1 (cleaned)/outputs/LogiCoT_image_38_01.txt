1. Identify the basis \(\mathcal{B}\) for \(\mathbb{P}_3\) as \(\{1,\, 2t,\, -2+4t^2,\, -12t+8t^3\}\). 
2. Express \(p(t) = 7 - 12t - 8t^2 + 12t^3\) as a linear combination of the basis vectors: \(p(t) = a\cdot 1 + b\cdot 2t + c\cdot (-2+4t^2) + d\cdot (-12t+8t^3)\). 
3. Expand the right side: \(a + 2b t + c(-2) + c(4t^2) + d(-12t) + d(8t^3) = a - 2c + (2b - 12d)t + 4c t^2 + 8d t^3\). 
4. Match coefficients with \(p(t)\): Constant: \(a - 2c = 7\), \(t\): \(2b - 12d = -12\), \(t^2\): \(4c = -8\), \(t^3\): \(8d = 12\). 
5. Solve \(8d = 12 \Rightarrow d = \frac{3}{2}\). 
6. Solve \(4c = -8 \Rightarrow c = -2\). 
7. Substitute \(c = -2\) into \(a - 2c = 7 \Rightarrow a - 2(-2) = 7 \Rightarrow a + 4 = 7 \Rightarrow a = 3\). 
8. Substitute \(d = \frac{3}{2}\) into \(2b - 12d = -12 \Rightarrow 2b - 12 \cdot \frac{3}{2} = -12 \Rightarrow 2b - 18 = -12 \Rightarrow 2b = 6 \Rightarrow b = 3\). 
9. The coordinate vector of \(p\) relative to \(\mathcal{B}\) is \(\begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 3 \\ -2 \\ \frac{3}{2} \end{bmatrix}\).
