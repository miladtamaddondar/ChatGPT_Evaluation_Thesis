1. To find a basis for the eigenspace corresponding to \(\lambda = 4\), we solve \((A - 4I)\vec{x} = 0\). 
2. Compute \(A - 4I = \begin{bmatrix} 3-4 & 0 & 2 & 0 \\ 1 & 3-4 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1-4 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 4-4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 0 & 2 & 0 \\ 1 & -1 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\). 
3. Set up the system \(\begin{bmatrix} -1 & 0 & 2 & 0 \\ 1 & -1 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \\ x_4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}\). 
4. The last row gives no information, so focus on the first three equations: \(-x_1 + 2x_3 = 0\), \(x_1 - x_2 + x_3 = 0\), \(x_2 - 3x_3 = 0\). 
5. From the third equation, \(x_2 = 3x_3\). 
6. Substitute \(x_2 = 3x_3\) into the second equation: \(x_1 - 3x_3 + x_3 = x_1 - 2x_3 = 0\), so \(x_1 = 2x_3\). 
7. The first equation \(-x_1 + 2x_3 = 0\) is satisfied by \(x_1 = 2x_3\), confirming consistency. 
8. Let \(x_3 = t\) and \(x_4 = s\) (free variables), so the general solution is \(\begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \\ x_4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2t \\ 3t \\ t \\ s \end{bmatrix} = t\begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} + s\begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}\). 
9. Therefore, a basis for the eigenspace corresponding to \(\lambda = 4\) is \(\left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \right\}\).
