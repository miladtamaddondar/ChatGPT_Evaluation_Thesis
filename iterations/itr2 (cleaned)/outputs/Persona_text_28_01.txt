1. The transformation $T$ maps $\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix}$ to $x_1\mathbf{v}_1 + x_2\mathbf{v}_2$, so we write $T(\mathbf{x}) = x_1\begin{bmatrix} -2 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix} + x_2\begin{bmatrix} 7 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix}$.  
2. By the properties of matrix-vector multiplication, $T(\mathbf{x})$ can be written as $T(\mathbf{x}) = \begin{bmatrix} -2 & 7 \\ 5 & -3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix}$.  
3. Therefore, the matrix $A$ such that $T(\mathbf{x}) = A\mathbf{x}$ is $A = \begin{bmatrix} -2 & 7 \\ 5 & -3 \end{bmatrix}$.
