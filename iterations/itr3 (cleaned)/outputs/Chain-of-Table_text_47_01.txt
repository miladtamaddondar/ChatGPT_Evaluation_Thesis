1. To find the least-squares solution, set up the normal equations: \(A^T A \mathbf{x} = A^T \mathbf{b}\).  
2. Compute \(A^T\): \(A^T = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix}\).  
3. Compute \(A^T A\): \(A^T A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 3 & 1 \\ -2 & 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} (1)(1)+(3)(3)+(-2)(-2) & (1)(5)+(3)(1)+(-2)(4) \\ (5)(1)+(1)(3)+(4)(-2) & (5)(5)+(1)(1)+(4)(4) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1+9+4 & 5+3-8 \\ 5+3-8 & 25+1+16 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 14 & 0 \\ 0 & 42 \end{bmatrix}\).  
4. Compute \(A^T \mathbf{b}\): \(A^T \mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ -2 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} (1)(4)+(3)(-2)+(-2)(-3) \\ (5)(4)+(1)(-2)+(4)(-3) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4-6+6 \\ 20-2-12 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix}\).  
5. The normal equations are \(\begin{bmatrix} 14 & 0 \\ 0 & 42 \end{bmatrix} \mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix}\).  
6. Solve for \(\mathbf{x}\): \(14x_1 = 4 \implies x_1 = \frac{2}{7}\), \(42x_2 = 6 \implies x_2 = \frac{1}{7}\), so \(\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{7} \\ \frac{1}{7} \end{bmatrix}\).  
7. The least-squares solution is \(\boxed{\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{7} \\ \frac{1}{7} \end{bmatrix}}\).  
8. The orthogonal projection of \(\mathbf{b}\) onto \(\text{Col }A\) is \(A\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 3 & 1 \\ -2 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{7} \\ \frac{1}{7} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1\cdot\frac{2}{7} + 5\cdot\frac{1}{7} \\ 3\cdot\frac{2}{7} + 1\cdot\frac{1}{7} \\ -2\cdot\frac{2}{7} + 4\cdot\frac{1}{7} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{7} + \frac{5}{7} \\ \frac{6}{7} + \frac{1}{7} \\ -\frac{4}{7} + \frac{4}{7} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}\).  
9. The orthogonal projection of \(\mathbf{b}\) onto \(\text{Col }A\) is \(\boxed{\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}}\).
