1. To find a basis of eigenvectors, first find the eigenvalues of \(A\) by solving \(\det(A - \lambda I) = 0\): \(\det\left(\begin{bmatrix}0.6-\lambda & 0.3\\0.4 & 0.7-\lambda\end{bmatrix}\right) = (0.6-\lambda)(0.7-\lambda) - (0.3)(0.4) = 0\).
2. Expanding, \((0.6-\lambda)(0.7-\lambda) - 0.12 = 0.42 - 0.6\lambda - 0.7\lambda + \lambda^2 - 0.12 = \lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3 = 0\).
3. Solve the quadratic equation: \(\lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3 = 0\) gives \(\lambda = \frac{1.3 \pm \sqrt{1.3^2 - 4 \cdot 0.3}}{2} = \frac{1.3 \pm \sqrt{0.49}}{2} = \frac{1.3 \pm 0.7}{2}\).
4. Thus, the eigenvalues are \(\lambda_1 = 1\) and \(\lambda_2 = 0.3\).
5. We are given that \(\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix}3/7 \\ 4/7\end{bmatrix}\) is an eigenvector for \(\lambda_1 = 1\).
6. To find an eigenvector for \(\lambda_2 = 0.3\), solve \((A - 0.3I)\mathbf{v}_2 = 0\): \(A - 0.3I = \begin{bmatrix}0.3 & 0.3\\0.4 & 0.4\end{bmatrix}\).
7. Set up the equation \(\begin{bmatrix}0.3 & 0.3\\0.4 & 0.4\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}x\\y\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0\\0\end{bmatrix}\), which gives \(0.3x + 0.3y = 0\) and \(0.4x + 0.4y = 0\).
8. Both equations reduce to \(x + y = 0\), so \(y = -x\).
9. Thus, an eigenvector for \(\lambda_2 = 0.3\) is \(\mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}\).
10. Therefore, a basis for \(\mathbb{R}^2\) consisting of eigenvectors of \(A\) is \(\left\{\begin{bmatrix}3/7 \\ 4/7\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}\right\}\).
