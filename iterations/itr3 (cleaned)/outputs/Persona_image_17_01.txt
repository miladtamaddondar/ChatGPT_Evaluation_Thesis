1. To find the image under \( T \) of \( p(t) = 5 + 3t \), substitute \( p(t) \) into the definition of \( T \): \( T(p) = \begin{bmatrix} p(-1) \\ p(0) \\ p(1) \end{bmatrix} \). 
2. Compute \( p(-1) = 5 + 3(-1) = 2 \). 
3. Compute \( p(0) = 5 + 3(0) = 5 \). 
4. Compute \( p(1) = 5 + 3(1) = 8 \). 
5. Therefore, the image is \( T(5 + 3t) = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 5 \\ 8 \end{bmatrix} \).
6. To find the matrix for \( T \) relative to the basis \( \{1, t, t^2\} \) for \( \mathbb{P}_2 \) and the standard basis for \( \mathbb{R}^3 \), apply \( T \) to each basis vector: \( T(1) = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \), \( T(t) = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \), \( T(t^2) = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \). 
7. The matrix for \( T \) is formed by placing these results as columns: \( [T] = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 1 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \).
