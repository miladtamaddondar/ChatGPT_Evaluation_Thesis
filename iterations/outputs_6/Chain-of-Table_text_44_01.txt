1. Start with the original matrix: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 5 \\ -1 & 1 & -4 \\ -1 & 4 & -3 \\ 1 & -4 & 7 \\ 1 & 2 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\). Let the columns be \(v_1, v_2, v_3\).
2. Set \(u_1 = v_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \\ -1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}\).
3. Compute the projection of \(v_2\) onto \(u_1\): \(\text{proj}_{u_1}(v_2) = \frac{u_1^T v_2}{u_1^T u_1} u_1\), where \(u_1^T v_2 = 2 - 1 + 4 - 4 + 2 = 3\) and \(u_1^T u_1 = 1 + 1 + 1 + 1 + 1 = 5\), so \(\text{proj}_{u_1}(v_2) = \frac{3}{5} u_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 3/5 \\ -3/5 \\ -3/5 \\ 3/5 \\ 3/5 \end{bmatrix}\).
4. Subtract the projection from \(v_2\) to get \(u_2\): \(u_2 = v_2 - \text{proj}_{u_1}(v_2) = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \\ 4 \\ -4 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} 3/5 \\ -3/5 \\ -3/5 \\ 3/5 \\ 3/5 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 7/5 \\ 8/5 \\ 23/5 \\ -23/5 \\ 7/5 \end{bmatrix}\).
5. Compute the projection of \(v_3\) onto \(u_1\): \(u_1^T v_3 = 5 + 4 + 3 + 7 + 1 = 20\), so \(\text{proj}_{u_1}(v_3) = \frac{20}{5} u_1 = 4u_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ -4 \\ -4 \\ 4 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix}\).
6. Compute the projection of \(v_3\) onto \(u_2\): \(u_2^T v_3 = (7/5) \cdot 5 + (8/5) \cdot (-4) + (23/5) \cdot (-3) + (-23/5) \cdot 7 + (7/5) \cdot 1 = 7 - 32/5 - 69/5 - 161/5 + 7/5 = 7 - (32+69+161-7)/5 = 7 - 255/5 = 7 - 51 = -44\), and \(u_2^T u_2 = (7/5)^2 + (8/5)^2 + (23/5)^2 + (-23/5)^2 + (7/5)^2 = (49 + 64 + 529 + 529 + 49)/25 = 1220/25 = 48.8\), so \(\text{proj}_{u_2}(v_3) = \frac{-44}{48.8} u_2\).
7. Calculate \(\frac{-44}{48.8} = -\frac{220}{244} = -\frac{55}{61}\), so \(\text{proj}_{u_2}(v_3) = -\frac{55}{61} u_2 = \begin{bmatrix} -\frac{55}{61} \cdot \frac{7}{5} \\ -\frac{55}{61} \cdot \frac{8}{5} \\ -\frac{55}{61} \cdot \frac{23}{5} \\ -\frac{55}{61} \cdot \frac{-23}{5} \\ -\frac{55}{61} \cdot \frac{7}{5} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -\frac{77}{61} \\ -\frac{88}{61} \\ -\frac{253}{61} \\ \frac{253}{61} \\ -\frac{77}{61} \end{bmatrix}\).
8. Subtract both projections from \(v_3\) to get \(u_3\): \(u_3 = v_3 - \text{proj}_{u_1}(v_3) - \text{proj}_{u_2}(v_3) = \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ -4 \\ -3 \\ 7 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ -4 \\ -4 \\ 4 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} -\frac{77}{61} \\ -\frac{88}{61} \\ -\frac{253}{61} \\ \frac{253}{61} \\ -\frac{77}{61} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 + \frac{77}{61} \\ 0 + \frac{88}{61} \\ 1 + \frac{253}{61} \\ 3 - \frac{253}{61} \\ -3 + \frac{77}{61} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{138}{61} \\ \frac{88}{61} \\ \frac{314}{61} \\ \frac{130}{61} \\ -\frac{106}{61} \end{bmatrix}\).
9. The orthogonal basis for the column space is: \(\left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \\ -1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 7/5 \\ 8/5 \\ 23/5 \\ -23/5 \\ 7/5 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 138/61 \\ 88/61 \\ 314/61 \\ 130/61 \\ -106/61 \end{bmatrix} \right\}\).
