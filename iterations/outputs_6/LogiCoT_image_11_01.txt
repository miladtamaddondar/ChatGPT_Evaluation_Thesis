1. To find the eigenvalues of the matrix \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 2 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \), we solve \( \det(A - \lambda I) = 0 \).  
2. Compute \( A - \lambda I = \begin{bmatrix} 0-\lambda & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 2-\lambda & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 1-\lambda \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -\lambda & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 2-\lambda & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 1-\lambda \end{bmatrix} \).  
3. The determinant is \( \det(A - \lambda I) = (-\lambda) \cdot \det\begin{bmatrix} 2-\lambda & 5 \\ 0 & 1-\lambda \end{bmatrix} \).  
4. Compute the \( 2 \times 2 \) determinant: \( \det\begin{bmatrix} 2-\lambda & 5 \\ 0 & 1-\lambda \end{bmatrix} = (2-\lambda)(1-\lambda) - 0 = (2-\lambda)(1-\lambda) \).  
5. So, \( \det(A - \lambda I) = -\lambda (2-\lambda)(1-\lambda) \).  
6. Set the determinant to zero: \( -\lambda (2-\lambda)(1-\lambda) = 0 \).  
7. The solutions are \( \lambda = 0 \), \( \lambda = 1 \), and \( \lambda = 2 \).  
8. The eigenvalues of the matrix are \( \boxed{0, 1, 2} \).
