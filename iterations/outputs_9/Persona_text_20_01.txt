1. To find a basis of $\mathbb{R}^2$ consisting of eigenvectors, first find all eigenvalues of $A$ by solving $\det(A - \lambda I) = 0$: $\det\left(\begin{bmatrix}0.6-\lambda & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.7-\lambda\end{bmatrix}\right) = (0.6-\lambda)(0.7-\lambda)-0.12 = \lambda^2-1.3\lambda+0.3=0$.
2. Solve the quadratic equation $\lambda^2-1.3\lambda+0.3=0$ to find eigenvalues: $\lambda = \frac{1.3 \pm \sqrt{1.69-1.2}}{2} = \frac{1.3 \pm 0.7}{2}$, so $\lambda_1 = 1$ and $\lambda_2 = 0.3$.
3. Since $\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix}3/7 \\ 4/7\end{bmatrix}$ is given, check that it is an eigenvector for $\lambda_1=1$: $A\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix}0.6 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.7\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}3/7 \\ 4/7\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}3/7 \\ 4/7\end{bmatrix} = 1\cdot\mathbf{v}_1$.
4. Find an eigenvector $\mathbf{v}_2$ for $\lambda_2=0.3$ by solving $(A-0.3I)\mathbf{v}_2=0$: $A-0.3I = \begin{bmatrix}0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.4\end{bmatrix}$, so $0.3x+0.3y=0$ and $0.4x+0.4y=0$.
5. The equations $0.3x+0.3y=0$ give $x=-y$, so take $\mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}$.
6. Therefore, a basis for $\mathbb{R}^2$ consisting of eigenvectors of $A$ is $\left\{\begin{bmatrix}3/7 \\ 4/7\end{bmatrix},\begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}\right\}$.
