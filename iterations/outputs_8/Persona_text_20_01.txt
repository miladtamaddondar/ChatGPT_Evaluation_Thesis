1. To find another eigenvector, first compute the eigenvalues of $A$ by solving $\det(A - \lambda I) = 0$: $\det\left(\begin{bmatrix}0.6-\lambda & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.7-\lambda\end{bmatrix}\right) = (0.6-\lambda)(0.7-\lambda) - (0.4)(0.3) = 0$.
2. Expanding, $(0.6-\lambda)(0.7-\lambda) - 0.12 = 0 \implies 0.42 - 0.6\lambda - 0.7\lambda + \lambda^2 - 0.12 = 0$.
3. Simplify: $0.42 - 1.3\lambda + \lambda^2 - 0.12 = 0 \implies \lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3 = 0$.
4. Solve the quadratic: $\lambda = \frac{1.3 \pm \sqrt{1.3^2 - 4 \cdot 0.3}}{2} = \frac{1.3 \pm \sqrt{1.69 - 1.2}}{2} = \frac{1.3 \pm \sqrt{0.49}}{2}$.
5. Since $\sqrt{0.49} = 0.7$, the eigenvalues are $\lambda_1 = \frac{1.3 + 0.7}{2} = 1$ and $\lambda_2 = \frac{1.3 - 0.7}{2} = 0.3$.
6. We are given $\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix}3/7 \\ 4/7\end{bmatrix}$, which corresponds to $\lambda_1 = 1$.
7. To find $\mathbf{v}_2$ for $\lambda_2 = 0.3$, solve $(A - 0.3I)\mathbf{v}_2 = 0$: $A - 0.3I = \begin{bmatrix}0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.4\end{bmatrix}$.
8. Set up the system: $0.3x + 0.3y = 0$, $0.4x + 0.4y = 0$.
9. Both equations reduce to $x + y = 0$, so $y = -x$.
10. Thus, an eigenvector for $\lambda_2 = 0.3$ is $\mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}$.
11. Therefore, a basis for $\mathbb{R}^2$ consisting of eigenvectors of $A$ is $\left\{\begin{bmatrix}3/7 \\ 4/7\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}\right\}$.
