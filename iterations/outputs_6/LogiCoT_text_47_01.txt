2. Compute $A^T$: $A^T = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix}$ (verified by transposing $A$).
3. Compute $A^TA$: $A^TA = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 3 & 1 \\ -2 & 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1^2 + 3^2 + (-2)^2 & 1\cdot5 + 3\cdot1 + (-2)\cdot4 \\ 5\cdot1 + 1\cdot3 + 4\cdot(-2) & 5^2 + 1^2 + 4^2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1+9+4 & 5+3-8 \\ 5+3-8 & 25+1+16 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 14 & 0 \\ 0 & 42 \end{bmatrix}$.
4. Compute $A^T\mathbf{b}$: $A^T\mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ -2 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1\cdot4 + 3\cdot(-2) + (-2)\cdot(-3) \\ 5\cdot4 + 1\cdot(-2) + 4\cdot(-3) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 - 6 + 6 \\ 20 - 2 - 12 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix}$.
5. Solve $A^TA\mathbf{x} = A^T\mathbf{b}$: $\begin{bmatrix} 14 & 0 \\ 0 & 42 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix}$, so $x_1 = \frac{4}{14} = \frac{2}{7}$, $x_2 = \frac{6}{42} = \frac{1}{7}$.
6. The least-squares solution is $\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{7} \\ \frac{1}{7} \end{bmatrix}$ (verified by substituting back into the normal equations).
7. The orthogonal projection of $\mathbf{b}$ onto $\text{Col }A$ is $A\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 3 & 1 \\ -2 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{7} \\ \frac{1}{7} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1\cdot\frac{2}{7} + 5\cdot\frac{1}{7} \\ 3\cdot\frac{2}{7} + 1\cdot\frac{1}{7} \\ -2\cdot\frac{2}{7} + 4\cdot\frac{1}{7} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{7} + \frac{5}{7} \\ \frac{6}{7} + \frac{1}{7} \\ -\frac{4}{7} + \frac{4}{7} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}$.
