1. To find a basis for the eigenspace corresponding to \(\lambda = 4\), solve \((A - 4I)\vec{x} = 0\). 2. Compute \(A - 4I = \begin{bmatrix}3-4 & 0 & 2 & 0 \\ 1 & 3-4 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1-4 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 4-4\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}-1 & 0 & 2 & 0 \\ 1 & -1 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0\end{bmatrix}\). 3. Set up the system \(\begin{bmatrix}-1 & 0 & 2 & 0 \\ 1 & -1 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \\ x_4\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0\end{bmatrix}\). 4. Write the equations: \(-x_1 + 2x_3 = 0\), \(x_1 - x_2 + x_3 = 0\), \(x_2 - 3x_3 = 0\), \(0 = 0\). 5. From the third equation, \(x_2 = 3x_3\). 6. Substitute \(x_2 = 3x_3\) into the second equation: \(x_1 - 3x_3 + x_3 = x_1 - 2x_3 = 0 \implies x_1 = 2x_3\). 7. From the first equation, \(-x_1 + 2x_3 = 0 \implies x_1 = 2x_3\), which is consistent. 8. Let \(x_3 = t\) (free variable), \(x_4 = s\) (free variable), so \(x_1 = 2t\), \(x_2 = 3t\), \(x_3 = t\), \(x_4 = s\). 9. The general solution is \(\vec{x} = t\begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ 0\end{bmatrix} + s\begin{bmatrix}0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 1\end{bmatrix}\). 10. Therefore, a basis for the eigenspace corresponding to \(\lambda = 4\) is \(\left\{\begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ 0\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 1\end{bmatrix}\right\}\).
