1. Write the matrix \( A = \begin{bmatrix}6 & 9 \\ 4 & 5\end{bmatrix} \) and recall that LU factorization expresses \( A = LU \), where \( L \) is lower triangular with ones on the diagonal and \( U \) is upper triangular.  
2. Let \( L = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 \\ l_{21} & 1\end{bmatrix} \) and \( U = \begin{bmatrix}u_{11} & u_{12} \\ 0 & u_{22}\end{bmatrix} \), so \( LU = \begin{bmatrix}u_{11} & u_{12} \\ l_{21}u_{11} & l_{21}u_{12} + u_{22}\end{bmatrix} \).  
3. Set \( u_{11} = 6 \) and \( u_{12} = 9 \) by matching the first row of \( LU \) to \( A \), since \( 1 \cdot u_{11} = 6 \) and \( 1 \cdot u_{12} = 9 \).  
4. Solve for \( l_{21} \) using the (2,1) entry: \( l_{21}u_{11} = 4 \implies l_{21} = \frac{4}{6} = \frac{2}{3} \).  
5. Solve for \( u_{22} \) using the (2,2) entry: \( l_{21}u_{12} + u_{22} = 5 \implies \frac{2}{3} \cdot 9 + u_{22} = 5 \implies 6 + u_{22} = 5 \implies u_{22} = -1 \).  
6. Thus, \( L = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 \\ \frac{2}{3} & 1\end{bmatrix} \) and \( U = \begin{bmatrix}6 & 9 \\ 0 & -1\end{bmatrix} \); verify by multiplying: \( LU = \begin{bmatrix}6 & 9 \\ \frac{2}{3} \cdot 6 & \frac{2}{3} \cdot 9 - 1\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}6 & 9 \\ 4 & 5\end{bmatrix} \), which matches \( A \).  
7. Final answer: The LU factorization is \( \boxed{A = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 \\ \frac{2}{3} & 1\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}6 & 9 \\ 0 & -1\end{bmatrix}} \).
