1. We are given \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -2 \\ -2 & 1 & 6 \\ 3 & -2 & -5 \end{bmatrix}\) and \(\mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 7 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix}\), and must solve \(A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{b}\).
2. Write the augmented matrix: \(\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & -2 & -1 \\ -2 & 1 & 6 & 7 \\ 3 & -2 & -5 & -3 \end{array}\right]\).
3. Use row 1 to eliminate the \(x_1\) terms in rows 2 and 3: Row 2 + 2*Row 1 \(\rightarrow\) Row 2, Row 3 - 3*Row 1 \(\rightarrow\) Row 3.
4. The new matrix is \(\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & -2 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & 5 \\ 0 & -2 & 1 & 0 \end{array}\right]\).
5. Use row 2 to eliminate the \(x_2\) term in row 3: Row 3 + 2*Row 2 \(\rightarrow\) Row 3.
6. The new matrix is \(\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & -2 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 & 10 \end{array}\right]\).
7. Solve for \(x_3\) from row 3: \(5x_3 = 10 \implies x_3 = 2\).
8. Substitute \(x_3 = 2\) into row 2: \(x_2 + 2(2) = 5 \implies x_2 = 1\).
9. Substitute \(x_2 = 1\), \(x_3 = 2\) into row 1: \(x_1 - 2(2) = -1 \implies x_1 = 3\).
10. The solution is \(\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}\).
11. Since the matrix reduces to row-echelon form with no free variables, the solution is unique.
