1. Write the matrix \( A = \begin{bmatrix}6 & 9 \\ 4 & 5\end{bmatrix} \) and recall that LU factorization expresses \( A = LU \), where \( L \) is lower triangular with ones on the diagonal and \( U \) is upper triangular. 
2. Set \( L = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 \\ l_{21} & 1\end{bmatrix} \) and \( U = \begin{bmatrix}u_{11} & u_{12} \\ 0 & u_{22}\end{bmatrix} \), then compute \( LU = \begin{bmatrix}u_{11} & u_{12} \\ l_{21}u_{11} & l_{21}u_{12} + u_{22}\end{bmatrix} \). 
3. Equate \( LU \) to \( A \) to get \( u_{11} = 6 \), \( u_{12} = 9 \), \( l_{21}u_{11} = 4 \), and \( l_{21}u_{12} + u_{22} = 5 \). 
4. Solve \( l_{21}u_{11} = 4 \) to get \( l_{21} = \frac{4}{6} = \frac{2}{3} \). 
5. Substitute \( l_{21} \) and \( u_{12} \) into \( l_{21}u_{12} + u_{22} = 5 \) to get \( \frac{2}{3} \times 9 + u_{22} = 5 \), so \( 6 + u_{22} = 5 \), thus \( u_{22} = -1 \). 
6. Write the LU factorization as \( L = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 \\ \frac{2}{3} & 1\end{bmatrix} \), \( U = \begin{bmatrix}6 & 9 \\ 0 & -1\end{bmatrix} \). 
7. Verify by multiplying \( L \) and \( U \): \( \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 \\ \frac{2}{3} & 1\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}6 & 9 \\ 0 & -1\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}6 & 9 \\ 4 & 5\end{bmatrix} \), confirming correctness. 
8. The LU factorization of \( \begin{bmatrix}6 & 9 \\ 4 & 5\end{bmatrix} \) is \( L = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 \\ \frac{2}{3} & 1\end{bmatrix} \), \( U = \begin{bmatrix}6 & 9 \\ 0 & -1\end{bmatrix} \).
