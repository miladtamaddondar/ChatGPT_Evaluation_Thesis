1. Write the augmented matrix: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & -2 & -1 \\ -2 & 1 & 6 & 7 \\ 3 & -2 & -5 & -3 \end{array}\right]$.
2. $R_2 \to R_2 + 2R_1$: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & -2 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & 5 \\ 3 & -2 & -5 & -3 \end{array}\right]$.
3. $R_3 \to R_3 - 3R_1$: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & -2 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & 5 \\ 0 & -2 & 1 & 0 \end{array}\right]$.
4. $R_3 \to R_3 + 2R_2$: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & -2 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 & 10 \end{array}\right]$.
5. $R_3 \to \frac{1}{5}R_3$: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & -2 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 2 \end{array}\right]$.
6. $R_2 \to R_2 - 2R_3$: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & -2 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 2 \end{array}\right]$.
7. $R_1 \to R_1 + 2R_3$: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & 0 & 3 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 2 \end{array}\right]$.
8. The unique solution is $\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix}3 \\ 1 \\ 2\end{bmatrix}$, so $\mathbf{x}$ is unique.
