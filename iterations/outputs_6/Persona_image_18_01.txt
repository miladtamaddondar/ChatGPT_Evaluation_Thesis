1. To diagonalize \(A\), first find its eigenvalues by solving \(\det(A - \lambda I) = 0\): \(\det\left(\begin{bmatrix}0-\lambda & 1\\-3 & 4-\lambda\end{bmatrix}\right) = (0-\lambda)(4-\lambda) - (-3)(1) = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 3 = 0\).
2. Solve the characteristic equation \(\lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 3 = 0\) to get eigenvalues: \(\lambda = 1\) and \(\lambda = 3\).
3. For \(\lambda = 1\), solve \((A - I)\vec{v} = 0\): \(A - I = \begin{bmatrix}-1 & 1\\-3 & 3\end{bmatrix}\), so \(-v_1 + v_2 = 0\) gives eigenvector \(\vec{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix}1\\1\end{bmatrix}\).
4. For \(\lambda = 3\), solve \((A - 3I)\vec{v} = 0\): \(A - 3I = \begin{bmatrix}-3 & 1\\-3 & 1\end{bmatrix}\), so \(-3v_1 + v_2 = 0\) gives eigenvector \(\vec{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}1\\3\end{bmatrix}\).
5. The basis \(\mathcal{B}\) for \(\mathbb{R}^2\) that diagonalizes \(A\) is \(\left\{\begin{bmatrix}1\\1\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}1\\3\end{bmatrix}\right\}\).
