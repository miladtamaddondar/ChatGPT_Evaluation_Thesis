1. To construct a \( 4 \times 3 \) matrix of rank 1, recall that such a matrix can be written as the outer product of a nonzero \( 4 \times 1 \) column vector and a nonzero \( 1 \times 3 \) row vector: \( A = \mathbf{u} \mathbf{v}^T \).
2. Choose \( \mathbf{u} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( \mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \).
3. Compute the outer product \( A = \mathbf{u} \mathbf{v}^T = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & 2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & 2 \\ 2 & -2 & 4 \\ 3 & -3 & 6 \\ 4 & -4 & 8 \end{bmatrix} \).
4. The resulting matrix \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & 2 \\ 2 & -2 & 4 \\ 3 & -3 & 6 \\ 4 & -4 & 8 \end{bmatrix} \) is a \( 4 \times 3 \) matrix of rank 1.
