2. The pivot columns in the echelon form are columns 1, 3, and 4, so the corresponding columns of $A$ form a basis for $\text{Col }A$: $\left\{ \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 3 \\ 2 \\ -4\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}2 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 2\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}-4 \\ 5 \\ -3 \\ 7\end{bmatrix} \right\}$.
3. To find a basis for $\text{Nul }A$, write the solution to $A\vec{x} = \vec{0}$ using the reduced echelon form: let $x_2$ be free, then back-substitute to get $x_4 = 0$, $x_3 = 0$, $x_1 = 3x_2$.
4. The general solution is $\vec{x} = x_2 \begin{bmatrix}3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0\end{bmatrix}$, so a basis for $\text{Nul }A$ is $\left\{ \begin{bmatrix}3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0\end{bmatrix} \right\}$.
5. The dimension of $\text{Col }A$ is $3$ (number of pivot columns), and the dimension of $\text{Nul }A$ is $1$ (number of free variables).
