1. We are given $W = \left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 3a + b \\ 4 \\ a - 5b \end{bmatrix} : a, b, c \in \mathbb{R} \right\}$, but notice that $c$ does not appear in the vector, so $W = \left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 3a + b \\ 4 \\ a - 5b \end{bmatrix} : a, b \in \mathbb{R} \right\}$.  
2. To check if $W$ is a vector space, we test closure under addition: let $\mathbf{u} = \begin{bmatrix} 3a_1 + b_1 \\ 4 \\ a_1 - 5b_1 \end{bmatrix}$ and $\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 3a_2 + b_2 \\ 4 \\ a_2 - 5b_2 \end{bmatrix}$, then $\mathbf{u} + \mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} (3a_1 + b_1) + (3a_2 + b_2) \\ 4 + 4 \\ (a_1 - 5b_1) + (a_2 - 5b_2) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3(a_1 + a_2) + (b_1 + b_2) \\ 8 \\ (a_1 + a_2) - 5(b_1 + b_2) \end{bmatrix}$.  
3. The second entry of the sum is $8$, not $4$, so $\mathbf{u} + \mathbf{v} \notin W$ because vectors in $W$ must have $4$ as their second entry, thus $W$ is not closed under addition.  
4. Therefore, $W$ is not a vector space; for example, $\begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 4 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 4 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 8 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}$, but $\begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 8 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \notin W$ since the second entry is not $4$.
