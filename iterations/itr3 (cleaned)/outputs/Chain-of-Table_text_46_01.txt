1. To find all least-squares solutions, solve the normal equations $A^T A \mathbf{x} = A^T \mathbf{b}$; first, compute $A^T A = \begin{bmatrix} 4 & 2 & 2 \\ 2 & 2 & 0 \\ 2 & 0 & 2 \end{bmatrix}$ and $A^T \mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 14 \\ 4 \\ 10 \end{bmatrix}$.  
2. Write the augmented matrix: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 4 & 2 & 2 & 14 \\ 2 & 2 & 0 & 4 \\ 2 & 0 & 2 & 10 \end{array}\right]$.  
3. $R_2 \leftarrow R_2 - \frac{1}{2}R_1$: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 4 & 2 & 2 & 14 \\ 0 & 1 & -1 & -3 \\ 2 & 0 & 2 & 10 \end{array}\right]$.  
4. $R_3 \leftarrow R_3 - \frac{1}{2}R_1$: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 4 & 2 & 2 & 14 \\ 0 & 1 & -1 & -3 \\ 0 & -1 & 1 & 3 \end{array}\right]$.  
5. $R_3 \leftarrow R_3 + R_2$: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 4 & 2 & 2 & 14 \\ 0 & 1 & -1 & -3 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{array}\right]$.  
6. Back-substitute: From $R_2$, $x_2 - x_3 = -3 \implies x_2 = x_3 - 3$; from $R_1$, $4x_1 + 2x_2 + 2x_3 = 14 \implies 4x_1 + 2(x_3 - 3) + 2x_3 = 14 \implies 4x_1 + 4x_3 - 6 = 14 \implies 4x_1 + 4x_3 = 20 \implies x_1 + x_3 = 5 \implies x_1 = 5 - x_3$.  
7. The general least-squares solution is $\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 5 - t \\ t - 3 \\ t \end{bmatrix}$ for all $t \in \mathbb{R}$.
