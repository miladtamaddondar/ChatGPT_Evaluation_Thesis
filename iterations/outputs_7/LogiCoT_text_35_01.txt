1. Let the coordinate vector be $\begin{bmatrix} c_1 \\ c_2 \\ c_3 \end{bmatrix}$ so that $p(t) = c_1(1+t^2) + c_2(t+t^2) + c_3(1+2t+t^2)$; expand the right side: $c_1(1+t^2) + c_2(t+t^2) + c_3(1+2t+t^2) = c_1 + c_1 t^2 + c_2 t + c_2 t^2 + c_3 + 2c_3 t + c_3 t^2$.
2. Combine like terms: constant term: $c_1 + c_3$, $t$ term: $c_2 + 2c_3$, $t^2$ term: $c_1 + c_2 + c_3$.
3. Set up the system by equating coefficients with $p(t) = 1 + 4t + 7t^2$: $c_1 + c_3 = 1$, $c_2 + 2c_3 = 4$, $c_1 + c_2 + c_3 = 7$.
4. Write the system as an augmented matrix: $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 1 & | & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & | & 4 \\ 1 & 1 & 1 & | & 7 \end{bmatrix}$.
5. Subtract row 1 from row 3 to eliminate $c_1$ in row 3: row 3 becomes $(1-1, 1-0, 1-1, 7-1) = (0, 1, 0, 6)$.
6. The new matrix is $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 1 & | & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & | & 4 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & | & 6 \end{bmatrix}$; subtract row 2 from row 3 to eliminate $c_2$ in row 3: row 3 becomes $(0, 1-1, 0-2, 6-4) = (0, 0, -2, 2)$.
7. The matrix is now $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 1 & | & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & | & 4 \\ 0 & 0 & -2 & | & 2 \end{bmatrix}$; solve for $c_3$ from row 3: $-2c_3 = 2 \implies c_3 = -1$.
8. Substitute $c_3 = -1$ into row 2: $c_2 + 2(-1) = 4 \implies c_2 - 2 = 4 \implies c_2 = 6$.
9. Substitute $c_3 = -1$ into row 1: $c_1 + (-1) = 1 \implies c_1 = 2$.
10. The coordinate vector of $p(t)$ relative to $\mathcal{B}$ is $\boxed{\begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 6 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix}}$.
