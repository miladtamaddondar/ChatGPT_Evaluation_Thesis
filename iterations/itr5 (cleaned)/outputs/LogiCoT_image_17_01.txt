2. Calculate \( p(-1) = 5 + 3(-1) = 2 \), \( p(0) = 5 + 3(0) = 5 \), and \( p(1) = 5 + 3(1) = 8 \); each step uses direct substitution and evaluation of the polynomial.  
3. Thus, the image is \( T(5 + 3t) = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 5 \\ 8 \end{bmatrix} \); the correctness follows from the definition of \( T \) and polynomial evaluation.  
4. To find the matrix for \( T \) relative to the basis \( \{1, t, t^2\} \) for \( \mathbb{P}_2 \) and the standard basis for \( \mathbb{R}^3 \), apply \( T \) to each basis vector:  
5. For \( 1 \), \( T(1) = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \) because \( 1 \) evaluated at any point is 1.  
6. For \( t \), \( T(t) = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \) because \( t(-1) = -1 \), \( t(0) = 0 \), \( t(1) = 1 \).  
7. For \( t^2 \), \( T(t^2) = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \) because \( t^2(-1) = 1 \), \( t^2(0) = 0 \), \( t^2(1) = 1 \).  
8. The matrix for \( T \) is formed by placing these results as columns: \( \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 1 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \); this follows from the definition of the matrix representation of a linear transformation.
