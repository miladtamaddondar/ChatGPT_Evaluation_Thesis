1. To write $\mathbf{y}$ as the sum of a vector in $W$ and a vector orthogonal to $W$, we seek $\mathbf{y} = \mathbf{y}_W + \mathbf{y}_\perp$, where $\mathbf{y}_W \in W$ and $\mathbf{y}_\perp \perp W$.
2. The vector $\mathbf{y}_W$ is the orthogonal projection of $\mathbf{y}$ onto $W$, so we write $\mathbf{y}_W = a_1\mathbf{u}_1 + a_2\mathbf{u}_2 + a_3\mathbf{u}_3$ for some scalars $a_1, a_2, a_3$.
3. The orthogonality condition requires $\mathbf{y}_\perp = \mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W$ to be orthogonal to each $\mathbf{u}_i$, so $\mathbf{u}_i^T(\mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W) = 0$ for $i=1,2,3$.
4. This gives the system: $\mathbf{u}_i^T\mathbf{y} = \mathbf{u}_i^T(a_1\mathbf{u}_1 + a_2\mathbf{u}_2 + a_3\mathbf{u}_3)$ for $i=1,2,3$.
5. Let $U = [\mathbf{u}_1\ \mathbf{u}_2\ \mathbf{u}_3]$ be the $4\times3$ matrix with the $\mathbf{u}_i$ as columns; then the system is $U^T\mathbf{y} = U^TU\mathbf{a}$, where $\mathbf{a} = \begin{bmatrix} a_1 \\ a_2 \\ a_3 \end{bmatrix}$.
6. Compute $U^T\mathbf{y}$: $\mathbf{u}_1^T\mathbf{y} = 4+3+0+(-1) = 6$, $\mathbf{u}_2^T\mathbf{y} = -4+9+3+2 = 10$, $\mathbf{u}_3^T\mathbf{y} = -4+0+3+(-1) = -2$, so $U^T\mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 10 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix}$.
7. Compute $U^TU$ by calculating all dot products: $\mathbf{u}_1^T\mathbf{u}_1 = 1+1+0+1=3$, $\mathbf{u}_1^T\mathbf{u}_2 = -1+3+0-2=0$, $\mathbf{u}_1^T\mathbf{u}_3 = -1+0+0+1=0$, $\mathbf{u}_2^T\mathbf{u}_2 = 1+9+1+4=15$, $\mathbf{u}_2^T\mathbf{u}_3 = 1+0+1-2=0$, $\mathbf{u}_3^T\mathbf{u}_3 = 1+0+1+1=3$, so $U^TU = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 15 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 3 \end{bmatrix}$.
8. The system $U^TU\mathbf{a} = U^T\mathbf{y}$ becomes $\begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 15 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} a_1 \\ a_2 \\ a_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 10 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix}$, which gives $a_1 = 2$, $a_2 = \frac{2}{3}$, $a_3 = -\frac{2}{3}$.
9. Substitute back: $\mathbf{y}_W = 2\mathbf{u}_1 + \frac{2}{3}\mathbf{u}_2 - \frac{2}{3}\mathbf{u}_3$.
10. Compute $\mathbf{y}_W = 2\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} + \frac{2}{3}\begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix} - \frac{2}{3}\begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 2 \\ 0 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} -2/3 \\ 2 \\ 2/3 \\ -4/3 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 2/3 \\ 0 \\ -2/3 \\ -2/3 \end{bmatrix}$.
11. Add the vectors: first entry $2 - 2/3 + 2/3 = 2$, second entry $2 + 2 + 0 = 4$, third entry $0 + 2/3 - 2/3 = 0$, fourth entry $2 - 4/3 - 2/3 = 2 - 2 = 0$, so $\mathbf{y}_W = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 4 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}$.
12. Compute $\mathbf{y}_\perp = \mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 4 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix}$.
13. Final answer: $\boxed{\mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 4 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix}}$, where the first vector is in $W$ and the second is orthogonal to $W$.
