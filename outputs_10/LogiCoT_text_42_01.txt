1. Find a basis for the subspace \( W \) spanned by \( \mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_2, \mathbf{u}_3 \) by checking their linear independence using the matrix \( U = [\mathbf{u}_1 \ \mathbf{u}_2 \ \mathbf{u}_3] = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \).  
2. Compute the rank of \( U \) by row reducing: Subtract row 1 from row 2 and row 4, yielding \( \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 0 & -1 & 2 \end{bmatrix} \); then subtract \(\frac{1}{4}\) of row 2 from row 3 and add row 2 to row 4, giving \( \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & \frac{3}{4} \\ 0 & 0 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \), so the rank is 3 and the vectors are linearly independent.  
3. The subspace \( W \) is 3-dimensional and spanned by \( \mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_2, \mathbf{u}_3 \), so the orthogonal decomposition of \( \mathbf{y} \) is \( \mathbf{y} = \mathbf{y}_W + \mathbf{y}_\perp \), where \( \mathbf{y}_W \in W \) and \( \mathbf{y}_\perp \perp W \).  
4. Express \( \mathbf{y}_W \) as a linear combination: \( \mathbf{y}_W = c_1 \mathbf{u}_1 + c_2 \mathbf{u}_2 + c_3 \mathbf{u}_3 \), and set up the equation \( U\mathbf{c} = \mathbf{y} \), where \( \mathbf{c} = \begin{bmatrix} c_1 \\ c_2 \\ c_3 \end{bmatrix} \).  
5. Write the system: \( \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} c_1 \\ c_2 \\ c_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} \).  
6. Solve for \( c_1, c_2, c_3 \): The equations are \( c_1 - c_2 - c_3 = 4 \), \( c_1 + 3c_2 = 3 \), \( c_2 + c_3 = 3 \), \( c_1 - 2c_2 + c_3 = -1 \).  
7. From equation 3, \( c_3 = 3 - c_2 \); substitute into equation 1: \( c_1 - c_2 - (3 - c_2) = 4 \implies c_1 - c_2 - 3 + c_2 = 4 \implies c_1 = 7 \).  
8. Substitute \( c_1 = 7 \) into equation 2: \( 7 + 3c_2 = 3 \implies 3c_2 = -4 \implies c_2 = -\frac{4}{3} \).  
9. Substitute \( c_2 = -\frac{4}{3} \) into equation 3: \( c_3 = 3 - \left(-\frac{4}{3}\right) = \frac{13}{3} \).  
10. Check equation 4: \( c_1 - 2c_2 + c_3 = 7 - 2\left(-\frac{4}{3}\right) + \frac{13}{3} = 7 + \frac{8}{3} + \frac{13}{3} = 7 + 7 = 14 \), but the right side is \(-1\), so there is a contradiction, indicating the system is inconsistent and \( \mathbf{y} \notin W \).  
11. Since \( \mathbf{y} \notin W \), find the orthogonal projection of \( \mathbf{y} \) onto \( W \) using \( \mathbf{y}_W = U(U^T U)^{-1} U^T \mathbf{y} \).  
12. Compute \( U^T U = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 & 1 & -2 \\ -1 & 0 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 15 & 6 \\ 1 & 6 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \).  
13. Compute \( U^T \mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 & 1 & -2 \\ -1 & 0 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 + 3 + 0 - 1 = 6 \\ -4 + 9 + 3 + 2 = 10 \\ -4 + 0 + 3 - 1 = -2 \end{bmatrix} \), so \( U^T \mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 10 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix} \).  
14. Solve \( (U^T U) \mathbf{c} = U^T \mathbf{y} \), i.e., \( \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 15 & 6 \\ 1 & 6 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} c_1 \\ c_2 \\ c_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 10 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix} \).  
15. Use Gaussian elimination or matrix inversion to solve for \( \mathbf{c} \): First, from the first equation, \( 3c_1 + c_3 = 6 \implies c_3 = 6 - 3c_1 \); second equation, \( 15c_2 + 6c_3 = 10 \), substitute \( c_3 \): \( 15c_2 + 6(6 - 3c_1) = 10 \implies 15c_2 + 36 - 18c_1 = 10 \implies 15c_2 - 18c_1 = -26 \); third equation, \( c_1 + 6c_2 + 4c_3 = -2 \), substitute \( c_3 \): \( c_1 + 6c_2 + 4(6 - 3c_1) = -2 \implies c_1 + 6c_2 + 24 - 12c_1 = -2 \implies -11c_1 + 6c_2 = -26 \).  
16. Now solve the two equations: (a) \( 15c_2 - 18c_1 = -26 \), (b) \( -11c_1 + 6c_2 = -26 \); multiply (b) by 15: \( -165c_1 + 90c_2 = -390 \); multiply (a) by 6: \( 90c_2 - 108c_1 = -156 \); subtract: \( (-165c_1 + 90c_2) - ( -108c_1 + 90c_2 ) = -390 - (-156) \implies -57c_1 = -234 \implies c_1 = \frac{-234}{-57} = 4.105... \), but let's check calculations for arithmetic errors.  
17. Let's solve equations (a) and (b) directly: From (b), \( -11c_1 + 6c_2 = -26 \implies 6c_2 = -26 + 11c_1 \implies c_2 = \frac{-26 + 11c_1}{6} \); substitute into (a): \( 15c_2 - 18c_1 = -26 \implies 15 \left( \frac{-26 + 11c_1}{6} \right) - 18c_1 = -26 \implies \frac{-390 + 165c_1}{6} - 18c_1 = -26 \implies \frac{-390 + 165c_1 - 108c_1}{6} = -26 \implies \frac{-390 + 57c_1}{6} = -26 \implies -390 + 57c_1 = -156 \implies 57c_1 = 234 \implies c_1 = \frac{234}{57} = \frac{82}{19} \).  
18. Substitute \( c_1 = \frac{82}{19} \) into the expression for \( c_2 \): \( c_2 = \frac{-26 + 11 \cdot \frac{82}{19}}{6} = \frac{-26 + \frac{902}{19}}{6} = \frac{ \frac{-494 + 902}{19} }{6 } = \frac{ \frac{408}{19} }{6 } = \frac{408}{114} = \frac{68}{19} \).  
19. Substitute \( c_1 = \frac{82}{19} \) into \( c_3 = 6 - 3c_1 = 6 - 3 \cdot \frac{82}{19} = 6 - \frac{246}{19} = \frac{114 - 246}{19} = \frac{-132}{19} \).  
20. Now compute \( \mathbf{y}_W = c_1 \mathbf{u}_1 + c_2 \mathbf{u}_2 + c_3 \mathbf{u}_3 = \frac{82}{19} \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} + \frac{68}{19} \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix} + \frac{-132}{19} \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \).  
21. Compute each component: First entry: \( \frac{82}{19} \cdot 1 + \frac{68}{19} \cdot (-1) + \frac{-132}{19} \cdot (-1) = \frac{82 - 68 + 132}{19} = \frac{146}{19} = 7.684 \); second entry: \( \frac{82}{19} \cdot 1 + \frac{68}{19} \cdot 3 + \frac{-132}{19} \cdot 0 = \frac{82 + 204 + 0}{19} = \frac{286}{19} = 15.053 \); third entry: \( \frac{82}{19} \cdot 0 + \frac{68}{19} \cdot 1 + \frac{-132}{19} \cdot 1 = \frac{0 + 68 - 132}{19} = \frac{-64}{19} = -3.368 \); fourth entry: \( \frac{82}{19} \cdot 1 + \frac{68}{19} \cdot (-2) + \frac{-132}{19} \cdot 1 = \frac{82 - 136 - 132}{19} = \frac{-186}{19} = -9.789 \).  
22. Therefore, \( \mathbf{y}_W = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{146}{19} \\ \frac{286}{19} \\ \frac{-64}{19} \\ \frac{-186}{19} \end{bmatrix} \).  
23. The orthogonal component is \( \mathbf{y}_\perp = \mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} \frac{146}{19} \\ \frac{286}{19} \\ \frac{-64}{19} \\ \frac{-186}{19} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 - \frac{146}{19} \\ 3 - \frac{286}{19} \\ 3 - \frac{-64}{19} \\ -1 - \frac{-186}{19} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{76 - 146}{19} \\ \frac{57 - 286}{19} \\ \frac{57 + 64}{19} \\ \frac{-19 + 186}{19} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{-70}{19} \\ \frac{-229}{19} \\ \frac{121}{19} \\ \frac{167}{19} \end{bmatrix} \).  
24. Final answer: \( \boxed{ \mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{146}{19} \\ \frac{286}{19} \\ \frac{-64}{19} \\ \frac{-186}{19} \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} \frac{-70}{19} \\ \frac{-229}{19} \\ \frac{121}{19} \\ \frac{167}{19} \end{bmatrix} } \), where the first vector is in \( W \) and the second is orthogonal to \( W \).
