2. The null space of \(A\) consists of all vectors \(x\) such that \(Ax = 0\), so let \(x = \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix}\) and solve \(A x = 0\):  
3. Compute \(A x = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & -6 \\ -1 & 2 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3x_1 - 6x_2 \\ -x_1 + 2x_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}\).  
4. Set up the system: \(3x_1 - 6x_2 = 0\) and \(-x_1 + 2x_2 = 0\).  
5. Solve the first equation for \(x_1\): \(3x_1 = 6x_2 \implies x_1 = 2x_2\).  
6. Substitute \(x_1 = 2x_2\) into the second equation: \(-2x_2 + 2x_2 = 0\), which is always true, so the null space is all scalar multiples of \(\begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}\).  
7. To use two different nonzero columns for \(B\), choose two different nonzero multiples, e.g., \(\begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}\) and \(\begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}\).  
8. Construct \(B = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 4 \\ 1 & 2 \end{bmatrix}\).  
9. Final answer: A matrix \(B = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 4 \\ 1 & 2 \end{bmatrix}\) satisfies \(AB = 0\) with two different nonzero columns.
