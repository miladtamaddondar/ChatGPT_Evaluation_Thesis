1. To find the standard matrix of a rotation by angle $\theta$, we use $A = \begin{bmatrix} \cos\theta & -\sin\theta \\ \sin\theta & \cos\theta \end{bmatrix}$.
2. Substitute $\theta = \frac{3\pi}{2}$: $A = \begin{bmatrix} \cos\frac{3\pi}{2} & -\sin\frac{3\pi}{2} \\ \sin\frac{3\pi}{2} & \cos\frac{3\pi}{2} \end{bmatrix}$.
3. Compute $\cos\frac{3\pi}{2} = 0$ and $\sin\frac{3\pi}{2} = -1$.
4. Substitute values: $A = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & -(-1) \\ -1 & 0 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 0 \end{bmatrix}$.
5. The standard matrix of $T$ is $\boxed{\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 0 \end{bmatrix}}$.
