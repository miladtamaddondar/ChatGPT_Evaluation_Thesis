1. Find the eigenvalues of \(A\) by solving \(\det(A - \lambda I) = 0\): \(\det\begin{bmatrix}0-\lambda & 1\\ -3 & 4-\lambda\end{bmatrix} = (0-\lambda)(4-\lambda) - (-3)(1) = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 3 = 0\).
2. Factor and solve for \(\lambda\): \(\lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 3 = (\lambda-1)(\lambda-3) = 0 \implies \lambda_1 = 1, \lambda_2 = 3\).
3. Find eigenvector for \(\lambda_1 = 1\) by solving \((A - I)\mathbf{v} = 0\): \(A - I = \begin{bmatrix}-1 & 1\\ -3 & 3\end{bmatrix}\).
4. Row reduce \(\begin{bmatrix}-1 & 1 \\ -3 & 3\end{bmatrix}\): Add 3 times row 1 to row 2: \(\begin{bmatrix}-1 & 1 \\ 0 & 0\end{bmatrix}\).
5. The equation \(-v_1 + v_2 = 0\) gives \(v_2 = v_1\), so an eigenvector is \(\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 1\end{bmatrix}\).
6. Find eigenvector for \(\lambda_2 = 3\) by solving \((A - 3I)\mathbf{v} = 0\): \(A - 3I = \begin{bmatrix}-3 & 1\\ -3 & 1\end{bmatrix}\).
7. Row reduce \(\begin{bmatrix}-3 & 1 \\ -3 & 1\end{bmatrix}\): Subtract row 1 from row 2: \(\begin{bmatrix}-3 & 1 \\ 0 & 0\end{bmatrix}\).
8. The equation \(-3v_1 + v_2 = 0\) gives \(v_2 = 3v_1\), so an eigenvector is \(\mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 3\end{bmatrix}\).
9. Therefore, a basis \(\mathcal{B} = \left\{ \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 1\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 3\end{bmatrix} \right\}\) diagonalizes \(A\), and \([T]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0\\ 0 & 3\end{bmatrix}\).
