The equation $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 4 \\ 2 & -6 & 8 \end{bmatrix} = \mathbf{u}\mathbf{v}^T$ means $\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} v_1 & v_2 & v_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 4 \\ 2 & -6 & 8 \end{bmatrix}$, so the matrix multiplication gives $\begin{bmatrix} 1 v_1 & 1 v_2 & 1 v_3 \\ 2 v_1 & 2 v_2 & 2 v_3 \end{bmatrix}$.
2. Set up equations: $1 v_1 = 1$, $1 v_2 = -3$, $1 v_3 = 4$, $2 v_1 = 2$, $2 v_2 = -6$, $2 v_3 = 8$.
3. Solve for $v_1$: $1 v_1 = 1 \implies v_1 = 1$.
4. Solve for $v_2$: $1 v_2 = -3 \implies v_2 = -3$.
5. Solve for $v_3$: $1 v_3 = 4 \implies v_3 = 4$.
6. Check consistency: $2 v_1 = 2 \implies 2 \times 1 = 2$, $2 v_2 = -6 \implies 2 \times (-3) = -6$, $2 v_3 = 8 \implies 2 \times 4 = 8$.
7. Therefore, $\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix}$.
