1. To find another eigenvector \(\mathbf{v}_2\) of \(A\), first find the eigenvalues by solving \(\det(A - \lambda I) = 0\): \(\left|\begin{matrix}0.6-\lambda & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.7-\lambda\end{matrix}\right| = (0.6-\lambda)(0.7-\lambda) - 0.12 = \lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3 = 0\).
2. Solve the quadratic equation: \(\lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3 = 0\) gives \(\lambda_1 = 1\), \(\lambda_2 = 0.3\).
3. To find the eigenvector for \(\lambda_2 = 0.3\), solve \((A - 0.3I)\mathbf{v}_2 = 0\): \(\begin{pmatrix}0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.4\end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix}x \\ y\end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix}0 \\ 0\end{pmatrix}\).
4. Row reduce the matrix: Subtract \(\frac{4}{3}\) times row 1 from row 2: \(\begin{pmatrix}0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0 & 0\end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix}x \\ y\end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix}0 \\ 0\end{pmatrix}\).
5. The first row gives \(x + y = 0\), so an eigenvector is \(\mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{pmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{pmatrix}\).
6. Thus, a basis for \(\mathbb{R}^2\) consisting of eigenvectors of \(A\) is \(\left\{\begin{pmatrix}\frac{3}{7} \\ \frac{4}{7}\end{pmatrix}, \begin{pmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{pmatrix}\right\}\).
