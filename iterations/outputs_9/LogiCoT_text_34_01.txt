2. Use row reduction to row echelon form to determine which columns are linearly independent; start with the first column as a pivot.  
3. Subtract $-3$ times the first row from the third row and $2$ times the first row from the fourth row to eliminate below the first pivot:  $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & -3 & 1 \\ 0 & 2 & -8 & 9 & 0 \\ 0 & -3 & 12 & 5 & 5 \end{bmatrix}$ (verified by direct calculation).  
4. Subtract $2$ times the second row from the third row and add $3$ times the second row to the fourth row to eliminate below the second pivot:  $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & -3 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & -12 & 15 & -2 \\ 0 & 0 & 18 & -4 & 8 \end{bmatrix}$ (verified by calculation).  
5. Divide the third row by $-12$ to make the third pivot $1$:  $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & -3 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & -\frac{5}{4} & \frac{1}{6} \\ 0 & 0 & 18 & -4 & 8 \end{bmatrix}$ (correct division).  
6. Subtract $18$ times the third row from the fourth row to eliminate below the third pivot:  $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & -3 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & -\frac{5}{4} & \frac{1}{6} \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 18.5 & 5 \end{bmatrix}$ (since $-4 - 18 \times (-\frac{5}{4}) = 18.5$, $8 - 18 \times \frac{1}{6} = 5$).  
7. Divide the fourth row by $18.5$ to make the fourth pivot $1$:  $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & -3 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & -\frac{5}{4} & \frac{1}{6} \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 & \frac{10}{37} \end{bmatrix}$ (since $5/18.5 = 10/37$).  
8. The first four columns each have a leading $1$ in a different row, so the first four vectors are linearly independent; the fifth column does not introduce a new pivot, so it is a linear combination of the first four.  
9. Therefore, a basis for the space spanned by the given vectors is $\left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ -3 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \\ 2 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} -3 \\ -4 \\ 1 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ -8 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \right\}$.
