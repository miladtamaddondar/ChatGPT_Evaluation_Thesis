The problem asks to write \(\mathbf{y}\) as the sum of a vector in the subspace \(W\) (spanned by \(\mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_2, \mathbf{u}_3\)) and a vector orthogonal to \(W\), which means finding the orthogonal projection of \(\mathbf{y}\) onto \(W\), denoted \(\mathbf{y}_W\), and the orthogonal component \(\mathbf{y}_{W^\perp} = \mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W\). 
2. Since \(\mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_2, \mathbf{u}_3\) are not guaranteed to be orthogonal, we need to use the projection formula for a subspace: \(\mathbf{y}_W = \mathbf{U}(\mathbf{U}^T\mathbf{U})^{-1}\mathbf{U}^T\mathbf{y}\), where \(\mathbf{U} = [\mathbf{u}_1 \ \mathbf{u}_2 \ \mathbf{u}_3]\) is a \(4 \times 3\) matrix. 
3. Construct \(\mathbf{U}\): \(\mathbf{U} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\). 
4. Compute \(\mathbf{U}^T\mathbf{U}\): \(\mathbf{U}^T\mathbf{U} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 & 1 & -2 \\ -1 & 0 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 15 & -4 \\ 1 & -4 & 3 \end{bmatrix}\). 
5. Compute \(\mathbf{U}^T\mathbf{y}\): \(\mathbf{U}^T\mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 & 1 & -2 \\ -1 & 0 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 + 3 + 0 - 1 \\ -4 + 9 + 3 + 2 \\ -4 + 0 + 3 - 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 10 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix}\). 
6. Solve for coefficients: \(\mathbf{c} = (\mathbf{U}^T\mathbf{U})^{-1}\mathbf{U}^T\mathbf{y}\), so solve \(\begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 15 & -4 \\ 1 & -4 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} c_1 \\ c_2 \\ c_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 10 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix}\). 
7. Using Gaussian elimination or a calculator, we find \(\begin{bmatrix} c_1 \\ c_2 \\ c_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix}\). 
8. Compute \(\mathbf{y}_W = c_1\mathbf{u}_1 + c_2\mathbf{u}_2 + c_3\mathbf{u}_3 = 2\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} + 1\begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix} + (-2)\begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 2 \\ 0 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 0 \\ -2 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 5 \\ -1 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix}\). 
9. Compute the orthogonal component: \(\mathbf{y}_{W^\perp} = \mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 5 \\ -1 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -2 \\ 4 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}\). 
10. Final answer: \(\mathbf{y} = \underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 5 \\ -1 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix}}_{\text{in } W} + \underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -2 \\ 4 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}}_{\text{orthogonal to } W}\).
