1. Write $U$ as a $3 \times 2$ matrix: $U = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} & -\frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{1}{3} & \frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix}$.
2. Compute $U^T U$, a $2 \times 2$ matrix: $U^T U = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} & \frac{2}{3} \\ -\frac{2}{3} & \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} & -\frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{1}{3} & \frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$, since $\mathbf{u}_1$ and $\mathbf{u}_2$ are orthonormal.
3. Verify $U^T U$ by calculating the dot products: $\mathbf{u}_1 \cdot \mathbf{u}_1 = 1$, $\mathbf{u}_2 \cdot \mathbf{u}_2 = 1$, $\mathbf{u}_1 \cdot \mathbf{u}_2 = 0$, confirming orthonormality and correctness of $U^T U$.
4. Compute $UU^T$, a $3 \times 3$ matrix: $UU^T = \mathbf{u}_1 \mathbf{u}_1^T + \mathbf{u}_2 \mathbf{u}_2^T$.
5. Calculate $\mathbf{u}_1 \mathbf{u}_1^T = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{1}{3} \\ \frac{2}{3} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} & \frac{2}{3} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{4}{9} & \frac{2}{9} & \frac{4}{9} \\ \frac{2}{9} & \frac{1}{9} & \frac{2}{9} \\ \frac{4}{9} & \frac{2}{9} & \frac{4}{9} \end{bmatrix}$.
6. Calculate $\mathbf{u}_2 \mathbf{u}_2^T = \begin{bmatrix} -\frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} -\frac{2}{3} & \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{4}{9} & -\frac{4}{9} & -\frac{2}{9} \\ -\frac{4}{9} & \frac{4}{9} & \frac{2}{9} \\ -\frac{2}{9} & \frac{2}{9} & \frac{1}{9} \end{bmatrix}$.
7. Add the matrices to get $UU^T = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{8}{9} & -\frac{2}{9} & \frac{2}{9} \\ -\frac{2}{9} & \frac{5}{9} & \frac{4}{9} \\ \frac{2}{9} & \frac{4}{9} & \frac{5}{9} \end{bmatrix}$.
8. To compute $\operatorname{proj}_W \mathbf{y}$, use $\operatorname{proj}_W \mathbf{y} = UU^T \mathbf{y}$ since $\mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_2$ are orthonormal.
9. Compute $(UU^T)\mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{8}{9} & -\frac{2}{9} & \frac{2}{9} \\ -\frac{2}{9} & \frac{5}{9} & \frac{4}{9} \\ \frac{2}{9} & \frac{4}{9} & \frac{5}{9} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 8 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{8}{9} \cdot 4 + (-\frac{2}{9}) \cdot 8 + \frac{2}{9} \cdot 1 \\ (-\frac{2}{9}) \cdot 4 + \frac{5}{9} \cdot 8 + \frac{4}{9} \cdot 1 \\ \frac{2}{9} \cdot 4 + \frac{4}{9} \cdot 8 + \frac{5}{9} \cdot 1 \end{bmatrix}$.
10. Simplify each entry: first row: $\frac{32}{9} - \frac{16}{9} + \frac{2}{9} = \frac{18}{9} = 2$; second row: $-\frac{8}{9} + \frac{40}{9} + \frac{4}{9} = \frac{36}{9} = 4$; third row: $\frac{8}{9} + \frac{32}{9} + \frac{5}{9} = \frac{45}{9} = 5$.
11. Therefore, $\operatorname{proj}_W \mathbf{y} = (UU^T)\mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 4 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix}$.
