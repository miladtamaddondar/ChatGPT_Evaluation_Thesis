2. Add 3 times row 1 to row 2: \(\left[\begin{array}{cccc} 1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 & -7 \\ 2 & -6 & 4 & -3 \\ -4 & 12 & 2 & 7 \end{array}\right]\).
3. Subtract 2 times row 1 from row 3: \(\left[\begin{array}{cccc} 1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 & -7 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 5 \\ -4 & 12 & 2 & 7 \end{array}\right]\).
4. Add 4 times row 1 to row 4: \(\left[\begin{array}{cccc} 1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 & -7 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 10 & -9 \end{array}\right]\).
5. Subtract 2 times row 2 from row 4: \(\left[\begin{array}{cccc} 1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 & -7 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 5 \end{array}\right]\).

The matrix is now in row echelon form, with three nonzero rows, so the vectors corresponding to the pivot columns form a basis: \(\left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} -4 \\ 5 \\ -3 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \right\}\) is a basis and the dimension is 3.
