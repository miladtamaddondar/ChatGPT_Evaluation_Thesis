1. Start with the matrix \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 9 \\ 4 & 5 \end{bmatrix} \) and aim to write \( A = LU \) where \( L \) is lower triangular and \( U \) is upper triangular. 
2. To eliminate the 4 in the lower left, use the multiplier \( m = \frac{4}{6} = \frac{2}{3} \). 
3. Subtract \( \frac{2}{3} \) times the first row from the second row: \( R_2 \to R_2 - \frac{2}{3}R_1 \), so \( \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 9 \\ 4 & 5 \end{bmatrix} \xrightarrow{R_2 - \frac{2}{3}R_1} \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 9 \\ 0 & 5 - \frac{2}{3} \times 9 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 9 \\ 0 & 5 - 6 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 9 \\ 0 & -1 \end{bmatrix} \). 
4. The multipliers form the \( L \) matrix: \( L = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ \frac{2}{3} & 1 \end{bmatrix} \). 
5. The \( U \) matrix is the result after elimination: \( U = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 9 \\ 0 & -1 \end{bmatrix} \). 
6. Therefore, the LU factorization is \( \boxed{A = LU = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ \frac{2}{3} & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 9 \\ 0 & -1 \end{bmatrix}} \).
