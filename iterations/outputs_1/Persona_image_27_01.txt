The linear transformation \( T: \mathbb{R}^2 \to \mathbb{R}^2 \) is defined by \( T(\mathbf{e}_1) = \mathbf{y}_1 \) and \( T(\mathbf{e}_2) = \mathbf{y}_2 \), where \( \mathbf{e}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \), \( \mathbf{e}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \), \( \mathbf{y}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix} \), and \( \mathbf{y}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix} \).  
2. The standard matrix \( A \) of \( T \) has columns \( \mathbf{y}_1 \) and \( \mathbf{y}_2 \), so \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 5 & 6 \end{bmatrix} \).  
3. To find the image of \( \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} \), compute \( T\left(\begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix}\right) = A \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 5 & 6 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} \).  
4. Multiply: \( \begin{bmatrix} 2 \cdot 5 + (-1) \cdot (-3) \\ 5 \cdot 5 + 6 \cdot (-3) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 10 + 3 \\ 25 - 18 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 13 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \).  
5. To find the image of \( \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} \), compute \( T\left(\begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix}\right) = A \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 5 & 6 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} \).  
6. Multiply: \( \begin{bmatrix} 2x_1 - x_2 \\ 5x_1 + 6x_2 \end{bmatrix} \).  
7. Final answers: The image of \( \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} \) is \( \begin{bmatrix} 13 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \) and the image of \( \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} \) is \( \begin{bmatrix} 2x_1 - x_2 \\ 5x_1 + 6x_2 \end{bmatrix} \).
