1. Let \( U = [\mathbf{u}_1 \ \mathbf{u}_2] = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} & -\frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{1}{3} & \frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} \); this forms a \(3 \times 2\) matrix by concatenating the given vectors.  
2. Compute \( U^T U \): \( U^T U = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} & \frac{2}{3} \\ -\frac{2}{3} & \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} & -\frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{1}{3} & \frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \); this is the identity matrix, confirming that \(\mathbf{u}_1\) and \(\mathbf{u}_2\) are orthonormal.  
3. Compute \( U U^T \): \( U U^T = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} & -\frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{1}{3} & \frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} & \frac{2}{3} \\ -\frac{2}{3} & \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{8}{9} & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & \frac{5}{9} & \frac{4}{9} \\ 0 & \frac{4}{9} & \frac{5}{9} \end{bmatrix} \); this projects onto the column space of \(U\).  
4. Compute \(\text{proj}_W \mathbf{y} = U U^T \mathbf{y}\): \( U U^T \mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{8}{9} & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & \frac{5}{9} & \frac{4}{9} \\ 0 & \frac{4}{9} & \frac{5}{9} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 8 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{32}{9} \\ \frac{52}{9} \\ \frac{37}{9} \end{bmatrix} \); this is the orthogonal projection of \(\mathbf{y}\) onto \(W\).  
5. The final answers are: \( U^T U = I \), \( U U^T = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{8}{9} & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & \frac{5}{9} & \frac{4}{9} \\ 0 & \frac{4}{9} & \frac{5}{9} \end{bmatrix} \), and \( \text{proj}_W \mathbf{y} = (U U^T)\mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{32}{9} \\ \frac{52}{9} \\ \frac{37}{9} \end{bmatrix} \).
