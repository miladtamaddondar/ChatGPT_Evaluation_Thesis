1. Find the eigenvalues of \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -3 & 4 \end{bmatrix}\): Solve \(\det(A - \lambda I) = 0\): \(\det\left(\begin{bmatrix} 0-\lambda & 1 \\ -3 & 4-\lambda \end{bmatrix}\right) = (0-\lambda)(4-\lambda) - (-3)(1) = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 3 = 0\), so the eigenvalues are \(\lambda = 1, 3\).
2. Find the eigenvector for \(\lambda = 1\): Solve \((A - I)\vec{v} = 0\): \(A - I = \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ -3 & 3 \end{bmatrix}\), so \(-v_1 + v_2 = 0\) (second row is a multiple of the first), thus an eigenvector is \(\vec{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}\).
3. Find the eigenvector for \(\lambda = 3\): Solve \((A - 3I)\vec{v} = 0\): \(A - 3I = \begin{bmatrix} -3 & 1 \\ -3 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\), so \(-3v_1 + v_2 = 0\), thus an eigenvector is \(\vec{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix}\).
4. The basis \(\mathcal{B}\) that diagonalizes \(A\) is \(\left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} \right\}\), and in this basis, \([T]_{\mathcal{B}}\) is diagonal with entries 1 and 3.
