1. Write the normal equations for the least-squares solution: \(A^T A \vec{x} = A^T \vec{b}\), where \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 3 & 1 \\ -2 & 4 \end{bmatrix}\), \(\vec{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ -2 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix}\).
2. Compute \(A^T A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 3 & 1 \\ -2 & 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 14 & 1 \\ 1 & 42 \end{bmatrix}\).
3. Compute \(A^T \vec{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ -2 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 + 3(-2) + (-2)(-3) \\ 5(4) + 1(-2) + 4(-3) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 - 6 + 6 \\ 20 - 2 - 12 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix}\).
4. Form the augmented matrix for the normal equations: \(\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 14 & 1 & 4 \\ 1 & 42 & 6 \end{array}\right]\).
5. Row 2 minus \(\frac{1}{14}\) times Row 1: \(\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 14 & 1 & 4 \\ 0 & 41.9286 & 5.7143 \end{array}\right]\).
6. Row 2 divided by 41.9286: \(\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 14 & 1 & 4 \\ 0 & 1 & 0.1363 \end{array}\right]\).
7. Row 1 minus 1 times Row 2: \(\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 14 & 0 & 3.8637 \\ 0 & 1 & 0.1363 \end{array}\right]\).
8. Row 1 divided by 14: \(\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & 0 & 0.2760 \\ 0 & 1 & 0.1363 \end{array}\right]\).
9. The least-squares solution is \(\vec{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 0.2760 \\ 0.1363 \end{bmatrix}\).
10. The orthogonal projection of \(\vec{b}\) onto \(\text{Col }A\) is \(A\vec{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 3 & 1 \\ -2 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 0.2760 \\ 0.1363 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0.2760 + 5 \times 0.1363 \\ 3 \times 0.2760 + 0.1363 \\ -2 \times 0.2760 + 4 \times 0.1363 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0.2760 + 0.6815 \\ 0.8280 + 0.1363 \\ -0.5520 + 0.5452 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0.9575 \\ 0.9643 \\ -0.0068 \end{bmatrix}\).
