1. Find the eigenvalues of \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 0.6 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.7 \end{bmatrix}\) by solving \(\det(A - \lambda I) = 0\): \(\det\left(\begin{bmatrix} 0.6-\lambda & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.7-\lambda \end{bmatrix}\right) = (0.6-\lambda)(0.7-\lambda) - 0.12 = \lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3 = 0\).
2. Solve the characteristic equation \(\lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3 = 0\): The roots are \(\lambda_1 = 1\), \(\lambda_2 = 0.3\).
3. Find the eigenvector for \(\lambda_2 = 0.3\) by solving \((A - 0.3I)\mathbf{v}_2 = 0\): \(A - 0.3I = \begin{bmatrix} 0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.4 \end{bmatrix}\), so \(\begin{bmatrix} 0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}\).
4. Row reduce: The first row gives \(0.3x + 0.3y = 0\) or \(x + y = 0\), so \(y = -x\). Thus, an eigenvector is \(\mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix}\).
5. The basis for \(\mathbb{R}^2\) consisting of \(\mathbf{v}_1\) and another eigenvector \(\mathbf{v}_2\) is \(\left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 3/7 \\ 4/7 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} \right\}\).
