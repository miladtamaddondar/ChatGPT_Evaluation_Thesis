Recognize that \( Q r_1, Q r_2, \dots, Q r_p \) are the images of \( r_1, \dots, r_p \) under the linear transformation \( Q \), so the matrix \( \left[ Q r_1 \; Q r_2 \; \dots \; Q r_p \right] \) can be written as the product \( Q \left[ r_1 \; r_2 \; \dots \; r_p \right] \), that is, \( \left[ Q r_1 \; Q r_2 \; \dots \; Q r_p \right] = Q \begin{bmatrix} r_1 & r_2 & \dots & r_p \end{bmatrix} \).
