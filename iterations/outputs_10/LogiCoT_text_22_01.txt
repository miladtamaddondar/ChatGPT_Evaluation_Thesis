1. The standard matrix for a rotation by angle $\theta$ counterclockwise in $\mathbb{R}^2$ is $\begin{bmatrix} \cos\theta & -\sin\theta \\ \sin\theta & \cos\theta \end{bmatrix}$; this follows from the definition of rotation in linear algebra.
2. Substitute $\theta = \frac{3\pi}{2}$: $\cos\left(\frac{3\pi}{2}\right) = 0$ and $\sin\left(\frac{3\pi}{2}\right) = -1$; this uses the unit circle values.
3. Plug these values into the rotation matrix: $\begin{bmatrix} 0 & -(-1) \\ -1 & 0 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 0 \end{bmatrix}$; this step uses arithmetic and matrix entry substitution.
4. Therefore, the standard matrix of $T$ is $\boxed{\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 0 \end{bmatrix}}$.
