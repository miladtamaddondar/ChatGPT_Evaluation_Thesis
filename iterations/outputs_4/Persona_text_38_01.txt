1. The basis $\mathcal{B}$ is given as $\{1,\, 2t,\, -2 + 4t^2,\, -12t + 8t^3\}$, and we are to find the coordinate vector $[p]_{\mathcal{B}}$ for $p(t) = 7 - 12t - 8t^2 + 12t^3$.
2. Express $p(t)$ as a linear combination of the basis vectors: $p(t) = a_1 \cdot 1 + a_2 \cdot 2t + a_3 \cdot (-2 + 4t^2) + a_4 \cdot (-12t + 8t^3)$.
3. Expand the right side: $a_1 \cdot 1 + a_2 \cdot 2t + a_3 \cdot (-2) + a_3 \cdot 4t^2 + a_4 \cdot (-12t) + a_4 \cdot 8t^3$.
4. Group like terms: $(a_1 - 2a_3) + (2a_2 - 12a_4)t + 4a_3 t^2 + 8a_4 t^3$.
5. Set up equations by matching coefficients with $p(t)$: $a_1 - 2a_3 = 7$, $2a_2 - 12a_4 = -12$, $4a_3 = -8$, $8a_4 = 12$.
6. Solve $4a_3 = -8$ to get $a_3 = -2$.
7. Solve $8a_4 = 12$ to get $a_4 = \frac{3}{2}$
8. Substitute $a_3 = -2$ into $a_1 - 2a_3 = 7$ to get $a_1 - 2(-2) = 7 \implies a_1 + 4 = 7 \implies a_1 = 3$.
9. Substitute $a_4 = \frac{3}{2}$ into $2a_2 - 12a_4 = -12$ to get $2a_2 - 12 \cdot \frac{3}{2} = -12 \implies 2a_2 - 18 = -12 \implies 2a_2 = 6 \implies a_2 = 3$.
10. Therefore, the coordinate vector of $p$ relative to $\mathcal{B}$ is $[p]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 3 \\ -2 \\ \frac{3}{2} \end{bmatrix}$.
