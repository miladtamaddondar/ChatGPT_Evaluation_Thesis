To find $\mathbf{x}$ such that $A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{b}$, we set up the equation $\begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 & -2 \\ -2 & 1 & 6 \\ 3 & -2 & -5\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}-1 \\ 7 \\ -3\end{bmatrix}$, which is a system of three linear equations in three variables.
2. To determine if the solution is unique, we check if $A$ is invertible by computing its determinant: $\det(A) = 1\begin{vmatrix}1 & 6 \\ -2 & -5\end{vmatrix} - 0\begin{vmatrix}-2 & 6 \\ 3 & -5\end{vmatrix} + (-2)\begin{vmatrix}-2 & 1 \\ 3 & -2\end{vmatrix}$.
3. Calculating minors: $\begin{vmatrix}1 & 6 \\ -2 & -5\end{vmatrix} = (1)(-5) - (6)(-2) = -5 + 12 = 7$ and $\begin{vmatrix}-2 & 1 \\ 3 & -2\end{vmatrix} = (-2)(-2) - (1)(3) = 4 - 3 = 1$.
4. Substitute minors into the determinant: $\det(A) = 1 \cdot 7 + 0 \cdot (\cdots) + (-2) \cdot 1 = 7 - 2 = 5$.
5. Since $\det(A) = 5 \neq 0$, $A$ is invertible, so the solution $\mathbf{x}$ is unique by the invertible matrix theorem.
6. To find $\mathbf{x}$, use $\mathbf{x} = A^{-1}\mathbf{b}$; since $A$ is $3 \times 3$, we can solve the system directly using row reduction or Cramer's rule.
7. Write the augmented matrix: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c}1 & 0 & -2 & -1 \\ -2 & 1 & 6 & 7 \\ 3 & -2 & -5 & -3\end{array}\right]$.
8. Use row 1 to eliminate $x_1$ from rows 2 and 3: Row 2 $\to$ Row 2 + 2(Row 1): $(-2+2\cdot1, 1+2\cdot0, 6+2\cdot(-2), 7+2\cdot(-1)) = (0, 1, 2, 5)$.
9. Row 3 $\to$ Row 3 - 3(Row 1): $(3-3\cdot1, -2-3\cdot0, -5-3\cdot(-2), -3-3\cdot(-1)) = (0, -2, 1, 0)$.
10. The matrix is now $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c}1 & 0 & -2 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & 5 \\ 0 & -2 & 1 & 0\end{array}\right]$.
11. Use row 2 to eliminate $x_2$ from row 3: Row 3 $\to$ Row 3 + 2(Row 2): $(0+2\cdot0, -2+2\cdot1, 1+2\cdot2, 0+2\cdot5) = (0, 0, 5, 10)$.
12. The matrix is now $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c}1 & 0 & -2 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 & 10\end{array}\right]$.
13. Solve for $x_3$ from row 3: $5x_3 = 10 \implies x_3 = 2$.
14. Substitute $x_3 = 2$ into row 2: $x_2 + 2x_3 = 5 \implies x_2 + 4 = 5 \implies x_2 = 1$.
15. Substitute $x_2 = 1$, $x_3 = 2$ into row 1: $x_1 - 2x_3 = -1 \implies x_1 - 4 = -1 \implies x_1 = 3$.
16. Thus, the unique solution is $\boxed{\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix}3 \\ 1 \\ 2\end{bmatrix}}$.
