1. Write the vectors as columns of a matrix: \[A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ -3 & 9 & -1 & 5 \\ 2 & -6 & 4 & -3 \\ -4 & 12 & 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix}\].  
2. Use row reduction to find the rank and a basis: Subtract \(-3\) times column 1 from column 2, \(2\) times column 1 from column 3, and \(-4\) times column 1 from column 4 to see if any columns become linearly dependent.  
3. Compute: Column 2: \([-3,9,-6,12] - (-3)\cdot[1,-3,2,-4] = [0,0,0,0]\); Column 3: \([2,-1,4,2] - 2\cdot[1,-3,2,-4] = [0,5,0,10]\); Column 4: \([-4,5,-3,7] - (-4)\cdot[1,-3,2,-4] = [0,-7,5,-9]\).  
4. The new matrix is \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ -3 & 0 & 5 & -7 \\ 2 & 0 & 0 & 5 \\ -4 & 0 & 10 & -9 \end{bmatrix}\), so column 2 is zero and can be discarded; check if columns 1, 3, and 4 are linearly independent.  
5. Set up the equation \(a\mathbf{v}_1 + b\mathbf{v}_3' + c\mathbf{v}_4' = 0\), where \(\mathbf{v}_1 = [1,-3,2,-4]\), \(\mathbf{v}_3' = [0,5,0,10]\), \(\mathbf{v}_4' = [0,-7,5,-9]\).  
6. The first entry gives \(a = 0\), so consider \(b\mathbf{v}_3' + c\mathbf{v}_4' = 0\): \(5b - 7c = 0\), \(0b + 5c = 0\), \(10b - 9c = 0\).  
7. From \(5c = 0\), \(c = 0\); then \(5b = 0\) so \(b = 0\); thus, columns 1, 3, and 4 are linearly independent.  
8. Therefore, a basis for the subspace is \(\left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 5 \\ 0 \\ 10 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ -7 \\ 5 \\ -9 \end{bmatrix} \right\}\).  
9. The dimension of the subspace is \(3\).
