1. Identify the pivot columns in the echelon form: The pivots are in columns 1, 3, and 4.  
2. The basis for Col \(A\) is given by the original columns of \(A\) corresponding to the pivot columns: \(\left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} -4 \\ 5 \\ -3 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \right\}\).  
3. The dimension of Col \(A\) (the rank of \(A\)) is the number of pivot columns: \(3\).  
4. To find a basis for Nul \(A\), set up the equation \(A\mathbf{x} = 0\) and write the general solution using the free variable (column 2, since column 2 is not a pivot column): Let \(\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \\ x_4 \end{bmatrix}\), where \(x_2\) is free.  
5. Express the leading variables in terms of the free variable using the row-echelon form: From row 3, \(5x_4 = 0 \implies x_4 = 0\); from row 2, \(5x_3 - 7x_4 = 0 \implies x_3 = 0\); from row 1, \(x_1 - 3x_2 + 2x_3 - 4x_4 = 0 \implies x_1 = 3x_2\).  
6. The general solution is \(\mathbf{x} = x_2 \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}\), so the basis for Nul \(A\) is \(\left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \right\}\).  
7. The dimension of Nul \(A\) is the number of free variables, which is \(1\).
