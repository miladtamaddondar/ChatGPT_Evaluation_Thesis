Write \( T(\mathbf{x}) \) as a matrix-vector product: \( T(\mathbf{x}) = x_1 \begin{bmatrix} -2 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix} + x_2 \begin{bmatrix} 7 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -2x_1 + 7x_2 \\ 5x_1 - 3x_2 \end{bmatrix} \); this follows from the distributive property of matrix addition and scalar multiplication. 
3. Express this as \( T(\mathbf{x}) = \begin{bmatrix} -2 & 7 \\ 5 & -3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} \); verify by multiplying the matrix by the vector to get the same result as in step 2.

Therefore, the matrix \( A \) such that \( T(\mathbf{x}) = A\mathbf{x} \) for all \( \mathbf{x} \) is \( \boxed{\begin{bmatrix} -2 & 7 \\ 5 & -3 \end{bmatrix}} \); this is correct because the columns of \( A \) are \( \mathbf{v}_1 \) and \( \mathbf{v}_2 \), matching the definition of the transformation.
