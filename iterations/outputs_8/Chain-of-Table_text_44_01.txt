1. Let the columns of the matrix be \( \mathbf{a}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \\ -1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \), \( \mathbf{a}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \\ 4 \\ -4 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \), \( \mathbf{a}_3 = \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ -4 \\ -3 \\ 7 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \). Begin Gram-Schmidt: Set \( \mathbf{u}_1 = \mathbf{a}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \\ -1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \).
2. Compute projection of \( \mathbf{a}_2 \) onto \( \mathbf{u}_1 \): \( \operatorname{proj}_{\mathbf{u}_1} \mathbf{a}_2 = \frac{\mathbf{a}_2 \cdot \mathbf{u}_1}{\mathbf{u}_1 \cdot \mathbf{u}_1} \mathbf{u}_1 = \frac{2(1) + 1(-1) + 4(-1) + (-4)(1) + 2(1)}{1^2 + (-1)^2 + (-1)^2 + 1^2 + 1^2} \mathbf{u}_1 = \frac{2 - 1 - 4 - 4 + 2}{5} \mathbf{u}_1 = \frac{-5}{5} \mathbf{u}_1 = -1 \mathbf{u}_1 \).
3. Subtract projection to get \( \mathbf{u}_2 \): \( \mathbf{u}_2 = \mathbf{a}_2 - \operatorname{proj}_{\mathbf{u}_1} \mathbf{a}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \\ 4 \\ -4 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} - (-1) \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \\ -1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2+1 \\ 1+1 \\ 4+1 \\ -4-1 \\ 2+1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 2 \\ 5 \\ -5 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} \).
4. Compute projection of \( \mathbf{a}_3 \) onto \( \mathbf{u}_1 \): \( \operatorname{proj}_{\mathbf{u}_1} \mathbf{a}_3 = \frac{\mathbf{a}_3 \cdot \mathbf{u}_1}{\mathbf{u}_1 \cdot \mathbf{u}_1} \mathbf{u}_1 = \frac{5(1) + (-4)(-1) + (-3)(-1) + 7(1) + 1(1)}{5} \mathbf{u}_1 = \frac{5+4+3+7+1}{5} \mathbf{u}_1 = \frac{20}{5} \mathbf{u}_1 = 4 \mathbf{u}_1 \).
5. Compute projection of \( \mathbf{a}_3 \) onto \( \mathbf{u}_2 \): \( \operatorname{proj}_{\mathbf{u}_2} \mathbf{a}_3 = \frac{\mathbf{a}_3 \cdot \mathbf{u}_2}{\mathbf{u}_2 \cdot \mathbf{u}_2} \mathbf{u}_2 \). Compute numerator: \( 5(3) + (-4)(2) + (-3)(5) + 7(-5) + 1(3) = 15 - 8 - 15 - 35 + 3 = -40 \). Compute denominator: \( 3^2 + 2^2 + 5^2 + (-5)^2 + 3^2 = 9 + 4 + 25 + 25 + 9 = 72 \). So \( \operatorname{proj}_{\mathbf{u}_2} \mathbf{a}_3 = \frac{-40}{72} \mathbf{u}_2 = -\frac{5}{9} \mathbf{u}_2 \).
6. Subtract projections to get \( \mathbf{u}_3 \): \( \mathbf{u}_3 = \mathbf{a}_3 - \operatorname{proj}_{\mathbf{u}_1} \mathbf{a}_3 - \operatorname{proj}_{\mathbf{u}_2} \mathbf{a}_3 = \mathbf{a}_3 - 4\mathbf{u}_1 + \frac{5}{9}\mathbf{u}_2 \). Compute \( \mathbf{a}_3 - 4\mathbf{u}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ -4 \\ -3 \\ 7 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} - 4\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \\ -1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ 3 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} \). Now add \( \frac{5}{9} \mathbf{u}_2 = \frac{5}{9} \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 2 \\ 5 \\ -5 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{15}{9} \\ \frac{10}{9} \\ \frac{25}{9} \\ -\frac{25}{9} \\ \frac{15}{9} \end{bmatrix} \). So \( \mathbf{u}_3 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ 3 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} \frac{15}{9} \\ \frac{10}{9} \\ \frac{25}{9} \\ -\frac{25}{9} \\ \frac{15}{9} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{24}{9} \\ \frac{10}{9} \\ \frac{34}{9} \\ \frac{2}{9} \\ -\frac{12}{9} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{8}{3} \\ \frac{10}{9} \\ \frac{34}{9} \\ \frac{2}{9} \\ -\frac{4}{3} \end{bmatrix} \).
7. The orthogonal basis for the column space is \( \boxed{ \left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \\ -1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix},\ \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 2 \\ 5 \\ -5 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix},\ \begin{bmatrix} \frac{8}{3} \\ \frac{10}{9} \\ \frac{34}{9} \\ \frac{2}{9} \\ -\frac{4}{3} \end{bmatrix} \right\} } \).
