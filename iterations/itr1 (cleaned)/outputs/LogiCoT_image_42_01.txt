1. The problem asks to write \(\mathbf{y}\) as the sum of a vector in the subspace \(W = \text{span}\{\mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_2, \mathbf{u}_3\}\) and a vector orthogonal to \(W\), which is the orthogonal projection of \(\mathbf{y}\) onto \(W\) plus the orthogonal complement. 
2. To find the projection, first check if \(\{\mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_2, \mathbf{u}_3\}\) are linearly independent by forming the matrix \(U = [\mathbf{u}_1\ \mathbf{u}_2\ \mathbf{u}_3]\) and checking its rank; the matrix is \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\), and by row reduction, we see the columns are linearly independent, so \(W\) is 3-dimensional. 
3. The projection of \(\mathbf{y}\) onto \(W\) is given by \(\mathbf{y}_W = U(U^TU)^{-1}U^T\mathbf{y}\), so compute \(U^TU\) and \(U^T\mathbf{y}\): \(U^TU = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & -2 & 1 \\ -2 & 15 & -4 \\ 1 & -4 & 3 \end{bmatrix}\), \(U^T\mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 9 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}\). 
4. Solve \(U^TU\mathbf{c} = U^T\mathbf{y}\) for \(\mathbf{c}\): \(\begin{bmatrix} 3 & -2 & 1 \\ -2 & 15 & -4 \\ 1 & -4 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} c_1 \\ c_2 \\ c_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 9 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}\), which by Gaussian elimination gives \(c_1 = 2\), \(c_2 = \frac{1}{2}\), \(c_3 = 1\). 
5. The projection is then \(\mathbf{y}_W = 2\mathbf{u}_1 + \frac{1}{2}\mathbf{u}_2 + 1\mathbf{u}_3 = 2\begin{bmatrix}1\\1\\0\\1\end{bmatrix} + \frac{1}{2}\begin{bmatrix}-1\\3\\1\\-2\end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix}-1\\0\\1\\1\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}2\\2\\0\\2\end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix}-0.5\\1.5\\0.5\\-1\end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix}-1\\0\\1\\1\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0.5\\3.5\\1.5\\2\end{bmatrix}\). 
6. The orthogonal complement is \(\mathbf{y}_\perp = \mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W = \begin{bmatrix}4\\3\\3\\-1\end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix}0.5\\3.5\\1.5\\2\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}3.5\\-0.5\\1.5\\-3\end{bmatrix}\). 
7. Therefore, the answer is \(\boxed{\mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix}0.5\\3.5\\1.5\\2\end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix}3.5\\-0.5\\1.5\\-3\end{bmatrix}}\), where the first term is in \(W\) and the second is orthogonal to \(W\).
