1. Write the matrix \( A = \begin{bmatrix}2 & 1 \\ -1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \) and recall the characteristic polynomial is \( \det(A - \lambda I) \).  
2. Compute \( A - \lambda I = \begin{bmatrix}2-\lambda & 1 \\ -1 & 4-\lambda \end{bmatrix} \).  
3. Calculate the determinant: \( \det(A - \lambda I) = (2-\lambda)(4-\lambda) - (1)(-1) \).  
4. Expand: \( (2-\lambda)(4-\lambda) + 1 = 8 - 2\lambda - 4\lambda + \lambda^2 + 1 \).  
5. Simplify: \( \lambda^2 - 6\lambda + 9 \).  
6. The characteristic polynomial is \( \boxed{\lambda^2 - 6\lambda + 9} \).  
7. Set the polynomial to zero: \( \lambda^2 - 6\lambda + 9 = 0 \).  
8. Factor: \( (\lambda - 3)^2 = 0 \).  
9. The eigenvalues are \( \boxed{3} \) (with algebraic multiplicity 2).
