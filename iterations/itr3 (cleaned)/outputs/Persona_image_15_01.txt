1. The problem asks us to find an invertible matrix \( P \) and a matrix \( C \) of the form \( \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix} \) such that \( A = PCP^{-1} \) for \( A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix} \). 
2. First, find the eigenvalues of \( A \) by solving \( \det(A - \lambda I) = 0 \): \( \det\begin{pmatrix} 1-\lambda & -2 \\ 1 & 3-\lambda \end{pmatrix} = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0 \). 
3. Solve the quadratic equation \( \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0 \) to get \( \lambda = 2 \pm i \).
4. Since the eigenvalues are complex conjugates, \( C \) will be \( \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \) because for eigenvalues \( a \pm bi \), the real canonical form is \( \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix} \). 
5. Next, find an eigenvector for \( \lambda = 2 + i \): solve \( (A - (2+i)I)x = 0 \), i.e., \( \begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{pmatrix} = 0 \). 
6. From the first row: \( (-1-i)x_1 - 2x_2 = 0 \implies x_1 = \frac{-2}{-1-i}x_2 = \frac{2}{1+i}x_2 \). 
7. Rationalize denominator: \( \frac{2}{1+i} = \frac{2(1-i)}{(1+i)(1-i)} = \frac{2(1-i)}{2} = 1-i \), so \( x_1 = (1-i)x_2 \). 
8. Let \( x_2 = 1 \), so eigenvector is \( v = \begin{pmatrix} 1-i \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \). 
9. To construct \( P \), use the real and imaginary parts of \( v \): \( \text{Re}(v) = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \), \( \text{Im}(v) = \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix} \), so \( P = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \). 
10. The final answer is: \( P = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \), \( C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \), such that \( A = P C P^{-1} \).
