The given matrix is \( A = \begin{bmatrix}1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ 3 & 9 & -1 & 5 \\ 2 & -6 & 4 & -3 \\ -4 & 12 & 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix} \) and its echelon form is \( U = \begin{bmatrix}1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 & -7 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \). 2. The pivot columns in the echelon form \( U \) are columns 1, 3, and 4, so the corresponding columns of \( A \) form a basis for \( \text{Col } A \): \( \left\{ \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 3 \\ 2 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}2 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}-4 \\ 5 \\ -3 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \right\} \). 3. To find a basis for \( \text{Nul } A \), solve \( U\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0} \) where \( \mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix}x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \\ x_4 \end{bmatrix} \): from the last nonzero row, \( 5x_4 = 0 \implies x_4 = 0 \); from the second nonzero row, \( 5x_3 - 7x_4 = 0 \implies x_3 = 0 \); from the first row, \( x_1 - 3x_2 + 2x_3 - 4x_4 = 0 \implies x_1 - 3x_2 = 0 \implies x_1 = 3x_2 \); so the general solution is \( \mathbf{x} = x_2 \begin{bmatrix}3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \), thus a basis for \( \text{Nul } A \) is \( \left\{ \begin{bmatrix}3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \right\} \). 4. The dimension of \( \text{Col } A \) is 3 (number of pivot columns), and the dimension of \( \text{Nul } A \) is 1 (number of free variables).
