1. The standard basis for \( \mathbb{P}_2 \) is \( \{1, t, t^2\} \).
2. Write each basis vector in \( \mathcal{B} \) in terms of the standard basis: \( 1 - 3t^2 = 1 \cdot 1 + 0 \cdot t + (-3)t^2 \).
3. \( 2 + t - 5t^2 = 2 \cdot 1 + 1 \cdot t + (-5)t^2 \).
4. \( 1 + 2t = 1 \cdot 1 + 2 \cdot t + 0 \cdot t^2 \).
5. The change-of-coordinates matrix from \( \mathcal{B} \) to the standard basis is formed by using these as columns: \( P_{\mathcal{B} \to \text{std}} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 \\ -3 & -5 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \).
6. To write \( t^2 \) as a linear combination of the polynomials in \( \mathcal{B} \), set \( t^2 = a(1 - 3t^2) + b(2 + t - 5t^2) + c(1 + 2t) \).
7. Expand and collect like terms: \( t^2 = (a + 2b + c) + (b + 2c)t + (-3a - 5b)t^2 \).
8. Set up the system by matching coefficients: \( a + 2b + c = 0 \), \( b + 2c = 0 \), \( -3a - 5b = 1 \).
9. Solve \( b + 2c = 0 \Rightarrow b = -2c \).
10. Substitute \( b = -2c \) into \( a + 2b + c = 0 \) to get \( a + 2(-2c) + c = 0 \Rightarrow a - 4c + c = 0 \Rightarrow a = 3c \).
11. Substitute \( a = 3c \) and \( b = -2c \) into \( -3a - 5b = 1 \) to get \( -3(3c) - 5(-2c) = 1 \Rightarrow -9c + 10c = 1 \Rightarrow c = 1 \).
12. Then \( b = -2(1) = -2 \) and \( a = 3(1) = 3 \).
13. Therefore, \( t^2 = 3(1 - 3t^2) - 2(2 + t - 5t^2) + 1(1 + 2t) \).
14. The change-of-coordinates matrix is \( \boxed{\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 \\ -3 & -5 & 0 \end{bmatrix}} \) and \( t^2 = 3(1 - 3t^2) - 2(2 + t - 5t^2) + (1 + 2t) \).
