The linear transformation \( T \) is defined by \( T(\mathbf{e}_1) = \mathbf{y}_1 \) and \( T(\mathbf{e}_2) = \mathbf{y}_2 \), so the standard matrix \( A \) for \( T \) has columns \( \mathbf{y}_1 \) and \( \mathbf{y}_2 \), i.e., \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 5 & 6 \end{bmatrix} \); this follows from the definition of the matrix of a linear transformation with respect to the standard basis. 
2. To find the image of \( \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} \), compute \( A \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 5 & 6 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \cdot 5 + (-1) \cdot (-3) \\ 5 \cdot 5 + 6 \cdot (-3) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 10 + 3 \\ 25 - 18 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 13 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \); this uses the rule for matrix-vector multiplication. 
3. To find the image of \( \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} \), compute \( A \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 5 & 6 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2x_1 - x_2 \\ 5x_1 + 6x_2 \end{bmatrix} \); this also follows from the definition of matrix-vector multiplication.
