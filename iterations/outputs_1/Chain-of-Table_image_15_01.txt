1. Find the eigenvalues of \( A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix} \) by solving \( \det(A - \lambda I) = 0 \): \( \det\begin{pmatrix} 1-\lambda & -2 \\ 1 & 3-\lambda \end{pmatrix} = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0 \).
2. Solve for \( \lambda \): \( \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0 \implies \lambda = \frac{4 \pm \sqrt{16-20}}{2} = \frac{4 \pm 2i}{2} = 2 \pm i \).
3. Let \( C = \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix} \) where \( a = 2, b = 1 \), so \( C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \).
4. Find an eigenvector for \( \lambda = 2+i \) by solving \( (A - (2+i)I)\vec{v} = 0 \): \( \begin{pmatrix} 1-(2+i) & -2 \\ 1 & 3-(2+i) \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix} \).
5. Set up the system: \( (-1-i)x - 2y = 0 \), \( x + (1-i)y = 0 \).
6. From the second equation, \( x = -(1-i)y \), so let \( y = 1 \), \( x = -(1-i) \).
7. The complex eigenvector is \( \vec{v} = \begin{pmatrix} -(1-i) \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \).
8. The real and imaginary parts give two real vectors: \( \Re(\vec{v}) = \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \), \( \Im(\vec{v}) = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix} \).
9. Let \( P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \), so \( P^{-1} = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \) after calculation.
10. Therefore, \( A = P C P^{-1} \) with \( P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \) and \( C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \).
