1. Write the vectors as columns of a matrix \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & -3 & 1 \\ -3 & 2 & 1 & -8 & -6 \\ 2 & -3 & 6 & 7 & 9 \end{bmatrix}\) to analyze linear independence.  
2. Use row reduction to row echelon form to find pivot columns, which correspond to linearly independent vectors.  
3. Subtract 2 times column 1 from column 5: column 5 becomes \([2-2\cdot1, 1-2\cdot0, -6-2\cdot(-3), 9-2\cdot2] = [0, 1, 0, 5]\).  
4. Subtract column 2 from column 5: column 5 becomes \([0-0, 1-1, 0-2, 5-(-3)] = [0, 0, -2, 8]\).  
5. Add 2 times column 3 to column 5: column 5 becomes \([0+2\cdot(-3), 0+2\cdot(-4), -2+2\cdot1, 8+2\cdot6] = [-6, -8, 0, 20]\).  
6. Observe that columns 1, 2, and 3 are not scalar multiples of each other and are not linear combinations of previous columns, so check if column 4 can be written as a combination of columns 1, 2, 3.  
7. Set up the equation \(a\mathbf{v}_1 + b\mathbf{v}_2 + c\mathbf{v}_3 = \mathbf{v}_4\), or \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 \\ -3 & 2 & 1 \\ 2 & -3 & 6 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} a \\ b \\ c \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ -8 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix}\).  
8. Solve the system: Row 1: \(a - 3c = 1\), Row 2: \(b - 4c = -3\), Row 3: \(-3a + 2b + c = -8\), Row 4: \(2a - 3b + 6c = 7\).  
9. From Row 1, \(a = 1 + 3c\); from Row 2, \(b = -3 + 4c\).  
10. Substitute \(a\) and \(b\) into Row 3: \(-3(1+3c) + 2(-3+4c) + c = -8\) gives \(-3 - 9c - 6 + 8c + c = -8\) or \(-9 - 9c + 9c = -8\), which simplifies to \(-9 = -8\), a contradiction.  
11. Since there is a contradiction, \(\mathbf{v}_4\) is not a linear combination of \(\mathbf{v}_1, \mathbf{v}_2, \mathbf{v}_3\), so it is linearly independent from them.  
12. Now check if \(\mathbf{v}_5\) can be written as a combination of the first four vectors by setting up a similar system; if not, include it in the basis.  
13. Since the matrix is \(4 \times 5\), the maximum number of linearly independent vectors is 4, and steps above show the first four are independent, so the basis is \(\{\mathbf{v}_1, \mathbf{v}_2, \mathbf{v}_3, \mathbf{v}_4\}\).  
14. Final answer: A basis for the space spanned by the vectors is \(\left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ -3 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \\ 2 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} -3 \\ -4 \\ 1 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ -8 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \right\}\).
