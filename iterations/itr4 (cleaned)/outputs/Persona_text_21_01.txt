1. We are given the linear transformation $T(\mathbf{x}) = A\mathbf{x}$, where $A = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 & -2 \\ -2 & 1 & 6 \\ 3 & -2 & -5 \end{bmatrix}$ and $\mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 7 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix}$; we want to find $\mathbf{x}$ such that $A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{b}$ and determine if $\mathbf{x}$ is unique.  
2. Let $\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \end{bmatrix}$; then $A\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 & -2 \\ -2 & 1 & 6 \\ 3 & -2 & -5 \end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} x_1 - 2x_3 \\ -2x_1 + x_2 + 6x_3 \\ 3x_1 - 2x_2 - 5x_3 \end{bmatrix}$.  
3. Set up the system: $x_1 - 2x_3 = -1$, $-2x_1 + x_2 + 6x_3 = 7$, $3x_1 - 2x_2 - 5x_3 = -3$.  
4. Solve the first equation for $x_1$: $x_1 = -1 + 2x_3$.  
5. Substitute $x_1$ into the second equation: $-2(-1 + 2x_3) + x_2 + 6x_3 = 7 \implies 2 - 4x_3 + x_2 + 6x_3 = 7 \implies x_2 + 2x_3 = 5 \implies x_2 = 5 - 2x_3$.  
6. Substitute $x_1$ and $x_2$ into the third equation: $3(-1 + 2x_3) - 2(5 - 2x_3) - 5x_3 = -3 \implies -3 + 6x_3 - 10 + 4x_3 - 5x_3 = -3 \implies (6x_3 + 4x_3 - 5x_3) + (-3 - 10) = -3 \implies (5x_3) - 13 = -3 \implies 5x_3 = 10 \implies x_3 = 2$.  
7. Substitute $x_3 = 2$ into $x_1$ and $x_2$: $x_1 = -1 + 2(2) = 3$, $x_2 = 5 - 2(2) = 1$.  
8. Therefore, the solution is $\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}$.  
9. To determine uniqueness, note that $A$ is a $3 \times 3$ matrix; if $A$ is invertible, the solution is unique, so check if $\det(A) \neq 0$.  
10. Compute $\det(A) = 1\begin{vmatrix}1 & 6 \\ -2 & -5\end{vmatrix} - 0\begin{vmatrix}-2 & 6 \\ 3 & -5\end{vmatrix} + (-2)\begin{vmatrix}-2 & 1 \\ 3 & -2\end{vmatrix} = 1(1 \cdot -5 - 6 \cdot -2) + 0 + (-2)((-2)\cdot -2 - 1 \cdot 3) = 1(-5 + 12) + (-2)(4 - 3) = 1(7) + (-2)(1) = 7 - 2 = 5 \neq 0$.  
11. Since $\det(A) \neq 0$, $A$ is invertible and the solution is unique.  
12. Final answer: The unique vector $\mathbf{x}$ whose image under $T$ is $\mathbf{b}$ is $\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}$.
