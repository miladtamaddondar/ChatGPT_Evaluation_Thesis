1. Find the eigenvalues of \(A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix}\) by solving \(\det(A - \lambda I) = 0\): \(\det\begin{pmatrix} 1-\lambda & -2 \\ 1 & 3-\lambda \end{pmatrix} = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0\).  
2. Solve for \(\lambda\): \(\lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0 \implies \lambda = \frac{4 \pm \sqrt{16-20}}{2} = \frac{4 \pm 2i}{2} = 2 \pm i\).  
3. For \(\lambda = 2+i\), solve \((A - \lambda I)\vec{v} = 0\): \(A - (2+i)I = \begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix}\).  
4. Set up the system: \((-1-i)x - 2y = 0\) and \(x + (1-i)y = 0\).  
5. Solve for \(x\) in terms of \(y\): From the second equation, \(x = -(1-i)y\).  
6. Substitute \(x\) into the first equation: \((-1-i)(-(1-i)y) - 2y = (1-i+1+i)y - 2y = (2 - 2y) = 0 \implies y\) is free.  
7. Choose \(y = 1\), then \(x = -(1-i)\), so the eigenvector is \(\vec{v}_1 = \begin{pmatrix} -(1-i) \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}\).  
8. For \(\lambda = 2-i\), the eigenvector is the conjugate: \(\vec{v}_2 = \begin{pmatrix} -(1+i) \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}\).  
9. Form \(P = \begin{pmatrix} -(1-i) & -(1+i) \\ 1 & 1 \end{pmatrix}\) and \(C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}\) (since \(C\) must be of the form \(\begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix}\) with \(a=2, b=1\)).  
10. Final answer: \(P = \begin{pmatrix} -(1-i) & -(1+i) \\ 1 & 1 \end{pmatrix}\), \(C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}\), and \(A = PCP^{-1}\).
