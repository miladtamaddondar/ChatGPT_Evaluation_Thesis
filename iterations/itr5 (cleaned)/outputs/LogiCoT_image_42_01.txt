To write \(\mathbf{y}\) as the sum of a vector in \(W\) and a vector orthogonal to \(W\), we need to find the orthogonal projection of \(\mathbf{y}\) onto the subspace \(W = \text{span}\{\mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_2, \mathbf{u}_3\}\); this is valid since any vector in \(\mathbb{R}^4\) can be written as the sum of its projection onto a subspace and a vector orthogonal to that subspace.  
2. Form the matrix \(U = [\mathbf{u}_1 \ \mathbf{u}_2 \ \mathbf{u}_3]\) so \(U = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\); this step is correct because the columns of \(U\) span \(W\).  
3. The projection of \(\mathbf{y}\) onto \(W\) is given by \(\mathbf{y}_W = U(U^TU)^{-1}U^T\mathbf{y}\); this formula is valid for projecting onto the column space of \(U\).  
4. Compute \(U^T U = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 & 1 & -2 \\ -1 & 0 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 15 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 & 3 \end{bmatrix}\); this is correct by matrix multiplication.  
5. Compute \(U^T\mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 & 1 & -2 \\ -1 & 0 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 + 3 + 0 - 1 \\ -4 + 9 + 3 + 2 \\ -4 + 0 + 3 - 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 10 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix}\); this is correct by dot product calculation.  
6. Compute \((U^TU)^{-1}\); since \(U^TU\) is block diagonal, invert each block: \((U^TU)^{-1} = \begin{bmatrix} 1/3 & 0 & -1/3 \\ 0 & 1/15 & 0 \\ -1/3 & 0 & 1/3 \end{bmatrix}\); this is correct by inverting the \(2 \times 2\) and \(1 \times 1\) blocks.  
7. Compute \((U^TU)^{-1}U^T\mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 1/3 & 0 & -1/3 \\ 0 & 1/15 & 0 \\ -1/3 & 0 & 1/3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 10 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} (1/3)6 + 0 \cdot 10 + (-1/3)(-2) \\ 0 \cdot 6 + (1/15)10 + 0 \cdot (-2) \\ (-1/3)6 + 0 \cdot 10 + (1/3)(-2) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 + 2/3 \\ 2/3 \\ -2 - 2/3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 8/3 \\ 2/3 \\ -8/3 \end{bmatrix}\); this is correct by matrix multiplication.  
8. Compute \(U \begin{bmatrix} 8/3 \\ 2/3 \\ -8/3 \end{bmatrix} = (8/3)\mathbf{u}_1 + (2/3)\mathbf{u}_2 + (-8/3)\mathbf{u}_3\); this gives the projection vector in \(W\).  
9. Calculate \((8/3)\mathbf{u}_1 + (2/3)\mathbf{u}_2 + (-8/3)\mathbf{u}_3 = (8/3)\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} + (2/3)\begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix} + (-8/3)\begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 8/3 \\ 8/3 \\ 0 \\ 8/3 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} -2/3 \\ 2 \\ 2/3 \\ -4/3 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 8/3 \\ 0 \\ -8/3 \\ -8/3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} (8/3) + (-2/3) + (8/3) \\ (8/3) + 2 + 0 \\ 0 + (2/3) + (-8/3) \\ (8/3) + (-4/3) + (-8/3) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 14/3 \\ 14/3 \\ -2 \\ -4/3 \end{bmatrix}\); this is correct by vector addition.  
10. The orthogonal vector is \(\mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} 14/3 \\ 14/3 \\ -2 \\ -4/3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 - 14/3 \\ 3 - 14/3 \\ 3 - (-2) \\ -1 - (-4/3) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -2/3 \\ -5/3 \\ 5 \\ 1/3 \end{bmatrix}\); this is correct by subtraction.  
11. Final answer: \(\mathbf{y} = \underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} 14/3 \\ 14/3 \\ -2 \\ -4/3 \end{bmatrix}}_{\text{in } W} + \underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} -2/3 \\ -5/3 \\ 5 \\ 1/3 \end{bmatrix}}_{\text{orthogonal to } W}\).
