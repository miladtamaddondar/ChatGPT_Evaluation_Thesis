1. The linear transformation \( T \) is determined by its action on the basis vectors: \( T(\mathbf{e}_1) = \mathbf{y}_1 \) and \( T(\mathbf{e}_2) = \mathbf{y}_2 \), so the matrix representation \( A \) of \( T \) has columns \( \mathbf{y}_1 \) and \( \mathbf{y}_2 \), i.e., \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 5 & 6 \end{bmatrix} \).  
2. To find the image of \( \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} \), compute \( A \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 5 & 6 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \cdot 5 + (-1) \cdot (-3) \\ 5 \cdot 5 + 6 \cdot (-3) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 10 + 3 \\ 25 - 18 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 13 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \).  
3. To verify, recall that a linear transformation applied to a vector is a linear combination of its images on the basis: \( T\left(\begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix}\right) = 5T(\mathbf{e}_1) + (-3)T(\mathbf{e}_2) = 5\mathbf{y}_1 - 3\mathbf{y}_2 = 5\begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix} - 3\begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 10 \\ 25 \end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} -3 \\ 18 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 13 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \), confirming the result.  
4. For a general vector \( \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} \), the image is \( A \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 5 & 6 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2x_1 - x_2 \\ 5x_1 + 6x_2 \end{bmatrix} \).  
5. Final answers: The image of \( \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} \) is \( \begin{bmatrix} 13 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \) and the image of \( \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} \) is \( \begin{bmatrix} 2x_1 - x_2 \\ 5x_1 + 6x_2 \end{bmatrix} \).
