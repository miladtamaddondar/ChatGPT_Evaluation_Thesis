2. Write \( T(1) \) as a coordinate vector: \( [T(1)]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 5 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \).
3. Apply \( T \) to the second basis vector: \( T(t) = 3(0) + (5 \cdot 0 - 2 \cdot 1)t + (4 \cdot 1 + 0)t^2 = -2t + 4t^2 \).
4. Write \( T(t) \) as a coordinate vector: \( [T(t)]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ -2 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} \).
5. Apply \( T \) to the third basis vector: \( T(t^2) = 3(0) + (5 \cdot 0 - 2 \cdot 0)t + (4 \cdot 0 + 1)t^2 = t^2 \).
6. Write \( T(t^2) \) as a coordinate vector: \( [T(t^2)]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \).
7. The matrix representation of \( T \) relative to \( \mathcal{B} \) is \( \boxed{\begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 0 \\ 5 & -2 & 0 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 \end{bmatrix}} \).
