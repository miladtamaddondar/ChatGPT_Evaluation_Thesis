1. Let \(\mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix}\), \(\mathbf{u}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}\), \(\mathbf{u}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix}\), \(\mathbf{u}_3 = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}\); we want to write \(\mathbf{y} = \mathbf{y}_W + \mathbf{y}_{W^\perp}\) where \(\mathbf{y}_W\) is in the span of \(\{\mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_2, \mathbf{u}_3\}\) and \(\mathbf{y}_{W^\perp}\) is orthogonal to \(W\). 
2. Set \(\mathbf{y}_W = a_1\mathbf{u}_1 + a_2\mathbf{u}_2 + a_3\mathbf{u}_3\) and solve for \(a_1, a_2, a_3\) such that \(\mathbf{y} = a_1\mathbf{u}_1 + a_2\mathbf{u}_2 + a_3\mathbf{u}_3 + \mathbf{y}_{W^\perp}\) with \(\mathbf{y}_{W^\perp}\) orthogonal to each \(\mathbf{u}_i\). 
3. Set up the normal equations for projection: \(\mathbf{U} = [\mathbf{u}_1\ \mathbf{u}_2\ \mathbf{u}_3]\), so \(\mathbf{U}^T\mathbf{U}\mathbf{a} = \mathbf{U}^T\mathbf{y}\) where \(\mathbf{a} = \begin{bmatrix} a_1 \\ a_2 \\ a_3 \end{bmatrix}\). 
4. Compute \(\mathbf{U}^T\mathbf{U}\): \[\mathbf{U}^T\mathbf{U} = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 1 & 0 & 1 \\-1 & 3 & 1 & -2 \\-1 & 0 & 1 & 1\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}1 & -1 & -1 \\1 & 3 & 0 \\0 & 1 & 1 \\1 & -2 & 1\end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix}3 & 0 & 1 \\0 & 15 & 0 \\1 & 0 & 3\end{bmatrix}\]
5. Compute \(\mathbf{U}^T\mathbf{y}\):\[\mathbf{U}^T\mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 1 & 0 & 1 \\-1 & 3 & 1 & -2 \\-1 & 0 & 1 & 1\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix}4 + 3 + 0 - 1 = 6 \\-4 + 9 + 3 + 2 = 10 \\-4 + 0 + 3 - 1 = -2\end{bmatrix}\] So \(\mathbf{U}^T\mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 10 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix}\).
6. Write the system: \(\begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 15 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} a_1 \\ a_2 \\ a_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 10 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix}\).
7. From the second equation: \(15a_2 = 10 \implies a_2 = \frac{2}{3}\).
8. From the first and third equations: \(3a_1 + a_3 = 6\), \(a_1 + 3a_3 = -2\).
9. Solve these two equations: Multiply the third equation by 3: \(3a_1 + 9a_3 = -6\). Subtract the first equation: \((3a_1 + 9a_3) - (3a_1 + a_3) = -6 - 6 \implies 8a_3 = -12 \implies a_3 = -\frac{3}{2}\).
10. Substitute \(a_3\) into the first equation: \(3a_1 - \frac{3}{2} = 6 \implies 3a_1 = 6 + \frac{3}{2} = \frac{15}{2} \implies a_1 = \frac{5}{2}\).
11. Thus, \(a_1 = \frac{5}{2}\), \(a_2 = \frac{2}{3}\), \(a_3 = -\frac{3}{2}\).
12. Compute \(\mathbf{y}_W = a_1\mathbf{u}_1 + a_2\mathbf{u}_2 + a_3\mathbf{u}_3 = \frac{5}{2}\begin{bmatrix}1\\1\\0\\1\end{bmatrix} + \frac{2}{3}\begin{bmatrix}-1\\3\\1\\-2\end{bmatrix} - \frac{3}{2}\begin{bmatrix}-1\\0\\1\\1\end{bmatrix}\).
13. Calculate each component: First entry: \(\frac{5}{2}(1) + \frac{2}{3}(-1) - \frac{3}{2}(-1) = \frac{5}{2} - \frac{2}{3} + \frac{3}{2} = \frac{5+3}{2} - \frac{2}{3} = 4 - \frac{2}{3} = \frac{10}{3}\); Second entry: \(\frac{5}{2}(1) + \frac{2}{3}(3) - \frac{3}{2}(0) = \frac{5}{2} + 2 = \frac{9}{2}\); Third entry: \(\frac{5}{2}(0) + \frac{2}{3}(1) - \frac{3}{2}(1) = \frac{2}{3} - \frac{3}{2} = -\frac{5}{6}\); Fourth entry: \(\frac{5}{2}(1) + \frac{2}{3}(-2) - \frac{3}{2}(1) = \frac{5}{2} - \frac{4}{3} - \frac{3}{2} = (1) - \frac{4}{3} = -\frac{1}{3}\).
14. So \(\mathbf{y}_W = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{10}{3} \\ \frac{9}{2} \\ -\frac{5}{6} \\ -\frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix}\).
15. Compute \(\mathbf{y}_{W^\perp} = \mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} \frac{10}{3} \\ \frac{9}{2} \\ -\frac{5}{6} \\ -\frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 - \frac{10}{3} \\ 3 - \frac{9}{2} \\ 3 + \frac{5}{6} \\ -1 + \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} \\ -\frac{3}{2} \\ \frac{23}{6} \\ -\frac{2}{3} \end{bmatrix}\).
16. Final answer: \(\mathbf{y} = \mathbf{y}_W + \mathbf{y}_{W^\perp} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{10}{3} \\ \frac{9}{2} \\ -\frac{5}{6} \\ -\frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} \\ -\frac{3}{2} \\ \frac{23}{6} \\ -\frac{2}{3} \end{bmatrix}\).
