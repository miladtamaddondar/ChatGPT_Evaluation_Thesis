1. Write \(\mathbf{y}\) as a linear combination: \(\mathbf{y} = c_1 \mathbf{u}_1 + c_2 \mathbf{u}_2 + c_3 \mathbf{u}_3 + \mathbf{y}_\perp\), where \(\mathbf{y}_\perp\) is orthogonal to \(W = \text{span}\{\mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_2, \mathbf{u}_3\}\).
2. Set up the augmented matrix to solve for \(c_1, c_2, c_3\): \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 & 3 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 & -1 \end{bmatrix}\).
3. Row 2 - Row 1: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 & -1 \end{bmatrix}\).
4. Row 4 - Row 1: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 0 & -1 & 2 & -5 \end{bmatrix}\).
5. Row 3 - (1/4)Row 2: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 0 & 3/4 & 13/4 \\ 0 & -1 & 2 & -5 \end{bmatrix}\).
6. Row 4 + (1/4)Row 2: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 0 & 3/4 & 13/4 \\ 0 & 0 & 9/4 & -21/4 \end{bmatrix}\).
7. Row 4 - (9/3)Row 3: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 0 & 3/4 & 13/4 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & -34 \end{bmatrix}\).
8. Back-substitute: From row 4, system is inconsistent, so \(\mathbf{y}\) is not in the span; find least squares solution. Set up normal equations: \(A^T A \vec{c} = A^T \mathbf{y}\) where \(A = [\mathbf{u}_1\ \mathbf{u}_2\ \mathbf{u}_3]\).
9. Compute \(A^T A = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & -2 & 2 \\ -2 & 15 & -4 \\ 2 & -4 & 3 \end{bmatrix}\), \(A^T \mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 0 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}\).
10. Solve \(\begin{bmatrix} 3 & -2 & 2 \\ -2 & 15 & -4 \\ 2 & -4 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} c_1 \\ c_2 \\ c_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 0 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}\) to get \(c_1 = 2\), \(c_2 = 0\), \(c_3 = 1\).
11. The projection is \(\mathbf{y}_W = 2\mathbf{u}_1 + 0\mathbf{u}_2 + 1\mathbf{u}_3 = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 2 \\ 1 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix}\).
12. The orthogonal component is \(\mathbf{y}_\perp = \mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \\ 2 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix}\).
13. Final answer: \(\mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 2 \\ 1 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \\ 2 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix}\), where the first vector is in \(W\) and the second is orthogonal to \(W\).
