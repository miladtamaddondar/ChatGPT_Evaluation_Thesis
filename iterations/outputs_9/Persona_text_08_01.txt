1. To construct a \( 4 \times 3 \) matrix of rank 1, recall that such a matrix can be written as the outer product of two nonzero vectors.  
2. Choose a nonzero column vector \( \mathbf{u} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} \in \mathbb{R}^4 \).  
3. Choose a nonzero row vector \( \mathbf{v}^T = \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 6 & 7 \end{bmatrix} \in \mathbb{R}^{1 \times 3} \).  
4. Compute the outer product \( A = \mathbf{u} \mathbf{v}^T \), so \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 6 & 7 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 6 & 7 \\ 10 & 12 & 14 \\ 15 & 18 & 21 \\ 20 & 24 & 28 \end{bmatrix} \).  
5. The matrix \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 6 & 7 \\ 10 & 12 & 14 \\ 15 & 18 & 21 \\ 20 & 24 & 28 \end{bmatrix} \) is a \( 4 \times 3 \) matrix with rank 1.
