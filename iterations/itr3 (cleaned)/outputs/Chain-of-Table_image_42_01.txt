1. Let \(\mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix}\), \(\mathbf{u}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}\), \(\mathbf{u}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix}\), \(\mathbf{u}_3 = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}\); we want to write \(\mathbf{y} = \mathbf{y}_W + \mathbf{y}_{W^\perp}\) where \(\mathbf{y}_W \in W = \text{span}\{\mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_2, \mathbf{u}_3\}\) and \(\mathbf{y}_{W^\perp}\) is orthogonal to \(W\). 
2. Express \(\mathbf{y}_W = c_1\mathbf{u}_1 + c_2\mathbf{u}_2 + c_3\mathbf{u}_3\) and set up the equation: \(\mathbf{y} = c_1\mathbf{u}_1 + c_2\mathbf{u}_2 + c_3\mathbf{u}_3 + \mathbf{y}_{W^\perp}\). 
3. To find \(c_1, c_2, c_3\), set up the augmented matrix for the system \(\mathbf{y} = c_1\mathbf{u}_1 + c_2\mathbf{u}_2 + c_3\mathbf{u}_3\): \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 & | & 4 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 & | & 3 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & | & 3 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 & | & -1 \end{bmatrix}\). 
4. Row 1 unchanged: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 & | & 4 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 & | & 3 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & | & 3 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 & | & -1 \end{bmatrix}\). 
5. Row 2 = Row 2 - Row 1: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 & | & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & | & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & | & 3 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 & | & -1 \end{bmatrix}\). 
6. Row 4 = Row 4 - Row 1: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 & | & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & | & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & | & 3 \\ 0 & -1 & 2 & | & -5 \end{bmatrix}\). 
7. Row 3 unchanged: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 & | & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & | & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & | & 3 \\ 0 & -1 & 2 & | & -5 \end{bmatrix}\). 
8. Row 3 = Row 3, Row 2 = Row 2, Row 4 = Row 4 + Row 3: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 & | & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & | & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & | & 3 \\ 0 & 0 & 3 & | & -2 \end{bmatrix}\). 
9. Row 4 = Row 4 / 3: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 & | & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & | & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & | & 3 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & | & -2/3 \end{bmatrix}\). 
10. Row 3 = Row 3 - Row 4: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 & | & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & | & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & | & 11/3 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & | & -2/3 \end{bmatrix}\). 
11. Row 2 = Row 2 - Row 4: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 & | & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 0 & | & -1 + 2/3 = -1/3 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & | & 11/3 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & | & -2/3 \end{bmatrix}\). 
12. Row 2 = Row 2 / 4: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 & | & 4 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & | & -1/12 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & | & 11/3 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & | & -2/3 \end{bmatrix}\). 
13. Now back substitute: \(c_3 = -2/3\), \(c_2 = -1/12\), \(c_1 = 4 + 1 \cdot (-1/12) + 1 \cdot (2/3) = 4 - 1/12 + 2/3 = 4 + 8/12 - 1/12 = 4 + 7/12 = 55/12\). 
14. Thus, \(\mathbf{y}_W = \frac{55}{12}\mathbf{u}_1 - \frac{1}{12}\mathbf{u}_2 - \frac{2}{3}\mathbf{u}_3\) and \(\mathbf{y}_{W^\perp} = \mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W\).
