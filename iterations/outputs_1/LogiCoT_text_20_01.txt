1. Find the eigenvalues of \(A\) by solving \(\det(A - \lambda I) = 0\): \(\det\left(\begin{bmatrix}0.6-\lambda & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.7-\lambda\end{bmatrix}\right) = (0.6-\lambda)(0.7-\lambda) - (0.4)(0.3) = 0\).  
2. Expand and simplify: \((0.6-\lambda)(0.7-\lambda) - 0.12 = 0 \implies 0.42 - 0.6\lambda - 0.7\lambda + \lambda^2 - 0.12 = 0 \implies \lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3 = 0\).  
3. Solve the quadratic equation: \(\lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3 = 0\) gives \(\lambda = \frac{1.3 \pm \sqrt{1.69 - 1.2}}{2} = \frac{1.3 \pm \sqrt{0.49}}{2} = \frac{1.3 \pm 0.7}{2}\), so \(\lambda_1 = 1\), \(\lambda_2 = 0.3\).  
4. Verify that \(\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix}3/7 \\ 4/7\end{bmatrix}\) is an eigenvector for \(\lambda_1 = 1\): \(A\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix}0.6 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.7\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}3/7 \\ 4/7\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0.6 \cdot 3/7 + 0.3 \cdot 4/7 \\ 0.4 \cdot 3/7 + 0.7 \cdot 4/7\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}(1.8+1.2)/7 \\ (1.2+2.8)/7\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}3/7 \\ 4/7\end{bmatrix} = \mathbf{v}_1\), so it is correct.  
5. Find an eigenvector \(\mathbf{v}_2\) for \(\lambda_2 = 0.3\) by solving \((A - 0.3I)\mathbf{v}_2 = 0\): \(A - 0.3I = \begin{bmatrix}0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.4\end{bmatrix}\), so \(\begin{bmatrix}0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.4\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}x \\ y\end{bmatrix} = \mathbf{0}\).  
6. Write the system: \(0.3x + 0.3y = 0\), \(0.4x + 0.4y = 0\), which both reduce to \(x + y = 0\), so \(y = -x\).  
7. Choose \(x = 1\), so \(\mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}\); verify: \(A\mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}0.6 \cdot 1 + 0.3 \cdot (-1) \\ 0.4 \cdot 1 + 0.7 \cdot (-1)\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0.6 - 0.3 \\ 0.4 - 0.7\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0.3 \\ -0.3\end{bmatrix} = 0.3\begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}\), confirming it is correct.  
8. The set \(\{\mathbf{v}_1, \mathbf{v}_2\} = \left\{\begin{bmatrix}3/7 \\ 4/7\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}\right\}\) forms a basis for \(\mathbb{R}^2\) because the vectors are linearly independent (their determinant is \(\frac{3}{7} \cdot (-1) - \frac{4}{7} \cdot 1 = -\frac{3}{7} - \frac{4}{7} = -1 \neq 0\)).  
9. Final answer: A basis for \(\mathbb{R}^2\) consisting of \(\mathbf{v}_1\) and another eigenvector \(\mathbf{v}_2\) of \(A\) is \(\left\{\begin{bmatrix}3/7 \\ 4/7\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}\right\}\).
