1. The given matrix is \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ -3 & 9 & -1 & 5 \\ 2 & -6 & 4 & -3 \\ -4 & 12 & 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix} \) and its echelon form is \( \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 & -7 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \). 
2. The pivot columns in the echelon form are columns 1, 3, and 4, so the basis for \(\text{Col }A\) is given by the corresponding columns of \(A\): \(\left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} -4 \\ 5 \\ -3 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \right\}\). 
3. The dimension of \(\text{Col }A\) is 3 since there are 3 pivot columns. 
4. To find a basis for \(\text{Nul }A\), set up the equation \(A\vec{x} = 0\) and use the echelon form: let \(x_2 = s\) (free variable), then back-substitute from the bottom up: from row 3, \(5x_4 = 0 \implies x_4 = 0\); from row 2, \(5x_3 - 7x_4 = 0 \implies x_3 = 0\); from row 1, \(x_1 - 3x_2 = 0 \implies x_1 = 3x_2\); so the general solution is \(\vec{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 3s \\ s \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}\), so a basis for \(\text{Nul }A\) is \(\left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \right\}\). 
5. The dimension of \(\text{Nul }A\) is 1 since there is one free variable.
