2. Use row 1 to eliminate the $x_1$ term in row 3: Row 3 $\to$ Row 3 $- 2 \times$ Row 1, so $[2, -6, 6, -4, 0] - 2 \times [1, -4, 7, -5, 0] = [0, 2, -8, 6, 0]$.
3. The matrix is now $\begin{bmatrix}1 & -4 & 7 & -5 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 2 & -8 & 6 & 0\end{bmatrix}$.
4. Use row 2 to eliminate the $x_2$ term in row 3: Row 3 $\to$ Row 3 $- 2 \times$ Row 2, so $[0, 2, -8, 6, 0] - 2 \times [0, 1, -4, 3, 0] = [0, 0, 0, 0, 0]$.
5. The matrix is now in row-echelon form: $\begin{bmatrix}1 & -4 & 7 & -5 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0\end{bmatrix}$.
6. Write the system of equations: $x_1 - 4x_2 + 7x_3 - 5x_4 = 0$, $x_2 - 4x_3 + 3x_4 = 0$.
7. Express leading variables in terms of free variables $x_3 = s$, $x_4 = t$: From equation 2, $x_2 = 4s - 3t$.
8. Substitute $x_2$ into equation 1: $x_1 - 4(4s - 3t) + 7s - 5t = 0 \implies x_1 - 16s + 12t + 7s - 5t = 0 \implies x_1 = 9s - 7t$.
9. The general solution is $\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix}x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \\ x_4\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}9s - 7t \\ 4s - 3t \\ s \\ t\end{bmatrix}$ for $s, t \in \mathbb{R}$.
