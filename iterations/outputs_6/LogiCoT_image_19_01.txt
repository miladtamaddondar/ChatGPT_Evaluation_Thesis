1. The matrix \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \) and the basis \( \mathcal{B} = \{\mathbf{b}_1, \mathbf{b}_2\} \) where \( \mathbf{b}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \), \( \mathbf{b}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} \); we want to find the matrix of \( T(\mathbf{x}) = A\mathbf{x} \) in the basis \( \mathcal{B} \).
2. To find the \( \mathcal{B} \)-matrix, compute \( T(\mathbf{b}_1) = A\mathbf{b}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \); verified by direct multiplication.
3. Express \( \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \) as a linear combination of \( \mathbf{b}_1 \) and \( \mathbf{b}_2 \): set \( \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} = c_1 \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} + c_2 \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} \), so \( 2 = c_1 + 5c_2 \) and \( 2 = c_1 + 4c_2 \).
4. Subtract the second equation from the first: \( (c_1 + 5c_2) - (c_1 + 4c_2) = 2 - 2 \implies c_2 = 0 \); substitute back to get \( c_1 = 2 \).
5. Thus, \( T(\mathbf{b}_1) = 2\mathbf{b}_1 + 0\mathbf{b}_2 \), so the first column of the \( \mathcal{B} \)-matrix is \( \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \); verified by substitution.
6. Next, compute \( T(\mathbf{b}_2) = A\mathbf{b}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 9 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \); verified by \( 1 \cdot 5 + 1 \cdot 4 = 9 \), \( -1 \cdot 5 + 3 \cdot 4 = -5 + 12 = 7 \).
7. Express \( \begin{bmatrix} 9 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \) as a linear combination of \( \mathbf{b}_1 \) and \( \mathbf{b}_2 \): \( 9 = d_1 + 5d_2 \), \( 7 = d_1 + 4d_2 \).
8. Subtract the second equation from the first: \( (d_1 + 5d_2) - (d_1 + 4d_2) = 9 - 7 \implies d_2 = 2 \); substitute back to get \( d_1 = 7 - 4 \cdot 2 = -1 \).
9. Thus, \( T(\mathbf{b}_2) = -1\mathbf{b}_1 + 2\mathbf{b}_2 \), so the second column of the \( \mathcal{B} \)-matrix is \( \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \); verified by substitution.
10. Therefore, the \( \mathcal{B} \)-matrix for \( T \) is \( [T]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 0 & 2 \end{bmatrix} \).
