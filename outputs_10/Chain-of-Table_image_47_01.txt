1. Write the problem: Given \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 3 & 1 \\ -2 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( \mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ -2 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} \), find (a) the orthogonal projection of \( \mathbf{b} \) onto \(\operatorname{Col} A\) and (b) a least-squares solution of \(A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{b}\).  
2. Compute \(A^T A\): \(A^T A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 3 & 1 \\ -2 & 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1^2 + 3^2 + (-2)^2 & 1 \cdot 5 + 3 \cdot 1 + (-2) \cdot 4 \\ 5 \cdot 1 + 1 \cdot 3 + 4 \cdot (-2) & 5^2 + 1^2 + 4^2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 14 & 4 \\ 4 & 42 \end{bmatrix}\).  
3. Compute \(A^T \mathbf{b}\): \(A^T \mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ -2 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \cdot 4 + 3 \cdot (-2) + (-2) \cdot (-3) \\ 5 \cdot 4 + 1 \cdot (-2) + 4 \cdot (-3) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 - 6 + 6 \\ 20 - 2 - 12 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix}\).  
4. Set up the normal equations: \(A^T A \mathbf{x} = A^T \mathbf{b}\) becomes \(\begin{bmatrix} 14 & 4 \\ 4 & 42 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix}\).  
5. Write the augmented matrix: \(\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 14 & 4 & 4 \\ 4 & 42 & 6 \end{array}\right]\).  
6. Row 1 divided by 14: \(\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & \frac{2}{7} & \frac{2}{7} \\ 4 & 42 & 6 \end{array}\right]\).  
7. Row 2 minus 4 times Row 1: \(\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & \frac{2}{7} & \frac{2}{7} \\ 0 & 40.857 & 4.857 \end{array}\right]\) (since \(42 - 4 \cdot \frac{2}{7} = 42 - \frac{8}{7} = \frac{294-8}{7} = \frac{286}{7} = 40.857\), \(6 - 4 \cdot \frac{2}{7} = 6 - \frac{8}{7} = \frac{42-8}{7} = \frac{34}{7} = 4.857\)).  
8. Row 2 divided by 40.857: \(\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & \frac{2}{7} & \frac{2}{7} \\ 0 & 1 & 0.119 \end{array}\right]\).  
9. Row 1 minus \(\frac{2}{7}\) times Row 2: \(\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & 0 & 0.238 \\ 0 & 1 & 0.119 \end{array}\right]\) (since \(\frac{2}{7} \cdot 0.119 \approx 0.034\), so \(\frac{2}{7} - 0.034 \approx 0\) and \(\frac{2}{7} - 0.034 = 0.238\)).  
10. Thus the least-squares solution is \(\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 0.238 \\ 0.119 \end{bmatrix}\).  
11. The orthogonal projection of \(\mathbf{b}\) onto \(\operatorname{Col} A\) is \(A\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 3 & 1 \\ -2 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 0.238 \\ 0.119 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \cdot 0.238 + 5 \cdot 0.119 \\ 3 \cdot 0.238 + 1 \cdot 0.119 \\ -2 \cdot 0.238 + 4 \cdot 0.119 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0.238 + 0.595 \\ 0.714 + 0.119 \\ -0.476 + 0.476 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0.833 \\ 0.833 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}\).  
12. Final answers: (a) The orthogonal projection is \(\begin{bmatrix} 0.833 \\ 0.833 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}\), (b) the least-squares solution is \(\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 0.238 \\ 0.119 \end{bmatrix}\).
