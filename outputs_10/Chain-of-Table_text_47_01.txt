1. Write the normal equations \(A^T A \mathbf{x} = A^T \mathbf{b}\): \(A^T A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 3 & 1 \\ -2 & 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 14 & 0 \\ 0 & 42 \end{bmatrix}\), \(A^T \mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ -2 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 16 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix}\).
2. Write the augmented matrix for the normal equations: \(\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 14 & 0 & 16 \\ 0 & 42 & 6 \end{array}\right]\).
3. Solve for \(x_1\) and \(x_2\): \(x_1 = \frac{16}{14} = \frac{8}{7}\), \(x_2 = \frac{6}{42} = \frac{1}{7}\).
4. The least-squares solution is \(\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{8}{7} \\ \frac{1}{7} \end{bmatrix}\).
5. Compute the projection: \(A\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 3 & 1 \\ -2 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \frac{8}{7} \\ \frac{1}{7} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \cdot \frac{8}{7} + 5 \cdot \frac{1}{7} \\ 3 \cdot \frac{8}{7} + 1 \cdot \frac{1}{7} \\ -2 \cdot \frac{8}{7} + 4 \cdot \frac{1}{7} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{8+5}{7} \\ \frac{24+1}{7} \\ \frac{-16+4}{7} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{13}{7} \\ \frac{25}{7} \\ \frac{-12}{7} \end{bmatrix}\).
6. The orthogonal projection of \(\mathbf{b}\) onto \(\mathrm{Col}\,A\) is \(\begin{bmatrix} \frac{13}{7} \\ \frac{25}{7} \\ -\frac{12}{7} \end{bmatrix}\) and the least-squares solution is \(\begin{bmatrix} \frac{8}{7} \\ \frac{1}{7} \end{bmatrix}\).
