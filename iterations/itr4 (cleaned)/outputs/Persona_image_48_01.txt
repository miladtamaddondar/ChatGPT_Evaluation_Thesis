1. To find a nonzero vector \(\mathbf{v}\) in \(\mathbb{R}^3\) that is orthogonal to both \(\mathbf{u}_1\) and \(\mathbf{u}_2\), we use the cross product: \(\mathbf{v} = \mathbf{u}_1 \times \mathbf{u}_2\).  
2. Compute the cross product: \(\mathbf{u}_1 \times \mathbf{u}_2 = \begin{vmatrix} \mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\ 1 & 1 & -2 \\ 5 & -1 & 2 \end{vmatrix}\).  
3. Expand the determinant: \(\mathbf{v} = \mathbf{i} \begin{vmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ -1 & 2 \end{vmatrix} - \mathbf{j} \begin{vmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 5 & 2 \end{vmatrix} + \mathbf{k} \begin{vmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 5 & -1 \end{vmatrix}\).  
4. Calculate each minor: \(\begin{vmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ -1 & 2 \end{vmatrix} = (1)(2) - (-2)(-1) = 2 - 2 = 0\), \(\begin{vmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 5 & 2 \end{vmatrix} = (1)(2) - (-2)(5) = 2 + 10 = 12\), \(\begin{vmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 5 & -1 \end{vmatrix} = (1)(-1) - (1)(5) = -1 - 5 = -6\).  
5. Substitute back: \(\mathbf{v} = 0\mathbf{i} - 12\mathbf{j} - 6\mathbf{k} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ -12 \\ -6 \end{bmatrix}\).  
6. The required nonzero vector orthogonal to both \(\mathbf{u}_1\) and \(\mathbf{u}_2\) is \(\boxed{\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ -12 \\ -6 \end{bmatrix}}\).
