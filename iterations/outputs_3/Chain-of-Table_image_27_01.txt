1. The linear transformation \(T\) maps the standard basis vectors \(\mathbf{e}_1\) and \(\mathbf{e}_2\) to \(\mathbf{y}_1\) and \(\mathbf{y}_2\), so the matrix of \(T\) is \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 5 & 6 \end{bmatrix}\). 
2. To find the image of \(\begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix}\), compute \(A \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 5 & 6 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \cdot 5 + (-1) \cdot (-3) \\ 5 \cdot 5 + 6 \cdot (-3) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 10 + 3 \\ 25 - 18 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 13 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix}\). 
3. To find the image of \(\begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix}\), compute \(A \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 5 & 6 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2x_1 - x_2 \\ 5x_1 + 6x_2 \end{bmatrix}\).
