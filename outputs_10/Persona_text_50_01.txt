1. The inner product on $\mathbb{P}_3$ is defined as $\langle f, g \rangle = f(-3)g(-3) + f(-1)g(-1) + f(1)g(1) + f(3)g(3)$.

2. To find the orthogonal projection of $p_2$ onto the subspace spanned by $p_0$ and $p_1$, we use $\mathrm{proj}_{W}(p_2) = a_0 p_0 + a_1 p_1$, where $a_0 = \frac{\langle p_2, p_0 \rangle}{\langle p_0, p_0 \rangle}$ and $a_1 = \frac{\langle p_2, p_1 \rangle}{\langle p_1, p_1 \rangle}$.

3. Compute $\langle p_0, p_0 \rangle = 1^2 + 1^2 + 1^2 + 1^2 = 4$.

4. Compute $\langle p_1, p_1 \rangle = (-3)^2 + (-1)^2 + 1^2 + 3^2 = 9 + 1 + 1 + 9 = 20$.

5. Compute $\langle p_2, p_0 \rangle = p_2(-3) \cdot 1 + p_2(-1) \cdot 1 + p_2(1) \cdot 1 + p_2(3) \cdot 1 = 9 + 1 + 1 + 9 = 20$.

6. Compute $\langle p_2, p_1 \rangle = p_2(-3) \cdot (-3) + p_2(-1) \cdot (-1) + p_2(1) \cdot 1 + p_2(3) \cdot 3 = 9 \cdot (-3) + 1 \cdot (-1) + 1 \cdot 1 + 9 \cdot 3 = -27 - 1 + 1 + 27 = 0$.

7. Therefore, $a_0 = \frac{20}{4} = 5$ and $a_1 = \frac{0}{20} = 0$.

8. So, the orthogonal projection of $p_2$ onto the subspace spanned by $p_0$ and $p_1$ is $5p_0$.

9. To find a polynomial $q$ orthogonal to $p_0$ and $p_1$, let $q(t) = p_2(t) - \mathrm{proj}_{W}(p_2) = t^2 - 5$.

10. Check orthogonality: $\langle q, p_0 \rangle = \langle t^2 - 5, 1 \rangle = (9-5) + (1-5) + (1-5) + (9-5) = 4 + (-4) + (-4) + 4 = 0$.

11. Check orthogonality: $\langle q, p_1 \rangle = \langle t^2 - 5, t \rangle = (9-5)(-3) + (1-5)(-1) + (1-5)(1) + (9-5)(3) = 4(-3) + (-4)(-1) + (-4)(1) + 4(3) = -12 + 4 - 4 + 12 = 0$.

12. The vector of values of $q$ at $(-3, -1, 1, 3)$ is $(9-5, 1-5, 1-5, 9-5) = (4, -4, -4, 4)$.

13. To scale $q$ so that its vector of values is $(1, -1, -1, 1)$, divide by $4$, so $q(t) = \frac{1}{4}(t^2 - 5)$.

14. Therefore, the answers are: a. The orthogonal projection of $p_2$ onto the subspace spanned by $p_0$ and $p_1$ is $5$; b. The orthogonal basis is $\{p_0, p_1, q\}$ where $q(t) = \frac{1}{4}(t^2 - 5)$.
