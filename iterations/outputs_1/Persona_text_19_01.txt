1. The $\mathcal{B}$-matrix for $T$ is the matrix representation of $T$ with respect to the basis $\mathcal{B}$, i.e., $[T]_{\mathcal{B}}$ where columns are $[T(\mathbf{b}_1)]_{\mathcal{B}}$ and $[T(\mathbf{b}_2)]_{\mathcal{B}}$.
2. Compute $T(\mathbf{b}_1) = A\mathbf{b}_1 = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 1 \\ -1 & 3\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 1\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 2\end{bmatrix}$.
3. Find $[T(\mathbf{b}_1)]_{\mathcal{B}}$: Set $\begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 2\end{bmatrix} = c_1\begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 1\end{bmatrix} + c_2\begin{bmatrix}5 \\ 4\end{bmatrix}$, so $2 = c_1 + 5c_2$ and $2 = c_1 + 4c_2$.
4. Subtract the second equation from the first: $(c_1 + 5c_2) - (c_1 + 4c_2) = 0 \implies c_2 = 0$.
5. Substitute $c_2 = 0$ into $2 = c_1 + 5c_2$ to get $c_1 = 2$.
6. Thus, $[T(\mathbf{b}_1)]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 0\end{bmatrix}$.
7. Compute $T(\mathbf{b}_2) = A\mathbf{b}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 1 \\ -1 & 3\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}5 \\ 4\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}9 \\ 7\end{bmatrix}$.
8. Find $[T(\mathbf{b}_2)]_{\mathcal{B}}$: Set $\begin{bmatrix}9 \\ 7\end{bmatrix} = d_1\begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 1\end{bmatrix} + d_2\begin{bmatrix}5 \\ 4\end{bmatrix}$, so $9 = d_1 + 5d_2$ and $7 = d_1 + 4d_2$.
9. Subtract the second equation from the first: $(d_1 + 5d_2) - (d_1 + 4d_2) = 2 \implies d_2 = 2$.
10. Substitute $d_2 = 2$ into $9 = d_1 + 5d_2$ to get $d_1 = 9 - 10 = -1$.
11. Thus, $[T(\mathbf{b}_2)]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix}-1 \\ 2\end{bmatrix}$.
12. Therefore, the $\mathcal{B}$-matrix for $T$ is $[T]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix}2 & -1 \\ 0 & 2\end{bmatrix}$.
