1. To find a basis for the eigenspace corresponding to \(\lambda = 4\), solve \((A - 4I)\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0}\) where \(I\) is the \(4 \times 4\) identity matrix.
2. Compute \(A - 4I = \begin{bmatrix}3-4 & 0 & 2 & 0 \\ 1 & 3-4 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1-4 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 4-4\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}-1 & 0 & 2 & 0 \\ 1 & -1 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0\end{bmatrix}\).
3. Let \(\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix}x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \\ x_4\end{bmatrix}\), then the system is \(\begin{bmatrix}-1 & 0 & 2 & 0 \\ 1 & -1 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \\ x_4\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0\end{bmatrix}\).
4. Write the equations: \(-x_1 + 2x_3 = 0\), \(x_1 - x_2 + x_3 = 0\), \(x_2 - 3x_3 = 0\), and \(0 = 0\).
5. From the third equation, \(x_2 - 3x_3 = 0 \implies x_2 = 3x_3\).
6. Substitute \(x_2 = 3x_3\) into the second equation: \(x_1 - 3x_3 + x_3 = 0 \implies x_1 - 2x_3 = 0 \implies x_1 = 2x_3\).
7. Substitute \(x_1 = 2x_3\) into the first equation: \(-2x_3 + 2x_3 = 0\), which is always true, confirming consistency.
8. The variable \(x_4\) does not appear in any equation, so it is a free variable.
9. The general solution is \(\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix}2x_3 \\ 3x_3 \\ x_3 \\ x_4\end{bmatrix} = x_3\begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ 0\end{bmatrix} + x_4\begin{bmatrix}0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 1\end{bmatrix}\).
10. Therefore, a basis for the eigenspace corresponding to \(\lambda = 4\) is \(\left\{ \begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ 0\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 1\end{bmatrix} \right\}\).
