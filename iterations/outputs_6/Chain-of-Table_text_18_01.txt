1. Find eigenvalues: Solve $\det(A - \lambda I) = 0$, so $\det\begin{bmatrix}0-\lambda & 1\\-3 & 4-\lambda\end{bmatrix} = (0-\lambda)(4-\lambda) - (-3)(1) = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 3 = 0$.
2. Factor: $(\lambda-1)(\lambda-3)=0$, so eigenvalues are $\lambda_1=1$, $\lambda_2=3$.
3. Find eigenvector for $\lambda=1$: Solve $(A - I)\mathbf{v} = 0$, i.e., $\begin{bmatrix}-1 & 1\\-3 & 3\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}x\\y\end{bmatrix}=0$.
4. Row reduce: $R_2 \to R_2 + 3R_1$: $\begin{bmatrix}-1 & 1\\0 & 0\end{bmatrix}$.
5. Solution: $-x + y = 0 \implies y = x$, so eigenvector is $\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix}1\\1\end{bmatrix}$.
6. Find eigenvector for $\lambda=3$: Solve $(A - 3I)\mathbf{v} = 0$, i.e., $\begin{bmatrix}-3 & 1\\-3 & 1\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}x\\y\end{bmatrix}=0$.
7. Row reduce: $R_2 \to R_2 - R_1$: $\begin{bmatrix}-3 & 1\\0 & 0\end{bmatrix}$.
8. Solution: $-3x + y = 0 \implies y = 3x$, so eigenvector is $\mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}1\\3\end{bmatrix}$.
9. Therefore, a basis $\mathcal{B} = \left\{\begin{bmatrix}1\\1\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}1\\3\end{bmatrix}\right\}$ diagonalizes $A$, and $[T]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0\\0 & 3\end{bmatrix}$.
