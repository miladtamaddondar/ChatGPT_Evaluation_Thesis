1. To find $P$ and $C$ such that $A = PCP^{-1}$ with $C = \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix}$, first compute the eigenvalues of $A$: $\det(A - \lambda I) = \begin{vmatrix} 1-\lambda & -2 \\ 1 & 3-\lambda \end{vmatrix} = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5$.
2. Solve the characteristic equation: $\lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0 \implies \lambda = 2 \pm i$.
3. Since the eigenvalues are complex conjugates, $C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}$.
4. Find an eigenvector for $\lambda = 2 + i$: $(A - (2+i)I)\vec{v} = 0 \implies \begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix} = 0$.
5. From the first row: $(-1-i)x - 2y = 0 \implies x = -\frac{2}{-1-i}y = \frac{2}{1+i}y = (1-i)y$.
6. So an eigenvector is $\vec{v}_1 = \begin{pmatrix} 1-i \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}$.
7. The real and imaginary parts of $\vec{v}_1$ are $\begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}$ and $\begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}$.
8. Thus, set $P = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}$.
9. The required matrices are $P = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}$ and $C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}$, so $A = P C P^{-1}$.
