1. We are given \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -2 \\ -2 & 1 & 6 \\ 3 & -2 & -5 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( \mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 7 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} \), and need to solve \( A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{b} \).
2. Write the augmented matrix: \( \left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & -2 & -1 \\ -2 & 1 & 6 & 7 \\ 3 & -2 & -5 & -3 \end{array}\right] \).
3. Use row 1 to eliminate the first entry in row 2: Row2 + 2*Row1 → Row2 gives \( \left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & -2 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & 5 \\ 3 & -2 & -5 & -3 \end{array}\right] \).
4. Use row 1 to eliminate the first entry in row 3: Row3 - 3*Row1 → Row3 gives \( \left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & -2 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & 5 \\ 0 & -2 & 1 & 0 \end{array}\right] \).
5. Use row 2 to eliminate the second entry in row 3: Row3 + 2*Row2 → Row3 gives \( \left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & -2 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 & 10 \end{array}\right] \).
6. Solve for \( x_3 \) from row 3: \( 5x_3 = 10 \implies x_3 = 2 \).
7. Substitute \( x_3 = 2 \) into row 2: \( x_2 + 2(2) = 5 \implies x_2 = 1 \).
8. Substitute \( x_2 = 1 \) and \( x_3 = 2 \) into row 1: \( x_1 - 2(2) = -1 \implies x_1 = 3 \).
9. The solution is \( \mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \).
10. Since the system has a unique solution (no free variables), \( \mathbf{x} \) is unique.
