1. First, find the eigenvalues of $A$ by solving $\det(A - \lambda I) = 0$: $\det\begin{pmatrix} 1-\lambda & -2 \\ 1 & 3-\lambda \end{pmatrix} = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0$.
2. Solve the quadratic equation $\lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0$ to get $\lambda = 2 \pm i$.
3. Since the eigenvalues are complex conjugates, $C$ will have the form $\begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix}$ with $a = \operatorname{Re}(\lambda) = 2$, $b = \operatorname{Im}(\lambda) = 1$, so $C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}$.
4. Find an eigenvector for $\lambda = 2 + i$ by solving $(A - (2+i)I)\vec{v} = 0$: $A - (2+i)I = \begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix}$.
5. Set up the system: $(-1-i)x - 2y = 0$ and $x + (1-i)y = 0$; from the first equation, $x = \frac{2}{-1-i}y$.
6. Simplify $\frac{2}{-1-i}$ by multiplying numerator and denominator by $-1+i$: $\frac{2}{-1-i} = \frac{2(-1+i)}{(-1-i)(-1+i)} = \frac{2(-1+i)}{1+1} = -1+i$.
7. Thus, $x = (-1+i)y$; choose $y = 1$, so eigenvector is $\vec{v} = \begin{pmatrix} -1+i \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}$.
8. The real and imaginary parts of this eigenvector are $\vec{v}_1 = \operatorname{Re}(\vec{v}) = \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}$ and $\vec{v}_2 = \operatorname{Im}(\vec{v}) = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}$.
9. Form $P$ with columns $\vec{v}_1$ and $\vec{v}_2$: $P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}$.
10. The invertible matrix $P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}$ and $C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}$ satisfy $A = P C P^{-1}$.
