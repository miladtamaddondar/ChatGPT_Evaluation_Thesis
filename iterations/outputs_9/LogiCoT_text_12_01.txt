1. The characteristic polynomial of a matrix \(A\) is given by \(\det(A - \lambda I)\); here, \(A = \begin{bmatrix}2 & 1 \\ -1 & 4 \end{bmatrix}\), so \(A - \lambda I = \begin{bmatrix}2-\lambda & 1 \\ -1 & 4-\lambda \end{bmatrix}\).
2. Compute the determinant: \(\det(A - \lambda I) = (2-\lambda)(4-\lambda) - (1)(-1) = (2-\lambda)(4-\lambda) + 1\).
3. Expand \((2-\lambda)(4-\lambda)\): \(2 \times 4 - 2\lambda - 4\lambda + \lambda^2 = 8 - 6\lambda + \lambda^2\).
4. Add 1 to the expansion: \(8 - 6\lambda + \lambda^2 + 1 = \lambda^2 - 6\lambda + 9\).
5. The characteristic polynomial is \(\boxed{\lambda^2 - 6\lambda + 9}\).
6. To find the eigenvalues, set the characteristic polynomial to zero: \(\lambda^2 - 6\lambda + 9 = 0\).
7. Factor the quadratic: \((\lambda - 3)^2 = 0\).
8. The eigenvalues are \(\boxed{\lambda = 3}\) (with algebraic multiplicity 2).
