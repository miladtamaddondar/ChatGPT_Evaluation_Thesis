1. We are asked to find all \(\mathbf{x} \in \mathbb{R}^4\) such that \(A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0}\), where \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -4 & 7 & -5 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & 3 \\ 2 & -6 & 6 & -4 \end{bmatrix}\); this is equivalent to finding the null space of \(A\).  
2. Let \(\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \\ x_4 \end{bmatrix}\); then \(A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0}\) gives the system: \(x_1 - 4x_2 + 7x_3 - 5x_4 = 0\), \(x_2 - 4x_3 + 3x_4 = 0\), \(2x_1 - 6x_2 + 6x_3 - 4x_4 = 0\).  
3. To solve, write the augmented matrix: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -4 & 7 & -5 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & 3 & 0 \\ 2 & -6 & 6 & -4 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\).  
4. Subtract 2 times row 1 from row 3 to eliminate \(x_1\) in row 3: row 3 becomes \([0, 2, -8, 6, 0]\).  
5. Divide row 3 by 2 to simplify: row 3 becomes \([0, 1, -4, 3, 0]\).  
6. Notice that row 2 and row 3 are now identical, so subtract row 2 from row 3 to get a row of zeros: row 3 becomes \([0, 0, 0, 0, 0]\).  
7. The reduced system is now: \(x_1 - 4x_2 + 7x_3 - 5x_4 = 0\), \(x_2 - 4x_3 + 3x_4 = 0\).  
8. Express \(x_2\) in terms of \(x_3\) and \(x_4\) from the second equation: \(x_2 = 4x_3 - 3x_4\).  
9. Substitute \(x_2\) into the first equation: \(x_1 - 4(4x_3 - 3x_4) + 7x_3 - 5x_4 = 0\).  
10. Simplify: \(x_1 - 16x_3 + 12x_4 + 7x_3 - 5x_4 = 0\) which gives \(x_1 - 9x_3 + 7x_4 = 0\).  
11. Solve for \(x_1\): \(x_1 = 9x_3 - 7x_4\).  
12. Thus, the general solution is \(\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 9x_3 - 7x_4 \\ 4x_3 - 3x_4 \\ x_3 \\ x_4 \end{bmatrix}\), where \(x_3, x_4 \in \mathbb{R}\).  
13. Therefore, the null space of \(A\) is \(\left\{ x_3 \begin{bmatrix} 9 \\ 4 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} + x_4 \begin{bmatrix} -7 \\ -3 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} : x_3, x_4 \in \mathbb{R} \right\}\).
