1. The matrix \(\left[ Q r_1 \; Q r_2 \; \dots \; Q r_p \right]\) has as its columns the vectors \(Q r_1, Q r_2, \dots, Q r_p\).
2. By the properties of matrix multiplication, this is equal to \(Q\) times the matrix whose columns are \(r_1, \dots, r_p\): \(\left[ Q r_1 \; Q r_2 \; \dots \; Q r_p \right] = Q \left[ r_1 \; r_2 \; \dots \; r_p \right]\).
3. Thus, the desired product is \(\boxed{Q \left[ r_1 \; r_2 \; \dots \; r_p \right]}\).
