To construct a \( 4 \times 3 \) matrix with rank 1, recall that a matrix has rank 1 if all its rows (or columns) are scalar multiples of a single row (or column), so we can write the matrix as the outer product of a nonzero \( 4 \times 1 \) column vector and a nonzero \( 1 \times 3 \) row vector.  
2. Let \( \mathbf{u} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 0 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( \mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 & 5 & -2 \end{bmatrix} \), then the matrix \( A = \mathbf{u} \mathbf{v} \) will have rank 1.  
3. Compute \( A = \mathbf{u} \mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 0 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 & 5 & -2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \times 4 & 2 \times 5 & 2 \times (-2) \\ -1 \times 4 & -1 \times 5 & -1 \times (-2) \\ 0 \times 4 & 0 \times 5 & 0 \times (-2) \\ 3 \times 4 & 3 \times 5 & 3 \times (-2) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 8 & 10 & -4 \\ -4 & -5 & 2 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 12 & 15 & -6 \end{bmatrix} \).  
4. Therefore, a \( 4 \times 3 \) matrix with rank 1 is \( \boxed{\begin{bmatrix} 8 & 10 & -4 \\ -4 & -5 & 2 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 12 & 15 & -6 \end{bmatrix}} \).
