To find a nonzero vector \(\mathbf{v}\) in \(\mathbb{R}^3\) that is orthogonal to both \(\mathbf{u}_1\) and \(\mathbf{u}_2\), we need \(\mathbf{v}\) such that \(\mathbf{u}_1 \cdot \mathbf{v} = 0\) and \(\mathbf{u}_2 \cdot \mathbf{v} = 0\). 2. Let \(\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \\ z \end{bmatrix}\), then the orthogonality conditions give the system: \(\begin{cases} x + y - 2z = 0 \\ 5x - y + 2z = 0 \end{cases}\). 3. Write the augmented matrix: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & -2 & 0 \\ 5 & -1 & 2 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\). 4. Row 2 \(\rightarrow\) Row 2 - 5*Row 1: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & -2 & 0 \\ 0 & -6 & 12 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\). 5. Row 2 \(\rightarrow\) Row 2 / (-6): \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & -2 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -2 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\). 6. Row 1 \(\rightarrow\) Row 1 - Row 2: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -2 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\). 7. The solution is \(x = 0\), \(y - 2z = 0 \implies y = 2z\), so \(\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 2z \\ z \end{bmatrix} = z \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 2 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}\) for any \(z \neq 0\). 8. Thus, a nonzero vector orthogonal to both \(\mathbf{u}_1\) and \(\mathbf{u}_2\) is \(\boxed{\begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 2 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}}\).
