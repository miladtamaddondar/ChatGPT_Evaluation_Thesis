The $\mathcal{B}$-matrix for $T$ is the matrix representation of $T$ with respect to the basis $\mathcal{B}$, denoted $[T]_{\mathcal{B}}$, and is given by $[T]_{\mathcal{B}} = P^{-1}AP$, where $P$ is the change of basis matrix whose columns are the basis vectors $\mathbf{b}_1$ and $\mathbf{b}_2$.  
2. Construct $P$ by placing $\mathbf{b}_1$ and $\mathbf{b}_2$ as columns: $P = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix}$.  
3. Compute $P^{-1}$ using the formula for the inverse of a $2 \times 2$ matrix: $P^{-1} = \frac{1}{(1)(4)-(5)(1)}\begin{bmatrix} 4 & -5 \\ -1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} = \frac{1}{-1}\begin{bmatrix} 4 & -5 \\ -1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -4 & 5 \\ 1 & -1 \end{bmatrix}$.  
4. Compute $AP$: $A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 \end{bmatrix}$, so $AP = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1\cdot1+1\cdot1 & 1\cdot5+1\cdot4 \\ -1\cdot1+3\cdot1 & -1\cdot5+3\cdot4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 9 \\ 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix}$.  
5. Compute $[T]_{\mathcal{B}} = P^{-1}AP = \begin{bmatrix} -4 & 5 \\ 1 & -1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 9 \\ 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -4\cdot2+5\cdot2 & -4\cdot9+5\cdot7 \\ 1\cdot2+(-1)\cdot2 & 1\cdot9+(-1)\cdot7 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} (-8+10) & (-36+35) \\ (2-2) & (9-7) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 0 & 2 \end{bmatrix}$.  
6. The $\mathcal{B}$-matrix for $T$ is $\boxed{\begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 0 & 2 \end{bmatrix}}$.
