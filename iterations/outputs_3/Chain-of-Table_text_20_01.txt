1. To find another eigenvector $\mathbf{v}_2$, first find all eigenvalues of $A$: $\det(A - \lambda I) = \begin{vmatrix}0.6-\lambda & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.7-\lambda\end{vmatrix} = (0.6-\lambda)(0.7-\lambda) - (0.4)(0.3) = 0.42 - 1.3\lambda + \lambda^2 - 0.12 = \lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3 = 0$.
2. Solve the quadratic: $\lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3 = 0 \implies \lambda = \frac{1.3 \pm \sqrt{1.69 - 1.2}}{2} = \frac{1.3 \pm 0.7}{2}$, so $\lambda_1 = 1$, $\lambda_2 = 0.3$.
3. We already have $\mathbf{v}_1$ for $\lambda_1 = 1$; now find an eigenvector $\mathbf{v}_2$ for $\lambda_2 = 0.3$ by solving $(A - 0.3I)\mathbf{v}_2 = 0$.
4. Compute $A - 0.3I = \begin{bmatrix}0.6-0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.7-0.3\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.4\end{bmatrix}$.
5. Set up the augmented matrix: $\left[\begin{array}{cc|c}0.3 & 0.3 & 0 \\ 0.4 & 0.4 & 0\end{array}\right]$.
6. Row 2 minus $\frac{4}{3}$ times Row 1: $\left[\begin{array}{cc|c}0.3 & 0.3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0\end{array}\right]$.
7. The equation $0.3x + 0.3y = 0 \implies x + y = 0$, so $y = -x$.
8. Thus, an eigenvector is $\mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}$
9. Therefore, a basis for $\mathbb{R}^2$ consisting of eigenvectors of $A$ is $\left\{\begin{bmatrix}\frac{3}{7} \\ \frac{4}{7}\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}\right\}$.
