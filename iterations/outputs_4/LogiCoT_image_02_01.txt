1. To find the vector $\mathbf{x}$ in the standard basis, use the formula $\mathbf{x} = P_B [\mathbf{x}]_B$, where $P_B$ is the matrix whose columns are the basis vectors in $B$ and $[\mathbf{x}]_B$ is the coordinate vector.  
2. Write $P_B = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 1 & -1 \\ 3 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$ and $[\mathbf{x}]_B = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}$, since the first column is the first basis vector and the second column is the second basis vector.  
3. Multiply $P_B$ by $[\mathbf{x}]_B$: $\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 1 & -1 \\ 3 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} = 3\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} + 2\begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}$.  
4. Compute the result: $3\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 3 \\ 9 \end{bmatrix}$ and $2\begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ -2 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}$.  
5. Add the vectors: $\begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 3 \\ 9 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ -2 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 7 \\ 1 \\ 11 \end{bmatrix}$.  
6. Final answer: The vector $\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 7 \\ 1 \\ 11 \end{bmatrix}$.
