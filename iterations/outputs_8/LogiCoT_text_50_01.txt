1. The inner product on $\mathbb{P}_3$ is defined by $\langle f, g \rangle = f(-3)g(-3) + f(-1)g(-1) + f(1)g(1) + f(3)g(3)$, which is a valid inner product because it is linear, symmetric, and positive definite.  
2. To project $p_2$ onto $\text{Span}\{p_0, p_1\}$, we use $\mathrm{proj}_W(p_2) = a_0 p_0 + a_1 p_1$, where $a_0$ and $a_1$ are chosen so that $p_2 - (a_0 p_0 + a_1 p_1)$ is orthogonal to both $p_0$ and $p_1$.  
3. Set up the orthogonality conditions: $\langle p_2 - (a_0 p_0 + a_1 p_1), p_0 \rangle = 0$ and $\langle p_2 - (a_0 p_0 + a_1 p_1), p_1 \rangle = 0$, which gives two linear equations in $a_0$ and $a_1$.

8. Therefore, the orthogonal projection of $p_2$ onto $\text{Span}\{p_0, p_1\}$ is $5p_0 + 0p_1 = 5$.  
9. To find a polynomial $q$ orthogonal to $p_0$ and $p_1$, let $q = p_2 - \mathrm{proj}_W(p_2) = t^2 - 5$.
