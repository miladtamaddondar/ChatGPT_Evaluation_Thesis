1. To write \( A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix} \) in the form \( A = P C P^{-1} \) with \( C = \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix} \), we first find the eigenvalues of \( A \) since \( C \) must have complex eigenvalues of the form \( a \pm bi \).  
2. The characteristic polynomial of \( A \) is \( \det(A - \lambda I) = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 \).  
3. Solving \( \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0 \) gives \( \lambda = 2 \pm i \), so \( a = 2 \), \( b = 1 \).  
4. Thus, \( C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \) and we verify this matches the required form.  
5. Next, we find an eigenvector for \( \lambda = 2 + i \) by solving \( (A - (2+i)I)\vec{v} = 0 \): \( \begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix} \vec{v} = 0 \).  
6. Let \( v_1 = 1 \), then \( -1-i - 2v_2 = 0 \implies v_2 = \frac{-1-i}{2} \).  
7. The eigenvector for \( \lambda = 2+i \) is \( \vec{v}_1 = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ \frac{-1-i}{2} \end{pmatrix} \); the eigenvector for \( \lambda = 2-i \) is its complex conjugate \( \vec{v}_2 = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ \frac{-1+i}{2} \end{pmatrix} \).  
8. To form a real invertible matrix \( P \), use the real and imaginary parts of \( \vec{v}_1 \): \( \Re(\vec{v}_1) = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ -\frac{1}{2} \end{pmatrix} \), \( \Im(\vec{v}_1) = \begin{pmatrix} 0 \\ -\frac{1}{2} \end{pmatrix} \).  
9. Thus, \( P = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ -\frac{1}{2} & -\frac{1}{2} \end{pmatrix} \) and we verify \( P \) is invertible since its determinant is \( -\frac{1}{2} \neq 0 \).  
10. The final answer is: \( P = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ -\frac{1}{2} & -\frac{1}{2} \end{pmatrix} \), \( C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \), so \( A = P C P^{-1} \).
