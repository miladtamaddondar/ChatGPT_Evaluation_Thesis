1. To find a \(2 \times 2\) matrix \(B\) such that \(AB = 0\), we need two different nonzero vectors in the null space of \(A\), i.e., solve \(A\vec{x} = 0\) for two different nonzero \(\vec{x}\). 
2. Write the system: \(\begin{bmatrix} 3 & -6 \\ -1 & 2 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}\). 
3. This gives the equations: \(3x_1 - 6x_2 = 0\) and \(-x_1 + 2x_2 = 0\). 
4. Row reduce the augmented matrix: \(\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 3 & -6 & 0 \\ -1 & 2 & 0 \end{array}\right]\). 
5. Multiply Row 2 by 3 and add to Row 1: Row 1 = Row 1 + 3*Row 2 \(\rightarrow\) \(\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 0 & 0 & 0 \\ -1 & 2 & 0 \end{array}\right]\). 
6. The system reduces to \(-x_1 + 2x_2 = 0\), so \(x_1 = 2x_2\). 
7. Let \(x_2 = 1\), then \(\vec{x}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}\); let \(x_2 = -1\), then \(\vec{x}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} -2 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix}\). 
8. Form \(B\) with these as columns: \(B = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -2 \\ 1 & -1 \end{bmatrix}\). 
9. Final answer: A \(2 \times 2\) matrix \(B\) such that \(AB = 0\) and both columns are nonzero and different is \(B = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -2 \\ 1 & -1 \end{bmatrix}\).
