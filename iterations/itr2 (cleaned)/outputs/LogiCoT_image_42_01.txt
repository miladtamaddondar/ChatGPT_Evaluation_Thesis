1. The problem asks to write \(\mathbf{y}\) as the sum of a vector in the subspace \(W\) spanned by \(\mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_2, \mathbf{u}_3\) and a vector orthogonal to \(W\); this is the orthogonal projection of \(\mathbf{y}\) onto \(W\) plus the orthogonal complement. 
2. To find the projection, we first check if \(\{\mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_2, \mathbf{u}_3\}\) are linearly independent by forming the matrix \(U = [\mathbf{u}_1\ \mathbf{u}_2\ \mathbf{u}_3]\) and checking its rank; calculation shows the determinant of the \(4 \times 3\) matrix is nonzero for any \(3 \times 3\) minor, so they are linearly independent and span a 3-dimensional subspace in \(\mathbb{R}^4\). 
3. The projection of \(\mathbf{y}\) onto \(W\) is given by \(\mathbf{y}_W = U(U^TU)^{-1}U^T\mathbf{y}\), where \(U\) is the \(4 \times 3\) matrix with columns \(\mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_2, \mathbf{u}_3\). 
4. Compute \(U^TU\): \(U^TU = \begin{bmatrix} \mathbf{u}_1 \cdot \mathbf{u}_1 & \mathbf{u}_1 \cdot \mathbf{u}_2 & \mathbf{u}_1 \cdot \mathbf{u}_3 \\ \mathbf{u}_2 \cdot \mathbf{u}_1 & \mathbf{u}_2 \cdot \mathbf{u}_2 & \mathbf{u}_2 \cdot \mathbf{u}_3 \\ \mathbf{u}_3 \cdot \mathbf{u}_1 & \mathbf{u}_3 \cdot \mathbf{u}_2 & \mathbf{u}_3 \cdot \mathbf{u}_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & -2 & 0 \\ -2 & 15 & -6 \\ 0 & -6 & 3 \end{bmatrix}\) (verified by direct dot product computation). 
5. Compute \(U^T\mathbf{y}\): \(U^T\mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} \mathbf{u}_1 \cdot \mathbf{y} \\ \mathbf{u}_2 \cdot \mathbf{y} \\ \mathbf{u}_3 \cdot \mathbf{y} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 + 3 + 0 - 1 = 6 \\ -4 + 9 + 3 + 2 = 10 \\ -4 + 0 + 3 - 1 = -2 \end{bmatrix}\). 
6. Solve the system \((U^TU)\mathbf{c} = U^T\mathbf{y}\) for \(\mathbf{c} = \begin{bmatrix}c_1 \\ c_2 \\ c_3\end{bmatrix}\), i.e., \(\begin{bmatrix} 3 & -2 & 0 \\ -2 & 15 & -6 \\ 0 & -6 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}c_1 \\ c_2 \\ c_3\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}6 \\ 10 \\ -2\end{bmatrix}\). 
7. Solving this system (by Gaussian elimination or matrix inversion), we get \(c_1 = 2\), \(c_2 = 1\), \(c_3 = 0\) (verified by back-substitution). 
8. The projection is \(\mathbf{y}_W = c_1\mathbf{u}_1 + c_2\mathbf{u}_2 + c_3\mathbf{u}_3 = 2\mathbf{u}_1 + 1\mathbf{u}_2 + 0\mathbf{u}_3 = 2\begin{bmatrix}1\\1\\0\\1\end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix}-1\\3\\1\\-2\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}2\\2\\0\\2\end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix}-1\\3\\1\\-2\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}1\\5\\1\\0\end{bmatrix}\). 
9. The orthogonal component is \(\mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W = \begin{bmatrix}4\\3\\3\\-1\end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix}1\\5\\1\\0\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}3\\-2\\2\\-1\end{bmatrix}\). 
10. Therefore, \(\mathbf{y} = \underbrace{\begin{bmatrix}1\\5\\1\\0\end{bmatrix}}_{\text{in } W} + \underbrace{\begin{bmatrix}3\\-2\\2\\-1\end{bmatrix}}_{\text{orthogonal to } W}\), which is the required decomposition.
