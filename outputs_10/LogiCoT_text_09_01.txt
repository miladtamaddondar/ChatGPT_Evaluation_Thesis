1. Write the vectors as columns of a matrix: \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ -3 & 9 & -1 & 5 \\ 2 & -6 & 4 & -3 \\ -4 & 12 & 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix} \). 2. Observe that \(\mathbf{v}_2 = -3\mathbf{v}_1\), so \(\mathbf{v}_2\) is linearly dependent on \(\mathbf{v}_1\). 3. Replace \(\mathbf{v}_2\) with zeros: \(A' = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 2 & -4 \\ -3 & 0 & -1 & 5 \\ 2 & 0 & 4 & -3 \\ -4 & 0 & 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix}\). 4. To check if \(\mathbf{v}_3\) is independent of \(\mathbf{v}_1\), set \(a\mathbf{v}_1 + b\mathbf{v}_3 = 0\) and solve: \(a\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix} + b\begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}\); this gives the system \(a + 2b = 0\), \(-3a - b = 0\), \(2a + 4b = 0\), \(-4a + 2b = 0\). 5. Solve: From the first equation, \(a = -2b\); substitute into the second: \(-3(-2b) - b = 6b - b = 5b = 0 \implies b = 0\), so \(a = 0\); thus, \(\mathbf{v}_1\) and \(\mathbf{v}_3\) are linearly independent. 6. Now check if \(\mathbf{v}_4\) is in the span of \(\mathbf{v}_1\) and \(\mathbf{v}_3\): solve \(a\mathbf{v}_1 + b\mathbf{v}_3 = \mathbf{v}_4\), i.e., \(a\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix} + b\begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -4 \\ 5 \\ -3 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix}\). 7. This gives the system: \(a + 2b = -4\), \(-3a - b = 5\), \(2a + 4b = -3\), \(-4a + 2b = 7\). 8. Solve the first equation for \(a\): \(a = -4 - 2b\); substitute into the second: \(-3(-4 - 2b) - b = 5 \implies 12 + 6b - b = 5 \implies 5b = 5 - 12 = -7 \implies b = -7/5\), then \(a = -4 - 2(-7/5) = -4 + 14/5 = (-20 + 14)/5 = -6/5\). 9. Check third equation: \(2a + 4b = 2(-6/5) + 4(-7/5) = -12/5 - 28/5 = -40/5 = -8\), but the right side is \(-3\), so the equations are inconsistent; thus, \(\mathbf{v}_4\) is not in the span of \(\mathbf{v}_1\) and \(\mathbf{v}_3\). 10. Therefore, \(\{\mathbf{v}_1, \mathbf{v}_3, \mathbf{v}_4\}\) are linearly independent and form a basis for the subspace. 11. The dimension of the subspace is \(3\).
