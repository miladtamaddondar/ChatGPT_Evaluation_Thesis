2. Let \( \mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix}x \\ y\end{bmatrix} \) and solve \( A\mathbf{b} = 0 \), so \( \begin{bmatrix}3 & -6 \\ 1 & -2\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}x \\ y\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0 \\ 0\end{bmatrix} \).
3. This gives the system: \( 3x - 6y = 0 \) and \( x - 2y = 0 \).
4. Solving \( x - 2y = 0 \) gives \( x = 2y \).
5. Substitute \( x = 2y \) into the first equation: \( 3(2y) - 6y = 0 \implies 6y - 6y = 0 \), which is always true.
6. Therefore, the null space is all vectors of the form \( \begin{bmatrix}2y \\ y\end{bmatrix} \) for any \( y \).
7. Choose two different nonzero values for \( y \), say \( y = 1 \) and \( y = -1 \), to get two linearly independent columns.
