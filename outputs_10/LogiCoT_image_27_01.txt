1. The linear transformation \( T \) is determined by its action on the standard basis: \( T(\mathbf{e}_1) = \mathbf{y}_1 \) and \( T(\mathbf{e}_2) = \mathbf{y}_2 \), so the matrix representation of \( T \) is \( A = [\mathbf{y}_1 \ \mathbf{y}_2] = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 5 & 6 \end{bmatrix} \); this is correct by the definition of a linear transformation on basis vectors.  
2. To find the image of \( \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} \), compute \( T\left(\begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix}\right) = A \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} = 5\mathbf{y}_1 + (-3)\mathbf{y}_2 = 5\begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix} - 3\begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 10 \\ 25 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ -18 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 13 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \); this follows from linearity and matrix multiplication.  
3. To find the image of \( \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} \), compute \( T\left(\begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix}\right) = A \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} = x_1\mathbf{y}_1 + x_2\mathbf{y}_2 = x_1\begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix} + x_2\begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2x_1 - x_2 \\ 5x_1 + 6x_2 \end{bmatrix} \); this is correct by the distributive property of matrix multiplication.
