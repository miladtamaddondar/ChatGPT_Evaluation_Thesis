1. Find the eigenvalues of \(A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix}\) by solving \(\det(A - \lambda I) = 0\): \(\det\begin{pmatrix} 1-\lambda & -2 \\ 1 & 3-\lambda \end{pmatrix} = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0\).  
2. Solve for \(\lambda\): \(\lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0 \implies \lambda = \frac{4 \pm \sqrt{16-20}}{2} = \frac{4 \pm 2i}{2} = 2 \pm i\).  
3. Since the eigenvalues are complex conjugates, the canonical form \(C\) is \(\begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix}\) with \(a = 2\), \(b = 1\), so \(C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}\).  
4. Find an eigenvector for \(\lambda = 2 + i\) by solving \((A - (2+i)I)\vec{v} = 0\): \(A - (2+i)I = \begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix}\).  
5. Let \(\vec{v} = \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix}\), then \((-1-i)x - 2y = 0\) and \(x + (1-i)y = 0\); solve the first equation for \(x\): \(x = \frac{-2y}{-1-i} = \frac{2y}{1+i}\).  
6. Rationalize denominator: \(\frac{2}{1+i} = \frac{2(1-i)}{(1+i)(1-i)} = \frac{2(1-i)}{2} = 1-i\), so \(x = (1-i)y\).  
7. Take \(y = 1\), so eigenvector is \(\vec{v} = \begin{pmatrix} 1-i \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}\); its real and imaginary parts are \(\vec{v}_1 = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}\), \(\vec{v}_2 = \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}\).  
8. Form \(P\) with columns \(\vec{v}_1, \vec{v}_2\): \(P = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}\); verify \(\det P = (1)(0) - (1)(-1) = 1\), so \(P\) is invertible.  
9. Compute \(P^{-1}\): for \(P = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}\), \(P^{-1} = \frac{1}{1}\begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 1 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 1 \end{pmatrix}\).  
10. Verify \(A = P C P^{-1}\): \(C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}\), so \(P C = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 3 & -1 \\ 2 & 1 \end{pmatrix}\), then \(P C P^{-1} = \begin{pmatrix} 3 & -1 \\ 2 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 1 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix} = A\).  
11. Final answer: \(P = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}\), \(C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}\), and \(A = P C P^{-1}\).
