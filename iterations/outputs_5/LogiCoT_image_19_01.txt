1. To find the matrix of \( T \) in the basis \( \mathcal{B} = \{\mathbf{b}_1, \mathbf{b}_2\} \), we need to compute \( [T]_{\mathcal{B}} \) whose columns are the coordinates of \( T(\mathbf{b}_1) \) and \( T(\mathbf{b}_2) \) in the basis \( \mathcal{B} \).  
2. Compute \( T(\mathbf{b}_1) = A\mathbf{b}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \); verified by matrix multiplication.  
3. Compute \( T(\mathbf{b}_2) = A\mathbf{b}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 9 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \); verified by matrix multiplication.  
4. Express \( \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \) as a linear combination of \( \mathbf{b}_1 \) and \( \mathbf{b}_2 \): set \( a\mathbf{b}_1 + b\mathbf{b}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \), i.e., \( a\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} + b\begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \), so \( a + 5b = 2 \), \( a + 4b = 2 \).  
5. Subtract the second equation from the first: \( (a + 5b) - (a + 4b) = 2 - 2 \implies b = 0 \); then \( a = 2 \), so \( [T(\mathbf{b}_1)]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \); verified by substitution.  
6. Express \( \begin{bmatrix} 9 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \) as a linear combination of \( \mathbf{b}_1 \) and \( \mathbf{b}_2 \): set \( a\mathbf{b}_1 + b\mathbf{b}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 9 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \), so \( a + 5b = 9 \), \( a + 4b = 7 \).  
7. Subtract the second equation from the first: \( (a + 5b) - (a + 4b) = 9 - 7 \implies b = 2 \); then \( a = 7 - 4b = 7 - 8 = -1 \), so \( [T(\mathbf{b}_2)]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \); verified by substitution.  
8. The matrix of \( T \) in the basis \( \mathcal{B} \) is \( [T]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 0 & 2 \end{bmatrix} \); this is constructed by placing the coordinate vectors from steps 5 and 7 as columns.
