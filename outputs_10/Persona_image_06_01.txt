1. We are given \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & -6 \\ -1 & 2 \end{bmatrix} \) and need to find a \( 2 \times 2 \) matrix \( B \) such that \( AB = 0 \) and the columns of \( B \) are nonzero and different.  
2. Let \( B = \begin{bmatrix} \mathbf{b}_1 & \mathbf{b}_2 \end{bmatrix} \), where each \( \mathbf{b}_i \) is a column vector in \( \mathbb{R}^2 \), and \( AB = \begin{bmatrix} A\mathbf{b}_1 & A\mathbf{b}_2 \end{bmatrix} = 0 \) means each \( \mathbf{b}_i \) is in the null space of \( A \).  
3. To find the null space, solve \( A\mathbf{x} = 0 \) where \( \mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} \) so \( \begin{bmatrix} 3 & -6 \\ -1 & 2 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3x_1 - 6x_2 \\ -x_1 + 2x_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \).  
4. The system is \( 3x_1 - 6x_2 = 0 \) and \( -x_1 + 2x_2 = 0 \); both equations are equivalent, so \( x_1 = 2x_2 \).  
5. The null space consists of all scalar multiples of \( \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \), so any nonzero column of \( B \) must be a nonzero multiple of \( \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \).  
6. To use two different nonzero columns, choose two different nonzero scalars, say 1 and -3, so \( \mathbf{b}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( \mathbf{b}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} -6 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} \).  
7. Therefore, a valid matrix is \( B = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -6 \\ 1 & -3 \end{bmatrix} \).
