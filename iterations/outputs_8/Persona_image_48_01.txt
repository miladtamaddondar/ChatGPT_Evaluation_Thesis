1. To find a nonzero vector \(\mathbf{v}\) orthogonal to both \(\mathbf{u}_1\) and \(\mathbf{u}_2\), we compute their cross product: \(\mathbf{v} = \mathbf{u}_1 \times \mathbf{u}_2\).
2. Set up the cross product using the determinant formula: \(\mathbf{v} = \begin{vmatrix} \mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\ 1 & 1 & -2 \\ 5 & -1 & 2 \end{vmatrix}\).
3. Compute the \(\mathbf{i}\) component: \( (1)(2) - (-2)(-1) = 2 - 2 = 0 \).
4. Compute the \(\mathbf{j}\) component (with a negative sign): \(-\left[(1)(2) - (-2)(5)\right] = -[2 + 10] = -12\).
5. Compute the \(\mathbf{k}\) component: \( (1)(-1) - (1)(5) = -1 - 5 = -6 \).
6. Combine the components to get \(\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ -12 \\ -6 \end{bmatrix}\).
7. The required nonzero vector orthogonal to both \(\mathbf{u}_1\) and \(\mathbf{u}_2\) is \(\boxed{\begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ -12 \\ -6 \end{bmatrix}}\).
