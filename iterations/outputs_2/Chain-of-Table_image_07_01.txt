1. Observe that \( Qr_1, Qr_2, \ldots, Qr_p \) are each the result of multiplying the matrix \( Q \) (of size \( m \times n \)) by the vectors \( r_1, r_2, \ldots, r_p \) (each of size \( n \times 1 \)). 
2. Note that the matrix \( [Qr_1 \ Qr_2 \ \ldots \ Qr_p] \) is formed by concatenating these column vectors, resulting in an \( m \times p \) matrix. 
3. Recognize that \( [Qr_1 \ Qr_2 \ \ldots \ Qr_p] = Q[r_1 \ r_2 \ \ldots \ r_p] \), where \( [r_1 \ r_2 \ \ldots \ r_p] \) is an \( n \times p \) matrix whose columns are the vectors \( r_1, r_2, \ldots, r_p \). 
4. Therefore, the answer is: \( [Qr_1 \ Qr_2 \ \ldots \ Qr_p] = Q \begin{bmatrix} r_1 & r_2 & \cdots & r_p \end{bmatrix} \).
