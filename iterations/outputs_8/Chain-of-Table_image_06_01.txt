1. Let \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & -6 \\ -1 & 2 \end{bmatrix} \). To find a \( 2 \times 2 \) matrix \( B \) such that \( AB = 0 \), each column of \( B \) must be in the null space of \( A \). Solve \( A\mathbf{x} = 0 \) for a general vector \( \mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} \): \(\begin{bmatrix} 3 & -6 \\ -1 & 2 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}\).
2. Write the augmented matrix: \(\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 3 & -6 & 0 \\ -1 & 2 & 0 \end{array}\right]\).

4. Divide Row 1 by 3: \(\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & -2 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{array}\right]\).
5. The solution is \( x_1 - 2x_2 = 0 \implies x_1 = 2x_2 \), so the general solution is \( \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} = x_2 \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \).
6. Choose two different nonzero values for \( x_2 \) to form two linearly dependent but different columns: let \( x_2 = 1 \) and \( x_2 = -1 \), so columns are \( \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( \begin{bmatrix} -2 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} \).
7. The required matrix is \( B = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -2 \\ 1 & -1 \end{bmatrix} \).
