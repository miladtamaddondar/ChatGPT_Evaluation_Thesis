1. The matrix \( A \) is given as \( \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ -3 & 9 & -1 & 5 \\ 2 & -6 & 4 & -3 \\ -4 & 12 & 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix} \).
2. The echelon form of \( A \) is \( \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 & -7 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \).
3. The pivot columns in the echelon form are columns 1, 3, and 4, so the basis for \( \text{Col } A \) is the set of columns 1, 3, and 4 of \( A \): \( \left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} -4 \\ 5 \\ -3 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \right\} \).
4. The number of pivot columns is 3, so \( \dim(\text{Col } A) = 3 \).
5. To find a basis for \( \text{Nul } A \), write the general solution to \( A\mathbf{x} = 0 \) using the free variable \( x_2 \) (since column 2 is not a pivot column).
6. Let \( \mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \\ x_4 \end{bmatrix} \), and from the echelon form, back-substitute to express all variables in terms of \( x_2 \).
7. From row 3: \( 5x_4 = 0 \implies x_4 = 0 \).
8. From row 2: \( 5x_3 - 7x_4 = 0 \implies x_3 = 0 \).
9. From row 1: \( x_1 - 3x_2 + 2x_3 - 4x_4 = 0 \implies x_1 = 3x_2 \).
10. Thus, the general solution is \( \mathbf{x} = x_2 \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \), so a basis for \( \text{Nul } A \) is \( \left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \right\} \).
11. The number of free variables is 1, so \( \dim(\text{Nul } A) = 1 \).
