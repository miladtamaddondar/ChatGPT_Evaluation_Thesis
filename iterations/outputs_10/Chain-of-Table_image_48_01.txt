To find a nonzero vector \(\mathbf{v}\) orthogonal to both \(\mathbf{u}_1\) and \(\mathbf{u}_2\), set up the system: \(\mathbf{v} \cdot \mathbf{u}_1 = 0\) and \(\mathbf{v} \cdot \mathbf{u}_2 = 0\), where \(\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \\ z \end{bmatrix}\). This gives the equations: \(x + y - 2z = 0\) and \(5x - y + 2z = 0\).
2. Write the augmented matrix: \(\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 1 & -2 & 0 \\ 5 & -1 & 2 & 0 \end{array}\right]\).
3. Row 2 minus 5 times Row 1: \(R_2 \to R_2 - 5R_1\): \(\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 1 & -2 & 0 \\ 0 & -6 & 12 & 0 \end{array}\right]\).
4. Row 2 divided by -6: \(R_2 \to \frac{1}{-6}R_2\): \(\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 1 & -2 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -2 & 0 \end{array}\right]\).
5. Row 1 minus Row 2: \(R_1 \to R_1 - R_2\): \(\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -2 & 0 \end{array}\right]\).
6. The solution is \(x = 0\), \(y = 2z\), \(z = z\), so a nonzero solution is \(\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 2 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}\).
