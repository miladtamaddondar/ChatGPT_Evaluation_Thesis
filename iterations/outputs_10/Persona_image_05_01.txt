To find values of \( k \) such that \( AB = BA \), first compute \( AB \): \( AB = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 5 \\ -3 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 & -5 \\ 3 & k \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \cdot 4 + 5 \cdot 3 & 2 \cdot (-5) + 5 \cdot k \\ -3 \cdot 4 + 1 \cdot 3 & -3 \cdot (-5) + 1 \cdot k \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 8 + 15 & -10 + 5k \\ -12 + 3 & 15 + k \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 23 & -10 + 5k \\ -9 & 15 + k \end{bmatrix} \).
2. Next, compute \( BA \): \( BA = \begin{bmatrix} 4 & -5 \\ 3 & k \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 5 \\ -3 & 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \cdot 2 + (-5) \cdot (-3) & 4 \cdot 5 + (-5) \cdot 1 \\ 3 \cdot 2 + k \cdot (-3) & 3 \cdot 5 + k \cdot 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 8 + 15 & 20 - 5 \\ 6 - 3k & 15 + k \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 23 & 15 \\ 6 - 3k & 15 + k \end{bmatrix} \).
3. Set \( AB = BA \) and equate corresponding entries: \( \begin{bmatrix} 23 & -10 + 5k \\ -9 & 15 + k \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 23 & 15 \\ 6 - 3k & 15 + k \end{bmatrix} \).
4. The (1,2) entry gives \( -10 + 5k = 15 \), so \( 5k = 25 \) and \( k = 5 \).
5. The (2,1) entry gives \( -9 = 6 - 3k \), so \( -9 - 6 = -3k \) and \( -15 = -3k \), so \( k = 5 \).
6. The (2,2) entry gives \( 15 + k = 15 + k \), which is always true for any \( k \).
7. The (1,1) entry gives \( 23 = 23 \), which is always true.
8. Therefore, the only value of \( k \) that makes \( AB = BA \) is \( \boxed{5} \).
