1. Let \( \mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} \), \( \mathbf{u}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \), \( \mathbf{u}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix} \), \( \mathbf{u}_3 = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \); write \( \mathbf{y} = \mathbf{y}_W + \mathbf{y}_{W^\perp} \) where \( \mathbf{y}_W \) is in the span of \( \mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_2, \mathbf{u}_3 \) and \( \mathbf{y}_{W^\perp} \) is orthogonal to \( W \).
2. Set up the equation \( \mathbf{y}_W = c_1 \mathbf{u}_1 + c_2 \mathbf{u}_2 + c_3 \mathbf{u}_3 \), so \( \mathbf{y} = c_1 \mathbf{u}_1 + c_2 \mathbf{u}_2 + c_3 \mathbf{u}_3 + \mathbf{y}_{W^\perp} \).
3. To find \( c_1, c_2, c_3 \), solve \( \mathbf{y} = c_1 \mathbf{u}_1 + c_2 \mathbf{u}_2 + c_3 \mathbf{u}_3 \), or \( \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} c_1 \\ c_2 \\ c_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} \).
4. Write the augmented matrix: \( \left[ \begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 & 3 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 & -1 \end{array} \right] \).
5. Row 2 minus Row 1: \( R_2 \leftarrow R_2 - R_1 \): \( \left[ \begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 & -1 \end{array} \right] \).
6. Row 4 minus Row 1: \( R_4 \leftarrow R_4 - R_1 \): \( \left[ \begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 0 & -1 & 2 & -5 \end{array} \right] \).
7. Row 3 unchanged, now Row 3 minus (1/4) Row 2: \( R_3 \leftarrow R_3 - \frac{1}{4}R_2 \): \( \left[ \begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 0 & \frac{3}{4} & \frac{13}{4} \\ 0 & -1 & 2 & -5 \end{array} \right] \).
8. Row 4 plus Row 2/4: \( R_4 \leftarrow R_4 + \frac{1}{4}R_2 \): \( \left[ \begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 0 & \frac{3}{4} & \frac{13}{4} \\ 0 & 0 & \frac{9}{4} & -\frac{21}{4} \end{array} \right] \).
9. Row 4 minus 3 times Row 3: \( R_4 \leftarrow R_4 - 3R_3 \): \( \left[ \begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 0 & \frac{3}{4} & \frac{13}{4} \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & -11 \end{array} \right] \).
10. Back-substitute: From Row 4, \( 0 = -11 \), so the system is inconsistent; however, the columns may be linearly dependent, so the best approximation is the projection of \( \mathbf{y} \) onto the column space (least squares solution).
11. Compute \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \), \( \mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} \), solve \( (A^T A) \mathbf{c} = A^T \mathbf{y} \).
12. Compute \( A^T A = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 15 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \), \( A^T \mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 13 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \).
13. Solve \( (A^T A) \mathbf{c} = A^T \mathbf{y} \): \( \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 15 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} c_1 \\ c_2 \\ c_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 13 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \).
14. \( c_1 = 2, c_2 = 13/15, c_3 = 7/4 \).
15. \( \mathbf{y}_W = 2\mathbf{u}_1 + \frac{13}{15}\mathbf{u}_2 + \frac{7}{4}\mathbf{u}_3 = 2\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} + \frac{13}{15}\begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix} + \frac{7}{4}\begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \).
16. \( \mathbf{y}_W = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 2 \\ 0 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} -\frac{13}{15} \\ \frac{39}{15} \\ \frac{13}{15} \\ -\frac{26}{15} \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} -\frac{7}{4} \\ 0 \\ \frac{7}{4} \\ \frac{7}{4} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 - \frac{13}{15} - \frac{7}{4} \\ 2 + \frac{39}{15} + 0 \\ 0 + \frac{13}{15} + \frac{7}{4} \\ 2 - \frac{26}{15} + \frac{7}{4} \end{bmatrix} \).
17. \( \mathbf{y}_W = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{120 - 52 - 105}{60} \\ \frac{120 + 156 + 0}{60} \\ \frac{0 + 52 + 105}{60} \\ \frac{120 - 104 + 105}{60} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{-37}{60} \\ \frac{276}{60} \\ \frac{157}{60} \\ \frac{121}{60} \end{bmatrix} \).
18. \( \mathbf{y}_{W^\perp} = \mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} -\frac{37}{60} \\ \frac{23}{5} \\ \frac{157}{60} \\ \frac{121}{60} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{277}{60} \\ \frac{-23}{60} \\ \frac{23}{60} \\ -\frac{181}{60} \end{bmatrix} \).
19. Final answer: \( \mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} -\frac{37}{60} \\ \frac{23}{5} \\ \frac{157}{60} \\ \frac{121}{60} \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} \frac{277}{60} \\ -\frac{23}{60} \\ \frac{23}{60} \\ -\frac{181}{60} \end{bmatrix} \), where the first vector is in \( W \) and the second is orthogonal to \( W \).
