1. To find the eigenvalues, set up the characteristic equation \(\det(A - \lambda I) = 0\), where \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 2 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\) and \(I\) is the identity matrix.  
2. Compute \(A - \lambda I = \begin{bmatrix} 0-\lambda & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 2-\lambda & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 1-\lambda \end{bmatrix}\).  
3. The determinant is \(\det(A - \lambda I) = (0-\lambda)\det\begin{bmatrix} 2-\lambda & 5 \\ 0 & 1-\lambda \end{bmatrix}\).  
4. Calculate the \(2 \times 2\) determinant: \(\det\begin{bmatrix} 2-\lambda & 5 \\ 0 & 1-\lambda \end{bmatrix} = (2-\lambda)(1-\lambda) - 0 = (2-\lambda)(1-\lambda)\).  
5. Multiply out: \(\det(A - \lambda I) = (-\lambda)(2-\lambda)(1-\lambda)\).  
6. Set the determinant to zero: \((-\lambda)(2-\lambda)(1-\lambda) = 0\).  
7. The solutions are \(\lambda = 0\), \(\lambda = 1\), and \(\lambda = 2\), which are the eigenvalues.  
8. Final answer: The eigenvalues of the matrix are \(0\), \(1\), and \(2\).
