1. Find the eigenvalues of \(A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix}\): Solve \(\det(A - \lambda I) = 0\), so \(\det\begin{pmatrix} 1-\lambda & -2 \\ 1 & 3-\lambda \end{pmatrix} = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0\).
2. Solve \(\lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0\): The roots are \(\lambda = 2 \pm i\).
3. The matrix \(C\) will be \(\begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix}\) with \(a = 2\), \(b = 1\), so \(C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}\).
4. Find an eigenvector for \(\lambda = 2 + i\): Solve \((A - (2+i)I)\vec{v} = 0\), so \(\begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix}\vec{v} = 0\).
5. Let \(v_1 = \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix}\), then \((-1-i)x - 2y = 0\) and \(x + (1-i)y = 0\). Solving, set \(y = 1\), then \(x = 2/(1+i) = (2(1-i))/((1+i)(1-i)) = (2-2i)/2 = 1-i\).
6. So, eigenvector for \(2+i\) is \(\vec{v}_1 = \begin{pmatrix} 1-i \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}\).
7. The real and imaginary parts of \(\vec{v}_1\) are \(\begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}\) and \(\begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}\).
8. Form \(P\) from these: \(P = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}\).
9.Final answer: \(P = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}\), \(C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}\), so \(A = P C P^{-1}\).
