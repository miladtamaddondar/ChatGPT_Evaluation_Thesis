1. To write \( A \) in the form \( A = P C P^{-1} \) with \( C = \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix} \), we first find the eigenvalues of \( A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix} \) by solving \( \det(A - \lambda I) = 0 \).  
2. Compute \( \det \left( \begin{pmatrix} 1-\lambda & -2 \\ 1 & 3-\lambda \end{pmatrix} \right) = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0 \).  
3. Solve the quadratic equation: \( \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0 \implies \lambda = 2 \pm i \).  
4. Since \( C \) must have the form \( \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix} \), set \( a = 2 \), \( b = 1 \) so that the eigenvalues are \( 2 \pm i \).  
5. Find an eigenvector for \( \lambda = 2 + i \) by solving \( (A - (2+i)I)\vec{v} = 0 \), i.e., \( \begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix} \vec{v} = 0 \).  
6. Let \( \vec{v} = \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix} \), then \( (-1-i)x - 2y = 0 \) and \( x + (1-i)y = 0 \).  
7. From the second equation, \( x = -(1-i)y \); substitute into the first: \( (-1-i)(-(1-i)y) - 2y = 0 \).  
8. Expand: \( (1+i)(1-i)y - 2y = (1^2 - i^2)y - 2y = (1 - (-1))y - 2y = 2y - 2y = 0 \), so any \( y \neq 0 \) works.  
9. Choose \( y = 1 \), then \( x = -(1-i) \), so the eigenvector is \( \vec{v}_1 = \begin{pmatrix} -(1-i) \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \).  
10. The complex conjugate eigenvector for \( \lambda = 2 - i \) is \( \vec{v}_2 = \begin{pmatrix} -(1+i) \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \).  
11. Form the matrix \( P \) with columns as the real and imaginary parts of \( \vec{v}_1 \): \( \text{Re}(\vec{v}_1) = \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \), \( \text{Im}(\vec{v}_1) = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix} \), so \( P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \).  
12. The matrix \( C \) is \( \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \) since its eigenvalues are \( 2 \pm i \).  
13. The answer: \( P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \), \( C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \), and \( A = P C P^{-1} \).
