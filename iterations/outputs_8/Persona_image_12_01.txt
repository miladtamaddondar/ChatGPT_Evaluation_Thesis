1. Let \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \) and recall the characteristic polynomial is \( \det(A - \lambda I) \).
2. Compute \( A - \lambda I = \begin{bmatrix} 2-\lambda & 1 \\ -1 & 4-\lambda \end{bmatrix} \).
3. The determinant is \( (2-\lambda)(4-\lambda) - (1)(-1) \).
4. Expand to get \( (2-\lambda)(4-\lambda) + 1 = 8 - 2\lambda - 4\lambda + \lambda^2 + 1 \).
5. Combine like terms to get \( \lambda^2 - 6\lambda + 9 \).
6. The characteristic polynomial is \( \lambda^2 - 6\lambda + 9 \).
7. Set the characteristic polynomial to zero: \( \lambda^2 - 6\lambda + 9 = 0 \).
8. Factor to get \( (\lambda - 3)^2 = 0 \).
9. The eigenvalues are \( \lambda = 3 \) (with algebraic multiplicity 2).
