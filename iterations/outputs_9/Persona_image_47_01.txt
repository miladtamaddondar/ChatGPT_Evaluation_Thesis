3. Solve $(A^T A)\mathbf{x} = A^T \mathbf{b}$, so $\begin{bmatrix} 14 & 5 \\ 5 & 42 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix}$.
4. Find the inverse of $A^T A$: $\det = 14 \cdot 42 - 5 \cdot 5 = 588 - 25 = 563$, so $(A^T A)^{-1} = \frac{1}{563} \begin{bmatrix} 42 & -5 \\ -5 & 14 \end{bmatrix}$.

Compute the least-squares solution $\mathbf{x} = (A^T A)^{-1}A^T\mathbf{b} = \frac{1}{563} \begin{bmatrix} 42 & -5 \\ -5 & 14 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix} = \frac{1}{563} \begin{bmatrix} 42 \cdot 4 - 5 \cdot 6 \\ -5 \cdot 4 + 14 \cdot 6 \end{bmatrix} = \frac{1}{563} \begin{bmatrix} 168 - 30 \\ -20 + 84 \end{bmatrix} = \frac{1}{563} \begin{bmatrix} 138 \\ 64 \end{bmatrix}$.
6. Thus, the least-squares solution is $\boxed{\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{138}{563} \\ \frac{64}{563} \end{bmatrix}}$.
