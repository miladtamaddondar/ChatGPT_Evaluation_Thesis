1. Since \( T \) is linear, \( T(3\mathbf{u}) = 3T(\mathbf{u}) = 3 \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} \).
2. Since \( T \) is linear, \( T(2\mathbf{v}) = 2T(\mathbf{v}) = 2 \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -2 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix} \).
3. By linearity, \( T(3\mathbf{u} + 2\mathbf{v}) = 3T(\mathbf{u}) + 2T(\mathbf{v}) = 3 \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} + 2 \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} -2 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 9 \end{bmatrix} \).
