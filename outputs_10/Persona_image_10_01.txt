2. The basis for Col \(A\) consists of the corresponding columns of \(A\): \(\left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} -4 \\ 5 \\ -3 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \right\}\).  
3. The dimension of Col \(A\) is the number of pivot columns: \(\dim(\text{Col }A) = 3\).  
4. To find a basis for Nul \(A\), set up the equation \(A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0}\) using the variables \(x_1, x_2, x_3, x_4\) and write the system from the echelon form:  
   \(\begin{cases} x_1 - 3x_2 + 2x_3 - 4x_4 = 0 \\ 5x_3 - 7x_4 = 0 \\ 5x_4 = 0 \end{cases}\).  
5. Solve the last equation: \(5x_4 = 0 \implies x_4 = 0\).  
6. Substitute \(x_4 = 0\) into the second equation: \(5x_3 = 0 \implies x_3 = 0\).  
7. Substitute \(x_3 = 0\) and \(x_4 = 0\) into the first equation: \(x_1 - 3x_2 = 0 \implies x_1 = 3x_2\).  
8. Express the general solution: \(\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \\ x_4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3x_2 \\ x_2 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} = x_2 \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}\).  
9. The basis for Nul \(A\) is \(\left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \right\}\).  
10. The dimension of Nul \(A\) is the number of free variables, which is 1: \(\dim(\text{Nul }A) = 1\).
