1. Find the eigenvalues of $A$: $\det\begin{pmatrix} 1-\lambda & -2 \\ 1 & 3-\lambda \end{pmatrix} = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0$ gives $\lambda = 2 \pm i$.
2. Find an eigenvector for $\lambda = 2+i$: Solve $\begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix} = 0$. From the first row: $(-1-i)x - 2y = 0 \implies x = \frac{-2}{-1-i}y = \frac{2}{1+i}y = (1-i)y$.
3. Let $y=1$, then eigenvector is $\begin{pmatrix} 1-i \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}$.
4. Separate real and imaginary parts: $\begin{pmatrix} 1-i \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} + i\begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}$.
5. Form $P$ from these vectors: $P = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}$.
6. The canonical form is $C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}$.
7. Final answer: $A = P C P^{-1}$, where $P = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}$ and $C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}$.
