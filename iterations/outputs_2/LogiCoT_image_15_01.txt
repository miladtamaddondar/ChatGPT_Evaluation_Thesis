1. Find the eigenvalues of \( A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix} \) by solving \( \det(A - \lambda I) = 0 \): \( \det\begin{pmatrix} 1-\lambda & -2 \\ 1 & 3-\lambda \end{pmatrix} = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0 \).  
2. Solve for \( \lambda \): \( \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0 \) gives \( \lambda = \frac{4 \pm \sqrt{16-20}}{2} = \frac{4 \pm 2i}{2} = 2 \pm i \).  
3. Verify the eigenvalues are complex conjugates, so \( C \) will be of the form \( \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix} \) with \( a = 2 \), \( b = 1 \).  
4. Find an eigenvector for \( \lambda = 2+i \): Solve \( (A - (2+i)I)\vec{v} = 0 \), so \( \begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix} = 0 \).  
5. From the first row: \( (-1-i)x - 2y = 0 \implies x = \frac{-2}{-1-i}y = \frac{2}{1+i}y = (1-i)y \) (rationalizing denominator).  
6. Choose \( y = 1 \), so eigenvector \( \vec{v}_1 = \begin{pmatrix} 1-i \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \); the conjugate eigenvector for \( 2-i \) is \( \vec{v}_2 = \begin{pmatrix} 1+i \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \).  
7. Construct \( P \) using the real and imaginary parts of \( \vec{v}_1 \): \( \vec{v}_1 = (1-i, 1)^T \) gives real part \( (1, 1)^T \) and imaginary part \( (-1, 0)^T \), so \( P = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \).  
8. Verify \( P \) is invertible: \( \det(P) = (1)(0) - (1)(-1) = 1 \), so \( P \) is invertible.  
9. The matrix \( C \) is \( \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \) since \( a = 2 \), \( b = 1 \).  
10. The answer is: \( P = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \), \( C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \), and \( A = P C P^{-1} \).
