1. To find $P$ and $C$ such that $A = PCP^{-1}$ with $C$ of the form $\begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix}$, first compute the eigenvalues of $A$ by solving $\det(A - \lambda I) = 0$: $\det\left(\begin{pmatrix} 1-\lambda & -2 \\ 1 & 3-\lambda \end{pmatrix}\right) = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) - (-2)(1) = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0$.

2. Solve the characteristic equation $\lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0$ to find the eigenvalues: $\lambda = \frac{4 \pm \sqrt{16-20}}{2} = \frac{4 \pm 2i}{2} = 2 \pm i$.

3. Since the eigenvalues are complex conjugates, $C$ will be a real matrix similar to $A$ with $a = 2$ and $b = 1$, so $C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}$.

4. Find an eigenvector for $\lambda = 2 + i$ by solving $(A - (2+i)I)\vec{v} = 0$: $A - (2+i)I = \begin{pmatrix} 1-(2+i) & -2 \\ 1 & 3-(2+i) \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix}$.

5. Set up the system: $(-1-i)x_1 - 2x_2 = 0$ and $x_1 + (1-i)x_2 = 0$; from the first equation, $(-1-i)x_1 = 2x_2 \implies x_1 = \frac{-2}{1+i}x_2$.

6. Rationalize the denominator: $\frac{-2}{1+i} = \frac{-2(1-i)}{(1+i)(1-i)} = \frac{-2(1-i)}{2} = -1 + i$, so $x_1 = (-1 + i)x_2$.

7. Choose $x_2 = 1$, so the eigenvector is $\vec{v} = \begin{pmatrix} -1 + i \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}$.

8. To get a real matrix $P$, take the real and imaginary parts of $\vec{v}$: $\operatorname{Re}(\vec{v}) = \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}$ and $\operatorname{Im}(\vec{v}) = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}$.

9. Form $P$ with these as columns: $P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}$.

10. The invertible matrix $P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}$ and $C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}$ satisfy $A = PCP^{-1}$.
