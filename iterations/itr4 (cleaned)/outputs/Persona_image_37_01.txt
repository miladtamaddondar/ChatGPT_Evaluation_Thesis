1. The vector \([q]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}\) represents the coordinates of \(q\) in the basis \(\mathcal{B} = \{p_1, p_2, p_3\}\). 
2. To find \(q\) in \(\mathbb{P}_2\), write \(q = -1 \cdot p_1 + 1 \cdot p_2 + 2 \cdot p_3\). 
3. Substitute the given polynomials: \(q = -1(1 + t^2) + 1(t - 3t^2) + 2(1 + t - 3t^2)\). 
4. Expand each term: \(q = (-1 - t^2) + (t - 3t^2) + (2 + 2t - 6t^2)\). 
5. Combine like terms: constant terms: \(-1 + 2 = 1\), \(t\) terms: \(t + 2t = 3t\), \(t^2\) terms: \(-t^2 - 3t^2 - 6t^2 = -10t^2\). 
6. Therefore, \(q = 1 + 3t - 10t^2\).
