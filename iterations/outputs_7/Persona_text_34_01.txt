2. Begin row reducing $A$ by leaving column 1 as is and using row 1 to eliminate entries below: add $3$ times row 1 to row 3 and subtract $2$ times row 1 from row 4.

4. Use row 2 to eliminate entries below and above in column 2: subtract $2$ times row 2 from row 3 and add $3$ times row 2 to row 4.

7. Use row 3 to eliminate the entry in row 4, column 3: subtract $18$ times row 3 from row 4.
8. Row 4 becomes $[0, 0, 0, 17.5, 5]$ or $[0, 0, 0, \frac{35}{2}, 5]$.
9. Divide row 4 by $17.5$ to make the leading entry $1$: row 4 becomes $[0, 0, 0, 1, \frac{2}{7}]$.
10. The row-reduced matrix is $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & -3 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & -\frac{5}{4} & \frac{1}{6} \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 & \frac{2}{7} \end{bmatrix}$.
11. The pivot columns are columns 1, 2, 3, and 4, so the corresponding vectors $\mathbf{v}_1, \mathbf{v}_2, \mathbf{v}_3, \mathbf{v}_4$ form a basis.
