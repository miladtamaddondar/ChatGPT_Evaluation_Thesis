1. To find a basis for the eigenspace for \(\lambda = 4\), solve \((A - 4I)\vec{x} = \vec{0}\): \(A - 4I = \begin{bmatrix}-1 & 0 & 2 & 0 \\ 1 & -1 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0\end{bmatrix}\).
2. Set up the homogeneous system: \(\begin{bmatrix}-1 & 0 & 2 & 0 \\ 1 & -1 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}x_1\\x_2\\x_3\\x_4\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0\\0\\0\\0\end{bmatrix}\).
3. Write the equations: \(-x_1 + 2x_3 = 0\), \(x_1 - x_2 + x_3 = 0\), \(x_2 - 3x_3 = 0\), \(0 = 0\).
4. From the third equation, \(x_2 = 3x_3\).
5. Substitute \(x_2 = 3x_3\) into the second equation: \(x_1 - 3x_3 + x_3 = 0 \implies x_1 - 2x_3 = 0\).
6. From this, \(x_1 = 2x_3\).
7. Let \(x_3 = t\) (free variable), then \(x_1 = 2t\), \(x_2 = 3t\), \(x_4 = s\) (since \(x_4\) is not present in any equation).
8. The general solution is \(\vec{x} = t\begin{bmatrix}2\\3\\1\\0\end{bmatrix} + s\begin{bmatrix}0\\0\\0\\1\end{bmatrix}\).
9. Therefore, a basis for the eigenspace for \(\lambda = 4\) is \(\left\{\begin{bmatrix}2\\3\\1\\0\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}0\\0\\0\\1\end{bmatrix}\right\}\).
