1. To find the orthogonal projection of \(\mathbf{b}\) onto \(\text{Col }A\), compute \(\mathbf{p} = A\hat{\mathbf{x}}\) where \(\hat{\mathbf{x}}\) is the least-squares solution to \(A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{b}\).  
2. The least-squares solution is \(\hat{\mathbf{x}} = (A^TA)^{-1}A^T\mathbf{b}\).  
3. Compute \(A^T = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix}\).  
4. Compute \(A^TA = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 3 & 1 \\ -2 & 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 14 & 1 \\ 1 & 42 \end{bmatrix}\).  
5. Compute \(A^T\mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ -2 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1*4 + 3*(-2) + (-2)*(-3) \\ 5*4 + 1*(-2) + 4*(-3) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 - 6 + 6 \\ 20 - 2 - 12 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix}\).  
6. Compute \((A^TA)^{-1}\) using the formula for a \(2 \times 2\) matrix: \((A^TA)^{-1} = \frac{1}{14*42 - 1*1} \begin{bmatrix} 42 & -1 \\ -1 & 14 \end{bmatrix} = \frac{1}{587} \begin{bmatrix} 42 & -1 \\ -1 & 14 \end{bmatrix}\).  
7. Compute \(\hat{\mathbf{x}} = (A^TA)^{-1}A^T\mathbf{b} = \frac{1}{587} \begin{bmatrix} 42 & -1 \\ -1 & 14 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix} = \frac{1}{587} \begin{bmatrix} 42*4 - 1*6 \\ -1*4 + 14*6 \end{bmatrix} = \frac{1}{587} \begin{bmatrix} 168 - 6 \\ -4 + 84 \end{bmatrix} = \frac{1}{587} \begin{bmatrix} 162 \\ 80 \end{bmatrix}\).  
8. Thus, \(\hat{\mathbf{x}} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{162}{587} \\ \frac{80}{587} \end{bmatrix}\).  
9. The orthogonal projection is \(\mathbf{p} = A\hat{\mathbf{x}} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 3 & 1 \\ -2 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \frac{162}{587} \\ \frac{80}{587} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{162}{587} + 5*\frac{80}{587} \\ 3*\frac{162}{587} + \frac{80}{587} \\ -2*\frac{162}{587} + 4*\frac{80}{587} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{162 + 400}{587} \\ \frac{486 + 80}{587} \\ \frac{-324 + 320}{587} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{562}{587} \\ \frac{566}{587} \\ \frac{-4}{587} \end{bmatrix}\).  
10. The answers are: (a) the orthogonal projection of \(\mathbf{b}\) onto \(\text{Col }A\) is \(\begin{bmatrix} \frac{562}{587} \\ \frac{566}{587} \\ \frac{-4}{587} \end{bmatrix}\), and (b) a least-squares solution to \(A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{b}\) is \(\begin{bmatrix} \frac{162}{587} \\ \frac{80}{587} \end{bmatrix}\).
