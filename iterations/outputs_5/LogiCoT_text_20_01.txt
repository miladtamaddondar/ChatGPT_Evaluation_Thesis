1. To find a basis of eigenvectors, first find all eigenvalues of \(A\) by solving \(\det(A - \lambda I) = 0\): \(\det\left(\begin{bmatrix}0.6-\lambda & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.7-\lambda\end{bmatrix}\right) = (0.6-\lambda)(0.7-\lambda) - (0.4)(0.3) = 0\).
2. Expanding the determinant: \((0.6-\lambda)(0.7-\lambda) - 0.12 = 0.42 - 0.6\lambda - 0.7\lambda + \lambda^2 - 0.12 = \lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3 = 0\).
3. Solve the quadratic equation: \(\lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3 = 0\) gives \(\lambda = \frac{1.3 \pm \sqrt{1.69 - 1.2}}{2} = \frac{1.3 \pm 0.7}{2}\), so \(\lambda_1 = 1\), \(\lambda_2 = 0.3\).
4. We are given \(\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix}3/7 \\ 4/7\end{bmatrix}\) as an eigenvector for \(\lambda_1 = 1\); verify: \(A\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix}0.6 \cdot 3/7 + 0.3 \cdot 4/7 \\ 0.4 \cdot 3/7 + 0.7 \cdot 4/7\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}(1.8+1.2)/7 \\ (1.2+2.8)/7\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}3/7 \\ 4/7\end{bmatrix} = \mathbf{v}_1\).
5. To find \(\mathbf{v}_2\), solve \((A - 0.3I)\mathbf{v}_2 = 0\): \(A - 0.3I = \begin{bmatrix}0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.4\end{bmatrix}\).
6. The nullspace equation is \(0.3x + 0.3y = 0\) and \(0.4x + 0.4y = 0\), which reduces to \(x + y = 0\), so \(\mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}\) (any scalar multiple).
7. Verify \(\mathbf{v}_2\) is an eigenvector: \(A\begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0.6-0.3 \\ 0.4-0.7\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0.3 \\ -0.3\end{bmatrix} = 0.3\begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}\).
8. Therefore, a basis for \(\mathbb{R}^2\) consisting of eigenvectors of \(A\) is \(\left\{ \begin{bmatrix}3/7 \\ 4/7\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix} \right\}\).
