1. Write the matrix \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 2 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \) and set up the characteristic equation \( \det(A - \lambda I) = 0 \). 2. Compute \( A - \lambda I = \begin{bmatrix} 0-\lambda & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 2-\lambda & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 1-\lambda \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -\lambda & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 2-\lambda & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 1-\lambda \end{bmatrix} \). 3. Find the determinant: \( \det(A - \lambda I) = (-\lambda) \cdot \det\begin{bmatrix} 2-\lambda & 5 \\ 0 & 1-\lambda \end{bmatrix} \). 4. Calculate the 2x2 determinant: \( \det\begin{bmatrix} 2-\lambda & 5 \\ 0 & 1-\lambda \end{bmatrix} = (2-\lambda)(1-\lambda) - 0 = (2-\lambda)(1-\lambda) \). 5. So, \( \det(A - \lambda I) = (-\lambda)(2-\lambda)(1-\lambda) \). 6. Set the determinant to zero: \( (-\lambda)(2-\lambda)(1-\lambda) = 0 \). 7. The solutions are \( \lambda_1 = 0 \), \( \lambda_2 = 2 \), \( \lambda_3 = 1 \). 8. Therefore, the eigenvalues of the matrix are \( \boxed{0,\, 2,\, 1} \).
