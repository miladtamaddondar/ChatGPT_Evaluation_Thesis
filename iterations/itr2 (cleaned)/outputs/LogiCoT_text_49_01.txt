1. The QR decomposition expresses \( A = QR \), where \( Q \) is orthonormal and \( R \) is upper triangular; since \( Q \) is given, solve for \( R \) by multiplying both sides by \( Q^T \): \( Q^T A = Q^T Q R = I R = R \), so \( R = Q^T A \).  
2. Compute \( Q^T \) (the transpose of \( Q \)): \( Q^T = \begin{bmatrix} 5/6 & 1/6 & -3/6 & 1/6 \\ -1/6 & 5/6 & 1/6 & 3/6 \end{bmatrix} \).  
3. Multiply \( Q^T \) by \( A \): \( R = Q^T A = \begin{bmatrix} 5/6 & 1/6 & -3/6 & 1/6 \\ -1/6 & 5/6 & 1/6 & 3/6 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 9 \\ 1 & 7 \\ -3 & -5 \\ 1 & 5 \end{bmatrix} \).  
4. Calculate the first row of \( R \): \( \left[ \frac{5}{6} \cdot 5 + \frac{1}{6} \cdot 1 + \frac{-3}{6} \cdot (-3) + \frac{1}{6} \cdot 1, \frac{5}{6} \cdot 9 + \frac{1}{6} \cdot 7 + \frac{-3}{6} \cdot (-5) + \frac{1}{6} \cdot 5 \right] = [\frac{25}{6} + \frac{1}{6} + \frac{9}{6} + \frac{1}{6}, \frac{45}{6} + \frac{7}{6} + \frac{15}{6} + \frac{5}{6}] = [\frac{36}{6}, \frac{72}{6}] = [6, 12] \).  
5. Calculate the second row of \( R \): \( \left[ \frac{-1}{6} \cdot 5 + \frac{5}{6} \cdot 1 + \frac{1}{6} \cdot (-3) + \frac{3}{6} \cdot 1, \frac{-1}{6} \cdot 9 + \frac{5}{6} \cdot 7 + \frac{1}{6} \cdot (-5) + \frac{3}{6} \cdot 5 \right] = [\frac{-5}{6} + \frac{5}{6} + \frac{-3}{6} + \frac{3}{6}, \frac{-9}{6} + \frac{35}{6} + \frac{-5}{6} + \frac{15}{6}] = [0, \frac{36}{6}] = [0, 6] \).  
6. Assemble \( R \) as \( R = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 12 \\ 0 & 6 \end{bmatrix} \); verify upper triangularity, which holds since all entries below the main diagonal are zero.  
7. Check the decomposition by computing \( QR = \begin{bmatrix} 5/6 & -1/6 \\ 1/6 & 5/6 \\ -3/6 & 1/6 \\ 1/6 & 3/6 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 12 \\ 0 & 6 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 9 \\ 1 & 7 \\ -3 & -5 \\ 1 & 5 \end{bmatrix} = A \), confirming correctness.  
8. Therefore, the upper triangular matrix \( R \) such that \( A = QR \) is \( \boxed{ \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 12 \\ 0 & 6 \end{bmatrix} } \).
