1. The $\mathcal{B}$-matrix for $T$ is the matrix representation of $T$ with respect to the basis $\mathcal{B}$, i.e., $[T]_{\mathcal{B}}$; by definition, its $j$-th column is $[T(\mathbf{b}_j)]_{\mathcal{B}}$ for $j=1,2$.
2. Compute $T(\mathbf{b}_1) = A\mathbf{b}_1 = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 1 \\ -1 & 3\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 1\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 2\end{bmatrix}$; this follows from matrix multiplication.
3. To write $\begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 2\end{bmatrix}$ as a linear combination of $\mathbf{b}_1$ and $\mathbf{b}_2$, solve $c_1\mathbf{b}_1 + c_2\mathbf{b}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 2\end{bmatrix}$, i.e., $c_1\begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 1\end{bmatrix} + c_2\begin{bmatrix}5 \\ 4\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 2\end{bmatrix}$.
4. Set up the system: $c_1 + 5c_2 = 2$, $c_1 + 4c_2 = 2$; subtracting the second from the first gives $c_2 = 0$, so $c_1 = 2$.
5. Thus, $[T(\mathbf{b}_1)]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 0\end{bmatrix}$; this is verified by reconstructing $2\mathbf{b}_1 = \begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 2\end{bmatrix}$.
6. Compute $T(\mathbf{b}_2) = A\mathbf{b}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 1 \\ -1 & 3\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}5 \\ 4\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}9 \\ 7\end{bmatrix}$; this follows from matrix multiplication.
7. To write $\begin{bmatrix}9 \\ 7\end{bmatrix}$ as a linear combination of $\mathbf{b}_1$ and $\mathbf{b}_2$, solve $d_1\mathbf{b}_1 + d_2\mathbf{b}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}9 \\ 7\end{bmatrix}$, i.e., $d_1 + 5d_2 = 9$, $d_1 + 4d_2 = 7$.
8. Subtract the second equation from the first: $(d_1 + 5d_2) - (d_1 + 4d_2) = 9 - 7 \implies d_2 = 2$, so $d_1 = 9 - 5 \times 2 = -1$.
9. Thus, $[T(\mathbf{b}_2)]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix}-1 \\ 2\end{bmatrix}$; this is verified by reconstructing $-1\mathbf{b}_1 + 2\mathbf{b}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}-1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix}10 \\ 8\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}9 \\ 7\end{bmatrix}$.
10. Therefore, the $\mathcal{B}$-matrix for $T$ is $[T]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix}2 & -1 \\ 0 & 2\end{bmatrix}$, where the first column is $[T(\mathbf{b}_1)]_{\mathcal{B}}$ and the second is $[T(\mathbf{b}_2)]_{\mathcal{B}}$.
