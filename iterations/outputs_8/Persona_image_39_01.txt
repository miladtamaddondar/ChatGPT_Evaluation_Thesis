1. The matrix equation \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 4 \\ 2 & -6 & 8 \end{bmatrix} = \mathbf{u}\mathbf{v}^T\) means each column of the left matrix is a scalar multiple of \(\mathbf{u}\).
2. Let \(\mathbf{u} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}\) and \(\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} v_1 \\ v_2 \\ v_3 \end{bmatrix}\), so \(\mathbf{u}\mathbf{v}^T = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} v_1 & v_2 & v_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} v_1 & v_2 & v_3 \\ 2v_1 & 2v_2 & 2v_3 \end{bmatrix}\).
3. Equate the matrices: \(\begin{bmatrix} v_1 & v_2 & v_3 \\ 2v_1 & 2v_2 & 2v_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 4 \\ 2 & -6 & 8 \end{bmatrix}\).
4. Set up equations for the first row: \(v_1 = 1\), \(v_2 = -3\), \(v_3 = 4\).
5. Set up equations for the second row: \(2v_1 = 2\), \(2v_2 = -6\), \(2v_3 = 8\).
6. Solve for \(v_1, v_2, v_3\) from the first row: \(v_1 = 1\), \(v_2 = -3\), \(v_3 = 4\).
7. Check consistency with the second row: \(2 \times 1 = 2\), \(2 \times -3 = -6\), \(2 \times 4 = 8\), all match.
8. The solution is \(\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix}\).
