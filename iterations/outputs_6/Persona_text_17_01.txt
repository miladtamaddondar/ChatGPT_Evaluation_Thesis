1. To find $T(p)$ for $p(t) = 5 + 3t$, evaluate $p$ at $-1$, $0$, and $1$: $p(-1) = 5 + 3(-1) = 2$, $p(0) = 5 + 3(0) = 5$, $p(1) = 5 + 3(1) = 8$.
2. Thus, $T(5 + 3t) = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 5 \\ 8 \end{bmatrix}$.
3. To find the matrix of $T$ relative to $\{1, t, t^2\}$ and the standard basis, compute $T(1)$, $T(t)$, and $T(t^2)$.
4. $T(1) = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}$ since $1$ evaluated at any $t$ is $1$.
5. $T(t) = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}$ since $t$ evaluated at $-1$, $0$, $1$ gives $-1$, $0$, $1$.
6. $T(t^2) = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}$ since $t^2$ evaluated at $-1$, $0$, $1$ gives $1$, $0$, $1$.
7. The matrix for $T$ is $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 1 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$.
