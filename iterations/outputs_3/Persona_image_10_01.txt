1. The matrix \( A \) is given as \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ -3 & 9 & -1 & 5 \\ 2 & -6 & 4 & -3 \\ -4 & 12 & 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix} \) and its echelon form is \( \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 & -7 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \). 
2. To find a basis for \(\text{Col}\,A\), we identify the pivot columns in the echelon form, which are columns 1, 3, and 4, so the corresponding columns from the original matrix form a basis: \( \left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} -4 \\ 5 \\ -3 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \right\} \). 
3. The dimension of \(\text{Col}\,A\) is the number of pivot columns, which is 3. 
4. To find a basis for \(\text{Nul}\,A\), we solve \(A\vec{x} = \vec{0}\) using the echelon form, letting \(x_2 = s\) (free variable) and expressing the leading variables in terms of \(s\): from row 3, \(5x_4 = 0 \implies x_4 = 0\); from row 2, \(5x_3 - 7x_4 = 0 \implies x_3 = 0\); from row 1, \(x_1 - 3x_2 + 2x_3 - 4x_4 = 0 \implies x_1 = 3x_2\); so the general solution is \( \vec{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 3s \\ s \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} = s\begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \). 
5. Therefore, a basis for \(\text{Nul}\,A\) is \( \left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \right\} \). 
6. The dimension of \(\text{Nul}\,A\) is 1.
