1. Find eigenvalues by solving $\det(A - \lambda I) = 0$: $\begin{vmatrix} 0-\lambda & 1 \\ -3 & 4-\lambda \end{vmatrix} = (0-\lambda)(4-\lambda) - (-3)(1) = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 3 = 0$.
2. Factor and solve: $(\lambda-1)(\lambda-3)=0 \implies \lambda_1=1, \lambda_2=3$.
3. For $\lambda=1$, solve $(A-I)\mathbf{v}=0$: $A-I = \begin{bmatrix}-1 & 1 \\ -3 & 3\end{bmatrix}$.
4. Row operation: $R_2 \to R_2 - 3R_1$, $\begin{bmatrix}-1 & 1 \\ 0 & 0\end{bmatrix}$.
5. Solution: $-v_1 + v_2 = 0 \implies v_1 = v_2$, so eigenvector is any nonzero scalar multiple of $\begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 1\end{bmatrix}$.
6. For $\lambda=3$, solve $(A-3I)\mathbf{v}=0$: $A-3I = \begin{bmatrix}-3 & 1 \\ -3 & 1\end{bmatrix}$.
7. Row operation: $R_2 \to R_2 - R_1$, $\begin{bmatrix}-3 & 1 \\ 0 & 0\end{bmatrix}$.
8. Solution: $-3v_1 + v_2 = 0 \implies v_2 = 3v_1$, so eigenvector is any nonzero scalar multiple of $\begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 3\end{bmatrix}$.
9. Thus, a basis $\mathcal{B} = \left\{ \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 1\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 3\end{bmatrix} \right\}$ diagonalizes $A$, so $[T]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 \\ 0 & 3\end{bmatrix}$.
