1. To find the orthogonal projection of \(\mathbf{b}\) onto \(\text{Col }A\), we use \(\text{proj}_{\text{Col }A}(\mathbf{b}) = A(A^T A)^{-1}A^T \mathbf{b}\).
2. Compute \(A^T A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 3 & 1 \\ -2 & 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1^2+3^2+(-2)^2 & 1\cdot5+3\cdot1+(-2)\cdot4 \\ 5\cdot1+1\cdot3+4\cdot(-2) & 5^2+1^2+4^2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 14 & 2 \\ 2 & 42 \end{bmatrix}\).
3. Compute \(A^T \mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ -2 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1\cdot4 + 3\cdot(-2) + (-2)\cdot(-3) \\ 5\cdot4 + 1\cdot(-2) + 4\cdot(-3) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4-6+6 \\ 20-2-12 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix}\).
4. Find \((A^T A)^{-1}\) by computing the inverse of \(\begin{bmatrix} 14 & 2 \\ 2 & 42 \end{bmatrix}\), whose determinant is \(14 \cdot 42 - 2 \cdot 2 = 588 - 4 = 584\), so \((A^T A)^{-1} = \frac{1}{584} \begin{bmatrix} 42 & -2 \\ -2 & 14 \end{bmatrix}\).
5. Compute \((A^T A)^{-1}A^T \mathbf{b} = \frac{1}{584} \begin{bmatrix} 42 & -2 \\ -2 & 14 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix} = \frac{1}{584} \begin{bmatrix} 42\cdot4 + (-2)\cdot6 \\ -2\cdot4 + 14\cdot6 \end{bmatrix} = \frac{1}{584} \begin{bmatrix} 168 - 12 \\ -8 + 84 \end{bmatrix} = \frac{1}{584} \begin{bmatrix} 156 \\ 76 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{39}{146} \\ \frac{19}{146} \end{bmatrix}\).
6. Compute \(A(A^T A)^{-1}A^T \mathbf{b} = A \begin{bmatrix} \frac{39}{146} \\ \frac{19}{146} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 3 & 1 \\ -2 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \frac{39}{146} \\ \frac{19}{146} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{39}{146} + 5\cdot\frac{19}{146} \\ 3\cdot\frac{39}{146} + \frac{19}{146} \\ -2\cdot\frac{39}{146} + 4\cdot\frac{19}{146} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{39+95}{146} \\ \frac{117+19}{146} \\ \frac{-78+76}{146} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{134}{146} \\ \frac{136}{146} \\ \frac{-2}{146} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{67}{73} \\ \frac{68}{73} \\ -\frac{1}{73} \end{bmatrix}\).
7. The orthogonal projection of \(\mathbf{b}\) onto \(\text{Col }A\) is \(\boxed{\begin{bmatrix} \frac{67}{73} \\ \frac{68}{73} \\ -\frac{1}{73} \end{bmatrix}}\).
8. The least-squares solution to \(A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{b}\) is \(\mathbf{x} = (A^T A)^{-1}A^T \mathbf{b} = \boxed{\begin{bmatrix} \frac{39}{146} \\ \frac{19}{146} \end{bmatrix}}\).
