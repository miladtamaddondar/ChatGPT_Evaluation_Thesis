To construct a \(4 \times 3\) matrix with rank 1, recall that a matrix has rank 1 if all its rows (or columns) are scalar multiples of a single nonzero row (or column), which means it can be written as the outer product of a nonzero \(4 \times 1\) column vector and a nonzero \(1 \times 3\) row vector. 
2. Let \(\mathbf{u} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix}\) and \(\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 6 & 7 \end{bmatrix}\), then the matrix \(\mathbf{A} = \mathbf{u} \mathbf{v}\) is a \(4 \times 3\) matrix with rank 1. 
3. Calculating the outer product, \(\mathbf{A} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 6 & 7 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 6 & 7 \\ 10 & 12 & 14 \\ 15 & 18 & 21 \\ 20 & 24 & 28 \end{bmatrix}\). 
4. Thus, one example of a \(4 \times 3\) matrix with rank 1 is \(\boxed{\begin{bmatrix} 5 & 6 & 7 \\ 10 & 12 & 14 \\ 15 & 18 & 21 \\ 20 & 24 & 28 \end{bmatrix}}\).
