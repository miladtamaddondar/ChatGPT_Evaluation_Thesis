1. Set up the equation $\mathbf{y} = c_1\mathbf{u}_1 + c_2\mathbf{u}_2 + c_3\mathbf{u}_3 + \mathbf{z}$, where $\mathbf{z}$ is orthogonal to $W$. To find the projection, solve $c_1\mathbf{u}_1 + c_2\mathbf{u}_2 + c_3\mathbf{u}_3 = \mathbf{y}$, which gives the augmented matrix: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 & 3 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 & -1 \end{array}\right]$.
2. Row 2 minus Row 1: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 & -1 \end{array}\right]$.
3. Row 4 minus Row 1: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 0 & -1 & 2 & -5 \end{array}\right]$.
4. Row 3 unchanged; Row 4 plus Row 3: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 0 & 0 & 3 & -2 \end{array}\right]$.
5. Row 2 divided by 4: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 1 & \frac{1}{4} & -\frac{1}{4} \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 0 & 0 & 3 & -2 \end{array}\right]$.
6. Row 3 minus Row 2: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 1 & \frac{1}{4} & -\frac{1}{4} \\ 0 & 0 & \frac{3}{4} & \frac{13}{4} \\ 0 & 0 & 3 & -2 \end{array}\right]$.
7. Row 4 minus 4 times Row 3: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 1 & \frac{1}{4} & -\frac{1}{4} \\ 0 & 0 & \frac{3}{4} & \frac{13}{4} \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & -18 \end{array}\right]$.
8. Back-substitute: From Row 3, $\frac{3}{4}c_3 = \frac{13}{4} \implies c_3 = \frac{13}{3}$. From Row 2, $c_2 + \frac{1}{4}c_3 = -\frac{1}{4} \implies c_2 = -\frac{1}{4} - \frac{1}{4}\cdot\frac{13}{3} = -\frac{1}{4} - \frac{13}{12} = -\frac{16}{12} = -\frac{4}{3}$. From Row 1, $c_1 - c_2 - c_3 = 4 \implies c_1 = 4 + c_2 + c_3 = 4 - \frac{4}{3} + \frac{13}{3} = 4 + \frac{9}{3} = 7$.
9. The projection is $7\mathbf{u}_1 - \frac{4}{3}\mathbf{u}_2 + \frac{13}{3}\mathbf{u}_3$. Compute this: $7\begin{bmatrix}1\\1\\0\\1\end{bmatrix} - \frac{4}{3}\begin{bmatrix}-1\\3\\1\\-2\end{bmatrix} + \frac{13}{3}\begin{bmatrix}-1\\0\\1\\1\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}7\\7\\0\\7\end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix}\frac{4}{3}\\-\frac{12}{3}\\-\frac{4}{3}\\\frac{8}{3}\end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix}-\frac{13}{3}\\0\\\frac{13}{3}\\\frac{13}{3}\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}7+\frac{4}{3}-\frac{13}{3}\\7-\frac{12}{3}+0\\0-\frac{4}{3}+\frac{13}{3}\\7+\frac{8}{3}+\frac{13}{3}\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}7-\frac{9}{3}\\7-4\\\frac{9}{3}\\7+\frac{21}{3}\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}4\\3\\3\\14\end{bmatrix}$.
10. The orthogonal component is $\mathbf{y} - \text{proj}_W\mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix}4\\3\\3\\-1\end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix}4\\3\\3\\14\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0\\0\\0\\-15\end{bmatrix}$.
11. Final answer: $\mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix}4\\3\\3\\14\end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix}0\\0\\0\\-15\end{bmatrix}$, where the first vector is in $W$ and the second is orthogonal to $W$.
