1. To find the LU factorization, write the matrix \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 9 \\ 4 & 5 \end{bmatrix} \) as \( A = LU \), where \( L \) is lower triangular and \( U \) is upper triangular.  
2. Assume \( L = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ l_{21} & 1 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( U = \begin{bmatrix} u_{11} & u_{12} \\ 0 & u_{22} \end{bmatrix} \), so \( LU = \begin{bmatrix} u_{11} & u_{12} \\ l_{21}u_{11} & l_{21}u_{12} + u_{22} \end{bmatrix} \).  
3. Set \( u_{11} = 6 \) and \( u_{12} = 9 \) by matching the first row of \( LU \) to \( A \).  
4. Set \( l_{21}u_{11} = 4 \implies l_{21} = \frac{4}{6} = \frac{2}{3} \) by matching the (2,1) entry.  
5. Set \( l_{21}u_{12} + u_{22} = 5 \implies \left(\frac{2}{3}\right) \cdot 9 + u_{22} = 5 \implies 6 + u_{22} = 5 \implies u_{22} = -1 \).  
6. Therefore, \( L = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ \frac{2}{3} & 1 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( U = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 9 \\ 0 & -1 \end{bmatrix} \).  
7. Verification: \( LU = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ \frac{2}{3} & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 9 \\ 0 & -1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 9 \\ 4 & 5 \end{bmatrix} \), which matches \( A \).  
8. Final answer: The LU factorization is \( \boxed{ \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 9 \\ 4 & 5 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ \frac{2}{3} & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 9 \\ 0 & -1 \end{bmatrix} } \).
