1. To diagonalize \( A \), we first find its eigenvalues by solving \( \det(A - \lambda I) = 0 \): \( \det\left(\begin{bmatrix} 0-\lambda & 1 \\ -3 & 4-\lambda \end{bmatrix}\right) = (0-\lambda)(4-\lambda) - (-3)(1) = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 3 = 0 \).  
2. Solve the characteristic equation \( \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 3 = 0 \): The roots are \( \lambda = 1 \) and \( \lambda = 3 \).  
3. For \( \lambda = 1 \), solve \( (A - I)\vec{v} = 0 \): \( A - I = \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ -3 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \), so \( \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ -3 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} v_1 \\ v_2 \end{bmatrix} = \vec{0} \) gives \( -v_1 + v_2 = 0 \), so \( v_2 = v_1 \).  
4. Thus, an eigenvector for \( \lambda = 1 \) is \( \vec{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \); verify: \( A\vec{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -3 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \), which is correct.  
5. For \( \lambda = 3 \), solve \( (A - 3I)\vec{v} = 0 \): \( A - 3I = \begin{bmatrix} -3 & 1 \\ -3 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \), so \( -3v_1 + v_2 = 0 \), so \( v_2 = 3v_1 \).  
6. Thus, an eigenvector for \( \lambda = 3 \) is \( \vec{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} \); verify: \( A\vec{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -3 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} = 3\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} \), which is correct.  
7. The basis \( \mathcal{B} = \left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} \right\} \) diagonalizes \( A \), so \( [T]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \).  
8. Final answer: A basis \( \mathcal{B} \) for \( \mathbb{R}^2 \) such that \( [T]_{\mathcal{B}} \) is diagonal is \( \left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} \right\} \).
