1. Let \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \), and recall the characteristic polynomial is \( \det(A - \lambda I) \).  
2. Compute \( A - \lambda I = \begin{bmatrix} 2-\lambda & 1 \\ -1 & 4-\lambda \end{bmatrix} \).  
3. The determinant is \( (2-\lambda)(4-\lambda) - (1)(-1) \).  
4. Simplify: \( (2-\lambda)(4-\lambda) + 1 = 8 - 2\lambda - 4\lambda + \lambda^2 + 1 \).  
5. Combine like terms: \( \lambda^2 - 6\lambda + 9 \).  
6. The characteristic polynomial is \( \lambda^2 - 6\lambda + 9 \).  
7. Set the polynomial to zero to find eigenvalues: \( \lambda^2 - 6\lambda + 9 = 0 \).  
8. Factor: \( (\lambda - 3)^2 = 0 \).  
9. The eigenvalue is \( \lambda = 3 \) (with algebraic multiplicity 2).
