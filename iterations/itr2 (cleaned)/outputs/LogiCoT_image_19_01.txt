1. The problem asks for the matrix of the linear transformation \( T(\mathbf{x}) = A\mathbf{x} \) with respect to the basis \( \mathcal{B} = \{\mathbf{b}_1, \mathbf{b}_2\} \), where \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \), \( \mathbf{b}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \), and \( \mathbf{b}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} \); we will compute the \(\mathcal{B}\)-matrix for \(T\).
2. To find the \(\mathcal{B}\)-matrix, we need to find \([T]_{\mathcal{B}}\) such that \([T]_{\mathcal{B}} = P^{-1}AP\), where \(P\) is the change of basis matrix whose columns are \(\mathbf{b}_1\) and \(\mathbf{b}_2\); thus, \(P = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix}\).
3. Verify \(P\) is invertible: \(\det(P) = (1)(4) - (5)(1) = 4 - 5 = -1 \neq 0\), so \(P\) is invertible.
4. Compute \(P^{-1}\): For a \(2 \times 2\) matrix \(\begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{bmatrix}\), the inverse is \(\frac{1}{ad-bc}\begin{bmatrix} d & -b \\ -c & a \end{bmatrix}\); thus, \(P^{-1} = \frac{1}{-1}\begin{bmatrix} 4 & -5 \\ -1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -4 & 5 \\ 1 & -1 \end{bmatrix}\).
5. Compute \(AP\): \(A P = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} (1)(1)+(1)(1) & (1)(5)+(1)(4) \\ (-1)(1)+(3)(1) & (-1)(5)+(3)(4) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 9 \\ 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix}\).
6. Compute \(P^{-1}AP\): Multiply \(P^{-1}\) and \(AP\): \(\begin{bmatrix} -4 & 5 \\ 1 & -1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 9 \\ 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} (-4)(2)+(5)(2) & (-4)(9)+(5)(7) \\ (1)(2)+(-1)(2) & (1)(9)+(-1)(7) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -8+10 & -36+35 \\ 2-2 & 9-7 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 0 & 2 \end{bmatrix}\).
7. Therefore, the \(\mathcal{B}\)-matrix for \(T\) is \(\boxed{\begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 0 & 2 \end{bmatrix}}\).
