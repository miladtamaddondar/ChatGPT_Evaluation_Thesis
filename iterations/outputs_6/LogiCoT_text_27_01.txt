1. Since $T$ is linear and $T(\mathbf{e}_1) = \mathbf{y}_1$, $T(\mathbf{e}_2) = \mathbf{y}_2$, the standard matrix $A$ of $T$ has columns $\mathbf{y}_1$ and $\mathbf{y}_2$, so $A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 5 & 6 \end{bmatrix}$.
2. To find $T\left(\begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix}\right)$, write $\begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} = 5\mathbf{e}_1 + (-3)\mathbf{e}_2$, and use linearity: $T\left(\begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix}\right) = 5T(\mathbf{e}_1) + (-3)T(\mathbf{e}_2)$.
3. Substitute the given images: $5\mathbf{y}_1 + (-3)\mathbf{y}_2 = 5\begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix} - 3\begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 10 \\ 25 \end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} -3 \\ 18 \end{bmatrix}$
4. Add the vectors: $\begin{bmatrix} 10 \\ 25 \end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} -3 \\ 18 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 10 - (-3) \\ 25 - 18 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 13 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix}$.
5. Therefore, $T\left(\begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix}\right) = \begin{bmatrix} 13 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix}$.
6. For a general vector $\begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix}$, by linearity, $T\left(\begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix}\right) = x_1T(\mathbf{e}_1) + x_2T(\mathbf{e}_2) = x_1\mathbf{y}_1 + x_2\mathbf{y}_2$.
7. Substitute the values: $x_1\begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix} + x_2\begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2x_1 - x_2 \\ 5x_1 + 6x_2 \end{bmatrix}$.
8. Therefore, $T\left(\begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix}\right) = \begin{bmatrix} 2x_1 - x_2 \\ 5x_1 + 6x_2 \end{bmatrix}$.
