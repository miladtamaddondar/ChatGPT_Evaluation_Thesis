1. Identify the basis \(\mathcal{B}\) for \(\mathbb{P}_3\): The basis is \(\{1, 2t, -2 + 4t^2, -12t + 8t^3\}\). 
2. Express \(p(t) = 7 - 12t - 8t^2 + 12t^3\) as a linear combination of the basis elements: \(p(t) = a_1 \cdot 1 + a_2 \cdot 2t + a_3(-2 + 4t^2) + a_4(-12t + 8t^3)\). 
3. Expand the linear combination: \(a_1 \cdot 1 + a_2 \cdot 2t + a_3(-2) + a_3 \cdot 4t^2 + a_4(-12t) + a_4 \cdot 8t^3\). 
4. Group like terms: \((a_1 - 2a_3) + (2a_2 - 12a_4)t + 4a_3 t^2 + 8a_4 t^3\). 
5. Set up equations by matching coefficients with \(p(t)\): \(a_1 - 2a_3 = 7\), \(2a_2 - 12a_4 = -12\), \(4a_3 = -8\), \(8a_4 = 12\). 
6. Solve \(4a_3 = -8\) to get \(a_3 = -2\). 7. Solve \(8a_4 = 12\) to get \(a_4 = \frac{3}{2}\). 
8. Substitute \(a_3 = -2\) into \(a_1 - 2a_3 = 7\): \(a_1 - 2(-2) = 7 \implies a_1 + 4 = 7 \implies a_1 = 3\). 
9. Substitute \(a_4 = \frac{3}{2}\) into \(2a_2 - 12a_4 = -12\): \(2a_2 - 12 \cdot \frac{3}{2} = -12 \implies 2a_2 - 18 = -12 \implies 2a_2 = 6 \implies a_2 = 3\). 
10. The coordinate vector of \(p\) relative to \(\mathcal{B}\) is \(\begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ 3 \\ -2 \\ \frac{3}{2} \end{pmatrix}\).
