1. The characteristic polynomial of a matrix \( A \) is given by \( \det(A - \lambda I) \).  
2. Subtract \( \lambda \) from the diagonal entries: \( A - \lambda I = \begin{bmatrix}2-\lambda & 1 \\ -1 & 4-\lambda\end{bmatrix} \).  
3. Compute the determinant: \( (2-\lambda)(4-\lambda) - (1)(-1) = (2-\lambda)(4-\lambda) + 1 \).  
4. Expand \( (2-\lambda)(4-\lambda) \): \( 8 - 2\lambda - 4\lambda + \lambda^2 = \lambda^2 - 6\lambda + 8 \).  
5. Add 1 to get the characteristic polynomial: \( \lambda^2 - 6\lambda + 9 \).  
6. Set the characteristic polynomial equal to zero to find eigenvalues: \( \lambda^2 - 6\lambda + 9 = 0 \).  
7. Factor the quadratic: \( (\lambda - 3)^2 = 0 \).  
8. The eigenvalue is \( \lambda = 3 \) (with algebraic multiplicity 2).
