1. To find the characteristic polynomial of the matrix \(A = \begin{bmatrix}2 & 1 \\ -1 & 4 \end{bmatrix}\), we compute \(\det(A - \lambda I)\). 2. Subtract \(\lambda\) from the diagonal entries: \(A - \lambda I = \begin{bmatrix}2-\lambda & 1 \\ -1 & 4-\lambda \end{bmatrix}\). 3. Compute the determinant: \(\det(A - \lambda I) = (2-\lambda)(4-\lambda) - (1)(-1) = (2-\lambda)(4-\lambda) + 1\). 4. Expand \((2-\lambda)(4-\lambda)\): \(2 \times 4 - 2\lambda - 4\lambda + \lambda^2 = 8 - 6\lambda + \lambda^2\). 5. Add 1 to the expansion: \(8 - 6\lambda + \lambda^2 + 1 = \lambda^2 - 6\lambda + 9\). 6. The characteristic polynomial is \(\lambda^2 - 6\lambda + 9\). 7. To find the eigenvalues, set the characteristic polynomial to zero: \(\lambda^2 - 6\lambda + 9 = 0\). 8. Factor the quadratic: \((\lambda - 3)^2 = 0\). 9. The eigenvalues are \(\lambda = 3\) (with algebraic multiplicity 2).
