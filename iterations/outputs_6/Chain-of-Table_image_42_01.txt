1. Let \( \mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} \), \( \mathbf{u}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \), \( \mathbf{u}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix} \), \( \mathbf{u}_3 = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \); we seek \( \mathbf{y} = \mathbf{y}_W + \mathbf{y}_{W^\perp} \) where \( \mathbf{y}_W \in W = \text{span}\{\mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_2, \mathbf{u}_3\} \) and \( \mathbf{y}_{W^\perp} \perp W \).
2. Express \( \mathbf{y}_W = c_1 \mathbf{u}_1 + c_2 \mathbf{u}_2 + c_3 \mathbf{u}_3 \), so set up the equation: \( \mathbf{y} = c_1 \mathbf{u}_1 + c_2 \mathbf{u}_2 + c_3 \mathbf{u}_3 + \mathbf{y}_{W^\perp} \).
3. To find \( c_1, c_2, c_3 \), project \( \mathbf{y} \) onto the subspace spanned by \( \mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_2, \mathbf{u}_3 \) by solving the normal equations \( U^T U \mathbf{c} = U^T \mathbf{y} \), where \( U = [\mathbf{u}_1 \ \mathbf{u}_2 \ \mathbf{u}_3] \).
4. Compute \( U^T U \): \[ U^T U = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 & 1 & -2 \\ -1 & 0 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 15 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \]
5. Compute \( U^T \mathbf{y} \): \[ U^T \mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 & 1 & -2 \\ -1 & 0 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 + 3 + 0 - 1 = 6 \\ -4 + 9 + 3 + 2 = 10 \\ -4 + 0 + 3 - 1 = -2 \end{bmatrix} \]
6. Set up the linear system: \( \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 15 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} c_1 \\ c_2 \\ c_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 10 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix} \).
7. Row 2 gives \( 15c_2 = 10 \implies c_2 = \frac{2}{3} \).
8. Substitute \( c_2 \) into rows 1 and 3: Row 1: \( 3c_1 + c_3 = 6 \) Row 3: \( c_1 + 3c_3 = -2 \)
9. Solve the two equations: Multiply row 3 by 3: \( 3c_1 + 9c_3 = -6 \) Subtract row 1: \( (3c_1 + 9c_3) - (3c_1 + c_3) = -6 - 6 \implies 8c_3 = -12 \implies c_3 = -\frac{3}{2} \)
10. Substitute \( c_3 \) into row 1: \( 3c_1 - \frac{3}{2} = 6 \implies 3c_1 = 6 + \frac{3}{2} = \frac{15}{2} \implies c_1 = \frac{5}{2} \)
11. Thus, \( \mathbf{y}_W = \frac{5}{2} \mathbf{u}_1 + \frac{2}{3} \mathbf{u}_2 - \frac{3}{2} \mathbf{u}_3 \).
12. Compute \( \mathbf{y}_W = \frac{5}{2} \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} + \frac{2}{3} \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix} - \frac{3}{2} \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{5}{2} - \frac{2}{3} + \frac{3}{2} \\ \frac{5}{2} + 2 + 0 \\ 0 + \frac{2}{3} - \frac{3}{2} \\ \frac{5}{2} - \frac{4}{3} - \frac{3}{2} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{5}{2} + \frac{3}{2} - \frac{2}{3} = 4 - \frac{2}{3} = \frac{10}{3} \\ \frac{5}{2} + 2 = \frac{9}{2} \\ \frac{2}{3} - \frac{3}{2} = -\frac{5}{6} \\ \frac{5}{2} - \frac{4}{3} - \frac{3}{2} = ( \frac{5}{2} - \frac{3}{2} ) - \frac{4}{3} = 1 - \frac{4}{3} = -\frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} \)
13. Therefore, \( \mathbf{y}_W = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{10}{3} \\ \frac{9}{2} \\ -\frac{5}{6} \\ -\frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} \).
14. The orthogonal component is \( \mathbf{y}_{W^\perp} = \mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} \frac{10}{3} \\ \frac{9}{2} \\ -\frac{5}{6} \\ -\frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 - \frac{10}{3} \\ 3 - \frac{9}{2} \\ 3 + \frac{5}{6} \\ -1 + \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} \\ -\frac{3}{2} \\ \frac{23}{6} \\ -\frac{2}{3} \end{bmatrix} \)
15. Final answer: \( \boxed{ \mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{10}{3} \\ \frac{9}{2} \\ -\frac{5}{6} \\ -\frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} \\ -\frac{3}{2} \\ \frac{23}{6} \\ -\frac{2}{3} \end{bmatrix} } \), where the first vector is in \( W \) and the second is orthogonal to \( W \).
