1. The given matrix is \( A = \begin{bmatrix}1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ 3 & 9 & -1 & 5 \\ 2 & -6 & 4 & -3 \\ -4 & 12 & 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix} \).
2. The echelon form of \( A \) is \( \begin{bmatrix}1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 & -7 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \).
3. The pivot columns in the echelon form are columns 1, 3, and 4, so the basis for \( \text{Col } A \) is \( \left\{ \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 3 \\ 2 \\ -4\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}2 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 2\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}-4 \\ 5 \\ -3 \\ 7\end{bmatrix} \right\} \).
4. To find a basis for \( \text{Nul } A \), set up the equation \( A\vec{x} = \vec{0} \) and write the general solution using the free variable \( x_2 \).
5. From back substitution, \( x_4 = 0 \), \( 5x_3 - 7x_4 = 0 \implies x_3 = 0 \), \( x_1 - 3x_2 + 2x_3 - 4x_4 = 0 \implies x_1 = 3x_2 \).
6. So, the general solution is \( \vec{x} = x_2 \begin{bmatrix}3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0\end{bmatrix} \), so a basis for \( \text{Nul } A \) is \( \left\{ \begin{bmatrix}3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0\end{bmatrix} \right\} \).
7. The dimension of \( \text{Col } A \) is 3 and the dimension of \( \text{Nul } A \) is 1.
