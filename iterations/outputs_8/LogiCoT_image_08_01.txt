1. A matrix has rank 1 if all its rows (or columns) are scalar multiples of a single row (or column), so we can construct such a matrix by taking the outer product of a nonzero \(4 \times 1\) vector and a nonzero \(1 \times 3\) vector.  
2. Let \( \mathbf{u} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( \mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 6 & 7 \end{bmatrix} \), then their outer product is \( A = \mathbf{u} \mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 6 & 7 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 6 & 7 \\ 10 & 12 & 14 \\ 15 & 18 & 21 \\ 20 & 24 & 28 \end{bmatrix} \).  
3. To verify the rank, note that each row is a scalar multiple of the first row, e.g., the second row is \(2\) times the first, the third is \(3\) times the first, and the fourth is \(4\) times the first, so the rank is 1.  
4. Final answer: \( \boxed{\begin{bmatrix} 5 & 6 & 7 \\ 10 & 12 & 14 \\ 15 & 18 & 21 \\ 20 & 24 & 28 \end{bmatrix}} \) is a \(4 \times 3\) matrix with rank 1.
