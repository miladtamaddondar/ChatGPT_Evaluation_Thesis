1. The problem asks us to write \( A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix} \) in the form \( A = P C P^{-1} \), where \( C = \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix} \) and \( P \) is invertible.  
2. To do this, we first find the eigenvalues of \( A \) by solving \( \det(A - \lambda I) = 0 \): \( \det\left(\begin{pmatrix} 1-\lambda & -2 \\ 1 & 3-\lambda \end{pmatrix}\right) = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0 \).  
3. Solving \( \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0 \) gives \( \lambda = 2 \pm i \).  
4. Since the eigenvalues are complex conjugates, \( C \) will have the form \( \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \) because \( a = \text{Re}(\lambda) = 2 \) and \( b = \text{Im}(\lambda) = 1 \).  
5. Next, we find an eigenvector for \( \lambda = 2 + i \) by solving \( (A - (2+i)I)\vec{v} = 0 \): \( A - (2+i)I = \begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix} \).  
6. Setting up the system: \( (-1-i)x - 2y = 0 \) and \( x + (1-i)y = 0 \), from the first equation \( x = \frac{2}{-1-i}y \), rationalizing denominator gives \( x = \frac{2(-1+i)}{(-1-i)(-1+i)}y = \frac{2(-1+i)}{1+1}y = (-1+i)y \).  
7. Choosing \( y = 1 \), the eigenvector is \( \vec{v} = \begin{pmatrix} -1+i \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \).  
8. The complex conjugate eigenvector for \( \lambda = 2 - i \) is \( \vec{v}^* = \begin{pmatrix} -1-i \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \).  
9. To get a real invertible matrix \( P \), take the real and imaginary parts of \( \vec{v} \): \( \text{Re}(\vec{v}) = \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \), \( \text{Im}(\vec{v}) = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix} \), so \( P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \).  
10. Therefore, the answer is: \( P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \), \( C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \), and \( A = P C P^{-1} \).
