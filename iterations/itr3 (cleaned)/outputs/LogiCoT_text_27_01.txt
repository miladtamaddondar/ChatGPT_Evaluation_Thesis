1. The standard basis vectors are \(\mathbf{e}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}\) and \(\mathbf{e}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}\), and the transformation \(T\) sends \(\mathbf{e}_1 \mapsto \mathbf{y}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix}\) and \(\mathbf{e}_2 \mapsto \mathbf{y}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix}\). 
2. Since \(T\) is linear, its matrix with respect to the standard basis is \(A = [\mathbf{y}_1 \ \mathbf{y}_2] = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 5 & 6 \end{bmatrix}\). 
3. To verify, \(A\mathbf{e}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 5 & 6 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix} = \mathbf{y}_1\) and \(A\mathbf{e}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 5 & 6 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix} = \mathbf{y}_2\), confirming correctness. 
4. The image of \(\begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix}\) is \(A \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} = 5\mathbf{y}_1 + (-3)\mathbf{y}_2 = 5\begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix} - 3\begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 10 \\ 25 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ -18 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 13 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix}\). 
5. The image of \(\begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix}\) is \(A \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} = x_1\mathbf{y}_1 + x_2\mathbf{y}_2 = x_1\begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix} + x_2\begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2x_1 - x_2 \\ 5x_1 + 6x_2 \end{bmatrix}\). 
6. Final answers: \(T\left(\begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix}\right) = \begin{bmatrix} 13 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix}\) and \(T\left(\begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix}\right) = \begin{bmatrix} 2x_1 - x_2 \\ 5x_1 + 6x_2 \end{bmatrix}\).
