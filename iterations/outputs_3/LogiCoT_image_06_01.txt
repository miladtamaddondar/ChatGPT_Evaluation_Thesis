1. To find a \(2 \times 2\) matrix \(B\) such that \(AB = 0\), each column of \(B\) must be in the null space of \(A\). 
2. Find the null space of \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & -6 \\ -1 & 2 \end{bmatrix}\) by solving \(A\mathbf{x} = 0\), i.e., \(\begin{bmatrix} 3 & -6 \\ -1 & 2 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}\). 
3. This gives the system: \(3x_1 - 6x_2 = 0\) and \(-x_1 + 2x_2 = 0\). 
4. Both equations are equivalent; solve \(-x_1 + 2x_2 = 0 \implies x_1 = 2x_2\). 
5. Thus, the null space is all vectors of the form \(\begin{bmatrix} 2t \\ t \end{bmatrix}\) for any \(t \neq 0\). 
6. Choose two different nonzero values for \(t\), say \(t=1\) and \(t=-1\), to get two different nonzero columns: \(\begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}\) and \(\begin{bmatrix} -2 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix}\). 
7. Construct \(B = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -2 \\ 1 & -1 \end{bmatrix}\). 
8. Verify: \(AB = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & -6 \\ -1 & 2 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -2 \\ 1 & -1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3(2) + (-6)(1) & 3(-2) + (-6)(-1) \\ -1(2) + 2(1) & -1(-2) + 2(-1) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 - 6 & -6 + 6 \\ -2 + 2 & 2 - 2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\). 
9. Thus, the correct answer is \(B = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -2 \\ 1 & -1 \end{bmatrix}\).
