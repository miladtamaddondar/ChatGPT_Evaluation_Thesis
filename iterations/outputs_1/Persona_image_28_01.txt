1. The linear transformation \( T \) maps \( \mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} \) to \( x_1\mathbf{v}_1 + x_2\mathbf{v}_2 \), where \( \mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} -2 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( \mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 7 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} \).  
2. We want to find a matrix \( A \) such that \( T(\mathbf{x}) = A\mathbf{x} \) for all \( \mathbf{x} \).  
3. By linearity, \( T(\mathbf{x}) = x_1\mathbf{v}_1 + x_2\mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} -2 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix} x_1 + \begin{bmatrix} 7 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} x_2 = \begin{bmatrix} -2x_1 + 7x_2 \\ 5x_1 - 3x_2 \end{bmatrix} \).  
4. We can write this as a matrix multiplication: \( \begin{bmatrix} -2x_1 + 7x_2 \\ 5x_1 - 3x_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -2 & 7 \\ 5 & -3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} \).  
5. Therefore, the matrix \( A \) such that \( T(\mathbf{x}) = A\mathbf{x} \) is \( \boxed{A = \begin{bmatrix} -2 & 7 \\ 5 & -3 \end{bmatrix}} \).
