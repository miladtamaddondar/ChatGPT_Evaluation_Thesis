1. We are given \(\mathbf{u} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}\) and need to find \(\mathbf{v} \in \mathbb{R}^3\) such that \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 4 \\ 2 & -6 & 8 \end{bmatrix} = \mathbf{u} \mathbf{v}^T\). 
2. The outer product \(\mathbf{u} \mathbf{v}^T\) gives a \(2 \times 3\) matrix: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} v_1 & v_2 & v_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \cdot v_1 & 1 \cdot v_2 & 1 \cdot v_3 \\ 2 \cdot v_1 & 2 \cdot v_2 & 2 \cdot v_3 \end{bmatrix}\). 
3. Set up equations by matching entries: \(1 \cdot v_1 = 1\), \(1 \cdot v_2 = -3\), \(1 \cdot v_3 = 4\), \(2 \cdot v_1 = 2\), \(2 \cdot v_2 = -6\), \(2 \cdot v_3 = 8\). 
4. Solve for \(v_1\): \(v_1 = 1\). 
5. Solve for \(v_2\): \(v_2 = -3\). 
6. Solve for \(v_3\): \(v_3 = 4\). 
7. The solution is \(\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix}\).
