1. The standard basis for \( \mathbb{P}_2 \) is \( \{1, t, t^2\} \).  
2. Write each basis vector in \( \mathcal{B} \) in terms of the standard basis: \( 1 - 3t^2 = 1 \cdot 1 + 0 \cdot t + (-3) \cdot t^2 \), \( 2 + t - 5t^2 = 2 \cdot 1 + 1 \cdot t + (-5) \cdot t^2 \), \( 1 + 2t = 1 \cdot 1 + 2 \cdot t + 0 \cdot t^2 \).
3. Form the change-of-coordinates matrix \( P \) by placing these coordinate vectors as columns: \( P = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 \\ -3 & -5 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \).
4. To write \( t^2 \) as a linear combination of the \( \mathcal{B} \) polynomials, set \( t^2 = a(1 - 3t^2) + b(2 + t - 5t^2) + c(1 + 2t) \).
5. Expand and collect coefficients: \( t^2 = (a + 2b + c) \cdot 1 + (b + 2c) \cdot t + (-3a - 5b) \cdot t^2 \).
6. Set up the system by matching coefficients: \( a + 2b + c = 0 \), \( b + 2c = 0 \), \( -3a - 5b = 1 \).
7. Solve \( b + 2c = 0 \implies b = -2c \).
8. Substitute \( b = -2c \) into \( a + 2b + c = 0 \): \( a + 2(-2c) + c = 0 \implies a - 4c + c = 0 \implies a = 3c \).
9. Substitute \( a = 3c \), \( b = -2c \) into \( -3a - 5b = 1 \): \( -3(3c) - 5(-2c) = 1 \implies -9c + 10c = 1 \implies c = 1 \).
10. Then \( a = 3c = 3 \), \( b = -2c = -2 \), \( c = 1 \).
11. Therefore, \( t^2 = 3(1 - 3t^2) - 2(2 + t - 5t^2) + 1(1 + 2t) \) in terms of the basis \( \mathcal{B} \).
