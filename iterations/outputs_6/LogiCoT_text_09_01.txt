1. Write the vectors as columns of a matrix \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ -3 & 9 & -1 & 5 \\ 2 & -6 & 4 & -3 \\ -4 & 12 & 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix}\) to analyze their linear dependence.  
2. Perform row reduction (Gaussian elimination) on \(A\) to find its rank and a basis: Subtract \(-3\) times row 1 from row 2, \(-6\) times row 1 from row 3, and \(-4\) times row 1 from row 4.  
3. Row 2: \(-3 - (-3 \times 1) = 0\), \(9 - (-3 \times -3) = 0\), \(-1 - (-3 \times 2) = 5\), \(5 - (-3 \times -4) = -7\). Row 3: \(2 - (2 \times 1) = 0\), \(-6 - (2 \times -3) = 0\), \(4 - (2 \times 2) = 0\), \(-3 - (2 \times -4) = 5\). Row 4: \(-4 - (-4 \times 1) = 0\), \(12 - (-4 \times -3) = 0\), \(2 - (-4 \times 2) = 10\), \(7 - (-4 \times -4) = -9\).  
4. The new matrix is \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 & -7 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 10 & -9 \end{bmatrix}\); verify correctness by checking arithmetic in each entry.  
5. Subtract 2 times row 3 from row 4: \(10 - 2 \times 0 = 10\), \(-9 - 2 \times 5 = -19\); so row 4 becomes \([0, 0, 10, -19]\). But since row 3 is \([0, 0, 0, 5]\), subtracting twice row 3 from row 4 only affects the last entry: \(10 - 2 \times 0 = 10\), \(-9 - 2 \times 5 = -19\), so row 4 is correct.  
6. Now, notice row 4 is a linear combination of rows 2 and 3: \(10 = 2 \times 5\), \(-19 = 2 \times -7 + 5\). Thus, row 4 is dependent on rows 2 and 3.  
7. The nonzero rows after row reduction are: row 1 \([1, -3, 2, -4]\), row 2 \([0, 0, 5, -7]\), row 3 \([0, 0, 0, 5]\); so the rank is 3.  
8. Thus, a basis for the subspace is \(\left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -6 \\ 4 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} -4 \\ 12 \\ 2 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \right\}\), corresponding to the pivot columns.  
9. The dimension of the subspace is 3.
