1. To find a \( 2 \times 2 \) matrix \( B \) such that \( AB = 0 \), we need each column of \( B \) to be in the null space of \( A \). 
2. Let \( B = \begin{bmatrix} b_1 & b_2 \end{bmatrix} \), where \( b_1 \) and \( b_2 \) are \( 2 \times 1 \) vectors in the null space of \( A \). 
3. To find the null space, solve \( A x = 0 \) where \( x = \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} \): \( \begin{bmatrix} 3 & -6 \\ -1 & 2 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \). 
4. This gives the equations: \( 3x_1 - 6x_2 = 0 \) and \( -x_1 + 2x_2 = 0 \). 
5. Both equations are equivalent, so \( x_1 = 2x_2 \). 
6. The general solution is \( x = x_2 \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \), so any nonzero scalar multiple of \( \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \) is in the null space. 
7. To use two different nonzero columns, pick two different nonzero scalars, say 1 and -3, so \( b_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \), \( b_2 = \begin{bmatrix} -6 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} \). 8. Thus, \( B = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -6 \\ 1 & -3 \end{bmatrix} \) is a \( 2 \times 2 \) matrix with two different nonzero columns in the null space of \( A \), so \( AB = 0 \).
