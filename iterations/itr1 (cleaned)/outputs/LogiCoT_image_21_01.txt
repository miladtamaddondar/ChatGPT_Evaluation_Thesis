1. Write the system as \(A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{b}\): \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -2 \\ -2 & 1 & 6 \\ 3 & -2 & -5 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 7 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix}\). 
2. Check if the solution is unique by computing \(\det(A)\): \(\det(A) = 1\left(1 \cdot -5 - 6 \cdot -2\right) - 0\left(-2 \cdot -5 - 6 \cdot 3\right) + (-2)\left(-2 \cdot -2 - 1 \cdot 3\right) = 1(-5 + 12) - 0(10 - 18) - 2(4 - 3) = 1(7) - 0 - 2(1) = 7 - 2 = 5\). 
3. Since \(\det(A) \neq 0\), the matrix is invertible and the solution is unique by the Invertible Matrix Theorem. 
4. Find \(\mathbf{x} = A^{-1}\mathbf{b}\); first, compute \(A^{-1}\) (using the formula for the inverse of a 3x3 matrix or row reduction, but here we will use Cramer's Rule for each variable): 
5. For \(x_1\), replace the first column of \(A\) with \(\mathbf{b}\): \(A_1 = \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 0 & -2 \\ 7 & 1 & 6 \\ -3 & -2 & -5 \end{bmatrix}\), so \(\det(A_1) = -1(1 \cdot -5 - 6 \cdot -2) - 0(7 \cdot -5 - 6 \cdot -3) + (-2)(7 \cdot -2 - 1 \cdot -3) = -1(-5 + 12) - 0(-35 + 18) - 2(-14 + 3) = -1(7) - 0 - 2(-11) = -7 + 22 = 15\), thus \(x_1 = \frac{15}{5} = 3\). 
6. For \(x_2\), replace the second column of \(A\) with \(\mathbf{b}\): \(A_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -2 \\ -2 & 7 & 6 \\ 3 & -3 & -5 \end{bmatrix}\), so \(\det(A_2) = 1(7 \cdot -5 - 6 \cdot -3) - (-1)(-2 \cdot -5 - 6 \cdot 3) + (-2)(-2 \cdot -3 - 7 \cdot 3) = 1(-35 + 18) + 1(10 - 18) - 2(6 - 21) = 1(-17) + 1(-8) - 2(-15) = -17 - 8 + 30 = 5\), thus \(x_2 = \frac{5}{5} = 1\). 
7. For \(x_3\), replace the third column of \(A\) with \(\mathbf{b}\): \(A_3 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -1 \\ -2 & 1 & 7 \\ 3 & -2 & -3 \end{bmatrix}\), so \(\det(A_3) = 1(1 \cdot -3 - 7 \cdot -2) - 0(-2 \cdot -3 - 7 \cdot 3) + (-1)(-2 \cdot -2 - 1 \cdot 3) = 1(-3 + 14) - 0(6 - 21) - 1(4 - 3) = 1(11) - 0 - 1(1) = 11 - 1 = 10\), thus \(x_3 = \frac{10}{5} = 2\). 
8. The unique solution is \(\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}\).
