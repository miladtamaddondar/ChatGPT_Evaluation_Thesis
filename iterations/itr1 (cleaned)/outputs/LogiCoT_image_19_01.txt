1. The problem asks for the matrix of the linear transformation \( T(\mathbf{x}) = A\mathbf{x} \) with respect to the basis \( \mathcal{B} = \{\mathbf{b}_1, \mathbf{b}_2\} \), where \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \), \( \mathbf{b}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \), and \( \mathbf{b}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} \); this requires us to find the matrix \( [T]_{\mathcal{B}} \). 
2. To find \( [T]_{\mathcal{B}} \), we compute \( T(\mathbf{b}_1) = A\mathbf{b}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \). 
3. Next, write \( \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \) as a linear combination of \( \mathbf{b}_1 \) and \( \mathbf{b}_2 \): let \( \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} = c_1 \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} + c_2 \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} \), so \( \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} c_1 + 5c_2 \\ c_1 + 4c_2 \end{bmatrix} \). 
4. Set up the system: \( c_1 + 5c_2 = 2 \), \( c_1 + 4c_2 = 2 \); subtract to get \( c_2 = 0 \), so \( c_1 = 2 \), thus \( [T(\mathbf{b}_1)]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \). 
5. Now compute \( T(\mathbf{b}_2) = A\mathbf{b}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 9 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \). 
6. Write \( \begin{bmatrix} 9 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} = d_1 \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} + d_2 \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} d_1 + 5d_2 \\ d_1 + 4d_2 \end{bmatrix} \); set up the system: \( d_1 + 5d_2 = 9 \), \( d_1 + 4d_2 = 7 \). 
7. Subtract to get \( d_2 = 2 \), so \( d_1 = 9 - 10 = -1 \), thus \( [T(\mathbf{b}_2)]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \). 
8. The matrix of \( T \) in the \( \mathcal{B} \)-basis is \( [T]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 0 & 2 \end{bmatrix} \).
