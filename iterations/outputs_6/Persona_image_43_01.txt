1. Write \( U \) as a \( 3 \times 2 \) matrix: \( U = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3} & -\frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{1}{3} & \frac{2}{3} \\ \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} \).
2. Compute \( U^T U \) by multiplying \( U^T \) (\( 2 \times 3 \)) and \( U \) (\( 3 \times 2 \)): \( U^T U = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \).
3. Compute \( U U^T \) by multiplying \( U \) (\( 3 \times 2 \)) and \( U^T \) (\( 2 \times 3 \)): \( U U^T = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{8}{9} & -\frac{1}{9} & \frac{2}{9} \\ -\frac{1}{9} & \frac{5}{9} & \frac{4}{9} \\ \frac{2}{9} & \frac{4}{9} & \frac{5}{9} \end{bmatrix} \).
4. The projection matrix onto \( W \) is \( U U^T \), so \( \mathrm{proj}_W \mathbf{y} = (U U^T)\mathbf{y} \).
5. Compute \( (U U^T)\mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{8}{9} & -\frac{1}{9} & \frac{2}{9} \\ -\frac{1}{9} & \frac{5}{9} & \frac{4}{9} \\ \frac{2}{9} & \frac{4}{9} & \frac{5}{9} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 8 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 6 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix} \).

The answer is: \( U^T U = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \), \( U U^T = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{8}{9} & -\frac{1}{9} & \frac{2}{9} \\ -\frac{1}{9} & \frac{5}{9} & \frac{4}{9} \\ \frac{2}{9} & \frac{4}{9} & \frac{5}{9} \end{bmatrix} \), \( \mathrm{proj}_W \mathbf{y} = (U U^T)\mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 6 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix} \).
