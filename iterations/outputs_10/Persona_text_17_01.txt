1. To find $T(p)$ for $p(t) = 5 + 3t$, evaluate $p$ at $-1$, $0$, and $1$: $p(-1) = 5 + 3(-1) = 2$, $p(0) = 5 + 3(0) = 5$, $p(1) = 5 + 3(1) = 8$.
2. Therefore, $T(5 + 3t) = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 5 \\ 8 \end{bmatrix}$.
3. To find the matrix of $T$ relative to the basis $\{1, t, t^2\}$, compute $T(1)$, $T(t)$, and $T(t^2)$: $T(1) = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}$, $T(t) = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}$, $T(t^2) = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}$.
4. Place these as columns in the matrix: the matrix for $T$ is $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 1 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$.
