1. Set up the equation $A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{b}$, where $A = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 & -2 \\ -2 & 1 & 6 \\ 3 & -2 & -5 \end{bmatrix}$ and $\mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 7 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix}$, so we need to solve $\begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 & -2 \\ -2 & 1 & 6 \\ 3 & -2 & -5 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 7 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix}$.
2. Write the system as equations: $1x_1 + 0x_2 - 2x_3 = -1$, $-2x_1 + 1x_2 + 6x_3 = 7$, $3x_1 - 2x_2 - 5x_3 = -3$.
3. Express $x_1$ in terms of $x_3$ from the first equation: $x_1 - 2x_3 = -1 \implies x_1 = -1 + 2x_3$.
4. Substitute $x_1$ into the second equation: $-2(-1 + 2x_3) + x_2 + 6x_3 = 7 \implies 2 - 4x_3 + x_2 + 6x_3 = 7 \implies x_2 + 2x_3 = 5 \implies x_2 = 5 - 2x_3$.
5. Substitute $x_1$ and $x_2$ into the third equation: $3(-1 + 2x_3) - 2(5 - 2x_3) - 5x_3 = -3 \implies -3 + 6x_3 - 10 + 4x_3 - 5x_3 = -3 \implies (-3 - 10) + (6x_3 + 4x_3 - 5x_3) = -3 \implies -13 + 5x_3 = -3$.
6. Solve for $x_3$: $-13 + 5x_3 = -3 \implies 5x_3 = 10 \implies x_3 = 2$.
7. Substitute $x_3 = 2$ back into previous expressions: $x_1 = -1 + 2(2) = -1 + 4 = 3$, $x_2 = 5 - 2(2) = 5 - 4 = 1$.
8. The solution is $\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}$; to check uniqueness, compute $\det(A)$: $\det(A) = 1\begin{vmatrix}1 & 6 \\ -2 & -5\end{vmatrix} - 0\begin{vmatrix}-2 & 6 \\ 3 & -5\end{vmatrix} + (-2)\begin{vmatrix}-2 & 1 \\ 3 & -2\end{vmatrix} = 1[(1)(-5)-(6)(-2)] - 0 + (-2)[(-2)(-2)-(1)(3)] = 1[-5+12] + (-2)[4-3] = 1[7] + (-2)[1] = 7 - 2 = 5$.
9. Since $\det(A) \neq 0$, $A$ is invertible, so the solution $\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}$ is unique.
