1. Find a basis for the subspace \( W \) spanned by \( \mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_2, \mathbf{u}_3 \) by checking their linear independence using the matrix \( U = [\mathbf{u}_1\ \mathbf{u}_2\ \mathbf{u}_3] = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \) and row reducing to see if the columns are linearly independent. 
2. Row reduce \( U \): Subtract column 1 from column 2 and column 3, giving \( \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -2 & -2 \\ 1 & 2 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -3 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \), then subtract row 1 from row 2 and row 4, yielding \( \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -2 & -2 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 0 & -1 & 2 \end{bmatrix} \), showing the columns are linearly independent since there are 3 pivots. 
3. Since the three vectors are linearly independent, they form a basis for \( W \), so \( W = \text{span}\{\mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_2, \mathbf{u}_3\} \) is 3-dimensional. 
4. To write \( \mathbf{y} \) as the sum of a vector in \( W \) and a vector orthogonal to \( W \), find the orthogonal projection of \( \mathbf{y} \) onto \( W \) using the formula \( \mathbf{y}_W = U(U^T U)^{-1}U^T \mathbf{y} \). 
5. Compute \( U^T U = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 & 1 & -2 \\ -1 & 0 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 15 & 4 \\ 1 & 4 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \). 
6. Compute \( U^T \mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 & 1 & -2 \\ -1 & 0 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 + 3 + 0 - 1 = 6 \\ -4 + 9 + 3 + 2 = 10 \\ -4 + 0 + 3 - 1 = -2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 10 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix} \). 
7. Solve \( (U^T U)\mathbf{c} = U^T \mathbf{y} \) for \( \mathbf{c} \): \( \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 15 & 4 \\ 1 & 4 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} c_1 \\ c_2 \\ c_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 10 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix} \). 
8. Solve for \( c_1 \): From the first equation, \( 3c_1 + c_3 = 6 \implies c_3 = 6 - 3c_1 \). 
9. Substitute \( c_3 \) into the third equation: \( c_1 + 4c_2 + 4c_3 = -2 \implies c_1 + 4c_2 + 4(6 - 3c_1) = -2 \implies c_1 + 4c_2 + 24 - 12c_1 = -2 \implies -11c_1 + 4c_2 = -26 \). 
10. From the second equation: \( 15c_2 + 4c_3 = 10 \implies 15c_2 + 4(6 - 3c_1) = 10 \implies 15c_2 + 24 - 12c_1 = 10 \implies 15c_2 - 12c_1 = -14 \). 
11. Now solve the two equations: \( -11c_1 + 4c_2 = -26 \) and \( -12c_1 + 15c_2 = -14 \). 
12. Multiply the first equation by 3 to align \( c_2 \): \( -33c_1 + 12c_2 = -78 \), add to the second equation: \( (-33c_1 + 12c_2) + (-12c_1 + 15c_2) = -78 + (-14) \implies -45c_1 + 27c_2 = -92 \). 
13. Divide by 9: \( -5c_1 + 3c_2 = -\frac{92}{9} \implies 3c_2 = -\frac{92}{9} + 5c_1 \implies c_2 = \frac{5c_1}{3} - \frac{92}{27} \). 
14. Substitute \( c_2 \) into the first equation: \( -11c_1 + 4\left(\frac{5c_1}{3} - \frac{92}{27}\right) = -26 \implies -11c_1 + \frac{20c_1}{3} - \frac{368}{27} = -26 \implies (-11 + \frac{20}{3})c_1 = -26 + \frac{368}{27} \implies (-\frac{13}{3})c_1 = -\frac{702}{27} + \frac{368}{27} = -\frac{334}{27} \implies c_1 = \frac{334}{27} \cdot \frac{3}{13} = \frac{334 \times 3}{27 \times 13} = \frac{1002}{351} \). 
15. Now \( c_2 = \frac{5c_1}{3} - \frac{92}{27} = \frac{5 \times 1002}{3 \times 351} - \frac{92}{27} = \frac{5010}{1053} - \frac{92}{27} \), convert to common denominator: \( \frac{5010 \times 27 - 92 \times 1053}{1053 \times 27} = \frac{135270 - 96876}{28431} = \frac{38494}{28431} \). 
16. \( c_3 = 6 - 3c_1 = 6 - 3 \times \frac{1002}{351} = 6 - \frac{3006}{351} = \frac{2106 - 3006}{351} = -\frac{900}{351} = -\frac{300}{117} \). 
17. The projection is \( \mathbf{y}_W = c_1 \mathbf{u}_1 + c_2 \mathbf{u}_2 + c_3 \mathbf{u}_3 = \frac{1002}{351} \mathbf{u}_1 + \frac{38494}{28431} \mathbf{u}_2 - \frac{300}{117} \mathbf{u}_3 \). 
18. The orthogonal component is \( \mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W \). 19. Therefore, \( \mathbf{y} = \mathbf{y}_W + (\mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W) \) where \( \mathbf{y}_W \in W \) and \( \mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W \) is orthogonal to \( W \).
