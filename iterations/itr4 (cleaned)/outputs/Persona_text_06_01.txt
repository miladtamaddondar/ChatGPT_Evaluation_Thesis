1. To find a \( 2 \times 2 \) matrix \( B \) such that \( AB = 0 \), we need each column of \( B \) to be in the null space of \( A \), i.e., for any vector \( x \), \( Ax = 0 \).  
2. Set up the equation \( A x = 0 \) where \( x = \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} \), so \( \begin{bmatrix} 3 & -6 \\ 1 & -2 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3x_1 - 6x_2 \\ x_1 - 2x_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \).  
3. Solve the system: From the second equation, \( x_1 - 2x_2 = 0 \implies x_1 = 2x_2 \); substitute into the first equation: \( 3(2x_2) - 6x_2 = 6x_2 - 6x_2 = 0 \), which is always true.  
4. Therefore, the null space consists of all scalar multiples of \( \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \), so any nonzero vector of this form is a valid column for \( B \).  
5. Choose two different nonzero vectors: let the first column be \( \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \) and the second column be \( \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \) (which is another nonzero multiple).  
6. Therefore, \( B = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 4 \\ 1 & 2 \end{bmatrix} \) is a \( 2 \times 2 \) matrix with two different nonzero columns in the null space of \( A \).  
7. Final answer: \( B = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 4 \\ 1 & 2 \end{bmatrix} \).
