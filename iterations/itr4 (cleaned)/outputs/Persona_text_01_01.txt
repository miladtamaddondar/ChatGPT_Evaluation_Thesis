1. To find the inverse of a 2x2 matrix \(\begin{bmatrix}a & b \\ c & d\end{bmatrix}\), use the formula: \(\frac{1}{ad-bc}\begin{bmatrix}d & -b \\ -c & a\end{bmatrix}\). 
2. For the given matrix \(\begin{bmatrix}8 & 6 \\ 5 & 4\end{bmatrix}\), compute the determinant: \(ad - bc = 8 \times 4 - 6 \times 5 = 32 - 30 = 2\). 
3. Swap the diagonal elements and change the signs of the off-diagonal elements to get: \(\begin{bmatrix}4 & -6 \\ -5 & 8\end{bmatrix}\). 
4. Multiply by the reciprocal of the determinant: \(\frac{1}{2}\begin{bmatrix}4 & -6 \\ -5 & 8\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}2 & -3 \\ -2.5 & 4\end{bmatrix}\). 
5. Therefore, the inverse of \(\begin{bmatrix}8 & 6 \\ 5 & 4\end{bmatrix}\) is \(\begin{bmatrix}2 & -3 \\ -2.5 & 4\end{bmatrix}\).
