1. Write down the given matrices: \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 5 \\ -3 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\), \(B = \begin{bmatrix} 4 & -5 \\ 3 & k \end{bmatrix}\); verify that both are \(2 \times 2\) matrices, so multiplication is defined.  
2. Compute \(AB\): \(AB = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 5 \\ -3 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 & -5 \\ 3 & k \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \times 4 + 5 \times 3 & 2 \times (-5) + 5 \times k \\ -3 \times 4 + 1 \times 3 & -3 \times (-5) + 1 \times k \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 8 + 15 & -10 + 5k \\ -12 + 3 & 15 + k \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 23 & -10 + 5k \\ -9 & 15 + k \end{bmatrix}\); verify by direct multiplication.  
3. Compute \(BA\): \(BA = \begin{bmatrix} 4 & -5 \\ 3 & k \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 5 \\ -3 & 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \times 2 + (-5) \times (-3) & 4 \times 5 + (-5) \times 1 \\ 3 \times 2 + k \times (-3) & 3 \times 5 + k \times 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 8 + 15 & 20 - 5 \\ 6 - 3k & 15 + k \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 23 & 15 \\ 6 - 3k & 15 + k \end{bmatrix}\); verify by direct multiplication.  
4. Set \(AB = BA\) and equate corresponding entries: \(\begin{bmatrix} 23 & -10 + 5k \\ -9 & 15 + k \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 23 & 15 \\ 6 - 3k & 15 + k \end{bmatrix}\); verify that equality of matrices requires equality of all corresponding entries.  
5. Equate the (1,2) entries: \(-10 + 5k = 15\) so \(5k = 25\) thus \(k = 5\); verify by substituting \(k = 5\) into the equation.  
6. Equate the (2,1) entries: \(-9 = 6 - 3k\) so \(-9 - 6 = -3k\) thus \(-15 = -3k\) so \(k = 5\); verify by substituting \(k = 5\) into the equation.  
7. Equate the (2,2) entries: \(15 + k = 15 + k\) which is always true; verify that this does not restrict \(k\).  
8. Since all equations are satisfied only when \(k = 5\), the value of \(k\) that makes \(AB = BA\) is \(\boxed{5}\); verify by substituting \(k = 5\) into both products and confirming equality.
