1. To find the eigenvalues, we solve $\det(A - \lambda I) = 0$ where $A = \begin{bmatrix}0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 2 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$ and $I$ is the $3 \times 3$ identity matrix.  
2. Compute $A - \lambda I = \begin{bmatrix}0-\lambda & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 2-\lambda & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 1-\lambda \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}-\lambda & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 2-\lambda & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 1-\lambda \end{bmatrix}$.  
3. The determinant of this upper triangular matrix is the product of its diagonal entries: $\det(A - \lambda I) = (-\lambda)(2-\lambda)(1-\lambda)$.  
4. Set the determinant equal to zero to find the eigenvalues: $(-\lambda)(2-\lambda)(1-\lambda) = 0$.  
5. Solve for $\lambda$: $\lambda_1 = 0$, $\lambda_2 = 2$, $\lambda_3 = 1$.  
6. The eigenvalues of the matrix are $\boxed{0,\ 1,\ 2}$.
