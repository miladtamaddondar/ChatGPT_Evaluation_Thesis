4. Solve the quadratic: \( \lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3 = 0 \) gives \( \lambda = \frac{1.3 \pm \sqrt{1.69 - 1.2}}{2} = \frac{1.3 \pm 0.7}{2} \), so \( \lambda_1 = 1 \), \( \lambda_2 = 0.3 \).

Since \( \mathbf{v}_1 \) is already given and is associated with \( \lambda_1 = 1 \), find \( \mathbf{v}_2 \) for \( \lambda_2 = 0.3 \) by solving \( (A - 0.3I)\mathbf{v}_2 = 0 \): \( \begin{bmatrix} 0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \).  
6. The system reduces to \( 0.3x + 0.3y = 0 \implies x + y = 0 \), so \( \mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} \) (any nonzero scalar multiple is valid).

8. Final answer: A basis for \( \mathbb{R}^2 \) consisting of eigenvectors of \( A \) is \( \left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 3/7 \\ 4/7 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} \right\} \).
