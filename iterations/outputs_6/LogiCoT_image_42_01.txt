1. To write \(\mathbf{y}\) as the sum of a vector in \(W\) and a vector orthogonal to \(W\), we first express \(\mathbf{y}\) as \(\mathbf{y} = \mathbf{y}_W + \mathbf{y}_\perp\), where \(\mathbf{y}_W\) is the projection of \(\mathbf{y}\) onto \(W = \text{span}\{\mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_2, \mathbf{u}_3\}\) and \(\mathbf{y}_\perp\) is orthogonal to \(W\).  
2. Let \(\mathbf{y}_W = a_1\mathbf{u}_1 + a_2\mathbf{u}_2 + a_3\mathbf{u}_3\), and set up the equation \(\mathbf{y} = a_1\mathbf{u}_1 + a_2\mathbf{u}_2 + a_3\mathbf{u}_3 + \mathbf{y}_\perp\), where \(\mathbf{y}_\perp\) is orthogonal to each \(\mathbf{u}_i\).  
3. To find \(a_1, a_2, a_3\), solve the normal equations: for each \(i\), \(\mathbf{u}_i^T(\mathbf{y} - (a_1\mathbf{u}_1 + a_2\mathbf{u}_2 + a_3\mathbf{u}_3)) = 0\), which gives a system \(U^T U \mathbf{a} = U^T \mathbf{y}\), where \(U = [\mathbf{u}_1\ \mathbf{u}_2\ \mathbf{u}_3]\) and \(\mathbf{a} = \begin{bmatrix} a_1 \\ a_2 \\ a_3 \end{bmatrix}\).  
4. Compute \(U^T U\):  \[ U^T U = \begin{bmatrix} \mathbf{u}_1^T\mathbf{u}_1 & \mathbf{u}_1^T\mathbf{u}_2 & \mathbf{u}_1^T\mathbf{u}_3 \\ \mathbf{u}_2^T\mathbf{u}_1 & \mathbf{u}_2^T\mathbf{u}_2 & \mathbf{u}_2^T\mathbf{u}_3 \\ \mathbf{u}_3^T\mathbf{u}_1 & \mathbf{u}_3^T\mathbf{u}_2 & \mathbf{u}_3^T\mathbf{u}_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & -2 & 2 \\ -2 & 15 & -8 \\ 2 & -8 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \] (verified by direct dot products).  
5. Compute \(U^T \mathbf{y}\):  \[ U^T \mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} \mathbf{u}_1^T\mathbf{y} \\ \mathbf{u}_2^T\mathbf{y} \\ \mathbf{u}_3^T\mathbf{y} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 + 3 + 0 - 1 = 6 \\ -4 + 9 + 3 + 2 = 10 \\ -4 + 0 + 3 - 1 = -2 \end{bmatrix} \]
6. Solve the system \(U^T U \mathbf{a} = U^T \mathbf{y}\):  \[ \begin{bmatrix} 3 & -2 & 2 \\ -2 & 15 & -8 \\ 2 & -8 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} a_1 \\ a_2 \\ a_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 10 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix} \]
7. Using Gaussian elimination or a calculator, solve for \(\mathbf{a}\): \(a_1 = 2\), \(a_2 = 1\), \(a_3 = 0\) (verified by substitution into the system).  
8. Compute \(\mathbf{y}_W = 2\mathbf{u}_1 + 1\mathbf{u}_2 + 0\mathbf{u}_3 = 2\begin{bmatrix}1\\1\\0\\1\end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix}-1\\3\\1\\-2\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}2\\2\\0\\2\end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix}-1\\3\\1\\-2\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}1\\5\\1\\0\end{bmatrix}\).  
9. Compute \(\mathbf{y}_\perp = \mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W = \begin{bmatrix}4\\3\\3\\-1\end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix}1\\5\\1\\0\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}3\\-2\\2\\-1\end{bmatrix}\).  
10. Final answer: \(\mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix}1\\5\\1\\0\end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix}3\\-2\\2\\-1\end{bmatrix}\), where the first vector is in \(W\) and the second is orthogonal to \(W\).
