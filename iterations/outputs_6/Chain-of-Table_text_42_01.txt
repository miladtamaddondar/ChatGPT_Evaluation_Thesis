Set up the equation $\mathbf{y} = a_1\mathbf{u}_1 + a_2\mathbf{u}_2 + a_3\mathbf{u}_3 + \mathbf{z}$, where $\mathbf{z}$ is orthogonal to $W$, and write the system: $a_1\begin{bmatrix}1\\1\\0\\1\end{bmatrix} + a_2\begin{bmatrix}-1\\3\\1\\-2\end{bmatrix} + a_3\begin{bmatrix}-1\\0\\1\\1\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}4\\3\\3\\-1\end{bmatrix}$.
2. Write the augmented matrix: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 & 3 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 & -1 \end{array}\right]$.
3. Row 2 minus Row 1: $R_2 \to R_2 - R_1$: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 & -1 \end{array}\right]$.
4. Row 4 minus Row 1: $R_4 \to R_4 - R_1$: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 0 & -1 & 2 & -5 \end{array}\right]$.
5. Row 3 minus $\frac{1}{4}$ Row 2: $R_3 \to R_3 - \frac{1}{4}R_2$: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 0 & \frac{3}{4} & \frac{13}{4} \\ 0 & -1 & 2 & -5 \end{array}\right]$.
6. Row 4 plus $\frac{1}{4}$ Row 2: $R_4 \to R_4 + \frac{1}{4}R_2$: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 0 & \frac{3}{4} & \frac{13}{4} \\ 0 & 0 & \frac{9}{4} & -\frac{21}{4} \end{array}\right]$.
7. Row 4 minus $3$ times Row 3: $R_4 \to R_4 - 3R_3$: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 0 & \frac{3}{4} & \frac{13}{4} \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & -10 \end{array}\right]$.
8. Back-substitute: From Row 4, $0 = -10$ is inconsistent, so the system has no solution; thus, $\mathbf{y}$ is not in $W$. To write $\mathbf{y}$ as the sum of a vector in $W$ and a vector orthogonal to $W$, we project $\mathbf{y}$ onto $W$ using the Gram-Schmidt process or projection formula.
9. Compute the projection $\mathbf{p}$ of $\mathbf{y}$ onto $W$ using the formula $\mathbf{p} = U(U^TU)^{-1}U^T\mathbf{y}$, where $U = [\mathbf{u}_1\ \mathbf{u}_2\ \mathbf{u}_3]$.
10. The final answer: $\mathbf{y} = \mathbf{p} + (\mathbf{y} - \mathbf{p})$, where $\mathbf{p}$ is the projection of $\mathbf{y}$ onto $W$ and $\mathbf{y} - \mathbf{p}$ is orthogonal to $W$.
