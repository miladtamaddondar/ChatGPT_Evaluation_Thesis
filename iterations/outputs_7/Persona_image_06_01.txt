1. To find a \(2 \times 2\) matrix \(B\) such that \(AB = 0\), each column of \(B\) must be in the null space of \(A\), i.e., \(A\vec{x} = \vec{0}\).
2. Let \(\vec{x} = \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix}\), then \(A\vec{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & -6 \\ -1 & 2 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3x_1 - 6x_2 \\ -x_1 + 2x_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}\).
3. Solve the system: \(3x_1 - 6x_2 = 0\) and \(-x_1 + 2x_2 = 0\).
4. From the first equation, \(3x_1 = 6x_2 \implies x_1 = 2x_2\).
5. Substitute \(x_1 = 2x_2\) into the second equation: \(-2x_2 + 2x_2 = 0\), which is always true, so the null space is all multiples of \(\begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}\).
6. Choose two different nonzero vectors in the null space, e.g., \(\begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}\) and \(\begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}\).
7. Construct \(B = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 4 \\ 1 & 2 \end{bmatrix}\).
8. The final answer is: \(B = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 4 \\ 1 & 2 \end{bmatrix}\) is a \(2 \times 2\) matrix with two different nonzero columns such that \(AB = 0\).
