1. Subtract \(4I\) from \(A\) to get \(A - 4I = \begin{bmatrix}3-4 & 0 & 2 & 0 \\ 1 & 3-4 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1-4 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 4-4\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}-1 & 0 & 2 & 0 \\ 1 & -1 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0\end{bmatrix}\)
2. Set up the augmented matrix for \((A-4I)\vec{x} = 0\): \(\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c}-1 & 0 & 2 & 0 \\ 1 & -1 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0\end{array}\right]\).
3. Add row 1 to row 2: \(R_2 \leftarrow R_2 + R_1\): \(\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c}-1 & 0 & 2 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0\end{array}\right]\).
4. Add row 2 to row 3: \(R_3 \leftarrow R_3 + R_2\): \(\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c}-1 & 0 & 2 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0\end{array}\right]\).
5. Multiply row 1 by \(-1\): \(R_1 \leftarrow -1 \cdot R_1\): \(\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c}1 & 0 & -2 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0\end{array}\right]\).
6. Multiply row 2 by \(-1\): \(R_2 \leftarrow -1 \cdot R_2\): \(\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c}1 & 0 & -2 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0\end{array}\right]\).
7. The system is now in row-echelon form, so write the equations: \(x_1 - 2x_3 = 0\), \(x_2 - 3x_3 = 0\), \(x_4\) is free.
8. Express the general solution: \(x_1 = 2x_3\), \(x_2 = 3x_3\), \(x_3 = t\), \(x_4 = s\), so \(\vec{x} = \begin{bmatrix}2t \\ 3t \\ t \\ s\end{bmatrix} = t\begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ 0\end{bmatrix} + s\begin{bmatrix}0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 1\end{bmatrix}\).
9. Therefore, a basis for the eigenspace for \(\lambda = 4\) is \(\left\{\begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ 0\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 1\end{bmatrix}\right\}\).
