3. Compute \( U U^T \): \( U U^T = \mathbf{u}_1 \mathbf{u}_1^T + \mathbf{u}_2 \mathbf{u}_2^T \); this projects onto the span of \(\mathbf{u}_1\) and \(\mathbf{u}_2\), which is correct by the properties of orthonormal bases.

5. 6. Add the two matrices: \( U U^T = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{4}{9}+\frac{4}{9} & \frac{2}{9}-\frac{4}{9} & \frac{4}{9}-\frac{2}{9} \\ \frac{2}{9}-\frac{4}{9} & \frac{1}{9}+\frac{4}{9} & \frac{2}{9}+\frac{2}{9} \\ \frac{4}{9}-\frac{2}{9} & \frac{2}{9}+\frac{2}{9} & \frac{4}{9}+\frac{1}{9} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{8}{9} & -\frac{2}{9} & \frac{2}{9} \\ -\frac{2}{9} & \frac{5}{9} & \frac{4}{9} \\ \frac{2}{9} & \frac{4}{9} & \frac{5}{9} \end{bmatrix} \); this follows from entrywise addition.  
7. The projection of \(\mathbf{y}\) onto \(W\) is \(\mathrm{proj}_W \mathbf{y} = U U^T \mathbf{y}\); this is correct for orthonormal columns in \(U\).  
8. Compute \( U U^T \mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{8}{9} & -\frac{2}{9} & \frac{2}{9} \\ -\frac{2}{9} & \frac{5}{9} & \frac{4}{9} \\ \frac{2}{9} & \frac{4}{9} & \frac{5}{9} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 8 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{8}{9} \cdot 4 + (-\frac{2}{9}) \cdot 8 + \frac{2}{9} \cdot 1 \\ -\frac{2}{9} \cdot 4 + \frac{5}{9} \cdot 8 + \frac{4}{9} \cdot 1 \\ \frac{2}{9} \cdot 4 + \frac{4}{9} \cdot 8 + \frac{5}{9} \cdot 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{32}{9} - \frac{16}{9} + \frac{2}{9} \\ -\frac{8}{9} + \frac{40}{9} + \frac{4}{9} \\ \frac{8}{9} + \frac{32}{9} + \frac{5}{9} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{18}{9} \\ \frac{36}{9} \\ \frac{45}{9} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 4 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix} \); this follows from matrix-vector multiplication.
