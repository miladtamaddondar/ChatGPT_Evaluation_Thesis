1. Find the eigenvalues of $A$ by solving $\det(A - \lambda I) = 0$: $\det\begin{bmatrix}0-\lambda & 1\\-3 & 4-\lambda\end{bmatrix} = (0-\lambda)(4-\lambda) - (-3)(1) = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 3 = 0$.
2. Factor the characteristic polynomial and solve for $\lambda$: $\lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 3 = (\lambda - 1)(\lambda - 3) = 0$, so the eigenvalues are $\lambda_1 = 1$ and $\lambda_2 = 3$.
3. Find an eigenvector for $\lambda_1 = 1$ by solving $(A - I)\mathbf{v} = 0$: $A - I = \begin{bmatrix}-1 & 1\\-3 & 3\end{bmatrix}$, so $-v_1 + v_2 = 0$ and $-3v_1 + 3v_2 = 0$; both equations are equivalent, so choose $v_1 = 1$, $v_2 = 1$, thus $\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix}1\\1\end{bmatrix}$.
4. Find an eigenvector for $\lambda_2 = 3$ by solving $(A - 3I)\mathbf{v} = 0$: $A - 3I = \begin{bmatrix}-3 & 1\\-3 & 1\end{bmatrix}$, so $-3v_1 + v_2 = 0$, choose $v_1 = 1$, $v_2 = 3$, thus $\mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}1\\3\end{bmatrix}$.
5. Verify that the eigenvectors $\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix}1\\1\end{bmatrix}$ and $\mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}1\\3\end{bmatrix}$ are linearly independent by checking that their determinant is nonzero: $\det\begin{bmatrix}1 & 1\\1 & 3\end{bmatrix} = (1)(3) - (1)(1) = 2 \neq 0$.
6. Conclude that the basis $\mathcal{B} = \left\{\begin{bmatrix}1\\1\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}1\\3\end{bmatrix}\right\}$ diagonalizes $A$, so $[T]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0\\0 & 3\end{bmatrix}$.
