4. The last two rows are \([0\ 0\ 0\ 4]\) and \([0\ 0\ 0\ -4]\), which are linearly dependent, so one can be removed.  
5. The reduced matrix is \(\begin{bmatrix} 0 & -2 & 6 & -1 \\ 0 & -2 & h & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 4 \end{bmatrix}\).  
6. The third row gives \(4x_4 = 0 \implies x_4 = 0\).

10. For the eigenspace to be two-dimensional, \(x_3\) must be free, so set \(h = 6\).  
11. Final answer: \(h = 6\) ensures the eigenspace for \(\lambda = 5\) is two-dimensional.
