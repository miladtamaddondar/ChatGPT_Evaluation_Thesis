1. To find the matrix representation of \( T \) relative to the basis \( \mathcal{B} = \{1, t, t^2\} \), we apply \( T \) to each basis vector. 
2. Compute \( T(1) \): Let \( a_0 = 1, a_1 = 0, a_2 = 0 \), so \( T(1) = 3(1) + (5 \cdot 1 - 2 \cdot 0)t + (4 \cdot 0 + 0)t^2 = 3 + 5t \). 
3. Express \( T(1) \) in coordinates relative to \( \mathcal{B} \): \( T(1) = 3 \cdot 1 + 5 \cdot t + 0 \cdot t^2 \Rightarrow [T(1)]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 5 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \). 
4. Compute \( T(t) \): Let \( a_0 = 0, a_1 = 1, a_2 = 0 \), so \( T(t) = 3(0) + (5 \cdot 0 - 2 \cdot 1)t + (4 \cdot 1 + 0)t^2 = -2t + 4t^2 \). 
5. Express \( T(t) \) in coordinates relative to \( \mathcal{B} \): \( T(t) = 0 \cdot 1 + (-2) \cdot t + 4 \cdot t^2 \Rightarrow [T(t)]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ -2 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} \). 
6. Compute \( T(t^2) \): Let \( a_0 = 0, a_1 = 0, a_2 = 1 \), so \( T(t^2) = 3(0) + (5 \cdot 0 - 2 \cdot 0)t + (4 \cdot 0 + 1)t^2 = t^2 \). 
7. Express \( T(t^2) \) in coordinates relative to \( \mathcal{B} \): \( T(t^2) = 0 \cdot 1 + 0 \cdot t + 1 \cdot t^2 \Rightarrow [T(t^2)]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \). 
8. Form the matrix representation by placing these coordinate vectors as columns: \[ [T]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 0 \\ 5 & -2 & 0 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \].
