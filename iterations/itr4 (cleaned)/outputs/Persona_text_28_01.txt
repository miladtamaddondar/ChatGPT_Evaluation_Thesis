1. The linear transformation $T$ maps any vector $\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix}$ to $T(\mathbf{x}) = x_1\mathbf{v}_1 + x_2\mathbf{v}_2 = x_1\begin{bmatrix} -2 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix} + x_2\begin{bmatrix} 7 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix}$.  
2. By properties of matrix-vector multiplication, $T(\mathbf{x})$ can be written as a linear combination of columns: $T(\mathbf{x}) = \begin{bmatrix} -2 & 7 \\ 5 & -3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix}$.  
3. Therefore, the matrix $A$ such that $T(\mathbf{x}) = A\mathbf{x}$ for all $\mathbf{x}$ is $A = \begin{bmatrix} -2 & 7 \\ 5 & -3 \end{bmatrix}$.
