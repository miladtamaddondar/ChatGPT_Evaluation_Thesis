1. To find a basis of $\mathbb{R}^2$ consisting of $\mathbf{v}_1$ and another eigenvector $\mathbf{v}_2$ of $A$, first verify that $\mathbf{v}_1$ is an eigenvector by checking if $A\mathbf{v}_1 = \lambda_1 \mathbf{v}_1$.  
2. Compute $A\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix}0.6 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.7\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}3/7 \\ 4/7\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0.6 \cdot 3/7 + 0.3 \cdot 4/7 \\ 0.4 \cdot 3/7 + 0.7 \cdot 4/7\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}(1.8 + 1.2)/7 \\ (1.2 + 2.8)/7\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}3/7 \\ 4/7\end{bmatrix}$, so $\lambda_1 = 1$.  
3. To find another eigenvector, compute the characteristic polynomial: $\det(A - \lambda I) = (0.6 - \lambda)(0.7 - \lambda) - (0.3)(0.4) = 0.42 - 1.3\lambda + \lambda^2 - 0.12 = \lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3$.  
4. Solve $\lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3 = 0$ to find the eigenvalues: $\lambda = \frac{1.3 \pm \sqrt{1.69 - 1.2}}{2} = \frac{1.3 \pm \sqrt{0.49}}{2} = \frac{1.3 \pm 0.7}{2}$, so $\lambda_1 = 1$, $\lambda_2 = 0.3$.  
5. Find an eigenvector for $\lambda_2 = 0.3$ by solving $(A - 0.3 I)\mathbf{v}_2 = 0$: $A - 0.3 I = \begin{bmatrix}0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.4\end{bmatrix}$.  
6. Set up the equation: $\begin{bmatrix}0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.4\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}x \\ y\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0 \\ 0\end{bmatrix}$, which gives $0.3x + 0.3y = 0$ and $0.4x + 0.4y = 0$, so $x + y = 0$.  
7. Thus, any vector of the form $\begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}$ is an eigenvector for $\lambda_2 = 0.3$; let $\mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}$.  
8. Verify that $\mathbf{v}_1$ and $\mathbf{v}_2$ are linearly independent: their determinant is $\det\left(\begin{bmatrix}3/7 & 1 \\ 4/7 & -1\end{bmatrix}\right) = (3/7)(-1) - (4/7)(1) = -3/7 - 4/7 = -1 \neq 0$, so they form a basis.  
9. Final answer: A basis for $\mathbb{R}^2$ consisting of $\mathbf{v}_1$ and another eigenvector $\mathbf{v}_2$ of $A$ is $\left\{ \begin{bmatrix}3/7 \\ 4/7\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix} \right\}$.
