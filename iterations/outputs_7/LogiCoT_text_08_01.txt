1. To construct a \( 4 \times 3 \) matrix with rank 1, recall that such a matrix can be written as the outer product of a nonzero \( 4 \times 1 \) column vector and a nonzero \( 1 \times 3 \) row vector, since the rank of an outer product is 1.  
2. Let \( \mathbf{u} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 0 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} \) (a nonzero \( 4 \times 1 \) vector) and \( \mathbf{v}^T = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 4 & -2 \end{bmatrix} \) (a nonzero \( 1 \times 3 \) vector).  
3. The outer product \( \mathbf{u} \mathbf{v}^T \) gives a \( 4 \times 3 \) matrix:  \[ \mathbf{A} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 0 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 4 & -2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \cdot 1 & 2 \cdot 4 & 2 \cdot (-2) \\ -1 \cdot 1 & -1 \cdot 4 & -1 \cdot (-2) \\ 0 \cdot 1 & 0 \cdot 4 & 0 \cdot (-2) \\ 3 \cdot 1 & 3 \cdot 4 & 3 \cdot (-2) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 8 & -4 \\ -1 & -4 & 2 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 3 & 12 & -6 \end{bmatrix} \]
4. To verify the rank is 1, note that every row is a scalar multiple of the first row: for example, the second row is \(-\frac{1}{2}\) times the first, the third row is 0 times the first, and the fourth row is \( \frac{3}{2} \) times the first; thus, the row space is 1-dimensional.  
5. Therefore, a \( 4 \times 3 \) matrix with rank 1 is \( \boxed{\begin{bmatrix} 2 & 8 & -4 \\ -1 & -4 & 2 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 3 & 12 & -6 \end{bmatrix}} \).
