1. To find the orthogonal projection of \(\mathbf{b}\) onto \(\text{Col }A\), we use the formula \(\text{proj}_{\text{Col }A}(\mathbf{b}) = A\hat{\mathbf{x}}\) where \(\hat{\mathbf{x}}\) is the least-squares solution to \(A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{b}\).  
2. The least-squares solution is given by \(\hat{\mathbf{x}} = (A^TA)^{-1}A^T\mathbf{b}\); first, compute \(A^T\): \(A^T = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix}\).  
3. Compute \(A^TA\): \(A^TA = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 3 & 1 \\ -2 & 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1^2 + 3^2 + (-2)^2 & 1 \cdot 5 + 3 \cdot 1 + (-2) \cdot 4 \\ 5 \cdot 1 + 1 \cdot 3 + 4 \cdot (-2) & 5^2 + 1^2 + 4^2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1+9+4 & 5+3-8 \\ 5+3-8 & 25+1+16 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 14 & 0 \\ 0 & 42 \end{bmatrix}\).  
4. Verify: \(A^TA\) is diagonal, so the calculation is correct since the off-diagonal terms sum to zero as shown.  
5. Compute \(A^T\mathbf{b}\): \(A^T\mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ -2 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \cdot 4 + 3 \cdot (-2) + (-2) \cdot (-3) \\ 5 \cdot 4 + 1 \cdot (-2) + 4 \cdot (-3) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 - 6 + 6 \\ 20 - 2 - 12 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix}\).  
6. Verify: \(4 - 6 + 6 = 4\), \(20 - 2 - 12 = 6\), so the calculation is correct.  
7. Compute \((A^TA)^{-1}\): Since \(A^TA\) is diagonal, its inverse is \(\begin{bmatrix} 1/14 & 0 \\ 0 & 1/42 \end{bmatrix}\).  
8. Compute \(\hat{\mathbf{x}} = (A^TA)^{-1}A^T\mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 1/14 & 0 \\ 0 & 1/42 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4/14 \\ 6/42 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2/7 \\ 1/7 \end{bmatrix}\).  
9. Verify: \(4/14 = 2/7\) and \(6/42 = 1/7\), so the calculation is correct.  
10. Compute the orthogonal projection: \(A\hat{\mathbf{x}} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 3 & 1 \\ -2 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 2/7 \\ 1/7 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \cdot 2/7 + 5 \cdot 1/7 \\ 3 \cdot 2/7 + 1 \cdot 1/7 \\ -2 \cdot 2/7 + 4 \cdot 1/7 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2/7 + 5/7 \\ 6/7 + 1/7 \\ -4/7 + 4/7 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}\).  
11. Verify: Each entry is correct by direct computation, so the projection is correct.  
12. The orthogonal projection of \(\mathbf{b}\) onto \(\text{Col }A\) is \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}\) and the least-squares solution to \(A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{b}\) is \(\hat{\mathbf{x}} = \begin{bmatrix} 2/7 \\ 1/7 \end{bmatrix}\).
