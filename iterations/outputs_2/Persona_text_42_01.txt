1. To write $\mathbf{y}$ as the sum of a vector in $W$ and a vector orthogonal to $W$, we need to find the orthogonal projection of $\mathbf{y}$ onto $W$ (call it $\mathbf{y}_W$), and then $\mathbf{y} = \mathbf{y}_W + \mathbf{z}$ where $\mathbf{z}$ is orthogonal to $W$.
2. First, form the matrix $A = [\mathbf{u}_1 \ \mathbf{u}_2 \ \mathbf{u}_3] = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$.
3. The projection formula is $\mathbf{y}_W = A(A^T A)^{-1}A^T \mathbf{y}$.
4. Compute $A^T A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 & 1 & -2 \\ -1 & 0 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 15 & 7 \\ 0 & 7 & 4 \end{bmatrix}$.
5. Compute $A^T \mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 & 1 & -2 \\ -1 & 0 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 + 3 + 0 - 1 = 6 \\ -4 + 9 + 3 + 2 = 10 \\ -4 + 0 + 3 - 1 = -2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 10 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix}$.
6. Solve $(A^T A)\mathbf{c} = A^T \mathbf{y}$ for $\mathbf{c}$, so $\begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 15 & 7 \\ 0 & 7 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} c_1 \\ c_2 \\ c_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 10 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix}$.
7. From the first row: $3c_1 = 6 \implies c_1 = 2$.
8. From the second and third rows: $15c_2 + 7c_3 = 10$ and $7c_2 + 4c_3 = -2$.
9. Solve the system for $c_2$ and $c_3$: Multiply the third equation by $-15/7$ and add to the second to eliminate $c_2$: $15c_2 + 7c_3 - 15c_2 - 60c_3/7 = 10 + 30/7$, so $7c_3 - 60c_3/7 = 10 + 30/7$, $49c_3 - 60c_3 = 70 + 30$, $-11c_3 = 100$, $c_3 = -100/11$.
10. Substitute $c_3$ into $7c_2 + 4c_3 = -2$: $7c_2 + 4(-100/11) = -2$, $7c_2 = -2 + 400/11 = (400 - 22)/11 = 378/11$, $c_2 = 378/(11 \times 7) = 378/77 = 42/7 = 6/11$.
11. Thus, $\mathbf{c} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 6/11 \\ -100/11 \end{bmatrix}$.
12. Compute $\mathbf{y}_W = c_1 \mathbf{u}_1 + c_2 \mathbf{u}_2 + c_3 \mathbf{u}_3 = 2\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} + \frac{6}{11}\begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix} + \frac{-100}{11}\begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}$.
13. Calculate each component: First, $2\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 2 \\ 0 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}$; Second, $\frac{6}{11}\begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -6/11 \\ 18/11 \\ 6/11 \\ -12/11 \end{bmatrix}$; Third, $\frac{-100}{11}\begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 100/11 \\ 0 \\ -100/11 \\ -100/11 \end{bmatrix}$.
14. Add the three vectors: $\begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 2 \\ 0 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} -6/11 \\ 18/11 \\ 6/11 \\ -12/11 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 100/11 \\ 0 \\ -100/11 \\ -100/11 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 - 6/11 + 100/11 \\ 2 + 18/11 + 0 \\ 0 + 6/11 - 100/11 \\ 2 - 12/11 - 100/11 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 + 94/11 \\ 2 + 18/11 \\ -94/11 \\ 2 - 112/11 \end{bmatrix}$.
15. Simplify: $2 = 22/11$, so the vector is $\begin{bmatrix} (22 + 94)/11 \\ (22 + 18)/11 \\ -94/11 \\ (22 - 112)/11 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 116/11 \\ 40/11 \\ -94/11 \\ -90/11 \end{bmatrix}$.
16. The orthogonal component is $\mathbf{z} = \mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} 116/11 \\ 40/11 \\ -94/11 \\ -90/11 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 44/11 - 116/11 \\ 33/11 - 40/11 \\ 33/11 + 94/11 \\ -11/11 + 90/11 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -72/11 \\ -7/11 \\ 127/11 \\ 79/11 \end{bmatrix}$.
17. Therefore, the final answer is: $\boxed{\mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 116/11 \\ 40/11 \\ -94/11 \\ -90/11 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} -72/11 \\ -7/11 \\ 127/11 \\ 79/11 \end{bmatrix}}$, where the first vector is in $W$ and the second is orthogonal to $W$.
