1. To diagonalize $A$, we first find its eigenvalues by solving $\det(A - \lambda I) = 0$: $\det\left(\begin{bmatrix}0-\lambda & 1 \\ -3 & 4-\lambda\end{bmatrix}\right) = (0-\lambda)(4-\lambda) - (-3)(1) = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 3 = 0$.
2. Solve the characteristic equation $\lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 3 = 0$ to find eigenvalues: $(\lambda - 1)(\lambda - 3) = 0$ so $\lambda_1 = 1$, $\lambda_2 = 3$.
3. Find an eigenvector for $\lambda_1 = 1$ by solving $(A - I)\mathbf{v} = 0$: $A - I = \begin{bmatrix}-1 & 1 \\ -3 & 3\end{bmatrix}$ so $-v_1 + v_2 = 0$ and $-3v_1 + 3v_2 = 0$; thus, $v_2 = v_1$, so one eigenvector is $\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 1\end{bmatrix}$.
4. Find an eigenvector for $\lambda_2 = 3$ by solving $(A - 3I)\mathbf{v} = 0$: $A - 3I = \begin{bmatrix}-3 & 1 \\ -3 & 1\end{bmatrix}$ so $-3v_1 + v_2 = 0$, thus $v_2 = 3v_1$, so one eigenvector is $\mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 3\end{bmatrix}$.
5. The set $\mathcal{B} = \left\{\begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 1\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 3\end{bmatrix}\right\}$ is a basis for $\mathbb{R}^2$ such that $[T]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 \\ 0 & 3\end{bmatrix}$.
