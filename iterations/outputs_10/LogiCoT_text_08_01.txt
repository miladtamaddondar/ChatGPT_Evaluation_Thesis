1. To construct a \( 4 \times 3 \) matrix with rank 1, recall that a matrix has rank 1 if all its columns are scalar multiples of a single nonzero vector; let's choose the column vector \( \mathbf{u} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} \).  
2. Let the row vector \( \mathbf{v}^T = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 5 & -1 \end{bmatrix} \), so the matrix \( A = \mathbf{u} \mathbf{v}^T \) will be \( 4 \times 3 \) and have rank 1 by construction.  
3. Compute \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 5 & -1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \cdot 2 & 1 \cdot 5 & 1 \cdot (-1) \\ 2 \cdot 2 & 2 \cdot 5 & 2 \cdot (-1) \\ 3 \cdot 2 & 3 \cdot 5 & 3 \cdot (-1) \\ 4 \cdot 2 & 4 \cdot 5 & 4 \cdot (-1) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 5 & -1 \\ 4 & 10 & -2 \\ 6 & 15 & -3 \\ 8 & 20 & -4 \end{bmatrix} \).  
4. Verify that each column is a scalar multiple of \( \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} \): the first column is \( 2 \times \mathbf{u} \), the second is \( 5 \times \mathbf{u} \), and the third is \( -1 \times \mathbf{u} \), confirming rank 1.  
5. Final answer: A \( 4 \times 3 \) matrix with rank 1 is \( \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 5 & -1 \\ 4 & 10 & -2 \\ 6 & 15 & -3 \\ 8 & 20 & -4 \end{bmatrix} \).
