1. Write the vectors as columns of a matrix: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & -3 & 1 \\ -3 & 2 & 1 & -8 & -6 \\ 2 & -3 & 6 & 7 & 9 \end{bmatrix}\).
2. Swap columns 2 and 3 to bring a nonzero entry into the (2,2) position: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 0 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & -4 & 1 & -3 & 1 \\ -3 & 1 & 2 & -8 & -6 \\ 2 & 6 & -3 & 7 & 9 \end{bmatrix}\).
3. Add 3 times row 1 to row 3: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 0 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & -4 & 1 & -3 & 1 \\ 0 & -8 & 2 & -5 & 0 \\ 2 & 6 & -3 & 7 & 9 \end{bmatrix}\).
4. Subtract 2 times row 1 from row 4: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 0 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & -4 & 1 & -3 & 1 \\ 0 & -8 & 2 & -5 & 0 \\ 0 & 12 & -3 & 5 & 5 \end{bmatrix}\).
5. Divide row 2 by \(-4\): \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 0 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & -\frac{1}{4} & \frac{3}{4} & -\frac{1}{4} \\ 0 & -8 & 2 & -5 & 0 \\ 0 & 12 & -3 & 5 & 5 \end{bmatrix}\).
6. Add 8 times row 2 to row 3: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 0 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & -\frac{1}{4} & \frac{3}{4} & -\frac{1}{4} \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 & -2 \\ 0 & 12 & -3 & 5 & 5 \end{bmatrix}\).
7. Subtract 12 times row 2 from row 4: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 0 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & -\frac{1}{4} & \frac{3}{4} & -\frac{1}{4} \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 & -2 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & -4 & 8 \end{bmatrix}\).
8. Add 4 times row 3 to row 4: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 0 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & -\frac{1}{4} & \frac{3}{4} & -\frac{1}{4} \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 & -2 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\).
9. The nonzero columns corresponding to the leading ones are columns 1, 2, and 4, so a basis is \(\left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ -3 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \\ 2 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ -8 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \right\}\).
