To compute \( DA \), recall that multiplying a diagonal matrix \( D \) by a matrix \( A \) on the left scales the rows of \( A \) by the corresponding diagonal entries of \( D \): \( DA = \begin{bmatrix}2 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 5\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}1 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 1 & 4 & 5\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}2 \cdot 1 & 2 \cdot 1 & 2 \cdot 1 \\ 3 \cdot 1 & 3 \cdot 2 & 3 \cdot 3 \\ 5 \cdot 1 & 5 \cdot 4 & 5 \cdot 5\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}2 & 2 & 2 \\ 3 & 6 & 9 \\ 5 & 20 & 25\end{bmatrix} \); this is correct because each row is scaled by the corresponding diagonal entry.
