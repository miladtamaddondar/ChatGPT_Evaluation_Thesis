1. To write $\mathbf{y}$ as the sum of a vector in $W$ and a vector orthogonal to $W$, we first form the matrix $A = [\mathbf{u}_1 \ \mathbf{u}_2 \ \mathbf{u}_3] = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$.
2. We seek $\mathbf{y} = \mathbf{y}_W + \mathbf{y}_\perp$ where $\mathbf{y}_W \in W$ and $\mathbf{y}_\perp \perp W$, so $\mathbf{y}_W = A\mathbf{c}$ for some $\mathbf{c} \in \mathbb{R}^3$.
3. The best choice for $\mathbf{y}_W$ is the orthogonal projection of $\mathbf{y}$ onto $W$, given by $\mathbf{y}_W = A(A^TA)^{-1}A^T\mathbf{y}$.
4. Compute $A^T A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 & 1 & -2 \\ -1 & 0 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 15 & 7 \\ 0 & 7 & 4 \end{bmatrix}$.
5. Compute $A^T \mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 & 1 & -2 \\ -1 & 0 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 + 3 + 0 - 1 = 6 \\ -4 + 9 + 3 + 2 = 10 \\ -4 + 0 + 3 - 1 = -2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 10 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix}$.
6. Solve $(A^T A)\mathbf{c} = A^T \mathbf{y}$, so $\begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 15 & 7 \\ 0 & 7 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} c_1 \\ c_2 \\ c_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 10 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix}$.
7. From the first row, $3c_1 = 6 \implies c_1 = 2$.
8. From the second and third rows, solve $\begin{bmatrix} 15 & 7 \\ 7 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} c_2 \\ c_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 10 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix}$.
9. Compute the determinant: $15 \cdot 4 - 7 \cdot 7 = 60 - 49 = 11$.
10. Use Cramer's rule: $c_2 = \frac{1}{11}(4 \cdot 10 - 7 \cdot (-2)) = \frac{1}{11}(40 + 14) = \frac{54}{11}$.
11. $c_3 = \frac{1}{11}(15 \cdot (-2) - 7 \cdot 10) = \frac{1}{11}(-30 - 70) = \frac{-100}{11}$.
12. Thus, $\mathbf{c} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ \frac{54}{11} \\ -\frac{100}{11} \end{bmatrix}$.
13. Compute $\mathbf{y}_W = A\mathbf{c} = 2\mathbf{u}_1 + \frac{54}{11}\mathbf{u}_2 - \frac{100}{11}\mathbf{u}_3$.
14. Calculate each term: $2\mathbf{u}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 2 \\ 0 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}$, $\frac{54}{11}\mathbf{u}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} -\frac{54}{11} \\ \frac{162}{11} \\ \frac{54}{11} \\ -\frac{108}{11} \end{bmatrix}$, $-\frac{100}{11}\mathbf{u}_3 = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{100}{11} \\ 0 \\ -\frac{100}{11} \\ -\frac{100}{11} \end{bmatrix}$.
15. Add the vectors: $\mathbf{y}_W = \begin{bmatrix} 2 - \frac{54}{11} + \frac{100}{11} \\ 2 + \frac{162}{11} + 0 \\ 0 + \frac{54}{11} - \frac{100}{11} \\ 2 - \frac{108}{11} - \frac{100}{11} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2 \cdot 11 + 46}{11} \\ \frac{2 \cdot 11 + 162}{11} \\ \frac{54 - 100}{11} \\ \frac{2 \cdot 11 - 208}{11} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{22 + 46}{11} \\ \frac{22 + 162}{11} \\ \frac{-46}{11} \\ \frac{22 - 208}{11} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{68}{11} \\ \frac{184}{11} \\ -\frac{46}{11} \\ -\frac{186}{11} \end{bmatrix}$.
16. The orthogonal component is $\mathbf{y}_\perp = \mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} \frac{68}{11} \\ \frac{184}{11} \\ -\frac{46}{11} \\ -\frac{186}{11} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 - \frac{68}{11} \\ 3 - \frac{184}{11} \\ 3 + \frac{46}{11} \\ -1 + \frac{186}{11} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{44 - 68}{11} \\ \frac{33 - 184}{11} \\ \frac{33 + 46}{11} \\ \frac{-11 + 186}{11} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -\frac{24}{11} \\ -\frac{151}{11} \\ \frac{79}{11} \\ \frac{175}{11} \end{bmatrix}$.
17. The final answer: $\boxed{\mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{68}{11} \\ \frac{184}{11} \\ -\frac{46}{11} \\ -\frac{186}{11} \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} -\frac{24}{11} \\ -\frac{151}{11} \\ \frac{79}{11} \\ \frac{175}{11} \end{bmatrix}}$, where the first vector is in $W$ and the second is orthogonal to $W$.
