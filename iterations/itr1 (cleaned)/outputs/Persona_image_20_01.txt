1. To find a basis for \(\mathbb{R}^2\) consisting of \(\mathbf{v}_1\) and another eigenvector \(\mathbf{v}_2\) of \(A\), first recall that eigenvectors correspond to eigenvalues, so we need to find all eigenvalues of \(A\) by solving \(\det(A - \lambda I) = 0\). 
2. Compute the characteristic polynomial: \(\det\left(\begin{bmatrix}0.6-\lambda & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.7-\lambda\end{bmatrix}\right) = (0.6-\lambda)(0.7-\lambda) - (0.3)(0.4) = 0\). 
3. Expand: \((0.6-\lambda)(0.7-\lambda) - 0.12 = 0.42 - 0.6\lambda - 0.7\lambda + \lambda^2 - 0.12 = \lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3 = 0\). 
4. Solve the quadratic equation: \(\lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3 = 0\) gives \(\lambda = \frac{1.3 \pm \sqrt{1.69 - 1.2}}{2} = \frac{1.3 \pm 0.7}{2}\), so \(\lambda_1 = 1\), \(\lambda_2 = 0.3\). 
5. \(\mathbf{v}_1\) is already given as an eigenvector for \(\lambda_1 = 1\), so find an eigenvector \(\mathbf{v}_2\) for \(\lambda_2 = 0.3\) by solving \((A - 0.3I)\mathbf{v}_2 = 0\). 
6. Compute \(A - 0.3I = \begin{bmatrix}0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.4\end{bmatrix}\), so set up \(\begin{bmatrix}0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.4\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}x \\ y\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0 \\ 0\end{bmatrix}\). 
7. The system is \(0.3x + 0.3y = 0\) and \(0.4x + 0.4y = 0\), both reduce to \(x + y = 0\), so \(y = -x\). 
8. Thus, an eigenvector for \(\lambda_2 = 0.3\) is \(\mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}\). 
9. Therefore, a basis for \(\mathbb{R}^2\) consisting of eigenvectors of \(A\) is \(\left\{\begin{bmatrix}\frac{3}{7} \\ \frac{4}{7}\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}\right\}\).
