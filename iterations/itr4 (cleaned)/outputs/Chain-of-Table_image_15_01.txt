1. Find the eigenvalues of \(A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix}\) by solving \(\det(A - \lambda I) = 0\): \(\det\begin{pmatrix} 1-\lambda & -2 \\ 1 & 3-\lambda \end{pmatrix} = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0\).
2. Solve for \(\lambda\): \(\lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0\) gives \(\lambda = 2 \pm i\).
3. The matrix \(C\) of the form \(\begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix}\) will have \(a = 2\), \(b = 1\), so \(C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}\).
4. Find an eigenvector for \(\lambda = 2+i\): Solve \(\left(A - (2+i)I\right)\vec{v} = 0\), so \(\begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix} \vec{v} = 0\).
5. Let \(v_1 = \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix}\), then \((-1-i)x - 2y = 0\) and \(x + (1-i)y = 0\). From the first equation, \(x = \frac{-2}{1+i}y = (-1+i)y\).
6. Take \(y = 1\), so \(v_1 = \begin{pmatrix} -1+i \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}\).
7. The real and imaginary parts of \(v_1\) are \(\begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}\) and \(\begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}\), so set \(P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}\).
8. The answer is: \(P = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}\), \(C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}\), and \(A = P C P^{-1}\).
