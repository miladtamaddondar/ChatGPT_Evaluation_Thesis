1. We are given \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -2 \\ -2 & 1 & 6 \\ 3 & -2 & -5 \end{bmatrix}\) and \(\mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 7 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix}\), and need to solve \(A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{b}\).
2. Write the augmented matrix: \(\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & -2 & -1 \\ -2 & 1 & 6 & 7 \\ 3 & -2 & -5 & -3 \end{array}\right]\).
3. Use row 1 to eliminate the \(x_1\) term in row 2: Row2 = Row2 + 2*Row1, so \([-2, 1, 6, 7] + 2*[1, 0, -2, -1] = [0, 1, 2, 5]\).
4. Use row 1 to eliminate the \(x_1\) term in row 3: Row3 = Row3 - 3*Row1, so \([3, -2, -5, -3] - 3*[1, 0, -2, -1] = [0, -2, 1, 0]\).
5. The matrix is now \(\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & -2 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & 5 \\ 0 & -2 & 1 & 0 \end{array}\right]\).
6. Use row 2 to eliminate the \(x_2\) term in row 3: Row3 = Row3 + 2*Row2, so \([0, -2, 1, 0] + 2*[0, 1, 2, 5] = [0, 0, 5, 10]\).
7. The matrix is now \(\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & -2 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 & 10 \end{array}\right]\).
8. Solve for \(x_3\) from row 3: \(5x_3 = 10 \implies x_3 = 2\).
9. Substitute \(x_3 = 2\) into row 2: \(x_2 + 2x_3 = 5 \implies x_2 + 4 = 5 \implies x_2 = 1\).
10. Substitute \(x_2 = 1\) and \(x_3 = 2\) into row 1: \(x_1 - 2x_3 = -1 \implies x_1 - 4 = -1 \implies x_1 = 3\).
11. The solution is \(\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}\).
12. Since we obtained a unique solution with no free variables, the solution is unique.
