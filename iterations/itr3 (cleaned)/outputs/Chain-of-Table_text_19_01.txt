1. Write the problem as: Find the matrix $[T]_{\mathcal{B}}$ such that $[T]_{\mathcal{B}}[\mathbf{x}]_{\mathcal{B}} = [T(\mathbf{x})]_{\mathcal{B}}$ for all $\mathbf{x} \in \mathbb{R}^2$.

2. Compute $T(\mathbf{b}_1) = A\mathbf{b}_1 = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 1 \\ -1 & 3\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 1\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 2\end{bmatrix}$.

3. Compute $T(\mathbf{b}_2) = A\mathbf{b}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 1 \\ -1 & 3\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}5 \\ 4\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}9 \\ 7\end{bmatrix}$.

4. Express $T(\mathbf{b}_1) = \begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 2\end{bmatrix}$ as a linear combination of $\mathbf{b}_1$ and $\mathbf{b}_2$: Set up the augmented matrix $\left[\begin{array}{cc|c}1 & 5 & 2 \\ 1 & 4 & 2\end{array}\right]$ for $a\mathbf{b}_1 + b\mathbf{b}_2 = T(\mathbf{b}_1)$.

5. Row 2 minus Row 1: $\left[\begin{array}{cc|c}1 & 5 & 2 \\ 0 & -1 & 0\end{array}\right]$.

6. Solve Row 2: $-b = 0 \implies b = 0$; Row 1: $a + 5b = 2 \implies a = 2$.

7. Thus, $T(\mathbf{b}_1) = 2\mathbf{b}_1 + 0\mathbf{b}_2$, so the first column of $[T]_{\mathcal{B}}$ is $\begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 0\end{bmatrix}$.

8. Express $T(\mathbf{b}_2) = \begin{bmatrix}9 \\ 7\end{bmatrix}$ as a linear combination of $\mathbf{b}_1$ and $\mathbf{b}_2$: Set up the augmented matrix $\left[\begin{array}{cc|c}1 & 5 & 9 \\ 1 & 4 & 7\end{array}\right]$ for $a\mathbf{b}_1 + b\mathbf{b}_2 = T(\mathbf{b}_2)$.

9. Row 2 minus Row 1: $\left[\begin{array}{cc|c}1 & 5 & 9 \\ 0 & -1 & -2\end{array}\right]$.

10. Solve Row 2: $-b = -2 \implies b = 2$; Row 1: $a + 5b = 9 \implies a = 9 - 10 = -1$.

11. Thus, $T(\mathbf{b}_2) = -1\mathbf{b}_1 + 2\mathbf{b}_2$, so the second column of $[T]_{\mathcal{B}}$ is $\begin{bmatrix}-1 \\ 2\end{bmatrix}$.

12. Therefore, the $\mathcal{B}$-matrix for $T$ is $[T]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix}2 & -1 \\ 0 & 2\end{bmatrix}$.
