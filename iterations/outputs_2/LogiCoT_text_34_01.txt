1. Write the vectors as rows of a matrix: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & -3 \\ -3 & -4 & 1 & 6 \\ 1 & -3 & -8 & 7 \\ 2 & 1 & -6 & 9 \end{bmatrix}\).  
2. Use row 1 to eliminate the first entry in rows 3, 4, and 5: Row 3 = Row 3 + 3*Row 1 \(\rightarrow\) \((-3)+3(1)=0\), \((-4)+3(0)=-4\), \(1+3(-3)=-8\), \(6+3(2)=12\); Row 3 becomes \([0, -4, -8, 12]\); Row 4 = Row 4 - Row 1 \(\rightarrow\) \(1-1=0\), \(-3-0=-3\), \(-8-(-3)=-5\), \(7-2=5\); Row 4 becomes \([0, -3, -5, 5]\); Row 5 = Row 5 - 2*Row 1 \(\rightarrow\) \(2-2(1)=0\), \(1-2(0)=1\), \(-6-2(-3)=0\), \(9-2(2)=5\); Row 5 becomes \([0, 1, 0, 5]\).  
3. The new matrix is \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & -3 \\ 0 & -4 & -8 & 12 \\ 0 & -3 & -5 & 5 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & 5 \end{bmatrix}\); use row 2 to eliminate the second entry in rows 3, 4, and 5: Row 3 = Row 3 + 4*Row 2 \(\rightarrow\) \(0+4(0)=0\), \(-4+4(1)=0\), \(-8+4(2)=0\), \(12+4(-3)=0\); Row 3 becomes \([0, 0, 0, 0]\); Row 4 = Row 4 + 3*Row 2 \(\rightarrow\) \(0+3(0)=0\), \(-3+3(1)=0\), \(-5+3(2)=1\), \(5+3(-3)=-4\); Row 4 becomes \([0, 0, 1, -4]\); Row 5 = Row 5 - 1*Row 2 \(\rightarrow\) \(0-0=0\), \(1-1=0\), \(0-2=-2\), \(5-(-3)=8\); Row 5 becomes \([0, 0, -2, 8]\).  
4. The new matrix is \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & -3 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & -4 \\ 0 & 0 & -2 & 8 \end{bmatrix}\); use row 4 to eliminate the third entry in row 5: Row 5 = Row 5 + 2*Row 4 \(\rightarrow\) \(0+2(0)=0\), \(0+2(0)=0\), \(-2+2(1)=0\), \(8+2(-4)=0\); Row 5 becomes \([0, 0, 0, 0]\).  
5. The row-reduced matrix is \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & -3 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & -4 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\); the nonzero rows are the first, second, and fourth, so the original vectors \(\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ -3 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}\), \(\mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \\ 2 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix}\), and \(\mathbf{v}_4 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ -8 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix}\) form a basis for the space spanned by the given vectors.
