1. To find the LU factorization, write the matrix as \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 9 \\ 4 & 5 \end{bmatrix} \) and assume \( A = LU \) where \( L = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ l_{21} & 1 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( U = \begin{bmatrix} u_{11} & u_{12} \\ 0 & u_{22} \end{bmatrix} \).  
2. Multiply \( L \) and \( U \) to get \( LU = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ l_{21} & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} u_{11} & u_{12} \\ 0 & u_{22} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} u_{11} & u_{12} \\ l_{21}u_{11} & l_{21}u_{12} + u_{22} \end{bmatrix} \).  
3. Set \( LU \) equal to \( A \) to get the equations: \( u_{11} = 6 \), \( u_{12} = 9 \), \( l_{21}u_{11} = 4 \), and \( l_{21}u_{12} + u_{22} = 5 \).  
4. Solve \( u_{11} = 6 \) and \( u_{12} = 9 \) directly from the first row; verify by matching the first row of \( A \).  
5. Solve \( l_{21}u_{11} = 4 \) to get \( l_{21} = \frac{4}{6} = \frac{2}{3} \); verify by substituting back: \( \frac{2}{3} \times 6 = 4 \).  
6. Solve \( l_{21}u_{12} + u_{22} = 5 \) to get \( \frac{2}{3} \times 9 + u_{22} = 5 \implies 6 + u_{22} = 5 \implies u_{22} = -1 \); verify by substituting values.  
7. Thus, \( L = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ \frac{2}{3} & 1 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( U = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 9 \\ 0 & -1 \end{bmatrix} \); verify by multiplying \( L \) and \( U \) to recover \( A \).  
8. Final answer: The LU factorization is \( \boxed{ \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 9 \\ 4 & 5 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ \frac{2}{3} & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 9 \\ 0 & -1 \end{bmatrix} } \).
