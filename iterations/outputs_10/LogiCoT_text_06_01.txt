3. The second equation gives \(x_1 = 2x_2\); substituting into the first equation, \(3(2x_2) - 6x_2 = 0 \implies 6x_2 - 6x_2 = 0\), which is always true, so the solution is all scalar multiples of \(\begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 1\end{bmatrix}\).

5. Construct \(B\) with these columns: \(B = \begin{bmatrix}2 & -2 \\ 1 & -1\end{bmatrix}\).
