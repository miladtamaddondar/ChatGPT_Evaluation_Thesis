1. To find $h$ such that the eigenspace for $\lambda=5$ is two-dimensional, we first compute $A - 5I$: $A - 5I = \begin{bmatrix}0 & -2 & 6 & -1 \\ 0 & -2 & h & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 4 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & -4 \end{bmatrix}$.
2. The eigenspace dimension is the nullity of $A-5I$, so we solve $(A-5I)\vec{x} = 0$; write the system: $0x_1 - 2x_2 + 6x_3 - x_4 = 0$, $0x_1 - 2x_2 + h x_3 + 0x_4 = 0$, $0x_1 + 0x_2 + 0x_3 + 4x_4 = 0$, $0x_1 + 0x_2 + 0x_3 - 4x_4 = 0$.
3. The last two equations give $x_4 = 0$.
4. Substitute $x_4 = 0$ into the first two equations: $-2x_2 + 6x_3 = 0$, $-2x_2 + h x_3 = 0$.
5. The system $-2x_2 + 6x_3 = 0$ gives $x_2 = 3x_3$.
6. Substitute $x_2 = 3x_3$ into $-2x_2 + h x_3 = 0$ to get $-2(3x_3) + h x_3 = 0 \implies -6x_3 + h x_3 = 0 \implies (h-6)x_3 = 0$.
7. For the eigenspace to be two-dimensional, we need two free variables, so $x_1$ and $x_3$ must be free, which happens if $h-6=0 \implies h=6$.
8. Therefore, the value of $h$ such that the eigenspace for $\lambda=5$ is two-dimensional is $\boxed{6}$.
