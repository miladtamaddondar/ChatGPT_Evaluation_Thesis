1. Find the eigenvalues of \( A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix} \) by solving \( \det(A - \lambda I) = 0 \): \( \det\begin{pmatrix} 1-\lambda & -2 \\ 1 & 3-\lambda \end{pmatrix} = (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) + 2 = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 5 = 0 \).
2. Solve for \( \lambda \): \( \lambda = \frac{4 \pm \sqrt{16-20}}{2} = \frac{4 \pm 2i}{2} = 2 \pm i \).
3. Find an eigenvector for \( \lambda = 2+i \): Solve \( (A - (2+i)I)\vec{v} = 0 \), so \( \begin{pmatrix} -1-i & -2 \\ 1 & 1-i \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix} = 0 \).
4. From the first row: \( (-1-i)x - 2y = 0 \implies x = \frac{-2}{-1-i}y = \frac{2}{1+i}y = (1-i)y \) (after multiplying numerator and denominator by \( 1-i \)).
5. Take \( y = 1 \), then \( x = 1-i \), so an eigenvector is \( \vec{v}_1 = \begin{pmatrix} 1-i \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \).
6. The other eigenvector for \( \lambda = 2-i \) is the complex conjugate: \( \vec{v}_2 = \begin{pmatrix} 1+i \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \).
7. Form \( P = \begin{pmatrix} 1-i & 1+i \\ 1 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \) and \( C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \) (since \( C \) must be of the form \( \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ -b & a \end{pmatrix} \) and matches the real and imaginary parts of the eigenvalues).
8. Final answer: \( P = \begin{pmatrix} 1-i & 1+i \\ 1 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \), \( C = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \), and \( A = P C P^{-1} \).
