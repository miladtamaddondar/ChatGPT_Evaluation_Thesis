1. To find a nonzero vector $\mathbf{v}$ orthogonal to both $\mathbf{u}_1$ and $\mathbf{u}_2$, we can compute their cross product: $\mathbf{v} = \mathbf{u}_1 \times \mathbf{u}_2$.
2. Write $\mathbf{u}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix}$ and $\mathbf{u}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ -1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}$.
3. Compute the cross product using the formula: $\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} u_{1,2}u_{2,3} - u_{1,3}u_{2,2} \\ u_{1,3}u_{2,1} - u_{1,1}u_{2,3} \\ u_{1,1}u_{2,2} - u_{1,2}u_{2,1} \end{bmatrix}$.
4. Calculate the first component: $1 \cdot 2 - (-2) \cdot (-1) = 2 - 2 = 0$.
5. Calculate the second component: $(-2) \cdot 5 - 1 \cdot 2 = -10 - 2 = -12$.
6. Calculate the third component: $1 \cdot (-1) - 1 \cdot 5 = -1 - 5 = -6$.
7. Thus, $\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ -12 \\ -6 \end{bmatrix}$ is a nonzero vector in $\mathbb{R}^3$ orthogonal to both $\mathbf{u}_1$ and $\mathbf{u}_2$.
