The vector \(\mathbf{x}\) in the standard basis is found by multiplying the basis matrix by the coordinate vector: \(\mathbf{x} = P_B [\mathbf{x}]_B\), where \(P_B = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 1 & -1 \\ 3 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\) and \([\mathbf{x}]_B = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}\); this follows from the definition of change of basis.  
2. Compute the product: \(\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 1 & -1 \\ 3 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \cdot 3 + 2 \cdot 2 \\ 1 \cdot 3 + (-1) \cdot 2 \\ 3 \cdot 3 + 0 \cdot 2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 + 4 \\ 3 - 2 \\ 9 + 0 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 7 \\ 1 \\ 9 \end{bmatrix}\); this uses matrix multiplication.  
3. Therefore, the vector \(\mathbf{x}\) is \(\begin{bmatrix} 7 \\ 1 \\ 9 \end{bmatrix}\); this is verified by reconstructing \(\mathbf{x}\) as a linear combination of the basis vectors with the given coordinates.
