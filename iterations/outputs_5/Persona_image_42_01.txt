1. To write \(\mathbf{y}\) as the sum of a vector in \(W\) and a vector orthogonal to \(W\), we first find the projection of \(\mathbf{y}\) onto \(W = \text{span}\{\mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_2, \mathbf{u}_3\}\).
2. Since \(\mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_2, \mathbf{u}_3\) are likely linearly independent, we set \(\mathbf{y}_W = c_1\mathbf{u}_1 + c_2\mathbf{u}_2 + c_3\mathbf{u}_3\) and solve for \(c_1, c_2, c_3\).
3. Set up the equation: \(\mathbf{y} = c_1\mathbf{u}_1 + c_2\mathbf{u}_2 + c_3\mathbf{u}_3 + \mathbf{y}_\perp\), where \(\mathbf{y}_\perp\) is orthogonal to each \(\mathbf{u}_i\).
4. Write the system: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} c_1 \\ c_2 \\ c_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix}\).
5. Solve the system for \(c_1, c_2, c_3\): the augmented matrix is \(\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 & 3 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 & -1 \end{array}\right]\).
6. Row 1 minus Row 2 gives new Row 2: \([0, -4, -1, 1]\).
7. Row 4 minus Row 1 gives new Row 4: \([0, -1, 2, -5]\).
8. Row 3 stays the same: \([0, 1, 1, 3]\).
9. Replace Row 2 with Row 2 divided by \(-4\): \([0, 1, 0.25, -0.25]\).
10. Row 3 minus Row 2: \([0, 0, 0.75, 3.25]\).
11. Row 4 plus Row 2: \([0, 0, 2.25, -5.25]\).
12. Row 3 divided by 0.75: \([0, 0, 1, 13/3]\).
13. Row 4 minus 2.25 times Row 3: \([0, 0, 0, -5.25 - 2.25 \times 13/3]\).
14. Calculate \(c_3 = 13/3\).
15. Substitute \(c_3\) into Row 2: \(c_2 + 0.25 \times 13/3 = -0.25\), so \(c_2 = -0.25 - (0.25 \times 13/3) = -0.25 - 13/12 = -16/12 = -4/3\).
16. Substitute \(c_2\) and \(c_3\) into Row 1: \(c_1 - (-4/3) - (13/3) = 4\), so \(c_1 + 4/3 - 13/3 = 4\), \(c_1 - 9/3 = 4\), \(c_1 = 4 + 3 = 7\).
17. The vector in \(W\) is \(\mathbf{y}_W = 7\mathbf{u}_1 - \frac{4}{3}\mathbf{u}_2 + \frac{13}{3}\mathbf{u}_3\).
18. The orthogonal component is \(\mathbf{y}_\perp = \mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W\).
19. The final answer: \(\mathbf{y} = \mathbf{y}_W + \mathbf{y}_\perp = 7\mathbf{u}_1 - \frac{4}{3}\mathbf{u}_2 + \frac{13}{3}\mathbf{u}_3 + \mathbf{y}_\perp\), where \(\mathbf{y}_\perp\) is orthogonal to \(W\).
