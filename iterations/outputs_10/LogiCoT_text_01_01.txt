1. Let \( A = \begin{bmatrix}8 & 6 \\ 5 & 4\end{bmatrix} \); to find \( A^{-1} \), first compute the determinant: \( \det(A) = (8)(4) - (6)(5) = 32 - 30 = 2 \).  
2. Since \( \det(A) \neq 0 \), \( A \) is invertible by the invertibility criterion for square matrices.  
3. The formula for the inverse of a \( 2 \times 2 \) matrix \( \begin{bmatrix}a & b \\ c & d\end{bmatrix} \) is \( \frac{1}{ad-bc} \begin{bmatrix}d & -b \\ -c & a\end{bmatrix} \); applying this to \( A \), we get \( A^{-1} = \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix}4 & -6 \\ -5 & 8\end{bmatrix} \).  
4. Multiply out the scalar to get \( A^{-1} = \begin{bmatrix}2 & -3 \\ -2.5 & 4\end{bmatrix} \).  
5. Final answer: The inverse of \( A \) is \( \boxed{\begin{bmatrix}2 & -3 \\ -2.5 & 4\end{bmatrix}} \).
