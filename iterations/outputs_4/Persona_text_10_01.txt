1. The given matrix is $A = \begin{bmatrix}1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ 3 & 9 & -1 & 5 \\ 2 & -6 & 4 & -3 \\ -4 & 12 & 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix}$ and its echelon form is $\begin{bmatrix}1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 & -7 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}$.
2. To find a basis for $\text{Col }A$, we identify the pivot columns in the echelon form, which are columns 1, 3, and 4, so the corresponding columns from the original matrix $A$ form a basis: $\left\{ \begin{bmatrix}1\\3\\2\\-4\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}2\\-1\\4\\2\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}-4\\5\\-3\\7\end{bmatrix} \right\}$.
3. To find a basis for $\text{Nul }A$, we solve $A\vec{x} = \vec{0}$ using the echelon form; let $\vec{x} = \begin{bmatrix}x_1\\x_2\\x_3\\x_4\end{bmatrix}$, and since columns 2 is free, set $x_2 = t$ and back-solve: from row 3, $5x_4 = 0 \implies x_4 = 0$; from row 2, $5x_3 - 7x_4 = 0 \implies x_3 = 0$; from row 1, $x_1 - 3x_2 + 2x_3 - 4x_4 = 0 \implies x_1 = 3x_2$; so the general solution is $\vec{x} = t\begin{bmatrix}3\\1\\0\\0\end{bmatrix}$, and thus a basis for $\text{Nul }A$ is $\left\{ \begin{bmatrix}3\\1\\0\\0\end{bmatrix} \right\}$.
4. The dimension of $\text{Col }A$ is $3$ (since there are 3 pivot columns), and the dimension of $\text{Nul }A$ is $1$ (since there is 1 free variable).
