1. Write the vectors as columns of a matrix \(A\): \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & -3 & 1 \\ -3 & 2 & 1 & -8 & -6 \\ 2 & -3 & 6 & 7 & 9 \end{bmatrix}\).  
2. Use row reduction to find the rank and a basis; start by keeping the first column as the pivot and eliminate below: add 3 times row 1 to row 3 and subtract 2 times row 1 from row 4:  \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & -3 & 1 \\ 0 & 2 & -8 & 9 & 0 \\ 0 & -3 & 12 & 5 & 5 \end{bmatrix}\).  
3. Eliminate below the second pivot (row 2, column 2): subtract 2 times row 2 from row 3 and add 3 times row 2 to row 4:  \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & -3 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & -12 & 15 & -2 \\ 0 & 0 & 18 & -4 & 8 \end{bmatrix}\).  
4. For the third pivot, divide row 3 by \(-12\) to make it 1:  \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & -3 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & -\frac{5}{4} & \frac{1}{6} \\ 0 & 0 & 18 & -4 & 8 \end{bmatrix}\).  
5. Eliminate below the third pivot by subtracting 18 times row 3 from row 4:  \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & -3 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & -\frac{5}{4} & \frac{1}{6} \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 18.5 & 5 \end{bmatrix}\).  
6. Divide row 4 by 18.5 to make the fourth pivot 1:  \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & -3 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & -\frac{5}{4} & \frac{1}{6} \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 & \frac{10}{37} \end{bmatrix}\).  
7. Since we have 4 pivots, the rank is 4, so any 4 linearly independent vectors form a basis; the first four columns correspond to the pivot columns.
