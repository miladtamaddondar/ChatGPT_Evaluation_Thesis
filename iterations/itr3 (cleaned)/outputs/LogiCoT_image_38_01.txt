1. Identify the basis \(\mathcal{B}\) for \(\mathbb{P}_3\): The basis is \(\{1,\, 2t,\, -2 + 4t^2,\, -12t + 8t^3\}\). 
2. Express \(p(t) = 7 - 12t - 8t^2 + 12t^3\) as a linear combination of the basis vectors: Let \(p(t) = a\cdot 1 + b\cdot 2t + c\cdot (-2 + 4t^2) + d\cdot (-12t + 8t^3)\). 
3. Expand the linear combination: \(p(t) = a + 2b t + c(-2) + 4c t^2 + d(-12t) + 8d t^3 = a - 2c + (2b - 12d)t + 4c t^2 + 8d t^3\). 
4. Match coefficients with \(p(t) = 7 - 12t - 8t^2 + 12t^3\): \(a - 2c = 7\), \(2b - 12d = -12\), \(4c = -8\), \(8d = 12\). 
5. Solve \(4c = -8\) to get \(c = -2\); verify by substitution: \(4(-2) = -8\), correct.
6. Solve \(8d = 12\) to get \(d = \frac{12}{8} = \frac{3}{2}\); verify by substitution: \(8 \times \frac{3}{2} = 12\), correct. 
7. Substitute \(c = -2\) into \(a - 2c = 7\): \(a - 2(-2) = 7 \implies a + 4 = 7 \implies a = 3\); verify: \(3 - 2(-2) = 3 + 4 = 7\), correct. 
8. Substitute \(d = \frac{3}{2}\) into \(2b - 12d = -12\): \(2b - 12 \times \frac{3}{2} = -12 \implies 2b - 18 = -12 \implies 2b = 6 \implies b = 3\); verify: \(2 \times 3 - 12 \times \frac{3}{2} = 6 - 18 = -12\), correct. 
9. The coordinate vector of \(p\) relative to \(\mathcal{B}\) is \(\begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 3 \\ -2 \\ \frac{3}{2} \end{bmatrix}\).
