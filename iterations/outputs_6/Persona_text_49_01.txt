1. Since $A = QR$ and $Q$ is given, solve for $R$ by multiplying both sides by $Q^T$ to get $Q^T A = Q^T Q R = R$ (since $Q$ is orthonormal, $Q^T Q = I$), so $R = Q^T A$.
2. Compute $Q^T$; since $Q = \begin{bmatrix} 5/6 & -1/6 \\ 1/6 & 5/6 \\ -3/6 & 1/6 \\ 1/6 & 3/6 \end{bmatrix}$, then $Q^T = \begin{bmatrix} 5/6 & 1/6 & -3/6 & 1/6 \\ -1/6 & 5/6 & 1/6 & 3/6 \end{bmatrix}$.
3. Compute $R = Q^T A$ by multiplying $Q^T$ and $A$; $R = \begin{bmatrix} 5/6 & 1/6 & -3/6 & 1/6 \\ -1/6 & 5/6 & 1/6 & 3/6 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 9 \\ 1 & 7 \\ -3 & -5 \\ 1 & 5 \end{bmatrix}$.
4. Calculate the $(1,1)$ entry: $(5/6) \cdot 5 + (1/6) \cdot 1 + (-3/6) \cdot (-3) + (1/6) \cdot 1 = 25/6 + 1/6 + 9/6 + 1/6 = 36/6 = 6$.
5. Calculate the $(1,2)$ entry: $(5/6) \cdot 9 + (1/6) \cdot 7 + (-3/6) \cdot (-5) + (1/6) \cdot 5 = 45/6 + 7/6 + 15/6 + 5/6 = 72/6 = 12$.
6. Calculate the $(2,1)$ entry: $(-1/6) \cdot 5 + (5/6) \cdot 1 + (1/6) \cdot (-3) + (3/6) \cdot 1 = -5/6 + 5/6 -3/6 + 3/6 = 0$.
7. Calculate the $(2,2)$ entry: $(-1/6) \cdot 9 + (5/6) \cdot 7 + (1/6) \cdot (-5) + (3/6) \cdot 5 = -9/6 + 35/6 -5/6 + 15/6 = (35 + 15 - 9 - 5)/6 = 36/6 = 6$.
8. Thus, $R = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 12 \\ 0 & 6 \end{bmatrix}$.
9. To check, compute $QR = \begin{bmatrix} 5/6 & -1/6 \\ 1/6 & 5/6 \\ -3/6 & 1/6 \\ 1/6 & 3/6 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 12 \\ 0 & 6 \end{bmatrix}$.
10. First column: $6 \cdot$ first column of $Q$ $= \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ 1 \\ -3 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}$, which matches the first column of $A$.
11. Second column: $12 \cdot$ first column of $Q$ $+ 6 \cdot$ second column of $Q$ $= 12 \cdot \begin{bmatrix} 5/6 \\ 1/6 \\ -3/6 \\ 1/6 \end{bmatrix} + 6 \cdot \begin{bmatrix} -1/6 \\ 5/6 \\ 1/6 \\ 3/6 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 10 \\ 2 \\ -6 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 5 \\ 1 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 9 \\ 7 \\ -5 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix}$, which matches the second column of $A$.
12. Therefore, the upper triangular matrix $R$ such that $A = QR$ is $\boxed{\begin{bmatrix} 6 & 12 \\ 0 & 6 \end{bmatrix}}$.
