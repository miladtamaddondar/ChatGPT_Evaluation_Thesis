1. Write each basis vector in \(\mathcal{B}\) in terms of the standard basis \(\{1, t, t^2\}\): \(1-3t^2 = \begin{bmatrix}1\\0\\-3\end{bmatrix}\), \(2+t-5t^2 = \begin{bmatrix}2\\1\\-5\end{bmatrix}\), \(1+2t = \begin{bmatrix}1\\2\\0\end{bmatrix}\).
2. The change-of-coordinates matrix from \(\mathcal{B}\) to the standard basis is formed by placing these as columns: \(\begin{bmatrix}1 & 2 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 \\ -3 & -5 & 0\end{bmatrix}\).
3. To write \(t^2\) as a linear combination of the polynomials in \(\mathcal{B}\), set up the equation \(a(1-3t^2) + b(2+t-5t^2) + c(1+2t) = t^2\), which gives the system: \(\begin{cases} a + 2b + c = 0 \\ b + 2c = 0 \\ -3a - 5b = 1 \end{cases}\).
4. Write the augmented matrix: \(\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 2 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & 0 \\ -3 & -5 & 0 & 1 \end{array}\right]\).
5. \(R_3 + 3R_1 \rightarrow R_3:\) \(\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 2 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 3 & 1 \end{array}\right]\).
6. \(R_3 - R_2 \rightarrow R_3:\) \(\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 2 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 1 \end{array}\right]\).
7. \(R_2 - 2R_3 \rightarrow R_2:\) \(\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 2 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & -2 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 1 \end{array}\right]\).
8. \(R_1 - R_3 \rightarrow R_1:\) \(\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 2 & 0 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & -2 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 1 \end{array}\right]\).
9. \(R_1 - 2R_2 \rightarrow R_1:\) \(\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & 0 & 3 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & -2 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 1 \end{array}\right]\).
10. The coordinates of \(t^2\) in basis \(\mathcal{B}\) are \(\begin{bmatrix}3\\-2\\1\end{bmatrix}\), so \(t^2 = 3(1-3t^2) - 2(2+t-5t^2) + 1(1+2t)\).
