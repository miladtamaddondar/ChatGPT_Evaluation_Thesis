1. The standard matrix for a rotation by angle \(\theta\) counterclockwise in \(\mathbb{R}^2\) is \(\begin{bmatrix} \cos\theta & -\sin\theta \\ \sin\theta & \cos\theta \end{bmatrix}\).  
2. Substitute \(\theta = \frac{3\pi}{2}\) into the rotation matrix: \(\cos\left(\frac{3\pi}{2}\right) = 0\) and \(\sin\left(\frac{3\pi}{2}\right) = -1\).  
3. Plugging these values in, the matrix becomes \(\begin{bmatrix} 0 & -(-1) \\ -1 & 0 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\).  
4. To verify, check that this matrix rotates the vector \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}\) to \(\begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix}\), which is correct for a \(270^\circ\) (or \(\frac{3\pi}{2}\) radians) counterclockwise rotation.  
5. Final answer: The standard matrix of \(T\) is \(\boxed{\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 0 \end{bmatrix}}\).
