1. To find a vector $\mathbf{x}$ such that $T(\mathbf{x}) = \mathbf{b}$, we need to solve $A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{b}$, where $A = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 & -2 \\ -2 & 1 & 6 \\ 3 & -2 & -5\end{bmatrix}$ and $\mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix}-1 \\ 7 \\ -3\end{bmatrix}$.
2. Let $\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix}x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3\end{bmatrix}$, so the system is $\begin{cases} x_1 - 2x_3 = -1 \\ -2x_1 + x_2 + 6x_3 = 7 \\ 3x_1 - 2x_2 - 5x_3 = -3 \end{cases}$.
3. From the first equation, $x_1 - 2x_3 = -1 \implies x_1 = -1 + 2x_3$.
4. Substitute $x_1$ into the second equation: $-2(-1 + 2x_3) + x_2 + 6x_3 = 7 \implies 2 - 4x_3 + x_2 + 6x_3 = 7$.
5. Simplify: $2 + x_2 + 2x_3 = 7 \implies x_2 + 2x_3 = 5 \implies x_2 = 5 - 2x_3$.
6. Substitute $x_1$ and $x_2$ into the third equation: $3(-1 + 2x_3) - 2(5 - 2x_3) - 5x_3 = -3$.
7. Expand: $-3 + 6x_3 - 10 + 4x_3 - 5x_3 = -3$.
8. Combine like terms: $(-3 - 10) + (6x_3 + 4x_3 - 5x_3) = -3 \implies -13 + 5x_3 = -3$.
9. Solve for $x_3$: $5x_3 = 10 \implies x_3 = 2$.
10. Substitute $x_3 = 2$ into previous results: $x_1 = -1 + 2(2) = 3$, $x_2 = 5 - 2(2) = 1$.
11. Thus, the solution is $\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix}3 \\ 1 \\ 2\end{bmatrix}$.
12. Since each variable has a unique value, the solution is unique.
