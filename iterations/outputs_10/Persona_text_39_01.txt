1. Recognize that $\mathbf{u}\mathbf{v}^T$ is the outer product of $\mathbf{u}$ and $\mathbf{v}$, producing a $2 \times 3$ matrix.
2. Let $\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} v_1 \\ v_2 \\ v_3 \end{bmatrix}$, so $\mathbf{u}\mathbf{v}^T = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} v_1 & v_2 & v_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1v_1 & 1v_2 & 1v_3 \\ 2v_1 & 2v_2 & 2v_3 \end{bmatrix}$.
3. Set the resulting matrix equal to the given matrix: $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 4 \\ 2 & -6 & 8 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} v_1 & v_2 & v_3 \\ 2v_1 & 2v_2 & 2v_3 \end{bmatrix}$.
4. Equate corresponding entries: $v_1 = 1$, $v_2 = -3$, $v_3 = 4$ from the first row.
5. Check the second row: $2v_1 = 2$, $2v_2 = -6$, $2v_3 = 8$; these are satisfied by $v_1 = 1$, $v_2 = -3$, $v_3 = 4$.
6. The solution is $\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix}$.
