1. Write the equation $T(\mathbf{x}) = (3, 8)$ in terms of components: $T(x_1, x_2) = (x_1 + x_2, 4x_1 + 5x_2) = (3, 8)$.  
2. Set up the system of linear equations: $x_1 + x_2 = 3$ and $4x_1 + 5x_2 = 8$.  
3. Express the system in matrix form: $\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 4 & 5 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ 8 \end{pmatrix}$.  
4. Compute the determinant of the coefficient matrix to check invertibility: $\det\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 4 & 5 \end{pmatrix} = (1)(5) - (1)(4) = 1$, which is nonzero, so the matrix is invertible.  
5. Find the inverse of the matrix: $\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 4 & 5 \end{pmatrix}^{-1} = \frac{1}{1} \begin{pmatrix} 5 & -1 \\ -4 & 1 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 5 & -1 \\ -4 & 1 \end{pmatrix}$.  
6. Multiply both sides by the inverse to solve for $\begin{pmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{pmatrix}$: $\begin{pmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 5 & -1 \\ -4 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ 8 \end{pmatrix}$.  
7. Compute $x_1$: $x_1 = 5 \cdot 3 + (-1) \cdot 8 = 15 - 8 = 7$.  
8. Compute $x_2$: $x_2 = -4 \cdot 3 + 1 \cdot 8 = -12 + 8 = -4$.  
9. Verify the solution by plugging back into the original equations: $x_1 + x_2 = 7 + (-4) = 3$ and $4x_1 + 5x_2 = 4 \cdot 7 + 5 \cdot (-4) = 28 - 20 = 8$, both correct.  
10. Final answer: $\boxed{\mathbf{x} = (7, -4)}$.
