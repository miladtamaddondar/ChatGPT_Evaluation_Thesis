1. The matrix $A$ has 2 columns and 4 rows: $A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -6 \\ -1 & 3 \\ -4 & 12 \\ 3 & -9 \\ \end{bmatrix}$.
2. (a) The null space $\text{Nul }A$ consists of all solutions $x$ to $Ax = 0$, where $x \in \mathbb{R}^2$, so $\text{Nul }A$ is a subspace of $\mathbb{R}^2$; thus, $k = 2$.
3. (b) The column space $\text{Col }A$ is the span of the columns of $A$, which are vectors in $\mathbb{R}^4$, so $\text{Col }A$ is a subspace of $\mathbb{R}^4$; thus, $k = 4$.
