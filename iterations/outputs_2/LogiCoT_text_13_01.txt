1. Subtract \(\lambda = 4\) times the identity matrix from \(A\) to form \(A - 4I\): \(A - 4I = \begin{bmatrix}3-4 & 0 & 2 & 0 \\ 1 & 3-4 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1-4 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 4-4\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}-1 & 0 & 2 & 0 \\ 1 & -1 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0\end{bmatrix}\).
2. Set up the equation \((A - 4I)\vec{x} = 0\) with \(\vec{x} = \begin{bmatrix}x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \\ x_4\end{bmatrix}\): \(\begin{bmatrix}-1 & 0 & 2 & 0 \\ 1 & -1 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \\ x_4\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0\end{bmatrix}\).
3. Write the system of equations: \(-x_1 + 2x_3 = 0\), \(x_1 - x_2 + x_3 = 0\), \(x_2 - 3x_3 = 0\), \(0 = 0\).
4. Solve the third equation for \(x_2\): \(x_2 - 3x_3 = 0 \implies x_2 = 3x_3\).
5. Substitute \(x_2 = 3x_3\) into the second equation: \(x_1 - (3x_3) + x_3 = 0 \implies x_1 - 2x_3 = 0 \implies x_1 = 2x_3\).
6. Substitute \(x_1 = 2x_3\) into the first equation: \(-x_1 + 2x_3 = -2x_3 + 2x_3 = 0\), which is satisfied for all \(x_3\).
7. The variable \(x_4\) does not appear in any equation, so it is free; thus, the general solution is \(\vec{x} = \begin{bmatrix}2x_3 \\ 3x_3 \\ x_3 \\ x_4\end{bmatrix} = x_3\begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ 0\end{bmatrix} + x_4\begin{bmatrix}0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 1\end{bmatrix}\).
8. Therefore, a basis for the eigenspace corresponding to \(\lambda = 4\) is \(\left\{ \begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ 0\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 1\end{bmatrix} \right\}\).
