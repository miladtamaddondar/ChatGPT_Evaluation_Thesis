1. Write the matrix \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 2 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \) and set up the characteristic equation \( \det(A - \lambda I) = 0 \). 
2. Compute \( A - \lambda I = \begin{bmatrix} 0-\lambda & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 2-\lambda & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 1-\lambda \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -\lambda & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 2-\lambda & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 1-\lambda \end{bmatrix} \). 
3. Find the determinant: \( \det(A - \lambda I) = (-\lambda) \cdot \det\begin{bmatrix} 2-\lambda & 5 \\ 0 & 1-\lambda \end{bmatrix} \). 
4. Compute the \(2 \times 2\) determinant: \( \det\begin{bmatrix} 2-\lambda & 5 \\ 0 & 1-\lambda \end{bmatrix} = (2-\lambda)(1-\lambda) - 0 = (2-\lambda)(1-\lambda) \). 
5. Multiply out: \( \det(A - \lambda I) = (-\lambda)(2-\lambda)(1-\lambda) \). 
6. Set the characteristic polynomial to zero: \( (-\lambda)(2-\lambda)(1-\lambda) = 0 \). 
7. Solve for eigenvalues: \( \lambda_1 = 0, \lambda_2 = 1, \lambda_3 = 2 \).
