1. To compute \( AD \), multiply matrix \( A \) by matrix \( D \): \( AD = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 1 & 4 & 5 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 \end{bmatrix} \). 
2. Multiply each row of \( A \) by the corresponding column of \( D \): \( AD = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \cdot 2 + 1 \cdot 0 + 1 \cdot 0 & 1 \cdot 0 + 1 \cdot 3 + 1 \cdot 0 & 1 \cdot 0 + 1 \cdot 0 + 1 \cdot 5 \\ 1 \cdot 2 + 2 \cdot 0 + 3 \cdot 0 & 1 \cdot 0 + 2 \cdot 3 + 3 \cdot 0 & 1 \cdot 0 + 2 \cdot 0 + 3 \cdot 5 \\ 1 \cdot 2 + 4 \cdot 0 + 5 \cdot 0 & 1 \cdot 0 + 4 \cdot 3 + 5 \cdot 0 & 1 \cdot 0 + 4 \cdot 0 + 5 \cdot 5 \end{bmatrix} \). 
3. Simplify the entries: \( AD = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 3 & 5 \\ 2 & 6 & 15 \\ 2 & 12 & 25 \end{bmatrix} \). 
4. To verify, recall that multiplying by a diagonal matrix scales each column of \( A \) by the corresponding diagonal entry in \( D \), which matches the result above. 
5. To compute \( DA \), multiply matrix \( D \) by matrix \( A \): \( DA = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 1 & 4 & 5 \end{bmatrix} \). 
6. 7. Simplify the entries: \( DA = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 2 & 2 \\ 3 & 6 & 9 \\ 5 & 20 & 25 \end{bmatrix} \). 
8. To verify, recall that multiplying by a diagonal matrix on the left scales each row of \( A \) by the corresponding diagonal entry in \( D \), which matches the result above.
