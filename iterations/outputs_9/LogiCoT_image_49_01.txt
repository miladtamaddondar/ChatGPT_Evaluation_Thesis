1. The problem asks us to find an upper triangular matrix \( R \) such that \( A = QR \), given \( A \) and \( Q \), where \( Q \) is already orthonormal (from Gram-Schmidt).  
2. To solve for \( R \), use the property that if \( Q \) has orthonormal columns, then \( Q^T Q = I \), so multiply both sides of \( A = QR \) by \( Q^T \) to get \( Q^T A = Q^T Q R = R \).  
3. Compute \( Q^T \), which is the transpose of \( Q \): \( Q^T = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{5}{6} & \frac{1}{6} & -\frac{3}{6} & \frac{1}{6} \\ -\frac{1}{6} & \frac{5}{6} & \frac{1}{6} & \frac{3}{6} \end{bmatrix} \).  
4. Compute \( R = Q^T A \):  \[R = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{5}{6} & \frac{1}{6} & -\frac{3}{6} & \frac{1}{6} \\ -\frac{1}{6} & \frac{5}{6} & \frac{1}{6} & \frac{3}{6} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 9 \\ 1 & 7 \\ -3 & -5 \\ 1 & 5 \end{bmatrix}\]
5. Calculate the first row of \( R \):  First column: \( \frac{5}{6} \cdot 5 + \frac{1}{6} \cdot 1 + (-\frac{3}{6}) \cdot (-3) + \frac{1}{6} \cdot 1 = \frac{25}{6} + \frac{1}{6} + \frac{9}{6} + \frac{1}{6} = \frac{36}{6} = 6 \).  Second column: \( \frac{5}{6} \cdot 9 + \frac{1}{6} \cdot 7 + (-\frac{3}{6}) \cdot (-5) + \frac{1}{6} \cdot 5 = \frac{45}{6} + \frac{7}{6} + \frac{15}{6} + \frac{5}{6} = \frac{72}{6} = 12 \).  
6. Calculate the second row of \( R \):  First column: \( -\frac{1}{6} \cdot 5 + \frac{5}{6} \cdot 1 + \frac{1}{6} \cdot (-3) + \frac{3}{6} \cdot 1 = -\frac{5}{6} + \frac{5}{6} -\frac{3}{6} + \frac{3}{6} = 0 \).  Second column: \( -\frac{1}{6} \cdot 9 + \frac{5}{6} \cdot 7 + \frac{1}{6} \cdot (-5) + \frac{3}{6} \cdot 5 = -\frac{9}{6} + \frac{35}{6} -\frac{5}{6} + \frac{15}{6} = \frac{36}{6} = 6 \).  
7. Thus, \( R = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 12 \\ 0 & 6 \end{bmatrix} \), which is upper triangular as required.  
8. Verification: Compute \( QR \) to check if it equals \( A \):  First column: \( 6 \cdot \begin{bmatrix} 5/6 \\ 1/6 \\ -3/6 \\ 1/6 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ 1 \\ -3 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \).  Second column: \( 12 \cdot \begin{bmatrix} 5/6 \\ 1/6 \\ -3/6 \\ 1/6 \end{bmatrix} + 6 \cdot \begin{bmatrix} -1/6 \\ 5/6 \\ 1/6 \\ 3/6 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 10 \\ 2 \\ -6 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 5 \\ 1 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 9 \\ 7 \\ -5 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix} \), which matches the second column of \( A \).  
9. Final answer: The upper triangular matrix \( R \) such that \( A = QR \) is \( \boxed{\begin{bmatrix} 6 & 12 \\ 0 & 6 \end{bmatrix}} \).
