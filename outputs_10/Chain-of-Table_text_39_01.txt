The equation $\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 4 \\ 2 & -6 & 8 \\ \end{bmatrix} = \mathbf{u}\mathbf{v}^T$ means the $2 \times 3$ matrix equals the outer product of $\mathbf{u} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}$ and $\mathbf{v}^T = \begin{bmatrix} v_1 & v_2 & v_3 \end{bmatrix}$, so $\mathbf{u}\mathbf{v}^T = \begin{bmatrix} 1 v_1 & 1 v_2 & 1 v_3 \\ 2 v_1 & 2 v_2 & 2 v_3 \end{bmatrix}$.

2. Set up the equations by matching entries: $1 v_1 = 1$, $1 v_2 = -3$, $1 v_3 = 4$, $2 v_1 = 2$, $2 v_2 = -6$, $2 v_3 = 8$.

3. Solve for $v_1, v_2, v_3$: From $1 v_1 = 1$ and $2 v_1 = 2$, $v_1 = 1$; from $1 v_2 = -3$ and $2 v_2 = -6$, $v_2 = -3$; from $1 v_3 = 4$ and $2 v_3 = 8$, $v_3 = 4$.

4. Therefore, the solution is $\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix}$.
