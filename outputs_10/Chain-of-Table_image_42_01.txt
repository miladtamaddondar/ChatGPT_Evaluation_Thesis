Express \( \mathbf{y}_W = c_1\mathbf{u}_1 + c_2\mathbf{u}_2 + c_3\mathbf{u}_3 \) and set up the equation \( \mathbf{y} = c_1\mathbf{u}_1 + c_2\mathbf{u}_2 + c_3\mathbf{u}_3 + \mathbf{y}_\perp \). 3. The system is inconsistent, so the vectors \( \mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_2, \mathbf{u}_3 \) are linearly dependent and do not span all of \( \mathbb{R}^4 \); thus, we solve the least squares problem using the normal equations \( \mathbf{U}^T\mathbf{U}\mathbf{c} = \mathbf{U}^T\mathbf{y} \). 11. Compute \( \mathbf{U}^T\mathbf{U} \) and \( \mathbf{U}^T\mathbf{y} \):

12. Solve the system:
\[
\begin{bmatrix}

0 & 15 & -2 \\

\begin{bmatrix}

13. Row 1: \( 3c_1 + c_3 = 6 \implies c_3 = 6 - 3c_1 \). 14. Row 3: \( c_1 - 2c_2 + 3c_3 = 2 \implies c_1 - 2c_2 + 3(6-3c_1) = 2 \implies c_1 - 2c_2 + 18 - 9c_1 = 2 \implies -8c_1 - 2c_2 = -16 \implies 4c_1 + c_2 = 8 \implies c_2 = 8 - 4c_1 \). 15. Row 2: \( 15c_2 - 2c_3 = 14 \implies 15c_2 - 2(6-3c_1) = 14 \implies 15c_2 - 12 + 6c_1 = 14 \implies 15c_2 + 6c_1 = 26 \). 16. Substitute \( c_2 = 8 - 4c_1 \) into previous: \( 15(8-4c_1) + 6c_1 = 26 \implies 120 - 60c_1 + 6c_1 = 26 \implies -54c_1 = -94 \implies c_1 = \frac{47}{27} \). 17. \( c_2 = 8 - 4 \cdot \frac{47}{27} = 8 - \frac{188}{27} = \frac{216-188}{27} = \frac{28}{27} \). 18. \( c_3 = 6 - 3 \cdot \frac{47}{27} = 6 - \frac{141}{27} = \frac{162-141}{27} = \frac{21}{27} = \frac{7}{9} \). 19. Thus, \( \mathbf{y}_W = \frac{47}{27}\mathbf{u}_1 + \frac{28}{27}\mathbf{u}_2 + \frac{7}{9}\mathbf{u}_3 \) and \( \mathbf{y}_\perp = \mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W \). 20. Final answer: \( \boxed{\mathbf{y} = \mathbf{y}_W + \mathbf{y}_\perp = \frac{47}{27}\mathbf{u}_1 + \frac{28}{27}\mathbf{u}_2 + \frac{7}{9}\mathbf{u}_3 + \mathbf{y}_\perp} \).
