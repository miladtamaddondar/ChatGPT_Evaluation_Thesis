1. The given matrix is $A = \begin{bmatrix}1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ 3 & 9 & -1 & 5 \\ 2 & -6 & 4 & -3 \\ -4 & 12 & 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix}$.
2. The echelon form of $A$ is $\begin{bmatrix}1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 & -7 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}$.
3. A basis for $\text{Col }A$ is given by the pivot columns of $A$: $\left\{ \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 3 \\ 2 \\ -4\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}2 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 2\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}-4 \\ 5 \\ -3 \\ 7\end{bmatrix} \right\}$.
4. To find a basis for $\text{Nul }A$, solve $A\vec{x} = 0$ using the echelon form. Let $x_4 = t$, $x_3 = \frac{7}{5}t$, $x_2$ is free, $x_1 = 3x_2 - 2x_3 + 4x_4$. Substitute $x_3$ and $x_4$ in terms of $t$: $x_1 = 3x_2 - 2(\frac{7}{5}t) + 4t = 3x_2 - \frac{14}{5}t + 4t = 3x_2 + \frac{6}{5}t$. Thus, the general solution is $\vec{x} = x_2\begin{bmatrix}3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0\end{bmatrix} + t\begin{bmatrix}\frac{6}{5} \\ 0 \\ \frac{7}{5} \\ 1\end{bmatrix}$, so a basis for $\text{Nul }A$ is $\left\{ \begin{bmatrix}3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}\frac{6}{5} \\ 0 \\ \frac{7}{5} \\ 1\end{bmatrix} \right\}$.
5. The dimension of $\text{Col }A$ is $3$, and the dimension of $\text{Nul }A$ is $2$.
