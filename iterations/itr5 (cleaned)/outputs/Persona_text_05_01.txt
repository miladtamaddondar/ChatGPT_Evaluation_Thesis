1. To find \( k \) such that \( AB = BA \), first compute \( AB \): \( AB = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 5 \\ -3 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 & -5 \\ 3 & k \end{bmatrix} \).
2. Multiply the matrices: \( AB = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \times 4 + 5 \times 3 & 2 \times (-5) + 5 \times k \\ -3 \times 4 + 1 \times 3 & -3 \times (-5) + 1 \times k \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 8 + 15 & -10 + 5k \\ -12 + 3 & 15 + k \end{bmatrix} \).
3. Simplify \( AB \): \( AB = \begin{bmatrix} 23 & -10 + 5k \\ -9 & 15 + k \end{bmatrix} \).
4. Now compute \( BA \): \( BA = \begin{bmatrix} 4 & -5 \\ 3 & k \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 5 \\ -3 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \).
5. Multiply the matrices: \( BA = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \times 2 + (-5) \times (-3) & 4 \times 5 + (-5) \times 1 \\ 3 \times 2 + k \times (-3) & 3 \times 5 + k \times 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 8 + 15 & 20 - 5 \\ 6 - 3k & 15 + k \end{bmatrix} \).
6. Simplify \( BA \): \( BA = \begin{bmatrix} 23 & 15 \\ 6 - 3k & 15 + k \end{bmatrix} \).
7. Set \( AB = BA \) and equate corresponding entries: \( \begin{bmatrix} 23 & -10 + 5k \\ -9 & 15 + k \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 23 & 15 \\ 6 - 3k & 15 + k \end{bmatrix} \).
8. The (1,2) entry gives \( -10 + 5k = 15 \), so \( 5k = 25 \) and \( k = 5 \).
9. The (2,1) entry gives \( -9 = 6 - 3k \), so \( -9 - 6 = -3k \) and \( -15 = -3k \), so \( k = 5 \).
10. The (2,2) entry gives \( 15 + k = 15 + k \), which is always true.
11. Therefore, the only value of \( k \) that makes \( AB = BA \) is \( \boxed{5} \).
