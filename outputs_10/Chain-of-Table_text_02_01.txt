1. Write the equation $\mathbf{x} = c_1 \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} + c_2 \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix}$ where $\left[ \mathbf{x} \right]_B = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}$ so $c_1 = 3$, $c_2 = 2$.
2. Substitute $c_1$ and $c_2$ into the equation: $\mathbf{x} = 3 \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} + 2 \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix}$.
3. Compute the scalar multiplications: $3 \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix}$ and $2 \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix}$.
4. Add the vectors: $\begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 7 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}$.
5. Therefore, the vector $\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} 7 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}$.
