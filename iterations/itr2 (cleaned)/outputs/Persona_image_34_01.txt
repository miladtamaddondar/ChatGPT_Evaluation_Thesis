1. To find a basis for the space spanned by the vectors, we arrange them as columns of a matrix \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -3 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 & -3 & 1 \\ -3 & 2 & 1 & -8 & -6 \\ 2 & -3 & 6 & 7 & 9 \end{bmatrix}\).  
2. We perform row reduction (Gaussian elimination) to find the pivot columns, which correspond to the linearly independent vectors.  
3. Subtract 2 times column 1 from column 5: \(2 - 2 \times 1 = 0\), \(1 - 2 \times 0 = 1\), \(-6 - 2 \times -3 = 0\), \(9 - 2 \times 2 = 5\), so column 5 becomes \(\begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix}\).  
4. Subtract column 2 from column 5: \(0 - 0 = 0\), \(1 - 1 = 0\), \(0 - 2 = -2\), \(5 - (-3) = 8\), so column 5 becomes \(\begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ -2 \\ 8 \end{bmatrix}\).  
5. Add 2 times column 3 to column 5: \(0 + 2 \times -3 = -6\), \(0 + 2 \times -4 = -8\), \(-2 + 2 \times 1 = 0\), \(8 + 2 \times 6 = 20\), so column 5 becomes \(\begin{bmatrix} -6 \\ -8 \\ 0 \\ 20 \end{bmatrix}\).  
6. Subtract 6 times column 1 from column 3: \(-3 - 6 \times 1 = -9\), \(-4 - 6 \times 0 = -4\), \(1 - 6 \times -3 = 19\), \(6 - 6 \times 2 = -6\), so column 3 becomes \(\begin{bmatrix} -9 \\ -4 \\ 19 \\ -6 \end{bmatrix}\).  
7. Subtract 4 times column 2 from column 3: \(-9 - 4 \times 0 = -9\), \(-4 - 4 \times 1 = -8\), \(19 - 4 \times 2 = 11\), \(-6 - 4 \times -3 = 6\), so column 3 becomes \(\begin{bmatrix} -9 \\ -8 \\ 11 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix}\).

The row reduction shows that columns 1, 2, and 4 are linearly independent (as their leading entries cannot be made zero by combinations of previous columns), so a basis is given by \(\left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ -3 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \\ 2 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ -8 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \right\}\).
