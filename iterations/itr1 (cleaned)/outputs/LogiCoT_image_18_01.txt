1. Find the eigenvalues of \(A\) by solving \(\det(A - \lambda I) = 0\): \(\det\left(\begin{bmatrix} 0-\lambda & 1 \\ -3 & 4-\lambda \end{bmatrix}\right) = (0-\lambda)(4-\lambda) - (-3)(1) = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 3 = 0\). 
2. Factor and solve the characteristic equation: \(\lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 3 = (\lambda-1)(\lambda-3) = 0\), so the eigenvalues are \(\lambda_1 = 1\) and \(\lambda_2 = 3\). 
3. Find the eigenvector for \(\lambda_1 = 1\) by solving \((A - I)\mathbf{v} = 0\): \(A - I = \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ -3 & 3 \end{bmatrix}\), so \(\begin{bmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ -3 & 3 \end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}\) gives \(-x + y = 0\), so \(y = x\); thus, an eigenvector is \(\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}\). 
4. Find the eigenvector for \(\lambda_2 = 3\) by solving \((A - 3I)\mathbf{v} = 0\): \(A - 3I = \begin{bmatrix} -3 & 1 \\ -3 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\), so \(-3x + y = 0\), so \(y = 3x\); thus, an eigenvector is \(\mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix}\). 
5. Verify that the eigenvectors \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}\) and \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix}\) are linearly independent (since their determinant is \(1 \times 3 - 1 \times 1 = 2 \neq 0\)), so they form a basis. 
6. The basis \(\mathcal{B} = \left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} \right\}\) is a basis for \(\mathbb{R}^2\) such that \([T]_{\mathcal{B}}\) is diagonal.
