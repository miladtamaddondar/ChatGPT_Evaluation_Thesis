1. To diagonalize $A$, we first find its eigenvalues by solving $\det(A - \lambda I) = 0$. The characteristic equation is $\begin{vmatrix} 0-\lambda & 1 \\ -3 & 4-\lambda \end{vmatrix} = (0-\lambda)(4-\lambda) - (-3)(1) = \lambda^2 - 4\lambda + 3 = 0$.
2. Factoring, we get $(\lambda-1)(\lambda-3)=0$, so the eigenvalues are $\lambda_1=1$ and $\lambda_2=3$.
3. For $\lambda=1$, solve $(A-I)\mathbf{v}=0$: $A-I = \begin{bmatrix}-1 & 1 \\ -3 & 3\end{bmatrix}$. The augmented matrix is $\left[\begin{array}{cc|c}-1 & 1 & 0 \\ -3 & 3 & 0\end{array}\right]$.
4. Row 2 $\to$ Row 2 + 3Ã—Row 1: $\left[\begin{array}{cc|c}-1 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0\end{array}\right]$. The solution is $-v_1 + v_2 = 0 \implies v_2 = v_1$, so an eigenvector is $\begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 1\end{bmatrix}$.
5. For $\lambda=3$, solve $(A-3I)\mathbf{v}=0$: $A-3I = \begin{bmatrix}-3 & 1 \\ -3 & 1\end{bmatrix}$. The augmented matrix is $\left[\begin{array}{cc|c}-3 & 1 & 0 \\ -3 & 1 & 0\end{array}\right]$.
6. Row 2 $\to$ Row 2 - Row 1: $\left[\begin{array}{cc|c}-3 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0\end{array}\right]$. The solution is $-3v_1 + v_2 = 0 \implies v_2 = 3v_1$, so an eigenvector is $\begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 3\end{bmatrix}$.
7. Thus, a basis $\mathcal{B} = \left\{\begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 1\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 3\end{bmatrix}\right\}$ diagonalizes $A$, and $[T]_{\mathcal{B}} = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 \\ 0 & 3\end{bmatrix}$.
