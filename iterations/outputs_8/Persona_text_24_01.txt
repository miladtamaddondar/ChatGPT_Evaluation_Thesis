2. Let $\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix}x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \\ x_4\end{bmatrix}$, then the system is $\begin{cases} x_1 - 4x_2 + 7x_3 - 5x_4 = 0 \\ x_2 - 4x_3 + 3x_4 = 0 \\ 2x_1 - 6x_2 + 6x_3 - 4x_4 = 0 \end{cases}$.
3. From the second equation, solve for $x_2$: $x_2 = 4x_3 - 3x_4$.
4. Substitute $x_2$ into the first equation: $x_1 - 4(4x_3 - 3x_4) + 7x_3 - 5x_4 = 0$.
5. Simplify: $x_1 - 16x_3 + 12x_4 + 7x_3 - 5x_4 = 0 \implies x_1 - 9x_3 + 7x_4 = 0$.
6. Solve for $x_1$: $x_1 = 9x_3 - 7x_4$.
7. Substitute $x_1$ and $x_2$ into the third equation: $2(9x_3 - 7x_4) - 6(4x_3 - 3x_4) + 6x_3 - 4x_4 = 0$.
8. Expand: $18x_3 - 14x_4 - 24x_3 + 18x_4 + 6x_3 - 4x_4 = 0$.
9. Combine like terms: $(18 - 24 + 6)x_3 + (-14 + 18 - 4)x_4 = 0 \implies 0x_3 + 0x_4 = 0$.
10. This equation is always true, so $x_3$ and $x_4$ are free variables.
11. Therefore, the general solution is $\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix}9x_3 - 7x_4 \\ 4x_3 - 3x_4 \\ x_3 \\ x_4\end{bmatrix}$ for $x_3, x_4 \in \mathbb{R}$.
12. Final answer: All $\mathbf{x}$ mapped to zero are $\mathbf{x} = x_3\begin{bmatrix}9 \\ 4 \\ 1 \\ 0\end{bmatrix} + x_4\begin{bmatrix}-7 \\ -3 \\ 0 \\ 1\end{bmatrix}$ for $x_3, x_4 \in \mathbb{R}$.
