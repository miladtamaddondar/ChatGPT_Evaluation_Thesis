1. Let the columns of the matrix be \( \mathbf{a}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \\ -1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \), \( \mathbf{a}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \\ 4 \\ -4 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \), and \( \mathbf{a}_3 = \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ -4 \\ -3 \\ 7 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \).
2. Set \( \mathbf{u}_1 = \mathbf{a}_1 \).
3. Compute the projection of \( \mathbf{a}_2 \) onto \( \mathbf{u}_1 \): \( \mathrm{proj}_{\mathbf{u}_1} \mathbf{a}_2 = \frac{\mathbf{a}_2 \cdot \mathbf{u}_1}{\mathbf{u}_1 \cdot \mathbf{u}_1} \mathbf{u}_1 = \frac{2(1) + 1(-1) + 4(-1) + (-4)(1) + 2(1)}{1^2 + (-1)^2 + (-1)^2 + 1^2 + 1^2} \mathbf{u}_1 = \frac{2 - 1 - 4 - 4 + 2}{5} \mathbf{u}_1 = \frac{-5}{5} \mathbf{u}_1 = -\mathbf{u}_1 \).
4. Set \( \mathbf{u}_2 = \mathbf{a}_2 - \mathrm{proj}_{\mathbf{u}_1} \mathbf{a}_2 = \mathbf{a}_2 - (-\mathbf{u}_1) = \mathbf{a}_2 + \mathbf{u}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \\ 4 \\ -4 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \\ -1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 0 \\ 3 \\ -3 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} \).
5. Compute the projection of \( \mathbf{a}_3 \) onto \( \mathbf{u}_1 \): \( \mathrm{proj}_{\mathbf{u}_1} \mathbf{a}_3 = \frac{\mathbf{a}_3 \cdot \mathbf{u}_1}{\mathbf{u}_1 \cdot \mathbf{u}_1} \mathbf{u}_1 = \frac{5(1) + (-4)(-1) + (-3)(-1) + 7(1) + 1(1)}{5} \mathbf{u}_1 = \frac{5 + 4 + 3 + 7 + 1}{5} \mathbf{u}_1 = \frac{20}{5} \mathbf{u}_1 = 4\mathbf{u}_1 \).
6. Compute the projection of \( \mathbf{a}_3 \) onto \( \mathbf{u}_2 \): \( \mathrm{proj}_{\mathbf{u}_2} \mathbf{a}_3 = \frac{\mathbf{a}_3 \cdot \mathbf{u}_2}{\mathbf{u}_2 \cdot \mathbf{u}_2} \mathbf{u}_2 \), where \( \mathbf{a}_3 \cdot \mathbf{u}_2 = 5(3) + (-4)(0) + (-3)(3) + 7(-3) + 1(3) = 15 + 0 - 9 - 21 + 3 = -12 \) and \( \mathbf{u}_2 \cdot \mathbf{u}_2 = 3^2 + 0^2 + 3^2 + (-3)^2 + 3^2 = 9 + 0 + 9 + 9 + 9 = 36 \), so \( \mathrm{proj}_{\mathbf{u}_2} \mathbf{a}_3 = \frac{-12}{36} \mathbf{u}_2 = -\frac{1}{3} \mathbf{u}_2 \).
7. Set \( \mathbf{u}_3 = \mathbf{a}_3 - \mathrm{proj}_{\mathbf{u}_1} \mathbf{a}_3 - \mathrm{proj}_{\mathbf{u}_2} \mathbf{a}_3 = \mathbf{a}_3 - 4\mathbf{u}_1 + \frac{1}{3}\mathbf{u}_2 \).
8. Compute \( \mathbf{a}_3 - 4\mathbf{u}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ -4 \\ -3 \\ 7 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} - 4\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \\ -1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 5-4 \\ -4+4 \\ -3+4 \\ 7-4 \\ 1-4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ 3 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} \).
9. Compute \( \frac{1}{3}\mathbf{u}_2 = \frac{1}{3}\begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 0 \\ 3 \\ -3 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ -1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \).
10. Thus, \( \mathbf{u}_3 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ 3 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ -1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 0 \\ 2 \\ 2 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix} \).
11. The orthogonal basis for the column space is \( \left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \\ -1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 0 \\ 3 \\ -3 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 0 \\ 2 \\ 2 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix} \right\} \).
