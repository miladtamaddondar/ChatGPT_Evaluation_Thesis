1. Write the eigenvector $\mathbf{v}_1$ as $\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix}3/7 \\ 4/7\end{bmatrix}$ and note it is associated with eigenvalue $\lambda_1 = 1$ since $A$ is stochastic.
2. To find another eigenvector $\mathbf{v}_2$, solve $(A - \lambda_2 I)\mathbf{v}_2 = 0$ for the other eigenvalue. The trace of $A$ is $0.6 + 0.7 = 1.3$ and the determinant is $0.6 \times 0.7 - 0.3 \times 0.4 = 0.42 - 0.12 = 0.3$. The characteristic polynomial is $\lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3 = 0$.
3. Solve for $\lambda_2$: $\lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3 = 0 \implies \lambda = \frac{1.3 \pm \sqrt{1.69 - 1.2}}{2} = \frac{1.3 \pm \sqrt{0.49}}{2} = \frac{1.3 \pm 0.7}{2}$. Thus, $\lambda_1 = 1$, $\lambda_2 = 0.3$.
4. Set up the equation $(A - 0.3I)\mathbf{v}_2 = 0$: $A - 0.3I = \begin{bmatrix}0.6-0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.7-0.3\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.4\end{bmatrix}$.
5. Write the augmented matrix: $\left[\begin{array}{cc|c}0.3 & 0.3 & 0 \\ 0.4 & 0.4 & 0\end{array}\right]$.
6. Row 2 minus $\frac{4}{3}$ times Row 1: Row 2 becomes $0$'s, so the system is $0.3x + 0.3y = 0$.
7. Divide Row 1 by $0.3$: $\left[\begin{array}{cc|c}1 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0\end{array}\right]$.
8. The solution is $x = -y$, so take $y = 1$, $x = -1$, giving $\mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}-1 \\ 1\end{bmatrix}$.
9. Final answer: A basis for $\mathbb{R}^2$ consisting of $\mathbf{v}_1$ and another eigenvector $\mathbf{v}_2$ of $A$ is $\left\{\begin{bmatrix}\frac{3}{7} \\ \frac{4}{7}\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}-1 \\ 1\end{bmatrix}\right\}$.
