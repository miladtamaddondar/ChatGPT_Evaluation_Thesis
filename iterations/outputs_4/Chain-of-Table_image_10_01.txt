1. The given matrix is \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ -3 & 9 & -1 & 5 \\ 2 & -6 & 4 & -3 \\ -4 & 12 & 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix} \).  
2. The echelon form of \( A \) is \( \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 & -7 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \), so the pivot columns are 1, 3, and 4.  
3. A basis for \( \operatorname{Col} A \) is given by the corresponding columns of \( A \): \( \left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -3 \\ 2 \\ -4 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 4 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} -4 \\ 5 \\ -3 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \right\} \).  
4. To find a basis for \( \operatorname{Nul} A \), solve \( A\vec{x} = 0 \) using the echelon form. Let \( x_2 = s \) (free variable), and back-substitute: \( x_4 = 0 \), \( 5x_3 - 7x_4 = 0 \implies x_3 = 0 \), \( x_1 - 3x_2 + 2x_3 - 4x_4 = 0 \implies x_1 = 3x_2 \).  
5. Thus, the general solution is \( \vec{x} = s \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \), so a basis for \( \operatorname{Nul} A \) is \( \left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \right\} \).  
6. The dimension of \( \operatorname{Col} A \) is 3 and the dimension of \( \operatorname{Nul} A \) is 1.
