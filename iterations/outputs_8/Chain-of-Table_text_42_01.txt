1. Set up the equation $\mathbf{y} = c_1 \mathbf{u}_1 + c_2 \mathbf{u}_2 + c_3 \mathbf{u}_3 + \mathbf{z}$, where $\mathbf{z}$ is orthogonal to $W$. To find the projection, solve $c_1 \mathbf{u}_1 + c_2 \mathbf{u}_2 + c_3 \mathbf{u}_3 = \mathbf{y}$, or as an augmented matrix: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 & 3 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 & -1 \end{array}\right]$.
2. Row 2 minus Row 1: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 & -1 \end{array}\right]$.
3. Row 4 minus Row 1: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 0 & -1 & 2 & -5 \end{array}\right]$.
4. Row 3 unchanged, Row 4 plus Row 3: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 0 & 0 & 3 & -2 \end{array}\right]$.
5. Row 2 divided by 4: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 1 & 0.25 & -0.25 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 0 & 0 & 3 & -2 \end{array}\right]$.
6. Row 3 minus Row 2: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 1 & 0.25 & -0.25 \\ 0 & 0 & 0.75 & 3.25 \\ 0 & 0 & 3 & -2 \end{array}\right]$.
7. Row 4 minus 4 times Row 3: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 1 & 0.25 & -0.25 \\ 0 & 0 & 0.75 & 3.25 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & -15 \end{array}\right]$.
8. Back-substitute: Row 3 divided by 0.75: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 1 & 0.25 & -0.25 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & \frac{13}{3} \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & -15 \end{array}\right]$.
9. Row 2 minus 0.25 times Row 3: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & -\frac{14}{3} \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & \frac{13}{3} \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & -15 \end{array}\right]$.
10. Row 1 plus Row 2 plus Row 3: $\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & 0 & 4 - \frac{14}{3} + \frac{13}{3} \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & -\frac{14}{3} \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & \frac{13}{3} \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & -15 \end{array}\right]$.
11. Simplify: $c_1 = 4 - \frac{14}{3} + \frac{13}{3} = 4 - \frac{1}{3} = \frac{11}{3}$, $c_2 = -\frac{14}{3}$, $c_3 = \frac{13}{3}$. The last row $0 = -15$ shows the system is inconsistent, so $\mathbf{y}$ is not in $W$; the least-squares solution gives the projection.
12. The projection is $\mathbf{p} = \frac{11}{3}\mathbf{u}_1 - \frac{14}{3}\mathbf{u}_2 + \frac{13}{3}\mathbf{u}_3$, and $\mathbf{y} = \mathbf{p} + \mathbf{z}$, where $\mathbf{z}$ is orthogonal to $W$.
