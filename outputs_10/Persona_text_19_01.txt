1. The $\mathcal{B}$-matrix for $T$ is the matrix representation of $T$ with respect to the basis $\mathcal{B}$, which is $[T]_{\mathcal{B}} = P^{-1}AP$ where $P$ is the change of basis matrix from $\mathcal{B}$ to the standard basis.  
2. Construct $P$ by placing $\mathbf{b}_1$ and $\mathbf{b}_2$ as columns: $P = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix}$.  
3. Compute $P^{-1}$; first, find $\det(P) = (1)(4) - (1)(5) = 4 - 5 = -1$, so $P^{-1} = \frac{1}{-1} \begin{bmatrix} 4 & -5 \\ -1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -4 & 5 \\ 1 & -1 \end{bmatrix}$.  
4. Compute $AP$: $AP = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ 1 & 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} (1)(1)+(1)(1) & (1)(5)+(1)(4) \\ (-1)(1)+(3)(1) & (-1)(5)+(3)(4) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 9 \\ 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix}$.  
5. Compute $[T]_{\mathcal{B}} = P^{-1}AP = P^{-1}(AP) = \begin{bmatrix} -4 & 5 \\ 1 & -1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 9 \\ 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} (-4)(2)+(5)(2) & (-4)(9)+(5)(7) \\ (1)(2)+(-1)(2) & (1)(9)+(-1)(7) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -8+10 & -36+35 \\ 2-2 & 9-7 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 0 & 2 \end{bmatrix}$.  
6. The $\mathcal{B}$-matrix for $T$ is $\boxed{\begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 0 & 2 \end{bmatrix}}$.
