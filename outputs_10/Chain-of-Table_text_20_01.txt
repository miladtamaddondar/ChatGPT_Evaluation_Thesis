To find a basis for $\mathbb{R}^2$ consisting of $\mathbf{v}_1$ and another eigenvector $\mathbf{v}_2$ of $A$, first find the eigenvalues of $A$ by solving $\det(A - \lambda I) = 0$: $\det\left(\begin{bmatrix}0.6-\lambda & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.7-\lambda\end{bmatrix}\right) = (0.6-\lambda)(0.7-\lambda) - (0.3)(0.4) = 0$.

2. Expand and solve: $(0.6-\lambda)(0.7-\lambda) - 0.12 = 0 \implies 0.42 - 0.6\lambda - 0.7\lambda + \lambda^2 - 0.12 = 0 \implies \lambda^2 - 1.3\lambda + 0.3 = 0$.

3. Solve the quadratic: $\lambda = \frac{1.3 \pm \sqrt{1.69 - 1.2}}{2} = \frac{1.3 \pm \sqrt{0.49}}{2} = \frac{1.3 \pm 0.7}{2}$, so $\lambda_1 = 1$, $\lambda_2 = 0.3$.

4. We are given that $\mathbf{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix}3/7 \\ 4/7\end{bmatrix}$ is an eigenvector for $\lambda_1 = 1$; now find an eigenvector $\mathbf{v}_2$ for $\lambda_2 = 0.3$ by solving $(A - 0.3 I)\mathbf{v}_2 = 0$.

5. Compute $A - 0.3 I = \begin{bmatrix}0.6-0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.7-0.3\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0.3 & 0.3 \\ 0.4 & 0.4\end{bmatrix}$.

6. Set up the augmented matrix: $\left[\begin{array}{cc|c}0.3 & 0.3 & 0 \\ 0.4 & 0.4 & 0\end{array}\right]$.

7. Row 2 $\to$ Row 2 $- \frac{4}{3}$ Row 1: $\left[\begin{array}{cc|c}0.3 & 0.3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0\end{array}\right]$.

8. The first row gives $0.3x + 0.3y = 0 \implies x + y = 0$, so $y = -x$; thus, an eigenvector is $\mathbf{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}$.

9. Therefore, a basis for $\mathbb{R}^2$ consisting of $\mathbf{v}_1$ and another eigenvector $\mathbf{v}_2$ of $A$ is $\left\{\begin{bmatrix}3/7 \\ 4/7\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -1\end{bmatrix}\right\}$.
