1. Let \( \mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} \), \( \mathbf{u}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \), \( \mathbf{u}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 3 \\ 1 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix} \), \( \mathbf{u}_3 = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \); write \( \mathbf{y} = \mathbf{y}_W + \mathbf{y}_{W^\perp} \), where \( \mathbf{y}_W \) is in the span of \( \mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_2, \mathbf{u}_3 \) and \( \mathbf{y}_{W^\perp} \) is orthogonal to \( W \).
2. Set \( \mathbf{y}_W = c_1 \mathbf{u}_1 + c_2 \mathbf{u}_2 + c_3 \mathbf{u}_3 \), so \( \mathbf{y}_W = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} c_1 \\ c_2 \\ c_3 \end{bmatrix} \).
3. Set up the equation \( \mathbf{y}_W = \mathbf{y} \), so \( \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} c_1 \\ c_2 \\ c_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} \).
4. Write the augmented matrix: \( \left[ \begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 & 3 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 & -1 \end{array} \right] \).
5. Row 2 minus Row 1: \( R_2 \to R_2 - R_1 \): \( \left[ \begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 & -1 \end{array} \right] \).
6. Row 4 minus Row 1: \( R_4 \to R_4 - R_1 \): \( \left[ \begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 0 & -1 & 2 & -5 \end{array} \right] \).
7. Row 3 unchanged, now Row 3 minus (1/4) Row 2: \( R_3 \to R_3 - \frac{1}{4}R_2 \): \( \left[ \begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 0 & \frac{3}{4} & \frac{13}{4} \\ 0 & -1 & 2 & -5 \end{array} \right] \).
8. Row 4 plus (1/4) Row 2: \( R_4 \to R_4 + \frac{1}{4}R_2 \): \( \left[ \begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 0 & \frac{3}{4} & \frac{13}{4} \\ 0 & 0 & \frac{9}{4} & -\frac{21}{4} \end{array} \right] \).
9. Row 4 minus 3 times Row 3: \( R_4 \to R_4 - 3R_3 \): \( \left[ \begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & -1 & 4 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 0 & \frac{3}{4} & \frac{13}{4} \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & -10 \end{array} \right] \).
10. Back-substitute: From Row 4, \( 0c_1 + 0c_2 + 0c_3 = -10 \), which is inconsistent; so the vectors \( \mathbf{u}_1, \mathbf{u}_2, \mathbf{u}_3 \) are linearly dependent and do not span all of \(\mathbb{R}^4\), but we can find the least-squares solution.
11. The least-squares solution is given by \( \mathbf{c} = (U^T U)^{-1} U^T \mathbf{y} \), where \( U = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & -1 \\ 1 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \).
12. Compute \( U^T U = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 14 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( U^T \mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 16 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \).
13. Solve \( (U^T U) \mathbf{c} = U^T \mathbf{y} \): \( \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 14 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} c_1 \\ c_2 \\ c_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 \\ 16 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \).
14. Therefore, \( c_1 = 2, c_2 = \frac{8}{7}, c_3 = \frac{1}{3} \).
15. The projection \( \mathbf{y}_W = 2\mathbf{u}_1 + \frac{8}{7}\mathbf{u}_2 + \frac{1}{3}\mathbf{u}_3 = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 2 \\ 0 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} -\frac{8}{7} \\ \frac{24}{7} \\ \frac{8}{7} \\ -\frac{16}{7} \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} -\frac{1}{3} \\ 0 \\ \frac{1}{3} \\ \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 - \frac{8}{7} - \frac{1}{3} \\ 2 + \frac{24}{7} + 0 \\ 0 + \frac{8}{7} + \frac{1}{3} \\ 2 - \frac{16}{7} + \frac{1}{3} \end{bmatrix} \).
16. Simplify: \( \mathbf{y}_W = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{42 - 24 - 7}{21} \\ \frac{14 + 72}{7} \\ \frac{0 + 24 + 7}{21} \\ \frac{42 - 48 + 7}{21} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{11}{21} \\ \frac{86}{7} \\ \frac{31}{21} \\ \frac{1}{21} \end{bmatrix} \).
17. The orthogonal component is \( \mathbf{y}_{W^\perp} = \mathbf{y} - \mathbf{y}_W = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} \frac{11}{21} \\ \frac{86}{7} \\ \frac{31}{21} \\ \frac{1}{21} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{73}{21} \\ -\frac{65}{7} \\ \frac{32}{21} \\ -\frac{22}{21} \end{bmatrix} \).
18. Final answer: \( \mathbf{y} = \boxed{\begin{bmatrix} \frac{11}{21} \\ \frac{86}{7} \\ \frac{31}{21} \\ \frac{1}{21} \end{bmatrix}} + \boxed{\begin{bmatrix} \frac{73}{21} \\ -\frac{65}{7} \\ \frac{32}{21} \\ -\frac{22}{21} \end{bmatrix}} \), where the first vector is in \( W \) and the second is orthogonal to \( W \).
