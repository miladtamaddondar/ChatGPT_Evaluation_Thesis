1. The given matrix is \( A = \begin{bmatrix}1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ 3 & 9 & -1 & 5 \\ 2 & -6 & 4 & -3 \\ -4 & 12 & 2 & 7 \end{bmatrix} \) and its echelon form is \( \begin{bmatrix}1 & -3 & 2 & -4 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 & -7 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \).
2. The pivot columns in the echelon form are columns 1, 3, and 4, so the basis for \( \text{Col } A \) consists of the corresponding columns from \( A \): \( \left\{ \begin{bmatrix}1\\3\\2\\-4\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}2\\-1\\4\\2\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix}-4\\5\\-3\\7\end{bmatrix} \right\} \).
3. To find a basis for \( \text{Nul } A \), set up the equation \( A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0} \) and write the system using the echelon form: \( x_1 - 3x_2 + 2x_3 - 4x_4 = 0 \), \( 5x_3 - 7x_4 = 0 \), \( 5x_4 = 0 \).
4. From the last equation, \( x_4 = 0 \); from the second, \( 5x_3 = 0 \Rightarrow x_3 = 0 \); from the first, \( x_1 - 3x_2 = 0 \Rightarrow x_1 = 3x_2 \).
5. Letting \( x_2 = t \), the general solution is \( \mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix}3t\\t\\0\\0\end{bmatrix} = t\begin{bmatrix}3\\1\\0\\0\end{bmatrix} \), so a basis for \( \text{Nul } A \) is \( \left\{ \begin{bmatrix}3\\1\\0\\0\end{bmatrix} \right\} \).
6. The dimension of \( \text{Col } A \) is 3 (number of pivot columns), and the dimension of \( \text{Nul } A \) is 1 (number of free variables).
